Basholi Pashmina ki
opportunity, Kathua mein.

Kathua district sits at the foothills of the Himalayas with access to premium Himalayan pashmina wool and a centuries-old tradition of fine weaving. The district's skilled artisan base, combined with high-altitude climate that supports superior pashmina quality, makes it the natural epicenter for Basholi pashmina production. The region's cultural heritage and existing GI recognition provide authenticity and market credibility.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kathua ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Basholi Pashmina Cluster in Kathua district (towns of Basholi and Kathua city). Connect with 5-10 master weavers through the Kathua District Industries Centre. Study designs at the Regional Craft Museum in Jammu. Research GI certification requirements via the Controller of Certifications (GI Registry).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw pashmina wool directly from Himalayan wool merchants in Srinagar or Leh (₹8,000-12,000 per kg for Grade-A wool). Buy natural dyes from traditional dyers in Kathua bazaar. First batch: 10 kg pashmina + 2 kg dyes + silk ≈ ₹1.5-2 lakhs. Build relationships with 2-3 artisan families for consistent quality.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered Basholi weaver artisans through the Kathua Handloom Cooperative (registered with Dept. of Handloom, J&K). Provide raw materials on consignment basis; pay per finished piece (₹500-1,500 per shawl depending on complexity). Start with 5-10 pieces monthly, scale to 50-100 by month 6.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro-enterprise at udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for IEC code via DGFT for export eligibility. Register brand name at IP India. Obtain GI certification confirmation from Controller of Certifications. File for trademark protection. Create artisan certification documentation for authenticity claims.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C website (Shopify + local payment gateways). List on luxury platforms: Etsy Pro, The Loom, Craftsvilla Premium, and Luxury.com. Contact 20-30 high-end boutiques in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore via email + portfolio PDF. Pitch to Fab India, Good Earth, and Abraham & Thakore. Start Instagram influencer partnerships with luxury lifestyle creators (engagement rate > 5%).
6
Scale
Hire a local sales manager for institutional B2B relationships. Expand artisan network to 30-50 weavers across Kathua and neighboring Samba. Launch seasonal collections (wedding, festive). Apply for export grants under PMEGP or SFURTI for international trade shows (IITF Delhi, IHGF, Heimtextil Frankfurt). Target ₹50-100 Cr revenue by Year 2-3.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
up to ₹15L loan at 5% interest for artisan-led enterprises and micro-enterprises in traditional crafts including handlooms
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (25-35%) for manufacturing units in textile and handloom sector in non-metropolitan areas
🏚
Jammu & Kashmir Craft Development Scheme
subsidized training, material support, and export incentives for GI-tagged products
🏚
J&K Handloom Weavers Comprehensive Welfare Scheme
direct support to registered artisans, pension, health insurance, and cluster infrastructure development grants
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
