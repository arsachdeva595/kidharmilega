opportunity, Kathua mein.

Basohli cluster in Kathua district sits at 1876 ft altitude on the right bank of River Ravi, within 80 km of Kashmir's Changthangi goat plateaus — the primary source of ultra-fine pashmina fibers. The cluster contains 200+ registered artisans and weavers, many trained under families spanning 4+ generations. Buyer markets include Delhi's Chandni Chowk and premium malls (2-3 day reach), wedding-season wholesale across Punjab and North India, and direct export via courier to USA, UK, UAE ports.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kathua ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Basohli cluster (20 km from Kathua town) on a Wednesday or Friday haat day — speak directly with 5+ active weavers, visit 2-3 collection centers. Call the Kathua District Handloom Office (0191-2245647) and ask for the ODOP nodal officer. Register on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in) using Aadhaar — free, takes 15 minutes. Zero cost, do this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw pashmina fiber from Srinagar's Lal Bazaar or direct from Changthangi goat herders via Ladakh pashmina cooperatives (e.g., Ladakh Pashmina). First batch of 10 kg fine fiber (grade A) costs ₹35,000-45,000. Alternatively, partner with 1-2 artisans in Basohli cluster who already hold fiber stock — they'll process it for 15-25% commission. Recommend starting with 5-8 kg batch.
3
Produce / Partner
Option 1: Rent a 200-300 sq ft unit in Basohli craft cluster (₹4,000-6,000/month) and hire 1-2 experienced weavers on contract (₹250-400/day per person). Option 2: Direct artisan partnership — provide fiber, pay ₹800-1,500 per finished shawl (1-1.5 meter premium shawl). First batch cost: ₹80K-120K for 10 shawls including yarn prep and overhead. Timeline: 20-30 days per batch of 10 pieces.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online). File GI certificate of authenticity with DC Handloom office (₹5,000-8,000 processing, 40-60 days turnaround). Apply for IEC code via ICEGATE if exporting (₹500-1,000, 5-7 days). Get FSSAI if selling online as textile (usually not required for apparel, check your platform). Timeline: 60-90 days total.
5
First Sale
List 3-5 shawls on Meesho first (lowest barrier, 10% commission) and Etsy (6.5% commission + payment fees). Target first sale within 2-3 weeks; typical first order ₹4,000-8,000 from D2C. Simultaneously cold-message 10 Delhi boutiques with product photos and wholesale pricing (MOQ typically ₹20K-30K). First month target: ₹15K-25K revenue from mix of D2C and 1-2 wholesale inquiries.
6
Scale
At ₹10L monthly: hire a dedicated quality checker (₹12K-15K/month), add 2-3 more contract artisans, invest ₹50K in Instagram/Google ads, attend Heimtextil (Frankfurt) or regional Delhi gift fairs (₹80K registration + travel). Set up a small showroom in Kathua town or partner with a Delhi warehouse for faster D2C fulfillment. Explore B2B partnerships with corporate gifting platforms (Titan Taneira, GiftNow) who can scale orders to ₹50K-100K monthly.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% of project cost for general category, up to ₹10 lakh for SC/ST); 6% RoI subsidy for 5 years
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 direct credit + ₹500 discount voucher for raw materials + free certification + access to buyers' portal; targeted at traditional artisans and weavers
🏚
Jammu & Kashmir Self-Employment Scheme
₹5 lakh subsidy for weaving-related enterprises, 3% interest rate subsidy on bank loans up to ₹25 lakh
🏚
Handloom Weaver Welfare Scheme J&K
₹10,000 annual stipend per registered weaver employed + health insurance coverage
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
