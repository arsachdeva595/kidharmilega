Groundnuts/ Peanuts ki
opportunity, Botad mein.

Botad's semi-arid climate with black soil and low rainfall (500-700mm annually) creates ideal conditions for groundnut cultivation. The district has been a traditional groundnut hub for over 40 years with established farmer networks, proven agronomic practices, and ready access to processing infrastructure in nearby Ahmedabad and Rajkot. Local farming expertise and irrigation systems via Narmada canal make it uniquely suited for consistent, high-quality production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Botad ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Botad's agricultural extension center and attend groundnut farmer meetings at Botad Krishi Mandi. Connect with 3-5 established groundnut processors near Ahmedabad (Sabarmati region). Study export-quality standards at Gujarat Agro Exports Cluster in Rajkot. Review crop yield data from local ICAR research station.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified groundnut seeds from Gujarat Seed Corporation or seed villages in Godhra/Panchmahal. First batch: 10-20 tonnes from established farmers in Botad taluka at ₹4,500-5,500/tonne = ₹45-110L. Establish direct farmer purchase agreements for sustainable supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing processing units in Botad or Rajkot for cleaning, grading, and roasting (processing cost ₹2-3/kg). Alternatively, lease small de-hulling and grading equipment (₹15-25L capex). Ensure FSSAI-certified facility. Test for quality (moisture <8%, aflatoxin levels).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as MSME (agricultural processing). Apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office for export eligibility. Register brand name with DIPs. Obtain FSSAI license, ISO 9001 certification for export-quality assurance. Register with Spice Board if applicable.
5
First Sale
Launch on B2B platforms: IndiaMART, TradeKey, Alibaba. Directly approach FMCG companies (ITC, Britannia) and confectionery buyers. Sell retail through Amazon, BigBasket, local supermarkets. Export via APEDA-registered export houses. Target wholesale at Rajkot's Vegetable Market and Ahmedabad's Vegetable Mandi.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to value-added products (peanut butter, oil-pressed varieties). Build certified organic cultivation partnerships for premium positioning. Develop B2B export contracts via trade shows (Gulfood Dubai, Anuga Cologne). Scale processing capacity by 30-50% through additional leasing. Launch DTC e-commerce brand with storytelling around Botad's terroir.
