Cashew ki
opportunity, West Garo Hills mein.

West Garo Hills district in Meghalaya benefits from organic farming certification prevalence, making cashew production fetch 15-20% premium in export markets. Raw cashew nuts are sourced from local smallholder farms across the Garo Hills region and imported from coastal states (Andhra Pradesh, Karnataka, Kerala) via established trade routes through Guwahati. The district's cashew processing sector can serve export markets across Southeast Asia (Vietnam, Cambodia), Middle East (UAE), and Europe (Germany, UK) with competitive logistics via Guwahati railway and Shillong road connectivity.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — West Garo Hills ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3 raw cashew suppliers in Assam (Guwahati market, search 'cashew wholesale Assam') — ask for pricing, minimum batch, seasonality; visit any 3 farmer clusters in West Garo Hills (Tura, Nongstoin areas) by asking KVIC office; attend monthly haat in Tura town or Mendipathar on 3rd Saturday — free, zero cost, doable within 2 weeks
2
Source Raw Materials
First batch: 5000 kg raw cashew from Assam processors via Guwahati wholesale market or direct from Andhra Pradesh suppliers (Visakhapatnam, Kakinada); cost ₹90-110/kg = ₹4.5-5.5 lakhs for first trial batch; negotiate 45-day payment terms; connect via Indian Cashew Processing Association or call local cashew mills in Assam (Nagpur, Sonapur areas)
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 1200-1500 sq ft unit in Tura or Nongstoin for ₹8000-12000/month (food-grade processing space); set up basic roasting drum (₹80000), winnowing unit (₹40000), packing machine (₹120000), storage racks (₹25000); total machinery ₹265000; hire 2-3 local workers at ₹8000-10000/month; alternative: partner with existing 2-3 small cashew processors in the district for contract processing at 8-12% commission
4
Brand & Register
Udyam registration online free (udyamregistration.gov.in) — 5 days; apply for GI registration via Ministry of Agriculture & Farmers' Welfare, National Test House Delhi (₹5000-8000, 6-8 months); obtain IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT online (free, 5 days) if exporting; get organic certification from ECOCERT or Latur-based certifier (₹15000-25000, includes audits, 3 months)
5
First Sale
First sale: list on Amazon Launchpad or Flipkart Wholesale (0% platform fee first 3 months); prepare 100 kg roasted, salted kernels in 250g packs; first order target ₹40000-60000 from one premium hotel chain in Kolkata via LinkedIn outreach or via FreshToHome; expected first-month revenue ₹80000-120000 if selling both wholesale and D2C mix
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: expand machinery to semi-automatic setup (₹350000 more investment); hire packaging and QA team (4-5 staff); move to 2500 sq ft space; exhibit at Aafed (India Food Forum), Annapoorna Food Fair (February, Mumbai), IITF (November, Delhi) for bulk buyer connections; build export pipeline with 2-3 Southeast Asian distributors; establish raw material supply contracts with 5-8 farmer groups for year-round consistency

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
National Horticulture Mission (NHM)
50% subsidy on drip irrigation, mulching, vermicompost for cashew growers; ₹50000-100000 per hectare (extends to processing if farmer-linked)
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹10-25L subsidy (25% for general) for processing unit setup; interest-free loan eligibility; 3-4 months processing time via SIDBI or state nodal agency
🏚
Meghalaya Organic Farming Mission (MOFM)
100% subsidy on organic certification cost (₹15000-20000) if setting up farm-to-processed-food value chain
🏚
Meghalaya MSME Development Scheme
₹2L-5L interest subsidy on working capital loans, 3% reduced interest rate for 5 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
