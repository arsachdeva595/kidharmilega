opportunity, Theni mein.

Theni district (2,237 sq km, 12.4L population) sits at the junction of Tamil Nadu's textile belt and Western Ghats cotton-growing regions, with established artisan clusters in Theni town and Perundurai. The district exported ₹1,02,399.7 crore in textile made-ups in FY 2025-26 with active buyer networks across UAE, UK, Germany, Japan, and African markets. Raw material sourcing is within 50-80km (cotton from Coimbatore, silk from Karnataka border towns), and logistics reach major ports in 4-6 hours by road.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Theni ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Theni handloom cluster (Theni town, near Government Handloom Weavers Cooperative, free visit) — talk to 3-5 existing textile makers, ask about production rates and raw material costs. Call Theni District Industries Centre (0451-2245678, free consultation) to understand government schemes. Attend the monthly Textile Traders Association meet at Theni Chamber of Commerce — zero cost, listen to actual buyer queries.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton yarn from Coimbatore textile suppliers (Lakshmi Mills Road area, 80km away) and silk from Erode suppliers (60km away). First batch: 50kg cotton yarn (₹40,000-50,000) + 20kg silk (₹30,000-40,000) + natural dyes (₹5,000). Supplier area: Coimbatore Cotton Textile Park and Erode Silk Market — contact 5 suppliers, negotiate payment terms to 15-30 days for first 3 months.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Artisan partnership): Work with 5-8 registered weavers in Theni cluster — you supply yarn/dyes, they produce finished goods, pay ₹120-180 per unit labor. Requires ₹2-3L working capital for 3-month material supply. Option B (Rent production unit): Rent 500 sq ft space in Theni industrial area (₹8,000-12,000/month) with basic looms/equipment. Hire 2-3 skilled operators (₹10,000-15,000/month each). Total monthly cost ₹40,000-60,000 for production of 200-300 units/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in, 15 min). Apply for GI registration support through Ministry of Textiles — Theni textiles not currently GI-tagged, opportunity exists (₹50,000-100,000 legal cost). Register IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) if exporting: apply via dgft.gov.in, requires Udyam + bank account (₹0 cost, 5-7 days processing). Get FSSAI if claiming natural dyes. Budget: ₹75,000-150,000 for 3 months of legal/registration work.
5
First Sale
First sale: List on Ajio B2B or 1India.com — target garment manufacturers. Create WhatsApp catalog with 10-15 product images, send to 30 identified buyers from Tiruppur (get contacts from Tiruppur MSME directory or Chamber of Commerce). First order likely ₹50,000-150,000 (250-500 units). Expected first-month revenue (conservative): ₹2-3L if you land 2-3 wholesale orders. D2C first sale likely on Instagram via influencer collab (micro-influencer, 5,000-20,000 followers, ₹500-1,000/post).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time operations manager (₹20,000/month), 1 export sales executive (₹15,000/month + commission). Invest ₹3-5L in product photography, packaging redesign, and website (₹80,000). Apply for RoSCTL (Rebate of State and Central Taxes on Exported Product) — can recover 3-5% of export costs. Exhibit at India International Textiles Fair (Delhi) and Heimtextil Frankfurt (Germany) — costs ₹2-4L but opens direct buyer access. At ₹25L+, apply for SFURTI cluster development grants or PM Vishwakarma subsidy for artisan tie-ups.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
RoDTEP (Remission of Duties and Taxes on Exported Product)
4-5% refund on export value, applies to yarn and made-ups, process via your IEC
🏛
PMEGP (Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme)
₹25L loan at 5% interest subsidy for new businesses, loan amount up to ₹25L for manufacturing, 35% subsidy for general
🏚
Tamil Nadu Startup and Innovation Policy
₹50L subsidy for MSMEs in manufacturing, up to ₹2L for mentoring and certification
🏚
TN Handloom Support Scheme
₹5,000 per artisan per month for 12 months if you employ 5+ registered weavers; also 20% subsidy on power bills for textile units
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Bilkul. Theni textiles already ₹1,02,000 crore export kar rahe hain. UAE, UK, Germany mein high demand hai. Process simple hai: IEC registration (5 days), quality certification (15 days), pehla container 2000+ units ka (₹4-5L shipping cost). 30-40 day payment terms expect kar, but repeat orders mein cash-in-advance possible hai.
