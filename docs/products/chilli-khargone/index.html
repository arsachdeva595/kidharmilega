opportunity, Khargone mein.

Khargone district in Madhya Pradesh spans 6,477.89 sq. km across the Nimad-Malwa plateau, where 25,000 hectares are dedicated to chilli farming under ideal temperature conditions (20-27°C). The district's 1,421 farming villages are concentrated in the fertile regions around Khargone town, ensuring steady raw material supply within 20-30 km radius. Established trade routes connect to Maharashtra's major spice hubs (Nashik, Aurangabad) and Indore, with chilli already being exported from local cooperative societies.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Khargone ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khargone vegetable mandi (central market) and talk to 3-4 chilli traders — ask them current procurement costs, peak/off-season pricing, and buyer names. Free. Then call Khargone District Agricultural Office (0281-242-2333) and request list of chilli farming co-ops and APEDA-registered exporters nearby. Attend weekly haat in Bamkhul or Kasrawad village to see live chilli transactions. Do this by end of week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source from local farming co-ops (Khargone Horticulture Cooperative Society) or leased/owned farmland. First-season pilot: lease 3-5 hectares (₹15,000-25,000/hectare/season via Bamkhul or Palsud talukas). Raw chilli procurement: ₹40-60/kg during peak season (Mar-May). Dried chilli: ₹400-500/kg from local drying units. Supplier cluster: Kasrawad, Bamkhul villages (20-25 km from Khargone city).
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (low-cost): partner with 2-3 local farmer-artisans for drying and basic processing (your capital: ₹3-5L for 5-10 metric tons inventory, rent shared drying space ₹8,000-12,000/month). Option B (moderate): rent small drying/milling unit (500 sq. ft.) in Khargone town (₹12,000-18,000/month, 15-year lease available). Setup: solar dryers, basic mill (grinder + sieve). Invest ₹8-12L for equipment, inventory, 3 months operations.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 10 mins at khargone.nic.in). Apply for FSSAI registration (₹5,000-8,000, 30 days processing). Get IEC code from APEDA if exporting (free online, 5 days). Consider GI registration study (no GI exists for Khargone chilli yet — ₹2-3L upfront investment, 2-3 year process if pursuing). Packaging: get BIS-approved labels + FSSAI mandated text printed (₹15,000-25,000 for 10,000 units).
5
First Sale
First sale: contact 1-2 spice traders in Nashik, Maharashtra (via LinkedIn or district chamber of commerce referral) for trial order (50-100 kg dried chilli, ₹22,000-35,000). Simultaneously list on Amazon Seller Central or Flipkart (₹500 annual subscription) — start with chilli powder in 100g/250g packs (₹250-400 retail). First-month realistic revenue: ₹1.5-2.5L if you land 1 small B2B order + 200 D2C units.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire 1 operations manager (₹15,000/month) + 2 packing staff (₹10,000 each). Scale to 2 drying units, increase inventory to 20-25 tons. Exhibit at Spice Board trade shows (IIFC, Mumbai in Sep-Oct) and Maharashtra Food Tech Expo. Build WhatsApp/email list of 50+ B2B buyers (spice traders, exporters, QSR chains). Expand to premium products (smoked chilli, chilli salt) at 60-70% margin. Target ₹25-30L/month by month 18.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY)
₹10 lakh collateral-free loan for chilli processing unit, 5% interest subvention for first 2 years (₹5,000/year max)
🏛
Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)
₹6,000/year direct to farming account if you own cultivated land (3 installments, ₹2,000 each)
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Spices Board Subsidy
25% subsidy on drying equipment, milling machinery, packaging for FSSAI-registered units (max ₹2 lakh per unit, apply via khargone.nic.in)
🏚
Madhya Pradesh MSME Export Support
10% subsidy on freight for first 3 shipments to overseas buyers under Spice Board partnership
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
