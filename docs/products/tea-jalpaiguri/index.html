Jalpaiguri's location in the foothills of the Himalayas along the Teesta river creates the ideal micro-climate for cultivating high-grade tea. The district's temperate weather, naturally mineral-rich soil, and 17,000+ hectares of tea gardens make it one of India's finest tea-producing regions. The region's 150+ years of tea cultivation expertise and established supply chains provide immediate market access.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jalpaiguri ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jalpaiguri's major tea estates (Teesta Valley, Himalayan Bliss Gardens) in Siliguri cluster; meet with 3+ tea processors and small exporters; study Tea Board India's grading standards and export protocols at www.teaboard.gov.in.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contract with established tea processors in Siliguri (₹4-6 lakhs per 5000 kg batch); alternatively, lease 5-10 acres from land banks in Jalpaiguri district (₹1-2 lakhs/year); first-batch fresh leaf sourcing costs ₹2.5-3.5 lakhs for processing 10,000 kg.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with IPTA (Indian Institute of Tea Plantation Management)-certified processors in Jalpaiguri; or lease a co-processing facility (₹30-50K/month) with quality control labs; establish relationships with 2-3 estate suppliers for consistent leaf supply.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (free, 10 mins); apply for IEC code via dgft.gov.in; obtain APEDA registration for exports; register brand name at Ipindia.gov.in (₹4,500); get ISO 22000 food safety certification (₹1-1.5L) for export credibility.
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Fresh & BigBasket with 300-500g premium packs (₹400-800); contact Specialty Tea Association members and café chains (e.g., Blue Tokai, Brew Home); reach B2B importers via TradeKey and Global Sources; ship 5kg samples to 10 European tea wholesalers with invoices.
6
Scale
After 6 months, invest ₹15-25L in vertical integration (lease small processing unit); develop 3-4 blended variants; hire export manager for international B2B growth; enter regional supermarket chains; build direct-to-consumer subscription model (₹500-800/month boxes).
