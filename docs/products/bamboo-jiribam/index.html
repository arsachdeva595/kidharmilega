Jiribam district has abundant bamboo forests and a strong tradition of bamboo handicraft passed through generations. The region's skilled artisans combine sustainable harvesting practices with intricate craftsmanship, making it uniquely positioned to produce world-class bamboo products. The district's climatic conditions and soil composition create ideal growing conditions for premium bamboo species suitable for furniture and fine crafts.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jiribam ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jiribam district bamboo clusters in Sanakoubung and surrounding villages; connect with Manipur Bamboo Development Agency; study existing makers at Secretariat Craft Centre, Imphal; attend Northeast Craft Mela in Delhi for market insights.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw bamboo directly from sustainable forests in Jiribam district (₹8,000-12,000 per ton); contact local forest departments for licensed suppliers; first batch raw material cost: ₹80,000-1,20,000 for 10-15 tons.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 skilled bamboo artisans in Jiribam (average ₹15,000-25,000 monthly per artisan); establish small workshop space (500 sq ft rental ₹5,000-8,000/month); invest in basic tools (splitting, weaving, finishing equipment ~₹75,000); train team on design standardization.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME within 5 days online; apply for IEC (Import-Export Code) from DGFT for export; register brand name with Indian Intellectual Property Office; obtain Handicraft Certification from Ministry of DC; apply for FSSAI if producing food-contact items.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade, Etsy, and Flipkart Artisan (upload 15-20 product photos with detailed descriptions); contact interior designers via Instagram and Pinterest with lookbooks; reach out to fair-trade exporters like Craft India and SEWA; pitch to boutique home décor retailers in metros; leverage local handicraft networks.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan team to 10-15 people; develop 5-8 signature product lines; hire dedicated e-commerce manager for D2C scaling; pursue B2B export contracts with 2-3 international distributors; attend international trade fairs (Handmade in India, World Crafts Council); invest in design collaborations with renowned designers.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 one-time grant for artisans plus ₹500 skill development subsidy per artisan
🏛
PMEGP
up to ₹25 lakh government subsidy for MSMEs in handicraft sector with 35% capital subsidy for general category
🏚
Manipur Handicraft Development Scheme
25% subsidy on machinery and equipment for registered handicraft units
🏚
Manipur MSME Promotion Scheme
10% subsidy on working capital loans and 5% interest subvention for first 3 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
