Gorakhpur Terracotta ki
opportunity, Gorakhpur mein.

Gorakhpur has a 500+ year legacy of terracotta craftsmanship with deep-rooted artisan communities specialized in clay molding and kiln-firing. The region's abundant alluvial clay deposits and traditional knowledge transfer across generations make it the natural epicenter for authentic Indian pottery. Proximity to major markets like Lucknow and Delhi ensures consistent demand.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Gorakhpur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gorakhpur's primary terracotta clusters in Taramandal, Kachori Bazar, and Bansgaon villages. Spend 2-3 weeks apprenticing with senior artisans like those in Gorakhpur Pottery Association. Study GI certification requirements from DC Handicraft office. Budget: ₹5,000-10,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw alluvial clay directly from local clay suppliers in Gorakhpur (₹50-80 per bag). Identify kiln operators (e.g., traditional coal/wood kilns in Taramandal). First batch of 500 units (small figurines, pots) costs ₹25,000-40,000 including clay, fuel, and labor.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established artisan families in Gorakhpur for production (revenue sharing: 40-50% to artisans). Register with Gorakhpur Pottery Association for skill certification. Set up a small showroom space in Gorakhpur city or work from artisan studios. Initial production capacity: 100-200 pieces/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam registration (online, free) and IEC code from nearest DGFT office. Apply for GI certification tag using District DC Handicraft's template. Create brand identity emphasizing 'Gorakhpur Heritage' and 'Handmade by Master Artisans.' Obtain FSSAI if selling food-related items. Estimated cost: ₹15,000.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (list 50+ products, optimize SEO for 'Gorakhpur terracotta'), Amazon Handmade, and own Shopify store. Approach home décor boutiques in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore directly. Post behind-the-scenes artisan videos on Instagram and YouTube. Engage in 2-3 craft fairs (Crafts Council of India events). Target: 50-100 orders in Month 2-3.
6
Scale
After profitability, hire 3-5 additional artisans and upgrade kiln capacity. Develop 20-30 new product designs (planters, wall tiles, animal figurines). Expand to wholesale B2B channels (interior designer networks, corporate bulk orders). Apply for export subsidy schemes. Target ₹50L annual revenue by Month 18.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15,000 non-dilutive grant + 5% concession interest on loans + free certification for traditional artisans
🏛
PMEGP (Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for registered artisan business with 25-35% margin requirement
🏚
UP Handicraft Development Scheme
₹5L interest subsidy on term loans and 50% subsidy on marketing expenses for registered artisans
🏚
Uttar Pradesh One District One Product (ODOP) Promotion Scheme
30% subsidy on exports and brand registration support
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
