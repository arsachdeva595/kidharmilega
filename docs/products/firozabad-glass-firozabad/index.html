opportunity, Firozabad mein.

Firozabad, 40 km from Agra and 250 km from Delhi, is India's glass capital with 150+ integrated production units and 6 lakh workers across bangle-making and decoration. Raw materials (silica sand, soda ash, cullet) are sourced from Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh, and recycled glass streams within the cluster. Buyers range from Indian home décor retailers to institutional labs to overseas markets in Sri Lanka, Southeast Asia, and Middle East, with 50% of production exported.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Firozabad ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nai Sarak cluster in Firozabad city (center of bangle/glassware production); call 3-4 established unit owners and ask for a 1-hour factory walk-through; attend the monthly AIGMF (All India Glass Manufacturers Federation) meetup if scheduled; register free on Udyam MSME portal to understand compliance requirements — zero cost, doable within 5 days.
2
Source Raw Materials
Raw glass cullet and soda ash sourced from Rajasthan suppliers (Kota, Udaipur region) via established cluster traders; first batch of 500 kg mixed cullet costs ₹8,000-12,000. Connect via Firozabad Glass Merchants Association or directly approach traders in Nai Sarak bazaar. First-batch material cost ₹15,000-25,000 for diversified product trial.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a semi-equipped glass unit in Nai Sarak cluster (₹8,000-15,000/month with furnace access) or partner with a master artisan on 60-40 profit-share basis to avoid upfront capex. If renting, budget ₹2-3L for molds, tools, and initial material stock. If artisan partnership, ₹50,000 advance + material cost only. Production capacity: 50-200 pieces/day depending on product complexity.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (₹0, online, 10 min), apply for GI tag certification through Ministry MSME (₹3,000-8,000 for documentation, 6-12 months approval), obtain PAN and GST (₹0-2,000 if filing yourself), and apply for IEC (Import-Export Code) via DGFT portal if targeting export (₹0, 7-10 days). Total regulatory cost: ₹5,000-12,000, timeline 4-6 months for full setup.
5
First Sale
First sale: post 10-15 product photos on Instagram and Etsy, target niche hashtags (#GlassGiftIdeas, #FirozabadGlass, #IndianHandcraft); list on Amazon Handmade with FBA; reach out to 20-30 Delhi/Agra interior design studios via email with wholesale rates; expect first order (₹5,000-15,000) within 30 days from retail or B2B buyer. First-month revenue projection: ₹20,000-50,000 from 3-5 orders.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue, hire 1 senior artisan (₹15,000/month) and 2 junior helpers (₹8,000/month each); scale furnace capacity or add second unit; apply for MSME subsidy schemes (PMEGP up to ₹25L with 25% subsidy); exhibit at India Handloom Expo, Diwali Home & Décor Fairs, and export trade shows in Mumbai/Bangalore; launch B2B export channel via Sri Lankan and Middle Eastern trade associations; build private-label contracts with 3-5 hospitality chains.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Up to ₹25 lakh loan with 25% subsidy (10% for general category, 35% for SC/ST/Women), 7-year repayment, 24% interest; apply via District Industries Centre.
🏛
MUDRA Scheme (Shishu/Kishor/Tarun)
Up to ₹50 lakh unsecured loan at 8-12% interest; Tarun limit suitable for scaling; apply via scheduled bank or NBFC.
🏚
Uttar Pradesh MSME Cluster Development Scheme
30% subsidy on machinery and equipment up to ₹5 lakh per unit; apply via State Industries Department.
🏚
UP Export Promotion Scheme
5% rebate on export turnover; incentives on freight for first-time exporters; coordinate via UPSIDA or Export Promotion Council.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
