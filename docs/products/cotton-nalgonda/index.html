Cotton ki
opportunity, Nalgonda mein.

Nalgonda has a centuries-old cotton weaving tradition with skilled artisans who have perfected the craft across generations. The region's climate and soil conditions are ideal for cotton cultivation, and the proximity to major textile hubs enables efficient raw material sourcing and distribution. The district's established weaver communities provide a ready talent pool and cultural ecosystem for cotton product manufacturing.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Nalgonda ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nalgonda weaver clusters in Sadasivpet and Bhongir towns; connect with Nalgonda Weavers Cooperative Society; study competitor products on Etsy, Amazon India, and local boutiques; attend textile trade shows at IGTC Hyderabad.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton from Nalgonda district cotton farmers or NAFED procurement centers; first batch of 500 kg raw cotton costs ₹60,000-80,000; establish relationships with 2-3 dye suppliers in Hyderabad (₹15,000-25,000 for first dyeing batch); negotiate bulk pricing.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 registered artisan groups in Sadasivpet cluster or individual master weavers; visit workshops to select partners; agree on per-piece production cost (₹150-300 per meter depending on design complexity); start with 50-100 pieces first production run.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME online via udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC code through DGFT portal; register trademark with IP India for brand name; get GST registration (12%); obtain required textile certificates from quality testing labs.
5
First Sale
Launch on Shopify/WooCommerce store targeting international audience; list on Amazon India and Flipkart with premium positioning; set up Instagram & Pinterest content marketing; approach 5-10 online boutiques for wholesale partnerships; reach out to sustainable fashion retailers.
6
Scale
Expand to 10-15 artisan partners after proving first 500 orders; develop 3-4 new seasonal collections every 6 months; hire operations/logistics manager; explore B2B corporate gifting contracts; apply for export subsidies and APEDA support; expand to presence in 2-3 major international markets.

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹3L loan at 0% interest for artisans and weavers in Nalgonda cluster
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy and ₹10L collateral-free loan for textile manufacturing startups
🏚
Telangana Handloom Policy 2020
₹5L subsidy for establishing new handloom units + buyer-seller linkage support
🏚
T-Fibre (Telangana Fibre Policy)
₹2L technology/equipment grants and 5% subsidy on raw material procurement for textile processors
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
