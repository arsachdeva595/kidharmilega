Kodo Kutki ki
opportunity, Mandla mein.

Mandla's tribal-dominated regions have cultivated Kodo Kutki for centuries using traditional rain-fed farming methods on mineral-rich soil. The district's agro-climatic conditions and farmer expertise make it naturally suited for these drought-resistant millets. Local knowledge systems and established farming networks provide competitive advantage in consistent quality production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mandla ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mandla's farming clusters in Niwas and Mandla tahsil; connect with Department of Agriculture Mandla for yield data and farmer associations like Mandla Krishi Vikas Samiti; study competitor brands like Namdhari, Conscious Food on Amazon.
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner directly with farmer cooperatives in Mandla district (contact District Cooperative Bank) for first batch of 10-20 MT at ₹45-55/kg (wholesale cost ₹4.5-5.5 lakh for 10MT); ensure organic certification documentation.
3
Produce / Partner
Set up processing unit (cleaning, sorting, packaging) in Mandla town with local equipment suppliers; OR outsource to existing millet processing units in Madhya Pradesh; target capacity of 20MT/month minimum.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online portal for subsidy access; obtain IEC code from DGFT Indore office; register brand name with Intellectual Property office; get FSSAi licensing for processing/packaging unit; ISO 9001 certification for export.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Pantry, BigBasket, Blinkit with 2kg premium packs at ₹320-380; approach 50-100 organic retailers in metros via sales reps; create LinkedIn B2B presence for export inquiries; launch Instagram with farm-to-table storytelling content.
6
Scale
After first 6 months, expand product line to Kodo flour and breakfast blends; establish direct relationships with 3-5 export distributors in USA/Europe; secure corporate bulk orders from health chains; scale farmer network to 200+ households across Mandla.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan with 35% subsidy for food processing MSME in Tier 2/3 towns
🏛
PM Kisan Samman Nidhi
ensures farmer partners get ₹6000/year supporting supply chain stability
🏚
Madhya Pradesh State Agri-Export Policy
5% subsidy on FOB value for agricultural exports; support for IEC registration
🏚
MP Kisan Kalyan Yojana
encourages cultivation of nutri-cereals through MSP support and free seeds for Kodo Kutki farmers
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
