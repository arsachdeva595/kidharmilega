Nagali Products ki
opportunity, Dang mein.

Dang district has a strong tradition of millet cultivation, particularly Nagali (finger millet), which thrives in the region's soil and climate. The tribal communities of Dang have perfected time-honored recipes for Nagali products over generations, making this district the natural hub for authentic production. Local availability of raw materials and established artisan networks provide significant cost and quality advantages.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Dang ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dang district ODOP clusters in Ahwa and Subir taluka; meet artisan groups under SFURTI; study Nagali cultivation in villages near Waghai; interact with existing producers at Dang Food Park if operational
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Nagali millet directly from farmer cooperatives in Dang (₹80-120/kg bulk rate); procure jaggery from Dang jaggery makers; first batch raw material cost ₹60,000-80,000 for 500kg production trial
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with ODOP-registered artisan groups in Dang for initial contract production; or lease space in Ministry-approved food processing cluster; hire trained food technologists for recipe standardization and FSSAI compliance
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification); obtain IEC code for export; apply FSSAI license (₹5,000-15,000); trademark brand name; create packaging with nutritional labels and origin story; GI certification research (currently not GI-tagged)
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart, and JioMart with focus on 'traditional' and 'organic' categories; create Instagram/YouTube content on Nagali health benefits and tribal heritage; approach specialty stores in Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi NCR; list on Airtable and local Gujarati food communities
6
Scale
Expand product range (Nagali bars, cookies, flakes); build direct wholesale partnerships with 50+ health food stores across 5 metro cities; apply for PMEGP grant for facility upgrade; explore export certifications (ISO, organic); hire sales team for B2B channels
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan at 5% interest for food processing MSMEs with partial credit guarantee
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹50 lakh per cluster for artisan skill development and common facility centers in Dang
🏚
Gujarat ODOP Marketing Support Scheme
co-branding and exhibition participation support for registered ODOP entrepreneurs
🏚
Gujarat Food Processing Policy 2020
capital subsidy of 25% for food processing units with concessional land and utilities in designated zones
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
