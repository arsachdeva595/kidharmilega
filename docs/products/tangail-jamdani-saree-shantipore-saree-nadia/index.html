The district's water quality, access to skilled weavers trained in traditional techniques, and existing cooperative networks make it ideal for premium saree production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Nadia ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Shantipur weaving cluster (15km from Krishnanagar), meet master weavers at Nadia Handloom Cooperative Society, study existing brands (Arang, Deepjyoti), understand GI compliance requirements from DC Handloom office.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 60s-120s cotton yarn from Coimbatore suppliers (₹180-220/kg), gold zari from Surat wholesale market (₹800-1200/roll), build relationships with 2-3 dye suppliers in Kolkata; first batch cost ₹50,000-100,000 for 10-15 sarees.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 master weavers in Shantipur earning 30-40% of saree price per piece; formalize through Udyam registration naming weavers as partners; ensure GI certification compliance by sourcing only certified materials and maintaining production records.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME, obtain IEC code for export, file GI claim at trademark office, create brand identity emphasizing heritage + artisan names, register 'Crafted in Nadia' branding with DC Handloom documentation.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram & Pinterest (visual-heavy), list on Etsy (20-30% commission but 60M international buyers), open Myntra Luxe storefront, build own Shopify store for D2C, target wedding platforms like Shaadisaga; initial 50-100 pieces in first 3 months.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 15-20 weavers, introduce 4 seasonal collections, establish B2B wholesale partnerships with 10-15 boutiques, launch referral & influencer program targeting fashion bloggers, invest in paid Instagram/Pinterest ads, consider pop-up stores in Bangalore & Mumbai.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma – up to ₹15,000 toolkit grant + 5% interest subvention on loans up to ₹1L for handloom artisans and entrepreneurs
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme) – 35% subsidy (₹87.5L max) for enterprises with ₹25L+ project cost in manufacturing sector
🏚
West Bengal Handloom Development Scheme – 15% capital subsidy for looms, raw material subsidies, and export incentives up to 10% of invoice value
🏚
Udyam Plus Scheme (West Bengal) – Free business registration, tax incentives for MSMEs, and access to skill development programs for handloom weavers
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
