Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — East Jaintia Hills ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jowai town spice markets and connect with existing turmeric farmers in Synrang and Mynkong clusters. Interview 5-10 farmers about yield, costs, and certifications. Study Assam Agricultural University turmeric varieties suited to East Jaintia climate.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source turmeric rhizome seeds from ICAR-IISR Calicut or local Jowai farmers (₹2-3 L for 1-2 hectare initial batch). Identify organic fertilizer suppliers in Shillong (₹40-60K for first season). Budget ₹80-120K for initial nursery setup and irrigation.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 certified organic farmers in Mynkong block via contract farming model. Set up a basic processing unit (drying, grinding, packaging) in Jowai town (₹5-8 L investment). Alternatively, process through Meghalaya Agro Industries (MAI) cooperative in Shillong.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam enterprise under MSME classification. Obtain IEC code from nearest customs office in Shillong for exports. Get organic certification from IFOAM-accredited body like OneCert or NEA (₹40-60K, 4-6 month process). Register trademark with 'Jaintia Gold' or similar name.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart Wholesale, and BigBasket with minimum 500kg inventory. Approach Ayurvedic stores in Delhi, Bangalore via LinkedIn/direct outreach. List on Tradeindia and Alibaba for B2B export. Partner with 2-3 organic retail chains in metropolitan cities.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 5-8 hectares through contract farming. Develop 2-3 value-added products (turmeric milk powder, face masks). Secure bulk export orders from Malaysia, Singapore via trade fairs. Build D2C brand awareness through Instagram wellness content and affiliate partnerships with health influencers.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for agri-processing with 25-35% government contribution.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme for Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹20L for processing cluster development and skill training.
🏚
Meghalaya Agricultural Mechanization Scheme
subsidized processing equipment up to 50% cost for ODOP clusters.
🏚
Meghalaya Start-Up Fund
₹10-20L equity-free grant for agri-startups with export potential registered in state.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
