Naga Mircha ki
opportunity, Peren mein.

Peren district, 45 km from Kohima capital, is the epicenter of Naga Mircha cultivation in the Zeliangrong region with 150 active farming households concentrated in Seiyhama village, Botsa block. Raw materials — soil with natural water retention, year-round sunlight, and existing bamboo/banana plantations — are on-farm or within 5 km radius. Buyers span metros (Delhi, Mumbai), international markets (USA, UK, Singapore, Australia), and emerging 3rd-party platforms (Amazon, Flipkart) selling at ₹300-500/250g retail, with wholesale rates at ₹150-200/kg.

✓ Verified Success Story
Seiyhama Chilli Farmer Collective

200 households in Seiyhama village collectively generated ₹27 lakh revenue in 2022 by consolidating Naga Mircha production and selling through haat (village markets) and early e-commerce channels. Individual household earnings ranged ₹60,000-₹70,000 annually; larger-scale operators earned ₹4-5 lakhs. By 2024, they sold 2,000 kgs at the 3rd Organic King Chilli Festival and are targeting ₹1 crore collective turnover in 2023-24.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Peren ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Contact Seiyhama village farming group lead (ask Peren agriculture office for contact); attend 3rd Organic King Chilli Festival scheduled September 2024 or annual festival; visit Botsa block cluster office and request farm walk with 2-3 established farmers — all free, doable within 2 weeks
2
Source Raw Materials
Source first 500kg batch directly from Seiyhama farmers at ₹80-120/kg (harvest season July-October); first-batch cost ₹40,000-60,000 including logistics to your processing center. Supplier WhatsApp: contact through Peren District Agriculture Department, Botsa block office
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner with 2-3 Seiyhama farmers to contract-farm 0.5 hectare (upfront cost ₹30,000-50,000 for seeds, setup); Option B: Rent 1000 sq ft processing unit in Kohima/Dimapur (₹5,000-8,000/month) + buy dried chillies wholesale. Processing cost (drying, sorting, packaging): ₹20-30/kg. No pesticide spraying — natural sun-drying takes 7-10 days
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 15 mins); apply for GI certification use (Form GI-4 at IP Office Delhi, ₹5,000, 4-6 weeks); for export, file IEC application at DGFT portal (₹0, online, 3 days). Get FSSAI license (₹500-1,000, 30 days) for food processing. Certification cost: ORGANIC India certification ₹15,000-25,000 annually (if pursuing organic label); apply for Chief Minister's Micro Finance Initiative subsidy through Nagaland state MSME office
5
First Sale
List first batch (250g packs) on Amazon Seller Central / Flipkart; target initial order: 50-100 units to food bloggers and metro specialty shops via direct outreach (email, WhatsApp); expected first-month revenue from 100 units at ₹350 retail = ₹35,000. Reach out to 2-3 premium food distributors in Delhi (e.g., Soar Organics, FreshTake) with wholesale pricing
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (≈1,000kg/month sales): hire 1-2 packaging/QC staff (₹12,000-15,000/month each); establish supply agreement with 5-8 Seiyhama farmers for consistent 1,500kg/month; register for DGFT export incentive schemes (ROSL, MEIS if applicable); exhibit at ISM food shows (New Delhi, November), Specialty Food Asia (Singapore, March), and India Food Forum for wholesale buyer connections
