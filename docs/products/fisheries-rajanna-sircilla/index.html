Rajanna Sircilla district has extensive water bodies including rivers, tanks, and ponds ideal for both freshwater and brackish water aquaculture. The region has established fishing communities with traditional expertise, government fisheries infrastructure, and proximity to major consumption centers like Hyderabad and secondary cities. The cool water bodies and ideal pH levels support diverse fish species cultivation.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Rajanna Sircilla ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Rajanna Sircilla's fish markets in Tandur and Sircilla town (peak hours 4-7 AM). Connect with Department of Fisheries extension office in Sircilla. Study established fishery clusters at Tandur and Manakondur. Interview 5-10 existing fish farmers and wholesalers to understand seasonal patterns, average yields, and current pricing.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source quality fish seeds from APCCCF (Andhra Pradesh Cooperative Central Cattle Federation) hatcheries in Warangal or Vijayawada (₹4-8L for initial fingerlings). Arrange pond/tank lease in villages like Tandur or Neelakonda (₹15-30K per acre annually). Purchase fish feed from local suppliers like Alltech or Skretting through Hyderabad distributors. First batch production cost: ₹50-75K per acre for 6 months.
3
Produce / Partner
Lease 2-3 fishponds in established fishing villages (Tandur, Manakondur, Neelakonda). Partner with experienced local fishermen as co-operators (shared profit model 60-40). Install basic infrastructure: water testing kits, aerators, nets. Begin with indigenous fish species (Catla, Rohu, Mrigal) and expand to specialty fish (Tilapia, Shrimp) after 12 months. Expected yield: 3-4 tons per acre per cycle.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online within 5 minutes. Obtain IEC code from nearest APEDA-recognized office in Hyderabad (10 days, ₹2-3K). Register fishery business with Department of Fisheries, Rajanna Sircilla (₹1-2K). Obtain FSSAI license for any processing (₹2-5K). Create brand identity with local Telugu/Telangana cultural messaging. Trademark brand name with IP India.
5
First Sale
Launch on Blinkit and Zepto with fresh fish delivery in Hyderabad (50+ customers first month). Open account with major wholesale platforms like Ninjacart or BigBasket B2B. Directly supply to 10-15 restaurants/hotels in Hyderabad and Warangal via cold-chain logistics. Establish stall at weekly farmers markets in Hyderabad (Mehdipatnam, Somajiguda). Create WhatsApp broadcast list for recurring B2B customers.
6
Scale
After month 6, expand to 8-10 fishponds across district. Introduce value-added products: frozen fish fillets, fish curry paste, dried fish. Partner with cold-chain logistics providers for metro delivery (Delhi, Bangalore, Chennai). Build brand presence on Instagram with catch-to-table storytelling. Obtain organic certification and premium pricing (10-20% premium). Create contract farming with 20-30 additional farmer partners. Target ₹3-5 Cr revenue by year 2.
Government Schemes

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general, 50% for SC/ST) for fishery enterprises in Telangana
🏛
MUDRA Scheme
up to ₹10L collateral-free loan for micro-fishery businesses through scheduled banks
🏚
Telangana Fisheries Development Scheme
₹50K-₹1L per acre subsidy for pond construction and equipment
🏚
Telangana Agriculture/Fisheries Export Promotion Scheme
5-10% subsidy on certified exports through APEDA
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
