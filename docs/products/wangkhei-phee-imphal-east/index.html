Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Imphal East ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Wangkhei Konsam Leikai cluster in person — spend 2-3 hours talking to 5-6 weavers, ask about loom rent (₹2000-4000/month), production timelines, dye sources. Call DC Handloom Imphal East office (+91-385-2411284 approx) for GI registration details. Free, doable this month. Go on a haat day (ask locals) to see active selling.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source directly from Wangkhei area weavers (3-5 reliable contacts after step 1); negotiate first batch of 30-50 pieces in mixed designs (sarees, mufflers, shawls) — cost ₹80,000-120,000 total at ₹2000-2500 per piece. Budget 35-40 days production time. Alternative: approach Wangkhei Konsam cluster cooperative if registered.
3
Produce / Partner
Start as a designer-aggregator without owning looms — partner with 4-8 weavers on per-piece commission (₹300-500 per piece), you handle design, quality QC, finishing, packaging. Rent 800-1000 sqft workspace in Imphal East for storage, photography, packing — ₹8000-12000/month. Total recurring cost ₹15,000/month (space + materials). Alternatively, rent 2-3 looms directly in cluster — ₹6000-12000/month per loom plus artisan wages ₹15,000-25,000/month per loom.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry MSME portal, 15 mins, free) — upload on www.udyamregistration.gov.in. File GI Seller registration with DC Handloom Imphal East (no cost, 10-15 day approval). If exporting: apply for IEC code through DGFT portal (₹0, 5-7 days). File MSME annual return by March 31. Protect your brand name via unregistered trademark on social media first (₹0), consider TM registration later (₹4500 for 10 years).
5
First Sale
First sale: list 8-10 pieces on Etsy (free listing, 6.5% commission) + Instagram shop (free setup) simultaneously. Price at ₹5500-8500 per saree (50-60% margin). Target first order from design boutiques via email outreach to 20 boutiques (Sarangi, Fab India, local luxury stores) — expect 3-5 responses, first order likely 30-50 pieces. Expected first-month revenue ₹40,000-80,000 (mix of D2C + bulk wholesale).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time operations manager (₹18,000/month), 1 quality controller (₹15,000/month). Expand weaver base from 4-8 to 15-20. Add export sales — reach out to Bangalore-based export houses (Craftsindia, Dastkar) who sell to Japan/Korea. Apply for Integrated Handloom Development Scheme subsidy (₹10-25L) to mechanize finishing and dyeing. Attend IITF Delhi and Handloom Expo (annual), get press coverage with Bollywood angle (designer collaborations). Invest in professional product photography (₹20,000-30,000).

Frequently Asked Questions

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

Minimum ₹1.5-2 lakhs: ₹50,000 for first batch of 30-40 pieces (direct weaver payment), ₹30,000 workspace rental (3 months advance), ₹20,000 photography + branding, ₹20,000 operational buffer. Maximum ₹4-5 lakhs if you rent 2-3 looms + hire artisan manager. Most founders start at ₹2L and scale gradually.
No weaving experience needed if you hire artisans. You need: (1) design sense or ability to source designs, (2) sales/marketing background, (3) willingness to visit weavers every week for 6-8 months. Weaving is 100% artisan-dependent; your job is aggregation + branding + selling. Robert Naorem was a designer, not a weaver.
Integrated Handloom Development Scheme: ₹10-25 lakhs subsidy for loom upgrades, dye unit, storage. ODOP scheme: free design consultation, packaging subsidy (₹50K-1L), market event participation (IITF, Handloom Expo), zero direct cash but huge indirect support. Chief Minister's Enterprise Development Scheme (Manipur): ₹2-5 lakhs for new MSME. GI registration: free, helps in export pricing. Apply via DC Handloom office, Imphal East (near Secretariat).
(1) Etsy + Instagram: list 8-10 pieces, get first D2C sale in 5-10 days at ₹5000-7000 per piece. (2) Email 100 boutiques (Sarangi, Fab India, FabIndia, ethnic wear chains): expect 3-5 responses, first bulk order 30-50 pieces. (3) Attend IITF Delhi (Nov-Dec) and Handloom Expo (annual): meet 500+ wholesale buyers in 3 days. First customer likely bulk wholesale boutique, not D2C.
Bilkul. Get IEC code (free, 5 days via DGFT portal), register as GI seller with DC Handloom (₹0). Export to Japan, South Korea, USA, UK at 35-50% FOB margin. Japan importers pay ₹3000-4500 per saree, you provide at ₹2000. Handshake.co, Alibaba B2B, or direct outreach to fashion importers in Bangalore. 60-day payment terms standard. First export order takes 4-6 weeks to execute.
Worst case: you've invested ₹2L, sold nothing in 6 months. Exit options: (1) Sell finished inventory to boutique wholesalers at 15-20% loss = recover ₹1.4-1.6L. (2) Pivot to B2B corporate gifting (faster cash, lower risk). (3) Return to weaver partnership model, reduce overhead, focus on 2-3 bestselling designs only. Handloom business is not binary — you can downsize, not exit completely. Most founders who start don't fully flop; they plateau at ₹3-5L/year and survive. Upside is ₹20-50L/year if you scale to 20+ weavers and get export orders.
