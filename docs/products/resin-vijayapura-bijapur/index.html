Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Vijayapura (Bijapur) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Vijayapura forest division office and connect with collectors in Basavan Bagewadi, Londa, and Sindagi taluks. Study resin grades, drying standards, and pricing at local mandis. Attend NIFTEM or CSIR-IIIM workshops on natural product processing in nearby Bangalore/Bengaluru.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw tree bark and resin from forest collectors in Vijayapura district (₹40-60/kg for semi-processed resin). Negotiate seasonal contracts with 3-5 primary collectors. Initial 500kg batch costs approximately ₹30,000-40,000. Build relationships through local forest cooperative societies.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established resin processors in Vijayapura town or Belagavi (30km away) for initial batches. Invest in basic drying, filtering, and storage setup (₹3-5 lakhs). Once production stabilizes, establish your own small unit with solar drying racks and manual processing tools.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) classification as a manufacturing unit. Apply for IEC (Import-Export Code) from nearest DGFT office in Bengaluru. Register trademark with IP India. Obtain Forest Department clearance for resin trade. Get ISO 9001 certification for quality assurance (cost: ₹50,000-1,00,000).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Business, IndiaMART, and TradeKey targeting B2B buyers. Start D2C via Instagram/Facebook with focus on natural varnish and craft communities. Reach out to Ayurveda and cosmetics brands for bulk orders. List on Spice Park marketplaces and regional agricultural export forums.
6
Scale
After first 6 months, expand product line to include essential oil extracts and incense sticks. Partner with 2-3 export trading houses for international sales. Invest in value-addition equipment (extraction, blending). Target organized retail chains like Nature's Basket and local Ayurveda shops. Build B2B relationships with 5+ pharmaceutical companies.
