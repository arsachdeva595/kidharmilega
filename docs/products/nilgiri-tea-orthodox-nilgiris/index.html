Nilgiri Tea (Orthodox) ki
opportunity, Nilgiris mein.

Nilgiris district in the Western Ghats (1,000-2,500m elevation) produces Orthodox teas from Camellia sinensis var. sinensis planted since 1835, with first commercial sales in 1862 — making it India's second-oldest tea region. The district has 10,000+ hectares of tea estates concentrated around Coonoor, Ooty, and Ketti, supplying raw materials and processing infrastructure. Cochin port (120km via road) and proximity to UK, German, Japanese importers enable exports; domestic buyers are premium cafes, hotels, and tea traders in Bengaluru, Chennai, Delhi.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Nilgiris ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call Nilgiri Planters' Association (Coonoor, +91-423-2440xxx) and attend their quarterly meetings (free). Visit Coonoor tea auction market (every Tuesday) and talk to 5 tea merchants about Orthodox production volumes, pricing, buyer demand. Register on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in, 10 minutes, free). Zero cost, start this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw tea leaves from tea estates in Coonoor (Jaiprakash Estate, Glendale, Highland estates) or tea processing units near Ooty. First batch: 50kg loose Orthodox leaves = ₹15,000-25,000 from estate directly or ₹18,000-30,000 from merchant wholesalers. For 500kg starter batch = ₹150,000-300,000. Supplier area: Coonoor town (main wholesale market), 8km from Ooty town.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner with an existing tea processor (Nilgiri Tea Factory, Coonoor) for contract manufacturing — provide raw leaves, pay ₹500-800/kg processing fee, get finished Orthodox tea. Option B: Rent a small 500 sq.ft. processing unit in Coonoor town (₹5,000-8,000/month) with withering beds, rolling machines (buy used: ₹1.5-2.5L), drying chamber. Initial setup: ₹3-5L (equipment, working capital). Timeline: 3-4 weeks to first batch.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 10 min). Apply for GI certification via Nilgiri Planters' Association (no additional cost for collective GI, already registered 2005). Get FSSAI registration (₹2,000, online). If exporting: get IEC (Import-Export Code) from DGFT Bengaluru (+₹500, 7-10 days). Trademark registration: ₹9,000-15,000, 3-4 months. Total compliance cost: ₹11,500-17,500.
5
First Sale
First sale: list on Amazon / Flipkart (₹50-100 per unit commission + category fee). Start with 250g packs at ₹1,200 retail = ₹600 wholesale to platform. Target first order: 50 units = ₹30,000 revenue. Use email outreach to premium cafes (Chai Point, Indian Chai Company) — offer sample + ₹1,500/kg bulk pricing. Expected first-month revenue (blended D2C + B2B): ₹2-4L from 5-8 orders across channels.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (~40kg/day production): hire 2 production workers (₹10,000/month each), 1 digital marketer (₹15,000/month), 1 operations person (₹12,000/month). Exhibit at India International Tea & Coffee Expo (Delhi, Feb/Mar) and Premium Food & Beverage Expo (Mumbai) — budget ₹1.5-2.5L. Open B2B sales to HoReCa buyers (₹500,000 contracts avg). Launch export sales via Alibaba or direct EU importer outreach.
