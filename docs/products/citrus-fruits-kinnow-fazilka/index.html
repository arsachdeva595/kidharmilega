Fazilka district in southwestern Punjab spans 5,258 km² with Abohar and Balluana as primary Kinnow clusters covering 35,000 hectares. The region's canal water infrastructure and moderate climate support year-round cultivation, while saline groundwater forces farmers to depend on canal supply, creating natural barriers to entry. 12 lakh tonnes annual production flows to Delhi-NCR markets within 400 km and international buyers through Ludhiana export houses.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Fazilka ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Abohar or Balluana haat on market day (Tuesday/Friday). Call 3 established Kinnow growers — ask them directly about 2024 dieback costs, canal water availability, and buyer contacts. Register on Udyam portal (free, 15 minutes). Zero rupees spent.
2
Source Raw Materials
Lease 10-20 hectares in Abohar or Balluana blocks (₹30,000-50,000/hectare/year). Source 2-3 year old grafted Kinnow saplings from Punjab Agricultural University (PAU) Ludhiana or registered nurseries in Abohar at ₹80-150 per sapling. First batch: 1,500-3,000 saplings = ₹150,000-400,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish orchard through direct cultivation on leased land or sharecrop partnership (50-50 profit split common). Install drip irrigation (₹150,000-300,000 for 15 hectares). Budget Year 1: land lease (₹400,000), saplings (₹300,000), irrigation (₹200,000), labor, fertilizers, pest control (₹200,000) = ₹1.1-1.5M. Trees fruit in 3-4 years; mature orchard yields 30-40 tonnes/hectare.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, online). Apply for Mission for Integrated Development of Horticulture subsidy (up to 40% cost for drip irrigation). Skip GI registration (no GI tag yet; focus on quality certifications like GlobalGAP or organic). For export: obtain IEC from DGFT (₹10,000 filing fee, 7 days), get Agmark/phytosanitary certificate from APEDA.
5
First Sale
Month 1-3: register with nearest APMC market (Abohar APMC) — ₹500 membership. Sell 20-30 tonnes directly to wholesale buyers at ₹60-75/kg = ₹12-20L gross revenue. Simultaneously approach JioMart's B2B portal and 1-2 cold storage operators in Ludhiana who aggregate small growers for retail chains. First institutional order: 2-3 tonnes at ₹80/kg via aggregator.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (30-40 tonnes sold): hire 1 full-time farm manager + 2 seasonal coordinators. Acquire certification for premium positioning (GlobalGAP ₹80,000-150,000). Join Fazilka district horticulture cooperative for collective export batching. Exhibit at Horticulture Expo in Chandigarh or Ludhiana trade shows. Develop direct relationship with 2-3 retail chains' procurement. Scale to 50+ hectares through lease expansion or partnership farms by Year 4.

Frequently Asked Questions

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

₹45-150 lakh. Minimum: 10 hectares land lease (₹3-5L/year) + saplings + basic drip setup = ₹45-60L Year 1. Maximum: 20 hectares + modern infrastructure + certification = ₹120-150L. Returns start Year 3-4.
Bilkul zaruri. Kinnow growing needs knowledge of drip irrigation, pest management, soil testing, grafting. You either learn from experienced orchardists (1-2 seasons apprenticeship) or hire a qualified farm manager. Arvind Setia example shows quality is possible with right techniques, but lazy farming fails — especially post-2024 dieback crisis.
MIDH: 40% subsidy on drip irrigation + high-density planting. PMEGP loan: ₹25L at 24% subsidy. Punjab schemes: 50% on machinery, cold storage up to ₹5L. Horticulture dept gives free soil testing. Total subsidy can cover 30-40% of setup cost if you apply properly.
Abohar APMC mandi — walk in, register, sell daily. Or contact cold storage operators directly — they aggregate small growers. BigBasket/Blinkit B2B program accepts Fazilka Kinnow at ₹70-90/kg wholesale. Export aggregators in Ludhiana want consistent supply — call 3-4, send samples.
Haan. International buyers (UAE, UK, Netherlands, Malaysia, Singapore) take Fazilka Kinnow via registered exporters. Process: GlobalGAP certification (₹100-150K) + Agmark/phytosanitary certificate from APEDA + IEC registration. Margins: ₹90-120/kg FOB. Volumes: 50-100 tonnes per season. But export needs consistent supply and quality — not for first 3 years usually.
Real risk: 2024 widespread dieback shows this is hard. If your trees get infected or climate fails, you lose ₹50-100L investment. Exit options: (1) Lease land back out to other growers, (2) Pivot to mixed horticulture (guava, citrus mix), (3) Sell mature orchard at depreciated price after Year 5. Diversification is safer — don't go all-in on Kinnow alone.
