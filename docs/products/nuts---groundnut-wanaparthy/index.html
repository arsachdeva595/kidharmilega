Nuts - Groundnut ki
opportunity, Wanaparthy mein.

Wanaparthy lies in Telangana's southern agricultural belt where soil composition and semi-arid climate create ideal groundnut growing conditions; the district is part of a 4-district groundnut corridor (Wanaparthy, Nagarkurnool, Gadwal, Mahabubnagar) with over 1 lakh acres under active cultivation. A functional groundnut crushing and processing plant already operates in the district, reducing raw material logistics to local sourcing. Buyers include domestic supermarket chains, online grocery platforms, and international markets across Southeast Asia and the Middle East seeking aflatoxin-free, cold-pressed oil.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Wanaparthy ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call the Wanaparthy groundnut crushing and processing unit (contact via Wanaparthy APMC Mandi or district agriculture office) and request a facility tour — zero cost, 2-hour visit. Visit Wanaparthy APMC mandi (open daily) to understand groundnut grading, pricing (current: ₹6,639/quintal as of Apr 2026, range ₹4,529-₹7,357), and supplier ecosystem. Attend a district agriculture department workshop on groundnut cultivation and post-harvest handling — usually held quarterly, free registration.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source groundnut from Wanaparthy APMC mandi directly at current market rate (₹6,639/quintal average); bulk order 10-20 quintals (₹6.6-₹13.3L) for first batch. Alternatively, contract with 2-3 smallholder farmers in Wanaparthy taluk during rabi season (October-February) at ₹6,300-₹6,800/quintal for year-round supply. Verify aflatoxin-free certification from supplier before purchase — this is mandatory for export.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Asset-light): Partner with the existing Wanaparthy groundnut crushing and processing unit as a co-packer — provide raw material, pay ₹40-60 per litre processing fee, receive finished cold-pressed oil in your bottles. Cost: ₹150-250 per litre for 500L monthly batch (raw material + processing). Option B (Own unit): Lease a 2000-3000 sq.ft. space in Wanaparthy town (₹8,000-15,000/month), install a cold-press oil extraction machine (₹250-400K), establish basic cleaning/grading area (₹50-100K), hire 2-3 workers (₹15-20K/month salary). Full setup cost: ₹350-600K for own unit.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 15 minutes at https://udyam.gov.in). Apply for FSSAI Food Safety Licence (₹1,000-5,000 processing fee, 2-4 weeks). If exporting, obtain IEC code from DGFT (free, online at https://www.dgft.gov.in, 1-2 weeks). Consider GI registration for Wanaparthy Groundnut Oil once brand is established — coordinate with district administration or apply through state commerce ministry (₹5,000-15,000 application cost, 18-24 month process). Obtain ISO 22000 certification if targeting premium supermarkets (₹30-50K, 6-8 weeks).
5
First Sale
First sale: Approach local Reliance Smart or Spencer's store manager with FSSAI licence and aflatoxin-free test certificate; offer 15-20 bottles on 30-day consignment. Expected first order: 50-100 units (₹17,500-45,000 gross value). Launch D2C on BigBasket seller portal simultaneously (free listing, 5% commission) — first month target: 200-300 bottles, ₹70-135K revenue. Margin on first sale: 35-40% wholesale, 55% D2C.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (≈2000 litres/month): Hire a part-time accountant (₹8-12K/month), a sales/export coordinator (₹15-20K/month), and 2 additional production workers (₹30-40K combined). Invest in bottle design and labelling upgrade (₹50-80K one-time). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (25% capex subsidy, up to ₹10L) to expand to your own cold-press unit. Attend food and agricultural expos (AAFEX, India International Food Show) to source bulk buyers. Set up exports: partner with an EXIM merchant or freight forwarder in Hyderabad (30km away) to handle shipping to Singapore, Malaysia, UAE. Scale to 500L/day within 18 months.
