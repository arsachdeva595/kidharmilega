Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Chikkamagaluru ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chikkamagaluru district areca processing clusters near Mudigere and Tarikere towns; meet 3-5 existing areca leaf product manufacturers; study production techniques at IIMM (Indian Institute of Maternity Management) artisan training centers; research export certifications and FDA compliance requirements.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw areca leaves from farmers in Chikkamagaluru taluk (₹8-12 per kg); contact APMC Chikkamagaluru for bulk supplier lists; procure natural binders from Chennai-based suppliers (₹15-20 per liter); first batch of 10,000 units will cost approximately ₹120,000-150,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisan groups in Mudigere (production cost ₹2.50-4 per unit); alternatively, lease a 2,000 sq ft facility near Tarikere (₹15,000-25,000 monthly rent) and hire 8-10 skilled workers; install basic leaf pressing and molding machinery (₹400,000-600,000).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME portal) within 24 hours online; apply for IEC code from DGFT for exports; register brand name with IP India; obtain food safety license from FSSAI (₹2,000-5,000); get BIS certification for biodegradability standards.
5
First Sale
List products on Amazon Business, BigBasket B2B, and Flipkart for Business within first month; approach 20 restaurants in Bangalore using targeted LinkedIn outreach; join EnvironMart and GreenSwag marketplaces; launch Instagram content around eco-living (3 posts weekly); offer sample packs at ₹499 with free shipping.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range to bowls and serving trays; establish distribution in 2-3 metro cities through regional distributors; secure 2-3 bulk export orders through trade shows; hire sales team for B2B account management; invest in semi-automated production to reduce unit costs by 20%.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (25% of project cost) for manufacturing-based enterprises in non-metro areas.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹10 lakh per artisan cluster for machinery and skills training in traditional industries.
🏚
Karnataka Make in India
₹5-10 lakh incentive for agro-processing manufacturing units in rural districts with export potential.
🏚
Karnataka Startup Policy
₹2 lakh seed funding and incubation support for sustainable manufacturing startups registered in Tier 2 cities.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
