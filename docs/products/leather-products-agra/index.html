The region's access to high-quality raw materials, established supply chains, and a concentrated community of master craftsmen make it uniquely suited for premium leather goods production. The GI tag validates authenticity and commands premium pricing globally.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Agra ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Agra's Jatpura and Firozabad leather clusters; meet master craftsmen in Sadar Bazaar workshops; attend leather craft trade fairs in Delhi and Mumbai; study competitor brands on Etsy and Instagram.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source hides from regulated suppliers in Jajmau (Kanpur) leather market or Agra local tanneries; purchase vegetable dyes and finishing oils from specialty suppliers in Agra; first batch startup cost ₹50,000-100,000 for 50-100 quality hides and materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisan collectives in Agra (e.g., through DC Handicraft office); commission production from master craftsmen in Sadar Bazaar workshops; alternatively, set up own small workshop (₹5-8 sq ft rental space, ₹1-1.5L for tools and workstations).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSMEs via udyamregistration.gov.in); obtain IEC from DGFT for export; register GI certification claim with DC Handicraft Ministry; file trademark with IP India; create brand story emphasizing Mughal heritage and artisan authenticity.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram/Pinterest visual storytelling showcasing craft process and artisan stories; list on Etsy with detailed heritage narratives; approach high-end Indian boutiques (Anita Dongre, FabIndia) and international leather goods retailers; participate in Vibrant India, Taste of India, and international leather expos.
6
Scale
Expand product line (wallets, belts, accessories) after proving core category; build B2B relationships with international distributors and luxury retailers; establish second production unit; hire and train additional artisans; invest in branded storefront in tourist areas (Taj East Gate area); seek exports via trade bodies like IEML (Indian Exporters & Manufacturers League).
