Sambalpuri Bandha Saree ki
opportunity, Sambalpur mein.

Sambalpur has been a traditional handloom hub for over 500 years with a concentrated artisan community skilled in the Bandha tie-and-dye technique. The district's water resources, climate, and cultural continuity make it the natural epicenter for authentic Sambalpuri saree production. Strong supplier ecosystem and established export networks already exist in the cluster.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sambalpur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sambalpur's weaver cluster (Chakra Saree Park, Kala Niketan, Sambalpur Saree Cooperative) for 5-7 days. Interview 8-10 master weavers, understand production timelines (35-45 days per saree), and observe the Bandha process end-to-end. Attend local handloom melas and connect with existing exporters.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source silk/cotton yarn from Sambalpur's primary suppliers (₹8,000-12,000 per kg for quality silk), natural dyes from local herbalists or IndiaCSR suppliers (₹2,000-3,000 per batch), binding materials locally. Budget ₹1.5-2L for first batch of 8-10 sample sarees in 2-3 designs.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 4-6 registered weaver groups in Sambalpur Saree Cooperative or work via DC Handicraft's artisan network. Establish 50% advance + 50% on delivery payment model. Start with 5 designs, 20-30 pieces per design monthly. Consider subcontracting design innovation to young weavers.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry of MSME) with Handloom sector code, obtain IEC code from local DGFT office for exports. Create GST registration (5% applicable for handloom). Build brand identity emphasizing heritage and artisan stories. Apply for Handloom Mark certification from DC Handicraft Sambalpur.
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho with 15-20 SKUs (price: ₹3,500-7,500 per saree wholesale), list on Flipkart's Sarees vertical, set up Shopify D2C store (target ₹10K+ retail price with artisan storytelling). Approach ethnic wear boutiques in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with physical samples. Email 30-50 international buyers on Alibaba, Global Sources.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 8-10 designs, increase artisan partnerships to 10-12 weavers, launch Instagram/YouTube content series on Bandha process (target 50K followers in 12 months), apply for export subsidy schemes, partner with 2-3 international distributors, consider pop-up stores in metro wedding seasons.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
₹15,000 credit for tooling + 5% interest subsidy for handloom artisans linked to your business
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹10-25L subsidy (20-35% of project cost) for setting up handloom processing unit
🏚
Odisha Handloom Weaver Assistance Scheme
₹5,000-10,000 direct benefit transfer to partnered weavers + export incentive of 5% on FOB value
🏚
Odisha's SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹25L for cluster development, design innovation, and market linkages
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
