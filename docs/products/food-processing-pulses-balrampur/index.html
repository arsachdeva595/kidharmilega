Food Processing (Pulses) ki
opportunity, Balrampur mein.

Balrampur district in eastern UP produces five certified small lentil varieties on fertile Indo-Gangetic soil and has already proven food export routes to Bengal, Assam, and Bangladesh. The Kumbhi cluster houses 2 automated processing units with expansion investments of ₹100 crore underway, creating job opportunities for food processors. High-quality lentil pulses are locally grown and competitively priced; buyers include modern retail (Amazon, BigBasket), kirana wholesalers across UP-Bihar-Bengal region, and SAARC export ports in Kolkata (160 km away).

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Balrampur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kumbhi cluster (Balrampur town center); speak with 3 existing pulse snack makers; call District Food Processing Officer (contact via balrampur.nic.in); attend weekly Rapti Market (Thursday) to observe lentil price movement. Zero cost. Do this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw lentils from Balrampur-area mandi (Pipra Mandi is primary hub) or direct farmer groups; negotiate ₹50-65 per kg for bulk. First batch (500 kg) will cost ₹25-32.5K. Supplier contacts: District Cooperative Society, local dal mill owners in Kumbhi.
3
Produce / Partner
Lease a 500-sq-ft unit near Kumbhi cluster (₹8-15K/month rent) or partner with an existing artisan (take 60% margin on their 40% cost). Equipment: roasting drum (₹1.2-2L), grinder, sieve, sealing machine (₹3-5L total). First 3-month operating cost: ₹1.2-1.5L (rent, labor, utilities, packaging). Alternatively, outsource first batches to existing producers for 25-30% margin while you build brand.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (5 mins online, free, gov.in); apply for Agmark certification at District Food Processing Office (₹2-5K, 30 days); file for FSSAI registration (₹5K, 45 days). If exporting: Apply for IEC through DGFT portal (₹1K, 7 days). Get product tested at any NABL lab (₹3-8K). Total compliance cost: ₹12-20K over 60 days.
5
First Sale
First month: List 2-3 SKUs on Amazon Pantry and BigBasket Seller Central (free listing, 2-3 weeks approval). Approach 3 wholesale distributors in Lucknow (calls direct — no cost). First order size likely: 50-100 kg D2C, 200-500 kg wholesale. Expected first-month revenue: ₹30-60K (assuming 30% of units sold).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: Hire 1 full-time production manager and 2 part-time processors (₹15K salary combined). Move to 1,200-sq-ft rented unit (₹20-25K/month). Exhibit at UP Food Processing Trade Fair (Sept, Lucknow) and South Asian Food Expo (Bangladesh, ₹2-4L spend). Build 1-2 regional wholesale partnerships (Metro distributors, Jio Mart seller, kirana networks). Invest ₹50K in product photography and Amazon A+ content.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Pradhan Mantri Kisan SAMPADA Yojana (PMKSY)
up to 50% subsidy on infrastructure (unit setup, machinery), max ₹50 lakh per unit, processing focus.
🏛
MSME Schemes (PMEGP, SFURTI)
₹10-25 lakh credit at 5-6% interest, government guarantee; subsidy component up to 35% for SC/ST founders.
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Food Processing Policy 2017
25% capital subsidy on machinery, VAT exemption for 5 years, potential land lease concessions in MSME parks.
🏚
Integrated Horticulture Development Mission
support for agri-processing units with subsidy linked to export volumes (relevant for pulse exports to SAARC).
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
