Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Raigarh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Raigarh Mandi and Bilaspur Agricultural Market to understand pricing cycles, quality standards, and buyer preferences. Connect with existing tomato farmers in Manendragarh and Gharghoda clusters. Attend state agriculture department training at Raigarh Krishi Vigyan Kendra.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source hybrid seeds (Sungold, Roma, Arka Vishesh varieties) from IIHR Bangalore-certified dealers in Raipur (₹15,000-25,000 per 100g). Procure farmyard manure from local cattle farms (₹2-3 per kg). First batch for 1 acre: ₹80,000-120,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Lease 1-2 acres from local farmers through Raigarh district cooperative or direct agreements. Partner with established tomato farmers for contract farming. Set up drip irrigation (₹50,000-80,000 per acre). Engage ATMA (Agricultural Technology Management Agency) for soil testing and guidance.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 10 minutes). Apply for IEC code from Raigarh Customs office for exports. Register trademark for brand name with IP cell. Obtain FSSAI registration (₹4,500-8,000) for value-added products. Get organic certification from NPOP-accredited certifier if pursuing premium positioning.
5
First Sale
Register on Pataafat and LocalCircles for D2C sales. Supply to Bilaspur and Raipur retail chains via direct outreach. List on BigBasket vendor dashboard. Create WhatsApp ordering groups with institutional buyers (schools, hospitals, restaurants). Sell fresh produce at Raigarh Mandi initially for cash flow.
6
Scale
After first harvest, invest in cold storage unit (₹20-30L) in Raigarh town. Develop processing facility for tomato paste (₹40-60L capex). Acquire adjacent farmland to increase acreage to 5-10 acres. Establish supply contracts with food processing companies. Launch private label products for organized retail.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs grant for food processing units with 35% subsidy on processing equipment.
🏛
Agriculture Infrastructure Development Fund (AIDF)
concessional loans up to ₹2 crore for storage and cold chain infrastructure.
🏚
Rajiv Gandhi Kisan Nyay Yojana (Chhattisgarh)
direct input assistance of ₹9,000 per acre for farmers; subsidy on quality seeds and fertilizers.
🏚
Chhattisgarh Krishi Karman Yojana
incentives for productive farming with productivity-linked bonuses; support for crop diversification into vegetables.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
