Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Barpeta ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Barpeta town artisan clusters around Pathsala and South Kamrup areas; spend 2-3 weeks learning from master craftsmen like those in the Barpeta Shilp Vikash Kendra; study existing bell metal designs and export patterns via Assam Handicrafts Association.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source copper and tin from Kolkata metal suppliers (Apollo Metals, Hindustan Metals) or direct from Jamshedpur; local availability in Guwahati. First batch cost (50 kg metals + materials): ₹40,000-60,000. Build relationships with 2-3 local suppliers for steady material flow.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established artisans in Barpeta (contact through District Handicraft Officer); negotiate production on commission basis (15-25% per piece) or hire 1-2 apprentices for ₹8,000-12,000/month. Lease a 400-500 sq ft workshop in artisan clusters for ₹5,000-8,000/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam MSME scheme (udyamregistration.gov.in); apply for IEC code via nearest DGFT office in Guwahati; register business as Partnership or Private Limited; get hallmark certificate from Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) in Guwahati for authenticity.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart Handloom by month 2; reach out to heritage home décor retailers (Fabindia, Good Earth, Rang Decor) with bulk samples; create Instagram content showing artisan process; list on craft-specific platforms like Jaypore and The Indus Valley; target temple procurement through district administration.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range (cooking vessels, decorative masks, idols); hire 2-3 more artisans; set up quality control system; apply for GI certification to boost export pricing; establish export network via trade bodies like APEDA; attend international craft fairs (Frankfurt, London); collaborate with interior designers for bulk custom orders.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for MSMEs in manufacturing; covers 25% of project cost for General category, 35% for SC/ST.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 credit-linked support and ₹500 monthly allowance for artisans engaged in traditional crafts like metalwork; free training and certification.
🏚
Assam Handicrafts Development Scheme
25% subsidy on raw material costs and equipment purchase for registered artisans; available through District Handicraft Office.
🏚
Assam Industries Investment Promotion Policy (AIIPP)
15% subsidy on machinery and equipment; land cost support for manufacturing units in non-metro districts like Barpeta.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
