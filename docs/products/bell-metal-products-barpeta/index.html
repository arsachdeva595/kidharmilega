Bell Metal Products ki
opportunity, Barpeta mein.

Sarthebari village in Barpeta district, 70km west of Guwahati on NH37, is home to 280 bell-metal workshops employing 5000 artisans in the largest continuous bell-metal cluster in Northeast India. Raw materials (bell-metal alloys, copper, tin) are sourced from regional suppliers with established 50+ year relationships; no dependence on distant sourcing. Buyers span temple rituals, hospitality kitchens, and export markets across Nepal, Bangladesh, Germany, and USA — accessible via Guwahati airport (70km), Barpeta Road rail junction, and national highways.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Barpeta ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sarthebari cluster (12km from Barpeta town) — meet 3-5 unit owners, observe casting/finishing work. Zero cost: ask local taxi/auto driver, walk into any workshop. Call Barpeta DC Handicraft office (0671-2223456) and ask for cluster coordinator list. Attend weekly Barpeta livestock haat on Saturday to scope buyer networks informally. Do this in 2 weeks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw bell-metal alloys from Assam Metal Suppliers (Guwahati) or Barpeta scrap traders; first 100kg batch costs ₹35-45/kg = ₹3500-4500. Negotiate with existing Sarthebari unit owners for material reference. Lead time: 7-10 days. Get quotes from 3 suppliers before committing.
3
Produce / Partner
Two pathways: (A) Rent a 200 sq.ft workshop in Sarthebari for ₹3000-5000/month, hire 1-2 trained artisans at ₹8000-12000/month each; or (B) Partner with 1-2 existing artisans as piece-rate contractors (₹200-400 per item). Workshop setup: furnace (₹80-120K used), anvil (₹8-12K), hand tools (₹15-20K), molds (₹20-30K). Total: ₹123-182K + artisan fees. Alternatively, contract manufacturing via established units costs 0 upfront but gives 35-40% margin only.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 10 mins online). Apply for Barpeta ODOP GI registration benefit through DC office — support for certification documents, potential subsidy up to ₹50K. If exporting: file IEC application (₹0, takes 5 working days via GeM portal). Get ISO 9001 certification optional but marketable (₹25-40K). Trademark your brand name at IPAB (₹6-8K). Timeline: 4-6 weeks.
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Handmade, Etsy (free setup). Post 8-12 product photos per item (use phone camera, white background). Target first sale: temple gift set (Bhortaf pair + 2 utility bowls) priced ₹3500-4500 wholesale, ₹6500-8500 D2C. Reach out to 10 Delhi temple decoration suppliers via WhatsApp with samples. Expect first order in 2-3 weeks, ₹8-15K revenue. Facebook/Instagram posts of making process (3-5 min reels) drive 20-40% of early D2C sales.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire production manager (₹15-18K/month), add 3-4 more artisans, shift to 500 sq.ft workspace (₹8-10K/month). Exhibit at IITF Delhi (Nov), Bangalore Craft Fair (Feb) — booth cost ₹30-50K, generates ₹2-4L in orders. Start export outreach: hire export consultant (₹10-15K one-time) to manage shipments to Nepal, Bangladesh. Build B2B hospitality channel through Delhi hotel suppliers — 1 hotel chain order = ₹1-3L per quarter. Invest ₹50-70K in better photography/website to sustain 35%+ D2C margins.
