Nagpur Orange ki
opportunity, Nagpur mein.

Nagpur Orange is cultivated across the Satpura Hills cluster (Nagpur, Amroati, Wardha in Maharashtra; Coorg in Karnataka) at 1500m elevation with 100-200cm annual rainfall and optimal soil pH of 6.0-8.0. NOGA, the primary processor, operates a plant at Hingna with capacity of 50,000 bottles of squash annually and sells to institutional buyers including hotels and government agencies. The Mrig crop (January harvest) offers sweeter fruit; Ambiya variety (September-December) provides slightly sour options; processed products include juice, juice concentrate, canned segments, jams, marmalades, essential oil, pectin, and alcohol.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Nagpur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Hingna to locate NOGA's processing plant and meet their procurement team (free, ₹100 transport); call Citrus India Private Limited at Nanded to understand their production model; attend Nagpur Fruit Market (Dhantoli wholesale market) on a Tuesday and speak with orange suppliers about farm-gate prices and seasonal volumes — all doable this week with zero cost beyond travel.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source directly from NOGA supplier network or Wardha/Amroati growers (harvest December-March, Mrig crop sweeter). First batch: 5-10 quintal of oranges costs ₹2,000-2,500 per quintal (total ₹10K-25K for small pilot). Wardha district has highest concentration of growers; negotiate directly or contact NOGA for supplier list.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 500-1000 sqft food-processing unit in Hingna or Katol MSME cluster (₹8K-12K/month + ₹50K-100K refundable deposit). Option B: Partner with existing NOGA member on contract processing (50% revenue share, zero capex). Minimal equipment needed for start: fruit wash tank (₹15K), manual juicer (₹8K), pasteurizer (₹60K-80K for small batch). Total capex for Option A: ₹150K-250K; Option B: ₹0-50K.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 10 min) with agricultural processing classification. Apply for GI registration support through Ministry of Agriculture — Nagpur Orange GI tag (2014) extends to processed products if sourced from notified cluster. Obtain Food Safety License (FSSAI) from Nagpur Municipal Corporation: ₹1,000-2,000, 15 days. If exporting: Get IEC code from DGFT (free, online, 1 week). File trademark for brand name at Delhi IP Office: ₹5,000-8,000, 4-6 months. Total time: 2-3 months.
5
First Sale
First sale: Approach 2-3 hotels in Nagpur (5-star preferred) with 50L sample of juice concentrate. Expected order: 200L minimum at ₹90/L = ₹18,000 gross. Simultaneously list squash on BigBasket and Blinkit (each requires ₹10K-15K deposit, 7-day approval). First month revenue target: ₹50K-80K from 3-4 institutional clients + ₹20K-30K from online.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: Move to 2000-3000 sqft unit with continuous pasteurizer (₹150K-200K). Hire 2 full-time production staff (₹8K-10K each) + 1 sales/export coordinator (₹12K-15K). Participate in National Agroprocess Federation's food expos (quarterly, ₹20K booth fee). Approach Patanjali Ayurved (they planned Nagpur orange juice production per govt data) for bulk supply contract. Export to Middle East/Southeast Asia via APEDA's export promotion program.
