opportunity, Pratapgarh mein.

Pratapgarh district is the exclusive birthplace and custodian of Thewa art, with multi-generational artisan families concentrated in Khimsar and surrounding villages. The unique climate, mineral-rich glass sources, and deep cultural heritage of the region make it the only place in the world where authentic Thewa jewelry is produced with the original 400-year-old technique.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Pratapgarh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khimsar village cluster in Pratapgarh (30km from district HQ), spend 2-3 weeks learning from established Thewa artisans like families in Khimsar-Ramsar region. Study GI certificate details, connect with Rajasthan Handicraft Association (Jaipur office) and visit the district handicraft development office for training schedules.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 24-carat gold foil from certified bullion dealers in Jaipur's Bapu Bazaar (₹8-12K per gram minimum order); procure colored glass sheets from suppliers in Khimsar or nearby Bhilwara; initial 500-piece batch startup cost ₹2-3L including materials, tools, and 2-week artisan wages.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with established Thewa artisans in Khimsar village on revenue-share basis (40-50% artisan share) or hire trained apprentices from district government training centers. Establish small production unit within or near Khimsar to maintain traditional technique integrity and artisan community connection.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) with business address in Pratapgarh district, obtain IEC code from nearest office (Ajmer/Udaipur regional center), file for GI-authentic product certification through DC Handicraft ministry, trademark brand name at IP office, and set up basic GST registration. Create heritage narrative documentation for brand authenticity.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy with 50+ high-resolution photos and heritage storytelling (target: ₹5-8L first 3 months); approach luxury platforms like TheArtling and Craftizen; reach out to luxury boutiques in Delhi/Mumbai/Bangalore; initiate Instagram/Pinterest-driven content strategy highlighting artisan stories and 400-year heritage; secure initial 5-10 orders via direct outreach to wedding planners and bridal wear retailers.
6
Scale
Post 6 months: expand export partnerships through APEDA and Export Council; open retail presence in 2-3 metro luxury malls; develop tiered product line (daily-wear accessible pieces ₹5-15K, premium bridal ₹50K+); hire 2-3 additional experienced artisans; launch limited-edition seasonal collections; build B2B wholesale network across 30+ boutiques; invest in brand collaborations with fashion designers.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹50L subsidy for cluster development, artisan training, and quality improvement in Thewa cluster of Pratapgarh
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 toolkit allowance + ₹5L loan at zero interest for artisans and micro-entrepreneurs in traditional crafts like Thewa
🏚
Rajasthan Handicraft Promotion Scheme
25% subsidy on technology upgrades, packaging, branding, and export promotion activities for registered artisans
🏚
Mukhyamantri Laghu Padp Udyog Loan Scheme
up to ₹25L loan at 5% interest subsidy for small handicraft units registered with state handicraft department
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
