Thewa Jewellery ki
opportunity, Pratapgarh mein.

Pratapgarh district (population 870,000; 4,117 sq km) in southern Rajasthan produces Thewa jewelry through approximately two dozen artisan families concentrated in the Pratapgarh cluster. Raw materials — 24-karat gold sheets, colored glass in deep red/emerald/blue, silver wire — source from Jaipur (180 km) and local suppliers. Direct export markets span Japan, Singapore, America, England, and Europe; annual production value is ₹6-7 crore with gross margins of 30-40% wholesale and 60-75% D2C, making this a high-value, low-volume craft business.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Pratapgarh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pratapgarh cluster this month: meet 2-3 artisan families directly (contact District Administration or ODOP coordinator at DC office for introductions — free). Call Jaipur gold suppliers (search 'Jaipur gold foil suppliers' + phone) to understand pricing. Attend a local haat or craft exhibition if scheduled. Zero cost; takes 3-5 days.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 24-karat gold foil from Jaipur suppliers (Meenakari Bazaar area; typical first batch ₹50,000-₹100,000 for 50-100 pieces of foil). Colored glass in small quantities (₹5,000-₹10,000 per color for trial batch) from Jaipur or Indore. Silver wire from local Pratapgarh suppliers. Total first-batch material cost: ₹80,000-₹150,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner with an existing artisan family in Pratapgarh cluster (10-25% revenue share, no upfront cost beyond material supply). Option B: Rent a small 200-400 sq ft workshop in Pratapgarh town (₹4,000-₹8,000/month) and hire 1 experienced Thewa artisan (₹15,000-₹25,000/month). Initial setup (rent deposit + basic tools + first artisan wages): ₹60,000-₹150,000. Production time per piece: 8-15 hours for premium necklace.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in, 30 min). Apply for Rajasthan Craft Guarantee Scheme if eligible (contact Rajasthan Laghu Udyog Nigam). For export: apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at nearest DGFT office (₹500-₹1,000, 15 days). GI mark already on district product — use in branding free. Total cost for registration: ₹2,000-₹5,000; timeline 4-6 weeks.
5
First Sale
First sale: list 3-5 pieces on Etsy or Instagram Shop (free). Target: ₹40,000-₹80,000 first-month revenue from 3-5 orders at ₹12,000-₹18,000 per necklace wholesale. Send product photos + certificates of authenticity to 10 boutiques in Delhi/Mumbai (free email outreach). First wholesale order expected 2-4 weeks in.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 2nd artisan (₹25k/month), upgrade to 600-800 sq ft unit (₹12,000-₹18,000/month), invest ₹2-3L in inventory. Attend Indian craft fairs (IITF Delhi Oct-Nov, India International Jewelry Show). Approach Rajasthan tourism boards and luxury hotel chains for institutional bulk orders. Partner with 2-3 export aggregators (TradeKey, Global Sources) for consistent international orders. Target: ₹20-30L annual revenue by month 18.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
5% government-backed interest subsidy on loans up to ₹5 lakh for traditional artisans and their businesses; eligibility check at nearest bank
🏛
One District One Product (ODOP)
export marketing support, branding costs, trade fair participation grants (₹50k-₹200k per event) for registered artisans; apply via State ODOP office
🏚
Rajasthan Craft Guarantee Scheme
80% guarantee cover on MSME loans for registered handicraft units, interest rates 5-7%; apply at Rajasthan Laghu Udyog Nigam office
🏚
Rajasthan Employment Promotion Scheme
50% wage subsidy for first 50 employees in registered MSMEs for 2 years; applicable if you scale to 10+ workers
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
