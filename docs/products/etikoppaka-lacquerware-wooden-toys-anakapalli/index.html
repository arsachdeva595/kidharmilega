Anakapalli has a centuries-old tradition of wooden toy-making using cashew wood, with an established cluster of master artisans who have perfected the craft. The region's abundant cashew tree forests provide sustainable raw material, and the district benefits from low cost of living and skilled labor concentration in the craft.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Anakapalli ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Etikoppaka cluster near Anakapalli town; meet master artisans at Government Handicraft Cooperative (contact DCCW Anakapalli); study existing players like Etikoppaka Wooden Toys Co. and regional exporters; understand GI compliance requirements through DC Handicraft office.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cashew wood from licensed timber merchants in Visakhapatnam district (₹8,000-12,000 per 50 kg); procure natural dyes and lacquer from Hyderabad handicraft suppliers (₹3,000-5,000 for first batch); first batch material cost ~₹25,000-40,000 for 50-60 toys.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 trained artisans in Etikoppaka cluster (₹15,000-20,000/month per artisan); or apprentice under master craftsmen for 2-3 months to learn hand-carving and lacquering; register as artisan-led cooperative under SFURTI if scaling with multiple artisans.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise) online via udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC code from DGFT office Visakhapatnam for export; register trademark with IP India; obtain GI certification reference letter from DC Handicraft; get APEDA registration for handicraft exports.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, Flipkart Handmade with 20-30 SKUs; pitch to The Artisan Box, Craft Souk, and similar Indian artisan platforms; reach out to 10-15 gift shops and toy retailers in Hyderabad, Bangalore, and Mumbai; create Instagram account showcasing artisan process and GI heritage.
6
Scale
After ₹5-8L revenue, hire 2-3 additional artisans; establish workshop showroom in Visakhapatnam city; apply for SFURTI cluster development grants; explore B2B contracts with boutique hotels and corporate gifting firms; target international craft fairs (Frankfurt Christmas Market, London Craft Fair) for direct exports.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs loan with 5-6% subsidy for registered MSMEs in handicraft sector
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
dedicated support for artisans including ₹15,000 annual toolkit grant and collateral-free loans up to ₹5 lakhs
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Handicraft Development Scheme
30% subsidy on machinery/tools and marketing support for registered handicraft enterprises
🏚
APSSDC Artisan Upskilling Programme
free advanced craftsmanship training and certification for cluster artisans; also links to market linkages
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
