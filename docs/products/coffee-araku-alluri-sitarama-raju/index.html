Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Alluri Sitarama Raju ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Araku town weekly haat (Thursday/Saturday) and speak to 3-5 coffee farmers — get pricing, yield info, certifications free. Call Araku Coffee Cooperative office (Araku) or contact a processing unit owner already operating — ask sourcing cost and buyer list. Register free on Udyam (udyamregistration.gov.in) using your Aadhaar and PAN.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw coffee cherries directly from farmers in Araku, Chintapalli, or Ananthagiri mandals at ₹20-30 per kg (harvest season June-September). First batch: 500kg of dried parchment coffee at ₹120-150/kg = ₹60,000-75,000. Contact cooperative societies in Agency mandals for bulk supply agreements.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a small processing unit (500 sq ft) in Araku town at ₹3,000-5,000/month with basic pulper, dryer, sorter equipment (₹2.5-4L one-time). Or partner with existing farmer processing centers for custom processing at ₹15-20/kg. Start with 1-2 tonnes/batch, hire 2-3 seasonal workers at ₹300/day.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 2 days online). File for GI tag certification use application (₹5,000-10,000, 30-60 days with legal help). Get FSSAI food license (₹500, 5 days). If exporting: file IEC application via ICEGATE (₹500-1,000, 7 days). Total regulatory cost: ₹6,000-12,000.
5
First Sale
First sale: Upload to Amazon Fresh or BlueTriton (48-hour onboarding), price at ₹550/kg roasted. Or cold-email 20 specialty roasters with samples (₹500 postage). First order likely 25-50kg = ₹13,750-27,500 revenue. Push for second order within 2 weeks.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (≈180kg/month), hire 1 fulltime sales person (₹10K/month), invest in packaging design (₹15K), attend Coffee Board's India Coffee House trade show (Delhi, Jan), apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹25L) if scaling processing capacity. Add branded retail packs at ₹999/250g for D2C margin boost.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹25 lakh subsidy (35% for general, 45% for SC/ST) for setting up coffee processing unit with machinery.
🏛
Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi
₹6,000/year direct income support to coffee farmers (if you source direct and formalize farmer partnerships).
🏚
Andhra Pradesh One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
₹5-10L grant for branding, packaging, and export promotion for registered coffee processors.
🏚
AP Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises (MSME) Subsidy
25% subsidy on machinery and equipment up to ₹5L for coffee processing units in Agency area.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
