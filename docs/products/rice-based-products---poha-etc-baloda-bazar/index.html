Proximity to major rice markets and grain mills provides raw material advantage.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Baloda bazar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Baloda Bazar rice milling cluster near Mandir Hasaud area; study production at Chhattisgarh Rice Research Institute in nearby Raipur; analyze competitors on Amazon/Flipkart selling poha brands; visit local mandis to understand pricing layers
2
Source Raw Materials
Source parboiled rice from Baloda Bazar agricultural cooperative or Durg rice markets (₹25-32/kg). Initial 5-ton batch costs ₹150K-160K. Contact established mills: Baloda Bazar Rice Mill Association for bulk agreements with 15-20% advance discount
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing MSME rice mills in Baloda Bazar having flattening machinery (avoid ₹15-20L capex initially). Negotiate job-work contract at ₹3-5/kg processing fee. Alternatively register with Chhattisgarh Food Processing cluster to access shared processing infrastructure at subsidized rates
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry portal — free, 10 min online). Apply for IEC through DGFT portal for export eligibility. Obtain FSSAI 5-digit license (₹5000-12000, 15-30 days). Design packaging meeting food labeling standards; trademark brand name at IP India. Register GST (mandatory for >₹40L turnover)
5
First Sale
Launch on Flipkart Retail+, Blinkit, and Amazon FreshStart with ₹2-3L marketing spend. Simultaneously approach General Trade distributors via Baloda Bazar/Durg wholesalers. Target 50-100 institutional orders from schools/hospitals. Email samples to 30+ export agents identified on TradeKey/Alibaba
6
Scale
After ₹25L first-year revenue, invest in own flattening machinery (₹18-25L) to improve margins. Add complementary products (murmura, chikli) to reduce SKU risk. Establish exports through freight forwarders in Durg port area. Build B2B SaaS ordering system for retail distribution; hire dedicated export sales team
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidized loan (25% subsidy for general category) for food processing units registered in Baloda Bazar
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹40L cluster support; Baloda Bazar food processing cluster eligible for common facility center support
🏚
Chhattisgarh Rojgar Srijan Kosh
₹5L-10L term loan at 5% interest for food processing entrepreneurs in ODOP districts
🏚
Chhattisgarh Industrial Investment and Employment Promotion Scheme
15% capital subsidy on machinery for food processing units; women entrepreneurs get 25% subsidy
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
