Patiala has a 500+ year heritage of Phulkari embroidery with established artisan communities, abundant skilled labor, and deep cultural roots in this craft. The district's proximity to raw material suppliers and existing embroidery clusters in Patiala city and Nabha town create natural competitive advantages. Strong GI protection and government support for handloom development make it an ideal hub.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Patiala ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Patiala city embroidery clusters (Daresi Mohalla, Lakhanpal area) and Nabha town to study artisan networks, production costs, and quality standards. Attend Suraj Kund Mela (February) and Punjab Handloom Awards (December) for market insights. Connect with DC Handloom office in Patiala for GI protocol details.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 100% cotton fabric from Punjab Mills near Ludhiana (₹8-12 per meter, 500m starter lot ₹5,000-6,000), silk/wool threads from Agroha or Chandni Chowk Delhi suppliers (₹15,000-25,000 per 5kg assortment), and natural dyes from Punjab Agricultural University or ASDC Amritsar (₹3,000-5,000). First batch total: ₹25,000-35,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established Phulkari artisans through Patiala District Handloom Cooperative Society or Needle Arts Association Patiala. Start with 5-8 artisans on piece-rate basis (₹500-1,500 per piece). Each dupatta takes 15-25 days for intricate work. Maintain design consistency through sample templates and regular quality checks.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as Micro/Small business on udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from DGFT Chandigarh for export readiness. Register brand name with IP India office (Delhi) for trademark protection emphasizing GI connection. Implement 'Patiala Phulkari' GI certification protocol in product packaging and marketing collateral.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart Saree categories with GI tag messaging. Create Instagram/Pinterest content showcasing artisan stories and creation process. Approach ethnic wear boutiques in Delhi (Karol Bagh), Mumbai (Kala Ghoda), and Bangalore (Commercial Street) for B2B placement. List on Craftroots and Indian Heritage platforms within first 90 days.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan base to 20-30 workers, introduce fusion Phulkari designs (modern cuts, contemporary colors). Launch subscription boxes for seasonal collections. Establish direct export channels through fashion trade shows (Heimtextil Frankfurt, Intertex) and B2B platforms (Global Sources, Trade Key). Open showroom in Patiala city by month 12.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh grant + subsidy for setting up handloom production unit
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
free toolkit, training, and credit linkage up to ₹5 lakh for registered artisans and entrepreneurs
🏚
Punjab Handloom Development Scheme
direct cash support for weavers/embroiderers and marketing subsidy for GI-tagged products
🏚
Punjab Small Business Promotion Scheme
working capital assistance and technology grants for handloom clusters
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
