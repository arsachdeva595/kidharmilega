Durgi Stone Carving ki
opportunity, Palnadu (Narasaraopeta) mein.

Palnadu district spans 6,150 km² across the lower Krishna basin in A.P., with soft limestone (Suddha Rai) deposits naturally occurring under the surface and currently machine-quarried from nearby sites. Three active carving clusters (Durgi, Obulesunipalli, Macherla mandal) employ 40-50 skilled Viswa Brahmin artisans producing idols, Buddha statues, animal miniatures, and architectural carvings. Buyers include residential customers across metros (Hyderabad, Bangalore, Chennai), temple trusts, hospitality brands, and export markets in Middle East and Southeast Asia reachable via Narasaraopeta railway station and National Highways 16/44.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Palnadu (Narasaraopeta) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Durgi village artisan cluster on a Friday (market day) — call Narasaraopeta Municipal Corporation office (0868-246-5000) to get artisan contact list, free. Attend one carving session, photograph examples, estimate current wholesale pricing. Call 3-4 established sculptors directly to understand raw material costs and production timelines. Register free on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in) to confirm MSME eligibility.
2
Source Raw Materials
Soft limestone (Suddha Rai) is quarried in Durgi taluk and Macherla villages — negotiate directly with quarry operators in Durgi (₹400-₹600 per ton, transport ₹2K-₹4K per ton). First batch: ₹25K-₹35K for 500 kg mixed sizes. Partner with an established artisan in Durgi or Obulesunipalli cluster who will source and store material; typical artisan take: ₹4K-₹8K per finished piece at wholesale rates.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Zero-risk): Partner with 2-3 established artisans in Durgi cluster under revenue-share model (you take 20-25% margin on their carving output). Option B (Own workshop): Rent 400-600 sq ft space in Narasaraopeta town or Durgi (~₹4K-₹6K/month), hire 2-3 semi-skilled carvers at ₹8K-₹12K/month each, invest in hand tools (₹35K), chisels and hammers (₹8K), and stone dust management system (₹12K). Total setup: ₹65K-₹95K for Option B.
4
Brand & Register
Step 4a: Register Udyam (free, online, 5 mins). Step 4b: Apply for GI registration support through DC Handicraft office, Narasaraopeta (currently GI-tagged at state level, claim in your branding). Step 4c: If exporting, register for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via DGFT portal (₹0, online). Step 4d: Open current account at SBI/ICICI in Narasaraopeta (₹1K-₹2K first deposit). Timeline: 6-8 weeks total. Cost: ₹2K-₹5K (bank charges + misc filing).
5
First Sale
First platform: Udaan.com (B2B bulk sales, lower commission than D2C). Post 5-8 best pieces with clear photos, 2-3 inch scale reference. Expected first order: ₹35K-₹80K from regional home décor retailers. Parallel: Create Instagram business account, post 3 process videos (artisan carving) weekly. First D2C sale typically within 3 weeks on Etsy at ₹12K-₹22K per piece. Projected first month revenue from both channels: ₹60K-₹150K.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire a dedicated packing/QC person (₹8K/month), sign 1-2 long-term artisan partnerships (commit ₹2L-₹3L/month production), invest in photography studio setup (₹25K), and social media content creation (₹3K/month freelancer). Exhibit at IITF Delhi (Dec, ₹40K booth), ArtJog Jaipur (Jan), and Craft Mela Hyderabad (quarterly). Scale export by registering with APSWDC (A.P. State Handicrafts Development Corp.) and approaching Gulf hospitality importers directly via LinkedIn. Hire an export operations person at ₹15K-₹18K/month.

Haan, bilkul. Durgi stone carvings are currently exported to UAE, Saudi Arabia, Singapore, USA (albeit through middlemen). You can export directly after IEC registration (free) and building relationships with importers. Margin is 42-58% FOB. Challenges: packing cost (₹1.5K-₹3K per piece), custom documentation (₹5K per shipment), and 6-8 week payment cycle. Start with small shipments to test buyers.
Honest risk: handcraft demand is mood-dependent and seasonal (high Oct-Dec, dips Jul-Aug). Safest exit: maintain artisan partnership without fixed rent commitment, so you can scale down to zero cost in 2-3 weeks. Worst case: you've learned e-commerce, built some customer relationships, and can pivot to another handicraft (pottery, weaving) using same supply chains and customer base. Market is not dead — it's just unorganised, so organised sellers actually win.
