Kannur has a 400+ year handloom weaving tradition with 30,000+ traditional looms still operational. The district's geographic proximity to ports and established export infrastructure makes it ideal for scaling home textiles and furnishing fabrics to international markets. Local artisan clusters and cooperative societies provide ready access to skilled weavers and raw material supply chains.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kannur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kannur Handloom Park in Kannur city and the Nileshwaram handloom cluster. Spend 2-3 weeks understanding loom operations, yarn sourcing, and dye processes. Connect with Kerala Handloom Weavers Cooperative Society and interview 5-10 senior weavers. Study export documentation at Cochin Port Authority. Benchmark products on global platforms (Etsy, NotOnTheHighStreet).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton and silk yarn from Kannur Yarn Suppliers Cluster (₹2-5 L for first 500 kg batch) or directly from Ludhiana yarn mills. Source dyes from Kannur textile chemical dealers. Negotiate bulk orders with 3-4 artisan cooperatives for initial production capacity. First-batch material cost: ₹4-7 lakhs for 50 finished pieces.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 registered handloom weaving cooperatives in Kannur (Kannur Handloom Cooperative, Thalassery Weavers Collective) to avoid factory setup costs. Establish quality control checkpoints. Train partner weavers on design specifications and delivery timelines. Alternatively, lease 5-10 looms directly and hire 8-12 weavers on contract basis.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in) as micro/small enterprise. Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export eligibility. File trademark for brand name via IP India. Open current + EXIM bank account. Obtain Handloom Mark certification from Ministry of Textiles. Set up GST registration (5% for handlooms). Create brand identity: logo, color palette, sustainability story.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C store on Shopify with SEO targeting 'handloom bedspreads export' + 'Kerala home textiles.' List on Amazon Global, Etsy Pro, and eBay with 50+ lifestyle photos. Approach 15-20 high-end home décor retailers (Urban Ladder, Pepperfry, Fabindia) for wholesale distribution. Attend International Home + Housewares Show (Chicago) and Canton Fair (China) for B2B leads. Email 100+ interior designers with portfolio + samples.
6
Scale
After ₹20L revenue, hire dedicated export manager and designer. Expand artisan network to 10-15 cooperatives across Kerala. Introduce 3-4 new product lines (pillowcases, table runners, scarves). Apply for PMEGP grant (₹25L max) for capacity expansion. Establish sustainability certification (GOTS, Fair Trade) for premium positioning. Build wholesale partnerships with 5+ international distributors. Invest in Instagram/Pinterest influencer campaigns targeting home décor audience.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15K toolkit grant + ₹5L collateral-free loan at 5% interest for artisan-entrepreneur partnerships
🏛
PMEGP
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for SC/ST/women) for setting up handloom micro-enterprises with capacity building support
🏚
Kerala Handloom Weavers Comprehensive Rehabilitation Scheme
subsidized raw material, market linkage support, and 10% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹10L
🏚
Kerala Startup Mission
₹1 Cr corpus for textile/handicraft startups + incubation support, mentorship, and investor connections for handloom-based businesses

Marketing — Zero Budget Se Shuru Karo
  • Instagram Reels showing weaver artisans at traditional looms with behind-the-scenes production (2-3 per week). Tag eco-conscious influencers (100K+ followers) in home décor niche. Use hashtags #HandloomArt #KeralaTextiles #SustainableHome to reach 5-10K monthly impressions.
  • Email nurture campaigns to 50+ interior designers with seasonal collections, design inspiration PDFs, and wholesale pricing. Offer 20% referral bonus for designer recommendations. Target US/EU designers via LinkedIn outreach with case studies of previous designer collaborations.
  • Content marketing: publish 2 blog posts monthly on Medium, Substack on 'Indian handloom heritage,' 'sustainable home textiles,' 'weaver stories' with rich visuals. Optimize for long-tail keywords. Embed product links and CTAs to drive organic traffic to e-commerce store.
