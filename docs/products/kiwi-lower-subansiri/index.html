Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Lower Subansiri ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ziro Valley kiwi farms in Arunachal Pradesh and connect with existing growers; study soil testing reports from ICAR-ATARI Northeast; attend fruit cultivation workshops at Pasighat Agricultural Office; analyze pricing on ITC eChoupal and National Agricultural Market (e-NAM)
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure certified kiwi saplings (Hayward/Abbotts varieties) from ICAR regional station at Barapani, Meghalaya or State Agriculture Department nurseries; first-batch cost for 500 plants: ₹200,000-300,000; arrange organic inputs from local biodiversity centers
3
Produce / Partner
Lease minimum 1 hectare land in Ziro/Daporijo area (₹2-5L annually); establish trellised garden beds; partner with NABARD for technical guidance and co-investment schemes; hire local trained farmworkers; implement drip irrigation system (cost: ₹200,000 per hectare)
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online on Udyam portal); obtain IEC code from nearest port authority (Kolkata/Bangalore); register trademark on 'Subansiri Kiwi' or chosen brand name; obtain organic certification from APEDA-accredited certifier (₹30,000-50,000); comply with FSSAI food safety standards
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Fresh, Blinkit, and JioMart with ₹150-200/kg price point; engage Jio-affiliated fruit aggregators; supply to Organic India retail chains and health food stores in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai; launch Instagram account showcasing farm practices and nutrition benefits; contact export-import houses in Kolkata for international distribution
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 3-5 hectares; develop packaged products (dried kiwi, juice concentrate); establish cold chain storage facility (₹50-70L investment); partner with e-commerce logistics (Flipkart Logistics, XpressBees); launch B2B corporate gifting during festive season; explore APEDA export subsidy programs for international markets
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan with ₹5L subsidy (25%) for food processing and horticulture ventures
🏛
NABARD Farmers' Promotion of Agricultural Clusters (NFAC)
grant up to ₹50L for fruit production cluster development in Arunachal Pradesh
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Promotion Scheme
₹40% subsidy on plant/saplings, irrigation, greenhouse setup (check with State Agriculture Department)
🏚
AP Agriculture Mechanization Scheme
₹50% subsidy on tractors, harvesters, and processing equipment for registered farm enterprises
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
