Sheohar has ideal agro-climatic conditions with fertile alluvial soil and dependable monsoon patterns, making it a natural maize belt. The district has strong farmer networks and established grain collection centers. Proximity to major milling hubs in Bihar and direct access to feed manufacturers in North India makes it ideal for value-added maize products.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sheohar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sheohar's primary mandi (Sheohar Grain Market) and connect with grain merchants; study competition at Bihar Grain Mills Association (BGMA) in Patna; learn processing from existing corn flour mills in Sitamarhi and Darbhanga clusters.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source maize grain directly from Sheohar farmers cooperatives (e.g., Sheohar District Cooperative Union) at ₹18-22 per kg; first batch of 5,000 kg will cost ₹90K-110K; establish direct links with 3-4 farmer groups for consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing mini-mills in Sheohar or rent milling capacity from cooperative societies; or start with contract milling through established players like Siliguri Food Tech (serves Bihar region) before scaling to own processing unit.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as food processor; apply for IEC code through Patna Chamber of Commerce; obtain FSSAI license (Food Safety certification) from Sheohar district office; register with APEDA if exporting; brand as 'SheoharGold Maize' or similar with Geographical Indication association messaging.
5
First Sale
List on IndiaMART, TradeKey for B2B bulk orders; sell on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket for packaged products; approach regional distributors in Darbhanga, Sitamarhi; target institutional buyers (school midday meal schemes in Bihar); cold-call poultry feed manufacturers in Tiruppur.
6
Scale
After ₹5L monthly revenue, upgrade to own processing plant (₹15-25L capex); expand product line with fortified maize flour, cornmeal, popcorn; build direct export partnerships with Bangladesh and Nepal agro-traders; scale to ₹50L annual revenue within 18 months.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Formalisation of Micro Food Enterprises (PMFME)
up to ₹25L subsidy for food processing setup, 10% own contribution required
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan + 25% subsidy for food business entrepreneurs
🏚
Bihar Pradhan Mantri Kisan Sinchayee Yojana (CM Kisan Samman)
zero-interest crop loans up to ₹5L for farmers and agro-processors
🏚
Bihar Industrial Investment Promotion Policy 2016
5% VAT concession + 10-year property tax exemption for food processing units in tier-3 districts like Sheohar
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
