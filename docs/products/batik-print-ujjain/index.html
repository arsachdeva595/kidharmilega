Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Ujjain ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ujjain's established batik cluster in Vijayanagar Ward and surrounding artisan colonies. Connect with Ujjain Chamber of Commerce and local SEWA members. Study 3-5 existing successful batik producers; spend 2-3 weeks learning traditional wax-resist techniques from master artisans. Cost: ₹5,000-10,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source pure cotton (60-80 count) from Indore textile mills (₹150-250/meter). Procure beeswax from local suppliers or Nagpur via wholesale markets (₹400-600/kg). Purchase natural indigo and synthetic batik dyes from Ahmedabad or Mumbai chemical wholesalers (₹2,000-5,000 for starter kit). First batch of 50 meters fabric + materials: ₹15,000-20,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 experienced batik artisans in Ujjain (₹200-400 per saree labor cost) or establish in-house production with trained helpers. Set up small workshop (200-300 sq ft) in Vijayanagar or Freeganj area (₹8,000-12,000/month rent). Initial production capacity: 40-60 sarees per month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online at udyamregistration.gov.in (free, 10 minutes). Obtain IEC code from DGFT portal for export capability (₹0, online). Create brand identity with logo and tagline emphasizing GI heritage. Register trademark with IP India (₹9,000-15,000). Open current + FCRA account at nearest bank.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram and Facebook with high-quality product photography (₹5,000 for photoshoot). List on Meesho, Flipkart sellers, and Amazon Handmade (0% commission initially). Approach 10-15 ethnic boutiques in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with wholesale samples. Create WhatsApp catalog for direct orders. Target initial monthly sales: ₹50,000-80,000.
6
Scale
After first 6 months, expand artisan team by 5-10 members. Build email list and execute monthly campaigns via Mailchimp. Partner with 3-5 export agents for international orders. Attend trade fairs (IHGF Delhi Fair, Summer Cottage Delhi) to secure bulk orders. Launch batik craft workshop experiences (₹1,000/person, 2-hour session) to generate brand loyalty and secondary revenue. Target ₹3-5 Lakh monthly revenue by Month 12.

🏛
PMEGP
up to ₹25 Lakh loan (75% subsidy for SC/ST/women) for setting up batik production micro-enterprise in rural/semi-rural Ujjain
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Laghu Udyog Vikas Nidhi
interest subsidy of 2-3% on working capital loans up to ₹50 Lakh for textile artisans
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Handicraft Cluster Development Scheme
₹5-15 Lakh grant for infrastructure, skill training, and marketing support for GI-tagged batik clusters
