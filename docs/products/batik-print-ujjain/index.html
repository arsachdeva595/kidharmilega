Ujjain's Bherugarh village on the Shipra River banks hosts 800 active batik artisans using wax-resist and crushed-wax cracking techniques passed down through Mughal-era craft traditions. Raw materials (hot wax, textile-grade dyes, cotton/silk fabric) are available locally or within 50 km from wholesalers in Ujjain city. Products sell via internet platforms and directly to exporters targeting USA, Europe, and Japan fashion markets.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Ujjain ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bherugarh village (8 km from Ujjain city) on any weekday; meet 2-3 artisans directly to watch wax-resist dyeing; call District Industries Centre Ujjain (0734-2516041) to get list of registered batik artisan cooperatives; attend Ujjain Haat held Thursdays in Mahakal Bazaar to see final products and buyer interest—all free, doable this week
2
Source Raw Materials
Source hot wax (food-grade or batik-grade, ₹80-120/kg) from chemical wholesalers in Ujjain city (Badshah Nagar area); import fiber-reactive dyes (Procion dyes, ₹200-400/250g jar) from Ahmedabad textile chemical suppliers or online (TM Chemical, Texpal); buy 50m of cotton fabric (₹150-250/m) or silk (₹400-800/m) from Ujjain textile wholesale market; first batch of materials for 5 sarees: ₹8,000-12,000
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 400-500 sq ft unit in Bherugarh or nearby Ujjain (₹3,000-5,000/month); partner with 1-2 experienced batik artisans on per-piece piecework (₹400-800 per saree) rather than hiring; set up basic tools (wax pots, brushes, dyeing vats, drying frames)—₹40,000-60,000 one-time; produce 2-3 sarees/week initially, increasing to 10-15/week by month 6
4
Brand & Register
Register as proprietorship under Udyam (udyamregistration.gov.in)—free, takes 10 min; file for GI mark usage form with Ministry of Commerce via district office (GI protection already granted to Bhairagarh Print Batik, so you can use 'Ujjain Batik' with GI tag)—₹2,000-5,000 filing; if exporting, apply for IEC code from DGFT portal (takes 5-7 days, ₹0 cost); register business name with local municipal/gram panchayat—₹500-1,000
5
First Sale
List first 10 pieces (5 sarees, 5 dress material) on Etsy (18% commission) and Instagram Shop (0% setup fee); price sarees at ₹4,500-6,000, dress material at ₹1,500-2,500; expect first order within 10-20 days; first-month revenue target: ₹25,000-40,000 (5-8 pieces sold)
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (≈80-100 sarees/month), hire 1 full-time artisan (₹10,000-15,000/month) + 1 part-time quality checker (₹5,000/month); upgrade to 800 sq ft workshop (₹6,000-8,000/month); expand design library (hire 1 design assistant, ₹8,000/month); apply for SFURTI cluster support scheme for additional artisan training; exhibit at National Handloom Expo, India Handloom Festival, or GiftExpo

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹10L subsidy (25% of project cost for general, 35% for SC/ST) for setting up registered workshop; loan from partner bank at 5-6% interest
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 toolkit allowance + 5% interest subsidy on ₹1L collateral-free loan for registered artisans; priority for GI-tagged craft
🏚
Madhya Pradesh ODOP Scheme
interest subsidy on collateral-free MUDRA loans up to ₹10L; priority support for artisan cluster linkage
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Artisan Welfare Fund
₹500-2,000 per registered artisan for tool upgrades, material grants; apply via District Industries Centre
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
