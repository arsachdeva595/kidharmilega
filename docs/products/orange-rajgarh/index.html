Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Rajgarh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Rajgarh's agricultural office at Rajgarh Collectorate and meet district horticulture officer; study existing orange growers in Khilchipur and Biargarh blocks; join Rajgarh District Farmer Producer Company for market insights and benchmarking.
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure certified orange saplings from ICAR research stations in Jabalpur or Gwalior (₹25-40 per sapling, initial 5000 saplings = ₹150,000); buy irrigation equipment and fertilizers from regional agricultural supply stores in Rajgarh town; initial infrastructure cost approximately ₹8-12 lakhs for 50 acres.
3
Produce / Partner
Either purchase 50-100 acres of land (or lease via 15-year contracts); partner with local agricultural colleges for agronomic training; hire farm managers experienced in citrus cultivation; establish micro-irrigation system; tree maturity takes 3-4 years before commercial production.
4
Brand & Register
Register as agricultural producer company under Multi-State Cooperative Societies Act; obtain Udyam registration for processing unit if adding value; apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) through DGFT; register brand with Trademark office in Delhi; obtain FSSAI license if packaging/processing juice or concentrates.
5
First Sale
List fresh oranges on BigBasket, Blinkit, JioMart with ₹800-1200/kg pricing; register with fruit export associations; contact wholesale traders in Indore's APMC market; partner with juice manufacturers in Maharashtra; approach organized retail chains (Reliance Fresh, Dmart, Spencer's) with pilot shipments.
6
Scale
Expand to 200-300 acres by year 2-3; establish on-farm cold storage facility (₹20-30 lakhs); develop export certifications (GlobalGAP, Organic); launch branded packaged juice or concentrate; build direct B2B relationships with 5-10 large juice manufacturers; explore contract farming with neighboring smallholders.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for agricultural business setup with 25% margin money
🏛
MUDRA Scheme
up to ₹10 lakh credit without collateral for agribusiness expansion through NABARD partner banks
🏚
Rajiv Gandhi Kisaan Nyay Yojana (MP)
direct income support ₹4,000-6,000 per acre for farmers including horticulture crops
🏚
MP Horticulture Development Program
50% subsidy on drip irrigation and greenhouse infrastructure for citrus cultivation
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
