Nalanda has a 500+ year weaving tradition rooted in its historical significance as a Buddhist learning center. The region's artisan community possesses deep expertise in complex motif patterns and sustainable cotton-silk blending. Local soil and water conditions naturally suit indigo and natural dye production, making Bawan Booti's 52-motif design intrinsically tied to Nalanda's cultural fabric.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Nalanda ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nalanda weaving clusters in Biharsharif and Silao; connect with Nalanda Handicrafts Association; study competitor saris on Etsy, Sarangi, and Jaypore; analyze 52-motif design variations in the region.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton-silk blended yarn from Varanasi yarn merchants (₹8000-12000 per kg); procure natural indigo and madder from Jaipur natural dye suppliers (₹2000-3000 per kg); first batch of 10 saris requires ₹80000-120000 in raw materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established weaver families in Biharsharif (minimum 5-8 looms); negotiate per-sari rates of ₹3500-5500 based on motif complexity; formalize contracts with artisan groups registered under SFURTI; hire a design coordinator to ensure motif consistency.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (free, within 15 days); obtain IEC code from DGFT Patna office (₹5000, 10 days); trademark brand name via IPO (₹4500-9000); get textile export certificate from SRTEPC Chennai office; apply for Handloom Mark.
5
First Sale
List on Handloom Haat, Sarangi, Amar Handloom, and Jaypore within 30 days; create Instagram content showing weaver stories and motif meanings; launch WhatsApp catalog for direct customer sales; approach boutiques in Delhi, Bangalore, and Mumbai with samples; participate in VillageMart or Craftroots aggregator platforms.
6
Scale
After 6 months, introduce capsule collections with seasonal themes; expand to 15-20 artisan looms; establish a design studio in Biharsharif; develop B2B relationships with luxury hotel chains and wedding planners; apply for PMEGP second-phase grant for production facility upgrade; explore international wholesale partnerships through trade shows.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
up to ₹25 lakh loan with 35% subsidy for SC/ST and 15% for general category to establish production unit.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15000 toolkits and ₹1 lakh concessional loan for artisan skill upgrades and tool modernization.
🏚
Bihar Handloom Weaver Support Scheme
direct subsidy of ₹5000 per loom and raw material subsidy up to 30% for registered artisans.
🏚
Bihar MSME Export Promotion Scheme
up to ₹2 lakh grant for international trade fair participation and export marketing support.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
