Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Wayanad ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Wayanad Coffee Planters Association in Sultan Bathery; study farms in Kalpetta and Mananthavady clusters; attend 3-month coffee farming apprenticeship; analyze Wayanad Coffee Board data on yield, pricing, pest management
2
Source Raw Materials
Acquire land (minimum 1-2 hectares) in mid-altitude zones (1000-1600m) near Ambalavayal or Kalpetta; source Arabica saplings from IISC Bangalore or local Wayanad nurseries (₹200-400/sapling); first-batch cost for 2 hectares: ₹8-12 lakhs including shade trees, fertilizer, labor setup
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established Wayanad estates for initial contract growing or lease model; invest in pulping and drying infrastructure (₹15-20 lakhs for small 2-hectare operation); hire experienced farm manager from local farming community; join Wayanad Coffee Growers Cooperative for technical support
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as agricultural enterprise (₹0 cost); apply for IEC code from Kozhikode DG office (₹0 cost); get coffee GI certification by registering with GI Registry Kolkata; register trademark with unique name; obtain APEDA certification for exports; create FSSAI approval for packaged products
5
First Sale
Launch on specialty coffee platforms: Inkitt Coffee, Third Wave Coffee marketplace, Amazon Global; approach premium cafe chains (Indian Coffee House, Cafe Coffee Day specialty); partner with export houses in Kochi (SpiceRoute Exports, Wayanad Coffee Exports); start Instagram/Facebook with farm content; attend Coffee India Expo and HoReCa trade shows; offer samples to specialty roasters via email B2B outreach
6
Scale
Expand to 5-10 hectares by Month 36; develop packaged D2C brand with 20% margin improvement; build direct relationships with 5-7 international roasters in EU/USA; establish processing unit (wet mill, dry mill) to increase value-add by 15-20%; create agritourism revenue (farm stays at ₹3000-5000/day); hire sales team for HoReCa channel expansion across tier-1 cities

up to ₹25 lakh loan for coffee processing and value-addition unit with subsidy component

Kerala Agri-Infrastructure Fund
subsidized loans up to ₹50 lakh for irrigation, storage, processing in agriculture sector
🏚
VKSY Spice Park Initiative
dedicated infrastructure support for registered coffee enterprises in Wayanad with subsidy on cold storage and processing
