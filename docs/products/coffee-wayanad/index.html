Coffee ki
opportunity, Wayanad mein.

Wayanad's 67,000-hectare coffee belt spans elevations of 700–2,100 meters in clustered zones: Meppady, Poothadi, Kaniambetta, Noolpuzha, Ambalavayal, Thavinhal, Panamaram, and Thirunelli panchayats, producing 50,000–60,000 tonnes of Robusta coffee annually. Six FPCs connect 60,000 farmers (60%+ small/marginal/tribal), ensuring reliable raw material access within 10–50 km of any processing unit in Kalpetta. Export-ready infrastructure: Wayanad Coffee Park (20 acres, Kalpetta), specialty coffee samples scoring 83–86 cup scores (specialty threshold: 80), and direct ties to international buyers via World Coffee Conference networks and Denmark-based importers.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Wayanad ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call Brahmagiri Development Society (Meppady, 13,000-farmer cooperative) or Wayanad Coffee Board, visit any of the 6 FPC cluster yards in Poothadi or Kaniambetta on a Monday haat day, speak directly with farmers on current bean prices and contract terms. Free. Register Udyam MSME portal (free, 10 minutes, online). Zero cost, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Grade A Robusta beans directly from Meppady, Poothadi, or Kaniambetta FPC yards at ₹200–240/kg (2024 rate). Minimum first batch: 500 kg (₹1L–1.2L). Check cup scores (ask for 80+ samples). Alternatively, buy roasted beans from existing roasters in Kalpetta at 20% markup to start D2C faster.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent 800–1,200 sq ft processing unit in Wayanad Coffee Park (Kalpetta) or Meppady MSME shed at ₹8,000–15,000/month. Setup cost (roaster, grinder, packaging, certifications): ₹1.5–3L. Option B: Partner with an existing small roaster for co-production on 50/50 margin split (₹0 upfront, but lower margins). Processing timeline: 15–30 days bean-to-packaged product.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, online). Apply for GI certification under Wayanad Coffee GI tag (₹15,000–30,000, 60–90 days via Kerala startup office). Obtain ISO 22000 food safety certification (₹30,000–50,000). If exporting: IEC (Importer Exporter Code) via DGFT online (free, 7 days). Get FSSAI food license (₹5,000–10,000). Total: ₹50,000–90,000, 120 days.
5
First Sale
First sale: list on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, or specialty coffee marketplace (CoffeeBoard, Roastery Club) — free tier, commission 15–20%. First order: expect 50–200 units in month 1 from D2C. Wholesale: pitch to 10 local café chains (Trivandrum, Kochi) directly; typical first order: 50 kg at ₹1,400/kg = ₹70,000 revenue. Expected first-month revenue: ₹1–2L (blended).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire a part-time marketer (social media, coffee storytelling) ₹15,000/month, add export fulfillment (partner with Gurgaon-based specialty coffee distributor), exhibit at Indian Coffee Houses Convention (annual, New Delhi) and Copenhagen World Coffee Conference. Expand to 2,000 kg monthly processing. Upgrade to 1,500 sq ft unit (₹20,000/month). Target: ₹30L annual revenue by month 18–24.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
Working Capital Subsidy up to ₹25 Lakhs, subsidy 30–35% for processing units meeting cluster criteria, processing through Ministry of Agriculture
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Loan up to ₹25L with 35% subsidy (General) or 50% (SC/ST/OBC/Minority), processing via KVIC/KVIB
🏚
Wayanad Carbon Neutral Coffee Park Initiative
Lease land in 20-acre park (Kalpetta) at concessional ₹30–50/sq ft (vs. ₹100–150 market rate); committed to GI-compliant units
🏚
Kerala Agritech Fund
₹50L grants for value-added coffee processing tech (roasting automation, packaging); apply via State Agricultural Department
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Haan, strong potential. Wayanad coffee is specialty-grade (83–86 cup scores), GI-tagged (2019), and already exported to Denmark. Process: get IEC (free, 1 week via DGFT), ISO 22000 (₹30–50K, 60 days), Fairtrade/UTZ certification (optional, ₹50–100K, 90 days). FOB price: ₹2,500–4,000/kg for 83+ beans. Buyer search: Specialty Coffee Association Europe, Nordic Specialty Coffee Association, UK importers. First export order: 5–10 tonnes (₹1.25–4Cr revenue). Logistics: Cochin Port, 350 km away; freight ₹30–50/kg.
Real risk: coffee prices fluctuate (₹180–280/kg wholesale), bad harvest = raw material scarcity, low cup scores = zero buyer interest, instant coffee factory requires ₹2+ Cr. Downside: 50% of startups don't survive past year 2 due to quality or cash flow. Exit options: (1) Sell processing unit to larger roaster, (2) Supply raw beans back to FPC (₹200–240/kg, lower margin but safe), (3) Pivot to coffee tourism + plantation stays (₹500–1,500/person), (4) Merge with successful FPC to become co-roaster. Insurance: FPCs offer contract farming at guaranteed ₹200/kg minimum — reduces price risk.
