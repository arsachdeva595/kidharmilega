Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Arwal ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established mushroom farms in Arwal district (especially near Arwal town and Karpi block); interact with 5-10 farmers growing oyster and button mushrooms; study spawn suppliers in Bihar Agricultural Department's network and connect with Patna-based mushroom research centers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source spawn from recognized suppliers like Directorate of Mushroom Research (DMR) ICAR, Solan or Bihar Agricultural Department vendors at ₹200-300 per kg; procure agricultural waste (paddy straw) locally at ₹2,000-3,000 per ton; first-batch raw material cost (500 kg spawn + substrate for 5,000 bags) approximately ₹80,000-120,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish a small 500-1,000 sq ft shed with basic climate control (200-300 sq ft production area); partner with trained farmers through contract farming or hire certified mushroom cultivators; start with 5,000-10,000 bags in first cycle (45-60 day harvest cycle); ensure humidity (80-90%) and temperature (15-22°C) management.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in as MSME (likely ₹0-500 registration); obtain IEC from DGFT (Directorate General of Foreign Trade) if targeting exports; register business under FSSAI for packaged mushroom products (₹2,000-5,000); create brand with mushroom-focused packaging highlighting freshness and nutritional benefits.
5
First Sale
List on BigBasket, Blinkit, Zepto for direct-to-consumer sales in Patna and nearby cities; supply to hotel chains and restaurants in Arwal and Gaya through direct B2B pitching; partner with regional FMCG distributors; create WhatsApp catalog and local Facebook groups for bulk buyers; attend Patna's weekly vegetable markets.
6
Scale
Expand to 20,000+ bags production capacity; develop dried mushroom and value-added product lines; establish cold chain logistics for regional and interstate distribution; launch e-commerce presence on Amazon/Flipkart with packaged products; begin export shipments to Nepal and Bangladesh through food export agents; train 20-30 farmers as contract growers to secure supply.

₹6,000 annual support to eligible farmers; supplement mushroom farming income
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (25-35% of project cost) for mushroom cultivation units under agribusiness category
🏚
Bihar State Agricultural Mission
direct subsidies for horticulture and value-added agriculture including mushroom cultivation at 25-40% project cost
🏚
Bihar Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) scheme
free technical training, spawn supply support, and certification for mushroom farmers across Arwal district
