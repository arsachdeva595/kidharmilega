Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Meerut ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Meerut's sports equipment clusters in Kharkhauda and Sardhana areas; spend 2-3 weeks understanding production workflows, meet 5-10 established manufacturers; research export certifications (ISO 9001, CE mark for hockey sticks). Cost: ₹15,000-20,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium willow wood from Kashmir suppliers (contact via Meerut Sports Goods Association); source leather from Kanpur tanneries; synthetic materials from Delhi suppliers. First batch of 100 cricket bats: ₹2-3L; 50 hockey sticks: ₹1.5-2L. Total first-batch cost: ₹4-5L.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established bat/stick makers in Kharkhauda industrial area for contract manufacturing (production capacity: 500-1000 units/month); negotiate revenue-share or per-unit pricing model. Alternatively, lease small 2000 sqft workshop and hire 3-5 skilled craftsmen (₹1L/month total wages).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME registration via udyamregistration.gov.in); obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office for exports; register brand name + logo via IP India; get ISO 9001 certification from TUV/NABL (₹40,000-60,000); comply with sports equipment standards (BIS ISI mark for certain products).
5
First Sale
List on Amazon, Flipkart, Cricketnext, and Sportsuncle within 2 months; reach out to 20-30 district-level sports retailers; launch LinkedIn B2B outreach to school/academy procurement teams; participate in IITF (India International Trade Fair) for export buyer connections; contact 5-10 export houses in Delhi for distribution.
6
Scale
After ₹20L revenue: invest in semi-automated machinery (band saws, seasoning kilns) for 40% cost reduction; hire dedicated export sales manager; build relationships with 2-3 large European distributors; introduce new product lines (badminton rackets, protective gear); establish QC lab to capture premium segment.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general category) for manufacturing setup with focus on job creation.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster development support including common facility centers, skill training, and working capital for registered artisans in Meerut sports cluster.
🏚
UP MSMEs Development Fund
up to ₹10L term loan at 4% interest subsidy for manufacturing units in notified ODOP clusters; apply via MSME-DI Meerut.
🏚
UP Rozgar Sangharsh Bhatta Yojana
monthly stipend for trained youth joining sports equipment manufacturing; coordination through State Industrial Development Authority (SIDA).
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
