Sikkim Large Cardamom ki
opportunity, Soreng mein.

Soreng district in West Sikkim (293.22 sq km, population 64,760) is the national epicenter of large cardamom cultivation at 900-2000m elevation, with raw material directly accessible from natural forest reserves managed by indigenous Lepchas. The cluster spans multiple villages across Soreng with an estimated 5,000-8,000 smallholder farmer families; production is entirely organic under forest shade, naturally suited to premium and export markets. Buyer markets are concentrated in Delhi (Khari Baoli spice hub), Mumbai, Bangalore domestically, and UK, US, Germany internationally—each importing at premium FOB rates of ₹900-1500/kg.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Soreng ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Soreng district farmer cooperative office (contact via NIC Soreng: soreng.nic.in) and ask for introduction to cardamom farmers in Soreng, Sangkhola, Legship blocks. Call 3-4 cardamom traders in Khari Baoli (Delhi) directly—ask wholesale prices, minimum order, quality specs. Zero cost, doable within 2 weeks via phone and one visit.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source dried large cardamom from Soreng district farmers (post-September harvest) or established cardamom mandi suppliers like those in Gangtok (Sikkim capital, 65km away). First batch cost: ₹3-5L for 500kg at ₹600-700/kg raw. Contact Soreng Farmers Cooperative or wholesalers in Gangtok Spice Mandi.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent 500-800 sq ft unit in Soreng/Gangtok (₹5-8k/month) and install 1-2 traditional Bhatti ovens (₹1-2L each for quality build). Partner with local skilled curer (₹15-20k monthly stipend). Option B: Contract with existing Bhatti operators in Soreng (40-50 exist locally)—pay per kg curing cost (₹80-120/kg). Total operational cost for 500kg monthly batch: ₹1.5-2.5L.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam within 1 week (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in). File for GI tag co-use immediately (Ministry of Commerce CGPDTM office supports existing GI users; ₹10-15k registration, 2-3 months processing). If exporting: apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at DGFT portal (₹0, 7-10 days). Register FSSAI if packaging (₹100, 1 week online). Total: ₹15k, 4-6 weeks for all approvals.
5
First Sale
First sale: List on Amazon/Blinkit as seller (free listing, ₹45/transaction fee) with Udyam number + FSSAI cert. Target: First order 50 packs (100gm) at ₹350 MRP, ₹210 net to you = ₹10.5k revenue. Parallel: Reach out to Khari Baoli traders (via initial calls) with sample + invoice; first bulk order typically ₹50-80k for 50kg. Expected Month 1 revenue: ₹75-120k from mixed channels.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: Hire 1 operations manager (₹25-30k/month) to handle farmer sourcing and Bhatti scheduling. Invest ₹5L in private labeling for bulk B2B orders. Exhibit at Indian Spice Trade Association trade shows (January, April), and Food & Hotel India Expo (November). Launch export via customs broker (₹20-30k setup); engage with UK/US importers via TradeKey; target 2-3 regular export clients by month 18. Scale packaging from 100gm to 250gm, 500gm variants.
