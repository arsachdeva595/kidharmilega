Kadaknath Chicken ki
opportunity, Jhabua mein.

Jhabua district in southwestern Madhya Pradesh (bordering Gujarat and Rajasthan) is the native breeding ground for Kadaknath chicken, with 5000-8000 backyard farming households primarily among Bhil and Bhilala tribal communities. The breed thrives in the region's semi-arid terrain and benefits from readily available kitchen waste as sustainable feed. Domestic market demand spans South India (Kerala, Telangana, Andhra Pradesh, Tamil Nadu), with GI-certified status commanding 150-200% price premium over regular chicken.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jhabua ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 2-3 existing tribal Kadaknath farmers in Jhabua town or Meghnagar tehsil (ask district office for names); visit a backyard farm cluster near Jhabua or Thandla; register free Udyam account on udyamregistration.gov.in; zero cost, 3 hours total
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Kadaknath chicks from Poultry Breeding Farm, Jhabua district (or Dhar district partner farms); first batch of 30 chicks costs ₹600-800 per chick (₹18,000-24,000 for starter flock); 100-125 days to reach 1.10-1.25 kg body weight; feed cost ₹40-60/bird using kitchen waste and local grain mixes
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 500-1000 sqft backyard space in village periphery (₹2,000-5,000/month) or use own land; build basic shed with bamboo/wood frame, wire mesh, water system (₹15,000-30,000 one-time); partnership with existing tribal farmers also viable for shared infrastructure; no advanced machinery needed — gravity feeders and drinkers only
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 15 mins online); apply for GI certification support via ODOP scheme (₹0 cost, 30 days processing through district ODOP cell); get FSSAI registration for meat processing if value-adding (₹500-2000, 10-15 days); apply for IEC code if exporting (₹500, 5 days via nearest customs office in Indore)
5
First Sale
First sales: cold-call specialty poultry shops and health food stores in Indore (30km from Jhabua); post-harvest direct sales to neighbors/village; register on BigBasket seller portal (free listing, 3-5% commission); first order size: 10-20 birds per buyer; expect ₹8,000-15,000 first-month revenue from 4-5 retail sales
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1-2 workers for daily feed/care (₹8,000-12,000/month); invest in cold storage unit (₹80,000-150,000) for better preservation; partner with 2-3 regional logistics providers (Indore-based) for daily evacuation to Kerala/Hyderabad; participate in state ODOP exhibitions (3-4 per year) and food trade shows in Bangalore, Delhi; develop basic website and WhatsApp catalog for direct B2B orders

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Prime Minister Matsya Sampad Yojana (for livestock/poultry): up to ₹5L credit at 0% interest for 2 years if eligible through NABARD
🏛
PMEGP (Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme): ₹10L subsidy + ₹5L bank loan at 8% for poultry farm setup; 35% subsidy for general category applicants
🏚
Madhya Pradesh ODOP scheme: 90% cost subsidy for GI certification, packaging design, export certification; food processing units get ₹50L capital subsidy
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Pashu Samridhi Yojana: ₹2,000-5,000 subsidy per livestock/poultry unit for tribal farmers; free veterinary services for 2 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Nahi zaroor nahi. Lekin tribal communities mein generation-wise practice hai — agar gaon mein koi experienced farmer ho toh unse 1 mahine apprentice kar lo. Jhabua mein 5000+ backyard farmers hain, sabse seekh sakta hai. Poultry center from district office bhi training dete hain (free).
ODOP scheme se ₹1.5L-₹3L subsidy GI certification, cold chain, packaging ke liye. Pashu Samridhi Yojana MP se ₹2-5K direct. PMEGP se ₹10L subsidy + ₹5L loan possible. NABARD se livestock loan 0% interest. Apna accountant ya district ODOP office mein pooch.
BigBasket, Blinkit, Amazon Fresh ko direct pitch kar — health food niche mein tere GI status bahut achchha lagega. Specialty poultry shops Indore mein hain jo premium varieties chahte hain. WhatsApp par ayurveda colleges aur naturopathy clinics ko direct DM kar. Local mela aur haat mein 20-30 direct customers mil jaate hain.
Ha, par sirf India ke andar — Kerala, Hyderabad, Telangana, Tamil Nadu. International export mein GI rules strict hain aur Kadaknath breed-specific regulations hain, jo difficult hai. Domestic export mein cold logistics chahiye (₹50-100 per kg transport), lekin margin acche hain 35-50% FOB level par.
Honest baat: bird mortality risk hai (medical reasons); market demand niche ho sakta hai (sirf premium segments mein); cold chain fail ho sakta hai. Exit option: apne feed grain stock ko local farmers ko bech dena (loss kam), birds ko live market mein sell karna (30-40% loss possible). Lekin Jhabua mein 5000+ successfully running hain, toh risk managed hai agar patient rahe 12+ months.
