Surat's diamond cutting hub is concentrated in South Gujarat with 250,000 skilled artisans within 20 km radius, fed by rough stones imported through Mumbai port (380 km southwest). The city processes 8 million carats annually and exports ₹45,000 Cr worth of finished gems/jewelry, with direct shipping access via Kandla Port (180 km north) and GUJEX seaport. US markets account for 60%+ of export volume, making it the primary buyer hub for Surat jewelry manufacturers.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Surat ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Diamond Bourse in Mahidharpura (South Surat) on a weekday morning — free walk-in, no appointment. Talk to 3-5 cutting masters about how they operate, raw material sourcing, and order volumes. Call 2 polishing units to understand their per-carat costs and turnaround. Register free on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in) with your Aadhaar. Zero cost, 4-5 hours total.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source rough diamonds via Mumbai diamond market (Bandra Talao area, 380 km away) or directly from importer contacts in Surat (City Light area). First batch: order ₹3-5L worth of rough carats (1,000-2,000 carats depending on quality) from an established importer. Turnaround from rough to polished: 15-30 days via Mahidharpura artisan network at ₹80-200 per carat cutting+polishing cost.
3
Produce / Partner
Option 1: Rent a 500-1000 sqft unit in Mahidharpura Diamond Bourse complex (₹8,000-12,000/month, ready infrastructure with electricity, water). Stock rough stones and subcontract cutting/polishing to master artisans on per-carat basis. Option 2: Partnership model — work with a cutting master (50-50 profit share after costs, zero rent). Start with ₹2-3L for first raw material batch + working capital.
4
Brand & Register
Get Udyam registration (free, 10 mins online, MSME classification). Apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via online portal at icegate.indiaport.gov.in (₹0, 5-7 days processing). File GST registration (free, 3-5 days). For export, register with Gem & Jewellery Export Promotion Council (GJEPC) in Mumbai (₹10,000 annual membership). GI registration for 'Surat Gems' is pending approval — track status via DC Handicrafts office.
5
First Sale
First sale: list 10-15 designs on Etsy (₹2,000 annual shop fee + 3% per transaction). Target 2-3 orders in first month (₹8,000-15,000 revenue per piece at D2C). Simultaneously contact 5-10 jewelry retailers in Mumbai via direct calls + WhatsApp with catalog + per-carat pricing. Expected first month: ₹40,000-80,000 (mix of D2C + local wholesale).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time designer (₹15,000/month), 1 logistics manager (₹12,000/month), 1 sales coordinator (₹10,000/month). Formalize artisan contracts (SLA on quality/turnaround). Export to 2-3 US distributors via B2B platforms (Global Sources, Alibaba premium). Exhibit at Gem & Jewellery India (GJIE) in Mumbai annually (₹3-5L booth + travel). Move to dedicated 2,000 sqft unit with CNC equipment (₹5-8L capex).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 grant + 5% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹1 Cr for artisans and jewelry craftspeople. Self-enrollment online at pmvishwakarma.gov.in.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹25-30L loan @ 24% subsidy for 25% of loan amount. Mandatory SIDBI bank partnership required, processing 2-3 months.
🏚
Gujarat ODOP Development Fund
Direct grant up to ₹5L for formalization + marketing for recognized ODOP artisans and manufacturers. Apply through District Administration Surat, once annually.
🏚
Gujarat Artisan Welfare Scheme
₹2,000/month pension after age 60 for registered craftspeople. Enroll via state handicrafts office with Udyam number.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
