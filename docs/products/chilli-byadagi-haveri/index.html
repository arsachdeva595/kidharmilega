Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Haveri ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Byadagi town and Haveri district farmer clusters (Byadagi APMC market, nearby villages like Tonda, Magod). Spend 2-3 days with local farmers and traders to understand cultivation cycles, yield variations, pricing trends. Connect with Haveri District Agriculture Department and local farmer cooperatives to understand post-harvest handling practices. Cost: ₹15,000-25,000
2
Source Raw Materials
Approach established chilli farmers in Byadagi cluster directly or through local farmer cooperatives like Haveri District Chilli Growers Association. Source directly post-harvest (December-February) at ₹30,000-40,000 per quintal for quality Grade A chillis. First batch: 5 quintals (₹1.5-2 Lakhs) for testing, drying, and processing. Establish supply agreements with 3-5 farmers to ensure consistent sourcing.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing spice processing units in Haveri (Byadagi has 40+ registered processing units) for drying, grinding, and packaging on commission basis (₹2,000-4,000 per quintal). Alternatively, lease a 500 sq.ft. space in Byadagi APMC cluster (₹8,000-12,000/month), install basic drying racks and storage. Start with sun-drying and hand-sorting, upgrade to mechanical dryers after 6 months once volume reaches 50 quintals/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification online, free). Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Belgaum (45km away, ₹0, online process). Get Spice Board registration from Regional Office in Bangalore. Register brand name with trademark office (₹5,000-10,000). Obtain FSSAI license for food processing (₹5,000). Total: ₹10,000-15,000
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Seller Central and Flipkart Seller Hub with 'Authentic Byadagi Chilli GI-certified' positioning. Price at ₹280-350 per 100g, target 50 units/week initially. Simultaneously, contact spice wholesalers in Bangalore, Mumbai, Hyderabad spice markets and offer 15-20% trade margins. Reach out to 30-40 specialty food retailers and ayurvedic product companies for B2B orders. Use Instagram and YouTube to build brand credibility with GI story and farmer testimonials.
6
Scale
After month 6, if you hit ₹10-15L revenue, hire a sales team for direct B2B outreach. Develop value-added products (chilli powder, smoked varieties) to increase SKU count. Launch export operations via freight forwarders in Bangalore/Belgaum targeting North America and Europe (±30% higher margins). Negotiate bulk supply agreements with food manufacturers. Expand to 200+ quintals/year processing capacity by partnering with additional processing units or setting up your own 2,000 sq.ft. facility (₹50-75L capex).
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakhs loan for setting up spice processing unit, 35% subsidy for SC/ST applicants
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹15 Lakhs for cluster development and agri-processing infrastructure in Byadagi cluster
🏚
Karnataka Startup Policy 2020
tax incentives and GST refunds for registered startups processing agricultural products in rural areas like Haveri
🏚
Pradhan Mantri Formalisation of Micro Food Processing Enterprises (PM FME)
₹10 Lakh credit-linked subsidy for setting up food processing units in agricultural clusters
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Marketing

Ye marketing ideas try karna mat bhulna

  • Create YouTube video series 'From Farm to Table' featuring Haveri farmers, their cultivation techniques, and the GI certification story. Target food bloggers and health/spice enthusiasts. Distribute 500-1000 sample packs to micro-influencers in the spice/food niche for organic reach.
  • Partner with 5-10 premium restaurant chains (South Indian, fusion cuisine) in Bangalore/Hyderabad to use your branded chilli, create co-branded menu items, and place point-of-sale branding. Generate word-of-mouth from high-end diners.
