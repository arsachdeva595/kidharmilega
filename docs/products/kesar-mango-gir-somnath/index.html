Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Gir Somnath ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kesar mango clusters in Somnath, Veraval, and Una taluks; meet established growers at Gir Somnath Agricultural Produce Market; study GI certification standards and export protocols at Ministry of Commerce website; attend APEDA export training workshops
2
Source Raw Materials
Source grafted Kesar saplings from ICAR-CTCRI and Gujarat Agricultural University (₹200-300 per sapling); negotiate bulk contracts with local irrigation suppliers; first 1-acre setup requires ₹3-4L for land prep, saplings, and irrigation
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing Kesar mango farmers for contract farming or co-ownership models; join Gir Somnath Agricultural Cooperative Society; establish tie-ups with cold storage facilities at Veraval port for post-harvest handling; hire seasonal labor during June-July peak season
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) on udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT for export eligibility; apply for GI certificate validation with Ministry of Agriculture; register brand at Indian Institute of Intellectual Property; get APEDA registration for agricultural export
5
First Sale
List on BigBasket, Grofers, and Amazon Fresh for D2C in metros; partner with Spice Route Exports and Maersk Fresh for B2B distribution; reach out to Taj Hotels, ITC, and premium restaurant chains; join APEDA's Agricultural and Processed Food Export Authority for export leads; create Instagram content highlighting GI tag and heritage story
6
Scale
Expand to 5-10 acre farmland in years 2-3; build branded cold storage facility near Veraval; develop value-added product line (pulp, juice) for off-season revenue; partner with e-commerce platforms for gift bundles during Diwali and festive seasons; establish direct export relationships with 2-3 European and Middle Eastern importers; create subscription model for NRI diaspora communities
