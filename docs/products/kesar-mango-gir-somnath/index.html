Kesar Mango ki
opportunity, Gir Somnath mein.

Gir Somnath district sits 250 km south of Ahmedabad in coastal Gujarat, with two dedicated Kesar mango production clusters (Gir and Talalaa) spanning thousands of acres of mature orchards. The unique soil minerals and semi-coastal climate produce the distinctive saffron hue and concentrated sweetness that commands 40-50% price premium over regular mangoes. Direct connectivity to Ahmedabad APMC, Surat wholesale markets, and air/sea cargo terminals at Ahmedabad airport (200 km) and Kandla port (180 km) enable rapid domestic and export distribution.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Gir Somnath ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 2-3 established Kesar mango traders in Gir or Talalaa clusters (contact Gir Somnath District Panchayat office for farmer contact list). Visit the Gir cluster during flowering season (Feb-Mar) to see orchards and understand production calendar. Register free Udyam account on udyamregistration.gov.in — takes 15 minutes online. Attend the weekly Ahmedabad APMC mango auction (Thursdays 6-8 AM) to see pricing, buyer behavior, and grading standards.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source directly from farmer-suppliers in Gir or Talalaa clusters via local middlemen (avoid commission agents initially). First seasonal batch: 5-10 tonnes fruit cost ₹40,000-60,000 (₹4,000-6,000 per tonne). Negotiate long-term contracts with 3-4 orchardists during Feb-Mar for guaranteed May-Aug supply. Arrange quick transport to cold storage in Ahmedabad APMC or Surat (within 12 hours of harvest).
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 2,000-3,000 sq ft unit in Ahmedabad's APMC wholesale zone or Surat's Ashram Road wholesale market (₹15,000-25,000/month) with existing cold storage access. Partner with a contract packhouse (grading, sizing, washing, packaging) for ₹0.50-1 per fruit. Start with 5-10 tonne batches in May, scale to 50 tonnes/month by July. Total first-month cost: ₹2,50,000-4,50,000 (fruit + cold storage + packaging + transport).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 15 min online). File GI certification application via Gir Somnath District Panchayat (₹10,000-15,000 filing fee; GI benefit allows use of "Gir Kesar" protected mark — already awarded 2010). If exporting: apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via DGFT portal (₹0, digital). Obtain FSSAI food license (₹5,000-10,000, 30 days). Get cold storage certification from state horticulture dept (₹5,000). Total: ₹20,000-40,000, 45-60 days.
5
First Sale
First sale: approach Ahmedabad's Hypercity or Reliance Fresh procurement office (near APMC) with 1-2 tonne sample (no upfront cost — consignment model for first order). Alternatively, list on Grofers/JioMart marketplace (platform takes 15-20% commission). First order size likely: 500-1,000 boxes (50-100 kg). Expected first-month revenue: ₹1.2-2L (if wholesale), ₹2-3L (if D2C). Payment terms: net 7-15 days from retailers, instant on D2C.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire a supply manager (₹25,000/month) to source fruit from 5-10 orchards; add packaging designer (freelance, ₹5,000/month) for premium gift box branding; establish your own cold storage unit (₹25-40L investment) to reduce per-unit cost. Apply for One District One Product (ODOP) subsidy (up to ₹10L support for production infra). Attend Vibrant Gujarat summit (biennial) and Agrotech India expo to connect with retail chains and exporters. Scale export volume from 5 tonnes/month to 50+ tonnes/month by partnering with 2-3 freight forwarders.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
Up to ₹15,000 first subsidy + ₹500/month bank interest subsidy for 18 months if registered as a fruit processing/trading enterprise (check eligibility at pmvishwakarma.gov.in).
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
Shishu (₹50,000), Kishor (₹5,00,000), Tarun (₹1,00,00,000) loans at 8-10% interest from SBI, ICICI, HDFC Bank for cold storage infra, packaging equipment, working capital — no collateral for loans up to ₹50L.
🏚
Vajpayee Bankable Scheme (Gujarat)
Covers 50% of equipment cost for processing/packaging units engaged in ODOP products (max ₹5L subsidy). Apply via District Industries Centre, Gir Somnath.
🏚
Dattopant Thengadi Artisan Interest Subsidy Scheme (Gujarat)
5% annual interest subsidy on bank loans for ODOP artisans/producers (max ₹10L loan coverage). Apply via state cooperative dept.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Three routes: (1) Ahmedabad APMC retail chain procurement offices (Reliance Fresh, Big Bazaar, Spencers) — walk in with 1 tonne sample, no upfront cost — consignment model. (2) Online platforms: list on JioMart/Grofers — 15-20% commission, but reach 10,000+ customers in 2 weeks. (3) Corporate gifting: email 50 5-star hotels + corporate offices in Ahmedabad with offer "Premium Gir Kesar boxes ₹2,000-2,500 per box" — expect 5-10 bulk orders (50+ boxes each) from hotels in May-June.
Bilkul haan. Kesar mango exports from Gujarat to UAE, Saudi Arabia, Malaysia, Singapore, UK, USA. Process: Get IEC (free, 2 days online via DGFT). Partner with a freight forwarder (Ahmedabad/Surat has 20+ options). Pack fruit in export-grade cartons (₹150-200/carton). Air freight to UAE/UK costs ₹40-60 per kg; sea freight to Singapore/Malaysia costs ₹8-12 per kg. Prices: UAE buyers pay ₹1,400-1,800 FOB per 10 kg box (40-50% margin after freight). Start with 2-5 tonne trial shipments to build buyer relationships.
Biggest risk: Poor harvest (bad weather kills 30-50% yield some years). Exit: Sell your cold storage lease to another trader (breakeven in 6 months). Second risk: Price crash (happened 2023-24 when prices dropped ₹400-600/box year-on-year). Exit: Process fruit into pulp/juice for off-season sale (35-45% margin, less perishable). Third risk: Retail buyer cancels order mid-season. Exit: Pivot to export or online platforms (higher price, lower volume). Keep 10-15% cash reserve for seasonal losses. Most traders break even by month 10-12; full profit by season 2.
