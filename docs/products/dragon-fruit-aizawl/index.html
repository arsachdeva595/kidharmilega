Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Aizawl ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Aizawl Agricultural Department office (near Aizawl Secretariat) to understand local dragon fruit cultivation practices; connect with existing 15-20 farmers in Durtlang and Bungkawn clusters; study market rates at Aizawl's wholesale fruit market (Khaiyaung Market).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 100-200 healthy dragon fruit saplings from National Horticulture Mission nurseries (Mizoram) at ₹50-80 per sapling; procure organic fertilizer, coir, and stakes from local agricultural input stores (first batch: ₹80,000-150,000); negotiate for 0.5-1 acre leased land in outskirts at ₹3,000-6,000/month.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 Aizawl-based organic farming groups (registered with APEDA) for plot management; employ drip irrigation on day 1; engage local agri-engineer for trellis design (dragon fruit requires vertical stakes); expect first fruit harvest in 18-20 months (2,000-3,000 kg per acre annually at maturity).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (e-governance portal, 10 mins online) as small farm enterprise; apply for IEC code from APEDA office in Guwahati (40 km, 4-6 weeks) if exporting; file brand name with IP Cell; get organic certification from certifying bodies (LACON, Naturland) — costs ₹25,000-50,000 for 2 acres.
5
First Sale
Launch on Blinkit, JioMart Fresh for Aizawl/Guwahati delivery within 3-6 months; partner with Nature's Basket, Godrej Nature's Basket for metro metro distribution; contact exporters in Bangalore (Genesis Exports, Globallycs) for Southeast Asia orders; use Instagram/YouTube to showcase farm story and health benefits.
6
Scale
After first 6 months, expand to 2-3 acres; develop value-added line (freeze-dried powder, cold-pressed juice) in partnership with food processing units in Mizoram; establish direct B2B contracts with juice brands and wellness chains; target ₹30-50 Cr revenue by year 3 with 5-6 farm clusters.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM-KISAN
₹6,000/year direct cash transfer to farmer-founders (register via State Agriculture Department)
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for micro-enterprise setup including farm infrastructure, irrigation, processing equipment
🏚
Mizoram Agricultural Loan Subsidy Scheme
25-30% interest subsidy on crop loans up to ₹5L from cooperative/commercial banks
🏚
Mizoram Horticulture Mission
subsidized saplings, training workshops, and drip irrigation equipment support (40% cost sharing) for fruit farmers in Aizawl district
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
