Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mon ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mon district's cardamom farms in Longkhim, Wona, and Aboi villages (Aug-Oct harvest season); connect with Mon District Agriculture Department; study intercropping models at Nagaland State Agricultural University demonstration plots in Kohima
2
Source Raw Materials
Source seeds/saplings from Nagaland State Seed Farm (Kohima) or tribal cooperative gardens in Tuensang; budget ₹50,000-80,000 for 0.5 hectare initial planting (saplings + setup) from local nurseries
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with Mon district tribal farming cooperatives or lease 0.5-1 hectare land in hill zones; employ 2-3 trained harvesters; set up basic drying sheds (₹100,000) using bamboo and thatching; coordinate with Nagaland Cardamom Producers Association for processing guidance
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under 'Spices & Condiments' category; apply for IEC code via DGFT portal; register brand name with IP cell; obtain Spices Board registration (₹5,000); comply with FSSAI for processing unit if adding value-addition
5
First Sale
First sales via local Mon District Agricultural Cooperative Market (Longkhim hub); list on 1mg Ayurveda, Amazon Fresh, Flipkart Supermart; approach regional spice traders in Dimapur and Guwahati; use WhatsApp B2B groups for bulk inquiry outreach
6
Scale
Expand to 3-5 hectares by Month 8-12; invest in electric dryer (₹300,000) for consistent quality; build direct relationships with 5-10 export agents; launch private label cardamom tea and spice blends on D2C channels; establish farm tourism (cardamom estate visits) as revenue diversifier

40% subsidy on improved cardamom cultivation equipment and organic certification
🏚
NAFED Spices Scheme
Interest-free loans up to ₹5L for cardamom farmers and post-harvest infrastructure in identified ODOP districts
