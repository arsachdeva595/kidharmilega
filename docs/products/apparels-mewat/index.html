Mewat has a centuries-old tradition of skilled embroidery artisans who specialize in Phulkari and traditional garment construction. The region's cultural diversity and proximity to Delhi NCR provides access to both heritage craftsmanship and urban markets. Strong community practices of embroidery and tailoring make it naturally suited for scaling apparel production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mewat ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mewat town and Nuh cluster to meet established artisans and embroidery workshop owners. Study Phulkari techniques at local centers and understand production timelines. Connect with existing ODOP vendors at Mewat Handicrafts Market and interview 5-10 established producers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium cotton and silk fabrics from Panipat textile mills (wholesale rates ₹200-400/meter). Procure embroidery thread from Jaipur wholesale markets (₹8000-12000 per kg for quality metallic thread). First batch of 50 pieces requires ₹80,000-120,000. Partner with local dyers in Mewat for customization.
3
Produce / Partner
Register with 3-5 established embroidery artisan groups in Nuh or Mewat town who work on contract basis. Start with 30-50 pieces monthly. Establish quality control checkpoints. Consider small factory setup with 8-10 stitching and embroidery stations costing ₹400,000-600,000, or work with decentralized artisan network.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) within 48 hours online at udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC code at DGFT portal for export readiness. Trademark brand name at IP India. Obtain GST registration and textile sector compliance certificates. Create brand story emphasizing Mewat heritage.
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho and Amazon Handmade with 20-30 SKUs. Create Instagram and YouTube accounts showcasing embroidery process and customer stories. Target wedding season (Oct-Feb) with seasonal collections. Reach out to 50 ethnic wear boutiques in Delhi NCR offering 35% wholesale margin. List on Flipkart Fashion via artisan program.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range to include fusion wear blending traditional and contemporary styles. Launch subscription boxes for seasonal ethnic wear. Expand export to UAE and UK through trade portals. Hire 2-3 experienced quality managers. Increase production to 200-300 pieces monthly. Attend Heimtex and other international textile fairs for B2B connections.

PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 cashless toolkits and ₹3 lakh collateral-free loans for artisans and small manufacturers in textile sector
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (25% of project cost for general category) for establishing manufacturing unit in textile apparel sector
🏚
Haryana Kaushal Vikas Niti
skill development training subsidies for 100 artisans in embroidery with ₹500 per participant monthly stipend during 6-month training
🏚
Haryana ODOP Export Promotion Scheme
50% subsidy on international trade fair participation costs and ₹1 lakh subsidy for obtaining GI tag and quality certifications
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
