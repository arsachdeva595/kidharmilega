opportunity, Khairagarh-Chhuikhadan-Gandai mein.

Khairagarh-Chhuikhadan-Gandai district spans 2,499.6 sq km in Chhattisgarh's agricultural zone with dark soil and monsoon-fed water suitable for soyabean cultivation at scale. The district produces soyabean across 0.270 lakh hectares with expected output of 0.185 lakh tonnes in Kharif 2025, positioning it as a supplier to oil mills in Nagpur, Jabalpur, and Indore (160-280 km away). Access to contract farming networks, seed suppliers in Nagpur, and export traders near Tatanagar/Kolkata creates a complete supply chain from farm to international markets.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Khairagarh-Chhuikhadan-Gandai ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khairagarh agricultural market on a Tuesday or Thursday haat day; call 3 local soyabean farmers and 2 oil mill operators in Nagpur (directory: local agriculture office has supplier list); register for free on Udyam portal; attend a district agriculture department meeting (free, held monthly). Cost: ₹0, time: 1 week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified soyabean seeds from NARP (National Agriculture Research Platform) certified vendors in Nagpur or IIPR Indore. First-batch cost: ₹15,000-25,000 for 1 quintal seeds (covers 5-7 hectares). Supplier area: Nagpur seed market, Jabalpur agricultural inputs zone. Bulk suppliers: Indian Soyabean Research Institute (ISRI) network, local cooperative unions.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Lease 10-15 hectares (₹20,000-35,000/acre/year) and hire local farm labor (₹8,000-12,000/month per worker for 4-5 months). Total cost: ₹2,50,000-4,00,000 for inputs, labor, equipment rental. Option B: Partner with 3-5 contract farmers who own land; provide seeds + subsidy, buy their output at fixed price (reduces capital to ₹80,000-1,50,000). Timeline: 120-135 days crop cycle.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (1-2 days, free). Apply for FSSAI registration if processing soy products (₹5,000-10,000, 15-20 days). IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) for export (₹free from DGFT online, 5-7 days). GI registration not applicable. MSE scheme: apply for Pradhan Mantri Micro Unit Development Refinance Agency (MUDRA) loan up to ₹10L at 6-7% interest. Timeline: 30-45 days total.
5
First Sale
First sale: approach 2-3 local soy oil mills directly with 10-20 quintals stock (ask for ₹4,200-4,500/quintal rates). Register on Furesh (agri-commerce platform) and Udaan (B2B) for processor connections. Expected first-month revenue: ₹1,50,000-2,00,000 (33-40 quintals at ₹4,300/quintal). Minimum viable order: 5 quintals. Payment: 7-15 days credit from mills, immediate cash from retail platforms.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month (250 quintals/month): hire a farm manager (₹18,000/month) and logistics coordinator (₹12,000/month). Expand to 30-40 hectares or manage 15-20 contract farmers. Set up a cleaning/grading unit (₹1,50,000 capex) to move from raw to semi-processed (10-15% value add). Join agri-export trade house association; apply for Agricultural Infrastructure Development Fund (AIDF) subsidy for grain storage. Exhibit at India International Food & Beverage Fair (IIFB), Agri Intex (Bangalore), Soy Symposium (Indore).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Pradhan Mantri Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (PMKSY)
50% subsidy on drip/sprinkler irrigation setup up to ₹80,000 per hectare for soyabean fields
🏛
Pradhan Mantri Fasal Bima Yojana (PMFBY)
crop insurance at ₹450-500/acre covering 75% of average yield; claims paid within 60 days of harvest loss
🏚
Chhattisgarh State Krishi Vikas Patrata Yojana
free soil testing, ₹10,000/hectare subsidy for certified seeds and bio-fertilizers for ODOP crops
🏚
Chhattisgarh Agriculture Byproduct Utilization Scheme
₹5,000-8,000/acre subsidy for setting up farm residue collection, processing, and sale infrastructure
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Soyabean is resilient — even poor yields average 400-500 kg/hectare (vs 685 target). Worst case: 30-40% loss covered by PMFBY insurance. Fallback: sell at government MSP (₹3,700/quintal, updated yearly). Second worst: pivot to selling crop residue as cattle feed (₹3,000-5,000/tonne). Historical data: Chhattisgarh hasn't had total crop failure in soyabean due to diversity of seasons. Exit option: end lease, return land, minimal recurring cost. Risk is real but not ruin-level if you hedge with insurance + diversify 2-3 suppliers of seeds/inputs.
