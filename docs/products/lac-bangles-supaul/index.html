Lac Bangles ki
opportunity, Supaul mein.

Supaul has a centuries-old tradition of lac craftsmanship passed down through generations of artisans. The district's access to quality lac resin, skilled labor, and established bangle-making clusters make it naturally suited for scaling this heritage product. Local artisans possess deep expertise in lacquer molding, stone embedding, and design innovation that cannot be easily replicated elsewhere.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Supaul ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Supaul town and Pipra bangle-making cluster; spend 2-3 days observing artisan workshops. Connect with Supaul Handicrafts Association and DC Handicraft office to understand production methods, material sourcing, and existing market linkages.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source lac resin from Kolkata lac merchants (contact Lac Export Association of India) at ₹400-600/kg; wooden/metal blanks from Moradabad bangle suppliers; stones/mirrors from Jaipur wholesale markets. First batch (5000 bangles) costs ₹80,000-120,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established artisan groups in Supaul town (wages ₹250-350/day per artisan). Establish a small 500 sq ft workspace for design finalization and quality checks. Begin with 500-1000 bangles/month production.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam within 48 hours online (www.udyamregistration.gov.in); apply for IEC code from nearest APEDA office for export eligibility. Trademark brand name with IP office (₹4,500). Create GST registration for B2B compliance.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade and Flipkart Artisan within 2 weeks with 20-30 SKUs (designs). Contact 5-10 ethnic wear brands (Biba, Libas, Global Desi) with wholesale catalogs. Post 3x weekly on Instagram/Pinterest with artisan-process reels emphasizing heritage and craftsmanship.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire a full-time designer to innovate 5 new design collections quarterly. Establish presence at 2-3 major trade fairs (Delhi Craft Mela, India Expo Centre). Negotiate B2B contracts with 5-10 larger retailers. Launch a 'Made in Supaul' narrative on social media and collaborate with micro-influencers (fashion/wedding niches) for 15-20% discounts.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15,000 assistance for tool kits and ₹1.5 Lakh collateral-free credit for registered artisans
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakhs grant (35% subsidy) for new business setup in handicraft sector
🏚
Bihar State Handicrafts Development Policy
marketing support grants and participation subsidies for state-level craft exhibitions
🏚
Mukhyamantri Kanya Utthan Scheme
supports women artisans and entrepreneurs with skill training and working capital loans up to ₹5 Lakhs
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Marketing — Zero Budget Se Shuru Karo
  • Create a 90-second Instagram Reel series 'Bangle Birth Stories' showing 5 artisans crafting one bangle from raw lac to final polish, posted weekly with hashtag #SupaulLacTradition. Target wedding-season audiences via Instagram Ads (₹500/day budget).
  • Partner with 3-5 micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in ethnic fashion and wedding planning niches for product seeding. Offer 20% wholesale discounts in exchange for 6-8 authentic Instagram posts over 2 months.
