Guna district's semi-arid climate, well-drained black and alluvial soil, and December-February harvest season make it ideal for high-quality coriander cultivation. The district produces coriander with superior oleoresin content (3-4%) and aromatic properties valued globally. Local farmer networks and proximity to Indore spice markets provide established supply chain infrastructure.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Guna ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Guna's agricultural research centers and connect with coriander farmer clusters in Kundalpur and Aran villages. Study the Indore spice market (IMCP) operations and attend coriander trader meetings. Spend 2-3 weeks understanding soil testing, seed varieties (RCr-436, Gujrat Coriander-2), and seasonal cycles.
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure certified coriander seeds from IARI New Delhi or local certified suppliers at ₹80-120/kg. Source farmland (2-5 acres minimum) in Guna taluka at ₹3-5 lakh/acre lease. Initial seed + fertilizer cost for 1 acre: ₹15,000-25,000. Build supplier relationships through Guna APMC market (wholesale vegetable market).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing farmer collectives like Guna Spice Farmers Association or register as a producer with local agriculture department for subsidy eligibility. Contract with 10-15 small farmers for aggregation model to reach 5-10 MT production in year 1. Establish basic drying and sorting infrastructure (₹2-3 lakh).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online within 2 days at udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from ICCR Indore (₹500, 5 days). Get FSSAI license for processed products (₹3,000-7,000). Trademark brand at www.ipindia.gov.in. Certifications: Organic (APEDA) + Fair Trade if export-focused.
5
First Sale
Launch on Indiamart, Tradeindia for B2B buyers and Meesho, Amazon Fresh for D2C. Partner with Agrostar app (links directly to traders) and Agribusiness cooperatives in Indore. Attend Guna-MICA (Madhya Pradesh Spice Cluster meetings) quarterly. First order target: 500 kg to local traders at wholesale rate.
6
Scale
After 6 months, add value-added products (powder, paste). Secure APEDA export registration and connect with Indore-based export houses for shipments to 3-4 countries. Build farmer network to 30-50 suppliers. Invest in cold storage (₹8-10 lakh) and process 20-30 MT by year 2. Explore B2B SaaS platforms like NinjaCart for organized retail.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidized loan for agri-processing startups at 5% interest.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grant up to ₹20 lakh for spice clusters and value-addition units.
🏚
Madhya Pradesh State Agriculture Loan Scheme
subsidized ₹10-15 lakh agricultural loans at 2% interest for farmers and agri-entrepreneurs.
🏚
MPSIDC (Madhya Pradesh State Industrial Development Corporation)
export promotion grants and market access support for spice exporters.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
