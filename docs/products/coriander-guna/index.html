Coriander ki
opportunity, Guna mein.

Guna district in Madhya Pradesh sits in India's prime coriander-growing region with 70,000+ hectares under cultivation and soil naturally suited for high-quality seed and herb production. The Kumbhraj and Mawan village spice clusters operate as established export hubs with direct connections to European, UK, Middle Eastern, and Malaysian buyers. Raw material (seeds and fresh plants) is sourced locally at farmgate prices, and the district's position on NH 44 enables rapid logistics to ports and inland markets.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Guna ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3 suppliers in Kumbhraj cluster (contact local haat or KRIBCO cooperative), visit Mawan village spice park and talk to 2 existing coriander processors, attend the weekly haat in Guna town to understand farmgate pricing — all free, doable this week without spending money
2
Source Raw Materials
Source coriander seeds from Kumbhraj farmgate suppliers at ₹80-120/kg (first 100kg batch = ₹8,000-12,000). Fresh coriander sourced from contract farmers in Mawan and surrounding villages at ₹15-25/kg. Build a list of 4-5 seed suppliers and 2-3 fresh herb farmers; negotiate terms for 50-100kg weekly supplies.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (low-cost): Partner with an existing processor in Mawan spice park for toll processing (you supply seeds, they process and pack) at ₹8-15/kg. Option B (owned unit): Rent a 500-800 sqft processing unit in Guna or Kumbhraj for ₹5,000-8,000/month and buy a basic seed dryer (₹60,000-80,000) and grinder (₹40,000-60,000). Start with toll processing (₹0 machinery), scale to owned unit at ₹1,50,000-2,50,000 after 3-4 months of revenue.
4
Brand & Register
Register business under Udyam portal (free, 5 min online). Obtain FSSAI License (₹5,000-10,000, 2-3 weeks). Apply for IEC code from Directorate General of Foreign Trade for export (₹0, online, 1 week). File for GI tag application under Kumbhraj coriander (govt authorities in process — being first mover gives competitive edge, ₹10,000-20,000 in legal costs). Get ISO 22000 certification if targeting European buyers (₹25,000-40,000, 2-3 months).
5
First Sale
First sales: List on Amazon Pantry and Blinkit in Guna-MP region and adjacent states (free listing, start with 5kg packs at ₹150-180 per 100g). Simultaneously contact 2-3 wholesale spice traders in Guna district with a ₹5,000 sample pack. Target first month: 50-80 online units (₹7,500-14,400) + 1 wholesale order of 10kg (₹1,000-1,500) = realistic ₹8,500-15,900 first-month revenue.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (₹120L annualized), hire 1 full-time quality controller (₹12,000/month), 1 export operations person (₹15,000/month), and 2 part-time packers (₹8,000/month each). Invest ₹50,000 in a coriander essential oil extraction unit (demand in EU is high). Begin attending food trade expos in Delhi and Mumbai (₹30,000-50,000 per expo). Partner with 2-3 export trading houses in Mumbai or Delhi to access larger international shipments.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy on fixed assets up to ₹10 lakh for manufacturing units, maximum subsidy ₹2.5 lakh
🏛
MUDRA Loan
up to ₹10 lakh unsecured working capital loan at ~8-10% interest for spice processing businesses
🏚
Madhya Pradesh One District One Product Scheme
subsidized infrastructure access, training, and export promotion support for ODOP clusters (Kumbhraj and Mawan qualify)
🏚
Madhya Pradesh SFURTI Scheme
support to traditional industries, including coriander processing, with capital subsidy up to ₹5 lakh and marketing support
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
