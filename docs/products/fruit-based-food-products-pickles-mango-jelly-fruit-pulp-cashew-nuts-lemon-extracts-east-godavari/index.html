Fruit Based Food Products (Pickles, Mango Jelly, Fruit Pulp, Cashew nuts, Lemon extracts) ki
opportunity, East Godavari mein.

East Godavari's fertile Godavari delta soil and tropical climate create ideal conditions for growing mangoes, lemons, and cashew nuts year-round. The district has established food processing clusters in Kakinada and surrounding areas with access to skilled labor, modern facilities, and strong backward linkage to agriculture. Government support through SFURTI clusters and existing infrastructure makes scaling fruit-based products efficient.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — East Godavari ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kakinada Food Processing Cluster and Samalkot area to meet existing producers; attend meetings at East Godavari Chamber of Commerce; study competitor brands like Kandhari, Saptagyani; speak with 5-10 existing food processors in the district about production costs and buyer networks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh mangoes from Razole, Peddapalli villages (₹12-18/kg for processing grade); lemons from Mummidivaram region (₹8-12/kg); cashew nuts from local traders in Kakinada; first 500kg batch raw material cost approximately ₹80,000-120,000; negotiate farmer contracts for seasonal supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing SFURTI-supported food processing units in Kakinada or Samalkot for initial 2-3 batches (cost ₹25,000-40,000 per 500kg batch) while setting up your own FSSAI-certified kitchen (6-12 months); alternatively, hire food technology graduate to oversee production; minimum facility: 500 sq ft rented space with gas stove, mixing vessels, bottling station.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (online, free, 10 mins); apply for FSSAI License (₹6,000-12,000, 2-4 weeks); get IEC code from DGFT for exports; register trademark with IP India (₹4,500-9,000); design packaging with local designer (₹15,000-30,000 for initial design); get barcode from GS1 India (₹500-2,000 per barcode).
5
First Sale
Launch on Flipkart and Amazon (25-30% commission) with 10 SKUs and target 500 sales in first month; simultaneously approach modern trade buyers (Big Basket, Blinkit, local chains) with samples and bulk pricing; create Instagram and YouTube accounts showcasing production process; offer ₹300-500 per unit wholesale to 20-30 local retail shops in Visakhapatnam and Vijayawada; target 2,000 units monthly in first 6 months.
6
Scale
After 6 months, if doing ₹15-20L monthly: invest ₹50-75L in larger facility and automation equipment; hire 3-5 production staff; expand to 8-10 states via direct distribution; develop 5-10 new product SKUs (fruit spreads, RTS drinks); approach NRI networks through cultural organizations for export orders; negotiate with regional FMCG distributers for wider market reach.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general category) for food processing micro-enterprises with turnover target.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹5L per unit for common facilities and technology upgradation in food processing clusters in East Godavari.
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Food Processing Policy 2021
₹5-10L subsidy for food processing units plus tax incentives for 5 years; applicable for FSSAI-licensed producers.
🏚
APIIC (Andhra Pradesh Industrial Infrastructure Corporation)
discounted rent (50% subsidy) for 3 years on industrial plots in designated food processing parks near Kakinada.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
