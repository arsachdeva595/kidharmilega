Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — East Godavari ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kakinada mango market (Kothapeta wholesale market) on a Monday morning. Call 3 existing pickle/jelly makers in Amalapuram asking their first-month costs and supplier names. Register free on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in). Cost: ₹0, time: 1 week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mangoes and lemons directly from Kakinada wholesale market (Kothapeta) during season (April-July for mangoes, Nov-Feb for lemons). First batch cost for ₹200L setup: ₹40-50K for 2-3 tonnes fruit, ₹15K for packaging materials, ₹8K for spices and additives. Supplier area: Kakinada Dakshin Tehsil.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 800-1200 sqft space in Amalapuram food processing cluster (₹8-12K/month rent). Partner with local artisans/retired food technicians (₹18-22K/month salary) for first 6 months, OR attend SFURTI training at DRDA office for ₹0 cost. Autoclave sterilizer (₹80-120K), stainless steel vessels (₹25K), bottles and caps (sourced monthly). Total 6-month cost: ₹2.2-2.5L including rent, labor, utilities, licenses.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 2 days). Apply for FSSAI food license (₹12K, 15-20 days at Kakinada district office). If exporting: apply for IEC code at DGFT online (free, 3-5 days). File GI application if creating region-specific product (₹10K, 18-24 months, non-mandatory). Total cost: ₹12K + ₹0 if no GI.
5
First Sale
First sale: Upload 3 SKUs (mango pickle, lemon pickle, mango jelly) on Amazon Accelerator or Flipkart seller portal. Min order batch: 100 units per SKU. Expected first order size: 200-400 units via D2C. First-month revenue: ₹35-55K if 300 units sell at ₹120-180 per unit (retail price ₹250-350).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: expand to 2000 sqft unit, hire quality checker and packaging lead (₹35K combined), stock 8-10 tonnes fruit per month. Exhibit at North India Food Fair (Jan, Delhi), Tamil Nadu Food Expo (Aug), UAE Trade shows. Reach regional distribution via meetings at Kolkata Food Park, Chennai Food Export Zone.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy on setup cost for general category (max ₹10L subsidy), 35% for SC/ST/women. Apply at DRDA office with ITI certificate or graduation proof.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹40L cluster-level grant if you form group of 20+ artisans; individual max ₹5L. Food Processing Ministry administers. Apply with 2-3 other local producers together.
🏚
Andhra Pradesh MSME Cluster Development Scheme
50% subsidy on machinery up to ₹20L for food processing units, interest-free SIDBI loans available. Apply at District Industries Centre, Kakinada.
🏚
AP Export Promotion Policy
5% subsidy on export freight, APEDA certification support, trade fair participation at 80% cost coverage. Apply at AP Food Tech cluster office, Visakhapatnam.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Helpful but not mandatory. If you have food service, restaurant, or kitchen background, first 4 months easier. If not, join SFURTI training (free 2-week course at DRDA office) or hire a retired food technician for ₹18-20K/month for first 6 months. FSSAI Food Safety course (₹2K, 5 days online) is compulsory.
PMEGP gives 25-35% subsidy on equipment (max ₹10L), SFURTI gives ₹5L for individual or ₹40L for group of 20+. AP state gives 50% subsidy on machinery (₹20L cap), export subsidy (5% freight cost). Total: you can offset ₹15-20L of your ₹25L max spend with govt money. Apply at District Industries Centre, Kakinada.
First orders come from 3 places: (1) Upload on Amazon Accelerator / Flipkart seller portal within 1 month — expect 20-50 orders by month 2. (2) Approach 5-10 local hotels, catering businesses, schools in Kakinada and Visakhapatnam directly with samples — bulk ₹5-10K orders possible. (3) Regional supermarket chains (More Retail, reliance) via wholesale distributor meetings — takes 2-3 months but ₹50K first order realistic.
Bilkul. Pickles and jelly highly demanded in UAE, Singapore, Malaysia, UK. You need FSSAI (mandatory), IEC code (free, 3 days online), APEDA registration (free). Freight cost ₹25-35 per kg to UAE by sea. Retail price UAE is 3-4x higher than India. Margin 45-55% FOB possible. Shipping takes 7-10 days to UAE port.
Real risk: seasonal fruit price spike (mangoes ₹40/kg in March, ₹80/kg in June). Solution: sign contract with suppliers in Jan-Feb for April-July. Second risk: compet ition from big brands. Mitigation: focus niche (organic, regional, specific flavor) and sell direct (D2C gives 60% margin vs 28% wholesale). Exit option: sell equipment and lease to another processor (equipment resale value 60-70% of cost in 2 years). If truly stuck after 18 months with ₹0 revenue, exit cost is ₹2-3L loss on ₹5L initial investment.
