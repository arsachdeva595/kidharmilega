Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Niuland ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established banana farms in Dimapur, Kohima and nearby valleys. Connect with Nagaland Agriculture Department, visit local agricultural extension centers, study farming practices at farmer clusters in Chumoukedima. Attend Nagaland Organic Farmers Association meetings.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source quality banana saplings (Cavendish, Poovan, Cultivated local varieties) from Nagaland Agricultural University farms or reputable nurseries in Dimapur. First-batch setup for 1 acre: ₹80,000-120,000 (saplings, manure, labor for land prep). Bulk soil testing through state agriculture office costs ₹5,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with experienced Naga farmers in Niuland valleys for co-farming or lease farmland (₹15,000-25,000 per acre annually). Establish organic certification through NPOP-registered agency (₹30,000-50,000 for 2-3 years). Start with 2-3 acres, scale after first harvest cycle (9-12 months).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under Farming/Agriculture category online. Apply for IEC code from nearest APEDA office in Kohima. Register trademark with Nagaland Organic Farmers Association logo. Get Organic certification (NPOP, USDA, or EU) for ₹40,000-60,000. Create GST registration for value-added products.
5
First Sale
Launch on JioMart, BigBasket organic sections targeting Northeast delivery zones. Create Instagram/Facebook pages showcasing farm-to-table journey, post weekly harvest updates. Contact Nagaland cooperatives (like NAFED branches) for bulk orders. Approach premium restaurants in Kohima, Dimapur (Vivanta, Summit hotels) with sample supplies. List on Local Organic portals and Naga cultural food e-commerce platforms.
6
Scale
After 6 months of consistent sales, expand to 5-10 acres, hire farm manager. Develop value-added line (banana chips, dried bananas) for higher margins. Establish cold chain logistics via Northeast Cold Chain Network. Explore Southeast Asian export via APEDA-aided export programs. Create farm experience packages for agri-tourism revenue. Build B2B contracts with 5+ premium restaurant chains across Northeast.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan subsidy for agri-business with 25-35% subsidy for general category, 5-year repayment
🏛
MUDRA Scheme
Kissan Credit Line up to ₹1.6L for farmers, zero collateral, 4% interest subsidy for first year
🏚
Nagaland State Horticulture Mission
50% subsidy on improved banana saplings and drip irrigation setup, up to ₹5L per hectare
🏚
Nagaland Organic Farming Scheme
₹10,000-20,000 per hectare subsidy for organic certification and compost units, applicable for 3-5 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Marketing

Ye marketing ideas try karna mat bhulna

  • Partner with micro-influencers in organic/wellness space (Instagram: health food accounts, 50K-200K followers) for farm visit content and product unboxing. Target health-conscious urban segments with stories of traditional farming. Cost: ₹10,000-30,000 per influencer per campaign.
  • Create WhatsApp Community groups for regular customers in Dimapur, Kohima, Guwahati with weekly harvest updates, farming tips, exclusive pre-orders. Use WhatsApp Business API for personalized outreach. Drive referral incentives (₹100 discount on 5th purchase).
  • Run Facebook/Instagram ads targeting premium fruit buyers in tier-2 Northeast cities (Assam, Manipur, Tripura) with farm footage showing heritage farming methods. Use Carousel ads showing before/after farm images. Daily budget ₹500-1,500, target 25-40 age group, income ₹10L+.
