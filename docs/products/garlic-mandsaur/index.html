opportunity, Mandsaur mein.

Mandsaur district sits in central Madhya Pradesh (24°N, 75°E) on black soil terrain ideal for garlic bulb development and long storage. The Mandsaur garlic cluster spans 80,000+ hectares across Mandsaur, Neemuch, and Ratlam taluks with 600+ registered procurement centers and 45+ cold-storage facilities (combined 240,000 MT capacity). Raw material is available at farm-gate year-round; export-ready garlic flows through Indore (85 km), Delhi (475 km), and Nhava Sheva port (520 km); institutional buyers include Haldiram's, Mother Dairy, and direct importers in UAE/Bangladesh.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mandsaur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3 procurement centers in Mandsaur town (list: Mandsaur Mandi, Neemuch APMC, RR Traders office). Visit Mandsaur Mandi on a Tuesday or Thursday (peak trading days) — no fees, free to observe sorting, grading, pricing. Register free on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in) to reserve business name. Ask mandi brokers for cold-storage rental rates and export logistics contacts — complete this in 4 days, ₹0 cost.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source from registered Mandsaur Mandi suppliers (400+ sellers available daily, Sept-May season). First batch: 5 MT (₹75,000-90,000 per MT at farm-gate, total ₹3.75-4.5L). Negotiate for G-2 or Riyavan silver variety. Use APMC broker (1-2% commission) to arrange transport to your rented cold storage (₹2,000-3,000 per MT for 50 km delivery). Supplier area: Mandsaur town, Neemuch, Ratlam.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 20-30 MT capacity cold-storage unit in Mandsaur town or Neemuch (₹4,000-7,000/month at 2-4°C, 65-70% RH). Partner with local sorting-grading artisans (2-3 workers at ₹500-800/day per worker) or invest ₹1.5-2L in simple grading machinery (mesh screens, weighing scales, packaging). First 5 MT batch processing cost: ₹50,000-75,000 (labor + utilities). Alternative: skip your own unit and partner with existing sorter-cum-exporter (they handle storage, you pay ₹5-8/kg processing fee).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 15 min online). Apply for One District One Product scheme (ODOP) subsidy via Madhya Pradesh MSME dept (₹3-5L grant, 6-8 week approval). Obtain FSSAI for value-added products (₹7,000, 7 days). For export: apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at DGFT office Indore (₹500, 3 days). File garlic GI claim if targeting premium segment (₹20,000 legal fees, 6 months processing). Total regulatory cost: ₹27,500-50,000 excluding subsidy.
5
First Sale
First sales: List on Amazon/Flipkart (₹10-15K setup fees, 1-2 weeks approval). Target first order via mandi broker network (5-10 MT to North India restaurants/wholesalers, ₹15-20L revenue at ₹350/kg). Parallel: pitch 1-2 MT samples to Haldiram's, ITC Agrotech, food exporters in Indore. Expected first month revenue: ₹18-25L gross (5-7 MT sales at ₹350-500/kg blended).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month ARR (~28 MT sold monthly): hire 1 dedicated sales manager (₹25-35K/month) to manage Flipkart/Amazon accounts and restaurant chain contracts. Invest ₹3-5L in secondary packaging (branded boxes, labels) and grow D2C share to 30%. Add 1-2 processors (₹400-600/day wage each) to produce garlic powder, paste, pickle (₹200-300/kg, 35-50% margins). Exhibit at India Intl Food Fair (IIFF, Delhi), Asian Agri-Food Expo (Bangkok) to access bulk export contracts. By month 14-16, target ₹40-50L/month with 3-4 employees.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM MUDRA Loan
up to ₹10L at 8-9% interest for cold-storage equipment and initial working capital, no collateral for first ₹5L.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹10L subsidy (25% of project cost) for registered units, available to first-time entrepreneurs with ₹2.5-5L investment.
🏚
Madhya Pradesh One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
₹3-5L non-repayable grant for Mandsaur garlic exporters, 6-8 week approval.
🏚
MP Agriculture Infrastructure Development Fund (AIDF)
₹1-2L subsidy for cold-storage construction/rental, apply via RAEO office.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

₹2-8 lakh: ₹1.5-4L for first 5 MT garlic stock, ₹30-40K/month cold-storage rent, ₹50-75K grading labor. With govt schemes (MUDRA ₹10L, ODOP ₹3-5L), you can start with ₹2L personal capital and borrow rest.
No formal experience needed, but 6-month apprenticeship in mandi (free, just visit weekly) is critical. You must understand grading standards, cold-chain handling, buyer negotiation, and export docs. RR Traders founder likely came from farming/trading background. Start by working 2-3 months as volunteer in a sorter's unit.
ODOP scheme: ₹3-5L grant (6-8 weeks, apply via MP MSME dept). PM MUDRA: ₹10L loan at 8-9%. PMEGP: ₹10L subsidy for 25% of ₹40L project. MP AIDF: ₹1-2L for cold-storage. Total available: ₹24-27L in grants + loans. Register on udyamregistration.gov.in first (free).
Mandi brokers (Mandsaur APMC): give them ₹500-1,000 and they'll connect you to 20+ bulk buyers. Restaurants in Indore, Delhi, Mumbai respond fast to ₹5-10/kg discounts on 10+ MT orders. Flipkart/Amazon: list in 2 weeks, first order usually 100-500 kg. GCC buyers: contact via IEC + emails to importers, expect 3-4 month first shipment lead time.
Haan. RR Traders exports 60-100 MT/month to UAE, Bangladesh, Middle East. Process: Get IEC (3 days, ₹500), FSSAI (if value-added, ₹7K), pack to importer specs. FOB price ₹200-280/kg → importer margin ₹45-60/kg. Shipping Nhava Sheva to Dubai costs ₹15-20/kg, takes 21 days. Bangladesh/Sri Lanka via Dhaka port, cheaper. First export order typically 10-15 MT minimum.
Garlic is commoditized; if your brand fails, you can always sell bulk to established exporters (RR Traders type) at ₹250-300/kg, break even in 6 months. Worst case: rent out cold-storage to other traders (₹40-50K/month income on ₹3.5L investment). Exit: sell cold-storage unit for ₹85-95% of cost (resale market strong). Main risk: price crash (happened 2019-20), so hedge with futures contracts. Volatility is real; margin of safety is 6 months runway minimum.
