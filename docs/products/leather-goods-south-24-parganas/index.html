The district has 500+ active handloom artisans producing tant sarees, kantha work, and designer sarees, with annual saree output estimated at 9600-12000 units. Proximity to Kolkata's retail ecosystem (New Market, Gariahat) and direct highway access to Delhi-Mumbai buyers makes this a cost-efficient base for both domestic and export businesses.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — South 24 Parganas ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Barasat saree market cluster (Old Bus Stand area) this week and spend 2 hours talking to 5-6 existing weavers or saree producers. No cost. Call Barasat Chamber of Commerce (0333-2564xxx) and ask for a list of active artisans. Attend the monthly Barasat Haat (community trade meet) held on the 2nd Saturday — free entry.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Dankuni silk yarn market (40km north, established supplier belt). First batch: 100 sarees of 1-2 designs = ₹80000-120000 in yarn + ₹15000 dyes/finishing. Partner with 2-3 artisans in Barasat on piece-rate basis (₹400-600 per saree for weaving labor) rather than buying finished stock.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 400-600 sqft unit in Barasat's textile cluster at ₹5000-8000/month for storage, branding, QC, and packaging. Alternatively, work directly with 5-10 home-based artisans on commission. Initial 3-month unit lease + equipment (packaging, labeling, steaming) = ₹35000-50000. Do NOT build a loom — work with existing artisans.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online in 20 minutes). Apply for Handloom Mark certificate via DC Handicraft office Kolkata (₹500-1500, 30 days processing). If exporting: get IEC code from Kolkata DGFT office (₹500, 5 days). GI registration for South 24 Parganas sarees is not active — skip for now.
5
First Sale
List 30 sarees on Meesho (commission 20-25%, first order expected week 3-4). Target ₹25000-35000 in first month (8-12 units at ₹3000-3500 wholesale to Meesho). Simultaneously, reach out to 20 regional boutiques in Kolkata via WhatsApp with product photos + wholesale pricing. First institutional order likely ₹40000-60000 (15-20 sarees).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 dedicated sales/marketing person (₹15000/month), scale artisan partnerships to 15-20 weavers, add 2-3 new saree designs monthly, launch Shopify D2C store (₹3000/month). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (30-35% subsidy on capex for expansion). Exhibit at Indian Handicrafts & Fashion Fair in Delhi (annual, ₹30000 stall cost). Build email list and run Instagram Reels showing artisan process.

Barasat Textile Cluster
Informal MSME cluster with 40+ registered saree production units, 100+ home-based artisans. Units range 200-800 sqft, rental ₹4000-10000/month. Shared facilities: dyeing vats, steaming units, packaging materials nearby.

Dankuni Industrial Estate
Formal industrial park 40km from Barasat hosting 80+ units including yarn suppliers, chemical dye manufacturers, fabric finishers. Plots 1000-5000 sqft available at ₹200-300/sqft. Direct raw material sourcing advantage.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
35% subsidy on capex (max ₹35 lakh for proprietorship) for women founders; up to ₹25 lakh capex eligible.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15000 upfront credit per artisan enrolled, ₹500 skill development grant, at 0% interest for 18 months if you partner with registered weavers.
🏚
West Bengal MSME Assistance Scheme
5% rebate on loan amount for units in textile/handicraft sector; available via all scheduled banks.
🏚
West Bengal Handicraft Development Board Fund
₹5-10 lakh concessional loan at 6% interest for registered handloom producers; apply via DC Handicraft office.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

PMEGP gives 35% subsidy on ₹25 lakh capex (up to ₹8.75 lakh back). PM Vishwakarma gives ₹15000 per artisan enrolled at 0% interest 18 months. WB Handicraft Board: ₹5-10 lakh loan at 6% interest. Bilkul haan. Get IEC code (₹500, 5 days from Kolkata DGFT). Export to USA, UK, Canada, Australia on Alibaba, TradeKey at ₹2800-4200 FOB per saree. Minimum order 50 units. Honest downside: handloom demand is seasonal (peaks Oct-Dec, Jun-Jul weddings). If sales don't happen in first 4 months, inventory sits. Exit option: sell remaining stock at 15-20% discount to bulk traders in Kolkata or liquidate at wholesale auctions. Worst case, pivot from sarees to scarves/dupattas (same artisans, lower inventory risk). Learn from Anamika BBO — they started slow but built to ₹10L annual by focusing on bulk B2B.
