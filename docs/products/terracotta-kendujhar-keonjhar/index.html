Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kendujhar (Keonjhar) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kendujhar's terracotta clusters in Dangaribara and surrounding villages; spend 2-3 days observing artisan families; connect with district handicraft association; study products at Kendujhar Craft Bazaar and Bhubaneswar State Museum handicraft section.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source clay directly from local quarries near Dangaribara (₹500-800/ton); establish relationship with 2-3 clay suppliers; procure firewood from forest departments; initial batch of 500kg clay costs ₹1,000-2,000; negotiate bulk rates for 5-10 tons monthly.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisan groups in Kendujhar district (contact District Handicraft Officer); outsource initial production to 3-5 skilled potters on piece-rate (₹50-150 per item); gradually hire in-house team; consider kiln rental initially (₹5,000-15,000/batch) before owning one.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (online, free, 10 mins); apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export capability; register business name with district administration; create GI-aspiration narrative on packaging; get ISO 9001 certification within 12 months for premium positioning.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (12% fee, global audience) and Amazon Handmade simultaneously; list on Flipkart Handloom; approach gift companies (FabBag, Cradle, Artisans' Village); pitch to 20+ interior designers on Instagram with DM outreach; participate in India Craft Week Delhi.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 15-20 craftspeople by month 9; launch branded retail experience at Bhubaneswar's heritage district; establish export logistics via ECGC; launch Instagram influencer collaborations with 5-10K followers in home decor niche; apply for SFURTI cluster certification to access government credit.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15,000 annual stipend + ₹1L soft loan for traditional artisans in terracotta
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy + ₹25L bank loan for new handicraft units
🏚
Odisha Handicraft Development Scheme
20-30% subsidy on kiln setup, tools, and working capital for MSME artisans
🏚
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster support including common facilities, training, and ₹5-10L for artisan groups in Kendujhar
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
