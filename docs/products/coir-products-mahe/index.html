Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mahe ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mahe SFURTI cluster office and engage with 15-20 existing coir manufacturers; study product range at Mahe Central Cooperative Society; analyze export-import data from APEDA database for global demand trends.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source dried coconut husks locally from Mahe plantations at ₹8-12 per kg; order processing machinery from Kochi suppliers (₹3-5L for basic setup); first batch of 500 kg husks costs ₹5,000-6,000; connect with SFURTI cluster suppliers for bulk discounts.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-10 trained artisans in Mahe through local NGOs or SFURTI network; establish small workshop space (200-400 sqft) at ₹5,000-8,000/month; implement quality control systems aligned with ISO 9001 standards for export readiness.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) within 2 weeks online; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Puducherry; register brand name with IP India; obtain handicraft certification from DC Handicraft Ministry for government procurement preference.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade and Flipkart Artisan with 8-10 bestselling products; contact 20-30 eco-friendly retail chains (Urban Ladder, Godrej Interio) with sample packs; create Instagram/Pinterest content targeting global audience; reach out to boutique export houses in Mumbai and Chennai.
6
Scale
After ₹15L revenue, expand product line to include coir composites and specialized industrial applications; hire full-time quality manager; target B2B contracts with government departments; establish distribution partnerships in 5-10 major Indian metros; apply for SFURTI cluster development funds for technology upgrades.

up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for small coir manufacturing units with 15-25% margin requirement

SFURTI
cluster development grants for Mahe coir cluster covering training, marketing, and quality infrastructure
🏚
Puducherry Industries Promotion Policy
machinery subsidy of 25-30% for handicraft-based MSMEs engaged in export production
🏚
Puducherry Artisan Welfare Scheme
direct support to artisans including skill upgrades and social security benefits
