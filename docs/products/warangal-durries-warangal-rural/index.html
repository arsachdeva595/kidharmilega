Warangal Durries ki
opportunity, Warangal Rural mein.

Warangal Rural sits in central Telangana with direct cotton supply from surrounding agricultural zones and jute/silk availability via regional suppliers within 80km radius. The durrie-weaving cluster spans 3-4 towns (Warangal city, Jangaon, Mulug) with 800+ active weavers producing reversible geometric-pattern durries. Amazon, Flipkart, and direct export buyers in USA/UK source from this cluster, with established shipping routes via Hyderabad and Vishakhapatnam ports just 350-400km away.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Warangal Rural ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Warangal city durrie market (near Warangal Fort area) or Jangaon weaving cluster on a Wednesday; call 2-3 established weavers (ask local fabric shop for contacts — zero cost); attend one bi-monthly ODOP artisan meet (contact DC Handicraft office for dates). Talk to 5 weavers about costs, turnaround time, and current buyer demand. This week, free.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source first batch from Jangaon weaving area (15km from Warangal city) or directly from weavers in Warangal cluster — negotiate ₹8K-14K per 12x12 cotton durrie for first 10-20 pieces; ask for 30-day production timeline and payment terms (50% advance, 50% on delivery). Total first batch: ₹1.2L-2.4L for 15-20 pieces to test market.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with artisan partnership model (no factory setup needed initially) — identify 3-4 weavers willing to produce on order; provide cotton + design specs; pay per piece on delivery. Rent a 300-400 sq ft space in Warangal city (₹4K-8K/month) for photography, quality check, storage, packing. Production timeline: 25-35 days per piece. Cost to start: ₹40K-60K/month (rent + 1 helper + packing materials).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 10 mins online); apply for GI certification support via DC Handicraft Telangana (free, 2-3 month process, already done for product category); get IEC code from DGFT if exporting (₹0, online, 2 weeks); create GST registration (₹500-1500, 5 days). Total compliance cost: ₹2K-3K. Timeline: 8-12 weeks to full readiness.
5
First Sale
First sale: Register on Amazon Seller Central (₹12-15 per transaction %) and Flipkart Seller (10% commission). Create 5-8 high-quality photos (₹2K-4K professional shoot, or DIY with phone). List 3-4 designs at ₹18K-25K per 12x12 durrie. Expected first month: 3-5 orders if marketing is active = ₹54K-125K gross revenue. Use first profits to fund next batch.
6
Scale
At ₹10L+/month revenue: hire a full-time operations person to manage weaver network (₹8K-12K/month); open a 600-800 sq ft retail showroom in Warangal city (₹800-1.2L one-time setup); apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹25L loan at 5% interest, 35% subsidy for non-EWS) to expand to 8-10 committed weavers; exhibit at Delhi Home Decor Expo (Jan-Feb) and Bangalore Design Week to reach B2B buyers; launch corporate gifting vertical targeting HQ offices in Hyderabad (30km away).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Prime Minister Employment Generation Scheme (PMEGP)
₹25 lakh credit at 5% interest, 35% subsidy (non-EWS), 25% (EWS/SC/ST), for working capital and machinery
🏛
Pradhan Mantri Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 cash transfer + ₹2.5L interest-free loan for artisans, toolkit support, social security coverage
🏚
Telangana One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
₹10-15L credit guarantee for durrie entrepreneurs, brand support, export facilitation, zero interest for 2 years
🏚
Telangana Startup Support Scheme
₹10L grant (non-dilutive) for GI-tagged product businesses with clear export/innovation angle
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
