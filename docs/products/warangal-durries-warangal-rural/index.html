opportunity, Warangal Rural mein.

Warangal Rural has a centuries-old tradition of durry weaving with established artisan clusters in Warangal and Jangaon. The district's abundant cotton supply, skilled weaver communities, and cultural legacy make it the perfect hub for authentic handwoven durries. The GI certification further validates the region's expertise and authenticity.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Warangal Rural ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Warangal weaver clusters in Warangal town and Jangaon; connect with artisans at Warangal Handicrafts Cluster (DC Handicraft office); attend workshops at NIFT Hyderabad on traditional weaving; study GI certification process documentation.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton yarn from Warangal's established suppliers (approx ₹8-12/meter for quality yarn) and natural dyes from local markets; budget ₹25,000-40,000 for first 50-100 meter production batch; negotiate direct relationships with 2-3 artisan cooperatives.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established weaver groups in Warangal Rural through DC Handicraft's artisan network; start with 3-5 artisans for capacity of 10-15 durries/month; establish quality control checkpoints; document production for GI compliance.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online within 10 days; obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Hyderabad; register brand name with IP office; apply GI authentication certificate from DC Handicraft; display GI logo on all packaging.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart Marketplace with HD photography; target interior design influencers on Instagram for collaborations; pitch to boutique home decor stores in Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi NCR; contact luxury hotel procurement teams directly with samples.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 15-20 weavers; introduce 3-4 design variations quarterly; establish showroom in Hyderabad's high-end area; launch B2B export pipeline through trade fairs (Heimtextil Frankfurt, etc.); build corporate gifting partnerships with IT/consulting firms.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
Free training, ₹15,000 toolkit, ₹125Cr credit guarantee for traditional artisans and micro-enterprises
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakh subsidy (20-35% of project cost) for manufacturing startups
🏚
Telangana Handloom Weaver Comprehensive Welfare Scheme
direct support to weavers, raw material subsidies, and export promotion assistance
🏚
Telangana SFURTI Scheme
cluster-based artisan support including common facility centres, design intervention, and market linkage for traditional crafts
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
