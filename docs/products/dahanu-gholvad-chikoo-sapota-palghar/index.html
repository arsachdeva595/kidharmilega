Dahanu Gholvad Chikoo (Sapota) ki
opportunity, Palghar mein.

Palghar district sits 90 km north of Mumbai on the Arabian Sea coast, with Dahanu and Gholvad taluks concentrating 500+ tonnes annual sapota harvest on Rayan rootstock unavailable anywhere else in India. Raw material — grafted saplings and rootstock — is sourced locally from Save Farm (50-year-old orchard) and licensed nurseries in Gholvad. Buyer proximity spans Mumbai's 70 km distance (premium retail, juice chains, exporters) and direct port access to Middle East/Southeast Asia via JNPT and Kandla ports.

✓ Verified Success Story
Sir Dinshaw Petit (1888) and Merwan Khodadad (1940s)

Sir Dinshaw Petit imported sapota saplings from South America to Palghar in 1888, establishing commercial cultivation. Merwan Khodadad grafted chikoo onto Rayan rootstock in the 1940s after storm devastation, creating the hardy Gholvad variety that dominates today. This deliberate breeding created the premium 2,000-fruit-per-tree genetics still used.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Palghar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dahanu or Gholvad mandi on a Tuesday or Friday morning (free). Call Save Farm orchard (contact via palghar.gov.in ODOP page). Attend Palghar District Agriculture Office monthly meeting or local NAFED haat to speak to 5-10 existing sapota traders. Spend ₹0, take 2-3 days.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact licensed nurseries in Gholvad (via ATMA office) for Kalipatti saplings at ₹40-60 per sapling. Fresh fruit sourcing: negotiate direct with 3-5 farmers in Dahanu cluster for ₹15-20/kg during July-September harvest. First batch trial: ₹20,000-30,000 gets 1-1.5 tonnes fresh sapota for testing markets.
3
Produce / Partner
For fresh fruit: zero manufacturing needed initially — buy, sort, package in food-grade cartons. For processed (chips/powder): rent 500 sqft space in Dahanu MSME cluster at ₹5,000-8,000/month with basic dryer + grinder setup (₹1.5-2.5L capital). Partner with a local SHG or farmer cooperative for first 6 months before going solo. Total setup: ₹2-4L including licensing and packaging equipment.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 15 mins). File GI certification claim at Intellectual Property Office Delhi mentioning Gholvad Sapota GI tag (₹5,000-10,000 filing, 4-6 month approval). Get Agmark certification for processed goods from NRCAP Nashik (₹8,000-15,000). If exporting: apply for IEC from DGFT Delhi online (₹500 + 2 weeks processing). Total time: 2-3 months for paperwork.
5
First Sale
First sale: List on BigBasket Organic or JioMart Fresh (₹5,000 onboarding, 3-4 week approval). Upload product with ₹400-500/kg MRP. First order: aim for ₹10,000-15,000 minimum bulk order. Month 1 realistic revenue: ₹30,000-60,000 if moving 2-3 bulk orders to 2-3 platforms. Parallel: email 10 juice brands (Nattu Naturals, etc.) with sample batch for B2B negotiations.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire 1 supply chain coordinator (₹25,000/month) to manage farmer relationships across 8-10 orchards. Invest ₹3-5L in cold storage unit (1-ton capacity) in Dahanu for off-season stock (chikoo stores 4-5 days fresh, needs managed logistics). Exhibit at ProPak India (Mumbai), India Food Forum (Delhi), Fruit Logistica Asia (Singapore). Formalize contracts with 2-3 anchor buyers (JNPT exporters, Metro chains). Launch value-added line: chikoo wine, chips under co-packing with Palghar food labs.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan with 25% subsidy for food processing, 10% for services; ₹10,000 application fee, 45-60 day approval via KVIC
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹3 lakh collateral-free loan at 0% interest for first 2 years if you hire 1+ worker; direct disbursement in 7-10 days
🏚
Maharashtra Agribusiness Cluster Development Scheme
up to ₹1 crore subsidy for cold storage, processing units in ODOP clusters; 50% capital subsidy, 12-18 month approval via DIC Palghar
🏚
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹50 lakh cluster development fund for food processing MSMEs in notified clusters (Dahanu, Gholvad); 85% subsidy for infrastructure, 6-month disbursal via Ministry of MSME
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Frequently Asked Questions

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

Fresh fruit trading: ₹1.5-2.5L (buying, cold storage, packaging, initial marketing). Processing (chips/powder): ₹2.5-4L (unit rent deposit, dryer, grinder, food license). GI+ export setup adds ₹15,000-25,000. Most founders start with fresh fruit trading and add processing after 4-6 months once demand is confirmed.
No farming experience needed — buy from licensed farmers/cooperatives. But you MUST understand fruit supply chains (ripeness grading, cold chain, spoilage rates) or hire someone. Palghar's ATMA office offers free 2-day fruit handling training. Trading experience or family connections in wholesale markets help a lot.
PMEGP: ₹25L loan with 25% subsidy. SFURTI: ₹50L cluster fund, 85% subsidy for processing units in Dahanu/Gholvad. Maharashtra Agribusiness: ₹1Cr subsidy, 50% capital support. Udyam registration: free, instant online. APEDA export support: free certification, buyer lists. You qualify for 2-3 schemes; total grant potential: ₹40-60L if you hit processing + export scale. Apply via DIC Palghar office.
Fresh fruit: List on BigBasket, JioMart Fresh within 4 weeks (first orders ₹10-20K). Juice brands: Email + cold call Nattu Naturals, Urban Platter with bulk pricing; expect 1-2 B2B responses in 2 weeks. Export: Contact UAE/UK importers via TradeKey.com (paid) or LinkedIn; samples take 2 weeks, first order 4-6 weeks. Local: Sell to Mumbai fruit importers directly — drive samples to Mahim fruit market, close deals in 3-4 days.
Haan, bilkul. GI-tagged Gholvad sapota exports to UAE, UK, Singapore, Bangladesh at premium pricing (₹60-80/kg FOB vs. ₹25-35/kg domestic wholesale). Requirements: IEC (₹500, 2 weeks), APEDA membership (free), Agmark/health certification (₹10,000, 2-3 weeks), cold chain logistics (partner with export freight forwarder ₹3,000-5,000 per shipment). First export order: 2-4 tonnes minimum. Margin: 35-50% FOB after costs.
Honest answer: Fresh fruit trading is lower-risk (buy, sell in 5 days) — max loss ₹50K if quality fails. Processing has 6-8 month breakeven, so stuck capital risk ₹2-3L if demand doesn't materialize. Exit: pivot to bulk supply to juice brands (lower margin but steady), or lease your processing unit to another food maker (recover 60-70% rent). Worst case: most chikoo businesses move to allied trades (mango processing, coconut oil) using same equipment. Know your exit before starting.
