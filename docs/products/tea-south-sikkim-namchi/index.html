Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — South Sikkim (Namchi) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established tea gardens in Namchi (Temi Tea Garden, Barnesbeg Tea Estate) and connect with local tea producer associations. Study organic certification requirements from APEDA and attend tea trade fairs in Kolkata and Delhi to understand market dynamics.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source green tea leaves directly from local farmers in Namchi at ₹200-300/kg. Establish relationships with 3-4 small tea gardens for consistent supply. First batch of 100kg processed tea requires ₹50,000-75,000 including drying, sorting, and initial packaging.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing small tea processing units in Namchi (contact South Sikkim Tea Planters Association) for initial production on contract basis, or invest in basic tea processing equipment (withering, rolling, drying machines) costing ₹800K-1.2M. Hire local skilled tea processors at ₹15,000-20,000/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME on udyamregistration.gov.in (₹0), obtain IEC code from APEDA (₹500), get Organic Certification from APEDA-approved certifier (₹30,000-50,000), file trademark with IPO (₹9,000-15,000), and register FSSAI food license (₹12,000).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon/Flipkart with 250g premium packs at ₹450-550. Simultaneously pitch to specialty tea retailers via Instagram and LinkedIn. Approach cafes in Bangalore, Pune, and Mumbai with wholesale rates (₹200-250/100g). Partner with 1-2 tea importers in Germany/UK for initial export orders.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to seasonal tea varieties (first flush, second flush blends). Open branded tea tasting lounges in tier 1 cities as franchise model. Launch subscription boxes at ₹999/month. Scale organic certification to full estate, aiming for GI tag within 3 years. Target ₹2-3Cr revenue by year 2.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for SC/ST) for setting up tea processing and packaging unit
🏛
APEDA Export Promotion Scheme
2% cash incentive on FOB value of exported tea to identified markets
🏚
Sikkim ODOP Scheme
marketing support, export promotion grants, and access to state-level tea processing clusters
🏚
Sikkim Organic Farming Mission
incentives for organic certification and sustainable agricultural practices (up to ₹10,000/hectare support)
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Marketing

Ye marketing ideas try karna mat bhulna

  • Instagram & YouTube: Create short-form content (15-30 sec) showing tea garden harvesting in Namchi with ASMR leaf sounds. Use hashtags #NamchiTea #OrganicTeaIndia #SikkimTea. Partner with 5-10 tea influencers (50K-500K followers) for unboxing reviews.
