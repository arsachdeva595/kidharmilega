Mau district in eastern Uttar Pradesh sits 250 km from Delhi and 400 km from Surat, enabling supply to both domestic wholesale markets and export gateways. The district is part of India's 108 powerloom clusters and has access to raw material suppliers across UP and Gujarat. Buyer proximity to saree wholesalers in Varanasi (80 km), Surat garment exporters, and Delhi apparel retailers creates immediate sales channels.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mau ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mau powerloom cluster (town center), talk to 3-5 loom owners about real costs and market rates. Call suppliers in Surat (synthetic yarn) and local cotton dealers. Register free on Udyam portal. Attend weekly textile haat in Mau town to see firsthand buyer-seller transactions. Zero cost, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source synthetic yarn from Surat yarn wholesalers (₹80-120/kg) or cotton yarn from Ghazipur suppliers (₹90-130/kg). First batch of 500 kg costs ₹50-60K. Meet suppliers in Surat or connect via textile brokers in Mau town market.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 500-sq-ft unit with 2-4 powerlooms in Mau powerloom cluster for ₹8-12K/month. OR partner with an existing weaver as a funding partner (you provide capital, they operate). Loom cost is ₹1-2L each; rent avoids upfront capital. Production capacity: 100-150 meters/day per loom.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 10 min). Apply for Solar Energy Scheme subsidy (₹2.50 lakh per unit) to reduce power costs over 3-4 years. If exporting, apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from nearest DGFT office (₹1000, 2 weeks). Consider GI registration support schemes if specializing in traditional designs.
5
First Sale
First sale: approach Varanasi saree wholesalers directly with 5-10 meter samples; expected first order is 200-500 meters at ₹150-200/meter wholesale. Simultaneously post 5-10 pieces on Meesho, Instagram with cost-price videos. Expected first month revenue: ₹40-60K.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire 1 manager and 2 loom operators (₹20-30K/month total). Add 2-3 more looms. Register with APEDA for export compliance. Attend Powerloom India Expo (biannual) or IITF Delhi for direct buyer meetings. Partner with 1-2 export houses in Delhi for consistent orders. Diversify into home textiles (bed sheets, curtains).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Solar Energy Scheme for Small Powerloom Units
₹2.50 lakh subsidy per unit; payback period 3-4 years on power costs.
🏛
Prime Minister Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency (MUDRA) Loan
up to ₹10 lakh at 0% govt subsidy for first 3-4 years.
🏚
UP Textile Policy 2017
capital subsidy 20% for powerloom clusters in notified areas; up to ₹25 lakh per unit.
🏚
UP ODOP Promotion Scheme
free registration, training, and marketing support for identified district products.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
