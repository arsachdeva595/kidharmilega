Udayagiri Wooden Cutlery ki
opportunity, SPSR Nellore mein.

Udayagiri is a specialized craft town in SPSR Nellore district, 150 km southwest of Chennai, with an established cluster of hand-carving artisans. Raw materials (sustainably sourced wood) are sourced locally and from adjacent regions, keeping material costs 20-30% below northern handicraft clusters. The cluster currently produces serving spoons, bowls, and cutlery sets with documented GI heritage; proximity to Chennai port and NH16 enables direct access to 5-star hotels, export freight forwarders, and coastal wholesale markets in Tamil Nadu.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — SPSR Nellore ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Udayagiri town cluster (contact Udayagiri Gram Panchayat or reach out via spsrnellore.ap.gov.in ODOP page). Spend a full day observing 3-4 active artisans at their units. Call the District Handicraft Office in Nellore city (free). This is ₹0 and doable within 1 week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Work directly with Udayagiri artisans. Initial trial order: 100 units mixed (spoons, bowls, plates) costs ₹12,000-18,000 at ₹120-180/unit raw. Request samples from 2-3 makers in Udayagiri cluster to evaluate quality, finish, and turnaround (10-14 days typical). Negotiate payment terms: 50% advance, 50% on delivery.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Recommended for first-gen): Artisan partnership model — pay artisans ₹150-200 per unit finished product, zero rental cost, 15-20 units/week capacity. Option B: Rent a 400-500 sq ft workshop space in Udayagiri or Nellore town at ₹4,000-7,000/month, hire 2-3 trained carvers at ₹8,000-12,000/month each; machine investment (sanders, drills) ₹60,000-100,000. First 3 months: ₹80,000-120,000 blended.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 10 mins at udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for GI brand use certificate through DC Handicraft office, Nellore (₹500-1,500 processing, 2-4 weeks). Get PAN and GST registration (₹0, online). If exporting: Apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at nearest DGFT office or online (₹0, 2-3 weeks). Get ISO/Eco-cert (optional, ₹20,000-40,000 for small batch) for export edge.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade (free listing) with 50 units stock (₹8,000 cost). First order likely ₹800-2,000 (4-10 units). Post unboxing/lifestyle photos on Instagram (free). Parallel: Email 30 organic kitchenware retailers in Bangalore, Delhi, Mumbai with catalog and ₹300/unit B2B pricing. Expected first month revenue from multiple channels: ₹25,000-50,000.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (~330 units/month at avg ₹300): Hire 1 dedicated export/order coordinator (₹15,000/month). Expand supplier network from 1-2 artisans to 5-7 to handle volume. List on Flipkart (10-15% commission). Attend international craft fairs (London, Berlin, New York) or use B2B trade platforms (Global Sources, Alibaba) for bulk orders. Implement quality control checklist. Rebrand with own mark/logo while respecting GI rules.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
₹15,000 toolkit subsidy + 5% interest subvention on MUDRA loans up to ₹5L for traditional artisans and new businesses
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy for general, 35% for SC/ST on fixed asset investment (max ₹25L) + 5% MUDRA loan interest subsidy
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Handicraft Development Scheme
25-30% subsidy on production equipment + exhibition support for GI-tagged products
🏚
AP One District One Product scheme
Marketing support, international trade fair participation grants, GI branding assistance (no direct cash, but logistics support)
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
