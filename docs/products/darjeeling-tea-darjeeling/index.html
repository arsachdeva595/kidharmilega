opportunity, Darjeeling mein.

Darjeeling district produces 8.5 million kg tea annually across 30,000+ hectares on Himalayan slopes (600-2000m), creating the only GI-protected tea in the world. The district has 87 major tea estates plus hundreds of small producers clustered around Darjeeling town (2,000m) and Kalimpong (1,250m), with established processing units within 10-30km of gardens. Buyers span Europe (40% CAGR growth), North America (35% of global market), Japan, and South Korea, with Siliguri rail hub (60km) and Bagdogra airport (70km) enabling year-round exports.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Darjeeling ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Darjeeling town tea market (Laden Bazaar) and call 3 tea estate offices in Kurseong or Kalimpong to understand leaf grades, seasons, and current spot prices (₹200-400/kg fresh leaf). Register free on Udyam portal. Attend Darjeeling Tea Association monthly auction preview. Zero cost, doable Week 1.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source dried tea leaf directly from 5-10 estates in Kurseong-Kalimpong belt (₹1200-3000/kg for unprocessed leaf, ₹2500-8000/kg for processed black tea ready to pack). First 50kg batch ₹125k-300k. Suppliers: contact Darjeeling Tea Association (DTA) for verified vendors, or scout Laden Bazaar and Darjeeling town trading offices.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with artisan partnership — contract with existing small processor (₹50k monthly for 100kg/month capacity) OR rent 400-600 sq ft unit in Darjeeling/Kalimpong at ₹8k-15k/month with electricity, water. Buy packaging: kraft pouches (₹2-5 per 50g), tea bags (₹3-8 per 25-bag box), labels (₹1-2 each). First 100kg batch production cost ₹200k-400k including labor, packaging, testing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online). Apply for GI tag usage under Ministry of Agriculture portal (₹15k-30k admin fee, approval 3-6 weeks if sourced from notified gardens). Register IEC code with customs for export (₹0, online via DGFT). Get FSSAI food license (₹5k-10k, 15 days). Open business bank account (₹0). Total compliance cost ₹20k-45k, 6-8 week timeline.
5
First Sale
First sale: list on Amazon Global or Etsy (10-15% fee), price 100g at $12-18. Join Darjeeling Tea Association or approach specialty retailers in Bangalore/Delhi directly via email with 500g free samples (₹2k). Expect first order 1-2kg via online within 2 weeks, first month revenue ₹15k-40k if targeting 5-10 orders.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month (₹120L/year): hire 2 full-time packers (₹12k/month each), 1 marketing person for social media (₹15k/month). Establish export agent in UK/Germany (5% commission per shipment). Exhibit at London Tea & Coffee Exchange or Specialty Food Association events ($2k-5k booth cost). Move to 1000+ sq ft dedicated unit with ColdChain for premium grades. Invest ₹3-5L in organic certification (adds 20-25% price premium).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP)
up to ₹25 lakhs loan with 35% subsidy for SC/ST or 25% for general, for tea processing and value-addition units.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹10-40 lakh subsidy for cluster-based tea producers, infrastructure development, common facility centers.
🏚
West Bengal One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
branding, packaging, marketing support, trade fair participation subsidies, export assistance up to ₹5 lakh.
🏚
Bangla Agri Business
subsidized credit up to ₹10 lakh at 4% interest for agri-processing, storage infrastructure development in Darjeeling district.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
