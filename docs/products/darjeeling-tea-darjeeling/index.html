Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Darjeeling ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Darjeeling tea clusters in Kurseong, Kalimpong, and Mirik; spend 2-3 weeks learning at 3-4 working estates like Makaibari, Glenburn, or Happy Valley; interact with tea brokers at Darjeeling Tea Auction Centre; study GI certification requirements and Darjeeling Tea Association guidelines
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh tea leaves directly from 2-3 GI-registered estates (₹400-600 per kg for premium leaves); establish relationship with leaf suppliers in April-June (first flush), July-August (second flush), and September-October (autumn flush); initial batch budget: ₹3-5 lakhs for 500kg processing trial
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with certified tea processors in Darjeeling town (Lebong, Sonada areas) who hold GI certification; alternatively, lease processing unit with existing machinery (₹50-100k/month); hire experienced tea masters (₹20-30k/month); start with 100-200kg batches for quality testing and brand development
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) as tea processor/dealer; apply for IEC code with customs; register brand name with Trademark office; obtain GI usage certificate from Darjeeling Tea Association (mandatory for 'Darjeeling' label); get ISO 22000 food safety certification; display GI logo on all packaging
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh (specialty category), JioMart Premium, and own Shopify/WooCommerce D2C store; approach specialty tea retailers in Delhi (24 Mantra, The Tea Spot), Mumbai, and Bangalore; register on B2B platform GlobalTrade; list on specialty export platforms like AliBaba and TradeKey; target tea subscription boxes (Teabox, Blue Tokai partnerships)
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish direct export relationships with UK (Whittard, Twinings), Germany, and USA importers; expand product line to include organic, white, green, and oolong variants; build tea tourism revenue through estate partnerships; scale to 5-10 tonnesworth annual production; invest in certified storage and blending facility
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs loan subsidy for tea processing MSMEs with 15-35% subsidy component
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
clusters support up to ₹2.5 Cr for tea processing infrastructure in Darjeeling
🏚
West Bengal Tea Board Development Scheme
subsidized inputs, training, and certification support for registered tea processors
🏚
Bengal Entrepreneurship Development Scheme
₹10 lakh credit guarantee and mentorship for agro-based businesses in hilly districts
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
