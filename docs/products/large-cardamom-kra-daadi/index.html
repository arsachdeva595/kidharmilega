Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kra Daadi ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kra Daadi district (Anini is the main hub) and connect with local farming cooperatives. Study cultivation techniques at Rajiv Gandhi University's Agricultural Extension Centre in nearby Arunachal Pradesh. Research cardamom markets in Kochi (Indian Spice Board) and Siliguri cardamom auction centres.
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner directly with local farmers in Anini or Palin villages for fresh large cardamom. First batch sourcing cost: ₹3-5 lakhs for 500 kg dried cardamom (₹600-700/kg wholesale rate). Connect via District Agriculture Office or AAFC (Arunachal Pradesh Agriculture & Food Corporation).
3
Produce / Partner
Establish a small drying and processing unit (₹2-5 lakhs) in Anini or outsource to local co-operatives like Kra Daadi Farmers Producer Company. Focus on quality grading, cleaning, and organic certification. Engage local youth through skill-training programs.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise) via udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Guwahati or Arunachal Pradesh. Apply for Organic Certification (FSSAI + APEDA) — costs ₹50k-1.5L over 3 years. Create brand identity emphasizing 'Himalayan Origin' and 'High Altitude Grown'.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart, Jio Mart under 'Premium Spices' category (target ₹2-5L GMV in first 3 months). Approach regional spice traders in Kolkata, Delhi, and Bangalore directly. Contact tea brands and Ayurvedic companies (Himalaya, Dabur) with B2B samples. Use Instagram/YouTube for story-driven health benefits content.
6
Scale
Expand to 3-5 farmer partnerships by month 6, targeting ₹50-80L annual revenue. Invest in processing automation (₹10-15L), cold storage, and quality testing lab. Apply for PMEGP/SFURTI subsidies. Build export relationships via APEDA and trade bodies. Launch subscription model for premium D2C customers.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy + ₹10L loan for small spice processing units in aspirational districts
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹5-50L cluster development grants for spice clusters in Arunachal Pradesh
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Agricultural Mechanization Scheme
50% subsidy on processing equipment, drying units, and cold storage facilities for agricultural entrepreneurs
🏚
Chief Minister's Employment Generation Scheme (CMEGS)
₹5-15L interest subsidy + training support for agribusiness ventures in hilly/tribal districts
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
