Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sepahijala ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sepahijala's Agartala weaving clusters (Kumarghat, Kailashahar) and meet master weavers; study Tripura Handloom Museum; attend National Handloom Day events; join Tripura Silk Federation.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cocoons from Tripura Sericulture Department-registered suppliers in Sepahijala (~₹8-12/kg for raw cocoons); establish relationships with local reeling units in Agartala; first batch budget: ₹80,000-1,20,000 for quality raw materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 registered weavers in Sepahijala through Tripura State Handloom Corporation; provide raw silk at cost + markup; maintain quality via third-party audits; alternatively, set up in-house loom workshop with 10-20 looms.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) through Udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC from DGFT Kolkata; register brand name with IP India; acquire GI certification application (even though current status is 'no') through Tripura Handicrafts Development Board; get FSSAI if selling dyed/processed textiles.
5
First Sale
Launch on Flipkart, Amazon, and Meesho with heritage storytelling; pitch to saree retailers in Jaipur, Surat, Chandni Chowk; contact Fab India, Aachho, IKEA for bulk; email 50 fashion designers in Mumbai/Delhi with samples; run Instagram/Facebook ads targeting 'traditional saree' + 'sustainable fabric' keywords.
6
Scale
After 6 months, apply for PMEGP subsidy to expand loom count; establish silk fabric design studio in Agartala; build B2B relationships with 20+ fashion labels; explore export to Japan/Germany via trade shows; hire design consultant to modernize traditional patterns; target ₹2 Cr revenue in Year 2.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% of capital for SC/ST/women, 25% general) for setting up weaving unit.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹5,000 grant + ₹2 lakh soft loan + training for registered artisans in sericulture and weaving.
🏚
Tripura Handloom Development Scheme
30% subsidy on loom purchases, raw material cost support, and market access guarantees for registered weavers.
🏚
Tripura Sericulture Mission
₹50,000-1,50,000 per acre mulberry garden subsidy; free cocoon supply to weavers; extension services for cocoon rearing.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
