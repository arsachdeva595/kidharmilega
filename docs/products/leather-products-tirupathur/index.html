Tirupathur district (formed 2019 from Vellore trifurcation) sits 140km southwest of Chennai in Tamil Nadu's leather corridor. The district has 195 revenue villages with established artisan communities trained in vegetable tanning and hand-finishing. Raw hide suppliers operate across North Arcot region; finished leather markets in Tirupathur town, Ambur, and Ranipet connect directly to Chennai's export hubs and international buyer networks.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Tirupathur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ambur leather cluster (15km from Tirupathur town) — meet 2-3 master artisans, understand raw material costs and production timelines. Call Tirupathur ODOP coordinator at district administration for current subsidy schemes. Register free on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in). Attend monthly haat at Tirupathur collectorate ground (3rd Saturday) where artisans display products.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw goat/sheep skins from Ambur leather market (₹800-1,200 per skin for grade A). Vegetable tannins from Ranipet suppliers (₹4,500-6,500 per 50kg bag). First batch: ₹40,000-60,000 for 40-50 skins + tanning materials. Establish relationship with 1-2 trusted suppliers; negotiate 30-45 day payment terms.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 600-800 sq ft unit in Tirupathur leather cluster (₹8,000-12,000/month) with ventilation and water access. Option B: Partner with 3-4 established artisans on revenue share (60-70% to you, 30-40% to artisans). Starting production cost: ₹50,000-75,000/month for 150-200 finished pieces. Hire 1-2 skilled cutters/finishers at ₹12,000-15,000/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 10 mins). Apply for GI certification support through DC Handicraft ministry (already GI-tagged, but can add your brand name — ₹5,000 application fee, 4-6 month timeline). Get IEC code from DGFT if targeting export (online, free, 1-2 weeks). Register company/partnership deed (₹500-2,000 with local registrar). Get GST registration (free, 3-5 days online).
5
First Sale
First sale: List on Etsy (2.4% + ₹20 listing fee per item, reach 200M buyers). Target first order: 10-15 bags at ₹3,000-4,500 each = ₹30-67k gross. Alternatively, contact 5 boutique leather retailers in Bangalore/Mumbai for stocking (net 30 payment terms, ₹5,000-10,000 first order). Expected first month: ₹60-100k revenue from combined D2C + retail outreach.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 operations manager (₹18k/mo), 2-3 additional artisans (₹48k/mo), 1 online marketer (₹12k/mo). Export: partner with ICCW or freight forwarder (1% commission on shipments). Exhibit at IHGF (Indian Handicrafts and Gifts Fair) in Delhi/Bangalore biannually (₹60-80k stall cost, ₹3-5L expected orders). Invest in Instagram/Pinterest ads (₹15-20k/month for targeted Western audience). Consider B2B wholesale reps in US/UK (8-12% commission).

Established cluster with 40+ registered tanneries and 150+ artisan units. Plot sizes 400-800 sq ft, shared water treatment facilities, raw material trading yards, nearby supplier shops for chemicals and tools. Rent: ₹8-15/sq ft/month.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 tooling grant + 5% interest subsidy on ₹1-5L loan for traditional artisans/craftspeople
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster-level grants up to ₹50 lakh for leather cluster development, processing facilities, common workshops
🏚
Tamil Nadu MSME Competitive Fund
3-5% capital subsidy (max ₹10 lakh) for new micro enterprises registered under Udyam in handicraft sectors
🏚
Chennai Metropolitan Area Export Promotion Scheme
₹1-2 lakh export marketing support for first-time exporters of handicrafts from Tamil Nadu
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
