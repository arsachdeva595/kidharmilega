Lakhisarai sits in north-central Bihar along the Ganges, with alluvial soil naturally suited to pulse cultivation and direct access to farmer networks across the district. The region is 180 km south of Patna with road and rail connectivity via NH 114 to national pulse markets in Delhi, Mumbai, and coastal export hubs. Proximity to Bangladesh border and direct export corridors via Kolkata port make this a strategic hub for Asian pulse markets.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Lakhisarai ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Lakhisarai haat (Monday/Friday market days) and talk to 3 masoor farmers or dal traders. Call Lakhisarai Agriculture Office (0612-2251522) and ask about farmer cooperative contacts. Attend next session at local Krishi Vigyan Kendra for free training on pulse processing. Zero cost, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source masoor seeds from Lakhisarai Cooperative Society (Mandi area) or direct farmers in Lakhisarai tehsil. First batch: 5 MT costs ₹2.0-2.3 lakhs depending on quality grade. Supplier names: Check with Lakhisarai Nagar Cooperative or contact district ATMA office for verified lists.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 1,200 sq ft processing unit in Lakhisarai industrial area (₹8-12K/month) and buy a dal mill machine (₹2.5-4 lakhs). Option B: Partner with existing artisan-run dal processors and provide capital, margin-sharing model (5-8% commission). Processing cost: ₹3-5 per kg of cleaned masoor to packaged dal.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online via udyamregistration.gov.in — 15 min). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (₹0 if filing online, process: 2 weeks). File GI registration for Lakhisarai Masoor with Ministry of Commerce (₹5,000-10,000 filing + legal, 6-12 month approval). Get FSSAI license (₹5,000-12,000 depending on category).
5
First Sale
First sale: List on Amazon Seller Central or local BigBasket vendor portal (₹0 to list, 15% commission). Create 500g premium organic packs at ₹140-160 retail. Target first month: 50-100 units from local D2C pushes via WhatsApp, Insta stories, local yoga/health groups. Expected first-month D2C revenue: ₹8-15K.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: Hire 1 packaging operator (₹12K/month), 1 logistics coordinator. Exhibit at APEDA trade fairs and BIOFACH India (food trade shows). Establish direct B2B ties with 2-3 regional food brands. Implement cold storage for moisture control. Move to export-grade processing certification (APEDA compliance, ₹25-40K, 4-6 weeks).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Up to ₹25 lakhs loan with 35% subsidy for general entrepreneurs (non-SC/ST), 10% margin money required.
🏛
MUDRA Scheme (Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana)
Up to ₹10 lakhs unsecured loan at 8-12% interest for small food processing units.
🏚
Bihar Krishi Sinchayee Yojana
Supports agricultural processing units with 50% subsidy on equipment up to ₹5 lakhs.
🏚
Bihar Laghu Udyog Vikas Nidhi (LUVN)
₹2 lakhs concessional loan at 4% interest for food processing micro-enterprises.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
