opportunity, Khammam mein.

Khammam district (14,142 km²) lies in Telangana's industrial belt with direct access to black soil ideal for chilli cultivation. The district produces 63.25 quintals/hectare average yield with net returns of ₹141,280/hectare, supported by 252.92 male and 376.63 female labour days per hectare. Connected to Hyderabad (200km west), Vijayawada (180km east), and major spice markets in Maharashtra via NH44, with Khammam Railway Station enabling bulk export to pan-India and international buyers.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Khammam ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khammam mandis (Khammam Central Market or Tamarlapalli APMC market) — call 3 chilli traders, ask about seasonal yields, input costs, buyer networks. Free. Also attend a Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) weekly session on chilli cultivation — no fee. Do this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw chillies from marginal/small farmers in Khammam taluk during Oct-Feb harvest season (₹30-45/kg fresh, ₹80-110/kg dried). Partner with 10-15 farmers via written supply agreements. First batch: 500kg fresh = ₹20-25K. Contact KHAMMAM KRISHI MANDI for farmer networks.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 500-1000 sq.ft. space in Khammam town or Tamarlapalli (₹3000-6000/month) for drying + sorting + packing. Set up solar dryer (₹80-120K) or use traditional sun-drying on own land. Initial investment: drying equipment ₹100-150K, packing material ₹25K, small machinery ₹75-100K. Total ₹250-350K for micro-producer setup.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (free, 15 mins online). File APEDA registration for export (₹5000, 2 weeks). Get IEC code (₹0 with APEDA). Apply for GI registration if positioning premium (₹20-40K, 4-6 months — consider joining a FPO to share cost). Get FSSAI license for food processing (₹100 for micro-business, 2 weeks).
5
First Sale
First sale: cold-call 5 regional spice processors in Hyderabad (via LinkedIn, APEDA directory) offering 100kg trial batch at ₹95/kg. List on Amazon Seller Central with 50kg stock (₹15/kg cost + ₹50/kg retail = ₹2500 revenue per unit). Expected first month: ₹30-50K (mix of direct orders + wholesale).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: invest in wet grinder (₹80K), packaging machine (₹120K), hire 2 full-time sorters (₹12K/month each). Exhibit at NASME Food Festival (Delhi, Jan), Spice Expos (Nashik, May). Launch 3-4 SKUs (whole dried, powder, flakes). Partner with 1-2 regional distributors in Karnataka/Maharashtra. Target export: 20kg/week to UAE importers via APEDA-registered freight forwarder.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM-KISAN Samman Nidhi
₹6000/year direct cash transfer to farmer suppliers (partner with them, ensure they register)
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹25L subsidy (35% for general, up to 45% for SC/ST/women) if processing chillies into value-added products; max ₹50L project cost
🏚
Telangana Rythu Bandhu Scheme
₹5000/acre to farmer suppliers per annum (link them to state portal to capture this benefit)
🏚
Telangana MSME Ceiling Loan Scheme
up to ₹50L at 6% interest for registered food/spice micro-enterprises
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
