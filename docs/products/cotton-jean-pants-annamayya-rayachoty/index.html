Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Annamayya (Rayachoty) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Rayachotu textile cluster and Annamayya SIDCO industrial park to understand production processes. Connect with existing garment manufacturers and power loom operators. Study market demand via Meesho, Amazon seller data for cotton-denim pants pricing (₹399-₹899 range). Join textile associations in district to understand supply chain.
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure cotton fabric from Warangal or Hyderabad textile wholesalers (₹80-120/meter), denim from Tiruppur mills (₹120-180/meter). For first batch of 500 units: approximately ₹95,000 for fabric, ₹25,000 for trims/accessories, ₹15,000 for thread/chemicals. Establish 30-45 day payment terms with suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established garment manufacturers in Rayachotu town or nearby Madanapalle who have cutting and stitching units. Average production cost per unit: ₹280-350. Quality check via sampling. Start with 200-500 unit pilot batch. Train QC team on sizing, stitch quality, seam strength.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise) online at udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office (Hyderabad regional office handles AP). Get GST registration (12% applicable for garments). Design brand identity and register trademark. Obtain FSSAI if adding any branded packaging with care instructions.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Seller Central and Flipkart Seller Hub with 2-3 SKUs (sizes XS-XXL in 2-3 colors). Simultaneously approach modern retail chains in Hyderabad, Bangalore, Chennai. Create Meesho storefront for tier-2/3 city penetration. Build WhatsApp Business catalog for B2B wholesale inquiries. Target ₹5-8L revenue in first 3 months.
6
Scale
After 6 months, introduce 3-5 new designs based on seasonal trends. Expand to premium cotton-denim blends (higher margins). Establish export relationships with Bangladesh and Middle East importers via TradeKey, Global Sources. Invest in in-house finishing unit to improve margins by 5-8%. Hire dedicated digital marketing manager to scale D2C revenue to 40-50% of total.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM EGIDI (Enterprises from Agriculture and Beyond): ₹50L grant for agro-based textiles and garments production clusters
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme): ₹25L subsidy for manufacturing setup with 10-25% equity contribution
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Industrial Infrastructure Corporation (APIIC): Land and shed at discounted rates in SIDCO parks; subsidy on utility costs
🏚
AP Skill Development: Free training on garment design and quality control for registered workers; apprenticeship stipends ₹8,000/month
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
