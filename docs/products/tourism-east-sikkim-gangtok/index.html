Gangtok sits at 5,410 ft elevation on the Darjeeling-Sikkim-Nepal border with year-round 12-20°C climate, attracting 650,000+ annual tourists. The district has 2,100+ hotels, 850+ homestays, and 40,000+ registered hospitality workers concentrated within 15 km of Gangtok city center. Raw materials (local guides, Lepcha/Tibetan handicrafts, organic produce, monastic access permits) are sourced within the district; buyer markets are predominantly domestic (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) and international (US, UK, EU) with flights via Delhi or Bagdogra airport (70 km).

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — East Sikkim (Gangtok) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gangtok Tourist Information Centre (MG Marg, free) and meet 5 active tour operators; call 2-3 homestay owners on Airbnb to understand occupancy rates (typically 45-60% year-round); attend Gangtok Tourism Department monthly networking meet (free, check Facebook); speak with 3 registered guides on MG Marg about demand patterns.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source local guides: Gangtok Tourism Board's list (₹0); connect with Lepcha/Sikkimese handicraft clusters in Gangtok bazaar and Lal Bazaar (no upfront cost); identify hotel partners for first 5-10 room rentals; call Bhutia/Lepcha homestay owners in Gangtok and suburbs — typical first batch of 20 visitor days costs ₹40-80K for guide + homestay coordination.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 600 sq ft office on MG Marg (₹15-25K/month) for booking and coordination; OR Option B: Partner with 2-3 existing hotels as sub-agent with zero rent (commission-only model). Both models start with guide services + pre-booked hotel packages. Initial staff: 1 person (you) + 1 part-time booking coordinator (₹8-12K/month). Cost range: ₹2-4L for registration, basic office setup, initial marketing, and 3-month operating costs if renting.
4
Brand & Register
Udyam registration online (free, 15 min); apply for tourism business license from Gangtok Municipal Corporation (₹3-5K, 15 days); open MSME bank account with SBI/HDFC for working capital access; GST registration (₹0 for TDS-exempt tourism if <₹20L annual); if exporting: IEC code from DGFT Kolkata branch (₹0, 10 days online). Total: ₹3-5K + 30 days timeline.
5
First Sale
First sale: List on Airbnb Experiences (10% commission), Google My Business (free), Facebook local groups (free). Post 5-10 experience packages (trekking 2-day = ₹4-6K/person, monastery + lake = ₹2-3K/person, homestay weekend = ₹3-5K/person). First order size: 2-4 bookings = ₹8-20K gross in week 1; expected first-month revenue ₹1.5-3L from 15-25 bookings if marketing starts week 1.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month (~150-200 bookings): hire 1 full-time ops manager (₹18K/month) + 2 freelance guides (₹500/day each). Expand: add adventure sports packages (paragliding ₹8-10K, climbing ₹5K), corporate offsite planning (₹3-8L per event), seasonal Khangchendzonga trek packages (₹15-25K/person, 6-day). Apply for Swadesh Darshan funding for infrastructure (₹50-200L) if partnering with hotels. Exhibit at India Travel Expo (Mumbai, Delhi) — ₹2-3L booth cost, but 50-100 qualified international B2B leads per expo.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Swadesh Darshan Scheme
₹50-200 Lakh for infrastructure development (hotels, roads, amenities) in notified tourism zones; 4-6 month processing, 50% subsidy on approved projects
🏛
PRASHAD Scheme
₹50 Lakh to ₹1 Crore for pilgrimage/religious tourism infrastructure (monasteries, heritage guides, facilities); 60% subsidy for non-profit components
🏚
Sikkim Tourism Board: Hospitality Business Grant
₹5-15L subsidy for new 3-5 star hotels/resorts; 25-50% subsidy on GST for 3 years if registered as tourism enterprise
🏚
Sikkim MSME Promotion Scheme
Working capital subsidy of ₹3-5L + 6% interest subvention for 5 years for tourism micro-enterprises; eligibility: annual turnover <₹1Cr
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
