Processing of Indian Gooseberry (Amla) ki
opportunity, Bilaspur mein.

Bilaspur district in Himachal Pradesh's lower Himalayan belt (600-1200m altitude) produces premium amla fruit with 0.9-1.3% ascorbic acid content, fed by local orchards capable of supplying 2.5 tons/hr to processing units. The proposed ODOP cluster has infrastructure for 1 ton/hr pulp production across 130-day season with stored pulp processable into value-added products year-round. Markets include FMCG brands in Chandigarh/Delhi NCR (320km away), ayurvedic manufacturers across India, and export-ready buyers in North America and Europe seeking certified herbal ingredients.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bilaspur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 5 existing amla orchards in Bilaspur (contact Bilaspur Agricultural Department), visit Nahan or Sunni mandi on a Saturday to see wholesale pricing, call 2 existing chayavanprash makers in Himachal (search nic.in for food processing units), register free Udyam account online — all free, doable in 1 week
2
Source Raw Materials
Source amla fruits from local orchards in Bilaspur district (contact via Horticulture Department, ₹40-60/kg during June-September harvest season). First batch: 5-10 tons = ₹2-6L. Orchards clustered around Dhalwin, Sunni, Nahan areas. Negotiate 10-15% bulk discount for season-long contracts.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 1500-2000 sqft food processing unit in Bilaspur Industrial Estate or Nahan (₹25-45K/month), install pulping machine (₹12-18L), cold storage 10-ton (₹8-12L), bottling line (₹4-8L). OR partner with existing 2-3 processing units on contract manufacturing basis (₹8-15L investment for equipment ownership, 5-8% processing fee to processor). Soft production cost: ₹80-120/kg pulp depending on scale.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 1 day), apply for FSSAI license (₹500-1500, 7-14 days), get ISO 22000 certification (₹40-60K, 4-6 weeks). Consider GI registration for Bilaspur Amla (₹5-10L, 18-24 months via Ministry ODOP support, may reduce to ₹1-2L with state backing). IEC registration for export (₹0, online Aadhar-based).
5
First Sale
First sales: contact 3-5 ayurvedic brands via email (list on Ayush Ministry website), offer 500kg trial batch at ₹110/kg wholesale (vs ₹90-140 market). Simultaneously list on Amazon Handmade and Nykaa (₹300-800/100g amla powder, expect 50-100 units/month initially). Target ₹2-4L revenue Month 1 via mix of wholesale enquiries and 10-15 D2C orders.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire quality manager (₹35K), add second processing line (₹15-20L), launch export sales via direct outreach to organic importers in USA/Germany (₹50K travel/samples). Exhibit at Agrotech India, Export Promotion Council food shows (₹3-5L). Build WhatsApp/email list of 50+ B2B buyers. Reach ₹25-35L/month by Month 12 with 60% wholesale, 25% export, 15% D2C.
