Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Shi Yomi ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Shi Yomi district's kiwi cultivation clusters around Taksing and Mechuka; interact with existing cultivators; study the Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Department's kiwi training modules; attend workshops at the Regional Horticultural Research Station in Namsai
2
Source Raw Materials
Source grafted kiwi saplings (Hayward/Abbott/Gold varieties) from ICAR institutes in Himachal Pradesh (Kullu) or directly from certified nurseries in Sikkim; budget ₹25,000–₹40,000 per acre for 111 plants; procure organic inputs from local farming cooperatives in Taksing
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2–5 existing kiwi farmers in Shi Yomi through lease or sharecropping arrangement; start with 0.5–1 acre trial plot; engage local agricultural extension officers; join Shi Yomi Farmers Cooperative for training and pest management; expect first commercial harvest in year 2–3
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) and apply for IEC with Ministry of Commerce; obtain organic certification from IFOAM-accredited certifier (₹30,000–₹60,000 for 2 years); trademark brand name with IP Office; obtain FSSAI license for any processing; register with state agriculture department for subsidy eligibility
5
First Sale
Launch D2C via WhatsApp/Instagram with photo content from farm; list on BigBasket, JioMart, and Farmer's Pride app with premium organic tag; partner with organic retailers in Delhi/Bangalore/Pune; approach exporters in Guwahati and Kolkata for bulk contracts; engage with Amazon Fresh for metro distribution
6
Scale
After 6 months of first harvest, expand cultivation to 2–3 acres; invest in cold storage unit (₹15–20L) near Taksing; develop processed products (dried kiwi, kiwi juice concentrate); establish export logistics via freight forwarders; participate in Arunachal Pradesh agri-export trade fairs; recruit and train 2–3 farmhands
Government Schemes

🏛
Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)
₹6,000/year direct support to small farmers
🏛
Rashtriya Krishi Vikas Yojana (RKVY)
subsidies up to 50% for horticulture infrastructure and cold storage in northeastern states
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Mission
up to ₹40,000/acre subsidy for perennial fruit cultivation and drip irrigation
🏚
State Organic Certification Scheme
50% reimbursement of organic certification costs for Arunachal Pradesh farmers
