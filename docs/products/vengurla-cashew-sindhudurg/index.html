Vengurla Cashew ki
opportunity, Sindhudurg mein.

Sindhudurg district on Maharashtra's southern coast controls 100+ cashew processing units across 8 established clusters (Vengurla, Sawantwadi, Malvan, Kudal, Kankavli, Dodamarg, Devgad, Vaibhavwadi) supplied by local farms with yields of 13-24 kg per tree. The Regional Fruit Research Station (est. 1957) in Vengurla developed 7 exclusive cashew varieties (V1-V7) and cashew apple products with documented properties: V5 and V7 juice content 86%, V2 shell thickness 32% shelling percentage. Export markets in UAE, Saudi Arabia, USA, UK demand Vengurla cashew at premium pricing due to GI status (awarded 2008) and superior taste profile.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sindhudurg ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call District Industries Centre, Sindhudurg at 02362-228705 and request introduction to 2-3 processing unit owners in Vengurla or Sawantwadi cluster. Visit one cluster in person (₹500 transport), meet a unit manager, see their sorting/drying process, ask about raw material sourcing and buyer channels. Attend next cashew trade gathering or haat in Malvan or Kudal (no entry fee). Zero cost: register on Udyam portal this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cashew nuts from Devgad or Vengurla wholesale market (₹450-550/kg, Feb-May season only). Establish relationship with 1-2 suppliers willing to sell 50-100 kg batches. Cost for first batch: ₹25-35K for 50-75 kg raw nuts. Contact cashew merchants in Vengurla town directly (ask DIC for contacts). Secondary source: Kankavli wholesale market for off-season stockists.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 1500-2000 sq ft unit in Vengurla or Sawantwadi industrial cluster (₹8-15K/month) with electricity supply and water access. Partner with 1-2 local processors for sorting/drying/roasting (₹30-50 per kg processing fee) instead of buying your own dryers (saves ₹4-6L). Alternatively: lease an existing underutilized processing unit from a retiring processor (₹15-25K/month, includes equipment). Total setup: ₹50-80K first-month working capital, ₹200-300K for licenses and packaging inventory.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 10 mins). Apply for GI mark usage through DIC (₹5-8K filing fee, 20-30 day approval if sourcing from Sindhudurg). Obtain FSSAI Food Business License (₹100-500 for micro business, 10-15 days). If exporting: apply for IEC code at nearest Customs office (free, 5-7 days). Trademark your brand name at IP office (₹4500, 18-24 month process, optional at start). Total timeline: 45 days, ₹10-15K.
5
First Sale
Upload 3-4 product SKUs (roasted salted cashews, Kaju Barfi, gift box assortment) on Amazon Seller Central and Flipkart Seller Hub (free registration). Target first order from bulk buyer: approach 10 corporate gifting consultants with samples (cost ₹3-5K for 10 sample boxes). First realistic sale: 5-10 kg via ecommerce by week 4 at ₹1200-1500/kg = ₹6-15K revenue. Parallel: cold-call 5 retailers in Pune/Mumbai wholesale markets with 2 kg samples. Expected first-month revenue: ₹20-40K.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time operations manager (₹18-22K/month) and 1 part-time sales/export coordinator. Invest in your own small sun-drying setup (₹2-3L) to control quality. Exhibit at food trade shows: India International Trade Fair Delhi (Sept), Nutrafoods Asia (Bangalore), World Food India (biennial). Target wholesale expansion: approach 50-100 general trade retailers via Pune/Mumbai distributor networks. Launch V5/V7 value-added products (cashew apple squash, juice) to differentiate. Set up 1-2 retail kiosks in high-footfall markets (Vile Parle, Bandra Mumbai) by month 18.
