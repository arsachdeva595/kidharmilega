Sikar has a centuries-old tradition of wooden furniture craftsmanship with access to premium hardwoods like teak and rosewood. The district's skilled artisan community has perfected intricate carving techniques that are uniquely suited to creating high-value antique and decorative furniture pieces. The region's proximity to timber markets and established export networks makes it ideal for scaling wooden furniture production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sikar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sikar city's furniture clusters in Mahendra Nagar and Khimsar Road; spend 3-5 days understanding carving techniques, wood sourcing, and pricing from established makers like those in Sikar's cooperative societies. Visit Jaipur's furniture export hubs to understand market standards.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium hardwoods from Sikar's timber merchants on Khimsar Road (₹2-5 Lakhs for first batch of 8-10 pieces); establish relationships with local sawmills. Negotiate bulk rates for recurring supplies. First batch should include 5-6 statement pieces.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-8 established craftsmen in Sikar through Sikar District Handicraft Cooperative Society; provide design briefs and quality specifications. Alternatively, set up small workshop with 3-4 skilled artisans earning ₹15-25K monthly each. Establish 4-6 week production cycles.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (MSE) scheme as handicraft enterprise; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Jaipur (2-week process). Register brand name with IP office. Obtain AGMARK or state handicraft certification from Rajasthan Handicraft Board.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Ikat.com, and Jaypore for D2C; reach out to 20-30 heritage hotels and resorts directly; list on auction platforms; attend IITF Delhi and Rajasthan International Craft Mela. Create Instagram portfolio showcasing craftsmanship. Target 2-3 first orders worth ₹3-8 Lakhs each.
6
Scale
After first 6 months, expand artisan network to 15-20 craftsmen; establish quality control systems; invest in small-scale workshop setup (₹30-50L); launch B2B export partnerships; build presence on 3-4 international marketplaces; start conducting heritage craft workshops for tourists.
