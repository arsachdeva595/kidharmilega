opportunity, Siaha mein.

Siaha district in southern Mizoram, elevation 300-1200m with humid subtropical climate and potash-rich soil, serves as the primary bird's eye chilli production zone for the state's 11,000 ha cultivation area. Government-installed solar drying tunnels, water tanks, and processing facilities are already operational in the district, reducing founder infrastructure burden. Buyer markets span domestic FMCG (Aizawl, Shillong), regional wholesale (Guwahati food traders), and direct export to Philippines, Vietnam, Thailand — with Zoram Mega Food Park and Mizo Food Processing Industries already proving the export model.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Siaha ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call District Agriculture Department office Siaha (dial 03862-221XXX or email agriculture office); visit one of the operating solar drying centers near Siaha town; ask for introduction to 2-3 chilli growers in Chhimtuipui or Zokhawthar (prime growing areas). Attend weekly vegetable haat in Siaha town to track chilli price movements and talk to middlemen. Free. Doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source bird's eye chilli from farmers in Chhimtuipui and Zokhawthar tehsils (20-30 km from Siaha town) directly or via Siaha Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC). Fresh chilli currently ₹50-80/kg (green), dry chilli ₹150/kg. First batch contract: 500 kg dry chilli = ₹75,000. Negotiate multi-month agreements (Aug-Dec) to lock rates.
3
Produce / Partner
Two routes: (A) Rent an existing government-installed drying facility in Siaha district (₹8-12K/month) or partner with an active drier operator (commission 8-10% on output). (B) Set up own small unit: 300 sq ft processing space rent ₹5-8K/month (near Siaha town), buy one electric grinder (₹25K), sealing machine (₹8K), food-grade containers (₹15K). Total capital route: ₹60-80K/month recurring + ₹50K upfront equipment = ₹130K to start. Process: dry fresh chilli (24-48 hrs in solar tunnel) → grind → pack. Yield: 1kg fresh = 0.25-0.3 kg powder.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in, 10 mins). File for GI tag use with Ministry of Agriculture (covered under existing GI — submit proof of production origin; ₹0-5K filing, 4-6 weeks). If exporting: apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (₹0, 1 week). Trademark brand name via IP India (₹5-8K, 3-4 months). Obtain FSSAI license (₹7-9K, 6 weeks). Total formal cost: ₹12-22K.
5
First Sale
First sale route: upload to Amazon Seller Central (₹5K registration) with 500g powdered chilli jar (₹120 COGS + ₹40 packaging = ₹160/unit) priced ₹399-449. Parallel: email 20 regional FMCG wholesalers (Aizawl, Shillong) with sample + ₹35/kg bulk pricing (5kg min = ₹175 net). First month realistic target: 40-50 online units (₹16-20K revenue) + 20kg wholesale (₹700). Total first-month gross revenue: ₹17-21K. Breakeven unit = 300-400 units/month at current margin.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time production manager (₹15K/month), 1 part-time packaging/QA staff (₹8K/month). Upgrade to dedicated 1000 sq ft facility (₹15K/month) with capacity for 50kg/day. Launch chilli paste/pickle line (adds 25-30% revenue). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹25-50L available) for equipment upgrade and working capital. Exhibit at Aizawl Food Fair and Northeast Trade Fair (Oct-Nov) for bulk buyer meetings. Target ₹30L annual by month 18.
