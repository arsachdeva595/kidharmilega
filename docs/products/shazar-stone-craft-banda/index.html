Shazar Stone Craft ki
opportunity, Banda mein.

Banda district in south-central Uttar Pradesh sits on the Ken River, the only waterway in UP producing naturally colored patterned Shazar stones — the second such river on Earth. A concentrated cluster of master artisans has preserved 400-year-old stone-cutting and jewelry-setting techniques handed down since Arab traders first discovered these gems. The product exports to Arab countries, Iran, and Britain, with domestic demand from Lucknow's jewelry hubs and Indian heritage institutions.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Banda ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Banda stone craft cluster in Banda town (ask for artisans near Ken River bank or local DC office for addresses). Call 3-4 established stone carvers to understand typical output, costs, and export channels — all free. Attend the nearest local haat or collector's market to see real pieces and pricing. Register on Udyam portal (free, online, 15 mins).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw Shazar stones directly from Ken River quarry operators in Banda; cost ₹2000-5000 per kg depending on color rarity. Smaller hand tools (Kaman, steel wire, silicon carbide powder) from Banda's local tool suppliers or Lucknow's craft supply shops — ₹15000-30000 for a starter kit. Gold and silver leaf from Lucknow's jewelry wholesalers — buy on credit from established dealers once you have proof of work.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 1-2 established artisans in Banda on commission basis (60-70% you keep, 30-40% to artisan per piece) rather than renting a unit upfront — saves ₹25000-40000/month rent. Alternatively, rent a small 200-300 sq ft workshop space near Ken River for ₹8000-15000/month. Initial working capital for 50 pieces: ₹80000-120000 (stones, materials, 4-6 weeks artisan fees).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online). Apply for GI registration support through DC Handicrafts Banda office (typically ₹5000-10000 subsidy, 60-90 day turnaround). If exporting, file IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at local customs office — ₹free, 5-7 days. Get a shop certificate from municipal office — ₹2000-5000.
5
First Sale
List on Etsy under 'Shazar Stone Jewelry' and 'Decorative Sculptures' (first 40 listings free, then ₹0.20 per listing). Target first order through direct outreach to Arab importers via TradeKey, Alibaba (post 5-10 hero pieces with full process video). Expected first order size: ₹25000-50000 from international buyer; first-month revenue ₹40000-80000 if 2-3 orders land.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue, hire 2 dedicated artisans on monthly retainer (₹20000-30000 each) + piece rate. Rent a 500-800 sq ft showroom-workshop (₹20000-35000/month) to host international buyers and tourist groups. Exhibit at Delhi Crafts Fair, India Handloom Expo, and Gulf-focused trade shows (₹50000-100000/year in show fees). Build a WhatsApp business catalog and email newsletter for returning buyers.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan with 35% subsidy for general category in rural Banda district.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹3 lakh direct credit per artisan, ₹15000 for toolkit, 5% rate of interest with govt interest subvention.
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Handicraft Development Fund
grant support up to ₹5 lakh for ODOP-registered artisans for tool purchase and workspace setup.
🏚
UP One District One Product (ODOP) Export Support Scheme
up to 50% subsidy on international trade fair participation and export documentation.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
