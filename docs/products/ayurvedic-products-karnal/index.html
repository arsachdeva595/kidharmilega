Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Karnal ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established Ayurvedic manufacturing clusters in Karnal (Old City area, Industrial Estate Sector-24) and meet 3-5 existing manufacturers. Attend Ayurveda Expo India or India Ayurveda Festival. Study regulations from Ministry of AYUSH website and ISO standards for herbal products.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Omax Agro (Hisar), Herbal Extracts supplier network in Panipat, and direct farmers in Himachal Pradesh. Budget ₹3-5 lakhs for first 500kg mixed batch of premium ingredients with GMP compliance documentation.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with GMP-certified contract manufacturers in Karnal Industrial Estate (₹15-25k per 1000 units for 100ml bottles) or establish in-house facility with ₹20-30 lakh investment. Register with Ayurveda manufacturing association (AMAI) and begin with 2-3 hero SKUs.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 15 mins), obtain IEC code from DGFT, file FSSAI license (₹5k, 10 days), and register brand name with IP India. Get AYUSH certification and ISO 22000 compliance (₹50-75k total). Create packaging compliant with ayurvedic labeling norms.
5
First Sale
Launch with 5-10 SKU lineup on Nykaa (highest Ayurveda category traffic), Flipkart Supermart, and Amazon's Ayurveda vertical. Simultaneously approach 20-30 local Ayurvedic pharmacies in Delhi-NCR for wholesale at 35% margin. Create Instagram/YouTube educational content on product benefits.
6
Scale
After 6 months and ₹25L+ revenue, expand SKU portfolio to 15+ products, establish distribution in tier-2 cities (Jaipur, Lucknow, Chandigarh), launch export division targeting USA/UK markets through specialty retailers, and consider private label contracts with established wellness brands.

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh grant + 25% subsidy for manufacturing units in Haryana
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹15-20 lakh support for clusters of Ayurvedic manufacturers with subsidy on infrastructure
🏚
Haryana Khad Subsidy Scheme
subsidized raw materials for organic herbal cultivation partnering with state farmers
🏚
Haryana Export Promotion Scheme
10-15% subsidy on international certification, testing, and export logistics for AYUSH products
