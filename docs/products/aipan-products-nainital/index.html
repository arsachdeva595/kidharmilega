Nainital has been the cultural epicenter of Kumaoni Aipan art for centuries, with generational knowledge embedded in local communities. The region's proximity to pilgrimage sites and wedding destinations creates natural demand for ceremonial Aipan products. Local artisans have perfected techniques using natural pigments sourced from the Himalayan foothills.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Nainital ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nainital's Aipan clusters in Bhimtal and surrounding villages; connect with DC Handicraft office in Nainital; attend Uttarakhand handicraft expos; document 10-15 master artisans' techniques and pricing structures.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source natural pigments from local suppliers in Nainital town (₹8,000-12,000 first batch); rice flour and cotton from Dehradun wholesale markets; establish relationships with 3-4 artisan families for fabric/canvas base materials (₹15,000-20,000 for initial inventory).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established Aipan artisans in Nainital district (identify via DC Handicraft) on revenue-share model (40-50% to artisans); or train 2-3 in-house apprentices (6-8 weeks training); start with home décor items and greeting cards before expanding to apparel.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online (micro/small enterprise); apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Dehradun; register brand name with IP India; get GI certification endorsement from Ministry and DC Handicraft; create ISO 9001 basic compliance for export readiness.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Amazon Handmade within 60 days (list 20-30 SKUs); approach 5-10 wedding planners in Delhi/Mumbai with samples; partner with 2-3 online home décor stores (UrbanLadder, Craftsvilla); create Instagram/Pinterest content showing artisan stories and process videos.
6
Scale
After 6 months profitability, hire a designer for product innovation; expand to apparel (scarves, shawls, dupattas); open 1 pop-up store in Nainital town or Delhi; apply for SFURTI cluster subsidy; target 3-5 export markets with dedicated wholesale outreach; train 10-15 more artisans to meet demand.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for MSMEs in handicraft sector with 35% subsidy on equipment
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
direct artisan support, skill training, and credit up to ₹3 lakh at 5% interest for traditional craftspeople
🏚
Uttarakhand Handicraft Development Board Subsidy
20-25% capital subsidy for handicraft units in registered clusters
🏚
Uttarakhand Skill Development Mission
free training and certification for artisans and entrepreneurs in traditional crafts with placement support
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
