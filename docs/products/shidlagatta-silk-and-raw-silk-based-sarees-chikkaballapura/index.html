Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Chikkaballapura ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Shidlagatta weaving cluster (15km from Chikkaballapura town) and spend 2-3 days studying 5-6 established weavers. Connect with Chikkaballapura Weavers Association and DC Handloom office. Attend IHGF Handloom Expo or Heritage India Fashion Show to understand market pricing and design trends.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw silk from Bangalore's silk markets (KR Market area) or directly from Karnataka sericulture farms. First 50-saree batch requires ₹4-6L for yarn, dyes, and materials. Establish relationship with 2-3 trusted dyers in Bangalore for bulk orders at ₹80-120/meter.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 master weavers in Shidlagatta via revenue-sharing model (60% weaver, 40% entrepreneur) or direct employment. Production time: 7-10 days per saree. Establish quality control checkpoint post-weaving. Consider renting small shared loom space (₹15-25K/month) near weaving cluster for design and quality oversight.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, 10 min, free) as handloom artisan MSME. Obtain IEC code from DGFT (₹500, 5-7 days) for export readiness. Register brand name (₹5-10K) and design a minimal visual identity. Obtain DC Handloom certification for authenticity and subsidy eligibility.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade and Meesho with 15-20 products; target ₹25-50K first-month revenue. Simultaneously approach 10-15 wedding boutiques in Bangalore with sample sarees on consignment. Run Instagram content around artisan stories and saree-making process. Pitch to corporate gifting platforms (Giftmyemotions, PerfectGift).
6
Scale
After 6 months, add custom/bespoke offerings (10-15% price premium). Expand to Flipkart, Jaypore, Craftsvilla. Hire 1 designer to create new motifs monthly. Launch WhatsApp Business and direct-message past buyers for repeat orders. Target international sales via Etsy, Shopify, or B2B export channels. Engage with export promotion councils (APEDA, AEPC) for subsidized participation in global trade fairs.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (25-35% of project cost) for registered MSME in handloom sector with employment generation.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15K credit per artisan for tool upgrades and working capital; applicable if employing registered artisans.
🏚
Karnataka Handloom Development Scheme
20-30% subsidy on machinery and raw material bulk purchases for registered handloom units.
🏚
Rajiv Gandhi Handloom Weavers Scheme (Karnataka)
subsidized raw material supply at 15-20% discount for silk yarn from state nodal agencies.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
