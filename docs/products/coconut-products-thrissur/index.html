Coconut Products ki
opportunity, Thrissur mein.

Thrissur (central Kerala, 303.2 sq km, 31.1L population) borders Palakkad and Malappuram, both major coconut-growing regions, and sits 60km from Cochin Port enabling direct export. The district's 85,000+ hectares of coconut plantations provide consistent raw material at ₹8-12 per coconut (wholesale). Cold-pressed coconut oil production clusters exist in Kunnamkulam, Vaikom, and Chavakad, where 200+ small units process 50-200 units/day each, supplying 40% of South India's organic coconut oil.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Thrissur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3 coconut oil processors in Kunnamkulam MSME cluster (area code 0487, ask via Kunnamkulam Municipality office for contact list), visit Vaikom Saturday market to check retail pricing and buyer behavior, register free Udyam (udyamregistration.gov.in) — all doable this week, ₹0 cost.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw coconuts from Chavakad wholesale market (₹8-12/nut, bulk lots 500+), or partner directly with 2-3 farmers in Ernakulam border villages (₹7-10/nut, fresher). First batch: ₹40,000 for 5000 coconuts = 1000-1200L of oil. Supplier area: Chavakad, Kunnamkulam, Vaikom.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 1200-1500 sqft unit in Kunnamkulam Food Processing Estate (₹8,000-12,000/month) or partner with existing cold-press operator (40% revenue share model). Setup cost ₹3.5-8L covers cold press machine (₹2L-3.5L used), storage, bottling jig, labels, first month rent. Alternatively, toll manufacturing with local unit at ₹8-12/L processing cost (min 500L batch).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online). Apply for Food Safety License via FSSAI portal (₹3,000-5,000, 20 days). Get AGMARK certification from Thrissur AGMARK office (₹5,000, 45 days) for export credibility. If exporting: Apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via DGFT portal (₹0, online, 3 days). GI registration for 'Thrissur Coconut Oil' possible but optional (₹50,000+ legal cost, 18-24 months, community-driven).
5
First Sale
First D2C sale: list 2-3 SKUs on Udyam Udaan marketplace (free) targeting bulk orders ₹5,000+. Simultaneously approach 3-4 Ayurveda / health retail chains (Keralites, Kottakkal, local chains) with ₹50,000-100,000 test orders. Expected first month: ₹60,000-150,000 from 10-20 orders mixed (retail + wholesale). Day 1 leads: call Thrissur District KVIC office for SFURTI group linkages.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: move to 3000 sqft unit, hire 2-3 processing staff (₹10,000/month each), invest ₹2L in 2nd cold press machine, launch premium 'cold-pressed raw' and 'filtered' SKUs. Hire 1 export manager (₹20,000/month). Exhibit at Natural Organic Product Expos (New Delhi, Mumbai) — cost ₹80,000/event. Target 40% export revenue by month 18. Seek PMEGP subsidy ₹12.5L at this scale for equipment upgrade.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
up to ₹25L loan, 35% subsidy for SC/ST/women (₹8.75L), 15% for others (₹3.75L), 5-6% interest via SIDBI banks
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹20L grant for 10-unit food processing clusters, tech upgrades, common testing facility, zero-interest working capital
🏚
Kerala Startup Mission
₹10-50L equity support for agri-food startups, mentorship, zero-interest loans for women founders via Ashraya scheme
🏚
Coconut Development Fund (CDF)
₹1-5L subsidy for modernizing coconut processing units, grant 50% of equipment cost up to ₹5L, apply via District Coconut Board office Thrissur
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

₹3.5-8L minimum. Basic setup: ₹2-3.5L for used cold press machine (new is ₹5-8L), ₹40,000 rent deposit, ₹50,000 bottles/labels/licenses, ₹1-2L raw material for first 1000L batch. PMEGP/CDF subsidy covers 35-50% for many founders, so actual out-of-pocket can be ₹1.5-4L.
No mandatory experience, but helpful: farming background (supply chain sense), food handling (FSSAI compliance mindset), or sales (if you'll handle wholesale yourself). Most successful founders here are first-gen, learned on-the-job via SFURTI training or by partnering with retired coconut oil processors for first 6 months.
PMEGP: ₹3.75-8.75L subsidy + ₹16.25-21.25L loan at 5-6% via banks. SFURTI: ₹20L grant for 10-unit clusters. Coconut Development Fund: ₹1-5L for equipment (50% of cost). AGMARK cert free in Thrissur. Udyam registration free. State schemes: Kerala Startup Mission ₹10-50L equity for women founders. Total potential support: ₹30-50L if you qualify for 2-3 schemes.
Udyam Udaan marketplace (free listing) for bulk D2C — get ₹50K+ orders within 2 weeks. Local health retailers via phone: call Keralites, Kottakkal Ayurveda outlets, ask for procurement manager. Wholesale market: attend Chavakad coconut oil wholesale market on Fridays, meet 20-30 buyers directly. Export: contact German/UK importers via TradeKey.com, minimum first order ₹2-3L.
Haan, bilkul. Cold-pressed coconut oil is high-demand in USA, Germany, UK, Canada, Australia for organic/wellness market. Process: AGMARK cert (45 days), FSSAI (20 days), IEC code (3 days, free), shipment via Cochin Port (60km, hassle-free). First export order typically ₹2-5L, happens by month 8-10. Margin: 35-50% FOB (better than domestic wholesale at 20-30%).
Honest reality: 15-20% coconut processors fail in first 2 years (price crash, quality issues, cash flow). Exit option 1: Sell equipment + goodwill to established processor at 60-70% cost. Exit option 2: Pivot to toll manufacturing (rent processing capacity to other brands, lower risk). Exit option 3: Shift to byproducts (coconut shell charcoal, coir) at lower margin but steadier demand. Thrissur advantage: 10+ buyers within 50km will absorb your inventory if you need quick exit, unlike other regions.
