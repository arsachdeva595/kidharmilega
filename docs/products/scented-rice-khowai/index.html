Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Khowai ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call Bisnupada Adhikari or visit Khowai gram panchayats this week to see five kani farms in operation — zero cost. Attend Agartala's weekly haat (Saturday Agartala Market) to meet rice traders and understand local supply-demand. Register free on Udyam MSME portal using Aadhaar.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source quality paddy seeds from Department of Agriculture, Tripura (Agartala office) or certified seed suppliers in Khowai town — 50kg seeds cost ₹8,000-12,000. Negotiate with local farmers already growing Harinarayan, black rice, or Kali Khasa for contract farming (rent paddy from them at ₹25,000-35,000 per kani for one season).
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 500 sq ft rice milling unit in Agartala's food processing cluster (₹5,000-8,000/month) or partner with established rice miller in Khowai town (20% revenue share). For first batch: cultivate or procure 2 kani paddy (yields 3-4 tonnes fresh = 2-2.5 tonnes milled rice). Manual processing costs ₹15,000-20,000 for cleaning, drying, packaging materials.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online). Apply for GI registration via Tripura Agricultural Department (₹8,000-12,000, 6-8 week timeline) if targeting premium positioning. For export: IEC registration via DGFT portal (₹500, 2 weeks). Pack in FSSAI-compliant 500g/1kg branded bags (₹1.5 per bag for 5,000 units).
5
First Sale
First sale on BigBasket seller app (₹3L inventory required) or Udaan B2B (₹50,000 MOQ). Expected first-month revenue: ₹1.2-1.8L from 500kg-1,000kg sales at ₹220-240/kg D2C price. Target 10-15 restaurant chains in Agartala/Tripura as bulk buyers (25kg bags).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue, hire one full-time supply manager and one QA person. Upgrade to 1,000 sq ft rented milling unit (₹12,000-15,000/month). Scale land under contract from 5 to 15-20 kani. Exhibit at All India Food Processing Industries Meet (AFPIM) and regional food trade shows in Kolkata/Guwahati. Build direct export relationships with Bangladesh importers through TEXPROCIL (Trade Export Promotion Council) network.
