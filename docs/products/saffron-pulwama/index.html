Buyers range from Delhi spice wholesale markets (Khari Baoli) to direct exports to North America and Europe via specialty importers.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Pulwama ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pampore saffron cluster (20 km from Srinagar) and meet 2–3 established farmers or traders at local haat (Pampore Saffron Bazaar); call Ministry of Agriculture's National Saffron Mission office in Srinagar (free consultation); register free Udyam account on udyamregistration.gov.in.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified saffron bulbs from Jammu & Kashmir Agriculture Department's seed farms (Achabal, Khrew, Pampore area) or registered nurseries like Kashmir Spice Producers' Association members; first batch of 100 kg dried saffron costs ₹8–12L depending on grade (Grade A vs Grade B); suppliers are located in Pampore town.
3
Produce / Partner
Option 1: Partner with 3–5 established farmers in Pampore for contract farming (₹50–100K per season as advance); Option 2: Lease 0.5–1 hectare irrigated land (₹30–50K annual lease) and hire local farm labor. Drying and packaging setup (small shed + drying racks + vacuum packer) costs ₹50–100K. Total production cost per kg (including labor, drying, packaging): ₹6,000–8,000.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 2 days online); GI tag is already awarded to Kashmir Saffron (you will add your brand as producer — no additional GI cost, just certification from Ministry); apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT portal (free, 3–5 days) if planning exports. Obtain FSSAI food license (₹5,000–10,000, 10–15 days) for D2C sales.
5
First Sale
First sale: Contact 2–3 specialty importers or hotel procurement teams via LinkedIn/email (0 cost); list on Amazon Seller program (registration ₹5K one-time); first order likely 5–10 kg via wholesale buyer at ₹10K per kg = ₹50–100K first month.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1–2 full-time quality checkers and 1 export manager; exhibit at India International Spice Fair (Delhi, Nov) and Gulfood (Dubai, Feb); invest in small cold storage unit (₹100–150K) to manage seasonal supply; develop 3–5 SKUs (single-origin, blends, saffron threads vs powder).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
National Saffron Mission
up to ₹50,000 subsidy per hectare for new saffron cultivation or processing units; apply via Ministry of Agriculture.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% for general, 25% for SC/ST) for processing/packaging unit; administered by KVIC/KVIB.
🏚
Jammu & Kashmir Single Window Policy
zero processing time for export licenses, saffron-specific support from J&K Agriculture Ministry.
🏚
J&K Export Promotion Scheme
10% reimbursement on export shipping, storage, and certification costs for GI-tagged products.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
