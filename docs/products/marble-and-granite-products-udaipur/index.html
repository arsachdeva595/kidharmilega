Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Udaipur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Udaipur's main marble clusters in Fatehpura and Khimsar areas; spend time at Makrana quarries (3 hours from Udaipur) to understand raw material sourcing and quality grades; connect with existing exporters through Udaipur Chamber of Commerce to learn pricing, certifications, and export protocols.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw marble and granite blocks from Makrana quarries (₹8-15L per truck load of 10-12 tons depending on quality grade); establish relationships with local quarry owners; first batch sourcing cost ₹15-20L for mixed quality stones for testing.
3
Produce / Partner
Either lease a 500-1000 sqft workshop in Fatehpura industrial area (₹5-8L per year) and invest in CNC cutting machines, polishing equipment, and skilled artisans, or partner with existing stone processing units on revenue-sharing basis (20-30% commission). Train 3-5 artisans on finishing and quality control.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSE account within 48 hours online; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office for export; obtain stone quality certifications (ISO standards); register trademark for brand name at IP office; get GST registration; obtain stone export license if applicable.
5
First Sale
Start with B2B outreach to 50+ architects and contractors via LinkedIn and industry directories; list product catalogs on TradeKey and Alibaba for export inquiries; launch Instagram and Pinterest showcasing finished pieces and project galleries; partner with local interior designers for commission-based referrals; approach real estate developers with sample offerings.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range (tiles, countertops, sculptures); open showroom in Udaipur city (₹10-15L investment); hire sales team for pan-India B2B outreach; establish 2-3 additional production units or partner workshops; invest in digital marketing (Google Shopping, FB Ads targeting international buyers); pursue ISO certifications for quality assurance; explore bulk contracts with 5-star hotel chains and luxury housing projects.
