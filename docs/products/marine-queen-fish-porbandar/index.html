Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Porbandar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Porbandar Fish Landing Centre and Fish Market near the harbor to understand daily catch patterns, pricing, and quality grading. Meet with local fish merchants at Veraval harbor (50km away, major fish trading hub) and fish exporters to learn market dynamics. Study cold chain requirements and export documentation at nearby seafood export associations in Porbandar and Veraval.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Queen Fish directly from Porbandar Port's fish auction at 4-5 AM when boats dock; typical first batch cost: ₹1.5-3L for 500kg from local fishing cooperatives. Establish relationships with 2-3 fishing boat operators for consistent supply. First shipment sourcing cost approximately ₹2-2.5L including ice, packaging, and transportation.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with local ice factories and cold storage facilities near Porbandar harbor for immediate post-catch processing. Collaborate with existing seafood processing units in Porbandar or Veraval for cleaning, filleting, and freezing (available at ₹8-12/kg processing fee). Alternatively, set up own small cold storage and basic processing unit near the harbor with capacity of 1-2 tonnes/day.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) classification as fish processor/exporter. Obtain IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from nearest DGFT office in Rajkot or Ahmedabad. Get FSSAI registration for seafood business. Register trademark for brand name with IP office. Obtain fisheries department certification and export certificate from Port Authority.
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and Blink with minimum order aggregation. Approach 15-20 premium restaurants and hotels in Ahmedabad and Mumbai with samples and wholesale pricing. Create WhatsApp Business account for direct B2B inquiries. Contact seafood export companies in Gujarat for bulk supply contracts. Launch Instagram and Facebook pages showcasing fresh catch daily.
6
Scale
After month 6, set up own cold storage facility (₹20-30L investment). Expand to 3-4 export markets via DIT (Department of International Trade) export schemes. Develop value-added products (fillets, marinated portions) to increase margins. Hire dedicated logistics manager to manage pan-India delivery. Partner with 2-3 additional fishing boats for consistent 2-3 tonne/day supply. Target ₹15-20L monthly revenue by month 12.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general, 50% for SC/ST) for establishing seafood processing/export business
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
up to ₹10L collateral-free loan for working capital and equipment in seafood sector; apply through authorized banks
🏚
Gujarat Marine Fishery Policy 2021
capital subsidy up to 35% for cold chain infrastructure and fishing vessel upgrades; apply via Fisheries Department
🏚
Gujarat Seafood Export Promotion Scheme
duty drawback and export incentives for seafood products; coordinated through Port Authority and DGFT
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
