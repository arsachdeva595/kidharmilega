Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — West Jaintia Hills ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Lakadong village cluster and connect with West Jaintia Hills District Agricultural Office (Jowai). Attend Meghalaya spice conclave. Study existing exporters like Meghalaya State Cooperative Marketing Federation and Spice Board India nodal office.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contract with local farming cooperative in West Jaintia Hills to source 5-10 tonnes fresh rhizomes (₹80-100 per kg). Partner with farmers or join APMC Jowai. First batch cost ₹5-8 lakhs. Ensure GI certification documentation.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish drying sheds (500 sqft, cost ₹2-3L) or partner with existing processors in Jowai town. Use solar dryers for quality. Target 70% yield (fresh to dry). Alternative: Contract manufacturing with certified GI processors in district.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (online, free). Apply for IEC code from DGFT Shillong regional office (₹1000). File trademark for brand name. Obtain organic certification from ECOCERT or LACON (₹30-50K). Acquire GI label usage rights from Spice Board.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon/Flipkart with 'Lakadong GI Certified' tag — offer 500gm and 1kg packs at ₹400-600. Cold-email international spice traders via TradeKey and Global Sources. Partner with health retailers (Patanjali distributors, local Ayurveda shops). Build landing page with COA and curcumin testing certificates.
6
Scale
Scale to 50+ tonnes annually by FY2. Add value-added products (golden milk, turmeric lattes). Export via Spice Board's APEDA certification to USA/EU. Establish brand showroom in Guwahati. Invest in automated packaging and cold chain (₹50L). Target B2B contracts with supplement brands.
