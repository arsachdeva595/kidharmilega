Chanderi Handloom ki
opportunity, Ashoknagar mein.

Ashoknagar district in Madhya Pradesh sits 280 km south of Delhi and 320 km north of Mumbai, making it a logistical sweet spot for Indian and export markets. The Chanderi handloom cluster spans 3 primary towns—Chanderi, Ashoknagar city, and Mungaoli—with 1000+ registered weavers and 200+ active looms producing 150-200 sarees daily. Raw material—mulberry silk, zari, and dyed cotton—arrives from established suppliers in Karnataka, Tamil Nadu, and local Madhya Pradesh mills, with zero transport distance to artisan cooperatives.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Ashoknagar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chanderi town market on any Saturday; walk the Loom Road area and talk directly with 3-4 active weavers about production timelines, per-piece costs, and quality variance. Call District Industries Centre (DIC) Ashoknagar — free consultation on ODOP schemes. Register free on Udyam (udyamregistration.gov.in) using your Aadhaar. Cost: ₹0, doable in 3 days.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Chanderi town suppliers (silk: ₹400-600/meter, zari: ₹80-150/meter, cotton: ₹120-200/meter) or direct from Tamil Nadu silk mills via Ludhiana trade brokers. First batch of 20 sarees = ₹80K-120K material cost. Artisan weaving cost per saree = ₹1500-3500 depending on complexity.
3
Produce / Partner
Model 1: Partner directly with 2-3 established weavers in Chanderi (zero rental cost, they own looms, you provide yarn + ₹1500-2500/saree labor). Model 2: Rent a 400 sq ft workshop in Ashoknagar city (₹8000-12000/month) and lease 2-3 looms (₹2000-3000/month per loom) for in-house production. Start with Model 1 to reduce overhead; scale to Model 2 after first ₹20L revenue.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 30 mins). Apply for GI registration as Chanderi Handloom through MP Handicrafts Board (₹5000 filing fee, 4-6 month process—already approved, leverage it). If exporting: apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (₹2500, 5-7 days). Get bank loan under MUDRA Shishu scheme (₹50K free, zero collateral) for working capital.
5
First Sale
First sale: list 10 sarees on Etsy (free listing, 6.5% commission); target 'Indian handloom sarees' search terms. Expect first order by week 3-4. First-month revenue: ₹25K-40K (2-3 orders at ₹12K-18K each). Parallel: email 15 boutiques in Delhi/Mumbai with product photos + wholesale pricing (₹7500/piece). Close 1-2 orders within 45 days.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire 1 part-time operations manager (₹15K/month); add 4-5 additional weavers; apply for SFURTI subsidy (₹25L cluster support, 6-month application); exhibit at India Handloom Expo (Delhi, Feb), Craft Mela (Mumbai, Dec), Vibrant Gujarat (Jan). Build WhatsApp broadcast list (1000+ metro customers) for seasonal collections. Partner with 10-15 boutiques across metros for weekly orders.
