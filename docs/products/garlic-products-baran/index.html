Garlic Products ki
opportunity, Baran mein.

Baran sits in southeast Rajasthan's Malwa plateau (21-23°N latitude, 75-76°E longitude) with black-to-brown fertile soils suited for garlic, located 180km south of Jaipur and 120km north of Indore, creating a natural hub between Rajasthan and Gujarat spice markets. The district's garlic cluster is concentrated in Chhabra, Kapren, and Baran city tehsils, with approximately 8,000-10,000 small and marginal farmers producing 400-600 tonnes yearly. Buyers include Indore's Khanda Mandi (major spice aggregation point), Delhi's Azadpur Mandi, Mumbai's VASHI, and direct institutional buyers (restaurants, food processors) across central and western India.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Baran ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3 garlic farmers in Chhabra tehsil (numbers available from Baran Agricultural Department) and ask about their yield per acre, input costs, and best season to source. Visit Baran city haat on Monday/Friday to observe garlic trading volumes and prices. Attend zero-cost online session by ICAR (Indian Council of Agricultural Research) Indore on garlic post-harvest handling — register on icar.org.in. Zero cost, doable in 1 week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Primary supply: directly contact 15-20 farmer groups in Chhabra or Kapren (addresses from Baran Farmer Producer Organization, Baran city). Expect ₹18-22 per kg for raw garlic (June-July harvest). First batch: 5,000 kg costs ₹90,000-110,000. Backup supply: Indore's Khanda Mandi (36km away) trades 200+ tonnes daily; negotiate for 2-5 tonne weekly supply at ₹19-23/kg in off-season.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent 1,500-2,000 sqft food-grade unit in Baran Industrial Estate for ₹8,000-12,000/month (processing + storage). Option B: Partnership with existing spice unit owner in Baran (20+ units available, split revenue 60-40). Needed: basic peeling/drying racks, weighing machine (₹15,000), packaging sealer (₹25,000), storage bins. Total equipment: ₹80,000-150,000. Working capital for first 3-month cycle: ₹200,000.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 10 minutes, udyam.gov.in). Apply for FSSAI Food Business License (₹100-500 application fee, 7-10 days approval for processing unit; gets you distribution rights). If exporting: apply for IEC number via DGFT portal (₹0 cost, 7 days). Optional: GI registration support through Rajasthan State Organic Board (free consultation, helps with branding premium). Total formal costs: ₹1,000-2,000.
5
First Sale
First sales: List on BigBasket Seller Hub or Blinkit Seller Center (platform takes 20-25% commission). First order size: 50-200 units of 500g packs. Expected first-month revenue: ₹40,000-80,000 at ₹120-150 per 500g pack. Alternatively: Call 5 organized retail buyers (Reliance Fresh, Aditya Birla outlets) and offer trial supply (10 kg weekly, ₹60/kg wholesale). Institutional buyers (canteen contractors, food processors) respond fastest to samples + GST invoice.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (target month 10-12): hire 2-3 part-time sorters/packers (₹8,000/month each). Invest ₹80,000-150,000 in cold storage unit (4-5°C, 2 tonne capacity) to extend seasonal supply. Shift to semi-automated peeler (₹300,000-400,000) to reduce labor costs by 40%. Attend Surajkund Craft Fair (Haryana, Feb) and Mumbai Trade Fair to meet 50+ bulk institutional buyers. Partner with 2 export trading houses (APEDA-registered) to add export channel. Target 5-7 distributors across Rajasthan, Gujarat, MP.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme): ₹10-25L subsidy for garlic processing unit (25% of project cost, max ₹10L); apply via district industries office
🏛
MUDRA Loan (SHISHU scheme): ₹50,000 unsecured loan for sole proprietor, no collateral, via bank; useful for initial inventory and equipment
🏚
Rajasthan State Organic Certification Subsidy: 50% cost reimbursement for FSSAI and organic certification (Jaivik Rajasthan scheme); up to ₹50,000 per unit
🏚
Rajasthan Export Promotion Scheme: GST refund of 1-2% on FOB value for APEDA-registered garlic exporters; managed by Rajasthan State Minerals Development Corporation
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
