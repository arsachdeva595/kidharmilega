South West Garo Hills possesses ideal tropical climate with high rainfall (250-300cm annually), volcanic red soil rich in potassium, and altitude range of 600-1200m that creates perfect conditions for pineapple cultivation. The region has 40+ years of established pineapple farming heritage with over 15,000 hectares under cultivation, making it India's largest pineapple production zone.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — South West Garo Hills ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established pineapple clusters in Tura and Gasuapara blocks (top production areas). Connect with SWGH Agricultural Department in Tura town. Attend monthly farmer meet at District Agriculture Office. Interview 5-10 successful pineapple cultivators to understand yield (25-30 tons/hectare), crop cycle (18-22 months), and current market rates (₹15-25 per kg wholesale).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified pineapple crowns/saplings from Garo Hills Horticultural Cooperative Society (₹8-12 per sapling). For 1 hectare trial: 4,000-5,000 saplings = ₹50,000-60,000. Procure organic manure from local composting units in Tura (₹200-300/quintal). First-batch cost for 0.5 hectare = ₹80,000-120,000 including land preparation, saplings, fertilizer.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with experienced farmers in Tura block for contract farming or joint venture (70-30 split). Alternatively, lease agricultural land (₹1.5-2.5 lakhs/hectare/year) and hire local laborers (₹300-400/day). Use drip irrigation systems (₹100,000-150,000 initial investment). Establish relationship with Meghalaya State Agricultural Department for technical guidance and subsidy eligibility.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (MSME) under 'Fruits & Vegetable' category at msme.gov.in (free, 1 hour online). Obtain IEC Code from nearest DGFT office in Shillong (₹500, 10 days processing). Register brand name with IP India if processing value-added products. Get FSSAI certification for processed products (₹5,000-10,000). Obtain state agricultural produce market license from Tura Mandi.
5
First Sale
Launch on Blinkit/JioMart Fresh with 10kg weekly supply to Tura/Shillong hubs (₹25-30/kg retail). Create WhatsApp Business catalog for bulk B2B buyers (traders, restaurants, hotels). List on organic e-commerce platforms (Natureland, BigBasket Organic) targeting metro consumers. Partner with 2-3 export trading companies in Kolkata/Assam for Bangladesh-Nepal corridor. Attend Tura Weekly Agricultural Market on Saturdays to establish direct buyer relationships.
6
Scale
By month 8-10, expand to 2-3 hectares using reinvested profits. Set up basic processing unit (juice/dried product) to capture ₹50-60/kg margins instead of ₹5-10/kg wholesale. Establish contract farming with 15-20 additional farmers. Launch direct subscription boxes to corporates in Bangalore/Pune via BigBasket B2B. Apply for APEDA export promotion incentives once exports reach ₹10L annually. Explore pulp export to juice manufacturers in South India.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy for agricultural processing units with 5-6 employee job creation.
🏛
APEDA Export Promotion Scheme
50% subsidy on airfreight for pineapple pulp/processed products exports, minimum ₹10L annual exports.
🏚
Meghalaya Agricultural Business Promotion Scheme
₹3-5 lakh subsidy for value-added agri-product production units.
🏚
Meghalaya Horticulture Development Scheme
₹40,000-50,000 per hectare subsidy for pineapple plantation on marginal lands.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
