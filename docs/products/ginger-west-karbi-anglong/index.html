opportunity, West Karbi Anglong mein.

Singhasan Hills in West Karbi Anglong district sits at 500-700m elevation with 2,600 hectares dedicated to organic ginger cultivation—three-fourths of all ginger produced in Assam state. Raw material supply is immediate and abundant with 10,000 registered farmers practicing certified organic methods. Major export corridors to Canada, Spain, UK, Germany, France, and Middle East are established, with historical annual exports of 50 MT raw ginger to European countries documented until 2016.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — West Karbi Anglong ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Singhasan Hills in West Karbi Anglong and meet 3-4 ginger farmers directly — call Assam Agricultural Department office in Diphu to arrange farm visits (free). Attend Bokajan haat on market days (weekly) to observe raw ginger pricing, quality grading, and trader networks. Zero cost, 1-2 weeks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source seed ginger (Aizol or Nadia variety) from established farmers in Singhasan Hills cluster via Assam Horticulture Mission. First batch: 500kg seed ginger costs ₹60,000-75,000. Supplier contact via Karbi Anglong Agriculture Extension Office. Sowing window: March-April.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Partnership): Partner with 2-3 established farmers in Singhasan Hills for lease of cultivation area (₹20,000-30,000/hectare/season) + shared harvest revenue split 50-50. Option B (Direct): Rent 0.5-1 hectare land in ginger-growing zone (₹50,000-100,000/season), hire 3-4 seasonal labourers (₹8,000/month each), manage organic manure + weeding. Total cost range: ₹3.5-8L for one season including labour, inputs, tools. Harvest January; storage facility needed (₹50,000 for basic cold storage shed).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in — 15 mins). Apply for GI Certificate of Origin from Intellectual Property Appellate Board using existing 2015 GI tag on Karbi Anglong ginger (₹5,000-10,000 legal fee, 2-3 months). Obtain Organic Certification from accredited body (APEDA, IFOAM list) — ₹15,000-25,000, 1-2 months. If exporting: Apply for IEC (Import-Export Code) from DGFT online (free, 3-5 days).
5
First Sale
First sale via BigBasket Seller Hub or Amazon Seller Central: list 100g/250g dried ginger powder at ₹95-125/pack. Minimum order: 50 units (cost ₹2,500-3,000). Expected first order: 200-500 units in month 2-3. First-month revenue: ₹15,000-25,000 (conservative). Alternatively, reach out to Cochin spice exporters directly (email + cold call) for 10kg test batch orders at ₹140/kg.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1-2 full-time operation staff (₹12,000-15,000/month each), lease 2,000 sq ft processing unit (₹30,000-40,000/month in Diphu), install mechanical dryer (₹1.5-2L one-time), purchase vacuum packing machine (₹80,000-1L). Exhibit at India Food Forum (September), Gourmet Foods India (June), and GI Products Trade Fair (Delhi). Approach modern trade chains (Nature's Basket, Whole Foods style stores) with B2B terms.
