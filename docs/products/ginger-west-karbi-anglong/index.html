Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — West Karbi Anglong ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Karbi Anglong's key ginger clusters in Denkanikotta and Diphu blocks; connect with 10-15 established ginger farmers to understand cultivation cycles, yields (20-25 tonnes/hectare), and seasonal labor costs; study APMC rates at Guwahati mandi over 3 months
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified ginger seeds from NCIPM or Jorhat Agricultural University (₹80-100/kg); negotiate farm leases in Denkanikotta (₹15,000-30,000/hectare annually); buy farmyard manure locally (₹200-300/tonne); first-batch cost for 0.5 hectare ≈ ₹1,20,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 local farmers under contract farming model with 15% premium on APMC rates; invest in basic drying shed (₹50,000-80,000) and storage facility; hire seasonal labor (₹300-400/day); consider co-operative membership in Karbi Anglong Farmers Cooperative
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam for agricultural processing (MSME status); obtain IEC code from DGFT for export-readiness; file FSSAI license for value-added products; trademark brand name with IP cell; get organic certification if targeting premium markets (₹30,000-50,000 for first 3 years)
5
First Sale
List fresh ginger on AgriMarket and GreenDaam platforms; launch dried ginger powder on Meesho, Amazon Accelerator, and Flipkart Wholesale; contact spice traders at Guwahati's spice complex; approach 5-10 ayurvedic companies (Himalaya, Baidyanath) with samples; build WhatsApp B2B network with local retailers
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 2-3 hectares with farmer partnerships; launch ginger tea blends and extract products with co-packing partner in Assam; establish direct export channel through APEDA to Japan and Korea (target ₹5L in first year); apply for SFURTI cluster grant to build shared processing unit in Diphu
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy-linked term loan for agro-processing units
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster development grant up to ₹40 lakhs for spice processing clusters in Northeast
🏚
Assam Accelerated Agribusiness & Agro-Industries Development (AAAD)
10% subsidy on agricultural equipment and processing machinery
🏚
Assam State Agriculture Department's Organic Farming Scheme
50% subsidy on organic certification and inputs for certified farmers
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
