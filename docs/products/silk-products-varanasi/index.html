opportunity, Varanasi mein.

Varanasi sits in eastern Uttar Pradesh on the Ganges, 320 km from Delhi and 650 km from Mumbai, with direct access to raw silk suppliers through established networks in Tamil Nadu and Karnataka. The city's three major silk clusters—Godaulia, Bhelpura, and Chinnagar—employ 50,000+ artisans across 5,000+ looms, producing everything from classic Benarasi sarees to contemporary dress materials and home furnishings. Buyers span tier-1 metro boutiques, international luxury retailers, NRI diaspora, and increasingly digital-first brands like Karagiri and Suta that have built ₹10-50 crore businesses by modernizing the supply chain.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Varanasi ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Godaulia silk cluster market (behind Vishwanath Gali) on any weekday morning, spend 2 hours talking to 5-10 weavers and traders, collect 3 business cards from suppliers. Call Ministry of Textile ODOP helpline 1800-2333-222 for free district briefing. Register free on Udyam.gov.in as MFG-TEXTILE. Attend the monthly All India Artisan Meet at Varanasi Silk Board office or message @VaranasiBureauOfDevelopment on WhatsApp for cluster visit dates.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source first batch from Godaulia traders (Bhelpura Road area has 30+ wholesale cloth merchants). First sample batch of 10-15 sarees in mixed designs costs ₹40,000-80,000 depending on zari weight and silk quality. Establish relationship with a primary supplier who can guarantee quality consistency, delivery timelines (7-14 days for ready stock, 21-30 days for custom orders). Request samples from 2-3 suppliers before committing.
3
Produce / Partner
Two models: (A) Stock-based—rent a 300-400 sq ft shop/storage in Godaulia or Chinnagar (₹5,000-15,000/month) to stock 50-100 sarees and manage fulfillment yourself. (B) Artisan partnership—work directly with 2-3 master weavers who create bespoke pieces on order (zero inventory, 20-25% markup on artisan cost). Start with model B (₹0 capital), shift to model A after first 10 sales (₹150-250K capital for stock + shop). Production happens with looms; you handle quality inspection, finishing, packaging.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (5 mins, free, online). Apply for GI-tagged Varanasi silk product certification through Varanasi Silk Board (₹500-2,000, 30-45 days). Open a business bank account (₹0, just Aadhaar + PAN). If exporting: register for IEC code at Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) online (₹0, 7 days). Register business name with district trade authority (₹1,000-3,000, 10 days). Get GST registered if turnover projected >₹20L (online, 5-10 days). Total legal cost: ₹2,500-6,000, 45 days.
5
First Sale
First sales: list on Meesho (free commission, 25% marketplace commission) and Instagram Shop (link in bio). Post 8-10 high-quality photos of your first 5 designs (phone + natural light, ₹0). Message 20 boutique owners in Delhi-Mumbai with your catalog (via email/WhatsApp). First order expected within 2-4 weeks, typical size ₹15-30K (3-5 sarees). Post one customer testimonial + styling video per week on Reels. Expected first-month revenue: ₹30-80K if D2C, ₹80-150K if you land one boutique order.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time account/logistics coordinator (₹15-20K salary) and 1 part-time designer/photographer (₹8-12K). Move to 800-1000 sq ft shop with stock of 150-200 sarees. Launch your own branded website (₹30-50K one-time). Apply for SFURTI scheme grant (up to ₹20L) to upgrade to 4-6 looms if you expand into production. Attend Suratex trade fair (January, Surat) or Craft India Delhi expo (May, Delhi) for B2B network. Scale to ₹30-50L/year within 18 months via multi-channel (D2C 40%, wholesale 35%, export 15%, B2B 10%).

Where to Set Up

Infrastructure ready hai — plot lo aur shuru karo

Varanasi Silk Cluster - Godaulia & Bhelpura
Historic handloom cluster with 2,500+ looms concentrated in Godaulia, Bhelpura, and Chinnagar areas. Shops and workspaces of 200-500 sq ft available for rent at ₹5-15K/month. Direct access to raw silk suppliers, finished goods traders, zari merchants, and dye houses within 500m radius. Existing tenants include individual weavers, family businesses, and small retail outlets.
Craft Village
Varanasi Handloom Export Promotion Council (HEPCO) Facility
Govt-aided facility offering shared workspace (100-300 sq ft at ₹3-5K/month), export documentation support, quality testing labs, and access to international buyer networks. Monthly export assistance trainings held.
MSME Cluster
Bhelupur MSME Industrial Cluster
Emerging cluster with plot sizes 1000-2000 sq ft available at ₹8-12L one-time for lease (10+ years), with common power supply, water, and waste management. Suitable for larger production units or integrated retail-manufacturing setups.

₹5-25 lakh loan at 5% interest for new MSME setup, 35% subsidy for Gen category, 10% for OBC

Uttar Pradesh One District One Product Scheme
₹5,000-10,000 per artisan annual support + export incentive 10% on FOB value for first 2 years

50% subsidy on raw material purchase up to ₹1 lakh for registered handloom units

Downside risk is real: handloom demand is seasonal (peaks Oct-Feb for weddings, dips Apr-Aug), and competition from machine-made silk is increasing. Exit option: sell remaining stock to Godaulia wholesalers at 10-20% discount, return loaned artisans to pool, rent out shop space if locked in longer. Realistic dropout rate in first year: 25-30% of new entrants. Mitigation: start lean, validate D2C channel before heavy stocking, maintain strong supplier relationships for easy pivoting.
