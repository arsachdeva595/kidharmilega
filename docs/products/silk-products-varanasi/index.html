The district's deep-rooted traditional knowledge, access to quality raw silk, and established supply chains for zari and dyes make it the natural epicenter for silk production. The GI tag validates the authenticity and market premium these products command globally.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Varanasi ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Varanasi's Shivala, Godaulia, and Madiyahun clusters; spend 2-3 weeks with master weavers; study Banarasi Silk Association records; understand GI certification benefits and market trends via Ministry of Textiles reports
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mulberry silk yarn from Bangalore silk merchants (₹800-1200/kg); procure gold zari from Surat suppliers (₹3000-5000/kg); natural dyes from Varanasi local suppliers; negotiate first batch of 50-100 meters at ₹60,000-80,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered weavers in Varanasi through SVAZ (Silk Varanasi Association) or SITCOA; start with 5-10 skilled artisans on revenue-share model; set up quality control at your premises or hire QC expert; establish production schedule for 200-300 meters/month initially
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online (MSME registration within 2 hours, free); obtain IEC code from DGFT portal for exports; trademark brand name at IP office (₹4500-9000); get GI mark permission letter from District ODOP officer; obtain GST registration (18% textile category)
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho and Myntra marketplace; create Instagram and TikTok content showing weaving process; list on Etsy for international buyers; partner with 2-3 ethnic wear boutiques in metros; run Facebook ads targeting wedding season (₹10,000-20,000/month budget); offer first 50 customers 10% discount to build reviews
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 15-20 weavers; develop seasonal collections (wedding, festive, everyday wear); invest in logistics partnership for faster delivery; hire designer for custom orders; apply for export credit from ECGC; explore B2B wholesale to international importers; launch experiential pop-ups in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 free skill training, ₹1 lakh interest-free credit for registered craftspeople and descendants
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy-linked loan for startups (30% subsidy for general category, higher for SC/ST/Women/NE)
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Handloom Development Policy 2020
subsidy on power looms, raw material support, export incentives up to 5% of FOB value
🏚
Bunkarhari Sambal Yojana (Uttar Pradesh)
direct cash assistance to registered handloom weavers (₹12,000/year) and pension schemes; government-backed employment linkages
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
