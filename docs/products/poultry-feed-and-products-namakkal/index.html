Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Namakkal ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Namakkal zone poultry cluster (Tiruchengode Road area) on any Saturday. Call 5-10 active farms asking about their feed costs, supplier contacts, and seasonal price cycles. Register for free on Udyam portal and join Tamil Nadu Poultry Farmers Association WhatsApp group. Zero cost, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source maize from Perundurai APMC market (45km away, bulk rate ₹28-32/kg in season) and soya from local crushers in Namakkal town (₹55-62/kg). First batch of 500kg premix costs ₹12,000-15,000. Meet suppliers at Namakkal APMC at 5am on Tuesday/Thursday auctions.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with contract arrangement with 1-2 existing farms (500-1000 birds total) — you supply customized feed, they handle birds and sales. Or rent 2000 sqft shed near Tiruchengode Road (₹8,000-12,000/month) for in-house broiler rearing. Feed and bird costs per batch (6 weeks, 2000 birds): ₹3.2-3.8L. Biosecurity setup: ₹1.5-2L one-time.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 15 mins online). Apply for TN Startup subsidy under Tamil Nadu Startup and Innovation Mission (30% capex reimbursement, up to ₹25L). Get FSSAI food license (₹5,000-8,000). If exporting, get IEC code (₹0 online) and APEDA registration (₹2,000). Total regulatory cost: ₹7,000-35,000. Timeline: 4-6 weeks.
5
First Sale
First sale: partner with 1-2 local haat vendors (Namakkal Sunday market, Tiruchengode weekly market) and 3-4 urban delivery platforms (Blinkit, Swiggy Instamart). First order: 500 dozen eggs minimum (₹3,000-4,000 cost, ₹5,200-6,500 revenue). Expected first month: ₹45,000-65,000 revenue at 60-80% capacity utilization.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 operations manager (₹18,000/month), 2 farm techs (₹12,000 each), 1 sales coordinator (₹15,000). Add feed milling machine (₹4-5L) to reduce COGS by 8-12%. Participate in APEDA export promotion haat (quarterly, Salem) and GCC buyer meetings in Chennai. Invest ₹2-3L in cold chain infrastructure (coolers, ice) to access export-grade certification.

Real risk: oversupply in summer = egg prices crash below ₹4.2/egg. You lose money if your cost is ₹5/egg (as per August 2023 data). Exit: sell birds to meat processors at ₹70-100/bird (one-time loss), shut operation, recover 40-50% investment through asset sale (feeders, coolers, sheds resold). Downside: ₹2-4L loss possible if you start at ₹8L and hit bad season. But 70% of Namakkal farms survive because they diversify into feed milling or value-added products (egg powder, poultry manure).
