Sandstone Tiles ki
opportunity, Gwalior mein.

Gwalior district in Madhya Pradesh is built on geological formations rich in natural sandstone, with quarries scattered across the region ensuring negligible raw material procurement costs. Stone Park Old Cantonment and MPIDC Industrial Area City Center together house 35 production units producing 17+ varieties of stone products (tiles, slabs, statues, jaali, benches, waterfalls, etc.), creating a fully integrated supply chain of suppliers, artisans, and exporters. The cluster actively exports to Gulf countries (UAE, Saudi Arabia, Qatar) with established shipping and payment channels.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Gwalior ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Stone Park Old Cantonment cluster in Gwalior (30-40 min from city center) on a Wednesday morning — walk through 5-6 unit workshops, talk to owners about tooling, suppliers, export process. Cost: ₹0 (public area). Call GM District Trade and Industry Centre Gwalior at 07512390046 for list of active exporters. Attend next Gwalior Chamber of Commerce meeting (free) to network with existing tile manufacturers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source sandstone blocks directly from quarries in Gwalior district (Morar, Bhitarwar area) at ₹800-1200 per cubic meter, or buy pre-cut tiles from 3-4 established suppliers in Stone Park Old Cantonment at ₹40-100 per sq ft depending on finish grade. First batch: ₹1-2L for 100-200 sq ft mixed inventory of tiles and cladding.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 500-1000 sq ft workshop space in MPIDC Industrial Area for ₹5K-12K/month with power connection available. Partner with 1-2 semi-skilled stone cutters from the existing cluster (available at ₹8K-15K/month wages) rather than buying ₹50L+ machinery. Start with design, finishing, packing, and quality control; outsource bulk cutting to cluster artisans. Initial setup: ₹2-4L (rent deposit + basic tools + packaging materials).
4
Brand & Register
Register on Udyam portal (free, online, 10 mins) to get MSME classification. File GI registration application for Gwalior sandstone tiles through Government of India's Geographical Indication registry (₹5K-10K legal cost, 6-12 month timeline, priority since ODOP product). Apply for IEC code (Importer-Exporter Code) with Directorate General of Foreign Trade if targeting exports (free, online, 7 days). Get FSSAI or relevant product certification if selling decorative elements.
5
First Sale
List 20-30 product SKUs on IndiaMART with high-quality photos (₹0, free tier available). Post 3-4 process videos + finished installation photos on Instagram Reels targeting #architecturedesign #interiordesign #sandstonetiles #wallcladding (₹0, organic reach). First order likely from regional architect or builder: ₹30K-80K, delivery in 10-15 days. Expected first-month revenue: ₹50K-150K from 2-3 orders if actively prospecting.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (roughly 4-5 large projects), hire a dedicated sales/design consultant (₹25K/month) and 1-2 full-time artisans (₹12K-15K each). Invest ₹3-5L in tile-cutting CNC equipment to reduce outsourcing costs. Exhibit at India EXPO MART (Delhi), International Architecture & Design Expo (Mumbai), or Gulf Building Show (Dubai) — ₹1.5-3L per show. Start exporting via partner distributor in UAE or direct container shipments; target ₹20L+ monthly turnover within 18 months.

Government Schemes

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy for manufacturing units up to ₹25L project cost; processing fee ₹5K, eligibility for ODOP products
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
up to ₹10L unsecured business loan at 8-10% interest for MSME manufacturing startups; collateral-free, approval in 7-10 days
🏚
Madhya Pradesh ODOP Support Scheme
Export promotion grant up to ₹2L for first overseas shipment, exhibition subsidy of 50% for ODOP-certified units
🏚
MP Skill Development Scheme
Free stone-cutting and stone-design training for 3-6 months, wage subsidy for first 100 days of employment
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
