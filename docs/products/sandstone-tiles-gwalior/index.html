Gwalior district sits atop naturally rich sandstone deposits with centuries-old quarrying expertise and established supply chains. The district has a legacy of stone craftsmanship, proximity to major construction hubs in central India, and access to skilled labor. Architectural demand from across India and exports make it an ideal production base.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Gwalior ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gwalior's active quarry clusters near Morar and Bhind Road; connect with 8-10 existing tile manufacturers via Gwalior Chamber of Commerce; study processing units in Agra (2 hours away) for best practices; attend Stone & Tile India Expo.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw sandstone blocks directly from Morar quarries (₹40-60/cft), cutting compounds from Delhi suppliers (₹2-4L for 6-month stock), and polishing tools from Jaipur stone tool dealers; budget ₹12-15L for first 3-month raw material and consumables inventory.
3
Produce / Partner
Lease 5,000-10,000 sq.ft. workshop space in Gwalior's industrial area (₹8-12/sqft/month); hire 8-12 skilled tile cutters (₹12-18K/month each); partner with local contractors for machinery setup (multi-blade saws, polishing units); target 500-800 sq.ft. daily production capacity within 3 months.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, 10 min), obtain IEC code from Gwalior DGFT office for exports, secure MSME registration, apply for ISO 9001 certification (₹2-3L), register trademark with IP Office India, open business GST account.
5
First Sale
Launch B2B outreach via ArchiDesign, BuildDirect, and Indiamart platforms; create portfolio with 15-20 project photos; contact 50 architects on ArchiDesign; pitch 20 real estate developers in Delhi-NCR via LinkedIn; secure first 2,000 sq.ft. order within 60 days via personal outreach.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish showrooms in Delhi and Mumbai (₹20L combined); hire sales team for corporate tie-ups; launch Instagram/Pinterest with designer testimonials; secure ISO 14001 for eco-conscious buyers; explore export via trade bodies; introduce 3-4 new finishes (honed, brushed, flamed) to expand average order value 30-40%.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% of project cost) for manufacturing units in MSME sector
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
up to ₹10L unsecured business loan at 8-10% interest for working capital and machinery
🏚
Madhya Pradesh MSME Clustering Scheme
subsidized cluster infrastructure, common facility centers, and 15% subsidy on machinery for stone/tile manufacturing units
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Technology Upgradation Fund
25% subsidy on modern tile cutting and polishing machinery for registered MSME units
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
