Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jayashanker Bhupalpally ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Jayashankar Bhupalpally agricultural extension center (under ICICLE project) and connect with farmer cooperatives in Tandur and Narayankhed taluks. Attend the Hyderabad Spice Market (Begum Bazar) to understand wholesale dynamics and pricing. Interview 5-10 established chilli farmers to learn seasonal cycles, yield expectations (15-20 MT/hectare), and pest management strategies.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium hybrid seeds (Indra, LCA-334) from Indian Seed Development Association suppliers in Telangana for ₹8,000-12,000 per hectare. Purchase organic manure and bio-fertilizers from ICIMOD-certified suppliers. First-batch cost for 1 hectare: ₹60,000-75,000 (including seeds, fertilizers, tools, labor for 90-120 day crop cycle).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing farming collectives in Bhupalpally mandal (3-5 farmer groups with 50+ hectares combined) via contract farming model with guaranteed buyback at ₹4,000-5,500/quintal. Alternatively, lease 2-5 hectares directly and hire local agricultural labor (₹300-400/day). Engage District Agriculture Department for subsidized input schemes and crop insurance (PMFBY).
4
Brand & Register
Register business under Udyam scheme (micro-enterprise for agri-processing). Obtain IEC code from customs for export eligibility. Secure FSSAI license for value-added products (chilli powder). Register under APEDA for spice exports. Create brand identity emphasizing 'Telangana Red Gold' positioning with GI application roadmap for future differentiation.
5
First Sale
Launch on Agritech platforms (Ninjacart, BigBasket for Restaurants, AgroMart.com) targeting restaurants and food manufacturers in Tier 1 cities. Simultaneously establish direct export via Global Trade Point (GTPI) portal and contact international spice importers in Germany and Vietnam. Secure one bulk order (₹5-10 L) from a regional spice company (e.g., MDH, Everest) within first 3 months.
6
Scale
Post first 6 months, expand to 10 hectares through additional farmer partnerships and establish a small processing unit (drying, grinding) to capture value-add margins. Target 3-4 export contracts worth ₹50L+ annually. Develop private label products for modern retail chains (Big Basket, Amazon Fresh). Build supply relationships with 15-20 food processing units across South India.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L grant for spice processing and export business setup, 35% subsidy for general category
🏛
APEDA Scheme for Processed Food Products
financial support for setting up spice processing units, quality certification, and export promotion up to ₹50L
🏚
Telangana Agriculture Mission
₹5,000-10,000 subsidy per hectare for high-value crops including chillies with assured irrigation support
🏚
Integrated Horticulture Development Mission (IHDM)
financial assistance up to ₹2L for value-added processing units, cold storage facilities, and export-linked production
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
