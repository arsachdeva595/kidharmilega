Zari and Zardozi ki
opportunity, Budaun mein.

Budaun district in central Uttar Pradesh (285 km from Delhi, near Bareilly cluster) historically produced zari-zardozi textiles using imported golden/silver threads and beads sourced through established supplier networks. The cluster, once employing 3 lakh artisans across 200+ units in 2010, has contracted to fewer than 100 manufacturing units and approximately 1 lakh skilled workers by 2023. Current production (₹10 crore/year) serves domestic wedding/bridal market and Israeli religious textile importers, with potential in Middle Eastern luxury export.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Budaun ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bareilly cluster (35 km from Budaun city) — call 2-3 established zardozi workshops via NIC Budaun directory (budaun.nic.in), ask to observe production for 1-2 hours free. Attend a local haat or mela in Budaun town to see finished products. Register free Udyam account on msme.gov.in. Zero cost, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Order first batch (50-100 pieces of dupatta/shawl blanks for embroidery) from fabric suppliers in Bareilly — Budget ₹40,000-60,000. Source golden and silver thread spools from established dealers in Budaun city or from importers in Delhi (₹15,000-25,000 for initial stock). Beads and sequins from wholesale markets in Chandni Chowk Delhi or online suppliers — ₹10,000-15,000. Total first batch: ₹65,000-100,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 300-400 sqft workshop space in Bareilly cluster for ₹5,000-8,000/month, hire 2-3 experienced artisans (₹250-350/day wages from local pool), manage production yourself. Option B: Partnership with existing artisans — place orders with 4-5 independent embroiderers working from home, pay per piece (₹200-400 per embroidered dupatta), avoid fixed overhead. Start with Option B (₹0 rent) for first 3 months, scale to Option A at ₹10L/month revenue. Equipment cost: manual embroidery frames, needles, threads holder ≈ ₹5,000-10,000.
4
Brand & Register
Udyam registration (free, 5 mins online), GST registration if turnover > ₹20L (₹0 cost, 1 week). If exporting to Israel: apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at DGFT portal (₹0, 2 weeks). For international credibility, pursue GI registration for Budaun Zari-Zardozi (under DC Handicraft Ministry support) — typically ₹50,000-100,000 and 18-24 months, but may be subsidized. Trademark your brand name at IPO (₹4,500-9,000).
5
First Sale
First sales channel: Instagram shop + WhatsApp direct sales to wedding planners and bridal stores (free setup). First order target: 10-15 embroidered dupattas/shawls to 2-3 bridal boutiques at ₹1,200 wholesale per piece (cost ₹500-600). Expected first month: ₹15,000-25,000 revenue from 15-20 pieces if you work through existing contacts. Join Meesho or Flipkart Seller as backup (no upfront fee). Post 10 sales, pitch to larger brands.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: move to formal 500 sqft workshop in Bareilly, hire dedicated embroidery supervisor + 5-6 artisans, implement quality control system. Attend India Handicraft Expo (Delhi), Export Summit (Mumbai) to meet bulk buyers. Apply for SFURTI subsidy (₹25L for 4-5 artisan groups under DC Handicraft Ministry) to expand production capacity. Build a B2B catalog with 30+ designs for designers and retailers. Consider hiring a sales manager for wholesale accounts.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 toolkit subsidy + ₹5L MUDRA loan at concessional rate for registered artisans and their employers, no collateral
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹25L per cluster for equipment, workspace, training; DC Handicraft manages, 4-5 artisan groups eligible
🏚
Uttar Pradesh One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
5% subsidy on GST for registered units, technology upgrades support (₹2-5L), export promotion subsidy (5-10% of FOB value for first 5 shipments)
🏚
UP Handicraft Development Corporation (UPHDC) Purchase and Marketing Support
guaranteed procurement from registered artisan groups, 10-15% premium over market rate for first 2 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
