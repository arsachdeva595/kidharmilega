Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Thane ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dombivali and Badlapur textile clusters to understand production standards, machinery, and costing. Meet 5-10 existing manufacturers at Thane Chamber of Commerce. Study pricing on Fibre2Fashion and TradeKey platforms. Attend India Textiles Expo or IITF in Delhi.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton from Gujarat Cotton Association or Indore wholesale markets (₹80-120/kg). Purchase dyes from Thane-based chemical suppliers like Chemsol or Colorants India (₹2-5L for initial batch). Buy notions (buttons, zippers) from wholesale markets in Nalasopara. First batch procurement: ₹15-25 lakhs for 500-1000 units.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established manufacturers in Dombivali Industrial Area or start with contract manufacturing at units in Badlapur. Alternatively, lease 2000-3000 sq ft space in Kalyan Industrial Estate (₹8-12/sq ft monthly) and hire 8-12 skilled tailors. Use CNC cutting machines and industrial sewing units already available in the cluster.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in) within 5 days. Obtain IEC code from DGFT (icegate.indiaport.gov.in). Register trademark with IP India (₹4500-9000). Open MSME bank account with collateral-free MUDRA loan eligibility.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Seller Central and Flipkart Business with private label or brand SKUs. Approach wholesale buyers via IndiaMART and TradeKey. Send samples to local retail chains (Westside, Reliance Trends). Partner with 2-3 trading houses for bulk export orders. Target corporate bulk orders via LinkedIn and local B2B networks.
6
Scale
Increase production capacity by 40-50% after 6 months using PMEGP subsidy. Build direct distribution in Tier-2 cities (Pune, Nagpur, Aurangabad). Launch own e-commerce website with paid search ads. Expand to seasonal collections and private label contracts. Explore sustainable fabric lines (organic cotton, recycled polyester).
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs grant (25-35% subsidy) for manufacturing units in textile sector
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
up to ₹10 lakhs unsecured loan for micro-enterprises in textile manufacturing
🏚
Maharashtra MSME Sector Development Scheme
5% interest subsidy on term loans up to ₹1 crore for registered MSME textile units
🏚
Maharashtra Export Promotion Scheme
3-5% subsidy on freight costs and export documentation for textile exporters from the state
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
