Garments & Textiles ki
opportunity, Thane mein.

Thane district comprises 4214 sq km and is the third-most populous in Maharashtra, with industrialized talukas in Kalyan, Ulhasnagar, Bhiwandi, and Ambernath forming a contiguous textile cluster. Cotton and polyester raw materials arrive via NH highways from Gujarat and Andhra Pradesh suppliers, with Bhiwandi acting as a 2,000+ unit manufacturing hub. The district's 27 km coastline and proximity to Mumbai Port (25 km) enable direct garment exports to USA, UAE, and Europe; domestic buyers access units via Dadar-Bhuleshwar wholesale fabric markets 30 km south.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Thane ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bhiwandi textile cluster on a Wednesday (largest market day) — walk through Narpoli Road and Tajpur Road, call 5 garment unit owners (cold calls, ask for 30 min visit), attend a free FICCI textile meetup in Kalyan (check FICCI Thane calendar), register free Udyam account online at udyamregistration.gov.in. Cost: ₹0, 1-2 days of your time.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton/polyester blends from Bhiwandi wholesalers (Narpoli Road area) or directly from Kandla Port suppliers (Gujarat, 120 km). First batch: 500-1000 meters fabric = ₹15,000-40,000. Buy dyes, buttons, thread from Ambernath MIDC vendors. Build 2-3 supplier relationships before committing.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 800-1200 sq ft unit in Bhiwandi Industrial Estate (MIDC) at ₹8,000-12,000/month + ₹50,000 deposit. Buy one computerized sewing machine (₹80,000), cutting table, pressing station (₹40,000 total). Hire 2-3 tailors at ₹8,000-12,000/month each. First-month production cost: ₹2.5-4L for materials + labor. OR partner with an existing Bhiwandi unit as contract manufacturer (0 upfront, 15-20% margin).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 5 min online). Apply for GST registration (₹0 cost, 5-7 days). Register brand name at Office of Registrar of Trademarks, Mumbai (₹5,000-9,500, 8-12 weeks). For export: apply for IEC code at nearest DGFT office (Thane or Mumbai, ₹0 cost, 1 week). Consider GI registration support via SFURTI scheme if planning cluster-wide scale.
5
First Sale
First sale: List on Meesho Supplier or Amazon Business, or cold-call 5 local retail shops with samples. First order typically ₹10,000-50,000 from retail. Target Flipkart seller account in month 2. Expected first-month revenue: ₹30,000-80,000 from 3-4 retail orders. Ramp to ₹3-5L/month by month 4 with consistent wholesale.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire a sales manager (₹25,000/month) to hunt institutional contracts, invest ₹2L in second production unit or partner with 2-3 more contract manufacturers in Bhiwandi, apply for PMEGP/SFURTI subsidy to add cutting/finishing machinery, exhibit at Fabric India Mumbai (Feb) and Texfair Gandhinagar (July) to find export agents. By ₹30L/month: open export QC team, apply for IEC-linked ECGC insurance, target direct EU/US buyer meetings.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% of project cost for general category), 6% loan rate for 5 years
🏛
MUDRA Loan
up to ₹10L unsecured loan at 8-12% interest, repay over 5 years, zero collateral
🏚
Maharashtra SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹10-25L cluster support for groups of 20+ weavers/garment makers, machinery subsidy up to ₹5L per unit
🏚
Maharashtra Employment Guarantee Scheme (MEGS) for textile artisans
skill training stipend ₹500/day for 90 days if hiring local labor
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
