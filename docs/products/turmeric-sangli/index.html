Shivrajya Halad Utpadak Shetkari Swayam Sahayata Gat (Turmeric Farmers Cooperative)

A collective of 200+ small turmeric farmers in Sangli fought for GI tag protection from 2013-2018 to prevent product misidentification and price collapse. They filed the GI application five years before getting approval in November 2018, enabling collective branding under 'Sangli chi halad' mark. This allowed members to achieve 25-30% price premium on exports and opened EU/USA markets previously unavailable.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sangli ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sangli APMC market (open 6am-11am daily) on a Tuesday or Friday, talk to 3-4 commission agents (arhatiyas) at turmeric section, call one farmer directly via local district agriculture office. Free. Doable this week. No money needed. Get a feel for price movements, quality grades, and supply rhythm.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source dried turmeric from Sangli APMC commission agents (Miraj Road section, Gate 2-5) or direct from boiling yards in Kupwad/Miraj. First batch: 1 metric tonne costs ₹1,80,000-2,20,000 depending on grade (Alleppey vs local). Connect via APMC office or call +91-233-2322-XXX (APMC direct).
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner with an existing boiling yard in Miraj (40+ yards available)—rent grinding/packing space for ₹15,000-30,000/month, bring dried turmeric, pay ₹8-12 per kg for grinding + packing into your bags. Option B: Lease a 1000-1500 sqft unit in Sangli MSME cluster with basic processing equipment (grinder, packer, sealer) for ₹25,000-40,000/month. Equipment cost: ₹2-5L for semi-automated setup.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 1 day). File GI registration claim via Sangli District Commerce Department (free legal support available via ODOP scheme). Apply for IEC code from APEDA (₹0, online, 3-5 days). Get FSSAI license (₹8,000-15,000, 7-10 days). Get organic certification if targeting export (₹30,000-60,000, 60-90 days via USDA/EU approved certifiers).
5
First Sale
First sale: Contact a BigBasket supplier manager for fresh onboarding or list directly on Amazon Handmade/FBA (free listing, commission 15-25%). First order target: 50 kg minimum from a small ayurveda clinic or restaurant. Expected first-month revenue: ₹40,000-80,000 from 3-4 small orders. Use Sangli APMC network to find first 5 corporate buyer inquiries.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month (50 MT/year): Hire 1 quality control person (₹15,000/month), 1 sales/export coordinator (₹18,000/month). Get APEDA certification (₹50,000-80,000). Exhibit at International Spice Expo (New Delhi, Feb), Anuga (Cologne, Oct) for export buyers. Partner with 1-2 B2B aggregators (Farmer Producer Organizations in Sangli). Build direct relationships with 3-4 corporate buyers at MDH/Everest.

Government Schemes

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
up to ₹25 lakh loan, 15-25% subsidy (₹3.75-6.25L), for turmeric processing units classified as agro-processing.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹10-20L grants for cluster-level processing infrastructure, if you form a producer group.
🏚
Maharashtra ODOP Scheme
free FSSAI licensing support, GI registration legal fees waived, APEDA IEC fast-tracked, export credit guarantee subsidy 2% interest subvention.
🏚
Maharashtra Turmeric Board Support
direct purchase guarantee at MSP rates if quality meets grade A standards, priority credit at 4-6% from cooperative banks in Sangli.

Risk is real. Turmeric is commodity with 10-15% annual price volatility. Downside: prices can drop ₹30-50/kg in oversupply (happened 2019-20). You're stuck with inventory if you can't sell. Exit: sell raw stock back to APMC at spot price (lose 10-15% margin but recover 85% capital). Or pivot to higher-margin turmeric powder/capsules (but needs more capex). Best mitigation: start small (1 MT), don't lever on debt, build 3-4 buyer contracts before scaling.
