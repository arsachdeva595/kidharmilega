Gems & Jewellery ki
opportunity, Jaipur mein.

Jaipur district sits at the epicenter of India's $29.80 billion gems and jewellery export ecosystem, with 50,000+ trained artisans and established raw material supply chains for diamonds, emeralds, rubies, sapphires, and semi-precious stones. The city's three primary clusters — Johari Bazaar, Bapu Bazaar, and Malviya Nagar — house 8,000+ operational units with direct connectivity to wholesale buyers in Delhi (280 km), Mumbai (650 km), and international markets via Indira Gandhi Airport. Raw material warehouses and certified gemstone laboratories operate year-round, enabling quick sourcing and quality certification for both domestic and export orders.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jaipur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Johari Bazaar and Bapu Bazaar in Jaipur city (free entry); meet 3-4 established jewellers, ask about raw material sourcing, production costs, and buyer channels. Call Rajasthan Gems and Jewellery Association (local contact via district MSME office) to understand cluster practices. Register your business name on Udyam portal (free, online, 15 minutes) to get UDYAM ID — required for all future govt benefits.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from established warehouses in Johari Bazaar and Malviya Nagar area. Budget ₹3-5L for initial stock: loose gemstones (emeralds, sapphires, semi-precious stones), precious metals (18K gold, sterling silver). Connect with 2-3 certified suppliers; request samples and pricing. First-batch sourcing takes 1-2 weeks for delivery.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 200-300 sq ft unit in Malviya Nagar or Vaishali Nagar cluster at ₹8,000-₹15,000/month; set up basic tools (polishing, cutting, setting equipment) at ₹1-1.5L. OR partner with 3-5 freelance artisans in the cluster (you provide designs and materials, pay piece rate: ₹200-₹500 per design depending on complexity). Production cycle: 5-15 days per batch depending on design intricacy.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 1 day). Obtain IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT if exporting (₹0, online, 2-3 days, required for export shipments). Apply for GST registration (free if under ₹40L turnover, online, 1 week). GI registration not applicable to Jaipur gems/jewellery product (no GI tag currently). Get business PAN and bank account in business name (₹0).
5
First Sale
First sale: list 20-30 designs on Instagram Shop + Meesho + Udaan (free listings). Price at ₹18,000-₹50,000 range for first collections. Target first order via wedding gifting or corporate bulk inquiry (100+ pieces); expect 4-8 week sales cycle. Expected first-month revenue: ₹1-2L (10-15 pieces sold at ₹12,000-₹15,000 blended price with wholesale buyers).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 2-3 dedicated artisans, expand to second production unit (₹8,000/month rent). Add 2-3 new buyer channels (B2B jeweller networks, wedding planners). Exhibit at Jaipur Jewellery Show (annual, June-July), India International Jewellery Week (Delhi, August). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹10-25L at 35% subsidy) if scaling capital needs exceed ₹20L. Expand export by 30-40% via Alibaba, direct buyer networking in UAE and USA.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
35% subsidy on capital investment up to ₹25 lakh for manufacturing units; requires ₹5L+ investment
🏛
MUDRA Loan
Up to ₹10 lakh at 5.5-6.5% interest from SIDBI for working capital and equipment; requires business registration + basic collateral
🏚
Rajasthan Artisan Promotion Scheme
₹1-2L subsidy for skilled artisans to set up independent units in craft clusters; requires Aadhar + local residency proof
🏚
Rajasthan One District One Product (ODOP) Initiative
₹50,000-₹2L support for branding, packaging, website development, and certification for registered MSME units in Jaipur gems sector
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
