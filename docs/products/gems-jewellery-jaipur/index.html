Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jaipur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Panna Patti cluster (main cutting and polishing hub) on a Tuesday morning — talk to 3-4 unit owners and their cutters, walk through Jauhari Bazaar and Chand Pol to see raw stone suppliers, attend the monthly Jaipur Jewellery Association meetup at Chamber of Commerce (free, just show up). Call two established traders for 30-min phone calls to understand pricing and supply. Zero cost.
2
Source Raw Materials
For starter inventory: buy rough stones from Jauhari Bazaar or Chand Pol merchants (₹50K-₹2L for mixed grades); connect with cutters in Bapu Nagar for semi-finished work; establish credit with 2-3 suppliers with 15-30 days payment terms. First batch: ₹3-5L for 100-150 pieces across designs.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 200-300 sqft shop-cum-workspace in Panna Patti or Bapu Nagar (₹8000-₹15000/month); either hire 2-3 experienced cutters/polishers OR partner with 1-2 freelance artisans who work from home (pay per piece ₹100-₹500 depending on complexity). Initial setup: rent deposit + tools/fixtures ₹2-₹4L. Quality control is critical — budget time for this.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 10 mins); apply for IEC code from DGFT office Jaipur (₹500, 15 days); file GI registration for 'Jaipur Gems' or similar name (₹10000, 6-8 months, but optional — many don't do this); get BIS hallmarking if dealing in gold/silver. Hire a CA for ₹3-5K/month for GST compliance.
5
First Sale
First sale: post 10-15 high-quality photos on Instagram with hashtags #JaipurJewellery #HandmadeEmeralds; join 2-3 marketplace groups on Facebook (India Jewellery Makers, Jaipur Business Women); offer first 5 customers 10% discount to get reviews. Expected first-month revenue: ₹30-50K (5-8 orders at ₹5-10K per order). Aim to sell 10-15 pieces in month 1.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire a social media manager (₹10-15K/month), scale inventory to ₹8-10L, rent a larger 500 sqft space, hire 1 dedicated account manager for wholesale buyers, attend India International Jewellery Week (IIJW) in Delhi for export leads, apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹25L max) to scale. Participate in JJA exhibitions quarterly.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Up to ₹25 lakhs loan with 35% subsidy for manufacturing units in Jaipur; 7-year repayment
🏛
MUDRA Shishu Loan
Up to ₹50000 at 8-10% interest for first-time entrepreneurs; instant approval if Udyam registered
🏚
Rajasthan Startup Policy
₹50 lakhs grant for manufacturing startups in Jaipur district if they create 10+ jobs within 2 years
🏚
Rajasthan MSME Cluster Development Scheme
₹10 lakhs reimbursement for common facility centres in identified clusters like Panna Patti
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Honest truth: jewellery demand is stable (never zero), but competition is intense. Your main risks are: bad quality (customers won't return), bad sourcing (paying too much for stones), no sales skills (sitting idle in shop). If you fail, you can sell off inventory at 10-20% loss and recover 60-70% of setup cost. Safety net: most Jaipur owners pivot to wholesale or export when D2C slows. Real risk is getting stuck with slow-moving inventory.
