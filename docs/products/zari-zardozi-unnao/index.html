Zari Zardozi ki
opportunity, Unnao mein.

Unnao district in central Uttar Pradesh sits at the heart of India's Mughal-era gold threadwork heritage, with the Bareilly cluster housing one lakh trained artisans specializing in zari zardozi. Raw materials — bullion threads, beads, sequins, silk and velvet base fabrics — are sourced locally and from Lucknow and Kanpur textile markets, 80-120 km away. Buyers span Delhi-NCR wedding markets, Lucknow couture houses, and direct export channels to UAE and UK, making logistics manageable for both domestic D2C and international wholesale.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Unnao ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bareilly zari zardozi cluster (30 km from Unnao city); call 3-5 master artisans directly to understand current daily rates (₹250), turnaround time per piece, and quality standards. Attend a local haat or wedding expo in Lucknow (80 km) to see live buyer demand. Free; doable in 1 week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Lucknow textile suppliers (Aminabad market) and Delhi bullion traders (Chandni Chowk). First batch: ₹30,000-50,000 for 100-150 meters of mixed fabrics, threads and beads. Identify 2-3 master artisans in Bareilly willing to work on small batches (start with 10 pieces). Bulk suppliers: Ujala Fabric Works, Bareilly; Ludhiana thread wholesalers.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with artisan partnership model — no factory rent needed. Advance ₹2-5L to 2-3 trusted master artisans in Bareilly for raw materials + labor; they produce 10-15 pieces per month. Once production stabilizes at ₹10-15L/month, rent a 500 sqft shared workshop in Bareilly (₹8,000-12,000/month) to add quality control, finishing, and packaging in-house. Cost range: ₹0 (partnership-based) to ₹3L (workshop + equipment for framing, ironing, packing).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 10 mins) using artisan identification. Apply for ODOP scheme support (free training at common facility center planned under scheme; check DC Handicraft office in Unnao). If exporting, apply for IEC (₹0, online via DGFT portal). Consider GI registration filing if scaling past ₹50L annual turnover (₹10,000-30,000 filing cost; consult ODOP ministry). Timelines: Udyam 1 day, IEC 3-5 days, GI 18-24 months.
5
First Sale
First sale: Post 5-8 high-quality product photos on Instagram, tag @indianweddingseason, @bridalindia hashtags. List on Amazon Handmade (free listing); typical first order ₹8,000-15,000. Reach out directly to 10 wedding boutiques in Delhi (search Google Maps 'embroidered wedding wear Delhi') with samples; typical bulk order ₹2-5L. Expected first-month revenue: ₹1-3L from 2-3 orders if marketing is active.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time design and order manager (₹20,000/month), expand artisan network to 8-12 specialists, rent 1,500 sqft workshop (₹25,000/month). Exhibit at India International Trade Fair (IITF, Delhi), Heimtextil (Frankfurt if exporting), and regional wedding expos. Build B2B relationships with 5-10 bulk buyers (hotels, event planners, corporate gifting companies). Launch export via trade agents in Dubai and London.

Honest downside: Market size shrunk 90% in last 13 years (₹100Cr in 2010 → ₹10Cr in 2023); younger generation not entering embroidery trade. Your revenue could plateau at ₹10-15L/year if you don't build strong D2C or export. Artisan wage pressure: As your volume grows, labor cost inflation (if demand is low) can squeeze margins. Exit options: (1) Sell your artisan network and customer list to larger handicraft company, (2) Pivot to B2B bulk corporate gifting (more stable), (3) Merge with a Lucknow or Delhi textile house.
