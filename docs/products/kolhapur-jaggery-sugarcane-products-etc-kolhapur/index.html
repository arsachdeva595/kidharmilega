Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kolhapur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call Raibag cluster coordinator (contact via Kolhapur District Office website) and visit one active jaggery maker's unit on a production day — observe the gurahla setup, 3-roller mill operation, and blending process. Attend Kolhapur Agricultural Produce Market Committee haat (weekly market day) to see live pricing of colour-free (₹41/kg) vs coloured (₹47/kg) jaggery. Zero cost; takes 1 day total.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source sugarcane from suppliers in Raibag or Kagal taluks; first batch of 500kg sugarcane costs ₹4,000-6,000 depending on season (Nov-Feb is peak). Establish supplier contact through Raibag cluster artisans or local mandis. Alternative: partner with an existing Kagal-based producer as co-manufacturer for first 3 months (saves equipment cost).
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a gurahla (jaggery production unit) in Raibag cluster for ₹8,000-15,000/month, or buy used 3-roller mill and basic copper vessels for ₹4-8L. If bootstrapping, partner with an established artisan in Raibag on revenue-share (you handle sales/brand, they handle production) — typical split is 60% founder, 40% artisan. First batch of 500kg sugarcane yields 300-350kg jaggery (60-70% extraction). Production cycle: 2-3 days per batch.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 5 minutes). File GI registration as a collective producer under Kolhapur Jaggery GI or partner with existing GI-certified artisan group (saves ₹20,000-40,000 registration cost). If exporting: apply for IEC (₹0, online via DGFT portal, 5-7 days). Get FSSAI license (₹1,000-5,000, 15-30 days processing). Obtain Agmark certification for colour-free variant (₹3,000-8,000, conducted by State Agricultural Dept).
5
First Sale
First sale: approach 5-10 restaurant/hotel owners in Kolhapur with 5kg sample packs (cost ₹200-250 to produce). First order likely: 20-25kg at ₹50/kg = ₹1,000-1,250 gross. Parallel: list on Amazon/Flipkart as seller with GI certification; expect first online sale within 2 weeks at ₹200-220 per 500g pack (₹100-120 production + packaging cost). Expected first-month revenue from 10-15 hotel orders + 50-100 online units: ₹35,000-50,000 gross.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (200kg jaggery @ ₹500/kg blended wholesale): hire 1-2 production helpers (₹12,000-15,000/month each), rent a larger gurahla (₹15,000-20,000/month), invest ₹50,000 in better packaging/branding, apply for SFURTI subsidy to upgrade equipment. Exhibit at India Organic Expo, GI India Summit, or state food processing shows to lock bulk export contracts. Launch powder and kakavi variants for 15-20% revenue uplift.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan at 5% interest with 35% subsidy for SC/ST, 25% for general; loan tenure 5-7 years
🏛
MUDRA Shishu Scheme
₹50,000 unsecured loan at 8-10% interest for food processing micro-enterprises; no collateral required
🏚
Maharashtra One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
50% subsidy on equipment purchase up to ₹10 lakhs, free skill training, market linkage support through district administration
🏚
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹18 lakh grant for jaggery clusters in upgrading equipment, packaging, quality testing; applicable to Raibag cluster
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
