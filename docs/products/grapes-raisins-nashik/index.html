Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Nashik ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nashik's Grape Cluster in Dindori-Loni-Sinnar belt; connect with MANAGE (Ministry of Agriculture training center) in Nashik; join Nashik Grape Growers Association; study export protocols at Nashik Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC).
2
Source Raw Materials
Purchase quality grapevine rootstock from ICAR-Indian Institute of Horticultural Research (Bangalore) or local nurseries in Nashik (cost: ₹80-120 per plant, 1000+ plants = ₹1-1.5L for 0.5 hectare); source inputs from Nashik's agricultural input suppliers in Old Nashik.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Lease 0.5-1 hectare through Nashik's established farmer cooperatives (Nashik Agro-Export Cooperative); Option B: Partner with existing grape farmers for contract farming (15-20% revenue share); Option C: Start as raisin processor by sourcing fresh grapes from APMC during harvest season (June-Sept).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online for MSE classification (₹0); apply for IEC code from nearest ICEGATE office in Nashik; register brand name with IP India; get FSSAI registration (₹6000-12000 for small business); obtain Nashik District Agricultural Department certification for organic/conventional practices.
5
First Sale
List on BigBasket, Blinkit, JioMart for D2C fresh grapes; register with TradeKey and Global Sources for B2B export leads; connect with importers via APEDA's export database; approach local wine manufacturers for direct supply contracts; partner with Nashik's established exporters as a supplier.
6
Scale
After 6 months profitability: expand acreage by 0.5-1 hectare via NABARD agricultural loans; invest in cold-chain infrastructure (cost: ₹20-30L for small unit); apply for GODREJ cold-chain partnership; obtain ISO 22000 certification for export-grade production; diversify into value-added products (juice, concentrate, organic raisins).
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidized loan for agri-processing units
🏛
APEDA (Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority)
50% subsidy on certification, packaging, and branding for export-oriented agri-SMEs
🏚
Maharashtra Agricultural Credit Relief Scheme
0-5% interest subvention for agricultural loans to farmers and agri-entrepreneurs
🏚
Mukhyamantri Krishak Samman Yojana
direct income support to agricultural entrepreneurs; registration bonus and additional support for GI-tagged ODOP products
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
