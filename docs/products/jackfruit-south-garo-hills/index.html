Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — South Garo Hills ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gasuapara jackfruit cluster and Bagmara village in South Garo Hills to study existing farmer cooperatives like Tura Fruit Growers Association. Conduct 10-15 farmer interviews on yield, harvest cycles, and pricing. Study Assam Organic Mission database for certification protocols.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh jackfruit directly from farmer cooperatives in Gasuapara (₹400-600 per fruit, seasonal June-August). Establish relationships with 3-5 orchard owners for consistent supply. First batch of 100 fruits costs approximately ₹45,000-60,000. Identify local packaging suppliers in Tura for cost optimization.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with Tura Food Processing Centre or establish micro-processing unit with food safety certification. Start with simple products: dried jackfruit chips and pulp paste. Hire 2-3 skilled workers experienced in fruit processing. Get FSSAI Small Scale category license (₹5,000-8,000).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in as food processing micro-enterprise. Obtain IEC code from DGFT Guwahati office (₹500). Register brand name with IP office. Get FSSAI manufacturing license. Suggested registration in Tura, South Garo Hills district office.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and BigBasket in Guwahati/Meghalaya region with minimum 50 kg stock. Set up WhatsApp business for B2B orders to hotels in Shillong. Approach local organic stores and fruit retailers in Northeast cities. Create Instagram content showing processing and farming story for D2C.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product line to jackfruit candy and ready-to-eat packets. Secure contract with major food service distributor covering Northeast hotels. Export to Bangladesh and Myanmar via registered food exporters. Invest in cold chain storage facilities. Apply for SFURTI support for cluster-level infrastructure.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for micro-enterprise in food processing with 25-35% beneficiary contribution.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹50-100 lakh for cluster-level infrastructure and common processing facilities in South Garo Hills.
🏚
Meghalaya State Agricultural Mission (MSAM)
₹5-10 lakh subsidy for farm value-addition enterprises and equipment for food processing units.
🏚
Meghalaya Scheduled Tribes (ST) Entrepreneurship Development Programme
₹8-15 lakh for ST entrepreneurs in food and agro-processing sectors.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
