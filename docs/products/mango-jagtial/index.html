Mango ki
opportunity, Jagtial mein.

Jagtial district in Telangana has 17,700 hectares dedicated to mango cultivation across 18 mandals, producing 1,24,900 tonnes of Benishan variety annually. The Chalgal Agricultural Market Committee (AMC) is the centralized wholesale node linking growers to buyers in northern India (Delhi, Chandigarh, Ahmedabad, Kolkata, Pune, Lucknow, Gwalior) and international markets. Jagtial mangoes arrive after the first week of April with lower water content and superior shelf-life compared to Andhra Pradesh varieties, commanding premium prices in wholesale markets.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jagtial ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chalgal Agricultural Market Committee on a Wednesday (main trading day). Call 2-3 mango wholesale traders there (ask AMC office for contacts). Register free Udyam via udyamregistration.gov.in. Attend one weekly haat at Jagtial town to observe grade, price, and buyer behavior — zero cost, takes 3 days total.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Benishan mangoes directly from Chalgal AMC wholesale traders during April-July season. First batch: 2-3 tonnes costs ₹40,000-₹60,000 (₹15-20/kg wholesale rate). Supplier area: mandals around Metpalli, Tandur, Manakonda within 30km of Chalgal. Negotiate directly with trader or through AMC agent — avoid middlemen.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with local fruit consolidators or cold storage operators near Chalgal AMC rather than building from scratch (saves ₹8-10L). Rent 1000 sqft cold storage + sorting unit for ₹8,000-12,000/month (April-July only). Grade, wash, and pack mangoes into 5kg/10kg consumer boxes. Setup cost: ₹3-5L (grading equipment, crates, packaging, initial inventory). Alternative: buy from 5-6 established traders and repackage under your brand (asset-light model).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online). Apply for GI certification through State Agricultural Ministry if branding as 'Jagtial mango' variant (₹15,000-30,000, 6-9 month process). Obtain FSSAI registration (₹5,000-10,000 for small business). If exporting: obtain IEC code from DGFT (₹5,000, online process, 5 days). Get Tetrapak/corrugated box printing with brand name (₹30,000 for first 5,000 units). Total registration cost: ₹50,000-70,000; timeline: 2-3 months.
5
First Sale
First sale channel: BigBasket or Jio Mart vendor registration (apply online). First order: negotiate ₹50,000-1,50,000 seasonal order (May). Expected first-month revenue: ₹3-5L (selling 2-3 tonnes at ₹150-180/kg D2C vs ₹18-20/kg wholesale). Secondary: approach Reliance Fresh category buyer in nearest metro (Delhi/Bangalore); pitch seasonal summer contracts worth ₹10L+.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 2-3 part-time sorters (₹8,000/month each). Add second cold storage unit (₹12,000/month). Expand to 3-4 metro retail chains simultaneously. Invest ₹80,000 in FMCG trade marketing (in-store demos, sampling). Explore export via fruit consolidators (Agritech export houses in Bangalore/Chennai) — they handle logistics, you supply ₹50L+ annual volumes at 8-12% margin. Exhibit at AAFEX (Asia Agri Food Export Summit) or Agricex Mumbai to acquire foreign buyers.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy on machinery for cold storage, processing units (max ₹25L subsidy for SC/ST, 15% for general category)
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
up to ₹10L working capital for seasonal mango trading with zero collateral, 7-8% interest via SIDBI banks
🏚
Telangana Rythu Bandhu
₹4000-5000 per acre soil health support (if you own land) — applicable if contracting with mango farmers
🏚
Horticulture Mission for North East and Himalayan States (HMNH) / State Horticulture Marketing Fund
₹50,000-2L for export consolidators and fruit processors with cold chain infrastructure
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
