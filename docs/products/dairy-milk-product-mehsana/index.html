Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mehsana ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mehsana District Cooperative Milk Producers' Union (MDCMPU) headquarters, observe operational dairy farms in Unjha and Visnagar taluks, attend dairy seminars at Gujarat Dairy Development Board, and analyze competitor ghee brands like Amul, Maha Ghee, and local brands in Mehsana market.
2
Source Raw Materials
Establish relationships with registered dairy farmers in Mehsana district (100-200 liters daily milk sourcing ₹3000-4000/day), source stainless steel dairy equipment from Vadodara industrial suppliers (₹2-3L for basic setup), procure culture and additives from approved dairy suppliers in Ahmedabad. First batch cost: ₹40000-60000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established dairy processing units in Unjha or Visnagar through lease/contract arrangements, or establish own small processing unit with dairy licensing from District Animal Husbandry Department. Ensure FSSAI certification (₹15000-25000) and dairy plant registration. Start with contract manufacturing model before own facility.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam at udyamregistration.gov.in (free), apply for IEC code through DGFT portal (₹10000), obtain FSSAI license (₹15000-25000), register trademark with IP India (₹5000-10000), design packaging with local design studio in Mehsana (₹20000-40000), and get BIS certification for ghee if pursuing premium positioning (₹30000-50000).
5
First Sale
Launch on Blinkit, Zepto, and local Gujarati e-commerce platforms with minimum orders of 500 units, establish direct B2B relationships with 20-30 local sweet shops and restaurants, register with AmazonFresh and BigBasket for bulk supply, create Instagram and Facebook presence with farm-to-table storytelling, and partner with 2-3 established dairy exporters for international channels.
6
Scale
After 6 months profitability, expand product line to paneer, butter, buttermilk, and flavored ghee variants, open pickup points in Ahmedabad and Gandhinagar, apply for PMEGP expansion grant, establish dedicated e-commerce website, hire experienced dairy technician to maintain quality standards, and explore co-packing opportunities with established FMCG brands.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs grant for new dairy micro-enterprises, 10-25% subsidy based on beneficiary category
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
collateral-free loans up to ₹10 lakhs for dairy business expansion with 0-2% concessional interest rates
🏚
Gujarat Dairy Processing & Value Addition Scheme
25-50% subsidy on processing equipment and packaging for registered dairy units
🏚
Gujarat Start-up Seed Fund Scheme
₹2-5 crores grants for agri-tech and dairy enterprises with innovation focus
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
