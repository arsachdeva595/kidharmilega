Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — West Champaran ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Marcha Chura production clusters around Bettiah and Raksaul town in West Champaran. Spend 2-3 weeks embedded with 3-4 established producers. Interview 15+ local retailers in Bettiah market. Study competitors on Blink It, Swiggy Instamart, and regional platforms. Cost: ₹15,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source flattened rice (chura) directly from rice mills in Motipur block (₹45-55/kg), green chilies from weekly mandis in Bettiah (₹30-40/kg), and mustard oil from local oil pressers (₹120-140/liter). For first batch of 500 kg finished product, budget ₹85,000-120,000. Establish relationships with 2-3 chura suppliers and 1-2 seasonal marcha growers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established Marcha Chura artisans operating from home kitchens or small licensed units in Bettiah (easier scale-up than starting solo). Alternatively, apply for shared kitchen space at Bettiah Food Processing Centre or rent a 400 sq.ft. FSSAI-compliant unit (₹8,000-12,000/month). Negotiate trial batches with 2-3 producers before committing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSE under MSME (Food Processing category). Apply for IEC code from regional DGFT office. Obtain FSSAI license (Proprietorship: ₹500, Partnership: ₹2,000). Design packaging highlighting Champaran heritage + GI-pending narrative. Trademark brand name at IPO office. Total cost: ₹18,000-25,000.
5
First Sale
Launch on JioMart and Swiggy Instamart West Champaran test regions (fastest adoption for regional snacks). Simultaneously, approach regional distributors supplying Biharnese groceries to Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore. Partner with 5-10 regional kirana chains in Bettiah for in-store placement. Run influencer campaigns with Bihar-focused food bloggers on Instagram (₹8,000-15,000 budget). First month sales target: ₹50,000-80,000.
6
Scale
After ₹25L annual sales, invest ₹50-80L in a 1,000 sq.ft. semi-automated facility (cost: ₹75-100L). Scale to 5-ton/month production. Launch 3-4 product variants (no-oil, whole grain, millet chura). Build export channels via Apex & regional APEDA networks. Hire 8-10 staff. Target ₹2-3 Cr turnover by Year 3.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (25% of project cost) for food processing MSEs in backward districts like West Champaran
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹10-50L cluster development grants for food clusters; applicable to Bettiah snack clusters
🏚
Bihar Startup Policy 2022
₹10L seed fund + 5-year GST exemption for registered food enterprises in ODOP categories
🏚
Bihar Food Processing Venture Capital Fund (BIFPCF)
up to ₹50L debt-linked capital for food processing units in ODOP products; 4-year moratorium
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
