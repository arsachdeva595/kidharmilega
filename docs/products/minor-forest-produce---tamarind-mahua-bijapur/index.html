Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bijapur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bijapur's primary forest clusters in Abhjanpur and Usri blocks; connect with Van Dhan Vikas Samiti groups and forest cooperative societies; study tamarind and mahua prices at Bijapur's wholesale markets and online APMC portals.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source directly from tribal collectors in Bijapur's forest villages (Bhansi, Juragarh); negotiate bulk purchase of 500-1000 kg tamarind (₹30-40/kg) and 50-100 kg mahua flowers (₹80-100/kg); first-batch cost approximately ₹50,000-80,000 for mixed batch.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with Bijapur's existing fruit processing units (Bijapur Agro-Industrial Park) or establish small drying and packaging facility using solar dryers; train 2-3 tribal women as primary production staff; obtain FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) certification from NAFPO guidelines.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam through udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC from DGFT Nagpur; obtain FSSAI license (₹2,500-5,000); trademark brand name with IP India; ensure GI exploration if quality premium justifies investment.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, Flipkart Wholesale, JioMart, and BigBasket; create Instagram/Facebook with forest-to-table storytelling; partner with regional organic chains like Naturally Yours and Nature's Basket; pitch to Ayurvedic companies and health supplement brands via LinkedIn B2B outreach.
6
Scale
Expand to 5-10 tribal collector groups across Bijapur; invest in food processing certification and capacity building; enter institutional buyers (corporates, schools); develop co-branded products with established wellness brands; explore EU/USDA organic certification for export premiums.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for food processing micro-enterprises with 25-35% subsidy on machinery and setup.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
clustered development support up to ₹25 lakh per unit for tribal food processing clusters.
🏚
Chhattisgarh Krishi Vikas Yojana
financial assistance up to ₹5 lakh for value-addition in agricultural and forest produce with interest subvention.
🏚
Chhattisgarh Van Dhan Vikas Yojana
direct support for tribal forest produce collectors, minimum procurement guarantee, and skill training for product development.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
