Mango Processing ki
opportunity, Krishnagiri mein.

Krishnagiri's agro-climatic conditions are ideal for cultivating Totapuri, Neelam, and Banganapalli mango varieties with superior sugar content and aroma. The district has a 40+ year legacy in mango cultivation across 28,000+ hectares, with established supply chains and a concentration of food processing units creating natural economies of scale. Proximity to Bangalore and Chennai markets ensures steady demand and export logistics infrastructure.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Krishnagiri ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Krishnagiri Mango Cluster in Hosur industrial zone and meet existing processors like Coorg Spice and local MSME units. Study seasonal pricing at Krishnagiri Fruit Market (peak harvest: May-July). Talk to 5-10 export traders at Chennai Port to understand quality specs and pricing. Attend the annual Krishnagiri Agri-Expo to benchmark competition and identify supplier networks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh mangoes directly from local farmers or Krishnagiri Fruit Market during peak season (May-July) at ₹3,000-5,000 per quintal. Contract 5-10 farmers for supply stability. For first batch (10 MT): approximately ₹75,000-100,000 for raw mangoes + ₹40,000 for packaging materials and spices. Use local cold storage units (₹500-800/MT/month) to manage seasonal glut.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing FSSAI-certified co-processing units in Hosur Industrial Estate (e.g., facilities available at ₹5,000-8,000 per batch/day) initially rather than owning equipment. Alternatively, secure land near Krishnagiri town and set up a 2,000 sq ft processing unit with boiler, pulping machine, and packaging line (₹18-25L capex). Hire experienced supervisors from closed/scaling units and train semi-skilled labor locally.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (online, free) and apply for IEC code via DGFT (₹0 cost). Obtain FSSAI license for food processing (₹4,000-6,000, 3-4 weeks). File trademark with IPAB (₹9,000-15,000). Get ISO 22000 certification (₹30,000-50,000, 6-8 weeks) for export-grade credibility. Design packaging with Tamil Nadu Packaging Technology Centre consultation.
5
First Sale
Launch on Krishnagiri B2B portals and contact regional distributors (Canco, Future Supply Chain). Post samples on Tradeindia and Global Spice marketplace. Approach Blinkit, BigBasket, Flipkart food channels with FSSAI + ISO docs. Cold-email export traders in Chennai and Bangalore mentioning HS codes (2007.99, 2008.99). Attend India FoodEx annually. Offer MAP of ₹200-300/500g retail to set D2C pricing credibility.
6
Scale
After first 6 months and ₹50L+ turnover, invest in company-owned processing facility with multiple product lines (pulp, pickle, juice concentrate). Secure export certification (ISO 9001, allergen testing). Hire dedicated export officer and enroll in APEDA's export schemes. Launch branded D2C website with Instagram + Google Ads targeting health/wellness keywords. Distribute through 200+ retail stores in Tamil Nadu and Karnataka. Apply for SFURTI cluster development grants for group infrastructure.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L grant (35% subsidy for SC/ST/women) for food processing unit setup in Tier 2 districts
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster-level infrastructure support including cold storage, sorting, processing equipment (₹5-10L per unit)
🏚
Tamil Nadu Agricultural Mechanization Scheme
subsidy up to 50% on processing equipment purchases for MSME food processing units
🏚
Tamil Nadu Food Processing Policy (2021)
SGSY loans at 4% interest, VAT exemption on processed food products for 3 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
