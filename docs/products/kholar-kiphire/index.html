Kholar ki
opportunity, Kiphire mein.

Kiphire district sits at 1500-2000m elevation across 1255 sq km in Nagaland's eastern reach, where cool, moist climate and naturally fertile volcanic soil create textbook conditions for kidney bean cultivation. Village cooperatives across Kiphire's 6 blocks source kholar from certified organic farms using zero pesticides, delivering 120-150 MT annually to MARCOFED collection centers. Urban buyers in Dimapur (160km), Kohima (180km), and tier-1 metros via NagaBazaar platform provide three distinct distribution channels.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kiphire ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call MARCOFED regional office in Dimapur (0385-2233XX) and ask for 2-3 farmer contact names from Kiphire Kholar cooperative. Visit Kiphire town bazaar on market day (typically Thursdays) and talk to 2-3 produce vendors about current farm-gate pricing and volumes. Register on APEDA website (free, 15 minutes) to understand export baseline requirements. Zero cost, all doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source from certified organic farms in Kiphire block villages: Sutsu, Pungro, or Chizami sub-cluster. First batch of 500kg costs ₹70,000-80,000 at farm-gate (₹140-160/kg). Cooperatives like Kiphire Organic Farmers Collective (contact via MARCOFED) offer direct access. Storage: rent 200 sq ft cool, dry warehouse in Kiphire town (₹3,000-4,000/month) or partner with MARCOFED collection center.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner directly with 2-3 organic farms for contract purchase and cleaning/grading (zero upfront cost, 8-10% commission on sales). Option B: Rent 500 sq ft processing unit in Kiphire MSME cluster or Dimapur (₹8,000-12,000/month) + basic cleaning/drying/packaging equipment (₹80,000-120,000 one-time). Employ 2 part-time workers at ₹200-250/day for grading, packing. Most founders start with partnership model.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (₹0, online, 10 minutes). Apply for GI registration support under Sampoornata Abhiyan 2.0 (contact Kiphire District KVIC for guidance, covers ₹20,000-30,000 of filing cost). If exporting: obtain IEC code from APEDA/DGFT Nagaland office (₹500, 1 week). Get organic certification copy from farm partners; if processing, apply for FSSAI license (₹500, 2-3 weeks). Total compliance cost: ₹40,000-60,000.
5
First Sale
First sale: list 100kg pack on NagaBazaar™ in B2C format (₹200/kg = ₹20,000 order value). Push via WhatsApp to 50 local health groups and nutritionists in Dimapur and Kohima (free). First institutional buyer: contact 5 local hospitals/clinics with pricing sheet (₹160/kg bulk). Expect ₹15,000-25,000 first-month revenue from B2C, ₹10,000-15,000 from first B2B order. Ramp: 3-4 weeks to ₹50,000/month if you execute steps 1-4 cleanly.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (500kg/month sales): hire dedicated sales person (₹12,000/month) to pitch to 20 tier-1 organic chains and hospital networks. Invest ₹1,50,000 in branded packaging design + printing. Set up cold storage (₹2,50,000) if targeting export (Singapore, Bangladesh buyer meetings = 1-2 trips at ₹50,000/trip). Exhibit at India Organic Expo (Delhi, Feb-March) or Natural and Organic Products Expo Asia (₹1,00,000 booth + travel). By 12-15 months: aim for ₹40-50L annual revenue across 5 channels.
