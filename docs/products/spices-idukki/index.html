Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Idukki ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pinavu spice cluster and Kumily market in Idukki; meet cooperative societies like Spice Board-registered groups; attend Kerala Spice Merchants Association meetings; study cardamom auctions at Pepperkka auction yards (Kottayam nearby)
2
Source Raw Materials
Source from registered farmer cooperatives in Thandawalam, Chithirapuram, or Kanthalloor blocks; negotiate contracts for 500kg initial batch (cardamom ₹800-1200/kg, ginger ₹60-80/kg, pepper ₹350-400/kg); first-batch cost ₹150,000-250,000 for mixed spices with certification
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing SFURTI-supported spice processing units in Idukki (drying, grading, packing facilities) or establish micro-unit with basic drying/packing setup; hire HACCP-certified manager; obtain Spice Board registration and ISO 22000 certification for exports
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) for ₹0 online; apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (₹0); trademark brand name with IP India (₹4,500); obtain FSSAI license (₹2,000-5,000); pursue ISO 22000 (₹30,000-50,000 one-time); consider organic certification from NPOP bodies (₹15,000-25,000 annually)
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Pantry and Flipkart for packaged variants; create Instagram/YouTube content about Idukki spice farming; partner with online premium portals (Bespoke India, Gourmet GlossaryIndia); contact food exporters directly via trade databases; attend India International Spice Summit quarterly
6
Scale
Secure farmer contracts for 5MT annual volumes; apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹10L-25L) for processing unit expansion; develop 3-4 SKU range (organic, ground, blended variants); hire export manager for Middle East clients; explore contract farming with 50+ farmers; build B2B relationships with 10+ food companies by month 12
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for spice processing micro-enterprise with 15-25% applicant contribution
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster development grant up to ₹50 lakh for spice processing groups in Idukki with infrastructure cost sharing
🏚
Kerala Agricultural Entrepreneurs Scheme
interest subsidy on agricultural loans up to ₹5L at 0% interest for spice cultivation and processing startups
🏚
Spice Board Certification Subsidy (Kerala Rubber Board extends)
50% reimbursement of ISO 22000, HACCP, organic certification costs up to ₹40,000
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
