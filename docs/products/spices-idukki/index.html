Idukki district sits in Kerala's Western Ghats at 600-2500m elevation, producing cardamom, pepper, ginger, and cinnamon in 8 micro-clusters (Peerumade, Thodupuzha, Vandiperiyar, Kanthalloor). Raw materials sourced from 2,000+ registered grower cooperatives within 30km radius; processing units already operational in 12 towns. Export corridor via Kochi port (140km) with established trade routes to Middle East handling 1,200+ tonnes cardamom/year.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Idukki ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call Idukki Spices Cooperative Federation office (Pinavu) — free; visit Thodupuzha spice market on any Friday (no cost); meet 2-3 cardamom growers at Peerumade farmer haat; attend Spice Board India webinar on export requirements (free registration); request sample price lists from 3 local traders — doable this week, zero cost.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source first batch (50kg trial) from Thodupuzha spice wholesale market or Peerumade grower cooperatives; cost ₹12,000-18,000 for mixed spices depending on grade; negotiate 15-20 day credit terms; request quality certifications for export-grade cardamom (ISO 9001, organic if possible).
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent 400 sq ft processing unit in Thodupuzha MSME cluster for ₹8,000-12,000/month; buy cleaner, sorter, packaging machine (₹2.5-4L); hire 2 part-time workers (₹12,000/month). Option B: Partner with existing processor in Peerumade for contract packing (₹5-8/kg markup); no capex needed. Total Option A: ₹3.5-5L; Option B: ₹1.5-2L setup.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 15 min); apply for Spice Board India Certification (₹5,000-10,000, 30 days); if exporting, apply for IEC from APEDA (₹2,000, 5 days); register brand name with state trademark office (₹5,000, 60 days). Total: ₹12,000-17,000, 90-day process.
5
First Sale
First sales via Udyam-registered small account on Udemy, Flipkart Seller Plus, or local B2B platform TradeKey; first wholesale order likely 100-200kg from regional distributor (revenue ₹25,000-35,000); launch with 5-7 SKUs (cardamom, pepper, ginger, blends); target first month ₹50,000-80,000 revenue from 3-4 bulk buyers.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 sales coordinator for B2B outreach; establish 2nd processing line; apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹10L+ subsidy) for machinery; exhibit at Spice India Expo (Kochi, annual); build WhatsApp seller community of 50+ small buyers; get GI certification for Idukki Cardamom brand (₹1-1.5L, enables 10-15% price premium); negotiate 30-45 day credit with 5-8 institutional buyers.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25-35% subsidy on machinery/capex up to ₹10L for SC/ST/women; neutral beneficiaries get 15% subsidy
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹5-15L cluster development grant if starting within Idukki MSME cluster; covers common facility centers
🏚
Kerala State Spice Industry Development Scheme
₹2L subsidy for quality certification (ISO, organic) and export-readiness training
🏚
Idukki Agricultural Export Promotion Scheme
10% subsidy on export logistics (containerization, testing) for registered spice exporters; max ₹5L/year
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
