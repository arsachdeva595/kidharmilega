Citrus Fruits (Kinnow) ki
opportunity, Bathinda mein.

Bathinda lies in Punjab's citrus belt at 29.8°N latitude with alluvial soil ideal for kinnow (soil pH 5.5-7.5), positioned 250km from Delhi and 350km from Mumbai via NH 15. The district has 2000+ hectares under kinnow cultivation with established agro-input supply chains in Bathinda City and Rampuraphul markets. Buyer access is direct to Azadpur (Delhi), Vashi (Mumbai), Bangalore City Market, and UK-EU importers via Ludhiana-based export houses.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bathinda ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Rampuraphul-Dirba cluster (40km south of Bathinda city) — call 3 established kinnow farmers (ask at Bathinda Agricultural Department office, free contact list), attend Bathinda Haat-e-Zameen agriculture meet (first Friday monthly, free entry), register free Udyam account online at udyamregistration.gov.in this week
2
Source Raw Materials
Source disease-free kinnow grafted saplings from Punjab Agricultural University (PAU) nursery in Ludhiana (₹15-25 per sapling, bulk order 500+ saplings = ₹7500-12500), or private nurseries in Rampuraphul area; organic fertilizer from Bathinda District Cooperative (₹8-12/kg). First 1-acre setup requires 111 saplings at 6×6m spacing.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 1-2 acres in Bathinda or Rampuraphul block (₹30000-60000/acre/year lease), invest ₹1.2-1.8L in borewell/drip irrigation per acre. Kinnow takes 3 years to mature, so interim cropping with lentils/moong yields ₹20000-30000/acre during wait period. Partner with local farmer-producer organizations (Bathinda FPO Ltd) for shared storage/drying facility at ₹5000/month instead of individual setup.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 30 min online), apply for Pradhan Mantri Formalisation of Micro Food Processing Enterprises Scheme (PM FME) for ₹10L subsidy if processing kinnow juice/concentrate (state nodal: Punjab Industries Department). GI registration for Bathinda Kinnow through Punjab State Horticultural Department (₹25000-50000, 12-month timeline). If exporting, register as IEC with DGFT (free, 2 weeks), open bank account as sole proprietor.
5
First Sale
First sale: Contact Azadpur Fruit Merchant Association (Delhi), schedule bulk order for 500kg minimum at ₹60-70/kg wholesale in Oct-Nov season. First direct export: reach out to UK organic importer ThaiFruits Ltd (established contact through APEDA), supply 5MT container (₹2-3L invoice). First digital sale: list on JioMart Seller Prime (commission 8-12%) starting ₹30000 initial batch.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (15-20 MT monthly sales): hire 1 farm manager (₹15000/month) + 2 harvest workers (₹300/day seasonal). Establish cold storage unit (₹15-20L investment) in Bathinda for 50MT capacity to reduce spoilage from 15% to 3%. Exhibit at Fruit & Vegetable Fair Chandigarh (Feb) and AAFEX Delhi (Nov) to lock institutional contracts. Expand to 5-acre portfolio and supply contract manufacturing partners.

Realistic downside: 3 years before harvest, so planning period is long. Pest outbreak (leaf roller, scale insect) can reduce yield 20-40% — budgeted.
