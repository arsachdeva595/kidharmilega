opportunity, Bareilly mein.

Bareilly has been a historical hub of Islamic metallic thread embroidery since the Mughal era, with a concentrated cluster of master craftsmen in areas like Shahi Baazar and Sadar Bazaar. The district's cultural continuity and established supply chains for gold and silver threads make it the natural epicenter for authentic Zari and Zardozi production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bareilly ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Zari clusters in Bareilly's Shahi Baazar and meet master craftsmen (Ostad level artisans); spend 2-3 weeks understanding stitch techniques, cost structures, and supplier networks; connect with Bareilly Chamber of Commerce and ODOP office for market data.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source gold and silver zari from established suppliers in Delhi's Chandni Chowk or Bareilly's local wholesalers (₹50,000-100,000 for initial thread stock); procure semi-precious stones and beads from Jaipur suppliers; estimated first-batch raw material cost ₹200,000-400,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish partnership with 3-5 experienced Zardozi artisans in Bareilly (on piece-rate or profit-share basis rather than employment); provide them with designs and raw materials; establish a small workspace for quality control and finishing; begin with 10-15 pieces/month to test market.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME under Handicraft category; apply for IEC code from DGFT for export readiness; register trademark for brand name at IP India; obtain GI certification awareness documentation from DC Handicraft; register on ODOP portal.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart Artisan within 2 months; approach wedding planners and designer studios in Delhi/Mumbai with portfolio; participate in Surajkund Mela (Feb) and UP Handicraft Expo; leverage Instagram with artisan storytelling content; initiate outreach to 20 boutiques in tier-1 cities.
6
Scale
After first 6 months, expand artisan network to 10-15 craftspeople; develop 3-4 signature Zardozi designs; establish B2B relationships with 5-10 apparel brands; launch WhatsApp catalog for bulk B2B orders; explore exports to UAE/UK through trade shows; hire a marketing coordinator for social media and influencer outreach.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 toolkit cost + access to credit at 5% interest for artisans; registered artisans get market linkage support.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (25-35%) for MSMEs in handicraft sector; low-interest term loan available.
🏚
Uttar Pradesh ODOP Scheme
interest-free working capital loans, subsidy on raw materials, and export promotion support for registered ODOP products.
🏚
UP Handicraft Development Scheme
free skill development training for artisans, subsidy on equipment, and market access through state trade shows.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
