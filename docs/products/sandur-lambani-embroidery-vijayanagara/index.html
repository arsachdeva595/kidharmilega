Sandur Lambani Embroidery ki
opportunity, Vijayanagara mein.

Vijayanagara district is the historical heartland of Lambani embroidery, with the Sandur taluk being the epicenter of this craft for centuries. The Lambani community has preserved and perfected this intricate needlework tradition, making the district uniquely positioned as the authentic source and custodian of this art form. The local supply chains, skilled artisans, and cultural continuity make it an ideal base for scaling this GI-tagged product.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Vijayanagara ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sandur taluk in Vijayanagara district; meet with Lambani Embroidery clusters at Sandur village, connect with Vijayanagara District Handloom Cooperative Society, study GI certification records and existing producer networks
2
Source Raw Materials
Source silk and cotton threads from wholesale suppliers in Bangalore's textile district (Shivajinagar); purchase embroidery-grade fabrics from Belgaum or Hubli textile markets; initial batch cost ₹25,000-₹40,000 for 100 sq meters of embroidered fabric stock
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with Lambani artisan groups in Sandur; negotiate per-piece production rates (₹200-₹800 per embroidered piece depending on complexity); formalize partnerships with written agreements; consider opening a small production coordination center in Sandur for quality control
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification); apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Bangalore; trademark your brand name with IP India; obtain GI licensing to use Sandur Lambani Embroidery tag; register GST
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Shopify within 2 months; approach ethnic brands like Biba, Anita Dongre, Soch for B2B partnerships; submit entries to international craft fairs (Ambiente Frankfurt, Top Drawer London); start Instagram storytelling on artisan lives and craft process
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan base to 50+ workers; open retail presence in Bangalore and Pune; partner with 3-5 major fashion e-commerce platforms; establish export logistics with freight forwarders; create tiered product lines (basics ₹1,500 to premium ₹15,000+)
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 loan at 0% interest and ₹500,000 collateral-free credit for traditional artisans and craftspeople
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% of project cost) for handloom and traditional craft businesses in General Category
🏚
Karnataka Handloom Development and Improvement Scheme
subsidy on technology upgrades, design development, and market linkage for registered handloom units
🏚
Rajiv Gandhi Handloom Weavers Scheme
interest-free loans up to ₹50,000 and social security benefits for registered handloom artisans
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
