Sandur Lambani Embroidery ki
opportunity, Vijayanagara mein.

Vijayanagara district in northern Karnataka houses Sandur Lambani embroidery in 7 villages with 450 established women artisans practicing the craft continuously. Raw materials (discarded fabrics, threads, mirrors) are sourced locally at near-zero cost, making unit economics superior to most handlooms. The cluster sits 180km from Bengaluru airport and 35km from Hampi tourist zone, providing direct access to domestic retail buyers (hospitality, wedding planners) and export freight corridors via road/rail to Chennai and Bangalore ports.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Vijayanagara ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Zero cost: Call Sandur artisan cooperative (ask Vijayanagara DC office for contact) and visit Sandur village cluster this month. Walk through Keri Tanda, Mariyammanahalli to observe production, stitching techniques, and current pricing. Visit Bengaluru's Fab India or Good Earth store to see how Lambani pieces are positioned and priced in retail.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Phase: Contact artisans directly in Sandur or through Vijayanagara Lambani Artisan Welfare Society. Discarded fabrics available at ₹10-30/kg in Sandur fabric scrap markets; threads at ₹50-200 per spool from Vikarabad Road market suppliers. First batch of 50 embroidered patches (10cm x 10cm) costs ₹1500-2500 if outsourced to artisan; 20 patchwork pieces (30cm x 30cm) cost ₹3000-5000.
3
Produce / Partner
Production: Rent 200-400 sqft workspace in Sandur or Keri Tanda (₹3000-6000/month) OR partner with 3-5 established artisans on revenue-share (40-50% to artisans, 50-60% to you). Hire one person for quality check, packing, storage (₹8000-12000/month). Monthly production capacity: 300-500 patches or 100-150 patchwork pieces depending on complexity.
4
Brand & Register
Register as sole proprietor Udyam (free, online, 15 mins via udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for GI certificate usage from Government (DC Handloom, Vijayanagara) — included in ODOP support, no cost. If exporting: Get IEC code (₹1500, 30 days via DGFT portal). TM registration for brand name: ₹9000-15000. All complete within 90 days.
5
First Sale
First sale: Create Instagram profile + upload 10-15 high-quality photos of pieces (phone camera is fine). Post reels of artisans stitching (drives engagement). First month: aim for 20-30 orders via Instagram DM + 1 wholesale inquiry to wedding planner = ₹4000-8000 revenue. List on Etsy (3.2% fee) and local craft bazaars to secure 2-3 retail stockists.
6
Scale
Scale Phase (₹10L/month trajectory): Hire dedicated social media manager + export logistics person (₹25000/month combined). Exhibit at Bangalore Craft Bazaar, Dilli Haat, NID Design Market. Approach bulk buyers (hotels, MNC corporate gifting). At ₹10L+: Launch own manufacturing unit with 15-20 artisans, expand to sarees and lehengas, apply for PMEGP subsidy (35% cap) to scale.
