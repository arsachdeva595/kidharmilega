Visakhapatnam Port handles 29.72% of India's seafood exports ($2.19 billion FY24) from a single port, processing 16,98,170 tonnes annually across frozen shrimp, fish, and squid. The Visakha Container Terminal has 6 lakh TEU capacity, 650 reefer sockets with 24-hour power backup, and real-time temperature monitoring (±5°C alert system) enabling year-round export to United States, EU, China, Japan, and Middle East. Raw material supply is guaranteed from local fishing fleets, with established APEDA-approved processing units within 10km of port.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Visakhapatnam ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Visakhapatnam Port's Port Examination Point (contact Visakha Port Authority, 0891-2545155) and Visakha Container Terminal to observe cold chain setup. Call 3-4 frozen seafood exporters listed on APEDA website (Apex Frozen Foods, Globalfish Ltd). Register free on Udyam portal. Attend Visakhapatnam Chamber of Commerce & Industry monthly buyer-seller meet. Zero cost, doable in 2 weeks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source frozen shrimp, fish, squid from Visakhapatnam fishing harbor suppliers or Port area cold storage operators (Waltair, Gajuwaka areas). First batch: 5-10 tonnes @ ₹300-400/kg shrimp, ₹150-200/kg fish = ₹15-40L. Supplier networks on APEDA-registered vendor list.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 5,000-10,000 sqft cold storage unit in Visakha Port area or Gajuwaka MSME cluster (₹1.5-3L/month), or partner with existing APEDA-approved processor for sorting/packaging (commission: 3-5%). Setup includes: IQF tunnel or blast freezer (if starting own unit: ₹30-60L), vacuum packaging machine (₹5-10L), reefer container access. Total: ₹40-100L for independent unit; ₹5-15L for partnership model.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 15 mins). Apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via DGFT portal (₹0, 3 days). Get APEDA registration (₹5,000, 10 days). Obtain FSSAI License (₹5,000-10,000, 30 days). Consider Geographical Indication (GI) registration for 'Visakhapatnam Seafood' (₹25,000-50,000, 18-24 months). Total compliance cost: ₹40-65,000, timeline: 45-60 days.
5
First Sale
First sale: Contact 3-5 US/EU importers via APEDA directory or TradeKey.com with samples (1-2 tonnes). Expected first order: 20-50 tonnes FOB ₹400-600/kg (depends on product) = ₹80-300L revenue. Platform: Direct B2B export contracts. First domestic sale: approach Spencer Retail Hyderabad, ITC Aashirvaad regional managers (2-5 tonnes). Expected: ₹10-20L first month if domestic + international combined.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: Hire quality control manager (₹40-60K/month), export documentation specialist (₹30-40K/month). Secure 2nd cold storage unit. Apply for PMEGP subsidy for expansion (20-35% capex support). Exhibit at Seafood Expo Global (Brussels) or Seafood Processing Asia (Bangkok). Build direct relationships with 5-10 repeat buyers. Invest in FSMS (Food Safety Management System) certification for premium pricing.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy for manufacturing, max ₹25L for SC/ST/Women, 20% for others; apply via SIDBI portal.
🏛
MUDRA Loan
₹10L unsecured loan at 8-9% interest via nearest bank for working capital; no collateral required for first-time borrowers.
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Marine Fisheries Policy 2024
₹2-5L subsidy per enterprise for cold chain infrastructure; apply via District Fisheries Department.
🏚
AP MSME Growth Fund
4% interest subvention on loans up to ₹100L; administered via Andhra Pradesh MSME Development Board.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
