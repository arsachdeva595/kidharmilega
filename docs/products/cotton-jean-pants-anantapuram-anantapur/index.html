Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Anantapuram (Anantapur) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Anantapuram textile clusters (Kalyandurg, Raptadu industrial area) and speak with 5+ mill owners. Attend India International Garment Fair (IIGF) in New Delhi. Study GST rates on cotton vs. synthetic blends and tariff impacts.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw denim from Tiruppur mills (₹120-180/meter) and cotton from local Anantapuram textile mills. Partner with button/zip suppliers in Bangalore. First batch cost for 500 units: ₹8-12 Lakhs.
3
Produce / Partner
Connect with established garment manufacturers in Anantapuram's Raptadu Industrial Area (40+ factories). Start with contract manufacturing (MOQ 500-1000 units). Alternatively, lease a 5,000 sq ft unit and hire 20-30 skilled tailors; expected cost ₹25-35 Lakhs for setup.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online at udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from DGFT portal. Trademark brand name at ipindia.gov.in. Get FSSAI certification if adding any value-added services. Budgeted cost: ₹1.5-2 Lakhs.
5
First Sale
List on Myntra (commission 25-30%) and Amazon India (15-18%) within 30 days. Simultaneously reach out to 20 retail chains (Lifestyle, Citi Trends, Max). Launch Instagram and YouTube with influencer partnerships (micro-influencers in fashion). Partner with 5-10 corporate procurement teams for bulk orders.
6
Scale
After first ₹2 Cr revenue, invest in company-owned retail (2-3 exclusive stores in Bangalore, Hyderabad). Develop 3-4 seasonal collections (summer, monsoon, winter, formal). Hire dedicated export team; target 30% export revenue by Year 2. Implement ERP system to manage inventory across channels.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
Grant up to ₹25L for setting up garment manufacturing unit with 10+ employee creation.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
Up to ₹75L for technology upgrades and cluster development in textile manufacturing.
🏚
Andhra Pradesh APIIC Industrial Estate Concessions
Land lease at reduced rates and subsidized power tariffs for textile units in notified industrial areas.
🏚
APCIL (Andhra Pradesh Clothtech Initiative Loan)
Subsidized credit up to ₹2 Cr at 2% interest subvention for garment exporters.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
