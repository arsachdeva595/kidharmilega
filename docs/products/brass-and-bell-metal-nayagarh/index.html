Brass and Bell Metal ki
opportunity, Nayagarh mein.

Nayagarh district in Odisha hosts 7 established brass and bell metal clusters spanning Kantilo, Bhuban, Balakati, Bhatimunda, Belaguntha, Remuna, and Tarabha, with 2,800+ registered Kansari artisans trained in lost-wax casting and conventional beating methods. Raw materials (copper, tin, zinc) are sourced locally at favorable cost due to proximity to mining regions and established supplier networks in Cuttack and Sambalpur. Key buyer markets include Bhubaneswar's institutional and retail segments (40km away), Cuttack's wholesale metalware bazaars (35km), and international ports at Paradip and Visakhapatnam (120-180km), making this ideal for domestic wholesale, direct-to-consumer, and export-oriented startups.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Nayagarh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kantilo cluster (Nayagarh town center) on a Tuesday haat or call the district Handicraft Office (0674-2221xxx) to request an artisan introduction. Spend 1-2 hours observing melting, beating, and finishing. Zero cost, doable this week. Ask about Kansari cooperative rates and typical unit costs. Also check local WhatsApp groups of metalcraft businesses in Cuttack — search 'Brass Utensil Suppliers Odisha'.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Cuttack metal suppliers (30-40 km away) — copper ₹580-620/kg, tin ₹1,200-1,400/kg, zinc ₹110-130/kg. First batch cost: ₹80,000-150,000 for 200-300 kg mixed materials (sufficient for 50-80 finished units). Supplier addresses: Brass Bazaar, Cuttack (near Raghunath Mandir) or Sambalpur mining supply shops. Build 2-3 supplier quotes before committing.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 400-500 sqft 'sala' (craft shed) in Kantilo or Bhuban cluster at ₹4,000-8,000/month; costs ₹50,000 one-time for basic furnace, stone floor, beating blocks, hand drills, mallets. Option B: Partner with 2-3 established artisans on revenue-share (40-50% margin to them for labor + materials). Pilot batch production: ₹200,000-400,000 including 3-month artisan wages, furnace setup, and raw materials. Time: 45-60 days to first saleable batch.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, 30 min online at udyamregistration.gov.in). File for GI registration support via Odisha State Handicrafts Directorate (₹15,000-30,000, 8-12 weeks). If exporting: apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via DGFT website (₹0, 2-3 weeks online). Register trademark for brand name (₹10,000-15,000, 18-24 months). Total: ₹25,000-45,000 + 3-4 months.
5
First Sale
First sale: List 20-30 hero products (cooking pots, bowls, figurines) on Etsy with professional photography (₹5,000 for photos). Expected first order in 2-4 weeks. Simultaneously: Email 15-20 home décor wholesalers in Delhi (B2B directory, Zauba, or IndiaMART) with 10-unit bulk quotes. First-month target: ₹40,000-80,000 (mix of 2-3 Etsy orders + 1-2 bulk wholesale inquiries). Use this to fund month 2 production.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: Move from artisan partnership to hiring 2-3 dedicated in-house craftspeople (₹12,000-18,000/month each); upgrade kiln to gas-fired (₹180,000); establish quality control station. Open accounts with 3-5 regional wholesale distributors (Bangalore, Mumbai, Pune). Apply for SFURTI cluster development grant (₹25-40L per unit for artisan clusters). Exhibit at Craftmark (Delhi, 2x yearly), Handmade Mela, or IITF (December) to tap institutional buyers. Scale to ₹25-30L/month by month 18-24.

Where to Set Up

Infrastructure ready hai — plot lo aur shuru karo

Kantilo Handicraft Cluster, Nayagarh
Dedicated craft cluster with 150+ registered artisan units; shared furnace facilities, tool libraries, and artisan cooperative. Individual shed sizes: 300-600 sqft. Monthly rent: ₹4,000-8,000. Existing tenants: brass makers, dhokra artisans, stone carvers. Basic utilities included.
Craft Cluster (ODOP Recognized)
Bhuban Metal Cluster, Dhenkal Tehsil
Second-largest brass cluster in district with 80+ units. Focus on large utensils and ceremonial ware. Shed sizes: 400-800 sqft. Rent: ₹5,000-9,000/month. Existing infrastructure: 2 common gas furnaces, tool rental center.
Craft Cluster (ODOP Recognized)
Balakati Brass Hub, near Bhubaneswar
Emerging cluster close to Bhubaneswar market (40 km). 45+ units focused on export-ready decorative pieces. Shed sizes: 250-500 sqft. Rent: ₹6,000-10,000/month. Advantage: nearest to Bhubaneswar wholesale buyers and Paradip port.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 tool kit subsidy + 5% interest subvention on loans up to ₹1 Lakh for registered artisans; 18% subsidy on collateral-free MUDRA loan up to ₹1 Lakh
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy on capital investment for Tier 3 districts (up to ₹10L subsidy), 35% if SC/ST entrepreneur
🏚
Odisha Handicraft Development Policy 2023
15% subsidy on machinery purchase + 12% interest subvention on bank loans up to ₹25 Lakh for registered ODOP units
🏚
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹40L cluster grant per artisan group for common facility center, training, and market linkage; Odisha has dedicated SFURTI officer in Bhubaneswar
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
