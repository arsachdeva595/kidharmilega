opportunity, Malda mein.

Malda District (3,733 sq km) sits on Bengal's Bangladesh border with 165.5 km international boundary, enabling raw cocoon imports and duty-favorable trade. Mulberry cultivation blankets 12,000+ hectares across Old Malda, English Bazar, and Kaliachak taluks, supplying the largest tribal sericulture network in Eastern India. The cluster exports 25-30% of production to EU and US markets, with 15,000+ weavers concentrated in Malda town, Kaliachak, and Bamangola haats handling 8-10 lakh meters of tussar fabric annually.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Malda ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Zero-cost: Visit Kaliachak haat on Tuesday morning (15 km from Malda town), meet 5-10 silk dyers and weavers, ask about cocoon sourcing and weaving turnaround. Call Malda Silk Cluster helpline (District ODOP coordinator) for free artisan registry. Register on Udyam portal (free, takes 20 mins online). Attend Malda Haat weekly bazaar meeting (no entry fee) to understand real buyer pain points.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cocoons from Kaliachak and English Bazar cocoon traders (₹500-700/kg, 50% quality variance). First batch: buy 30 kg cocoons (₹18,000-21,000) from 2-3 suppliers to test quality. Silk throwsters (spinning units) in Old Malda town convert cocoons to raw silk at ₹200-300/kg labor cost. Dyers in Kaliachak market offer natural dyeing at ₹150/kg for small batches.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 400 sq ft loom shed in Kaliachak cluster (₹3,000-5,000/month) with 2-4 hand looms already installed. Option B: Partner with 5-10 home-based tribal weavers on piece-rate model (₹800-1,500 per saree). Production timeline: 7-10 days for a saree from raw silk to finished product. Cost per saree: ₹1,800-2,500 (materials ₹1,200, labor ₹800-1,300).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, online, 15 mins). Apply for Malda ODOP GI registration support through District Industries Centre (DIC) — covers ₹50,000 of filing costs, 3-month process. If exporting: obtain IEC code from DGFT (₹500, 1 week) and bank import-export account (₹0, handled by bank). Get FSSAI registration if selling home products (₹3,000-5,000, 2 weeks).
5
First Sale
First sale: Post 5-10 product photos on Instagram with location tag (Malda silk, tropical tussar). Target micro-influencers (10K-50K followers) in Indian ethnic wear space with 30% commission on first 10 sales. Or: List on Etsy (30 cents listing + 3% transaction fee) targeting US/UK buyers searching 'tussar silk saree'. Expected first-month revenue: ₹40,000-60,000 from 8-12 sarees at ₹5,500 average D2C price. Cost of first batch (20 sarees): ₹45,000-50,000 (raw materials + labor + dyes).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: Hire a logistics coordinator (₹15,000/month) to manage 50+ bulk orders monthly. Establish 2-3 dedicated artisan groups (15-20 weavers each) on weekly piece-rate contracts. Partner with 2-3 wedding planners in Delhi NCR for seasonal bulk orders (₹2-5L per order). Exhibit at India Handloom Expo (New Delhi, bi-annual) and Craft Bazaar events (₹1-2L booth cost but ₹15-25L wholesale connections typical). Add B2B fabric wholesale vertical selling 500+ meters monthly to ethnic wear manufacturers.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹50 lakhs loan with 25-35% subsidy, 7-year repayment; covers machine, raw material, and working capital for textile MSMEs.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹40 lakhs per artisan group for cluster development, shared infrastructure, design training, and marketing; clusters of 30+ artisans eligible.
🏚
West Bengal Handloom Weaver Subsidy Scheme
₹5,000-15,000 per loom for new weavers, equipment upgrades, and quality certification; 2-week processing.
🏚
Bengal MSME Export Promotion Fund
5-10% subsidy on export shipment costs (freight, packaging, documentation) for registered MSME exporters; handled via District Industries Centre.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
