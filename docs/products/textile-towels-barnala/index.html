Barnala has a centuries-old tradition of handloom weaving with access to quality cotton yarn from regional suppliers. The district's skilled artisan base and low operational costs make it ideal for sustainable, high-quality towel production. Strong local infrastructure and existing textile clusters support both production and export.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Barnala ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Barnala textile clusters around Dhanaula and Burj villages; connect with Barnala Handloom Weavers Association; study competitor brands like Krishna Crafts and local cooperatives; attend Handcraft Expos in Delhi and Mumbai.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton yarn (20,000 kg for first batch) from Punjab textile suppliers in Ludhiana (cost ~₹8-12 lakh); buy natural dyes from Jaipur suppliers; negotiate bulk rates with 3-4 suppliers; first-batch material cost approximately ₹12-14 lakh.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 8-12 handloom artisans in Barnala via formal agreements; establish small production unit (500 sq ft) renting space at ₹8,000-15,000/month; implement quality control checkpoints; produce 50-100 towels per loom monthly.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small) online; apply for IEC code from Directorate General of Foreign Trade; register brand name with IP office; obtain Handloom Mark certification from Ministry of Textiles; get FSSAI approval for organic dye products.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C website with Shopify focusing on 'Artisan-Made' storytelling; list on Amazon Artisan and Flipkart Handmade with 10 SKUs; partner with 5-8 premium spa chains in NCR and Mumbai; reach out to corporate buyers via LinkedIn; launch Instagram content showcasing weaver stories.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 25+ weavers; introduce 5-7 new designs quarterly; secure 2-3 large corporate gifting contracts; launch export partnerships with distributors in UK and Germany; build B2B wholesale team for hotel chains across India.
