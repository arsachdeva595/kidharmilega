Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Amreli ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Amreli town APMC (Agricultural Produce Market Committee) on a market day (morning, 6-9am) to speak with 3-4 peanut traders about pricing, seasonal cycles, farm output. Call 2-3 existing processing units (search 'peanut oil manufacturer Amreli' on Google Maps) and ask what margins and challenges they face. Register for free on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in) to understand compliance baseline. Zero cost, doable within 3 days.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw peanuts from Amreli APMC directly or via 2-3 farmer networks already trading there. First batch: 1,000 kg of in-shell peanuts at ₹40-50 per kg (₹40-50k spend) for testing. Blanched/roasted varieties available at ₹80-120 per kg if you skip roasting. Storage: rent 500 sq ft cool, dry space in Amreli town for ₹2-3k/month.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent 1,500-2,000 sq ft processing unit in Amreli industrial area (₹8-12k/month) and source basic machinery (roaster, mill, grinder, packaging machine) for ₹3-8L total. Option B: Partner with 1-2 existing local processors on contract manufacturing (they process your branded product for ₹15-25 per kg labor cost, you handle packaging and branding). Option A total setup: ₹8-15L; Option B total setup: ₹3-5L (packaging, labels, storage only).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 10 minutes online). Obtain FSSAI license (₹5-10k, 15-20 days) via fssairegistration.food.gov.in. If exporting: apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via https://www.dgft.gov.in (free, 7-10 days online). Apply for GI registration through Ministry of Agriculture (non-urgent, ₹5-8k fee, 12-18 months processing, but not required to start selling). Trademark your brand name (₹4-8k, 8-12 months).
5
First Sale
First D2C sale: Create 2-3 SKUs (peanut butter 200g, roasted peanuts 150g, peanut flour 250g), list on Amazon Launchpad or Flipkart SMB at ₹200-350 per unit. First wholesale sale: Contact 5-10 local bakeries, snack makers, sweet shops in Ahmedabad via phone/WhatsApp offering samples at ₹100/kg (2-3% lower than market). Expected first month: 100-200 kg domestic sales (₹12-20k revenue if D2C, ₹10-15k if wholesale). Break-even: 8-14 months at consistent 150-200 kg/month production.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (~600-800 kg/month): hire 2-3 production staff (₹8-12k/month each), 1 logistics person, 1 part-time e-commerce manager. Upgrade to 3,500 sq ft processing unit and semi-automated machinery (₹25-35L additional investment). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹10-25L @ 35% subsidy) for facility expansion. Exhibit at Ahmedabad Food & Beverage Expo (Jan/Aug annually) and Gandhinagar Food Fest to meet bulk buyers and exporters. Set up WhatsApp Business with price catalogs for B2B inquiries. Partner with 2-3 export trading houses to handle international logistics.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% for general, 45% for SC/ST/women in rural areas) on fixed asset purchase; apply via DIC Amreli or online at pmegp.gov.in
🏛
MUDRA Yojana
unsecured loans up to ₹10 lakh for food processing micro-enterprises at 2-4% below base rate; apply via partner banks (ICICI, HDFC, SBI) with minimal collateral
🏚
Gujarat Industrial Development Board (GIDB) subsidy
20% capital subsidy on plant and machinery for food processing units in Amreli district; apply via district MSME office
🏚
Gujarat SIDBI Scheme
concessional loans for food processing, ₹5-50 lakh tenure at 8-10% interest; apply to SIDBI branch in Rajkot/Ahmedabad
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

₹3-5 lakh minimum (without machinery, contract manufacturing model) to ₹12-15 lakh (with your own processing unit). PMEGP can cover 35% of machinery cost as subsidy; MUDRA loans available up to ₹10 lakh at 8-10% interest from SBI/ICICI.
Helpful but not required if you partner with existing processors (contract manufacturing). If going solo with your own unit, you need 3-6 months hands-on learning at an existing processing unit (negotiate with local processors for ₹500-1000/day learning fee). Food safety knowledge (FSSAI compliance) is must — online course available free on FSSAI website.
PMEGP: ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% of cost). MUDRA: ₹10 lakh loans @ 8-10%. GIDB: 20% capital subsidy on machinery. SIDBI: ₹5-50 lakh concessional loans. All require Udyam registration (free) + bank account. FSSAI license: ₹5-10k, 15-20 days processing.
D2C: Amazon, Flipkart (list your product in 7-10 days, get first order in 2-3 weeks). Wholesale: Local bakeries, sweet shops, food manufacturers in Ahmedabad (call 10-20 shops, offer 5-10% discount for first trial). B2B: Attend Ahmedabad Food & Beverage Expo (next one: January), meet 50+ bulk buyers in 2 days. Export: Contact 2-3 trading houses in Ahmedabad/Mumbai, they'll handle shipping to SE Asia/EU.
Haan, bahut achha export potential hai. India already exports 4 million metric tons peanuts annually. Tum kar sakte ho: Get IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) free online in 7-10 days. FSSAI certification ke liye export-grade standards meet karni padegi (₹10-15k extra cost for testing). Contact 2-3 trading houses or directly email importers in Malaysia, Indonesia, Bangladesh, EU. First export order: 5-10 tons ke around, FOB pricing 30-40% higher than domestic wholesale.
Real talk: peanut processing low-barrier hai to restart, but risky if you don't get first customers in 6 months. Exit options: (1) Sell your processing unit machinery to another processor (recover 60-70% cost). (2) Switch to contract manufacturing model (lower risk, lower margin). (3) Pivot to peanut farming or trading directly. Risk mitigation: Start with contract manufacturing (₹3-5L only), test market for 3 months, then invest in your own unit if sales are ₹50k+/month.
