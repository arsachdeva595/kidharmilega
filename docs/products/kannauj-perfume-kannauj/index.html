Kannauj sits in western UP (2093 sq km, 13.75L population) with 350-500 active perfume units forming India's largest attar cluster, anchored by Perfume Park and the proposed industrial corridor along Agra Expressway. Raw material supply is secured from local jasmine gardens, rose farms, and botanical suppliers within 50-100 km radius. The district captures both the Rs 600 crore domestic market (weddings, festive seasons, daily wear) and international buyers in Gulf, Southeast Asia, and Europe via established export networks through Agra and Lucknow hubs.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kannauj ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Perfume Park Kannauj cluster or Moti Lal Oswal Market (main perfume trading hub in Kannauj town) — meet 3-5 attar makers, ask about their first year costs and buyer contacts. Call Fragrance & Flavour Development Centre (FFDC) Kannauj for free consultation on raw materials and techniques. Register for free on Udyam MSME portal. Zero cost, do this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source jasmine absolute, rose otto, sandalwood oil from established suppliers in Kannauj's Naya Bans locality (bulk cost Rs 2,000-8,000/kg for quality variants) or Grasse-standard oils from Bangalore traders. First batch of 50 bottles requires Rs 15,000-30,000 (oils + base alcohol + bottles + packaging). Contact Kannauj Perfumers' Association for vetted supplier list.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 200 sq ft unit in Perfume Park Kannauj (Rs 3,000-6,000/month) with basic distillation setup and Deg Bhapka equipment (Rs 50,000-1.5L). Option B: Partner with existing micro-unit owner to co-produce first 500 bottles at cost + 15% fee (requires 0 capital setup). Hire 1-2 skilled attarwala (wage: Rs 8,000-12,000/month). Total 3-month ramp-up cost: Rs 1-2.5L.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in). File GI registration application with Ministry of Commerce (Rs 500 fee per class, 4-6 month process) — leverage Kannauj's existing GI tag. Obtain IEC code (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT if exporting (free, online, 3-5 days). Get FSSAI approval for cosmetic products (Rs 5,000-15,000, 2 weeks). Apply for InvestUP and Nivesh Mitra portal subsidies (up to 25% capital subsidy).
5
First Sale
First sale target: B2B to Nykaa Beauty or local wedding planner via direct contact (typical order: 10-20 bottles at wholesale). Expect Rs 8,000-15,000 first order. Launch D2C on Etsy and Instagram simultaneously (cost: Rs 2,000 for photography + listing). First-month revenue realistic target: Rs 12,000-30,000 (mix of 2-3 small orders).
6
Scale
At Rs 10L/month revenue: hire 1 dedicated sales/marketing person (Rs 15,000/month), upgrade to 500 sq ft production unit (Rs 8,000-10,000/month), invest Rs 25,000 in paid Instagram/Facebook ads targeting GCC and Indian metros. Exhibit at India Fragrance & Flavour Fair (annual, Kanpur), GiftPalace expo (Delhi), and Hotelivory (trade show for hotels). Join FFDC's export promotion program. Aim to capture 2-3 export distributors by month 12.

Frequently Asked Questions

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

Minimum Rs 1.5-2 lakh (shared production unit rental, raw materials, 100-200 bottles first batch, basic branding). With own 200 sq ft unit and full setup: Rs 2.5-5 lakh. Government subsidies under InvestUP/PMEGP can cover 25-40% of this.
No formal degree required, but 3-6 months hands-on training with an existing attarwala in Kannauj is essential. Many cluster makers offer apprenticeships (free or Rs 5,000-10,000 for 3 months). Chemistry understanding helps quality control.
PMEGP gives up to Rs 25 lakh subsidy (25% for SC/ST). InvestUP gives 25% capital subsidy up to Rs 50 lakh + electricity subsidy. SFURTI offers cluster development funds. Nivesh Mitra portal handles all licenses with minimal fees. GI tag is already established for Kannauj attar — you can use it for brand recognition.
Contact Nykaa Beauty, Flipkart sellers hub (direct B2B), local wedding planners in your city, Ayurvedic shops. Post on Etsy, Instagram, Facebook Marketplace first week itself. Wholesalers in Agra, Lucknow eat attars — visit them with sample (Rs 200-500 cost). First 5-10 orders usually come within 4-8 weeks of launch.
Bilkul. Kannauj already exports Rs 55-60 crore/year of attars. Top markets: UAE, Saudi Arabia, UK, USA. You need IEC code (free, 5 days online), basic FSSAI/cosmetic compliance, and a distributor or direct buyer contact. Small export order: 50-100 bottles at Rs 300-600 FOB, 45-60% margin. Shipping costs Rs 10,000-15,000 for 100 bottles to Gulf.
Honest assessment: attar is high-margin but needs brand-building (3-6 months minimum before profitability). If customer traction is zero after 4 months, pivot to B2B corporate gifting (lower risk, faster cash), or wholesale to spice/Ayurveda chains (guaranteed offtake). Exit option: sell remaining inventory + equipment to other cluster makers (10-15% loss likely). Real risk: spoilage if storage conditions are poor — Rs 20,000-50,000 can go bad if humidity not managed.
