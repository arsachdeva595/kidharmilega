Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bargarh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bargarh handloom cluster in Saintala, Jagdpur, and Bijepur blocks; spend 2-3 weeks learning from established weaver cooperatives like Bargarh Handloom Weavers Cooperative Society; understand loom types (pit loom, throw shuttle, jacquard) and traditional pattern documentation
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton yarn (20-40 count) from Bhagalpur or local suppliers at ₹180-220/kg; natural dyes from Indore or Varanasi suppliers; establish relationship with 3-4 artisan weavers; first batch of 50 sarees requires ₹80,000-120,000 (yarn + dyes + labor)
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-10 skilled weavers through Bargarh Handloom Weavers Association or direct contracts; establish quality control checkpoints; begin with 50-100 pieces/month; document each product with artisan identification; ensure compliance with handloom certification standards
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) with DC Handloom endorsement for preferential status; apply for IEC code for exports through DGFT; obtain Handloom Mark certification from Ministry of Textiles; register brand trademark for logo and product names; set up GST registration
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho, Flipkart Handmade, and custom Shopify store within 30 days; approach premium boutiques in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore); reach out to interior designers and corporate gifting platforms; list on 1stDibs and Etsy for international reach; engage with fashion micro-influencers (100k-500k followers) for product reviews
6
Scale
After ₹15L revenue, expand artisan network to 20-30 weavers; introduce 3-4 new designs quarterly; establish own B2B sales team for institutional clients; scale export through trade missions; consider opening pop-up stores in metro cities; develop private label contracts with fashion e-commerce platforms
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 toolkit grant + 5% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹1 lakh for handloom weavers and entrepreneurs
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to 35% subsidy (₹10.5L max) for manufacturing handloom products in aspirational districts
🏚
Odisha Handloom Development Scheme
30% subsidy on raw material procurement and equipment up to ₹2 lakh for registered handloom units
🏚
Odisha MSME Udyam Scheme
₹1 lakh collateral-free loan + mentorship for first-time handloom entrepreneurs in cluster districts
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
