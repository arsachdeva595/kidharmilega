opportunity, Dhar (Pithampur) mein.

Dhar district, particularly Pithampur, has a centuries-old tradition of block printing passed down through tribal communities. The region's access to natural indigo, myrobalan, and mineral sources, combined with skilled artisan clusters, makes it the authentic heartland of Bagh Prints. The GI status protects the product's origin and cultural heritage.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Dhar (Pithampur) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Bagh Prints artisan clusters in Pithampur (near Indore, 50km away). Connect with existing master artisans through DC Handloom office in Dhar. Attend ICCW (Indian Chamber of Commerce & Welfare) handloom expos in Mumbai/Delhi. Study production costs and dye-sourcing at local natural color suppliers in Indore.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source natural indigo and dyes from Badwani district suppliers (₹15,000-25,000 for first 50kg batch). Procure 100-200 meters of high-quality cotton/silk from Pithampur local mills or Ahmedabad textile traders (₹40,000-60,000). Purchase or commission wooden block sets from traditional block carvers in Pithampur (₹8,000-15,000 per set). Budget ₹80,000-120,000 for initial material inventory.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 3-5 master artisans in Pithampur cooperative clusters (contact through Dhar DC Handloom). Establish informal production agreement with guaranteed monthly orders at ₹800-1,500 per meter of printed fabric. Alternatively, lease a small workspace (2,000 sq ft) in Pithampur for ₹15,000/month and train 2-3 local apprentices over 4-6 months. Start with small batches (50 meters/week) to manage quality and cash flow.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME under 'Handloom and Handicrafts' category at udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from DGFT portal for export eligibility. Apply for GI Bagh Print certification use rights through DC Handloom. File brand trademark with IP India for your label name. Register business as Partnership or Proprietorship with local Registrar.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Sarangi, and Amazon Handmade with high-quality product photography emphasizing GI heritage. Partner with 2-3 boutique retailers in metros (Kolkata, Mumbai, Delhi) for wholesale placement. Pitch to international import houses via TradeKey or Global Sources. Create Instagram/Facebook content showcasing artisan stories and natural dye process (target 500-1,000 followers in 3 months). Participate in ICCW handloom expos and international craft fairs.
6
Scale
After 6 months, scale production to 200-300 meters/week by adding 2-3 more artisan partnerships. Launch D2C website with SEO targeting 'block print fabric' and 'natural dye textiles'. Develop 3-4 signature apparel collections (sarees, dupattas, shirts) and list on major platforms. Expand export by attending Heimtextil (Frankfurt) and trade shows. Establish a workshop-retail space in Pithampur/Indore for experience-based tourism revenue.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
Up to ₹15,000 one-time tooling grant and 5% interest subvention on loans up to ₹125,000 for artisans
🏛
PMEGP (Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme)
Up to ₹25 lakhs grant (25% for NE states, 35% for SC/ST) for handloom and handicrafts ventures
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Laghu Udyog Vikas Nidhi Yojana
5% interest subvention on loans up to ₹50 lakhs for registered handloom units
🏚
MP Handloom Development Subsidy Scheme
25-50% subsidy on machinery, tools, and working capital for registered cooperative societies producing traditional textiles
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Marketing — Zero Budget Se Shuru Karo
  • Influencer collaboration on Instagram with eco-fashion micro-influencers (10k-50k followers). Send 3-4 sample pieces; target hashtags #EcoFashion #BlockPrint #IndianTextiles #SustainableFashion. Aim for 5-10 posts/month reaching 50,000-100,000 impressions.
  • Content marketing via blog posts on Medium and LinkedIn about 'Natural Dye Revival in Indian Textiles' and artisan profiles. Target keywords: Bagh Prints, block printing, sustainable fashion, natural indigo. Drive 20-30 quality leads/month.
  • Email nurture campaign to boutique retailers, interior designers, and fashion curators with monthly lookbooks and wholesale pricing. Use Mailchimp or ConvertKit. Target 100-150 quality B2B leads with 15-20% conversion rate.
