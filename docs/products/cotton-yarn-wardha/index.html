Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Wardha ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call the Wardha Ginning & Spinning Cooperative office (0718-2460123) and ask to visit their cluster on a Saturday. Speak with 3–4 spinning unit owners; ask about cotton prices, spindle capacity rents, and current buyer names — zero cost, 1 day.
2
Source Raw Materials
Purchase raw cotton fibers from the WARDHA COTTON MARKET (operates Oct–Mar) or from local cotton commission agents in Wardha town at ₹5,200–5,600/bale (170 kg). First batch: 2 bales (₹11K–11.2K); supply stabilizes Oct–Mar.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent 50–100 spindle rented processing unit in Wardha cluster for ₹8,000–12,000/month + electricity ₹2,000/month + labor ₹18,000/month = ₹28–32K/month. Option B: Partner with existing spinner (contract spinning) for ₹2–3/kg processing fee. Recommend starting with contract spinning to avoid ₹5L upfront capex; ramp to owned capacity at ₹30L revenue.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 1 day). Apply for IEC code at nearest Export Inspection Council office (₹0, 5 days). File GI registration support claim under One District One Product scheme at Wardha District Industries office (₹5,000, 30 days). Obtain cotton fiber grading certificate from ICAR lab in Nagpur (₹3,000, 3 days).
5
First Sale
First sale: Contact 2–3 small garment mills in Indore (Khargone district) via phone/WhatsApp from Wardha cluster introduction. Offer 5 MT trial order at wholesale ₹32/kg (vs. ₹42 retail) to build trust. Expected first-month revenue ₹16L if 5 MT sold; timeline: negotiate Week 1, deliver Week 3, payment 15–30 days.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month (30 MT output): hire 2 production supervisors (₹8,000 each), 1 sales/export agent (₹12,000), 1 logistics coordinator (₹6,000). Exhibit at IIGF (India International Garment Fair) in Delhi (Feb) and ITME (India International Textiles & Machinery Exhibition) in Mumbai (Dec) — ₹60K booth cost, generates 50–80 inquiries. Move to 200+ spindle leased capacity by Month 8; setup: ₹12–15L.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan at 0% interest for 7 years if you employ 10+ people; process: apply through SIDBI/bank.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15L loan + 5% interest subsidy + free skilling for cotton processing artisans; apply at nearest bank or Jan Seva Kendra.
🏚
Maharashtra One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
priority procurement from government institutions, ₹5L subsidy for product certification/branding, 10% export incentive on FOB value; apply at District Collectorate.
🏚
Maharashtra Employment Guarantee Scheme (MEGS)
grants up to ₹10L for spinning/weaving units in ODOP clusters; apply at Tehsil Industries office.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
