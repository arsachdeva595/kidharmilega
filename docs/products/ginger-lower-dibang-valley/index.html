Ginger ki
opportunity, Lower Dibang Valley mein.

Lower Dibang Valley sits at 1500-2500m altitude in northeast Arunachal Pradesh with annual rainfall of 2800-3500mm, creating ideal conditions for aromatic ginger rich in gingerols and shogaols bioactive compounds. Raw material sourcing requires direct relationships with 150-200 farming households concentrated in Anini and surrounding hamlets, accessible via NH-52. Export-ready infrastructure exists via Dibrugarh airport (280km) and Kolkata port (1400km), enabling direct shipment to USA, Europe, and Japan with COO as Arunachal Pradesh GI-certified.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Lower Dibang Valley ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Anini town (district headquarters) and meet 3-4 ginger farmer groups; call Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Department office in Itanagar (0360-2289898) to understand GI certification process; attend weekly vegetable haat in Anini town to observe pricing and buyer behavior — all free, doable within 1 week
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw ginger from farmer cooperatives in Anini, Rilo, and Jido hamlets at ₹40-55/kg during Oct-Jan harvest season. First batch of 500kg will cost ₹20,000-27,500 from primary agricultural cooperative (Anini AAFS) or individual farmers. Negotiate 20-30 day credit terms by establishing personal relationship with 2-3 suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 1000-1500 sqft commercial kitchen space in Anini (₹8,000-15,000/month) or partner with existing food processor already equipped. For paste/oil production: invest ₹3.5-6L in drying tunnel (₹2L), grinder-mixer (₹80K), oil extraction unit (₹1.2L), packaging equipment (₹1.2L). Alternative: contract manufacturing via Dibrugarh-based spice processors at ₹35-45/kg processing fee for paste, ₹280-320/L for oil.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in, 15 minutes). File GI usage application with Arunachal Pradesh Industries Department for product name verification (₹5,000-8,000, 45-60 days). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (₹0, 7 days). Register trademark with IP India for brand name (₹5,000-9,000, 18-24 months). Total cost: ₹10,000-17,000 and 2-3 months for legal setup.
5
First Sale
First sale via Amazon Seller Central (individual plan ₹10,000/month or professional plan ₹4,999/month) — upload 5-10 SKUs of ginger paste and fresh ginger in first week. Target first month: 50-100kg sales = ₹8,000-15,000 revenue. Simultaneously approach 2-3 regional Ayurvedic company procurement teams (Himalaya, Baidyanath) with product samples and pricing sheet — typical first order ₹50,000-80,000 for 500kg paste.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 quality control manager (₹25,000/month) and 2-3 processing/packaging staff (₹12,000 each). Shift to 2500-3000 sqft production unit (₹20,000-25,000/month rent). Participate in APEDA food export expo (Oct-Nov annually) and International Spice Board exhibitions in Delhi/Mumbai. Target export buyers by joining APEDA's Export Promotion Council — gain access to international buyer databases and logistics support.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy on fixed assets up to ₹25 lakh for manufacturing, maximum ₹10 lakh subsidy

MUDRA Scheme
loan up to ₹10 lakh at 5.5-7.5% interest for working capital and equipment, zero-collateral

15% investment subsidy on machinery and equipment up to ₹5 lakh for processing units in food sector
🏚

10% subsidy on packaging and labeling cost for APEDA-registered exporters, maximum ₹1 lakh per annum
