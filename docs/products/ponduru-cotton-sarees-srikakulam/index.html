Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Srikakulam ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tekkali town and walk the Kumpurlanka weaving cluster — meet 3-4 master weavers, observe pit loom production, call Srikakulam Handloom Department (0894-2240100) and ask for cluster contact. Free, doable this week. Attend Tekkali weekly haat on Thursday to see raw cotton and dye suppliers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton yarn from suppliers in Tekkali town (₹180-220/kg quality grade) and indigo from Visakhapatnam wholesale markets. First batch: 50 sarees = 250 kg yarn (₹45-55K) + dyes (₹8-10K). Supplier area: Kumpurlanka lane, Tekkali. Lead time 4-6 weeks for artisan weaving.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 500 sqft warehouse in Tekkali industrial pocket (₹5-8K/month) and hire 2-3 trained pit loom weavers at ₹8-12K/month each. Option B: Partner with 5-6 individual home-based weavers on a piece-rate model (₹200-300 per saree). Initial production cost: ₹3-5L for 50-unit batch with quality control setup.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in) — 15 min. Apply for GI certification via Ministry of Textile (₹25-40K fee, 6-8 month process, increases brand value 20-30%). If exporting: Get IEC code from DGFT (₹0, 5 days online). Apply PM Vishwakarma for artisan support and branding subsidy (₹15K-50K benefit).
5
First Sale
First sale: List 20 sarees on Etsy (free listing, 6.5% fee) and simultaneously pitch to 5-10 ethnic wear boutiques in Hyderabad via WhatsApp with product photos. First order likely: 5-8 sarees via boutique (₹8-12K revenue) or 3-4 via Etsy (₹7-10K revenue). Expected first month: ₹25-40K if both channels active.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: Hire a logistics coordinator (₹12-15K), shift to 8-10 weavers, invest ₹2-3L in branding/photography, exhibit at Hyderabad ethnic wear fairs (₹30-50K booth cost), launch Instagram/Pinterest content (1-2 posts/week showing weaving process). Parallel: Apply for SFURTI cluster upgrade grant (₹15-20L subsidy) if producing 300+ sarees/month.

₹15,000 toolkit subsidy + ₹5,000 cashback on PMMY loans for artisans and micro-enterprises
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Up to ₹25 lakh loan with 25% subsidy (35% for SC/ST/Women) for manufacturing unit setup
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Artisan Promotion Scheme
₹50,000-₹2,00,000 direct subsidy to individual weavers and microenterprises, processed via AP Handloom Directorate
🏚
AP MSME Export Support Scheme
5% refund on export shipping costs, marketing support up to ₹1,00,000 annually for registered units
