Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Dibang Valley ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Anini (Dibang Valley district headquarters) to study existing kiwi farmers; connect with Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Department in Itanagar; research Himachal Pradesh kiwi clusters in Kinnaur for comparative learning; join ICAR-IIMR kiwi farming webinars
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified kiwi saplings (Hayward variety) from ICAR-Central Horticultural Experiment Station, Ranchi (₹20-30 per sapling); bulk order 500-1000 saplings (₹12,000-30,000); source organic fertilizers from Arunachal Pradesh State Agriculture Department; first batch land prep and sapling cost: ₹1,200-1,500
3
Produce / Partner
Lease 2-3 acres in Anini or Roing area with proven kiwi compatibility; partner with 3-5 existing small kiwi farmers for knowledge transfer; invest in drip irrigation system (₹150,000-200,000); install shade net structures; 3-year maturation before commercial harvest
4
Brand & Register
Register business as Udyam (MSME online) under organic farming category; obtain IEC code from APEDA for exports; get organic certification from NPOP/Jaivik Bharat scheme (₹30,000-50,000); register trademark on IPINDIA for brand; get export-ready packaging certification
5
First Sale
Launch on FreshToHome premium fruits, Blinkit curated organic section; partner with local Anini/Roing fruit markets for wholesale; contact Arunachal Pradesh Organic Farmers Association for bulk orders; list on Amazon Fresh as premium item; start cold-chain exports to Bhutan via Guwahati consolidation centers
6
Scale
Expand to 5-10 acres by month 18; invest in cold storage facility (₹25-30L); develop processing unit for jam/concentrate (₹40-50L); hire 5-8 farm workers; establish contract farming with 10-15 neighboring farmers; build B2B relationships with juice companies and export wholesalers; target ₹2-3Cr annual turnover by year 3
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for micro-enterprise with 25-35% margin contribution
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster development support for agricultural products with infrastructure grants
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Agriculture Policy 2023
subsidies on drip irrigation (40-50%), seeds/saplings (25-30%), and cold storage (30-40%)
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Organic Farming Scheme
free certification, ₹5,000/hectare annual subsidy for 3 years, market linkage support
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
