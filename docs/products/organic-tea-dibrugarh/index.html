Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Dibrugarh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dibrugarh tea estates in Moran, Tingrai, and Chabua clusters. Connect with organic certification bodies (APEDA, Control Union). Study Assam Tea Board's organic tea directory. Attend Guwahati Tea Auction Centre sessions. Spend 4-6 weeks understanding cultivation, processing, and quality grading.
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner with ATDA-certified organic tea gardens or independent organic farmers in Dibrugarh district. First batch: 500-1000 kg loose leaf tea costs ₹3-5 lakhs. Contact Dibrugarh Tea Association members for verified suppliers. Ensure APEDA organic certification documentation.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with contract manufacturing at APEDA-certified processing units in Dibrugarh (Guwahati Tea Processing Co., local tea factories). Alternatively, establish your own small-batch processing unit (₹20-40 lakhs). Hire experienced tea processors from local tea gardens. Begin with 2-3 tea grades (Orthodox, CTC blends).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online via udyamregistration.gov.in). Obtain IEC code from DGFT portal. File FSSAI license for tea processing. Apply for APEDA organic certificate and GI-alternative branding. Design packaging with food-grade specifications. Trademark brand name with IP India.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon and Flipkart with 250g and 500g packs. Build DTC site via Shopify. Partner with Whole Foods-like premium retail chains (Metro, Reliance Fresh outlets). Pitch to corporate bulk order platforms (IndiaCorp, BulkBuy). Start Instagram influencer collaborations with wellness accounts. Email marketing to organic tea communities.
6
Scale
After ₹20L revenue: Expand SKUs (flavoured blends, tea bags). Open 2-3 exclusive retail stores in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai. Launch subscription box model. Apply for SFURTI cluster funding for processing expansion. Obtain EU/USDA organic certifications for US/European export. Partner with tea lounges and premium restaurants for bulk supply.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan at 5-6% interest for agro-processing startups with 5% government subsidy
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹2 crore grant for tea cluster development and artisan support
🏚
Assam Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Scheme (AARTS)
subsidies up to 40% for organic certification and processing equipment
🏚
Assam Startup Policy
₹50 lakh seed funding and 5-year GST exemption for agritech and organic food processing startups
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
