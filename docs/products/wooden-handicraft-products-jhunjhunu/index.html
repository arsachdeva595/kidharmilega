Jhunjhunu has a centuries-old tradition of woodcarving rooted in its merchant community and palace architecture. The district's access to quality timber from surrounding forests and skilled artisan clusters in towns like Khetri and Udaipurwati make it a natural hub for wooden handicrafts. Local demand from heritage conservation and tourism fuels sustained production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jhunjhunu ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khetri and Udaipurwati woodcarving clusters in Jhunjhunu; connect with Master Artisans Association; attend Jaipur Handicraft Mela (Feb-Mar); study local production units and pricing at Jhunjhunu District Administration office.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw timber from Jhunjhunu Forest Department approved suppliers or Jaipur timber markets (₹2-5L for initial 3-month inventory of mixed wood); establish relationships with 2-3 local wood suppliers for consistent quality.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established artisan groups in Udaipurwati or directly employ 2-3 master craftsmen; start with 1 small workshop space (500 sqft) costing ₹3-5k/month; focus on 5-6 signature product lines (doors, panels, furniture pieces, decorative boxes).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise) with district MSME office in Jhunjhunu; obtain IEC code from DGFT Jaipur; trademark brand name with IP office; get FSC certification for sustainable wood sourcing.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and own Shopify store with high-quality heritage photography; contact 10-15 interior design studios in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with portfolio; approach export agencies in Rajasthan; attend India Handicraft Week (Delhi) for B2B connections.
6
Scale
After ₹15-20L revenue, open showroom in Jaipur or Delhi; hire dedicated export manager; expand to 8-10 product categories; build retail presence in 3-4 tier-1 cities; establish workshop cooperative for supply chain stability.
