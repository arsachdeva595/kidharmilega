Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sheikhpura ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sheikhpura's primary agriculture department and connect with established onion farmer clusters in Sheikhpura town. Study crop cycles at Bihar Agricultural University, Sabour. Analyze pricing at Patna's Kesaria Mandi and Darbhanga APMC to understand market dynamics.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified onion seeds from Indian Institute of Vegetable Research (IIVR), Varanasi, or regional suppliers like Nuziveedu Seeds. Organic compost and fertilizers available from Bihar Cooperative Fertilizer Ltd. First-batch cost for 1 acre: ₹60,000-₹80,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Begin with contract farming under established landowners or lease 1-3 acres in Sheikhpura block. Partner with Bihar Rajya Bīj Nigam for hybrid seed supply. Implement drip irrigation systems subsidized under PM-KISAN schemes. Use cooperative extension services from Sheikhpura Krishi Vigyan Kendra for technical support.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam via udyamregistration.gov.in for agricultural enterprise status. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Patna for export. Register FSSAi license for any processing. Create brand identity emphasizing 'Sheikhpura Premium' with regional GI positioning (pending).
5
First Sale
List on BigHaat, AgroStar, and AgriNext platforms targeting bulk buyers. Attend Bihar Agri Summit and eastern India vegetable trade expos in Kolkata. Partner with local mandis in Patna, Darbhanga, and Varanasi for initial bulk sales. Build relationships with frozen food exporters in Mehsana (Gujarat) and Nashik (Maharashtra).
6
Scale
After first harvest, invest in cold storage (₹15-20L for 100MT capacity) to reduce post-harvest losses. Launch dehydrated onion powder and flakes targeting food processing companies. Establish direct buyer relationships in 2-3 export markets via trade missions. Expand acreage through cooperative model and hired labor.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM-KISAN Samman Nidhi
₹6,000 annual direct cash transfer to farmers registered under the scheme
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh government subsidy for agribusiness startups with 25-35% own contribution
🏚
Bihar Agricultural Input Subsidy Scheme
50% subsidy on seeds, fertilizers, and drip irrigation equipment for small and marginal farmers
🏚
Bihar Krishi Rath Scheme
subsidized transportation of agricultural produce from farm gate to mandi for registered farmers
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
