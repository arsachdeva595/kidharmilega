Onion ki
opportunity, Sheikhpura mein.

Sheikhpura district in central Bihar sits on productive black soil with abundant groundwater, 60km from Hilli mandi (gateway to Bangladesh border) and 80km from Mehndipur market in Malda district. About 100 active onion traders operate here with 1000+ farmers maintaining small farm-level storage godowns. The district's proximity to border towns (Bellughat, Bujadange) means onion-laden trucks move directly to Bangladesh markets, creating a mature export ecosystem.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sheikhpura ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Hilli mandi (60km away) on a Tuesday or Friday wholesale day. Talk to 3-4 commission agents. Call Sri Niranjan Kumar's unit in Sheikhpura (he operates the 400-tonne storage godown — contact via block office). Attend Mehndipur haat market meeting. Cost: ₹300 bus fare. Time: 1 day.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source onion seeds from Bangalore-based certified suppliers (Nuziveedu, Ever Green seeds) via agricultural input dealers in Sheikhpura town or Rohtas district (₹8000-12000 per kg for 10kg initial batch). Fertilizer from DCM Shriram or IFFCO dealer in Sheikhpura (bulk discount after 2nd season). First-season farm input cost: ₹80000-120000 per acre.
3
Produce / Partner
Lease 1-2 acres from local farmer (₹15000-25000 per acre per season, Apr-Oct cycle) or join an existing farmer collective in Sheikhpura block. Construct a 100-tonne storage godown: ₹250000-400000 (brick, roofing, ventilation). Alternatively, rent godown space from established traders for ₹30000-50000 per month. Total first-year produce cost: ₹300000-500000.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, online, 15 min). Apply for IEC from nearest customs office in Patna (₹0 fee, 7 days). Get agricultural export license from APEDA (₹2000, 20 days) if targeting Bangladesh sales. Attempt GI registration for Sheikhpura Onion (coordinated through district agricultural office, ₹50000-100000 processing cost shared among cluster members, 6-12 month timeline). Create 'Sheikhpura Swad' brand with local Urdu/Hindi label printing (₹5000).
5
First Sale
First sale: approach Hilli mandi commission agent with 5-10 tonnes (truck load). Expect ₹1.2-1.5L revenue, but take 15-20% cut. Alternatively, direct border sale to Bangladesh trader at Bellughat checkpoint (20-25 tonnes per truck, ₹2-2.5L per truck). First month: 1 wholesale order expected, ₹80000-150000 net revenue.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: construct second 200-tonne godown (₹400000), hire 3 permanent workers (₹3000/month each) + 8-10 seasonal laborers during harvest. Join vegetable producer association to access group export certifications. Exhibit at Bihar Agri Summit (annual, Patna). Establish direct contracts with 2-3 Bangladesh wholesalers (cuts mandi margin by 8-10%). Move from 500 tonnes/year to 1500+ tonnes/year within 18 months.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs loan at 5% interest for storage godown construction; 35% subsidy for SC/ST applicants

MUDRA Loan (Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana)
₹10 lakh unsecured loan at 8-10% for working capital and equipment; processing fee ₹0
🏚
Bihar Krishi Yantra Subsidy
50% subsidy on cold storage construction (max ₹2 lakhs) for farmers/farmer groups
🏚
Bihar Kisan Credit Card
revolving credit up to ₹3 lakhs for farm inputs and storage at 4% interest if taken within 7 days of account opening

Frequently Asked Questions

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

₹3-5 lakhs minimum: 1 acre farmland lease (₹20000), seeds/fertilizer (₹80000), godown rental for 5-6 months (₹150000-200000), licenses/Udyam (₹3000), transport for first batch (₹50000). Maximum ₹15-20 lakhs if you own land and build your own 200-tonne godown.
Preferred but not mandatory. Sri Niranjan Kumar started as a small farmer. You need: (a) 2-3 contacts in Hilli mandi or Bangladesh trade, (b) ability to learn soil/weather/variety selection in first season, (c) willingness to handle perishable risk. Most successful traders here have 5+ years farm background, but 2-3 season learning curve is standard.
PMEGP: ₹25 lakh loan at 5% + 35% subsidy (SC/ST). MUDRA: ₹10 lakh unsecured loan at 8-10%. Bihar Agri Subsidy: 50% on godown construction (max ₹2 lakh). Kisan Credit Card: ₹3 lakh revolving credit at 4%. IEC and APEDA export license: ₹0 fee total.
Direct: Hilli mandi commission agents (60km away, buy 5+ tonnes lots weekly). Border: Bangladesh traders at Bellughat checkpoint (25-30km, buy 20+ tonne truck loads). Institutional: Pickle units in Gaya/Nalanda need 50+ tonnes per month. WhatsApp outreach to 100 vegetable wholesalers in Patna/Arrah gets 3-5 first orders within month.
Haan, yeh Sheikhpura se Bangladesh primarily jaata hai. Process: Get IEC (7 days), get APEDA certificate (20 days, ₹2000), arrange border trucking to Hilli mandi, Bangladesh buyer arranges cross-border transfer at Bellughat or Bujadange checkpoint. Margin: ₹150-200 per tonne better than domestic mandi. 1600 tonnes annually possible (like Niranjan Kumar) if you scale to 2-3 godowns.
Biggest risk: price crash (happened 2020, onion fell to ₹8/kg). Mitigation: hold inventory 2-3 months to fetch 30-40% premium. Exit option: sell godown building to other traders (resale demand high in Sheikhpura). Worst case: ₹100000-200000 loss in year 1 if price crashes and you have to sell fresh. But 100 existing traders here survived 20+ years, so market is stable.
