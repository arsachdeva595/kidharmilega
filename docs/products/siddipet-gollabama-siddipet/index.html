Siddipet Gollabama ki
opportunity, Siddipet mein.

Siddipet district, 75km north of Hyderabad in Telangana, hosts the traditional Gollabama saree cluster with 2500+ active handlooms concentrated in Siddipet town. Raw materials (60s, 80s cotton yarn, mercerized cotton, silk) are sourced locally from Hyderabad textile markets (50km away) and supplied via established Siddipet mandis. Buyer markets include Hyderabad ethnic boutiques, Mumbai wedding circuit, Delhi department stores, and direct-to-consumer channels across metros, with export routes to USA, UK, Australia via courier and consolidators.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Siddipet ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Siddipet Handloom Cooperative (Siddipet town center) and Hanuman Nagar weaving cluster on a Tuesday to watch live production. Call 3-4 yarn suppliers in Hyderabad's Abids textile market to understand 60s/80s cotton pricing (free phone calls). Register free Udyam account on udyamregistration.gov.in. Attend the weekly Saturday textile buyer meet at Siddipet Saree Market (Siddipet town main bazaar) — zero cost, 2-3 hours.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source yarn from Hyderabad-based suppliers: Vijaya Textile Traders (Abids market, ₹85-120/kg for 60s/80s cotton), mercerized cotton from Pearl Exports (Nampally, ₹110-140/kg). First batch: 500m cotton yarn + 200m silk = ₹8000-12000. Order via phone or in-person from Abids. Lead time: 7-10 days.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner with 8-10 independent weavers in Siddipet town (pay ₹80-150 per saree weaving labor, you supply yarn). Option B: Rent a 400-500 sqft unit in Siddipet MSME cluster (₹4000-6000/month), hire 2-3 weavers as piece-rate (₹100-120/saree). Initial setup (looms + working capital): ₹200,000-400,000. Production timeline: 5-8 days per saree with artisan.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 10 mins online). Apply for GI usage certificate at DC Handloom office, Siddipet (₹500, 30 days). File IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at DGFT portal if planning exports (₹0, 3-5 days online). Trademark your brand name at IP office (₹4500, 12-18 months). Total time: 60 days for essentials.
5
First Sale
First channel: Instagram Shop (post 5-10 saree photos, use hashtags #SiddipetSaree #GollabamaSaree #HandwoveninTelangana, target ₹4500-5500/saree). Second: approach 5 boutiques in Hyderabad (Jubilee Hills, Begumpet ethnic stores) with samples, negotiate ₹2200-2500 wholesale. Third: list on Amazon Handmade (7-day approval, zero listing fee). Expected first month: 4-6 direct sales + 1-2 wholesale orders = ₹22,000-35,000 revenue.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (40-50 sarees/month): hire 1 full-time order manager + 2 logistics staff, rent 800 sqft production space (₹8000-10,000/month), expand weaver network to 15-20. Participate in IITF Delhi Handloom Pavilion (Dec, application deadline Sept) and Telangana Saree Fest (annual, April). Export: register with APEXCL (Andhra Pradesh Export Promotion body) for subsidy on freight. Apply for SFURTI cluster support if scaling as a group.

Where to Set Up

Infrastructure ready hai — plot lo aur shuru karo

Siddipet MSME Cluster
Dedicated handloom cluster 2km west of Siddipet town center with 50+ registered units, basic infrastructure (water, power), shared dyeing facilities, rent ₹4000-7000/month for 400-600 sqft units. Existing tenants: saree weavers, fabric dyers, packaging units.
MSME Cluster
Telangana Handloom House (DC Handloom Office)
State facility offering free design consultation, GI certification support, access to yarn supplier database, zero-subsidy rental of 200sqft office space for registered weavers. Located at Siddipet town center.
Craft Village
Hyderabad Export Processing Zone (HEPC)
95km away in Hyderabad, offers SEZ facilities for export-focused handloom businesses — duty-free yarn imports, tax sops up to 5 years, specialized freight handling. Relevant if scaling to ₹50L+ export revenue.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 free training + ₹500,000 soft loan at 0% for 18 months for handloom artisans
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25-35% subsidy on equipment cost (up to ₹10L loan, ₹2.5-3.5L subsidy for SC/ST/women) for new handloom units
🏚
Telangana Handloom Weavers Cooperative Society Scheme
₹50,000-100,000 interest-free loan for working capital + free raw material subsidy of 15% on yarn purchases
🏚
Telangana One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
Free GI registration support + market linkage to state-run exhibitions + branding subsidy of 50% up to ₹50,000 for new handloom startups
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
