Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kohima ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kohima's food markets (Kezadonuo Complex, traditional markets) and interview 10+ pickle makers. Study Hornbill Festival food clusters. Document traditional recipes from heritage keepers. Research Northeast ODOP network at Ministry of Food Processing office in Kohima.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source chillies from local Kohima farmers (₹80-120/kg), turmeric from Assam suppliers, vinegar from standard food suppliers. First batch cost for 100 jars (500g each): ₹35,000-45,000. Connect with Nagaland Agricultural Department for certified local sourcing.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with home kitchen certification (FSSAI); partner with 2-3 women's SHGs in Kohima who make pickles traditionally. Alternatively lease small commercial kitchen space in Kohima town for ₹15,000/month. Begin with 500-1000 jars monthly, employ 3-4 local helpers.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as food processing micro-enterprise. Obtain FSSAI license (₹5,000-7,000). Apply for IEC code from nearest customs authority. Trademark brand name via IPO (₹9,000-15,000). Design packaging with Nagaland's traditional motifs. Obtain ISO 22000 certification (₹25,000-40,000).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Seller Central and Flipkart. Create Instagram and Facebook pages showing artisan production process. Partner with 5-10 specialty stores in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai. Send samples to food bloggers and lifestyle influencers. Register on JioMart and Blinkit for metro penetration.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product line (3-4 regional pickle variants). Open second kitchen for 5000+ jars/month capacity. Establish partnerships with 50+ retail outlets. Launch subscription box service. Explore export to SAARC countries through food export aggregators. Invest in cold chain logistics.
