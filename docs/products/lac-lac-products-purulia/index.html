Lac & Lac Products ki
opportunity, Purulia mein.

Purulia district in south-western West Bengal contains 751 sq km of forest (12% of total area) with abundant Palas, Kusum, and Ber trees — the primary lac host trees. The region has an unbroken tradition of lac cultivation since ancient times with trained artisans in multiple clusters and access to raw brood lac year-round. Buyer markets include domestic manufacturers in nearby states (Maharashtra, Tamil Nadu) and export channels to USA, Germany, Japan via standard trade routes.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Purulia ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Purulia town or Bagmundi cluster and meet 3-4 lac farmers/processors (contact via Purulia District Administration or Purulia KVK office — free); attend monthly haat at Purulia hat or Jhalda market to observe pricing and supply chain; call 2-3 varnish/toy makers in Maharashtra to understand their sourcing process — all doable this week with no cost.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw brood lac from established suppliers in Bagmundi, Jhalda, or Purulia town (₹180-220/kg for quality brood lac); first batch of 20 kg will cost ₹3,600-4,400 and takes 5-7 days delivery; negotiate directly with farmer collectives or registered supplier co-ops to reduce middleman margin.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a small unit (200 sq ft) in Purulia town for ₹3,000-5,000/month with basic equipment (cooking pot, molds, scraper, bundling materials = ₹30,000-50,000 one-time). Option B: Partner with 2-3 existing artisans in Bagmundi cluster on commission basis (₹5-15 per piece) with zero rent; produce through them for first 6 months to learn, then scale to own unit.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online (free, 10 min) at udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for Handicraft Artisan Credit Card (HACC) under Ministry of DC Handicraft for ₹1-5L unsecured credit (eligibility: registered MSME); apply for IEC code from DGFT if exporting (₹500-1,000, 5-7 days); consider GI registration later if cluster achieves collective standard (₹10,000-15,000 govt support, 6-12 months).
5
First Sale
First sale via Instagram DM or WhatsApp to existing artisan networks or local buyers (₹5,000-10,000 first month expected). Simultaneously list 10-15 products on Etsy (500 Palas beads at ₹400 = ₹2L retail = ₹1.2L revenue if 30% conversion) and Amazon Handmade (registration takes 3 weeks, ₹25-30 commission per item); first bulk wholesale order from toy maker = ₹20,000-40,000 after 2-3 months of relationship building.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire 3-5 processors (artisans/family members), rent 500 sq ft unit in Purulia MSME cluster, invest ₹2L in equipment upgrade (shaping machines, packaging), attend 4 trade shows/year (Delhi Craft Fair, Mumbai Furniture Fair, Kolkata Export Fair). Partner with 2-3 trade houses for export orders. Hire 1 full-time sales person for B2B outreach to varnish/cable makers.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme (PMEGP)
up to ₹5 lakhs subsidy for artisans/first-gen founders; 25% subsidy for general category, 35% for SC/ST, application via KVIC/KVIB, 6-8 weeks approval
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 monthly stipend for 18 months + ₹1L interest-free loan for artisans; priority for existing craftspeople; applies to lac artisans, registration via DigiLocker
🏚
West Bengal Artisan Credit Card Scheme
₹1-5 lakhs unsecured credit at 4% interest for registered handicraft MSMEs; minimum documentation, 7-10 days approval via West Bengal Craft Development Council
🏚
West Bengal One District One Product (ODOP) Support
zero interest rate for first 2 years + ₹5,000 online marketing grant for registered ODOP artisans; application via District MSME Office Purulia
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
