opportunity, Madhubani mein.

Madhubani district (4.75M population, 2,197 km²) in north Bihar is 120 km north of Patna via NH-106, with Jitwarpur and Ranti clusters 15-25 km from district HQ. Raw materials—cotton, natural pigments, bamboo—sourced from local suppliers within 20 km radius. Jitwarpur and Ranti together have 400+ registered women artisans producing paintings on paper, cloth, and walls; production centres concentrated in villages within 10 km of Madhubani city, with buyer reach extending to Delhi (200 km), Kolkata (150 km), and international markets via Patna/Kolkata air/rail gateways.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Madhubani ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jitwarpur or Ranti cluster (0 rupees) — stay 1-2 days, meet 3-4 established painters, observe production flow, ask about material sourcing and pricing. Call Madhubani District Industries Centre (DIC) — phone number available on madhubani.nic.in — ask about artisan networks, recent sales, export challenges. Attend the annual Mithila Art Mela (typically Feb-Mar) if timing aligns. Register free on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source painters from Jitwarpur (5 km from Madhubani city) or Ranti (20 km) — visit village co-operatives or connect via DIC. First batch: commission 10-15 paintings on paper (A4, A3 size) at ₹800–₹2,500 per piece from individual artisans; total first batch ₹15-30K. Natural pigments sourced from local suppliers in Madhubani city market (₹200-500/kg for plant-based dyes). Cotton and bamboo brushes from local vendors near Madhubani bus stand (₹50-200 per unit).
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Artisan partnership model (recommended for ₹2-3L investment) — no factory needed. Directly engage 2-3 skilled painters from Jitwarpur/Ranti on per-piece commission (₹1,500–₹4,000/painting). Provide design inputs, materials, collection schedule. You handle design, sourcing quality paper/cloth, quality check, packaging, sales. Option B: Small studio (₹5-8L) — rent 200-300 sq ft space near Madhubani city or online marketplace hub, employ 2-3 painters, invest in material inventory. Option C: Hybrid — start with artisan partnerships (months 1-3), then open a small studio (month 4-6) as you secure first orders.
4
Brand & Register
Register on Udyam (₹0, 15 mins online). Apply for GI certification support under Ministry of Commerce ODOP program (free registration + 50% subsidy on certification costs, approx ₹10-15K total, 30-60 day timeline). Obtain IEC code (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT if planning export — free online registration via ICEGATE, 7-10 days. Trademark your brand name with IP India (₹4,500-9,000, 18-24 month process). Get ISO 9001 certification if targeting premium export buyers (₹30-50K, 2-3 months).
5
First Sale
First sale: list 5-10 paintings on Amazon Handmade or Etsy (free listing, 3.5% commission). Target first order size: ₹8-15K (2-3 pieces). Expected first-month revenue: ₹10-20K from D2C. Parallel: reach out to 5-10 FabIndia or Anasa stores with portfolio — 15-20% wholesale discount standard. Attend Delhi Craft Fair or Chennai Design Crafts (vendor booth ₹15-30K) to build direct B2B connections.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: employ a full-time operations manager (₹15-20K/month), hire 2-3 more skilled painters on fixed salary (₹8-12K/month each), open a retail studio in Madhubani city (₹20-30K/month rent), invest in website/e-commerce setup (₹50-80K one-time), participate in international craft fairs (Ambiente Frankfurt, Top Drawer London — ₹1.5-3L per fair). Aim for 60% D2C, 25% wholesale, 15% export revenue mix by month 18.

Where to Set Up

Infrastructure ready hai — plot lo aur shuru karo

Madhubani District ODOP Cluster (Jitwarpur-Ranti)
Informal craft cluster with 400+ registered women painters distributed across Jitwarpur (5 km from city) and Ranti (20 km); no centralized park but co-operative societies provide workspace sharing, material bulk purchase discounts, and quality certification support. Individual artisan units typically home-based (50-100 sq ft workspace).
Craft Village / MSME Cluster
Madhubani District Industries Centre (DIC) Craft Hub
Government facility at Madhubani city offering maker-space rental (₹2-5K/month for 100 sq ft), material storage, quality testing lab access, export documentation support, artisan network directory. Managed by Ministry of Industries.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 cash grant + ₹3L collateral-free loan at 5% for registered artisans/small producers; eligibility: Udyam registration + handcraft classification
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy on setup cost for women entrepreneurs (up to ₹10L subsidy on ₹40L project); requires business plan, 10% own contribution
🏚
Bihar State ODOP Advancement Fund
direct subsidy up to ₹5L for artisans producing GI-tagged products; apply via DIC Madhubani with Udyam registration
🏚
Bihar Handicraft Development Scheme
₹50,000 grant for first-time artisan exporters; includes IEC registration support and international fair participation subsidy (50% booth cost)
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
