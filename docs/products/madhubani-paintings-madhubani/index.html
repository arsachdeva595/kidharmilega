Madhubani Paintings ki
opportunity, Madhubani mein.

Madhubani district is the historic birthplace of Madhubani painting, with over 10,000 artisan families practicing this art for generations. The region's cultural ecosystem, natural pigment availability, and deep-rooted artistic tradition make it the only authentic source for genuine Madhubani paintings. Government and NGO support infrastructure in villages like Jitwarpur, Ranti, and Lohapur provide institutional backing.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Madhubani ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jitwarpur village (30km from Madhubani town) — the epicenter of Madhubani painting — and spend 2-3 weeks with master artisans like those in the Mithila Arts Cooperative. Study the GI-certified authentication process and pricing benchmarks at local artisan associations. Connect with SFURTI clusters operating in the district.
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure natural materials from local suppliers in Madhubani town (rice powder ₹50/kg, charcoal ₹30/kg, plant dyes ₹100-200/unit). Order first batch of 100 sheets of quality handmade paper (₹500-800/sheet) from suppliers in Bhagalpur. Initial raw material investment: ₹40,000-60,000 for 3-month production supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 registered artisans from Jitwarpur/Ranti villages through formal contracts ensuring IP rights and fair wages (₹300-500 per artwork). Alternatively, take 2-month intensive training from master painter Godawari Devi or similar senior artists. Set up small studio space (500 sq ft minimum) in Madhubani town (rent ₹3,000-5,000/month).
4
Brand & Register
Register business under Udyam Portal with 'Handicraft - Painting' classification. Obtain IEC code from DGFT for export eligibility. Apply for GI authentication certificate from DC Handicraft, Madhubani. Register brand name with IP office. Get FSSAI if offering workshop food. Setup GST registration (5% for handicrafts).
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (target 10-15 listings in first month) with photography showing artisan stories. List on Amazon Handmade (India + US) and Flipkart Handloom. Create Instagram shop with reels showing creation process. Approach 5-10 premium galleries in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) for consignment. Launch email nurture sequence to international art collectors via art forums.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire dedicated digital marketer for Pinterest/Instagram strategy (high visual platform ROI). Expand artisan network to 15-20 craftspeople. Approach corporate gifting companies for bulk orders. Set up presence in 2-3 international craft fairs (Handmade at Amazon US, London Craft Week). Create tiered product range (₹2,000-₹50,000) to capture mid-market. Launch artist certification program to build brand authority.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan + 35% subsidy for artisans, covers machinery and working capital
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
craft sector support with skill certification, tools, marketing assistance, and branding support for traditional artisans
🏚
Bihar Traditional Arts and Crafts Promotion Scheme
provides raw material subsidies (20-30%), training grants, and GI certification support for registered artisan groups
🏚
Bihar Kaushal Vikas Yojana
skill development funding for training artisans and production staff, includes wage subsidy for first 6 months
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
