Dhaniakhali Saree ki
opportunity, Hooghly mein.

Hooghly district has a 300+ year weaving tradition centered in villages like Haripal, Rajbalhat, and Antpur. The availability of sar reed (bamboo variety) uniquely suited to the region's climate enables production of flexible, ethnic-patterned sarees. Traditional weaver communities with inherited expertise make Hooghly the natural geographic cluster for this GI-protected product.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Hooghly ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Haripal and Antpur villages in Hooghly; spend 2 weeks understanding weaver communities, production timelines, and sar reed sourcing. Connect with Hooghly District Handloom Association. Study existing players like Tantuway and Sareeholic. Cost: ₹15,000-25,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source sar reed directly from local suppliers in Antpur/Haripal (₹8-12/kg); silk and cotton yarn from Varanasi merchants (₹200-400/kg). Partner with 2-3 weaver families for initial 50-100 saree batch. First-batch raw material cost: ₹80,000-120,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish direct artisan partnerships with master weavers in Haripal/Rajbalhat; provide raw materials and advance payments (₹500-800 per saree production cost). Avoid setting up your own looms initially—leverage existing community infrastructure. Timeline: 4-6 weeks for first batch.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Proprietorship under Udyam portal (MSME registration, 10 minutes online). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export. File GI product certification letter from Ministry of Handloom. Create brand identity emphasizing GI tag and artisan heritage. Cost: ₹5,000-10,000.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (10% commission) and Amazon Handmade India targeting global and domestic heritage buyers. Simultaneously approach ethnic wear boutiques in Delhi (Baba Market), Mumbai (Fort area), and Bangalore (Indiranagar). Offer first 20 units at 15% discount. Use #DhaniakhaliSaree, #HooghlySaree hashtags on Instagram with weaver story content.
6
Scale
After ₹10L in first-year revenue, expand weaver network to 10-15 families; launch seasonal collections aligned with festivals. Enter corporate gifting via platforms like GiftNifty. Open pop-up stores in metro malls during wedding seasons. Build B2B relationships with luxury hotel chains for in-room retail.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25-35% subsidy on startup cost (₹10L-25L) for manufacturing enterprises

West Bengal Handloom Development Scheme
20-30% subsidy on raw material procurement and technology upgrades for registered handloom businesses

Bengal Artisan Empowerment Scheme
direct working capital loans at 2% concessional interest for artisan-led production units
