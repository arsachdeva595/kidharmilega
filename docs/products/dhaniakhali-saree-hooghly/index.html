Dhaniakhali Saree ki
opportunity, Hooghly mein.

Dhaniakhali cluster is concentrated in 6 tehsils (Haripal, Rajbalhat, Rasidpur, Dwarhata, Ramnagar, Gurap, Antpur) within Hooghly district, 80km north of Kolkata. Sar reed bamboo is sourced locally and processed in-cluster, reducing raw material lead time. The cluster has deep artisan networks (estimated 200-400 weavers) and direct access to Kolkata's wholesale markets (Barabazar textile hubs) and Sealdah railway station for export dispatch.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Hooghly ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call Dhaniakhali Saree Museum (contact via Hooghly ODOP office) or visit Haripal/Rajbalhat cluster on a Tuesday to observe 2-3 weaving units. Attend Kolkata Book Fair or Craft Bazaar (search 'handloom mela Kolkata' for Feb/Nov dates) to see 5+ competitors' pricing and positioning. Register for Udyam (free, takes 30 mins online) and join 'West Bengal Handloom Artisans' WhatsApp groups.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 100-count cotton yarn from Barabazar cotton suppliers (Kolkata, ₹80-120/kg) or direct from mill agents in Tamil Nadu (bulk discount at ₹200kg+). Silk/zari from Surat suppliers (contact via CODISSIA or Sericultureboard.gov.in). Sar reed: negotiate with local processors in Haripal (₹30-50/kg, 50kg minimum). First 5 sarees: budget ₹3k-5k for raw materials if small batch with artisan.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 300 sqft weaving unit in Dhaniakhali cluster (₹3k-6k/month, shared shed available). Option B: Partner with 2-3 registered artisans on per-piece payment (₹800-1.5k per saree labor cost, 2-4 week turnaround). Option C: Buy a second-hand loom + rent space (₹40k loom + ₹50k initial setup). Expect ₹150k-300k for first 3-month cycle (materials + labor).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (₹0, online, instant). Apply for GI tag use certificate from DC Handloom office (₹500-2k, 6-8 weeks). Get IEC from DGFT portal (₹0, online) if planning export. File MSME registration (₹0, Udyam covers this). Cost: ₹5k total, timeline: 8-10 weeks.
5
First Sale
List 5 sarees on Meesho/Instagram Shop (₹2.5k-4k each, aiming ₹4-6k retail). Target 1st order from a boutique in Delhi via LinkedIn outreach (expected order: 3-5 pieces, ₹8k-12k). Expected first-month revenue: ₹15k-25k (2-4 sarees sold via D2C + 1 boutique order).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (25-30 sarees/week): hire 1 quality-check person (₹8k/month), partner with 5-8 artisans instead of 2-3, invest ₹50k in professional photoshoot + catalog, exhibit at India Handloom Expo (Delhi, Jan-Feb) and Textile India (Bangalore). Negotiate bulk rates with cotton suppliers (₹70/kg at 500kg+). Launch export channel via Alibaba/Global Sources platform.

Honest downside: Handloom sarees have low margins (20-30% wholesale) so cash flow is tight. If sales don't hit ₹50k/month by month 6, you'll burn capital fast. Exit options: (1) Sell inventory to bulk buyers at 40-50% markdown (₹800-1.5k per saree), (2) Partner with existing boutique chain (Indie brands, Jaypore) for distribution instead of D2C, (3) Shift to B2B (wedding planners, corporate gifting) which is more predictable. Sunk cost: ₹1-2L registration + initial stock will be difficult to recover. Do not start if you can't absorb 6-month loss.
