Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kurung Kumey ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kurung Kumey's main cardamom clusters around Koloriang and Sarli villages (Jan–Feb); meet with district agriculture office and cooperative societies like ABCS (Arunachal Bamboo & Cardamom Society); attend Spice Board mandi visits in Kolkata and Kochi to understand market pricing.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source dried large cardamom directly from ABCS cooperative or registered farmers in Sarli-Koloriang belt at ₹500–700/kg; first batch (500 kg) will cost ₹250K–350K including transport and storage setup.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with ABCS cooperative or individual farmer-processors for charring and sorting; alternatively, rent a 1000 sq ft space in Pasighat (nearest trade hub) for ₹8K/month and hire 2–3 skilled workers for fire-charring and grading at ₹12K/month each.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) at udyamregistration.gov.in (free); obtain IEC code from DGFT portal; register brand at ipindia.gov.in (₹9K); get FSSAI food license (₹5K–10K) and Spice Board export certification if targeting overseas markets.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Launchpad (spice category) and Flipkart Premium with ₹999/500g premium pack; pitch to organic/specialty retailers via IndiaMART and TradeKey; approach mid-range restaurants (biryani joints, high-end curry houses) in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai with samples; list on APEDA export portal for international inquiries.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish direct farmer contracts for guaranteed supply; expand to 5–10 restaurant partnerships per metro; apply for SFURTI cluster development grant if production exceeds 10 MT/year; hire a logistics partner (local or national) for D2C fulfillment; develop 2–3 value-added products (cardamom powder, oil extract) to increase margins.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for food processing ventures; 35% subsidy for general category in rural areas.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
support for spice clusters in Arunachal Pradesh, including infrastructure and marketing grants up to ₹10L per cluster.
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Agriculture & Allied Sector Development Programme
direct support for organic spice cultivation and processing units with subsidy on equipment.
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Export Promotion Policy
incentive for APEDA-registered exporters: 5% rebate on export value for agricultural products; facility for cold storage subsidies.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
