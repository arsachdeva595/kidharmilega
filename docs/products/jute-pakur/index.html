Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Pakur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pakur district jute clusters in Pakur town and surrounding villages; connect with existing ODOP artisans through DC Handicraft office; study jute fiber grades and traditional processing at National Institute of Design or handicraft training centers in Ranchi.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw jute plants from local farmers in Pakur (₹15,000-25,000 per 100 kg dried fiber); establish relationship with 3-4 farmer groups for consistent supply; first batch procurement: ₹80,000-120,000 for 500 kg quality fiber.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 8-12 established artisans/weavers in Pakur cluster through DC Handicraft's artisan registry; alternatively lease a small 500 sq ft production space in Pakur town (₹8,000-12,000/month) and hire trained weavers; establish quality control checkpoints for traditional hand-processing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise) via udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT Kolkata for export eligibility; register trademark with IP India; get Artisan Certificate from Directorate of Handicrafts; obtain GST registration.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart Handloom within first 60 days; approach eco-brands like Conscious Step and Ten Thousand Villages; pitch to corporate gifting platforms like CorporateGifting.com; attend India International Trade Fair (IITF) in Delhi; create Instagram/Pinterest with artisan storytelling content.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan partner base to 20-30; establish certified fair-trade partnerships with international importers; develop designer collaborations for premium product lines; set up a small showroom in Pakur town or partner city (Jharia/Godda); apply for SFURTI cluster development grant for infrastructure upgrades.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakh loan with 25% subsidy for manufacturing units in underdeveloped districts like Pakur.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster-level grants up to ₹50 Lakh for common facility centers, design development, and artisan training in jute handicrafts.
🏚
Jharkhand Artisan Development Scheme
provides direct skill training, production credit, and brand support for registered artisans in ODOP clusters.
🏚
Jharkhand Handicraft Export Promotion Scheme
partial subsidy for international trade fair participation and export documentation support.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
