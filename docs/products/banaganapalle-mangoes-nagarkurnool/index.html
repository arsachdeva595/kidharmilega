Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Nagarkurnool ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nagarkurnool mango clusters (Achampet, Tandur, Vemulawada villages). Connect with Nagarkurnool Mango Farmers Association. Study market dynamics at Secunderabad Fruit Market. Attend state agricultural department's mango cultivation training programs.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source saplings from certified nurseries in Tandur (₹200-300 per sapling, need 400-500 for 2 acre setup = ₹80,000-150,000). Negotiate with local fertilizer dealers for bulk orders. First-batch input cost: ₹150,000-250,000 for 2-acre farm setup.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing mango farmers in Nagarkurnool under contract farming model (40-50% revenue share) OR acquire 2-3 acres of cultivable land (₹8-12 lakhs per acre). Hire local farming consultants for crop management. Join cooperative societies like Nagarkurnool Farmers Cooperative for better support.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam certificate (online, free). Apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Hyderabad (₹0, online). Create brand: packaging design, FSSAI registration for processed products (₹10,000-15,000). Register trademark with IP India (₹4,000-9,000). Get organic certification if targeting premium segment (₹25,000-40,000).
5
First Sale
Launch D2C presence on Blink, BigBasket, Grofers, Fresho. Partner with Reliance Fresh and Spencer retail for in-store placement. Contact export traders in Hyderabad for international shipments. Use WhatsApp Business and Instagram for farm-to-table marketing. Negotiate with restaurants/5-star hotels for bulk orders.
6
Scale
After 6 months, invest in cold storage facility (₹20-30 lakhs for 50-ton capacity). Develop processing unit for value-added products (pulp, chips, pickle). Expand to 5-10 acres. Build export relationships with global fruit importers. Launch premium brand variant for international markets at 2.5x domestic pricing.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy for agri-based enterprise with 15-35% government subsidy on machinery and equipment
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹30 lakhs for processing unit, branding, and marketing for agricultural value-added products
🏚
Telangana Fruit Farmers Protection Scheme
₹10,000-15,000 per acre subsidy for mango cultivation and irrigation equipment
🏚
Telangana Agriculture Gold Loan
up to ₹50 lakhs at 4% interest for agricultural enterprises with land collateral through cooperative banks
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
