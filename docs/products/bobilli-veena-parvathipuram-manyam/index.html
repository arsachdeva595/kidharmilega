The district's cultural heritage, proximity to traditional music centers, and established supply chains for wood and craft materials make it the natural home for this specialized instrument.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Parvathipuram Manyam ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bobilli cluster in Parvathipuram Manyam; meet 3-5 master craftsmen; study at Surbandhan Music Academy in Vijayawada; research export trends on Alibaba and craft export data from APEDA.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source seasoned teak wood from timber merchants in Nellore or Tirupati (₹15,000-20,000 per unit); brass frets from Jaipur suppliers (₹2,000-3,000 per set); nylon strings from Chennai music suppliers. First batch of 5 instruments: ₹75,000-90,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 registered artisans in Bobilli through local clusters; establish workshop space (500 sq ft rented for ₹5,000/month); create production schedule for 2-3 instruments/month starting.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free); apply for IEC code via DGFT; trademark brand name; get GI certification letter from ministry; open MSME bank account; obtain Handicraft Export Certificate.
5
First Sale
List on Etsy (15-20% commission), Flipkart Handicraft, and Amazon Artisan; contact 10 music schools for institutional orders; reach out to classical music YouTubers for reviews; launch Instagram showcasing making process.
6
Scale
Hire 2-3 apprentices; increase production to 6-8 units/month; launch export campaigns to Singapore and UK through music retailer networks; establish B2B relationships with 5-10 music institutions; create premium 'Master Series' product line.
