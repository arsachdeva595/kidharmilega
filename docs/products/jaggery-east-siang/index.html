opportunity, East Siang mein.

East Siang district in Arunachal Pradesh sits at the foothills with direct access to sugarcane, date palm, and coconut plantations, eliminating middleman costs for raw materials. The district's growing cluster of 40-50 small jaggery units operates at ₹1-3 crore annual capacity, supplying regional markets and increasingly international buyers. Proximity to Guwahati (750 km) and seaports via road enables export logistics to North America and Europe within 25-30 days.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — East Siang ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3-4 existing jaggery makers in Pasighat or Pangin (East Siang towns) — ask their raw material costs, production per batch, and buyer channels. Attend the weekly haat in Pasighat on Thursdays. Register free on Udyam portal. Zero cost, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source sugarcane juice from local farmer networks in Pangin or Namsai blocks (15-30 km radius) at ₹8-12 per liter; date palm and coconut sap from Papum Pare district suppliers at ₹6-10 per liter. First 500 kg batch costs ₹28,000-35,000 in raw materials. Lock suppliers with written informal agreements.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 400-500 sqft unit in Pasighat industrial area or join an existing 3-4 person artisan group on revenue-share (40% to artisans, 60% to operator). Setup: large steel vessel (₹12,000), stirring paddle (₹2,000), molds/trays (₹5,000), thermometer (₹800), weighing scale (₹3,000). Total setup ₹18,000-25,000. Rent ₹5,000-8,000/month. Produce 20-30 kg per day in season (Oct-May).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online). Apply for FSSAI registration (₹12,000-18,000, 15-20 days). Packaging design and printing (₹8,000-12,000 for 5,000 units). GI status not available for East Siang jaggery — focus on organic/natural certifications if exporting (APEDA organic cert ₹25,000-35,000). IEC code registration for export (₹1,500, 5 days via customs broker).
5
First Sale
First sale: List on BigBasket seller panel (₹500 listing fee) with 5 kg starter batch. Or directly approach 2-3 organic food stores in Itanagar/Guwahati with 25 kg samples. Expected first order ₹8,000-15,000. First-month revenue ₹40,000-65,000 (12-18 kg sold D2C + wholesale).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 2 production staff (₹8,000/month each), 1 packing/QC person (₹6,000), 1 sales coordinator (₹7,000). Expand production to 150-200 kg/month. Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹25L loan, 35% subsidy on equipment). Set up branded packaging and cold storage (₹1.5-2L). Target export certifications and approach 5-6 European distributors. Participate in Guwahati Food Expo and Delhi Organic Expo.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹25 lakh loan at 5% interest, 35% subsidy (₹8.75 lakh) for food processing startups meeting criteria
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹25 lakh cluster development grant for jaggery clusters in Arunachal Pradesh, infrastructure and training support
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Enterprise Development Scheme
5% interest subsidy on MSME loans up to ₹10 lakh, soft loans for food processing units with 3-year repayment holiday
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh One District One Product (ODOP) scheme
15-20% subsidy on branding, packaging, certification costs for jaggery units; marketing support at state/national fairs
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
