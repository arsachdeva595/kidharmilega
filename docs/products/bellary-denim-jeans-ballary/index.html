Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Ballary ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bellary textile cluster (Ballari Nagar area) and meet existing denim manufacturers; study Tiruppur denim production (40 km radius) for benchmarking; attend India Tex Summit or International Denim Show to understand market trends.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source denim fabric from Ludhiana mills (₹180-250/meter for quality cotton) or Tiruppur suppliers; order 1000-1500 meters for pilot batch (₹2.5-3.5L); source hardware from Jaipur suppliers; first batch budget: ₹4-5L for 500 pairs including labor.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established garment units in Bellary industrial area or hire 3-5 skilled tailors in home-based unit; invest in basic machinery (flat lock machine ₹80K, buttonhole machine ₹60K, press ₹40K); begin with job-work from existing manufacturers to build expertise.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under MSE (Micro/Small category); obtain IEC from nearest DGFT office in Bangalore; trademark brand name with IPAB; obtain FSSAI equivalent textile compliance; register GST; create brand identity with local denim artisan story.
5
First Sale
Launch pilot on Instagram and Facebook with behind-the-scenes manufacturing content; approach 10-15 multi-brand retailers in Bangalore, Hyderabad, Pune; list on Meesho and Snapdeal (low commission, higher margins); create WhatsApp Business catalog for wholesale inquiries; attend regional trade fairs.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to own D2C website with SEO; partner with fashion influencers in tier-2 cities; apply for PMEGP for ₹10-15L expansion grant; establish regional distribution hubs; introduce premium variants and collaborations with local artisans; target 50-100 retail partners by month 12.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L grant (25% subsidy for general category) for textile manufacturing unit setup
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 credit plus skills training for traditional textile artisans involved in production
🏚
Karnataka Start-up Seed Fund
up to ₹10L for registered startups in textile sector with incubation support
🏚
Karnataka Textile Policy 2020
GST refund support and infrastructure subsidy for registered textile units in clusters
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
