Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Yamunanagar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Yamunanagar industrial clusters (Radaur, Saharanpur Road) to meet 20-30 established utensil makers; attend IITF New Delhi or IHGF exhibitions; study competitor catalogs on 1mg, Pepperfry, ThinkKitchen; join YMCA or CII Yamunanagar chapters.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source stainless steel 304 sheets from Jindal Steel (Hisar) or SAIL distributors; copper from Hindustan Copper dealers in Mandi (Himachal) or Delhi; budget ₹2-3L for 5-ton initial purchase; negotiate 30-day credit terms; source handles/knobs from Rajpura (Punjab) suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-4 experienced artisans in Yamunanagar clusters (average cost ₹15-20 per unit for production); or lease a 500 sqft unit in Radaur industrial estate (₹8-12K/month) and hire 2-3 skilled workers; invest in basic machinery (hydraulic press ₹150K, polishing unit ₹80K, welding setup ₹60K); quality check every 50-unit batch.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) registration online via udyamregistration.gov.in (free); obtain IEC code from DGFT for exports; register trademark with IP India; get ISI certification for stainless steel products (₹40-60K cost); open HDFC/ICICI current account with minimal 6-month manufacturing proof.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon.in & Flipkart within 90 days with 50-unit starter catalog; target kitchenware category; list on IndiaMART & TradeKey for B2B; reach out to 50 retail chains (FabIndia, Fab Home, Kitchen Tales) with samples; pitch to 10 export agents in Delhi/Bangalore; build basic WordPress website with payment gateway.
6
Scale
After ₹10L cumulative revenue, double production capacity via 2 additional artisans; launch 3-4 new SKU lines (specialty cookware, serving sets); apply for PMEGP subsidy for expansion; hire salesperson for institutional sales; explore Amazon Global for UK/Germany; attend Frankfurt Ambiente or Stuttgart housewares fairs; build influencer partnerships with food/kitchen YouTubers.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (25% for General, 35% for SC/ST) for manufacturing unit setup in ODOP category.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15K grant + 5% concessional loans for registered artisans and microenterprises in traditional metalwork.
🏚
Haryana New Enterprise Incentive Scheme (HNIS)
5% capital subsidy for small manufacturing units; ₹5-25L eligible project cost.
🏚
Haryana ODOP Fund
dedicated support for One District One Product manufacturers including credit guarantee and market linkage support.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
