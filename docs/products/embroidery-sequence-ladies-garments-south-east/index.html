South-East Delhi has centuries-old embroidery craftsmanship rooted in Mughal textile traditions. The district hosts concentrated clusters of skilled artisans in Kalkaji, Ghitorni, and Mehrauli who specialize in zari and zardozi work. Proximity to Delhi's wholesale markets (Chandni Chowk, Jama Masjid) and retail hubs provides direct access to pan-India distribution networks.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — South-East ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit embroidery clusters in Kalkaji and Ghitorni; spend 2 weeks observing artisans at work. Connect with established manufacturers via Delhi Chamber of Commerce. Analyze competitor offerings on Myntra and Flipkart Fashion. Budget: ₹15,000-25,000 for research trips and market intelligence.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source zari/sequin materials from wholesale suppliers in Chandni Chowk (Ballimaran area) or Jama Masjid lanes. Initial fabric purchase: contact suppliers like Shreeram Enterprises or V-Tex for ₹2-3 lakhs first batch. Join SEWA (Self Employed Women's Association) for bulk material discounts.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 experienced embroidery artisans in Kalkaji (avg. ₹300-500/piece labor cost). Establish a small design studio (₹2-3 lakhs rent/setup). Use WhatsApp/email for order tracking. Start with 50-100 pieces/month production capacity.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (single-day online via udyamregistration.gov.in). Obtain IEC code from DGFT for export. Trademark brand name via IP India. Register GST (estimated 5% slab). Open bank account for business. Total cost: ₹30,000-50,000.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram/Pinterest with lookbook photography (₹40,000 for professional shoot). List on Meesho with 20-30 initial SKUs. Email wholesalers on curated lists (100 boutiques in NCR region). Run Facebook ads targeting wedding planners (₹500/day budget). First target: 10-15 orders/month at ₹8,000-15,000 per piece.
6
Scale
Post 6 months: hire 2 in-house designers; expand artisan network to 10-15 people; launch own Shopify store; apply for export orders via TradeKey. Target ₹25L revenue by month 12. Seek PMEGP subsidy (₹50L max) for capacity expansion by month 8-10.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹50 lakh subsidy (35% for general) for micro-enterprises in textile/apparel
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 grant + 8% interest subvention for artisanal embroidery workers in supply chain
🏚
Delhi Enterprise Promotion Fund (DEPF)
subsidized interest rates (2-4%) on loans up to ₹10 lakh for textile MSMEs
🏚
Delhi Women Enterprise Fund (DWEF)
50% interest subvention for women entrepreneurs in fashion/textiles
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
