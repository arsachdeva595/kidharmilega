Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Tirunelveli ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tirunelveli's garment clusters (Pallivasal, Nagercoil Road) and Madurai textile markets; connect with existing garment manufacturers through TEXTECH (Tamil Nadu Textile Technology Centre); attend fabric trade shows at International Textile Centre (ITC), Chennai; study competitors on Meesho, Amazon, Flipkart who source from the region
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fabrics from Madurai Textile Market (₹40-80/meter for cotton blends), embellishments from Tirunelveli local suppliers (₹5,000-15,000 for starter bulk), threads from Coimbatore suppliers; first batch of 500 units costs approximately ₹2-3 lakhs depending on complexity and embroidery
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established garment units in Tirunelveli via TVIC (Tamil Nadu Apparel Cluster) for contract manufacturing, or hire 3-5 skilled tailors and embroiderers for in-house production; ensure quality control through size grading and fit samples; establish production timeline of 15-25 days per order
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (MSME) on udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT (40-50 days); register trademark at IP India for brand name; get GST registration (18% on garments); compliance includes factory license, ISO certification (optional but valued for B2B)
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho, Flipkart, Amazon with 50-100 SKUs; build Instagram/TikTok presence showcasing garment design process and customer testimonials; approach local boutiques and retail chains in Tamil Nadu, Karnataka, Telangana for wholesale orders (MOQ 50+ units); list on Business-to-business platforms like IndiaMART and TradeKey; participate in SIAM exhibitions and Chennai Fashion Week
6
Scale
Expand product range to include kids wear, plus sizes, and seasonal collections; invest in automated embroidery machines (₹8-15 lakhs) for faster production and consistency; build B2B export pipeline through Confederation of Indian Textile Industry (CITI); establish distribution centers in metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore); scale to 2,000-5,000 monthly units within 12-18 months
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister's Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs loan at 5% subsidy for manufacturing units with 100+ employment potential
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹40 lakhs for cluster development, training, and marketing in Tirunelveli garment cluster
🏚
Tamil Nadu Textile Policy 2017
₹50 lakhs subsidy for new textile manufacturing units; exemption from electricity surcharge for garment units
🏚
TNSDC (Tamil Nadu Skill Development Centre)
free training programs for garment design, embroidery, and quality control; placement assistance for hiring skilled workers
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
