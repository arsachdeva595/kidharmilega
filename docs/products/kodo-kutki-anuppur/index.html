Kodo Kutki ki
opportunity, Anuppur mein.

Anuppur's agro-climatic conditions—semi-arid terrain, adequate rainfall during monsoon, and iron-rich black soil—are ideal for Kodo Kutki cultivation. The district has a 50+ year tradition of growing these millets as a staple food crop, with established farmer networks and deep knowledge of seed selection and harvest timing. The lower production costs and availability of skilled agricultural labor make it the natural sourcing hub for this ODOP.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Anuppur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Anuppur's agricultural extension office (Block: Anuppur), connect with millet farmer cooperatives like Anuppur Millet Producers Association, and spend 2-3 weeks shadowing farms during harvest season (September-October). Research millet markets at ITC e-Choupal centers and NCDP (National Coarse Cereals Development Program) data centers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact registered seed suppliers and cooperative societies in Anuppur district; source 5 tonnes of Kodo+Kutki grain mix at ₹25-30 per kg (total ₹125-150K for first batch). Establish relationships with 3-4 farmer groups for consistent supply contracts. Visit Indore Agriculture Market (APMC) for bulk pricing benchmarks.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with SFURTI-registered millet processing units in Anuppur or Shahdol district for cleaning, grading, and packaging (₹3-5 per kg processing cost). Alternatively, lease a 1000 sq ft facility and invest in a small grain cleaner (₹200-400K), destoner, and packaging machine. Hire 2-3 skilled processors initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small Enterprise) with District Industries Center, obtain IEC code from the nearest DGFT office for export, acquire FSSAI food business license (online, ₹5-10K), and register trademark with IP India. Develop packaging with nutritional labels and sustainability story in Hindi and English.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Pantry and Flipkart Health+Nutrition categories with minimum 50kg stock; simultaneously pitch to 10-15 premium organic retailers in Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore using direct outreach. Send samples to 5 export-focused importers from TradeKey and Global Sources platforms. Create Instagram and LinkedIn presence highlighting farm-to-table story and gluten-free benefits.
6
Scale
After 6 months, if volume >50 tonnes/month: establish certified organic status (APEDA, ₹50-100K), launch private-label partnerships with e-commerce platforms, expand export to 3-5 new countries via distributor networks, and develop value-added products (flour, flakes, mixes). Build a direct-to-consumer subscription model with monthly recurring revenue targeting ₹20L+ ARR.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
up to ₹25L subsidy (25-35% for SC/ST/women entrepreneurs) for agri-processing unit setup
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹40L soft loan for millet processing cluster development and equipment procurement
🏚
Mukhya Mantri Swanirman Yojana (MP)
₹12L interest-free loan for agricultural processing units with subsidy up to ₹3L
🏚
MP Bhavantar Bhugtan Yojana
price support and minimum support price (MSP) guarantees for coarse cereals like Kodo and Kutki to ensure farmer income stability
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
