Kodo Kutki ki
opportunity, Anuppur mein.

Anuppur district in central Madhya Pradesh sits in the Vindhya plateau zone (101°E-102°E longitude, 23°-24°N latitude) where tribal farmers have cultivated Kodo and Kutki millets for centuries on marginal soils unsuitable for rice/wheat. The district produces both varieties across Anuppur, Jabalpur, Katni, Mandla, Dindori, Chhindwara, Shahdol, Umaria, Rewa, Sidhi, and Singrauli procurement zones totaling approximately 30,000 metric tonnes annually. Buyer proximity includes tier-2 cities (Jabalpur 120km), Delhi metros via NRHM/PDS supply chains, and direct-to-consumer channels through organic and health-food platforms.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Anuppur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call Shri Ann Federation office in Anuppur (contact via ODOP district portal) to understand Kharif 2025 procurement process and documentation. Visit Anuppur grain mandi on a market day (Tuesday/Friday) and speak directly with 2-3 millet farmers and local aggregators. Attend a free NABARD/ATMA farming demonstration session on millet cultivation in Anuppur tehsil. Zero cost, data-gathering only.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cleaned Kodo and Kutki from farmer networks in Anuppur, Umaria, and Shahdol tehsils via Shri Ann Federation (primary source for bulk) or direct farmer contacts from step 1. First batch cost: ₹80,000-₹150,000 for 5-10 quintal mix depending on quality grade. Supplier ecosystem concentrated in Anuppur grain market and Umaria mandi.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner with existing millet processing units (dehulling, grading) in Anuppur town (rent ₹8,000-₹12,000/month for 500 sq ft space). Option B: Work with FMCG contract manufacturers in Jabalpur (55km away) for pre-cleaned/packaged product (₹25-₹40/kg contract cost). Initial working capital ₹3-₹5L for first 2 batches, packaging, and certification.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME online (free, 5 mins). Obtain FSSAI food license (₹2,000-₹5,000, 15-20 days). If exporting: apply for IEC code with DGFT (₹0, 5 days online). Consider GI registration for Anuppur Kodo/Kutki (optional, costs ₹20,000-₹30,000 but currently No GI status — brand as district-origin premium millet instead). Trademark brand name (₹5,000-₹10,000). Total timeline: 45-60 days.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh/BigBasket organic supplier portal with 100kg initial order (lead time 10-15 days). Offer 50kg trial packs to local ayurveda clinics and school diet departments at ₹300-₹350/kg wholesale. First month realistic revenue: ₹80,000-₹150,000 from D2C + B2B combined depending on platform traction.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 packing operator + 1 quality checker (₹15,000/month each). Invest ₹2-₹3L in semi-automatic packaging machinery. Expand farmer network from 3-4 villages to 12-15 villages across Anuppur and Mandla. Apply for MUDRA business loan (₹50L easy approval for MSME with 6 months traction). Exhibit at Natural Expo India (Delhi), Organic Farming Expo (Bangalore), ITC Srujan food fairs to access institutional buyers.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
MUDRA Yojana
up to ₹10 lakh collateral-free loan at 5-7% interest for MSME food processing businesses
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25-35% subsidy on fixed asset cost for food processing unit up to ₹25 lakh
🏚
Rani Durgavati Shree Ann Promotion Scheme
direct procurement at guaranteed rates (₹2,500 Kodo, ₹3,500 Kutki per quintal) plus ₹1,000/quintal farmer incentive via DBT for cultivators in notified districts
🏚
Bhavantar Scheme (Madhya Pradesh)
price support and deficiency payment scheme for registered millet cultivators during harvest gluts; applicable in Anuppur
