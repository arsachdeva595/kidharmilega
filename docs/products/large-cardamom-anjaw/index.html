Raw material flows directly from farmer associations — every household grows cardamom. Annual production of 150 metric tonnes feeds domestic spice traders in Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata, plus international buyers in Germany, USA, and UAE.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Anjaw ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit T'Pani or Watong cluster in Chaglagam circle (cost: ₹0 if you're local or ₹2-3K bus/jeep fare if external). Spend 2 days talking to 5-6 farmers and one existing processor. Call the Anjaw District Agriculture Office (local phone) and ask about cardamom cooperative societies and current crop health status. Attend the nearest fortnightly haat market and track wholesale cardamom prices for 4 weeks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cardamom directly from T'Pani, Metalliang, or Bomna cluster farmers through local cooperative societies or individual farmer networks. First batch: 50kg dried cardamom pods at ₹200-240/kg = ₹10-12K cost. Arrange transport from cluster to your processing unit via local jeep (₹1-2K). Build 3-month supply contracts with 2-3 farmer groups to ensure steady flow.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with an existing small processor in Watong or Hawai (₹50-80K/month rent for 500 sq ft unit with cleaning, sorting, drying equipment already installed) OR invest ₹4-6L in a standalone 800 sq ft unit with electric dryer, sorter, packaging machine, and quality-check tools. Hire 2-3 local workers at ₹8-12K/month each. First batch turnaround: 3-4 weeks from raw to packaged product.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (1 day, free, online at udyam.gov.in). Apply for GI registration support through Ministry of Agriculture (₹15-25K cost, 6-9 month process) — contact State GI Cell in Itanagar. If exporting, obtain IEC from APEDA (₹2K, 5-7 days at Guwahati office). Obtain FSSAI registration for packaged spices (₹2-5K, 10-15 days).
5
First Sale
First sale: 10kg packaged (100g × 100 units) to Delhi wholesale market (Chandni Chowk or Khari Baoli) via a local distributor or direct. Expected revenue: ₹25-30K from first 10kg at ₹250-300/100g wholesale. Simultaneously list on Amazon Fresh and Blinkit (100g packs at ₹450-550). Expect first month gross revenue ₹80-120K from mixed channels if you secure 2-3 bulk orders + online traction.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue, hire a full-time operations manager (₹20-25K) and a sales/export coordinator (₹18-22K). Move to 1,500 sq ft unit. Set up dedicated export documentation team or outsource via EODA (Export Oriented Development Authority). Exhibit at AAHAR Food Trade Show (Delhi), Gulfood (Dubai), or Spice Board events. Develop 3-4 SKUs (whole pods, powder, specialty blends). Build brand partnerships with 3-5 premium restaurants. Target ₹50L/month by month 18.

Informal cluster of 8-12 small cardamom processing units in and around Chaglagam circle towns. Available: 300-800 sq ft shared processing spaces with basic drying, sorting, and packing equipment. Existing tenants include local farmers' cooperative processing units and 2-3 private processors.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹25 lakh loan at 5% interest with 35% subsidy for SC/ST, 25% for general category, for processing and value-addition units.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹10-20 lakh grants for common facility centers, training, and market linkages in spice clusters.
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Agriculture Department ODOP Support
free technical training on modern drying, sorting, and export-quality processing; marketing support through state pavilions at national food trade shows.
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Mission
subsidized equipment (electric dryers, sorters) at 25-40% cost reduction for cardamom producers registered under state cooperatives.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
