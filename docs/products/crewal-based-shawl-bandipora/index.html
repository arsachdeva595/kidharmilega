Crewal Based Shawl ki
opportunity, Bandipora mein.

Bandipora district in north Kashmir spans 1,772 sq km with direct access to 300,000 sheep and established Gurezi breed infrastructure via 7,200 breeders. Wool is sourced locally at ₹80-120/kg vs ₹180-250/kg in Srinagar, cutting raw material cost by half. Srinagar (90 km) and Delhi (500 km) are major wholesale and export hubs; major buyers include luxury retailers in Paharganj, Mumbai Colaba, and international platforms.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bandipora ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bandipora main bazaar on a market day (Tuesday/Friday), meet active shawl artisans if any exist (contact Bandipora District Admin office via phone 01950-250266), call Kashmir Crafts Council Srinagar (+91-194-2455024) to understand current crewel supply chains. Attend a local haat at Sadura or Bandipora town. Zero cost, doable in 3 days.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Gurezi wool from registered breeders in Tangmarg (10 km away) or direct from Bandipora Sheep & Wool Development Center. First batch: ₹25,000-35,000 for 15-20 kg quality wool. Crewel threads from Craft bazaar Srinagar (online from Kashmir Crafts emporium, ₹8,000-12,000 for full color set). Total first-batch material cost: ₹40,000-50,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Artisan Partnership): Hire 1-2 trained crewel artisans from Srinagar at ₹300-500/day + ₹5,000-8,000/month retainer. Rent small 200 sq ft workspace in Bandipora town (₹3,000-5,000/month). Handcraft per shawl takes 25-35 days. Option B (Self-Learn): Train yourself at a Srinagar crewel studio (₹20,000-30,000 for 4-6 week course), then produce solo. Total setup: ₹1.5-3L for space, tools, first artisan batch, stock.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 10 mins). Apply for GI registration support via Ministry of Textiles ODOP scheme (no cost, 2-3 month process). If exporting: IEC registration via DGFT (₹0, online). Get MSME certificate from Bandipora District Industries Centre (free, 5 days). Trademark brand name via IP India (₹5,000-8,000, 18-month process).
5
First Sale
First sale: List 5-8 pieces on Instagram shop + own Shopify store (₹5,000/month setup). Post 3x/week crewel-making reels, behind-the-scenes, customer testimonials. First month target: 2-3 units via organic reach = ₹24,000-45,000 revenue. Simultaneously send 15-20 units to Kashmir Crafts Council consignment network (3 week selling window, 40-50% margin after commission). Expect first institutional order by Month 2.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month (15-20 shawls/month): Hire 2-3 full-time artisans (₹12,000-15,000/month each). Move to 500 sq ft unit (₹8,000-10,000/month). Secure export contract with 1-2 international agents (3-4 month lead time). Launch WhatsApp Business catalog for B2B buyers. Exhibit at Kashmir Crafts Expo (Jan), India Handlooms Expo (Feb), IITF Delhi (Nov) — ₹80,000-120,000 per booth, 20-40 qualified leads per event. By Month 18, target ₹20L/month and hire a Sales + Export ops person.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMFME (Pradhan Mantri Formalisation of Micro food enterprises)
35% subsidy up to ₹10L for Micro Enterprises registered under Udyam, covers machinery and working capital
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 initial grant + ₹5L collateral-free MUDRA loan at 5% interest subsidy for registered artisans, toolkit support ₹500-15,000 depending on craft
🏚
J&K Entrepreneurship Development Institute (EDI) subsidy
25% setup cost support up to ₹5L for Micro & Small Enterprises in handicrafts sector, online application at jkedi.nic.in
🏚
J&K Textile Development Fund
Market linkage support, subsidy for participation in national/international exhibitions (50-75% cost), custom branding material ₹50,000-1,00,000
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
