opportunity, Dadra & Nagar Haveli mein.

Dadra & Nagar Haveli (3,491 km²) borders Gujarat and Maharashtra, placing it within 200 km of Surat (textile hub), Aurangabad (silk cluster), and the Indore-Malwa region producing Eri and Tasar cocoons. The union territory lacks GI status but sits in India's second-largest silk ecosystem: FY25 national silk production was 41,121 MT (up from 38,913 MT in FY24), with neighboring states dominating Mulberry (Karnataka, Andhra Pradesh) and Tasar (Chhattisgarh, Odisha) supply. Yarn exports to EU and USA grew consistently; local artisans specialize in hand-reeling techniques that command 20-30% price premiums over synthetic alternatives.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Dadra & Nagar Haveli ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dadra town textile cooperative to meet silk suppliers; call 3-5 Surat yarn traders (ask them: who buys specialty silk yarn, what counts as premium?); attend Daman textile fair (bi-annual, free entry); search 'silk yarn suppliers Dadra' on IndiaFilings and contact 5 registered units asking for reeler networks — zero cost, completable in 10 days
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cocoons from Andhra Pradesh cocoon mandis (Karimnagar, Warangal) or farmer networks via Sericulture Board contact (first batch: 500 kg cocoons = ₹35-45K); alternatively, buy ready-to-reel silk from established reelers in Belgaum, Karnataka (₹600-800/kg for Mulberry sliver). Establish relationship with 2-3 primary suppliers on 30-day credit.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 500-800 sq ft unit in Dadra industrial area (₹3-5K/month); purchase hand-reeling charkha (₹25-40K) or semi-automatic spindle setup (₹80-120K). Partner with 5-8 local women artisans trained in silk reeling (₹180-220/kg reeling wage); production: 20-30 kg/month per artisan (100-150 kg capacity within 6 months). Initial setup: ₹180-250K for unit + equipment + 1-month raw material stock.
4
Brand & Register
Register on Udyam MSME portal (free, online, 30 mins); apply for PM Vishwakarma scheme if partnering with artisans (₹15K non-repayable credit); obtain IEC code (₹500, Ministry of Commerce, 7 days); GI registration not required (no GI status for this product in this district, but apply to state textile department for 'Made in Dadra' certification (₹2-3K, 45 days). Open EXIM bank account for exports (zero cost).
5
First Sale
First sale: approach Surat textile traders directly (visit office, bring 500g samples); typical first order: 5-10 kg @ ₹400/kg = ₹2,000-4,000. Post samples on Instagram (hashtag #SilkYarnSupplier); reach out to 50 boutique designers via LinkedIn. Target first month: 1-2 orders totaling 15-20 kg = ₹6-8K revenue. Use WhatsApp groups of apparel manufacturers (search 'Tiruppur Hosiery Exporters Group').
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (~120 kg/month): hire production supervisor (₹8-10K/month), add 4-5 more artisans, upgrade to semi-automatic reeling (₹2-3L equipment). Exhibit at Texfair India (Surat, annual), India International Silk Fair, Agrotech Fair. Register as a formal SSI unit and apply for PLI scheme (Production Linked Incentive: ₹10-30L subsidy for ₹1Cr+ annual production, 5% subsidy on incremental exports). Build B2B partnerships with 10-15 steady buyers on 60-90 day credit.
