Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — South Andamans ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Port Blair Fish Market (open 5am) or call 3 licensed cold-chain operators listed on APEDA directory to understand daily catch quality, pricing volatility, and storage logistics. Attend the quarterly Port Blair Seafood Exporters Association meeting (free, details on UT fisheries website). Zero cost, this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source from licensed fishing vessels at Port Blair jetty (450+ active boats) or pre-arranged daily supplies from 8 registered suppliers clustered at Haddo area. First batch cost: ₹1.5-2.5L for 500 kg mixed jumbo prawns/squid depending on season. Direct supply agreements lock in ₹240-280/kg wholesale rates.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 300-400 sqft HACCP-certified cold-processing unit in Haddo or Phoenix Bay Industrial area (₹8-12K/month). Partner with a registered food safety consultant to set up IQF freezing lines (cost ₹15-25L for equipment, can be leased at ₹3-4K/month). Hire 2-3 trained gutting/processing staff at ₹8-10K/month each. Start with semi-processed (headed, gutted) shrimp to minimize labour cost.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, online, 10 mins). Apply for APEDA registration (₹1-2L fee, 6-8 weeks processing) mandatory for exports. Obtain Food Safety License (FSSAI) from UT authority (₹2-3K, 2 weeks). Get IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Port Blair (free, 5 days). Post-registration, file GI application for 'South Andaman Prawns' (₹25K filing fee, 18-24 month approval timeline) to unlock 15% export premium.
5
First Sale
First sale: list IQF jumbo prawns (2 kg vacuum packs) on Amazon Fresh or Blinkit (takes 2 weeks approval). Minimum first order: 10 kg/day from one restaurant chain via LinkedIn outreach or APEDA exporter network. Expected first-month revenue: ₹50-75K from 2-3 channel mix (D2C ₹20K, wholesale ₹35K, local resort supply ₹10-20K).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire a supply-chain manager (₹25K/month) to source from 5+ fishing vessels daily, upgrade to 800 sqft unit (₹15K/month). Hire 5-6 processing staff and 1 export coordinator. Exhibit at SEAFEX (Port Blair, annual), register with 2-3 online APEDA platforms, and launch branded packaging. By ₹25L/month, invest ₹50L in own IQF equipment and cold storage (ROI 18-22 months).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy for manufacturing units up to ₹25L, covers equipment, cold storage, building lease for first 2 years
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹1-3 Cr cluster grants for seafood processing, offered by Ministry of Fisheries to registered MSME clusters in Andaman
🏚
Andaman and Nicobar Islands Seafood Export Promotion Scheme
10% subsidy on cold storage rental and export logistics (airfreight to mainland ports), available via UT Fisheries Dept
🏚
Fisheries Infrastructure Development Fund (FIDF)
₹50L-1Cr loans at 4% interest for cold-chain and processing units in A&N, disbursed via NABARD
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
