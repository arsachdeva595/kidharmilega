Bidari ware ki
opportunity, Bidar mein.

Bidar district in north Karnataka has been the sole production center of Bidari ware for four centuries, with raw materials (zinc alloy, silver, gold) available through established suppliers in the old town cluster. The district's location 150km from Hyderabad and 250km from Bangalore provides direct access to affluent buyer markets. Government training institutes and cooperative networks support artisan partnerships and skill transfer.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bidar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bidar old town Bidari cluster — walk through Jumma Bazar and surrounding artisan lanes, observe production, talk to 3-4 established artisans or cooperative members (free, 1 day). Call Bidar DC Handicraft office (0584-222111) to understand training programs and GI export guidelines.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source zinc alloy, silver, and gold from established suppliers in Bidar old town — first batch of 20 pieces (mixed items) costs ₹60,000-90,000. Alternatively, contract work with cooperative members in Jumma Bazar cluster on piece-rate basis (₹800-2000 per finished item depending on complexity).
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent 400-600 sqft workshop in Bidar old town (₹8,000-12,000/month) and hire trained artisan (₹12,000-18,000/month) + your design/finishing labor. Option B: Partner with existing cooperative or individual master artisan as middleman (no rent, lower margin). Startup cost range ₹3-8L covers tools (₹40K), first raw material batch (₹80K), rent deposit (₹25K), initial artisan wages (₹3-6L for 3-6 months).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 30 mins). Apply for GI certification under ODOP scheme via DC Handicraft Bidar (₹0 cost, 6-8 weeks processing). If exporting, obtain IEC from nearest DGFT office in Hyderabad or Bangalore (₹0, online). Register trademark for brand name with IP office (₹4,500-9,000, 8-12 months).
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy with 15-20 product listings (photos, descriptions, pricing ₹2,500-12,000 per piece). List simultaneously on Amazon Handmade. First month target: 2-4 orders at average ₹5,000 each = ₹10-20K revenue. Approach 2-3 home décor retailers in Bangalore with wholesale pricing (₹1,800-4,000 per piece). Attend Hyderabad Gift Fair or New Delhi Handicrafts Trade Fair (booth cost ₹15-25K, expect 5-8 trade inquiries).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire a second artisan (₹15K/month), expand workshop to 800 sqft (₹15-18K/month), hire order fulfillment person (₹10K/month). Register IEC and open export account for bulk shipments to UAE and US importers. Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹10-25L loan) or MUDRA Business Loan (₹50L) for capacity expansion. Exhibit at International Trade Fairs (Delhi International Gifts Fair, Bangalore Exporting Forum). Develop 5-6 signature designs that can be mass-produced by multiple artisans.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 initial grant + 5% interest subsidy on MUDRA loans up to ₹1 lakh for registered artisans
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25-35% subsidy on setup cost (up to ₹25 lakh loan) for registered craft business, processing via nearest KVIC office
🏚
Karnataka Handloom Development Board
equipment subsidy up to 50% for tool purchases and workshop setup
🏚
Karnataka ODOP Export Promotion Scheme
₹2-5 lakh reimbursement for trade fair participation and export certification (check with DC Handicraft)
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
