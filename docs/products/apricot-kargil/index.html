Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kargil ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kargil apricot clusters near Drass and Panikhar villages; study GI certification standards on IndiaIP.gov; connect with existing producer groups at Kargil Agricultural Produce Cooperative Society; analyze export protocols at Leh Chamber of Commerce
2
Source Raw Materials
Source saplings from Leh Horticulture Department nurseries (₹50-70 per sapling); partner with local farmer groups in Kargil valley for bulk procurement; first 5-ton batch estimated at ₹2-3 lakhs including collection and packaging
3
Produce / Partner
Establish processing unit with cold storage facility (₹8-12 lakhs) or outsource to registered FSSAI-approved processors in Leh; partner with 20-30 local farmers through contract farming model; implement organic certification (APEDA/FSSAI) to unlock export premium
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam via udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT within 3 days; register brand at Ladakh Trademark Registry; get APEDA export certification (₹30,000) for organic status; obtain GI tag usage license from Ministry of Agriculture
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket, Blinkit, and Flipkart Fresh with premium positioning; approach Aditya Birla Fashion Group's organic retail partners; contact premium gifting platforms (Winni, FNP); reach out to European organic importers via Trade India portal; initiate negotiations with Whole Foods and other specialty retailers
6
Scale
Expand to 50+ farmer partnerships by month 8; launch dried apricot and value-added product lines; enter 5-10 international markets via e-commerce platforms; open pop-up stores in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai; pursue government subsidy for cold chain infrastructure (PMEGP); develop B2B contracts with chocolate and confectionery brands
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (25% for general, 35% for SC/ST) for food processing and agricultural value addition
🏛
APEDA Export Promotion Scheme
50% subsidy on certification, packaging, and marketing for agricultural exports; covers organic and GI products
🏚
Ladakh Agriculture Department's Organic Farming Promotion Scheme
₹3 lakh subsidy per acre for organic certification and high-value crops
🏚
Ladakh GFIS (Good For India Scheme)
support for GI product producers in branding, marketing, and e-commerce platform integration
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
