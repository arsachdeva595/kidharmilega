Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Lohardaga ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Lohardaga beekeeping cluster in Lohardaga town (free) — meet 2-3 established beekeepers to understand seasonal cycles, honey yields, and local practices. Call Lohardaga ATMA (Agricultural Technology Management Agency) office to request cluster details. Attend Ranchi wholesale market (Choti Chhatri or Ratu) to see current honey pricing — all free, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source from local beekeepers in Lohardaga and Gumla tehsils — first batch of 50-100 kg raw honey costs ₹15000-25000 depending on quality (₹150-250/kg wholesale from producer). Build relationships with 3-4 beekeepers for consistent supply during Oct-Dec (peak season) and lighter supply Jun-Sep.
3
Produce / Partner
Option 1: Partner with 1-2 established beekeepers as aggregator (lowest cost, ₹0 production capex). Option 2: Rent a 400-500 sq ft unit in Lohardaga town for honey processing/bottling (₹8000-12000/month) and buy basic equipment (strainer, bottling machine, capping) for ₹50000-80000. Start with artisan partnership model to test market before investing in own facility.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 10 min). File GI registration application with state agriculture ministry (₹5000-10000 fees, 6-9 month process) to establish Lohardaga honey authenticity — critical for premium pricing and export. If exporting, apply for IEC via DGFT (₹5000-8000, 2 weeks). Get FSSAI food license (₹10000-15000, 30 days). Total regulatory cost: ₹30000-50000.
5
First Sale
First sale: List on Amazon Fresh (15% commission) or BigBasket (10-12% commission) with 250g and 500g jars at ₹450-550. Parallel approach: sell 20-30 kg to Ranchi wellness stores (direct outreach, 35% margin). Target first month: 50-80 kg sales across channels = ₹25000-40000 revenue, ₹8000-15000 net profit after costs.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 part-time packaging assistant (₹10000/month), invest in branded packaging (₹40000), run Instagram/Facebook ads targeting health-conscious 25-45 age group (₹15000/month budget). Exhibit at AIHEX (All India Honey and Agri-Export Fair) in Delhi or Kolkata (₹50000-70000 per year). Expand to B2B corporate gifting (Diwali season) and institutional buyers (ayurveda companies). Explore honey + turmeric, honey + ginger variants to increase AOV.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM-KISAN SAMMAN NIDHI
₹6000/year to registered beekeeping farmers for inputs and equipment (if eligible as beekeeper)
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% for SC/ST, 25% general) for honey processing unit with matching capex; 5-year moratorium on loan repayment
🏚
Jharkhand State Beekeeping Promotion Scheme
₹10000-15000 subsidy per beekeeping unit for equipment and training (check Jharkhand Agriculture Department for current eligibility)
🏚
Jharkhand One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
tax benefits, priority lending via SIDBI, brand development support, and export facilitation assistance for honey producers registered under district ODOP
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
