Carpet (Panja Dari) ki
opportunity, Sidhi mein.

Sidhi district has a centuries-old tradition of carpet weaving inherited from local tribal and artisan communities. The region's access to high-quality wool from nearby pastoral areas and the skilled labor force trained in traditional patterns make it naturally suited for premium handwoven carpet production. The cultural heritage of geometric and floral designs unique to Sidhi provides a distinctive market identity.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sidhi ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sidhi town and surrounding villages (Singrauli, Chitrangi) to study existing weaver communities; interact with DC Handicraft office in Sidhi; visit carpet clusters in Banaras (Uttar Pradesh) to understand market benchmarks and design trends; study 5-6 successful ODOP carpet businesses in similar districts.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source merino wool from Rajasthan wool markets (Bikaner, Jodhpur) at ₹200-280/kg; procure cotton yarn from textile hubs in Maharashtra; identify natural dye suppliers in Indigo clusters; first batch cost estimate: ₹80,000-120,000 for 8-10 medium-sized (4x6 ft) sample carpets.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 5-8 skilled weavers in Sidhi district through DC Handicraft's artisan database; provide them wool and design specifications; establish weekly quality checkpoints; alternatively, start with small-scale production in a 500 sq ft rented workshop in Sidhi town with 2-3 looms; hire trained weavers at ₹8,000-12,000/month per person.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT for export capability; register brand trademark with IP India; acquire GST registration; get DC Handicraft certification for ODOP authenticity; create product authentication cards with artisan details to enhance D2C premium positioning.
5
First Sale
List on Etsy with detailed photography and artisan stories; approach 15-20 interior designers and luxury hotels in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with sample carpets and wholesale pricing; attend Numaish (Indore handicraft expo) and Indian Handicraft Fair (Delhi); leverage Instagram reels showing weaving process; partner with 1-2 luxury home décor e-commerce platforms; initiate email outreach to corporate procurement teams.
6
Scale
After 6 months and ₹15-20L revenue, expand artisan network to 20-30 weavers; invest in better natural dye processing setup; launch 2-3 signature design collections; hire dedicated export manager; approach international B2B platforms like Global Sources and Alibaba; open small showroom in Indore (200 km away, major city); develop corporate gifting packages; collaborate with interior design studios for bulk orders.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh margin money subsidy (35% for rural, 25% for urban)
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
up to ₹5 lakh collateral-free loan for traditional artisans and craftspeople at 5% interest
🏚
MP Kaushal Vikas Mission
free skills training and certification in carpet weaving for 2-3 employees; stipend of ₹500/day during training
🏚
MP Handicraft Development Corporation (MPHDC) Scheme
product design support, marketing subsidy, and bulk order guarantees for registered artisans and entrepreneurs
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
