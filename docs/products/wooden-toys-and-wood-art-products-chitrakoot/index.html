Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Chitrakoot ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chitrakoot's main artisan clusters in Karvi and Maihar towns (2-3 day immersion). Connect with 5-10 established toy makers through Chitrakoot Handicraft Association. Study GI-certified product specifications. Attend Surajkund Craft Mela (Haryana) in Feb and Delhi Crafts Bazaar to understand market positioning.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source primary wood from timber suppliers in Satna (Madhya Pradesh, 80km away) — budget ₹40,000-60,000 for first 500kg batch. Source finishing materials (stains, oils) from Kanpur suppliers. Negotiate with 2-3 local artisans for custom orders on contract basis.
3
Produce / Partner
Begin with 1-2 experienced artisans on profit-sharing model (40-50% share of wholesale revenue). Establish small workshop (500 sq ft, ₹8,000-12,000/month rent in Karvi). Implement quality control checklist aligned with GI standards. Target 200-300 pieces/month initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 5 minutes). Obtain IEC code from nearest APEDA office for export readiness. File trademark for brand name with IP Australia. Create GI tag certification reference in all marketing materials. Get ISO 9001 certification (₹30,000-50,000) for export credibility.
5
First Sale
Launch Etsy shop (2.5% + $0.20/transaction fees) with 50 SKUs and storytelling photography. List on Flipkart Handmade (8% commission). Approach 10-15 premium gift shops in Tier 1 cities with wholesale pricing. Pitch to corporate gifting companies (Giftcircle, CorporateGifts.com). Join Export Promotion Council of Handicrafts for trade fair access.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire 4-5 additional artisans, increase workshop to 1,500 sq ft. Partner with interior designers in Delhi/Mumbai for project orders. Develop 3-4 seasonal collections. Launch Instagram/Pinterest marketing (target pins: 'eco-friendly kids toys'). Attend international trade shows (Ambiente Frankfurt, NYC Gift Show) via EPCH subsidies. Build B2B wholesale catalog for international distributors.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 as advance tool kit support + 5% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹50L for individual artisans
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for handloom/handicraft units with 15-35% margin depending on entrepreneur category
🏚
UP Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises Policy 2018
Capital subsidy up to ₹10L + power tariff exemption for registered units
🏚
UP Handicraft Artisan Skill Development Scheme
Free skills training and ₹5,000 monthly stipend for registered artisans under craft clusters
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
