Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Ranchi ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Rani Sagar garment cluster in Ranchi (free walk, call 10 workshops for 20-min calls on costing and lead times) + register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (free, 15 mins) + attend Ranchi local retail meet at Chamber of Commerce to meet 5 buyers face-to-face.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fabric from Mohan Fabrics (Patna) or Kalgidhar Mills (Ahmedabad) — first batch 500 meters mixed fabrics ₹1.2-1.8L via phone order, delivery 7-10 days. Buttons/zippers from Chandni Chowk Delhi wholesalers or Kolkata Shyambazar (₹15-25K for 1000 pieces assorted).
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 1200-1500 sqft unit in Rani Sagar cluster (₹8-12K/month, 2-month deposit) or hire 5-8 artisans on wage basis (₹200-300/day each) for a home-based workshop. Sewing machines: 5 units @ ₹3-4K each, 1 cutting table, 1 pressing station. Total setup ₹4-6L includes rent deposit, 2 months payroll, machines, first batch fabrics.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 1 week online) + apply for MSME certification from District Industries Centre Ranchi. Open current account at SBI/ICICI for invoice tracking. If exporting, get IEC code from local DGFT office (₹500, 3-5 days) and register with Directorate of Foreign Trade (one-time online).
5
First Sale
Day 1-30: Contact 5 bulk buyers (school boards, corporate HR teams, retail scouts) via Indiamart or direct referral — offer first order 200-500 pieces @ wholesale price (₹150-250/piece depending on complexity). Expected first-month revenue ₹75-100K if 2-3 orders land. Simultaneously list 10 SKUs on Amazon Seller Central (kids wear, kurtis) with ₹400-800 retail price.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: Move to 3000 sqft unit, hire pattern-maker + quality checker (₹350-400/day each), upgrade to 12 machines, negotiate bulk fabric terms (5-8% discount at 2000m/month). Exhibit at Garment Expo India Delhi (annual), contact 20 export agents, implement WhatsApp CRM for buyer follow-ups. Profitability at ₹15-20L/month with 25-30% net margin.

₹3-5L is realistic minimum for small-batch production (10-15 units/day): machines ₹15K, rent deposit ₹24K, first bulk fabric ₹1.2-1.8L, labor for 2 months ₹80-120K, miscellaneous. Can start from home with 2-3 machines at ₹2L if bootstrapping strictly, but profit takes 14-18 months.
Sewing/garment knowledge helps (3-6 months learning possible by working in a workshop), but business sense, supplier negotiation, and buyer follow-up matter more. 60% successful starters here had 0 garment experience — they hired skilled pattern-makers and supervisors. Pick a core competency (kids wear OR ethnic wear, not both initially).
PM Vishwakarma gives ₹15K voucher + 5% interest subsidy on ₹1L loan. SFURTI can grant ₹50L if your cluster applies together. Jharkhand MSME Cluster scheme gives ₹25L for 2-3 units jointly. Udyam registration is free and gives bank loan eligibility at 2-5% lower rates. Export incentive: Merchandise Exports from India Scheme (MEIS) offers 2-5% cash back on FOB value for garment exports.

Yes, readymade garments is India's top textile export category with ₹1,39,349.6 crore FY26 exports. Ranchi exports to UAE, UK, Germany, Spain, Japan, Egypt, Nigeria, Senegal, Sudan. Process: Get IEC code (₹500, 1 week), connect with export agents in Delhi/Mumbai (they handle logistics, customs), negotiate 40-50% FOB margin (lower than domestic retail but higher volumes). First export order typically ₹5-15L, takes 45-60 days from order to shipment.
Honest risk: Retail buyer concentration is risky — losing 1 large buyer (30% of revenue) can hurt badly. Garment margins are razor-thin (20-30% wholesale, 50-65% D2C) if labor or fabric prices spike. Exit: Sell machines to other workshops (40-50% recovery), wind down labor (pay 1 month notice), lease out unit. Most failures happen in months 6-12 when cash runs dry. Mitigation: Keep 3-month emergency fund, diversify to 10-15 buyers by month 6, track cash weekly.
