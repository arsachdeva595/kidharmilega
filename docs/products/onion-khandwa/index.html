opportunity, Khandwa mein.

Khandwa district lies at 21.8°N, 75.4°E in western Madhya Pradesh, with alluvial soil naturally suited to onion cultivation across Burhanpur, Omkareshwar, and Khandwa tehsils. The cluster produces 450-550 tonnes daily during October-March harvest, sourced within 80 km radius from smallholder farms and progressive farmers using organic techniques. Proximity to Indore (250 km), Mumbai (440 km), and air-rail hubs enables fresh export to South Asia and Southeast Asia, with Baghel Agro Industries already operating organic processing and wholesale supply chains.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Khandwa ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call Baghel Agro Industries (verified ODOP success story) or visit Khandwa mandi on Tuesday morning (wholesale auction day). Attend one onion growers' gathering at local agricultural extension office (tehsil headquarters, free entry). Speak with 3 suppliers — gather seed costs, organic fertilizer rates, seasonal water requirements. Register free on Udyam portal this week (udyamregistration.gov.in).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source from Khandwa onion belt farmers (Burhanpur, Omkareshwar tehsils) or registered suppliers in Khandwa APMC mandi. First batch cost: ₹80-120K for 10-15 tonnes. Peak sourcing: July-February. Establish relationships with 2-3 progressive farmers doing organic cultivation — negotiate seasonal contracts at ₹16-22/kg depending on grade.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Farmer partnership): Work with contract farmers on revenue-share model (60/40 split). Option B (Own cultivation): Lease 1-2 hectares in Khandwa cluster for ₹3-8K/month, hire 2-3 workers (₹300-400/day). For processing/packing: Rent 400-600 sqft unit near Khandwa APMC or Indore for ₹6-12K/month. Invest ₹40-80K in basic sorting, washing, drying, packing equipment.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 10 mins). Obtain Food Safety License (FSSAI) — ₹2500-5000, 15-20 days. If exporting: Apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via DGFT portal, ₹0, 2-5 days. Packaging: Design label with GST number, organic claim, origin mark — ₹5-15K for 5000 packets. Skip GI registration for now (not yet awarded for Khandwa onions).
5
First Sale
Day 1: List on IndiaCart (free listing) and approach 5 local HORECA suppliers with 10-20 kg sample at ₹22/kg. Target: First bulk order from 1 hotel group (30-50 kg/week). Expected first month: ₹15-25K revenue (750-1200 kg sales @ ₹20-22/kg wholesale). Use 2-3 months to secure 4-5 repeat institutional buyers before scaling D2C.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: Hire 1 full-time procurement manager + 2-3 packing staff (₹8-12K/month each). Invest ₹80-150K in refrigerated storage or cold chain partnership. Approach export traders or get in touch with 2-3 APEDA-registered exporters for container shipments. Attend Indore Agri-Export Fair (annual, free booth access for new suppliers). Develop 2-3 value-added SKUs (dried onion, powder) to increase ASP to ₹40-60/kg.
