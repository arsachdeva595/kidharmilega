Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Changlang ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3-5 local areca nut cultivators in Changlang town or Miao sub-division (Changlang NIC can connect), ask raw material seasonality and pricing; visit nearest haat or agricultural mandi in Changlang to observe market standards and grading; register free Udyam account online (udyamregistration.gov.in) — takes 30 mins, no cost.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw areca nut from local farmers in Changlang district for ₹2000-2400 per quintal (April-May peak harvest); engage with Changlang Agricultural Produce Marketing Society (APMS) for bulk direct procurement at ₹2.8-3.2L per tonne; initial batch: 2-3 tonnes = ₹5.6-9.6L investment.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with contract processing: pay ₹8000-12000/month rent for a 500 sqft unit near Changlang market or Miao; install drying racks (₹30000), sorting equipment (₹50000), storage bins (₹25000); OR partner with 2-3 local areca nut processing units to process your material under contract for ₹150-200 per quintal processing fee, requiring zero capital outlay.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online); apply for FSSAI food business license (₹2000-5000, 15 days); arrange laboratory pesticide residue testing via NABL-accredited lab in Guwahati (₹3000-5000 per batch, enables 30% price premium on exports); apply for IEC code from nearest customs office for export (free, online); consider GI registration later (₹20000-50000 filing cost, 3-4 years process — currently Changlang areca nut has no GI tag).
5
First Sale
List on Udyam Marketplace and regional e-commerce (Amazon Pantry, Flipkart Supermarket) — first order from small retailers: 50kg at ₹3200/kg = ₹1.6L revenue. Parallel: contact spice wholesalers in Khari Baoli (Delhi) and Vile Parle (Mumbai) via phone/WhatsApp for trial orders of 100kg at ₹3000/kg. Expect first month: 200-300kg sales = ₹6-9L revenue.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (300kg/month), hire 1 part-time sorter (₹8000/month) and 1 logistics coordinator (₹12000/month); invest in automated sorting equipment (₹1.5-2L) to increase capacity to 800kg/month; attend Sial Asia (food trade show in Bangkok), GulfFood (Dubai) and Fancy Food Show (USA, online registration free) to acquire export contracts; establish export agency partnership with 1-2 trading houses in Assam or West Bengal for FTA compliance.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
subsidy up to ₹25 lakh for manufacturing unit, covers 25-35% of project cost for SC/ST/OBC founders.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme for Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹20 lakh for cluster-level infrastructure, applicable if operating in designated areca nut processing clusters.
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Agriculture Mechanization Scheme
50% subsidy on processing equipment (drying, sorting machines) capped at ₹2 lakh per beneficiary.
🏚
MSME Export Promotion Subsidy, Arunachal Pradesh
10% subsidy on FOB value for first-time exporters, applicable for 3 years.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
