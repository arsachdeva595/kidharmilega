Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Dhalai ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nalkata cluster in person — meet 3-4 pineapple farmers and small processors, ask about yield, cost per fruit, peak season, buyer contacts. Call Dhalai District Agriculture Department (+91-3823-XXXXXX) for cluster maps and recent GI case studies. Attend Nalkata weekly haat — listen to farmer prices and distributor talk. Zero cost, this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Nalkata cluster (30 km from Dhalai town) supplies fresh Queen Pineapples at farm gate rates ₹15-25 per fruit depending on season. First batch: ₹50,000-80,000 for 3,000-5,000 fruits (Apr-May peak season). Build relationship with 2-3 farms, ask for off-season contracts. Supplier names: contact Dhalai District Horticulture Office or Nalkata cooperative.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Fresh fruit aggregation): Rent 200 sq ft cold storage unit near Nalkata cluster, ₹8,000-12,000/month + ₹50,000 refrigeration setup. Wash, sort, pack in cardboard. No processing licence needed for simple aggregation. Turnaround: 2-3 days stock. Option B (Juice/preserve production): Rent 500 sq ft kitchen in food business zone near Dhalai town (₹12,000-18,000/month) + ₹150,000-250,000 for basic juice extractor, pasteuriser, bottling setup. FSSAI licence required: ₹5,000-10,000 one-time + 1-2 months processing. Total setup: ₹200,000-400,000.
4
Brand & Register
Register on Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in — 15 mins). Apply for Tripura MSME subsidy scheme if eligible. File IEC (Importer Exporter Code) with DGFT Guwahati branch (₹1,000, 5 days) if pursuing export. GI registration already exists for Queen Pineapple — obtain GI certification documents from Controller of GI Registry (₹2,000-3,000, reference PIB ODOP database). File FSSAI if processing (₹5,000-10,000). Total compliance cost: ₹15,000-25,000, 6-8 weeks.
5
First Sale
First sale: approach Kolkata-based produce distributors (e.g. Mohan Produce Pvt Ltd, HQ Kolkata) or list on BigBasket Seller Network (free listing, 40% commission). First order size: likely 500-1,000 fruits or 100 L of juice. Expected first-month revenue: ₹80,000-150,000 if 600 fruits move at ₹150 each (or 50 L juice @ ₹250/L). Use WhatsApp + call to close first 5 orders, build case studies, then scale to Blinkit/marketplace ads.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 quality checker + 1 packaging/labelling ops person (₹15,000/month each). Invest ₹200,000 in better cold chain (second freezer, temperature logger). Approach 5-6 export brokers in Kolkata/Chennai for ME shipments. Apply for SFURTI cluster support (₹20L grant, 10-month cycle) if forming producer group. Exhibit at Agrotech India or VivaTech India to attract bulk buyers. Launch Instagram content showing farm-to-table journey (3-4 posts/week).

Government Schemes

up to ₹20 lakh cluster grant + ₹10 lakh marketing support for producer groups; applies to pineapple processing clusters. 10-month project cycle.

₹10-25 lakh subsidy + ₹5-10 lakh loan for processing unit setup; 15-35% subsidy depending on category. 12-month processing.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

Fresh fruit aggregation: ₹3.5-5 lakh (cold storage rent 6 months + initial fruit procurement + packing). Juice/preserve production: ₹6-12 lakh (unit rent, equipment, FSSAI, marketing). Govt subsidy covers 15-35%, so real outlay ₹2.5-9 lakh depending on scheme approval.
Helpful but not must-have. Fresh fruit sales only needs trading sense. Processing requires basic food hygiene + FSSAI training (free courses available). Export needs export documentation knowledge — learnable via brokers and DGFT resources. Many Nalkata farmers started with zero expertise; you can too.
DoNER ₹132-crore programme gives direct equipment grants (₹50-150 lakh/cluster participant). SFURTI: ₹20 lakh cluster grant. PMEGP: ₹10-25 lakh subsidy + ₹5-10 lakh loan. Tripura MSME: 15% equipment subsidy. FSSAI licence cost absorbed under DoNER. Apply via state agriculture or MSME department.
BigBasket seller network (list yourself free, start selling in 1 week). Kolkata-based produce distributors — cold call 10, get 2-3 interested. WhatsApp to local café/restaurant owners (50-100 direct leads). Export brokers in Kolkata (referral from Nalkata cluster). Expect first order 1-3 weeks after outreach.
Bilkul haan. Queen Pineapple GI-certified, already exported 10.4 tonnes/year to Dubai, Oman, Qatar. Process: Get IEC code (1 week, ₹1,000), FSSAI, register with State Export Board, contact 3-4 brokers in Kolkata, prepare shipment (FOB margin 45-55%). First order: 2-5 tonnes, takes 2-3 months to close, 18-22 days to Gulf. No prior export experience required.
Honest truth: pineapple is perishable — oversupply in Apr-May crashes local prices by 50%. Processing de-risks this but requires scale. Exit: sell cold storage + equipment to another entrepreneur (recover 60-70% of setup cost). Pivot to agro-tourism if location good. But if you follow Nalkata cluster best practices and build buyer pipeline first, flop risk is low. Current 10.4 tonne/yr export volume proves demand exists.
