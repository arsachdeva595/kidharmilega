Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Katni ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jagriti Park cluster (3 km from Katni city center) on a Tuesday or Wednesday morning. Walk the cluster, speak with 5-10 artisans and unit owners about raw material sourcing, production timelines, and current buyer demand. Call the Katni District ODOP coordinator (0781-245xxxx) for a guided tour. This takes 1 day and costs ₹0.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw material from Katni quarry suppliers in Jagriti Park or nearby Ratnachapri tehsil (15 km away). For first batch of 10-15 pieces, budget ₹30000-80000 for marble/sandstone blocks. Negotiate directly with quarry owners or engage a supplier-artisan who can provide raw material + labor as bundled service.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 400-600 sqft workshop in Jagriti Park at ₹3000-5000/month (includes water and basic power). Hire 1-3 skilled artisans at ₹8000-15000/month each and buy basic tools (chisels, mallets, sanders, polishers) for ₹25000-50000. Option B: Partner with existing artisans in cluster who take raw material and deliver finished pieces on 30-50% commission. Total setup: ₹100000-300000.
4
Brand & Register
Register business on Udyam portal (free, 15 minutes online). Apply for GI registration for 'Katni Cut Stone' under Ministry of Commerce (₹2000-5000 processing cost, 6-8 month timeline). Get IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT if targeting exports (₹0 online filing). Open bank account under MSME category for MUDRA loan eligibility.
5
First Sale
First sale: List 5-8 pieces on Etsy or Houzz with detailed photos (total 2-3 week turnaround). OR directly contact 3-5 architects in your nearest metro via LinkedIn with portfolio and bulk pricing. OR exhibit at the Adharshila-Katni Stone Festival (Nov 9-28) and directly meet 50+ potential buyers. First order typically ₹30000-150000 from architects or wholesalers. First-month revenue target: ₹40000-80000.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire a dedicated sales person (₹10000-15000/month) and 2-4 additional artisans. Invest ₹50000 in professional photography and basic website. Apply for PMEGP or SFURTI subsidy to expand workshop size. Exhibit at national real estate and architecture expos (CREDAI, Design + Build India). Develop recurring B2B contracts with 3-5 architectural firms. Explore direct export partnerships with USA/UK stone importers.

Frequently Asked Questions

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

Minimum ₹1.5 Lakh (workshop rent 2 months + 2-3 artisans' wages + raw material for 8-10 pieces + tools). Maximum ₹25 Lakh if you want your own 1000 sqft workshop with 5 artisans. Most founders start at ₹2-5 Lakh and grow using profits.
No formal training required if you hire skilled artisans (available in Jagriti Park). But you MUST understand: stone cutting takes 2-8 weeks per piece, quality control is critical, and you need patience. If you've worked in construction or design, that helps.
PMEGP: ₹25 Lakh loan + 25-35% subsidy. PM Vishwakarma: ₹3 Lakh for artisans. MP MSME Cluster Scheme: ₹10-20 Lakh subsidy. ODOP: Exhibition grants ₹15000-50000 yearly. GI registration support: Free guidance + ₹2000-5000 processing by Ministry.
Architects/designers in metro cities (Instagram/LinkedIn outreach). Real estate developers (direct calls). Houzz/Etsy listings (wait 2-4 weeks). Adharshila-Katni Stone Festival (Nov): 50+ buyers attend. Landscaping contractors (Google local search). Your local supplier network (commission-based wholesale).
Bilkul haan. USA, UK, Germany, UAE, Singapore, Italy top markets. Need IEC code (free), product testing cert (₹3000-5000), and freight via air cargo or sea. FOB pricing ₹150-800 per piece. Margins 42-58%. Lead time 6-12 weeks. Start with 10-15 pieces per shipment to test.
Honest downside: stone is slow to sell (8-16 week payment cycles). If D2C fails, switch to B2B architect sales (faster payment). If high-end custom fails, shift to bulk paving/structural stone (lower margins but 3-4x volume). Raw material cost is sunk, but you can sell half-finished pieces to other artisans. Exit option: sell your artisan contacts and workshop lease to another founder. Real risk: market saturation from 250+ existing units.

Marketing
