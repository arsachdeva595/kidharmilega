Kakinada has a 400+ year legacy of handloom weaving, with artisans specialized in pit-loom techniques and Jamdani craftsmanship. The coastal climate and access to fine cotton yarns from Tamil Nadu mills make it the natural hub for delicate saree production. The GI tag ensures authenticity and commands premium pricing globally.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kakinada ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Uppada village (20km from Kakinada city center) to meet master weavers; attend Kakinada Textile Clusters workshops; study GI certification requirements at DC Handloom office, Hyderabad; benchmark competitors on Etsy and Craftsvilla.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fine cotton yarn from Coimbatore Mills (Apollo Mills, Shree Chakra) at ₹120-180/kg; natural dyes from Kanpur suppliers; first batch of 10 sarees requires ₹80,000-120,000 raw material investment plus ₹40,000 artisan advance.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established weaver families in Uppada (visit through Kakinada Handloom Federation); negotiate per-saree rates (₹3,500-5,500 depending on complexity); start with 4-5 simple patterns, expand after first 50 sales.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME on udyamregistration.gov.in (free, instant); apply for IEC via DGFT portal; obtain GI license from Ministry of DC Handloom (Hyderabad office, ₹5,000-15,000 certification fee); register trademark on ipindia.gov.in (₹4,500).
5
First Sale
Launch Etsy shop with 15-20 high-quality photos per saree; create Instagram reels showing weaving process; reach out to bridal blogs and ethnic wear influencers (gift first saree for review); list on Craftsvilla and use WhatsApp Business for direct B2B inquiry handling.
6
Scale
After ₹5L revenue, add 3-5 new patterns monthly; hire a part-time marketing manager; expand to 8-10 artisan partnerships; apply for Export Promotion Capital Goods (EPCG) scheme for bulk orders; establish pop-up stores in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore).
