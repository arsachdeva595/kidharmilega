Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kakinada ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Uppada weaver cluster (25 km from Kakinada city center) — spend a day observing pit-loom production at 3-4 workshops, talk to cluster head at Uppada Handloom Weavers Cooperative, call one existing saree exporter to understand order cycles. Zero cost, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source silk and threads from Kanchipuram (TN, 300 km) or Kolkata suppliers (WB, 800 km) — first batch of 10 sarees ₹1.5-2L if using artisan partnership model (you pay per saree ₹12-15k for weaving). Visit Uppada suppliers list published by District Handloom Office.
3
Produce / Partner
Do NOT rent a loom unit initially — partner with 2-3 pit-loom weaver families in Uppada cluster (they own looms). Give them design specifications, raw materials, pay ₹12-15k per saree as weaving charges. This costs ₹0 upfront capital, ₹12-18L for first 15-20 sarees including materials and labor.
4
Brand & Register
Register as MSME via Udyam (free, 15 mins online). Apply for GI certification endorsement from Ministry of Handlooms (can claim GI origin word but verify licensing with District Handloom Officer first). If exporting: apply for IEC code from nearest APEDA office in Kakinada (free, 5 days). Register brand name with IP office if desired (₹5-10k, 6 months).
5
First Sale
First sale: list 3-5 sarees on Instagram Shop + Meesho Supplier app (commission 10-15%, launch week 2). Target: 2-3 D2C orders month 1 (₹20-30k). Simultaneously email 5-10 tier-1 ethnic retailers (Mumbai, Delhi) with catalog + GI certificate copy — expect first wholesale order of 5-8 pieces by week 4 (₹60-80k). Expected first-month revenue: ₹80-110k.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 part-time operations manager to track artisan orders and quality. Hire 1 designer for custom pattern development. Scale artisan network from 3 to 8-10 pit looms to handle 20-30 sarees/month. Move design catalog online with 360° product photography (₹8-12k shoot). Apply to PM Vishwakarma scheme for working capital top-up (₹250k-₹1L). Exhibit at Bangalore Haat, Delhi Craft Bazaar, and international textile shows (₹50-80k per show).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Prime Minister Vishwakarma Yojana
₹250,000 collateral-free loan at 0% interest for 12 months, plus ₹15,000 toolkit subsidy for registered artisans and artisan clusters
🏛
SFURTI (Amended Amended Scheme)
up to ₹27 lakhs non-repayable grant for cluster-based intervention in handlooms, capital equipment, common facility, capacity building — apply via District Handloom Officer
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Handloom Weaver Assistance Scheme
₹5,000 quarterly subsidy per loom to registered weavers, applicable to artisan partners in your supply chain
🏚
APMSIDC Export Credit Facility
up to ₹10 lakhs working capital at 2% interest subsidy for registered handloom exporters in AP, valid for IEC holders
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
