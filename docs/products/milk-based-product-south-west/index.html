Milk Based Product ki
opportunity, South-West mein.

South West Delhi sits at the convergence of Delhi's metro demand (30+ million consumers) and Punjab-Haryana's milk belt — raw milk costs 20-25% lower than national average due to proximity to Dairy Cooperative networks like NDDB's supply hubs. The district has 3-4 operational dairy clusters (Dwarka, Mehrauli, Chhatarpur belts) with 200+ registered producers, 15+ cold storage units, and established FMCG wholesale channels to modern retail. Institutional buyers (Delhi schools, hospitals) consume 2000+ liters of dairy daily, and export corridors connect to ICD Tughlakabad (customs clearance 100 km away).

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — South-West ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dwarka Dairy Cluster (Sector 11, Dwarka) or Chhatarpur dairy hub — meet 5-10 existing producers, ask about milk sourcing costs, cold chain rentals, and bulk buyer networks. Call NDDB regional office Delhi (+91-11-4118-1111) to request dairy setup guide. Register for free on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in) — takes 10 minutes. Zero cost, do this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw milk from Delhi Cooperative Milk Union (DCMCU) or Haryana cooperative networks — ₹35-40/liter bulk, minimum 100 liters/day (₹3500-4000 daily spend). Alternatively, tie-up with 2-3 local gaushalas in Chhatarpur/Mehrauli (₹38-42/liter, more flexible on volumes). First batch cost: ₹3500 for 100L milk + ₹1500 for salt/culture = ₹5000 startup.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with artisan partnership: Rent dairy production unit from Dwarka/Chhatarpur cluster owner (100 sq.ft. unit with pasteurizer, cooling tank, molds) — ₹8000-12000/month. Alternatively, rent stainless steel equipment + kitchen shelf space from registered dairy (revenue-sharing 15-20%). First 3 months: produce 50 kg/day paneer + 200 liters yogurt. Total outlay: ₹80K (deposit) + ₹15K/month operational cost.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (food processing ISIC code 1050) — free, online, 10 minutes. Apply for FSSAI license (₹5000 one-time, 2-week turnaround, online via fssai.gov.in). Trademark your brand name at IP India (₹4500, 6 months). If exporting: Apply for IEC code via ICEGATE (₹1000, 1 week) and GI registration if claiming South West Delhi origin (₹5000, 6-month process). Total: ₹15.5K, 2-month timeline.
5
First Sale
First sales: Post 5-6 video reels on Instagram showing production (paneer-making process, farm-to-table) + launch on local Facebook group 'South Delhi Homemakers' and 'Delhi FMCG Buyers'. Offer ₹500 sample pack to 20 bulk buyers (schools, offices). First month target: 5 retail accounts (500 packs) + 1 institutional account (1000L yogurt supply) = ₹40-50K revenue. Use WhatsApp Business (free) for order management.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: Hire 1 operations lead (₹20K/month, oversee production QA and logistics). Rent second production unit (₹12K/month). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹50L loan, 35% subsidy, 5% interest) or MUDRA loan (₹1L unsecured, zero subsidy). Exhibit at India Food Processing Expo (Delhi, annual) and FSSAI dairy fairs to land 3-5 new wholesale accounts. Launch D2C website on Shopify (₹99/month) + Blinkit/Zepto merchant panel (free registration). Target: ₹40L/month in 18 months.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹50L loan at 5% interest with 35% subsidy (₹17.5L) for food processing units; application via districtsmse.gov.in
🏛
MUDRA Yojana (Shishu Loan)
₹1L unsecured loan at 6-8% interest for first-time entrepreneurs; no collateral, instant approval via partner banks
🏚
Delhi MSME Trade Facilitation Scheme
₹2L subsidy for ISO/FSSAI certification and trademark registration; apply via delhi.gov.in
🏚
Delhi Mukhya Mantri Saksham Yojana
₹10K/month stipend for 12 months for new entrepreneurs aged 18-35; requires Udyam registration
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
