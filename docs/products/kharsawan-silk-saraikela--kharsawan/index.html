opportunity, Saraikela- Kharsawan mein.

Saraikela-Kharsawan sits 80 km east of Kolkata in southern Jharkhand, with established weaving clusters in Khelarisai and Kharsawan towns. Raw silk supply chains connect directly to Bengal sericulture zones; informal production spans 150-200 looms producing 8-12 saris/day per loom. Buyer concentration in Kolkata wholesale markets, Mumbai fashion retailers, and growing D2C platforms makes logistics cost-effective.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Saraikela- Kharsawan ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Zero-cost: Visit Khelarisai weaving cluster on any Tuesday morning, meet 3-5 weavers directly, ask about raw silk prices and lead time. Call Saraikela District Industries Centre (05656-222891) for official weaver database. Register free on Udyam portal today. Attend the monthly Kharsawan haat at Panchkot Road — walk the fabric wholesale section.
2
Source Raw Materials
Kolkata silk merchants on Bowbazar Street supply raw silk at ₹650-850/kg for first batch of 10 kg. Initial 50-sari batch costs ₹80,000-120,000 in raw material (including dyes, cotton for blends). Lead time: 5-7 days after payment. Alternative: direct-source from West Bengal Sericulture Board recognized cocoon suppliers in Nadia district (20 km from your location).
3
Produce / Partner
Start with artisan partnership — rent 2-3 looms in Khelarisai cluster at ₹8,000-12,000/month each OR hire 2 master weavers as wage-workers at ₹18,000-25,000/month each. First batch: 50 saris in 4-6 weeks. Rent 150 sq ft workspace in existing cluster for ₹6,000/month (includes water, basic utilities). Total first-month operating cost: ₹45,000-60,000.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 15 min). File GI application with District Industries office once you have ₹3-5 saris as samples — GI registration takes 18-24 months but costs ₹0 if done via SIIDC or SISME (Silk Industry Support for Marginalized Entrepreneurs, Jharkhand). If exporting, apply for IEC from DGFT (₹2,000, 10 days). Get BIS certification optional but helpful: ₹25,000-50,000 for batch testing.
5
First Sale
First sale: List 10 saris on Meesho (free, 48-hour approval) at ₹7,500 each. Target 2-4 orders in month 1 = ₹15,000-30,000 revenue. Simultaneously pitch 5 Kolkata wholesalers with WhatsApp samples — typical first bulk order 20-30 saris at ₹4,800/sari = ₹96,000-144,000. First month realistic: ₹80,000-120,000 revenue from mixed channels.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month (33 saris/day production), hire production manager (₹20K), quality checker (₹12K), and customer service operator (₹10K). Invest ₹2L in loom ownership (3 additional looms). Exhibit at India Pavilion in Bangladesh textile expos (March-April), Jaipur Craft Bazaar (December). Start B2B outreach to hotel chains (ITC, Oberoi) for custom saree collections — bulk orders at ₹120,000+ per run.
