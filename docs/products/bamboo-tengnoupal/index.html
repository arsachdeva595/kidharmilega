Tengnoupal district borders Myanmar with 10,687 sq km of bamboo forest (61.36% of state area), housing 53 bamboo species across 202 sq km. Kwatha village cluster in Moreh Sub-Division has 382 residents across 75 clans producing fermented bamboo shoots and handicrafts; raw Usoi shoots arrive fresh June-July or imported from Myanmar year-round. Export markets reach Myanmar directly, plus regional demand from Assam, Nagaland, and coastal Indian cities via PMGSY road connectivity.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Tengnoupal ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kwatha village (35 km from Moreh town) on a Thursday or Sunday haat day — meet fermented bamboo shoot producers, watch fermentation process, ask about raw material costs and seasonal timing. Call Moreh Sub-Division DC office (free call) to ask for artisan contact names. Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (takes 10 mins, free). Zero cost.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh Usoi shoots directly from Kwatha village collectors (₹60-120/kg May-August) or import dried Usoi from Moreh Myanmar traders (₹180-280/kg year-round). First batch: 20 kg Usoi + 2 kg salt + spices = ₹3,500-5,200. Supplier area: Kwatha village, Moreh Sub-Division bazaar.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 200 sq ft fermentation/drying unit in Moreh town (₹4,000-6,000/month). Option B: Partner with 3-5 Kwatha village artisans (provide raw materials, they ferment 60-70% yield, split revenue). Setup: fermentation containers (₹15,000), drying racks (₹8,000), packing station (₹12,000). Total 6-month pilot: ₹35,000-45,000.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 10 mins). Obtain FSSAI food manufacturing license from Manipur Food Commissioner office, Imphal (₹5,000-8,000, 3 weeks). Apply for GI registration for Tengnoupal Bamboo Shoots with DC Handicraft Manipur (₹12,000-20,000, 6 months, yields export premium 15-25%). Get IEC code from DGFT Shillong (free after PAN+bank account, 5 days online). Total: ₹17,000-28,000.
5
First Sale
First sale target: BigBasket Local or JioMart seller dashboard (sign-up free). Launch with 50 units (500g packs) fermented Usoi at ₹280-320/pack (58% margin). Parallel: contact 5 Moreh/Imphal restaurants directly. Expected first month revenue: ₹12,000-18,000 (30-40 units sold via platforms + 10-15 via direct B2B).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 2 fermentation helpers (₹8,000/month each), upgrade to 500 sq ft production space (₹12,000/month), add handicraft product line (baskets, musical instruments). Exhibit at Aapale Haath (annual Delhi craft fair), Northeast India Craft Week (Assam). Partner with 2 Myanmar exporters for 200 kg monthly bulk orders. Target: ₹35-40L annual by month 18.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (25% for general, 35% for SC/ST), 3-5 year repayment, for micro-unit in food/handicraft processing
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹3L interest-free credit for bamboo artisans (if registered as traditional craftsperson), plus ₹15,000 toolkit subsidy
🏚
Manipur MSME Promotion Policy
20% subsidy on machinery for food/handicraft units, ₹50,000-2,00,000 depending on unit size
🏚
Manipur Bamboo Mission
free sapling distribution + training workshops for artisans, organized by State Bamboo Development Agency
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
