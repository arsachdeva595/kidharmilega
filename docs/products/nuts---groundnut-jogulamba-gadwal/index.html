Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jogulamba Gadwal ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tandur and Pargi mandals in Jogulamba Gadwal to study existing groundnut farming clusters and processing units. Connect with APAU (Acharya NG Ranga Agricultural University) extension center in Gadwal. Attend Commodity Exchange platforms like NCDEX for market trend analysis. Interview 10-15 active groundnut traders at Gadwal Main Market.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw groundnuts directly from farmer cooperative societies in Tandur (August-September harvest). First batch sourcing: 10 MT groundnuts at ₹4,500-5,200/MT = ₹45-52L for raw stock. Identify 3-4 reliable farmer contacts or cooperative societies like Gadwal Farmers Producer Company for consistent supply and volume discounts.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing small groundnut processing units in Gadwal town (shelling, drying, sorting services available for ₹2-3/kg). For roasting and packaging, either use rented shared kitchen space (₹8-12K/month at Gadwal Food Park or similar) or partner with local snack manufacturers offering toll processing. Buy basic equipment: roaster (₹80-120K), packaging machine (₹150-250K) for D2C line.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam-MSME (3-5 days, online at udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export eligibility. Register brand name with IPAB. Obtain food license (FSSAI) from Gadwal Municipal Corporation (₹5-8K). Get ISI mark for peanut butter if pursuing value-add. Create basic packaging design reflecting 'Gadwal' origin story.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Pantry, BigBasket, and Flipkart under regional groundnut category (negotiate 10-15% commission). Partner with 50-100 local grocery stores in Hyderabad for direct supply. Create Instagram and WhatsApp Business account showcasing farm-to-table narrative. List on Grofers and local Hyderabad apps. Approach corporate bulk buyers (offices, gyms, schools) for 5-10 MT monthly contracts at ₹6,500-7,000/MT wholesale.
6
Scale
Post-6 months: Launch peanut butter and flavored snack variants. Build B2B relationships with 5-7 confectionery manufacturers in Telangana and Karnataka. Explore export channels via APEDA-registered trading houses. Open branded retail outlet in Hyderabad's commercial hub (Banjara Hills, Secunderabad). Expand production capacity by 50% through additional processing partnerships. Build mandi network beyond Gadwal to Tandur, Vikarabad.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 L subsidy (35% for SC/ST, 25% for general) for processing and value-addition units
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster support for groundnut processing units; marketing and infrastructure grants up to ₹15-20 L
🏚
Telangana Seed Capital Assistance Scheme
₹15-25 L for startups in food processing and agricultural value-add sectors
🏚
Agriculture Infrastructure Development Fund (AIDF)
concessional loans for food processing infrastructure; 3-4% interest subsidy for 4 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
