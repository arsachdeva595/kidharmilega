Fabric scraps flow cheaply from Patna textile mills (150 km) and Varanasi weaving centers (100 km), making raw material costs 40% lower than Gujarat or Rajasthan competitors. Proximity to Delhi freight corridor (250 km via NH-19) enables export reach to ports at Kolkata (400 km) and Jawaharlal Nehru Port, Mumbai (1,400 km).

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bhojpur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 2-3 appliqué suppliers in Ara town (names via Bhojpur KVIC office or district handicrafts portal); visit Sahpur or Piro hamlet on a Saturday haat to watch artisans at work; register free on Udyam (udyamregistration.gov.in) with Aadhar. Cost: ₹0, time: 1 week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Buy first batch of fabric scraps from Patna's Loha Bazar or Laxmi Nagar textile wholesalers (₹50-80 per kg, min 10 kg = ₹500-800); source decorative thread, fusible webbing from Jumbo Electronics (Patna) or Amazon; pre-cut motifs from suppliers in Jaipur's Bapu Bazaar (₹2-10 per motif, min 100 = ₹200-1,000). First batch budget: ₹3,000-5,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 150-200 sqft shared unit in Ara industrial area or near Sahpur artisan cluster (₹3,000-5,000/month). OR partner with 2-3 local artisans (pay piece-rate ₹400-800 per garment, no facility cost). For small batches (50 units/month), artisan partnership is advised: ₹50-100K monthly production cost at ₹1,000-1,500 per piece.
4
Brand & Register
Register on Udyam (free, 30 mins); file GI certificate application through Bhojpur DC Handicraft office or apply via DC Handicraft's GI portal (₹0 cost, India-made products auto-eligible); get IEC code from nearby DGFT office if exporting (₹0, 7 days). Obtain FSSAI for any textile labeling if selling, and trademark search on ipindia.gov.in (₹4,500-9,000 for 5-year protection). Timeline: 4-8 weeks.
5
First Sale
List 10-15 pieces on Etsy (10% transaction fee, ₹100-500 setup) and Instagram Shop (free); target 5-10 first orders at ₹2,000-3,500 each = ₹10-35K first month. Pitch 2-3 local boutiques or ethnic wear retailers for wholesale order of 20-30 pieces at ₹700-900 per piece = ₹14-27K. Expected first-month revenue: ₹25-50K.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (~80-100 units): hire 1 full-time operations manager (₹15-20K/month) to coordinate 5-8 artisans; invest ₹50-80K in simple inventory management software (Shopify or Zoho); apply for SFURTI subsidy (₹10-25 lakh) to set up a mini production unit; exhibit at Surajkund Craft Mela (Feb-Mar), Indian Handicraft Expo (Delhi), and Mumbai International Crafts Fair; open a D2C website (Shopify: ₹29/month). Scale to ₹25L/month by adding export clients.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

Prime Minister's Crafts Cluster Development Scheme (PMCDS)
up to ₹25 lakh as subsidy for cluster infrastructure and skill training
🏛
MUDRA Shishu/Kishore loan
up to ₹1 lakh (Shishu) or ₹10 lakh (Kishore) at 5.5-6.5% interest for appliqué unit setup, collateral-free
🏚
Bihar Handicraft Development Scheme
25% capital subsidy on machinery and setup (max ₹2-3 lakh) for GI-registered products, apply through DC Handicraft
🏚
Bihar Women Entrepreneurship Fund
50% subsidy if founder is a woman, up to ₹5 lakh for appliqué unit setup, apply via SIDBI partner banks
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
