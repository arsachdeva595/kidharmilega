Bhojpur has a 400+ year legacy of applique craftsmanship, with densely concentrated artisan clusters in Marhaura, Piro, and Akshar villages. The district's cotton-growing heritage and established supply chains for silk and cotton fabrics make it the natural hub for this craft. Generational knowledge transfer ensures consistent quality and design innovation.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bhojpur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Marhaura and Piro applique clusters in Bhojpur; spend 2-4 weeks apprenticing with master artisans like those in Lohandpur cooperative. Study GI documentation and connect with Bhojpur Handicrafts Association for technical knowledge.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 50 meters colored cotton from Bhojpur local suppliers (₹2,500-4,000) and silk from Varanasi wholesale markets (₹3,000-5,000). Buy needles, thread, and backing fabric from local hardware stores. First-batch material cost: ₹8,000-12,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 3-5 experienced artisans in Marhaura village on piece-rate basis (₹300-600 per finished product). Alternatively, establish a small home-based unit and hire 2-3 trained stitchers. Begin with 10-15 pieces/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online via udyamregistration.gov.in (free). Apply for IEC code from nearest APEDA office (₹500-1,000). Create brand identity with Bhojpur heritage angle; register trademark with 'Bhojpur' or village name; open dedicated Instagram & website.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (15% fee) and Amazon Handmade (8% fee) with 20-30 initial listings of cushion covers, wall hangings, and apparel. Approach 5-10 urban home décor boutiques in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with sample products and wholesale pricing. Leverage hashtags #BhojpurApplique #TraditionalCraft on Instagram.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to Pinterest ads and TikTok craft videos for viral reach. Approach e-commerce platforms like Jaypore, Karigari, and FabIndia for partnership. Set up a physical pop-up store in a metro city; attend 2-3 annual craft fairs (Dastkar, India Design Fair); collaborate with fashion designers for limited editions.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 as toolkits + ₹5,000 annual credit support for registered artisans and their businesses.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% for general category) for new micro-enterprises in handicraft.
🏚
Bihar State Handicraft Development Policy
concessional credit, training subsidies, and export promotion support for registered artisan collectives.
🏚
Bihar State ODOP Support Scheme
dedicated marketing allowance, cluster infrastructure development, and international exhibition participation grants.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
