Shivpuri has a strong tradition of textile weaving and tailoring with an established artisan base skilled in fabric manipulation and embellishment. The district's proximity to major textile markets in central India and availability of skilled labor makes it ideal for developing fashionable cloth jackets. Historical cotton and silk trade routes have created a fabric sourcing ecosystem that supports quality jacket production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Shivpuri ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Shivpuri's textile clusters in Karera and Pichhore areas; meet established jacket makers; study Indore textile market (90km away) for design trends and pricing; attend Pragati Maidan textile exhibitions in Delhi.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium cotton and silk blends from Indore textile wholesalers (₹8,000-15,000 per meter for quality fabric); bulk buttons and zippers from Ujjain suppliers (₹30,000-50,000 per bulk order); first batch of 50 jackets requires ₹2,50,000-3,50,000 in raw materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisan groups in Shivpuri's Karera cluster; set up a small unit with 5-8 sewing machines (₹1-1.5L); or work on commission basis with existing tailors; ensure quality control through a dedicated supervisor.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam MSE with District Industries Centre Shivpuri; obtain IEC code from DGFT for export eligibility; register brand name with Intellectual Property Office; get ISO 9001 certification for quality; apply for textile industry registration with Ministry of Textiles.
5
First Sale
Launch on Myntra, Ajio, Flipkart Fashion within 3 months; reach out to 50+ boutique retailers across tier-2 cities; create Instagram and Pinterest accounts showcasing designs and artisan stories; pitch to corporate HR departments for bulk orders; attend trade shows in Mumbai and Delhi.
6
Scale
Expand product range to winter coats and blazers; open showrooms in Indore and Bhopal; hire a dedicated design team; increase production capacity to 500+ jackets monthly; establish export partnerships with distributors in Bangladesh and Nepal; launch subscription or seasonal collections; invest in digital marketing and influencer collaborations.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15,000 discount vouchers for tools and ₹35,000 credit at 5% interest for artisans in textile sector
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs grant for self-employment business with 25-35% subsidy for manufacturing
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Textile Industry Development Scheme
machinery subsidy up to 25% and working capital support for registered textile MSMEs
🏚
Mukhyamantri MSME Utthaan Yojana
₹1 lakh to ₹10 lakh loans at 0% interest for first 5 years for textile entrepreneurs
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
