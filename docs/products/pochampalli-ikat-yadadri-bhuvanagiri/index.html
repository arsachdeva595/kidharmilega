Pochampalli Ikat ki
opportunity, Yadadri Bhuvanagiri mein.

Yadadri Bhuvanagiri district, 50 km south of Hyderabad, hosts 5,000 traditional pit looms concentrated in Pochampally I, Pochampally II, and 80+ surrounding villages with documented ikat weaving since the 19th century. Raw materials — cotton from local spinners, silk from Bangalore, sico blends, and zari from Surat — arrive on NH44, ensuring predictable supply chains. The district's export networks already reach Burma, East Africa, and the Middle East, with 10,000 artisan families producing sarees, Telia Rumals, double-ikat fabrics, and specialty textiles.

Suitable For

First-gen founder with ₹4-8L savings, 12-18 months runway, willingness to work directly with artisan clusters and manage supply chain logistics. Ideal if you have design sense or can hire a designer, and can commit 6-8 hours weekly to sourcing and customer management.

Not for: Founders seeking passive income, those unable to visit Pochampally clusters 2-3 times monthly, or those with zero textile/fashion knowledge and no budget to hire expertise. Skip if you need immediate cash flow — production cycles are 7-10 days per saree minimum.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Yadadri Bhuvanagiri ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pochampally I or Pochampally II clusters on a Saturday morning — no cost. Walk through the villages, enter 3-4 weaver homes, watch the tie-dyeing and pit-loom process live. Talk to weavers directly about design preferences, lead time, and pricing. Call the Yadadri Bhuvanagiri District Industries Center (contact via district website) and ask for a cluster introduction. Attend the next textile haat at Hyderabad's Nampally or Begum Bazar — entry free.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source samples directly from Pochampally clusters (Pochampally I village, near the old school) or via established Hyderabad wholesalers like those in Laad Bazaar or Nimboliadda area. First batch: 10-15 sarees in cotton and sico blends, ₹20,000-₹35,000 total cost. Request 15-20 day turnaround. Get written quotes from 5 weavers; compare quality and payment terms (50% advance, 50% on delivery is standard).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 2-3 weaver families in Pochampally clusters (no factory setup required). Provide thread, dyes, and design specifications; they handle dyeing and weaving in their home looms. Alternatively, rent a 400-500 sq ft space in Hyderabad (₹8,000-₹15,000/month) for a small packaging and branding operation. Total production overhead for first 6 months: ₹1.5-₹2.5L.
4
Brand & Register
Register as MSME under Udyam (free, online in 10 minutes, aadhaar only). Apply for GI tag verification through DC Handloom office, Yadadri Bhuvanagiri (₹2,000-₹5,000 fees, no subsidy but legitimacy boost). If exporting, obtain IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT online within 5-7 days. Get a basic trademark for your brand name via iPaisa or LocalCircles (₹10,000-₹15,000). Total timeline: 4-6 weeks.
5
First Sale
Launch on Instagram with 20-30 product photos and reels of the dyeing/weaving process. Post 4x weekly. Target your first 5 sales via DM to friends and local wedding planning communities. Day 1 order goal: 2-3 sarees (₹5,000-₹15,000 revenue). Use Meesho to expand reach — list 15 designs, aim for 200-300 weekly views in month 1. Expected first-month revenue: ₹8,000-₹25,000.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue, hire a part-time social media manager (₹10,000-₹15,000/month) and a logistics coordinator for bulk orders. Transition to 8-10 regular weaver partners in Pochampally clusters to reduce dependency. Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹50,000-₹1L) if you scale to multiple looms. Exhibit at India Handloom Expo (Delhi, quarterly), Hyderabad fashion trade shows, and international textile expos in Dubai or Bangkok. Move to a 1,000 sq ft warehouse (₹25,000-₹40,000/month) for inventory and bulk packing.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹10-25L subsidy for setting up handloom production units; 25% subsidy for general category, 35% for SC/ST, 10% for women.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 tooling subsidy for registered weavers; ₹1-2L credit limit at 5% interest for artisan groups.
🏚
Telangana Handloom Development Scheme
₹2-5L subsidy for artisan producer groups and master weavers; zero-interest loans up to ₹5L for MSME expansion.
🏚
GI Registration Support (Telangana)
₹25,000-₹50,000 reimbursement for Pochampalli Ikat businesses renewing or maintaining GI compliance documentation.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
