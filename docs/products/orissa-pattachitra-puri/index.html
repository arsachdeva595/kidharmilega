The Pattachitra artisan belt spans Puri town, Pipli, and surrounding villages with 200+ registered GI artisans and access to natural mineral pigments from Odisha's mining regions. Export infrastructure exists via Visakhapatnam port (160 km) and Bhubaneswar airport (65 km), with established buyer networks in US, UK, Germany through fair-trade and craft export channels.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Puri ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Puri town Pattachitra artisan cluster (near Jagannath Temple foreground) — spend 2-3 days, meet 3-5 established GI-tagged artists, observe production, ask about raw material suppliers (no cost, free advice). Call Puri District Industries Centre (0674-2235678) for official artisan registry. Attend Odisha Habfest or Surajkund Mela if happening in your window (free entry, learn market pricing).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from: (1) Natural pigment suppliers in Berhampur (80 km away) — ₹5,000-8,000 for starter palette of 12+ colours. (2) Artisan-grade animal hair brushes from Delhi handicraft suppliers or local brush makers near Puri Raj Marg — ₹2,000-3,500 for set of 20. (3) 100% cotton cloth or canvas sheets from Odisha State Handloom Board outlet in Bhubaneswar (60 km) — ₹30-80 per meter. First batch startup: ₹20,000-30,000 for materials for 5-10 test pieces.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Artisan Partnership Model — recommended): Partner with 2-3 GI-registered artisans from Puri cluster on revenue-share (40-50% to artisan, 50-60% to you for marketing/sales). Rent a small display workshop (200-300 sq ft) in Puri town near temple or Bada Danda market at ₹4,000-6,000/month. Handle design briefs, customer communication, quality checks, delivery. Artisan produces 3-5 pieces/month per person. Option B (Direct Production): If you have painting skills, rent same unit and produce yourself or hire trained apprentices at ₹8,000-12,000/month. Monthly production: 8-15 pieces. Setup cost range: ₹100,000-200,000 (rent deposit + materials + workspace setup).
4
Brand & Register
Step 4.1: Register business under Udyam (Ministry MSMEs) — free online, 5 minutes, classifies you as artisan/handicraft MSME. Step 4.2: Apply for GI-tagged artisan certification through Puri District Industries Centre if producing authenticated hand-painted work (costs ₹5,000-10,000, timeline 4-8 weeks, gives legal right to use 'GI Orissa Pattachitra' label). Step 4.3: Get IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT if planning exports — ₹0 cost, online via DGFT portal, instant approval. Step 4.4: Open business bank account (₹0-500 with GST registration, optional at this stage unless invoicing B2B buyers >₹20L annual turnover).
5
First Sale
First sale strategy: (1) Launch Etsy shop (₹0, 3.2% transaction fee + $0.20/item) with 5-6 best pieces, 1-2 process photos, authenticated narrative about artisan/GI status. Target: 1-2 sales/month at ₹6,000-8,000 = ₹6,000-16,000 first month. (2) Approach 2-3 local Puri hotel gift shops (Mayfair, Toshali Royal, Odisha Nivas) — meet manager, offer 10-15% wholesale discount. Typical first order: 3-5 pieces at ₹3,500-5,000 each = ₹10,500-25,000 one-time. (3) List on Amazon Handmade India (free, 15% commission) — takes 2 weeks to activate. Expected first-month blended revenue: ₹15,000-30,000.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month gross: (1) Hire a dedicated operations person (₹15,000-18,000/month) to manage Etsy/Amazon/email orders, quality checks, shipping logistics. (2) Scale artisan network from 2-3 to 5-8 artists by recruiting through Puri DICs apprenticeship programs. (3) Exhibit at Surajkund Mela (annual), Habitat India exhibitions, and Odisha Habfest (₹40,000-80,000 booth cost, typical 8-15% sales lift). (4) Approach fair-trade export agencies (Fair Trade Federation members) for wholesale export channels — negotiate ₹6,000-10,000 FOB pricing for bulk orders. (5) Launch Instagram/Pinterest content marketing: behind-the-scenes artisan videos, mythology narratives, process reels (2-3x/week, target 20-30K followers by month 12). (6) Introduce complementary products: Pattachitra-style textile cushion covers, sarongs, greeting cards to upsell existing customer base.

Puri Artisan Craft Village (proposed ODOP cluster)
Government-recognized ODOP cluster in Puri town with subsidized workshop spaces (₹2,000-4,000/month for 150-300 sq ft units), shared cold storage, common display gallery for artisans. 80+ registered Pattachitra artists, active since 2019. Proximity to Jagannath Temple (walking distance) and Bada Danda tourist market.

Pipli Artisan Cluster (adjacent district extension)
Concentration of 50+ Pattachitra and appliqué work artisans in Pipli town (20 km from Puri), government MSME support center, raw material suppliers co-located. Lower rent (₹1,500-3,000/month) but slightly lower foot traffic than Puri town.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 non-repayable grant + ₹5L collateral-free MUDRA loan at 5% interest for artisans in registered handicraft categories (Pattachitra qualifies) — process through nearest bank or MSME-DI office
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
35% subsidy (max ₹10.5L) for unit setup cost if manufacturing business employs 10+ workers, applicable if scaling to multi-artisan model — process through KVIC/KVIB
🏚
Odisha Artisan Support Scheme
₹2L concessional loan for GI-tagged artisans at 2% annual interest, 5-year tenure, zero processing fee — process through Puri DICs
🏚
Odisha One District One Product (ODOP) Brand Development Fund
₹50,000-2,00,000 grant for branding, packaging, digital marketing, and exhibition participation for certified ODOP artisans — apply through District MSME DI, Puri
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
