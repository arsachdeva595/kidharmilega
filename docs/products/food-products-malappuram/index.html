Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Malappuram ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Malappuram's Kozhikode food markets (Sarovaram, Conolly Market) and Malappuram town food clusters. Connect with established biryani and pathiri producers in Manjeri and Perinthalmanna. Spend 2-3 weeks understanding traditional recipes, supplier networks, and local FSSAI requirements through direct mentorship with senior food entrepreneurs.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source basmati rice from local wholesale merchants in Malappuram town (₹80-100/kg), meat from certified halal suppliers in Perinthalmanna, seafood directly from Calicut fishery harbor. First batch of 500kg mixed products budget: ₹1.2-1.5 lakhs. Establish relationships with 3-4 reliable suppliers for consistent quality.
3
Produce / Partner
Register with a licensed commercial kitchen facility in Malappuram town or partner with existing food processors holding FSSAI license (₹15,000-25,000/month rental). Start with batch production 2-3 times weekly. Alternatively, partner with established biryani artisans and pathiri makers in Manjeri on revenue-sharing model (30-40% commission). Begin with 50-100kg daily production capacity.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online via udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC code through DGFT portal for export readiness. Obtain FSSAI license (₹5,000-8,000 for small business). Create brand identity with local designer (logo, packaging). Trademark brand name via IP India. Get BIS certification for packaged food products if targeting premium markets.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Food and Flipkart Fresh with 5-8 SKUs (biryani paste, ready-to-cook pathiri mix, mussel fry ready meal). Target food delivery partners in Malappuram and Kozhikode first. Approach 10-15 specialty restaurants and hotels in Fort Kochi and Kottayam with restaurant-grade offerings. Partner with 5-6 specialty food retailers in metros via B2B platforms like IndiaMART and TraceLink. Build WhatsApp community for bulk orders from Kerala-based corporates.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand SKU range to include ready-to-eat meal boxes, spice mixes, and desserts (Kozhikode halwa variants). Set up second production facility or increase capacity to 300-400kg daily. Launch dedicated e-commerce website with subscription model for monthly boxes. Expand B2B distribution to 50+ restaurants across Kerala and metro cities. Explore GI certification pathway for Malabar Biryani to enable premium positioning and export to EU (GI protection lasts 10+ years). Scale WhatsApp and Instagram influencer partnerships targeting food bloggers and NRI communities.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for food processing MSMEs with 25% entrepreneur contribution
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster development with ₹3-5L per unit for food processing artisans
🏚
Kerala Startup Mission
₹10-50L funding for food tech and premium food product startups with mentorship support
🏚
State Food Processing Policy
5-year tax exemption and subsidy on equipment for registered food processing units in non-metro areas
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Marketing — Zero Budget Se Shuru Karo
  • Partner with 8-10 Kerala food bloggers and NRI YouTube creators (100k+ subscribers) for authentic recipe storytelling and production facility tours, offering 15-20% commission on referral sales. Target diaspora communities in UAE, UK, and USA.
  • Launch Instagram Reels campaign featuring 30-60 second clips of traditional cooking methods, family recipe stories, and customer testimonials from restaurant chefs. Run targeted ads to food enthusiasts aged 25-45 in metros and NRI regions with ₹2,000-3,000/week budget.
  • Conduct 2-3 pop-up tasting events per quarter in metros (Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi) with 50-100 attendees each. Offer 20% discount on first purchase, collect email database for monthly recipe newsletter and product launches. Partner with co-working spaces and food clubs for venue access.
