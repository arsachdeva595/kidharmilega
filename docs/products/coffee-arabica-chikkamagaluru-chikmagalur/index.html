Chikkamagaluru district in Karnataka's Western Ghats at 900–1200m elevation produces the only GI-tagged Indian Arabica with verifiable fruity notes (chikku, red berries, brown sugar, jackfruit hints). The district has 25,000+ hectares of coffee plantations with 40+ established farmer networks concentrated in Mudigere, Tarikere, and Koppa taluks. Raw material is immediately available; proximity to Bangalore (250km) and Mangalore port (200km) enables rapid D2C fulfillment and FOB export at competitive freight costs.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Chikkamagaluru (Chikmagalur) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mudigere coffee cluster and speak to 3-5 planters or agents; call Chikkamagaluru Coffee Board office (free consultation) — ask about current availability, price trends, and processing options. Attend the Chikkamagaluru Coffee Festival (usually Feb-Mar) to meet 30+ suppliers in one day. Register on Udyam portal (free, online, 15 mins) for MSME status.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source green beans (unroasted) from established agents in Mudigere town (₹150-200/kg raw beans, 80kg minimum = ₹12,000-16,000 first batch). Alternative: buy directly from 2-3 planters for better rates (₹130-180/kg) but requires logistics coordination. Processing (washing, drying) can be outsourced to licensed processors in Tarikere (₹30-50/kg labour) or done in-house if you own roasting setup.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with artisan partnership: hire a master roaster (₹15,000-25,000/month, 2-3 people) and rent a 500 sqft unit with roaster equipment in Mudigere industrial area (₹5,000-8,000/month). Equipment cost: coffee roaster (₹4-8L), grinder (₹1-2L), packaging machine (₹2-3L) — total ₹7-13L for full in-house setup. Alternatively, outsource roasting to established processors (₹40-60/kg) and keep setup cost to ₹2-3L for packaging and branding.
4
Brand & Register
Udyam registration (free, 48 hours online). GI certification: file claim with Your Brand as authorized user of GI-tag Chikkamagaluru Arabica Coffee (₹10,000-15,000 legal fee, 30-45 days). IEC registration with DGFT (₹500, 5-7 days online) only if exporting. Trademark your brand name at IP office (₹5,000-8,000, 4-6 weeks). Food license (FSSAI) if value-added roasting in-house (₹2,000-3,000, 5 days).
5
First Sale
First batch: 50kg roasted coffee (₹8-12/kg production cost). List on Amazon Pantry and BigBasket as vendor (₹0 upfront, payment after 7-10 days). Price at ₹450/250g (₹1,800/kg wholesale parity). Expected first month: 40-60 units = ₹18-27K gross revenue. Simultaneously contact 5 specialty cafés for ₹25K+ initial orders. Use direct email to café owners — no middleman.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (55-60kg daily roasting): hire 2 roasters (₹25K each), 1 packaging person (₹15K), 1 sales/customer support (₹18K). Move to 1,500 sqft facility (₹12-15K rent). Invest in commercial roaster upgrade (₹15-20L). Target B2B café chains with curated pricing; launch subscription box on Freshworks CRM. Export: hire freight forwarder, apply for RCMC certificate (₹5,000), attend GulfFood (Dubai) or Specialty Coffee Expo to get European importer leads.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy on equipment for manufacturing unit up to ₹25 lakhs, max ₹6.25L subsidy
🏛
Mudra Loan (MUDRA scheme)
collateral-free loan up to ₹10 lakhs for manufacturing and processing, 0% interest for first 2 years under select banks
🏚
Karnataka Coffee Board Support
grants for value-addition infrastructure (roasting, packaging), up to ₹3 lakhs per unit, 2-month approval cycle
🏚
Karnataka Agricultural Export Policy
10% subsidy on export logistics (shipping, certification) for first ₹50 lakhs of annual exports
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
