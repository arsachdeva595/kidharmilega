Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Chikkamagaluru (Chikmagalur) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chikkamagaluru Coffee Belt (Mudigere, Koppa, Balehonnur taluks) for 2-3 weeks; meet established coffee farmers at Karnataka Coffee Board office in Chikkamagaluru town; study GI certification requirements at Geographical Indication Registry, Chennai; analyze competitor pricing on Amazon and specialty websites.
2
Source Raw Materials
Establish primary sourcing from certified Arabica growers in Chikkamagaluru district at ₹180-220 per kg raw beans; negotiate supply agreements for 5-10 MT first batch; source roasting equipment from vendors in Bangalore or imported roasters at ₹2-5 lakhs; estimated first-batch raw material cost: ₹10-15 lakhs.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing coffee processing units in Chikkamagaluru (many offer contract roasting/packaging services); OR establish small roastery near Chikkamagaluru town with 5-10 kg/day capacity; ensure ISO 9001, FSSAI, GI certificate compliance for every batch produced.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as MSME (online at msme.gov.in); obtain IEC code from DGFT for export; apply for GI usage rights through Karnataka Coffee Board; design brand identity aligned with GI certification; file trademark via Indian Patent Office; set up FSSAI food business license.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, Flipkart, BigBasket with ₹5K-10K promotional budget; approach 50-100 specialty cafes in Bangalore, Pune, Mumbai with product samples; list on specialty coffee marketplaces (CoffeeAndMore.in, ArabiaCoffee.com); initiate cold outreach to 20-30 international importers via email with GI certificates and samples.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand packaging range (250g, 500g, 1kg, gift boxes); develop subscription model (₹500-800/month); explore B2B supply to hotel chains and corporate canteens; increase export volume by 30-40% via trade shows (ISM Cologne, Specialty Coffee Expo); build social proof via YouTube roasting videos and Instagram farm-to-cup storytelling.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs government subsidy for registered MSMEs in agro-processing sector
🏛
APEDA Export Promotion Scheme
Concessions on registration, certification, and export documentation for agricultural products including coffee
🏚
Karnataka Agri-Export Promotion Scheme
Subsidy on export certifications and international quality testing for GI products
🏚
Karnataka Agriculture Department Coffee Subsidy
Support for processing equipment and cold chain infrastructure for coffee value addition
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
