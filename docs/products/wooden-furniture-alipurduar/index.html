Wooden Furniture ki
opportunity, Alipurduar mein.

Alipurduar district in north Bengal (3,136 sq km) is surrounded by forest regions supplying teak wood and solid wood at lower costs than plains. The 6 community development blocks and 2 municipalities comprise a concentrated pool of traditional woodworkers, many from SC/ST communities with multi-generational skills. Buyer proximity includes Kolkata (600 km), Guwahati (200 km), and Nepal/Bangladesh borders (100-150 km), plus direct export access via Kolkata port (650 km) and regional air cargo from Bagdogra (50 km).

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Alipurduar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Alipurduar town wooden furniture cluster around Main Road near Falakata area (zero cost — call DC Handicraft office at 0353-2520520 for artisan contacts); attend weekly haat at Alipurduar Market; call 2-3 teak wood suppliers in Jalpaiguri district to understand raw material pricing and lead time (1-2 weeks)
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw teak wood and solid wood from timber merchants in Jalpaiguri town (30 km south) or Siliguri (70 km); first batch of timber for 5-10 furniture pieces costs ₹40K-80K; negotiate 30-day payment terms; also source brass fittings from local Alipurduar blacksmith clusters or Kolkata (₹2K-5K per 10-piece batch)
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 250-400 sq ft workshop in Alipurduar town (₹3K-5K/month) or partner with 2-3 existing artisans on piece-rate basis (₹200-500 per chair, ₹1K-3K per table); buy basic tools (saws, sanders, chisels, lathe) for ₹30K-50K; design templates cost ₹0 if copied from local artisans or Etsy; production time is 2-4 weeks per 5-piece batch
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME online (free, 30 min) at udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for Artisan GST under 28% HSN 9401/9403 (free registration, ₹500 for ITC filing); explore GI registration via DC Handicraft (₹15K-30K, 12-18 month process, optional); if targeting export, apply for IEC from nearest Customs office in Kolkata (₹0 cost, 5 working days) and file RCMC application if using export subsidies
5
First Sale
First sale: list 5 pieces on Etsy + Instagram shop simultaneously (₹0 setup, Etsy charges 6.5% transaction fee); first regional wholesale order: approach 2-3 furniture retailers in Siliguri or Kolkata via WhatsApp with product photos (₹40K-100K order expected); post video reels on YouTube/Instagram showing furniture making (₹0 cost, 2-3 videos/week); expected first-month revenue ₹30K-60K (mix of 2-3 D2C and 1 wholesale order)
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 2-3 permanent artisans (₹8K-12K/month each); move to 800-1000 sq ft rented unit (₹8K-12K/month) to handle 20-30 pieces/month; invest ₹50K-100K in woodworking machinery (power drill, orbital sander, edge banding machine); exhibit at furniture trade shows in Delhi (India Furniture Fair) and Mumbai (Mumbai Furniture Fair) twice yearly; hire a sales/export coordinator (₹15K/month) to manage bulk orders and logistics; build B2B relationships with interior designers and corporate furniture procurement teams

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 as credit-linked capital subsidy, free skill training, interest subsidy of 5% for 18 months on loans up to ₹40 lakh
🏛
PMEGP
25% subsidy on fixed assets for General category (up to ₹10 lakh investment) or 35% for SC/ST categories; ₹15 lakh max loan facility
🏚
West Bengal One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
export subsidy of 2-5% on FOB value for registered artisans; cluster development fund support
🏚
West Bengal Handicrafts Corpus Fund
concessional credit up to ₹5 lakh at 4% interest for registered MSME artisans in handicraft sector
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
