Lucknow Chikan Craft & Lucknow Zardozi ki
opportunity, Lucknow mein.

Lucknow district sits in central Uttar Pradesh with Chowk and Daliganj as the epicenters of Chikan production, housing thousands of trained artisans within a 5km radius. Raw materials (muslin, silk, organza, gold/silver thread) are sourced locally or imported through established suppliers in Delhi and Mumbai, with ₹550 crore annual production supporting five lakh workers. Export infrastructure includes Lucknow Airport (12km), NH-25 connectivity, and established shipping routes to Gulf (Saudi Arabia, UAE), Europe (UK, France, Italy), and South Asia (Pakistan, Bangladesh).

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Lucknow ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chowk locality cluster on a Tuesday morning (quieter, easier to speak with artisans), spend 2 hours talking to 3-4 embroidery shop owners about stitch types, lead times, and current order flow — completely free. Call 2-3 exporters in Daliganj (numbers available through local textile association) to understand export margins and minimum orders. Attend the weekly haat in Chowk to see finished products and current pricing.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source base fabrics (muslin, silk, chiffon, organza) from Chowk textile wholesale market (Aminabad sub-market) — first batch of 10 sarees (base cost ₹500-800 per piece) requires ₹8000-10000 investment. For gold/silver thread (Zardozi work), connect with established suppliers in Chowk or Daliganj like Moin Threads or similar local distributors — ₹2000-5000 for initial stock. Embroidery work outsourcing: approach 2-3 clusters of artisans directly in Chowk, negotiate ₹1500-3000 per saree embroidery based on complexity (36 stitch types available: murri, bakhia, jali, tepchi, tappa, dhum, katao etc. determine pricing).
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Asset-light): Partnership model — provide fabric and design to 4-5 artisan groups in Chowk (each artisan produces 2-3 pieces/month), pay per piece on delivery, zero rental cost. Option B (Own unit): Rent a 500-600 sq ft space in Chowk/Daliganj at ₹8000-15000/month, hire 3-4 semi-skilled stitchers (embroidery requires training — ₹10000-25000/person onboarding), set up basic tables and work stations (₹30000-50000 one-time). Total setup: ₹60000-100000 monthly run-rate, ₹80000-150000 capital if doing own production. Embroidery lead time: 20-60 days per order depending on design density.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in, 10 minutes). File GI certification application under 'Lucknow Chikankari' — already GI-tagged at district level but individual products need clearance (₹2000-5000, 6-8 weeks). If exporting: Apply for IEC (Importer Exporter Code) through DGFT portal (₹5000, 2 weeks turnaround). Get FSSAI if selling home decor items as food-contact textiles (optional, ₹3000-5000). Legal structure: Register as MSME Proprietorship or Private Limited (₹1000-15000 depending on structure).
5
First Sale
First sales: List 5-10 designs on Meesho and Instagram Shop within week 2 (both free setup). Simultaneously approach 3-5 bridal retailers in Lucknow, offer ₹1500-2500 per piece wholesale (your cost ₹800-1200 base + ₹1000-1500 embroidery). First order: Target 20-30 pieces across channels in month 1, expected revenue ₹50000-75000 at mixed margin (30% wholesale average), keep ₹15000-25000 as profit. Repeat cycles every 30 days as you refine designs.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: Hire 1-2 dedicated designers (₹15000-20000/month) to create 8-10 new designs quarterly, expand artisan network to 8-10 groups to handle volume. Move into showroom in Lucknow (₹20000-30000/month) to attract walk-in retail customers and corporate bulk orders. Exhibit at International Handloom Expo (Jaipur, Feb-Mar annually), Vivaha Mandap wedding fairs (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, ₹20000-50000 stall cost). Hire export manager (₹18000-25000/month) to manage Gulf/European distributors and compliance (IEC, GST, shipping docs). Invest ₹2-3L in Instagram ads, Pinterest catalog, wedding planner partnerships to drive top-line.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹10 lakh subsidy on capital investment for MSMEs, 15-35% subsidy depending on location and applicant category

PM Vishwakarma
₹15000 one-time incentive + ₹500 monthly for registered artisans and craft MSMEs in traditional sectors like Chikankari
🏚
Uttar Pradesh ODOP Scheme
concessional credit up to ₹50 lakh through SIDBI at below-market rates for registered ODOP enterprises in Lucknow
🏚
UP Artisan Bonus Scheme
₹2000-5000 annual stipend to registered artisans and cooperative members engaged in traditional textiles
