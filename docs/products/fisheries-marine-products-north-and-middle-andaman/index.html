Fisheries/Marine products ki
opportunity, North and Middle Andaman mein.

North and Middle Andaman is surrounded by fishery grounds within 30–80 nautical miles, with 12 landing centers dotting the coastline (Port Blair, Diglipur, Rangat). The district processed 1,589,923 MT of marine products in 2023-24 through ~40 APEDA-registered processing units concentrated in Port Blair, Rangat, and Diglipur. Primary export buyers are US (35%), EU (28%), Japan (15%), and China (12%), with shipments via Port Blair Port and Chennai as transshipment hubs.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — North and Middle Andaman ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3 shrimp suppliers in Port Blair landing center (Haddo Wharf) — ask export prices, catch seasonality, processing margins. Visit Rangat Fish Market or Diglipur Landing Center on a landing day (Tuesday/Friday preferred). Attend Port Blair Chamber of Commerce monthly seafood exporter meet (free registration). Register Udyam on Udyam.gov.in (10 min).
2
Source Raw Materials
Raw shrimp sourcing: direct from Port Blair fish auctions (₹200–240/kg wet weight, minimum 500 kg daily) or pre-negotiate with 3–4 trawler operators for daily catches. First batch cost: ₹1,20,000–2,00,000 for 500–750 kg. Supplier area: Haddo Wharf, Rangat Landing Center. Negotiate 7-day payment terms once relationship is proven.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 500–1000 sq ft processing unit in Port Blair (₹40,000–60,000/month), or partner with 2–3 existing processors for co-packing at ₹15–25/kg labor cost. Unit must have FSSAI Class B license, frozen storage (-18°C), IQF (Individual Quick Freezing) capability, and packing line. Setup cost: ₹25–60L if owning, ₹0 if outsourced initially. Hire a FSSAI-trained unit head (₹30,000/month) and 4–6 workers (₹12,000/month each).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 2 days). Apply for FSSAI License Class B online at food.fssai.gov.in (₹1,000, 30 days). Obtain IEC code from DGFT portal (₹0, 2 days). Get APEDA registration online at apeda.gov.in (₹5,000, 10 days) — mandatory for seafood export. Export invoicing via ICEGATE (free govt portal). Total compliance cost: ₹6,000 + 60 days.
5
First Sale
First sale: approach 2–3 regional hotel chain procurement officers (ITC, Taj properties) with samples + FSSAI cert — order size typically 50–100 kg/week at ₹200/kg wholesale. Parallel: list on BigBasket Seller Center (2–3% commission) and Jiomart with 10 kg starter SKU. Expected first-month revenue: ₹3–8L (domestic) + ₹2–5L (initial B2B repeat). Cycle time: sample delivery (2 days), approval (5 days), first PO (10 days).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire QA manager (₹35,000), upgrade to IQF tunnel freezer (₹60L investment), shift to dedicated 2000 sq ft unit (₹1.2L/month rent), and build export pipeline via APEDA directory. Target: 5–8 active export clients. Attend Seafood Expo Asia (Bangkok, May) and Global Seafood Alliance meetings. Secure GLOBAL GAP or ASC certification (₹3–5L, 4 months) to unlock premium export pricing (+15–20%) and Walmart/EU retail channels.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy on fixed capital for manufacturing units up to ₹25L investment, 30% for SC/ST founders, application via DIC Andaman or online at pmegp.gov.in
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
up to ₹10L collateral-free loan at 8–11% interest for food processing via registered banks, 5-year tenure, women entrepreneurs get 0.5% concession
🏚
Andaman and Nicobar Islands Seafood Export Incentive Scheme
5% cash subsidy on FOB export value for APEDA-registered units, applies to all frozen products, administered by Department of Commerce
🏚
A&N Islands MSME Processing Unit Support
₹5L one-time grant for cold-storage infrastructure in processing units, application via District Industries Centre Port Blair, 3-month approval
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

₹8.5–50 lakh. Outsourced processing start: ₹8.5–15L (raw material buffer + initial marketing). Own unit with IQF: ₹35–50L (rent deposit 6 months, equipment, staffing, certifications, working capital). MUDRA + PMEGP can cover 25–50% of this.
Ideal: 2–3 years in fish trade, supply chain, or cold logistics. Not mandatory if you hire a unit manager (₹30–35K/month) with FSSAI certification. You need strong sales instinct and patience for export paperwork — it's tedious.
PMEGP: 25% subsidy up to ₹25L. MUDRA: ₹10L loan at 8–11% interest. Andaman State Scheme: ₹5L one-time for cold storage + 5% cash subsidy on export FOB value. FSSAI, APEDA, IEC: all free or ₹1–5K each. Total govt help potential: ₹30–50L for a ₹50L setup.
Direct: Hotel chains (ITC, Taj, Oberoi) via email outreach. Digital: BigBasket Seller, Jiomart, Licious (2–3% commission). B2B: Regional seafood distributors in Delhi/Mumbai (call 20, close 2–3). Export: APEDA directory + trade shows (Seafood Expo) + email to US/EU importers. First PO usually comes in 2–3 weeks.
Bilkul. Shrimp, fish exports are 85%+ of revenue. APEDA registration (₹5K, 10 days) + IEC code (free, 2 days) + FSSAI needed. FOB pricing: ₹280–350/kg frozen shrimp (vs ₹200–220 domestic wholesale). Margin: 35–50%. Buyers: USA, EU, Japan mostly. Shipping via Chennai to LA/Hamburg takes 20–25 days.
Downside: demand can crash 20–30% if exports slow (global recession). Raw material seasonality hits June–September (low catches). Freezer breakdown = ₹5–10L loss. Exit plan: Sell inventory to other processors at 80% cost (auctioneers in Port Blair buy). Unit can pivot to live fish export or other species. MUDRA loan still owed, but MSME insolvency clause protects some assets. Risk is real but manageable if you start with ₹15–25L (outsourced model), not ₹50L.
