opportunity, Jaunpur mein.

Jaunpur has a deep-rooted tradition of carpet weaving spanning centuries with a concentrated cluster of skilled artisans in Gopalpur and Bhitauli areas. The district's access to high-quality wool from local sheep herds and historical mastery of natural dyeing techniques make it uniquely positioned to produce premium Dari carpets with authentic provenance.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jaunpur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gopalpur and Bhitauli carpet clusters in Jaunpur (2-3 days immersion), interview 5-10 master weavers, document production workflows, visit Jaunpur's DC Handicraft office to understand ODOP support, study competitor pricing on Etsy and TakeRootIndia.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source wool from Jaunpur's local suppliers (contact District Industries Centre) at ₹300-400/kg; procure natural dyes from Shahpura near Jaipur or local herbalists at ₹2000-3500/kg. First batch of 5 small carpets (2x3 ft) requires ₹80,000-120,000 for materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established weaver families in Gopalpur offering work-order contracts; negotiate per-carpet piecework rates (₹8,000-15,000 per carpet), or establish a small production unit with 2-3 looms and employ apprentices. Join Jaunpur Handloom Society for collective backing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam at udyamregistration.gov.in (free, online); apply for IEC code via DGFT for export readiness; register trademark at ipindia.gov.in (₹4,500-9,000); obtain GI certification application through DC Handicraft (currently no GI status — positioning as authentic Jaunpur heritage brand).
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram + Facebook with high-quality photography of production process; list on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Indian marketplaces (Baggit, Tribe Alive, Artisera); pitch to interior design consultants and 5-star hotel procurement teams in Delhi/Mumbai; attend Heimtex and international craft fairs.
6
Scale
After 6 months and first 10-15 sales, expand to 6-8 looms, hire 2-3 full-time weavers, launch B2B wholesale program targeting Manas Forgings, FabIndia, and tier-1 hotel chains; establish showroom in Lucknow; apply for PMEGP expansion credit (₹10-25L) for unit scaling.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan subsidy (25-35% depending on location) for new micro/small enterprises in handloom sector
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 direct benefit + 5% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹2 lakhs for traditional artisans, carpet weavers eligible for free training and toolkit
🏚
UP Kaushal Vikas Mission
government-funded skill development training for carpet weavers and dyers; certificate courses offered free at ITIs in Jaunpur district
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Artisan Welfare Scheme
₹10,000 annual pension + health insurance for registered traditional artisans engaged in carpet weaving; registration through Jaunpur DC Handicraft
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
