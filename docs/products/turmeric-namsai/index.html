opportunity, Namsai mein.

Namsai, located in eastern Arunachal Pradesh at 1000-1500m elevation, has subtropical monsoon climate ideal for high-curcumin turmeric; the SFURTI Turmeric, Aloevera, Moringa and Mushroom cluster is anchored around Namsai town with farmer groups across the district supplying raw rhizomes. The Common Facility Centre (CFC) now offers shared processing, drying, and powder-making equipment, reducing individual capital burden; raw turmeric is grown within 10-30km of the cluster, ensuring fresh supply and low logistics cost.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Namsai ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call the SFURTI cluster coordinator (Namsai District Administration, 0368-2240200) and visit the Common Facility Centre to see processing setup and meet 2-3 farmer suppliers. Attend the next Namsai district farmer haat (usually monthly, ask at administration office). Zero cost, doable in 1 week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh turmeric rhizomes directly from Namsai farmers through the cluster (contact CFC manager) — ₹8-12 per kg for Grade A rhizomes, minimum 500kg first batch, delivered to CFC or your rented space. Suppliers are concentrated in Namsai town, Lekabali, and Chowkham villages.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent dried storage + processing space in or adjacent to the CFC (₹10-15K/month shared space) OR partner with existing CFC equipment for ₹200-300 per tonne processed. Buy a small turmeric polisher (₹40-60K), sifter (₹15-20K), and packing materials (₹20-30K). Total production setup: ₹80-150K. First batch of 500kg raw → 120-140kg dried powder → ₹2-2.5L gross input cost.
4
Brand & Register
Register on Udyam portal (free, online in 20 min — udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for GI registration support through Ministry of Agriculture (₹5-8L process, 18-24 month timeline, but provides premium positioning once approved; file with District Administration). If exporting, obtain IEC code from nearest APEDA office in Guwahati (₹2-5K, 15-20 days). Get FSSAI license (₹10-20K, 30 days) for packaged turmeric powder.
5
First Sale
First 3 months: sell 50kg lots to 5-8 local Ayurvedic shops in Namsai, Dibrugarh (₹220-280/kg wholesale = ₹11-14K per 50kg order). Parallel: create WhatsApp catalog + list on Udyam portal. By month 3, pitch 1-2 tonne orders to 2-3 Ayurvedic manufacturers identified via industry directories. Expected first-month revenue: ₹40-60K (mostly B2B bulk).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire 1 full-time operations person to manage CFC processing daily. Upgrade to own 1500 sq ft rental space (₹20-30K/month). Invest ₹2-3L in semi-automatic turmeric grader and vacuum sealer. Attend Aroma Ingredient Expo (Mumbai, Feb), AAHARTI trade shows (Delhi, Oct). Build direct relationships with 8-10 mid-sized Ayurvedic manufacturers, each taking 2-5 tonnes/month. Launch premium organic-certified line (₹15-20K certification cost) targeting D2C at ₹500/500g.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹20L subsidy for cluster infrastructure, equipment, and training; Namsai cluster is already SFURTI-funded.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan (90% subsidized for SC/ST, 80% for others) with 5-6 year repayment at 8% interest. Applicable for turmeric processing unit setup.
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh ODOP scheme
direct marketing support, subsidized stalls at state exhibitions, training grants for product diversification, typically ₹50-100K per unit annually.
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture & Spice Mission
price support guarantee, minimum ₹100/kg for certified organic turmeric, valid for approved cluster members.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
