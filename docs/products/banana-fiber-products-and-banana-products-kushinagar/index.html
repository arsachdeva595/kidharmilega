Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kushinagar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kushinagar's main banana cultivation belt near Kadsura and Padarauna villages; connect with existing artisan groups through DC Handicraft office; study fiber retting techniques at local craft centers; benchmark products at Delhi's Dilli Haat and Jaipur's Craft Hub.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source banana stems directly from farmers in Kushinagar's Tamkuhi Raj block (₹8,000-12,000 per ton for first batch); procure dried banana fruits from Uttar Pradesh Agricultural Produce Market Committee; establish contract with 3-4 farmer cooperatives for consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered artisan groups under DC Handicraft (available through district office); establish small retting and fiber processing unit (₹50,000-80,000 setup); train 5-8 artisans in quality control; create food processing facility with FSSAI certification for banana chips (₹200,000-300,000).
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam for MSME benefits; obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Gorakhpur (40 km away); get FSSAI license for food products (₹1,500-5,000); apply for GI certification documentation to strengthen brand positioning; register brand on India Brand Asset Foundation (IBAF) database.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Karigar, Flipkart Samarth, and Etsy for global reach; create Instagram and Pinterest accounts showcasing artisan stories; partner with 2-3 fair-trade export aggregators (e.g., India Craft House); list on speciality platforms like TheArtisansCollective and Fair Trade Federation; initiate bulk orders from corporate gift companies.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 20-30 craftspeople across 5 villages; establish own retting facility to control quality; launch international B2B export arm through trade bodies like APEDA; develop 3-4 new product variants (banana leather, eco-packaging); secure orders from top 50 sustainable lifestyle brands; explore Agri-Export Cluster funding.

PM Vishwakarma
₹500 monthly stipend + skill training for artisans; ₹15,000 toolkit grant; ₹3 lakh credit facility at 5% interest
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Handicraft Development Scheme
25% capital subsidy on machinery and equipment for registered handicraft units
🏚
UP Aaditya Vigyan Kendra Scheme
free design and technology support for traditional craft entrepreneurs in identified ODOP districts
