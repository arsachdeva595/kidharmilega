Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Indore ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Palda industrial area where Prataap Snacks facility is located (Khasra 378/1/4) — no cost, observe packaging lines and meet distributors. Call 3-4 potato suppliers in Mhow region (₹8-12/kg wholesale rate). Attend Indore Chamber of Commerce meeting or food processing meetup — many snack makers gather there. Register free Udyam account online.
2
Source Raw Materials
Bulk potatoes sourced from Mhow vegetable market (30km south) or Ujjain wholesale market at ₹8-12/kg. First batch of 500kg costs ₹5,000-6,000. Supplier names: Mhow Potato Cooperative, Ujjain Kisan Samiti. Arrange cold storage rental at Palda or Dewas APMC-linked coldchain (₹8-12 per quintal per month).
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 800-1200 sqft unit in Palda industrial area or Dewas Food Park at ₹15,000-25,000/month. Install cutting/frying line (₹8-15L), packaging machine (₹3-5L), or partner with co-packers like existing snack units. Hire 4-6 workers at ₹12,000-15,000/month. First production run of 2,000 packets/day requires ₹50,000-80,000 working capital.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online). Apply for FSSAI license (₹5,000-8,000, 15-20 days). Get APEDA registration if exporting (₹2,500-5,000, 7 days). Skip GI registration (Indore potato has no GI). Brand design + packaging mockup ₹15,000-25,000. Trademark filing ₹8,000-12,000.
5
First Sale
First sale: approach local kirana chains (Bhairagarh, Rajwada areas) with samples. Minimum first order typically 200-500 packets. Alternatively, list on Blinkit/Big Basket (₹20,000-50,000 platform fee + 25-30% commission). Expected first month: 5,000-10,000 packets = ₹60,000-150,000 gross revenue.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire operations manager (₹30,000/month), add second production line (₹8-10L capex), expand to 3-4 flavors. Exhibit at Food India trade shows (Delhi, Mumbai). Build export distributor relationships — contact food export agencies in Indore, establish RCMC (Registered Category Manufacturer). Target regional modern trade expansion (Bhopal, Ujjain, Nashik).

Government Schemes

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹25L subsidy for manufacturing setup (25% for general category, 35% for SC/ST)
🏛
MUDRA Loan
up to ₹10L for equipment and working capital at 4-6% interest via banks
🏚
Madhya Pradesh One District One Product (ODOP) Scheme
subsidy on packaging, certification, and brand development up to ₹5L
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Food Processing Subsidy
20-25% subsidy on machinery purchase for registered food processors
