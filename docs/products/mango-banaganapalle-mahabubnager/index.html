opportunity, Mahabubnager mein.

Mahabubnagar's semi-arid climate, nutrient-rich black soil, and altitude of 300-500m create ideal conditions for growing Banaganapalle mangoes. The district has 60+ years of heritage cultivation expertise and natural water resources from the Krishna River basin, making it the only region globally producing this specific cultivar with consistent quality and flavor profile.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mahabubnager ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Banaganapalle village and Hyderabad Agricultural Produce Market to understand harvest cycles, pricing, and quality grading standards. Meet 3-5 established mango exporters in Mahabubnagar district to learn supply chain logistics and international regulations.
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner directly with established mango farmers in Banaganapalle cluster (1000+ farmers organized via cooperatives like Banaganapalle Farmers Producer Company). First batch sourcing cost: ₹3-5 Lac for 10 metric tons during June-July season. Secure agreements for consistent supply from 5-10 farmer partners.
3
Produce / Partner
Skip production — instead aggregate & grade mangoes from farmer partners using existing cold storage facilities in Tandur town (25km from Banaganapalle). Partner with certified cold chain operators (₹1-2 Lac/season) for temperature-controlled storage at 13-16°C for extended shelf life (up to 4 weeks). Contract with packaging vendors in Hyderabad for food-grade boxes and tissue paper wrapping.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in) as fruit trading/aggregation enterprise. Obtain IEC code from APEDA for export eligibility. Register brand name with state Directorate of Agriculture. Get GI certification documentation (already awarded) and use official GI logo on packaging. Obtain FSSAI registration for value-added products.
5
First Sale
Launch on Ninjart and FarmOK with 'Fresh Banaganapalle Mangoes' listings during May-August peak season. Simultaneously contact 20-30 premium fruit retailers (Modern Bazaar, Reliance Supermarkets) with samples and batch pricing. Register with APEDA-approved export houses and submit 2-3 trial shipments to UAE and UK buyers via freight forwarders in Hyderabad Port.
6
Scale
After first season, expand farmer network to 30-50 partners across 500+ hectares. Invest in own cold storage facility (₹15-25 Lac) to increase margin and supply consistency. Launch private label products for modern retail chains. Establish export processing unit (EPU) to handle international logistics directly. Create social media presence (Instagram, YouTube) showcasing Banaganapalle heritage and farming practices to build D2C brand.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lac subsidy for agri-entrepreneurship and food processing with 5% to 6% GST exemption on mango produce.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹15-20 Lac for cluster development, value-addition, and common facilities in agricultural clusters including Banaganapalle.
🏚
Telangana Rythu Bandhu Scheme
direct income support to farming partners and incentive for GI-certified crop cultivation, ₹5000-10000 per farmer per season.
🏚
Telangana Export Promotion Policy
5% capital subsidy on export-related infrastructure investment (cold storage, packaging, freight) up to ₹10 Lac for registered agri-exporters.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
