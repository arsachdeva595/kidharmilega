opportunity, Mahabubnager mein.

Mahabubnagar lies in India's premier mango belt (Rayalaseema region) with 24.35 lakh metric tonnes annual production across AP-Telangana, supported by the Banaganapalle cluster's 100+ year cultivation history and Nizam-era cold-storage infrastructure. The district connects directly to farmer cooperatives in Banaganapalle, Paanyam, and Nandyal mandals (Kurnool) through established supply routes. Export shipments clear through Hyderabad and Bangalore airports (200-250 km away) to US, UK, Japan, and Gulf markets, with ₹20.68 crore annual export value demonstrating proven international demand.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mahabubnager ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3-4 fruit traders at Begumpet wholesale market Hyderabad (free call). Visit Banaganapalle mandal haat (Kurnool) during April-May season — 2 day trip, ₹500 travel cost, speak to farmer cooperative heads. Register on APEDA.gov.in (free) to see active mango exporters' profiles and export pricing. Zero-cost, doable within 2 weeks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source directly from Banaganapalle Farmer Producer Organization (FPO) or Paanyam mandal fruit collection centers during April-June season. First batch: 5 tonnes costs ₹50-75K (₹10-15/kg green fruit), transportable via refrigerated truck to Hyderabad (₹8-12K). Supplier area: Banaganapalle town (Kurnool district, 180 km south of Hyderabad).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner model preferred: rent cold-storage facility in Hyderabad industrial area (₹15-20K/month for 5-ton capacity) or Charminar/Malakpet food processing zones. Ripening chamber setup costs ₹80-120K (ethylene ripening room). Processing facility (pulping, packaging) requires ₹2.5-4.5L for basic semi-commercial unit. Alternatively, outsource processing to existing FSSAI-certified mango concentrate makers in Telangana (Tandur, Tandupally areas) at ₹3-5/kg processing cost.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) at udyam.gov.in free (same-day, online). Apply for GI 'Banaganapalle Mango' use license through Andhra Pradesh Intellectual Property office (₹5-10K, 2-3 months). Obtain FSSAI food license (₹500-1500, 1 month). If exporting: apply for IEC (Importer Exporter Code) at dgft.gov.in (free, 1-2 days) and APEDA export registration (₹5K, 2 weeks). Total: ₹10-17K, 2.5 months.
5
First Sale
First sale: list on BigBasket/Grofers platform with 10-15 kg/week premium mango pulp packs (₹400-500 per 500g). Expected 40-60 orders first month = ₹16-30K revenue. Parallel: approach 2-3 Begumpet fruit wholesalers with ₹30K bulk sample order (500 kg fresh fruit). First institutional sale via hotel procurement: pitch to a 4-5 star hotel group kitchen procurement (5-10 kg/week contract = ₹8-12K/month).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire 1 supply-chain manager (₹18-25K/month) to manage farmer relationships and logistics. Invest ₹3-5L in dedicated cold chain (2 refrigerated vehicles). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹10-25L at 25% subsidy) to set up 10-tonne processing facility. Register as FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) to export directly via APEDA at better margins (35-50% margins vs 40-55%). Exhibit at Fruit Logistica Berlin or India Food Forum to secure EU/UK importers. Target: ₹30-50L annual revenue by year 2.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹10-25L subsidy (25% of project cost, max ₹25L) for new units in food processing, with soft loan bridge financing up to ₹5L
🏛
GI registration support via Ministry of Agriculture
one-time ₹1-1.5L reimbursement for GI certification cost and legal fees (already granted to Banaganapalle in 2017; new users can leverage existing GI tag)
🏚
Telangana MSME Promotion Policy
₹1-5L subsidy for food processing units in specified zones (Tandur, Tandupally food parks); 5-year power subsidy (50% rebate on power bills)
🏚
Telangana Export Promotion Scheme
₹2-5L subsidy for agricultural exports, APEDA registration support, air-freight cost reimbursement (40% of freight for first 5 shipments)
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
