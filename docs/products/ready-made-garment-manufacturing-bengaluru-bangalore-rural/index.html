Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Bengaluru (Bangalore) Rural ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kanakapura textile cluster and Dobbispet industrial area; attend ITME (Indian Textile Machinery Exhibition) or Texfair India; connect with Karnataka Textile Development Board for market insights and cluster information
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fabrics from Bengaluru wholesale markets (Chickpete) or direct from mills in Tamil Nadu; procure trims from Leather Complex, Bengaluru; initial batch (500 units) requires ₹4-6 lakhs investment depending on product complexity
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing garment units in Kanakapura/Dobbispet clusters or lease 1000-2000 sq ft space with basic machinery (cutting, stitching, pressing machines ₹15-25 lakhs); hire experienced tailors and supervisors from local textile unions
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam portal as MSME (Small); obtain IEC code from DGFT for export eligibility; file trademark for brand name at IP office; get GST registration; obtain NOC from local pollution board; consider FSSAI if adding home textile products
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon/Flipkart with 50+ product SKUs; create Instagram shop targeting fashion-conscious millennials; approach multi-brand retail chains in Bengaluru (Lifestyle, Westside); join IndiaStack or Global Sources for B2B export enquiries; participate in IHGF Delhi or Engage trade shows
6
Scale
Expand to 3-4 production units by month 8-10; hire dedicated design and QA teams; invest in automated pressing/folding systems; launch own D2C website with influencer marketing; establish relationships with 5-7 export agents; apply for SFURTI cluster development support for shared infrastructure
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy for manufacturing units with 10% margin money from applicant
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
up to ₹10 lakhs collateral-free credit for MSME garment manufacturing through participating banks
🏚
Karnataka Textile Policy 2019-2024
5% subsidy on machinery investment and 3% subsidy on lease rent for textile manufacturing units
🏚
KASSIA (Karnataka Small Scale Industries Association) Support
cluster development grant and common facility center access in Dobbispet textile cluster
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
