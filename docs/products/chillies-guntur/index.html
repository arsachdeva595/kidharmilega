Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Guntur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tenali and Guntur APMC markets to study pricing, quality grades, and trader networks. Connect with Chilli Growers Association and visit processing units in Narasaraopet cluster. Analyze export pricing on APEDA portal and demand trends.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw chillies directly from farmers in Guntur, Tenali, or Narasaraopet during peak season (Nov-Jan) at ₹40-70/kg depending on grade. First batch: 5 MT costs ₹2-3.5 lakhs. Establish relationships with 2-3 reliable farmer suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with contract drying (hire local drying yards in Tenali at ₹10-15/kg) or small-scale mechanical dryer (₹5-8L investment). Partner with established grinders for initial volumes or invest in semi-automated chilli grinding unit (₹15-25L). Ensure FSSAI compliance through testing at local labs.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam for MSE status, obtain IEC code from Guntur DGFT office for export. File FSSAI registration (₹5K-10K), TDS registration, and GST. Register brand name with IP office. Apply for GI-certified producer membership through Guntur Chilli Growers Cooperative Society.
5
First Sale
Approach local spice wholesalers in Tenali market directly. List on IndiaMART and TradeKey for B2B bulk buyers. Create Amazon, Flipkart, and Jio Mart FMCG listings for packaged products. Contact export houses in Mumbai/Delhi for overseas orders. Attend Spice Board trade fairs.
6
Scale
After 6 months profitability, invest in cold storage (₹20-40L) to capture off-season pricing premiums. Develop private-label products for larger retailers. Build direct export channels via registering with Spice Board. Expand to complementary products (turmeric, coriander). Seek SIDBI/NABARD credit for processing unit expansion.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for agro-processing units in rural areas with 15-25% beneficiary contribution
🏛
PM Kisan Samman Nidhi
supports farmer-entrepreneurs with ₹6,000 annual support if engaged in value-addition through chilli processing
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Agro-Processing and Warehousing Corporation (APAWC)
provides easy credit and infrastructure support for chilli-based agro-processing units at concessional rates
🏚
Andhra Pradesh ODOP Promotion Scheme
state-level support for registered ODOP producers including brand building assistance and export credit guarantee
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
