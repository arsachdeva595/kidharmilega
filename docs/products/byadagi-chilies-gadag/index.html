Byadagi Chilies ki
opportunity, Gadag mein.

Gadag district in north Karnataka sits in a warm semi-arid zone ideal for Byadagi chili cultivation, with 8,000+ hectares under production and 300+ registered farmer groups. The chili cluster is concentrated in Gadag, Mundargi, and Londa taluks, with dried chili mandi at Gadag city handling 40-50 MT daily during harvest (Sept-Oct). Proximity to Belgaum port (80km), Mumbai port (400km), and direct air cargo to Europe via Bangalore Airport (300km) makes export viable; domestic buyers include spice traders in Delhi, Mumbai, and Kolkata.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Gadag ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gadag city mandi on a harvest day (Sept-Oct) and meet 3 chili traders to understand pricing, grades, and farmer networks — free. Call Gadag APMC office (0820-2206060) for harvest schedule. Register free on UDYAM portal (udyamregistration.gov.in) using Aadhar.
2
Source Raw Materials
Buy first batch (500 kg dried Byadagi chili at 85% cooking grade) from Gadag mandi traders or directly from farmer groups at Mundargi cooperative for ₹1.4-1.6L. Negotiate bulk rate (₹280-300/kg). Arrange local transport (₹2-3K) to your processing space.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a small 500 sq ft space (₹3-5K/month) in Gadag town or Belgaum with basic sorting, grading, and packing machines (₹80-120K one-time: vibrating sorter, moisture meter, vacuum sealer, labeling machine). OR partner with 2-3 existing local processors for contract packing at ₹15-20 per kg. Starting cost: ₹120-200K (machines) + ₹20K/month operations.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free online). Apply for GI registration under Byadagi Chili GI tag (already granted in 2009) — obtain GI certification from Gadag District Industries office for ₹5-10K. If exporting: apply for IEC code at nearest DGFT office or online (Directorate General of Foreign Trade) within 2 weeks, free. Get FSSAI registration for ₹500-1K if selling packaged spice.
5
First Sale
First sale: list on Amazon/BigBasket with 50 units (500g packs) — approval in 5-7 days. Target first order from a spice trader in Delhi via IndiaMART (post 20-30 MT lots). Expected first month: ₹40-60K from online, ₹50-80K from 1-2 bulk trade orders.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 2 packers + 1 QC person (₹15K/month total). Attend India Spice Expo (Jan, Delhi) and IFE Asia (biennial, Bangkok) for export buyer meetings. Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹25-50L) and state agriculture ministry export grant schemes once turnover crosses ₹5L. Establish direct farm contracts to lock in supply at 8-10% discount.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% of project cost for SC/ST, 25% for general) for food processing units with min ₹10L investment
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹10L for cluster-based projects including common facility centers and quality upgradation in spice clusters
🏚
Karnataka Agricultural Export Policy
5% subsidy on freight for agricultural exports above ₹10L, reimbursement via state horticulture department
🏚
Karnataka Food Processing Industry Promotion Scheme
₹50K GST refund for new FSSAI registrations in food processing + 5% interest subsidy on SIDBI loans up to ₹50L
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
