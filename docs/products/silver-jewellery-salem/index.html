Salem has a 400+ year heritage of silver jewelry making with a concentrated cluster of over 15,000 skilled artisans in the Old Town area. The district's proximity to major trade routes and established supply chains for pure silver, combined with generational expertise passed down through families, makes it uniquely positioned for high-quality, scalable silver jewelry production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Salem ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Salem Old Town (Zangani Street, Nethaji Road) to interact with existing master artisans; spend 5-7 days understanding design trends, quality standards, and pricing at clusters like Omalur Road; join Salem Silver Artisans Association for market intelligence.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 92.5% purity silver from certified dealers near Salem Central Market (₹3-4L for initial 50 kg batch); source gemstones from Omalur wholesale market; establish credit terms with 2-3 suppliers for consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 master artisans in Salem Old Town (₹15,000-25,000/month each) to start with 50-100 pieces/month; or set up own small workshop with casting/soldering equipment (₹4-6L investment); train 2-3 apprentices over 3-6 months.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online; obtain IEC code from nearest SEZ/DGFT office; register brand name with IP office; obtain Hallmark certification from BIS-approved assaying center in Salem; comply with Precious Metals Act.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C via Shopify/WooCommerce store with high-quality product photography; list on Etsy (lower fees for handicrafts) and Amazon Handmade; leverage Instagram/Pinterest for design storytelling; approach premium gifting platforms like GiftNxt, Ferns N Petals; send bulk samples to 10-15 boutique jewelry retailers.
6
Scale
After ₹15-20L revenue, hire dedicated design team; expand artisan network to 8-10 craftspeople; add customization services; explore B2B export partnerships via trade shows (Vibrant Gujarat, IITF); build wholesale distribution in metros; invest in brand awareness via influencer collaborations.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 credit at 0% interest for artisans, plus free toolkit and training in design/marketing
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for Gen category) for registered MSME setup
🏚
Tamil Nadu Handicraft Development Scheme
25% subsidy on machinery/equipment up to ₹5L, plus export promotion support
🏚
Chief Minister Integrated Scheme for Development of Weavers (CMISDW)
export incentives up to 5% for handmade jewelry artisans
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Marketing

Ye marketing ideas try karna mat bhulna

  • Create short-form video content on Instagram Reels & TikTok showing artisan craft process (silversmith at work, hammering, polishing) with story of the maker — aim for 500K views/month.
  • Partner with 5-7 micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in jewelry/wedding niche for unboxing and styling content; negotiate 15-20% commission on conversions.
  • Run targeted Pinterest ads (₹2-3K/month budget) promoting designs with keywords like 'silver jewelry India,' 'handmade jewelry,' 'sustainable jewelry' — high intent audience.
