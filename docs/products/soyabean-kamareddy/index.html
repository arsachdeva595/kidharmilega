Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kamareddy ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kamareddy Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) in Kamareddy town and connect with farmer cooperatives like Kamareddy Soybean Federation. Attend meetings at District Agriculture Office (DAC). Study export quality standards by visiting Hyderabad's agricultural export warehouses.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified soybean seeds from Kamareddy District Seed Association (₹30-40/kg for bulk). For first batch of 50 MT, budget ₹18-22 lakhs. Purchase from local farmers through primary agricultural societies at Kamareddy market or direct procurement from cooperatives at harvest time (Sept-Oct).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 progressive farmers in Kamareddy for contract farming on 50-100 acres. Alternatively, lease drying and cleaning facilities at local APMC or invest in semi-mechanized processing unit (₹8-12 lakhs for cleaning, grading, packaging machinery). Link with Kamareddy Women's Farmer Producer Organization for supply consistency.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in under Food Products Processing code). Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Hyderabad (₹5000, 7-10 days). Register brand name with IP office. Get FSSAI license (₹12000-25000 for startup category, 30 days). State GST registration mandatory.
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket, Blinkit, Zepto with 25kg bulk SKUs at ₹320-380/kg. Create wholesale account with local distributors in Hyderabad supplying to Tier-1 cities. Contact food processing units in Indore and Surat (soybean processing hubs) for bulk B2B. Use LinkedIn to reach international importers. Register on ExportGenius.in for export leads.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish certified processing unit (200 MT capacity) to capture value-addition margin. Launch ready-to-eat soybean snacks through local bakeries in Hyderabad. Partner with 5-star hotels for institutional supplies. Build export relationships through Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority (APEDA). Expand to 3 neighboring districts for sourcing.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% for general) for setting up food processing unit with machinery
🏛
APEDA Trade Development Scheme
₹5-10 lakh subsidy for export market development and overseas trade fair participation
🏚
Telangana Chief Minister's Agriculture Loan Scheme
provides ₹1-3 lakh concessional loans at 0% interest for agricultural entrepreneurs
🏚
Rythu Bandhu Scheme
₹5000 per acre direct cash transfer to farmers who can be contracted as supply partners for business
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
