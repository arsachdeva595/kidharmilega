Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — East Khasi Hills ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call Meghalaya Agriculture Marketing Board (Shillong office) and ask for Khasi Mandarin producer cooperative list. Visit Ri Bhoi or East Garo Hills cluster during harvest (Oct-Jan) — attend a local farmer haat. Download ODOP guidelines free from eastkhasihills.nic.in. Zero cost, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fruit directly from registered producer cooperatives in Ri Bhoi and East Garo Hills — first 5-tonne batch costs ₹4-5L at ₹80-100/kg from farmers. Negotiate post-harvest handling and sorting via Meghalaya Agriculture Marketing Board approved suppliers. Lead time 7-14 days.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 500-800 sqft cold-storage unit (₹15-25k/month) in Shillong or Cherrapunji with sorting/packing setup. Partnership model: work with 3-4 farmer groups (no capex) and take 5-8% commission on packed fruit. Packing unit setup (wooden crates, labels, ice pads) costs ₹50-80k. Total produce setup: ₹80-150k/month operating cost.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, 5 days online). File GI certification application under Khasi Mandarin GI (₹8-12k legal cost, 3-4 weeks). Register IEC code with DGFT for export (₹500, 5 days). Obtain APEDA registration if exporting (free). Total 60 days, ₹12-15k cost.
5
First Sale
First sale: contact BigBasket/Amazon Fresh vendor support — target ₹40-50k first order (500kg at ₹80-100/kg). Register on Blinkit marketplace (free). Approach 3-5 HORECA accounts (hotels, restaurants) for trial orders. Expected first-month revenue: ₹1.5-2.2L from wholesale + D2C mix.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 supply-chain manager (₹25k/month), 2 packing staff (₹12k each), 1 sales/account manager (₹20k/month). Expand to 3 cold-store units. Target Lulu Group export partnership via Meghalaya Agriculture Board. Exhibit at GulfFood Dubai or Chennai Food & Hospitality trade shows (₹2-3L booth cost). Increase sourcing to 15-20 MT/month.

₹25L subsidy cap (15-25% of project cost) for new agri-businesses, repayment 60-120 months

cost-sharing for cold-chain logistics, 50% subsidy up to ₹3L/unit for approved exporters
