Historical merchant networks and existing export infrastructure provide immediate market access.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kota ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kota city weaving clusters (Nayapura, Balmikinagar) to observe production; connect with Rajasthan Handloom Board office in Kota; study existing GI-certified producers like Samrat Silk Mills and Kota Doria Weavers Association.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton yarn (40s-60s count) from Jodhpur spinners (₹400-600/kg), silk yarn from Bangalore suppliers (₹800-1200/kg). First batch of 100 meters requires ₹25,000-35,000. Connect through Rajasthan State Handicrafts Development Corporation.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered Kota weaver clusters; negotiate rates of ₹150-250 per meter for standard Doria. Start with 50-100 meter monthly orders; scale with proven sales. Alternatively, lease pit-loom space in Nayapura cluster (₹5,000-8,000/loom/month).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry of MSME portal), obtain IEC code from DGFT Jaipur office, file GI product certification proof with District Industries Centre Kota. Trademark brand name at IP India (Delhi). Register GST under handloom category.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram/Pinterest with high-quality fabric photography and weaver stories; list on Flipkart Sarees/Fabrics, Amazon Handmade India, and Etsy (target international buyers). Approach ethnic wear retailers in Delhi (Chandni Chowk), Mumbai (Fort), and Bangalore (UB City area). Cold email luxury boutiques and fashion designers.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire a dedicated export manager; apply for SIRO/TUF schemes from Rajasthan; partner with overseas distributors in USA/UAE; launch branded saree collections; collaborate with fashion influencers on YouTube and Instagram; exhibit at GiftWorld Jaipur and Handloom Export shows.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15,000 as interest-free loan for handloom artisans and micro-entrepreneurs; applies to registered weavers and exporters.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh grant for handloom micro-enterprises; 25% subsidy for Gen/OBC, 35% for SC/ST.
🏚
Rajasthan MUDRA Scheme
up to ₹10 lakh collateral-free loan for handloom and textile businesses at concessional interest rates.
🏚
Rajasthan Handloom Export Promotion Scheme
10% subsidy on export turnover and free certification for GI products; administered by DC Handloom Rajasthan.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
