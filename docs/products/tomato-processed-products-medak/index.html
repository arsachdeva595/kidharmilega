opportunity, Medak mein.

Medak's agrarian economy centers on tomato cultivation with optimal soil pH and climate conditions supporting year-round harvests. The district's abundant supply chain infrastructure and proximity to major food processing hubs like Hyderabad enable efficient transformation of raw tomatoes into value-added products. Established farmer networks and cooperative societies provide stable raw material sourcing at competitive costs.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Medak ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit tomato processing clusters in Tandur (Ranga Reddy district) and Medak Agricultural Produce Marketing Committee (APMC). Meet with Medak Cooperative Farmers Society and analyze competitor brands like Shan Tomato Ketchup. Study FSSAi food processing regulations at Telangana FSSAI office, Hyderabad. Analyze 3-5 existing processing units' production models and cost structures.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw tomatoes directly from Medak APMC during peak season (Nov-Mar) at ₹8-12/kg or negotiate annual contracts with farmer cooperatives like Medak Farmers Society for ₹9/kg. For first batch of 5,000 liters ketchup/sauce: ₹450,000-550,000 for tomatoes + ₹200,000 for packaging + ₹150,000 for spices/additives. Identify 2-3 supplier backup options to ensure supply continuity.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with FSSAI-licensed contract manufacturers in Hyderabad (200km away) like food processing facilities in Jeedimetla industrial area, or set up in-house production in Medak with proper license. Minimum 500L batch production capacity needed. Hire food technologist for recipe formulation. Register with Medak District Administration for pollution control board (CPCB) approval and secure FSSAI manufacturing license (₹35,000-50,000 for small-scale).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online via udyamregistration.gov.in within 2 days (free). Obtain IEC from DGFT SEEPZ office, Hyderabad (₹200-500). Secure trademark registration with IP India (₹4,500-10,000, 18-month timeline). Design packaging adhering to FSSAI labeling norms with Nutrition Information, allergen declaration. Obtain state GST registration (₹0, online process) with 5% or 12% tax slab eligibility.
5
First Sale
Launch with strategic sampling at 50-100 retail outlets in Hyderabad and Medak. Activate Amazon Pantry + JioMart seller accounts (free, 15% commission). Approach DM Cart and Big Basket for category inclusion (3-4 week approval). Negotiate with 5-10 regional distributor networks for ₹20,000-40,000 bulk orders. Execute 2-3 Facebook + Instagram campaigns targeting 'healthy condiments' keywords (₹500/day budget). Partner with food bloggers for 5-10 review posts.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand SKU portfolio (tomato paste, garlic sauce, spiced ketchup variants). Achieve retail presence in 500+ outlets across Telangana and neighboring states. Invest ₹500,000-800,000 in regional distributor network and HORECA sales team. Launch private label manufacturing contracts with 2-3 retail chains. Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹10-25L) for capacity expansion to 10,000L production unit.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (25% of project cost) for food processing entrepreneurs in non-metro areas
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
up to ₹10 lakh unsecured business loan for working capital and machinery at 8-12% interest
🏚
Telangana Food Processing Policy 2022
capital subsidy of 25% for food processing units with priority for ODOP products, plus GST reimbursement for 5 years
🏚
Telangana Agro-Export Promotion Scheme
financial assistance for export-oriented food processing businesses, including logistics support and market linkage programs
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
