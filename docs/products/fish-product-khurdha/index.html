Khurdha benefits from direct access to the Bay of Bengal and established fishing ports in Paradip and coastal villages, ensuring consistent supply of fresh catch. The district has a long tradition of fish processing with existing infrastructure, skilled labor, and proximity to major consumption centers like Bhubaneswar. Government fisheries support and ODOP recognition position it as a hub for value-added fish products.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Khurdha ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Paradip and Baleshwar fishing clusters; study Odisha Fisheries Department reports; interview 5-10 existing fish paste and canned product makers in Bhubaneswar and coastal areas; analyze competitor products on Amazon and Flipkart.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh fish from Paradip New Harbour or local fish markets (₹200-400/kg depending on variety). Initial 50kg batch for testing: ₹15,000-20,000. Source spices from Odisha spice merchants or Guntur. Canning materials from Bangalore-based suppliers (approximately ₹4-6 per unit for jars/cans).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with licensed food processing units in Bhubaneswar's industrial area (Patia, Nayapalli) or Paradip; alternatively, set up a small FSSAI-certified kitchen with wet and dry zones. Start with contract manufacturing at existing units before scaling in-house.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) online; obtain FSSAI license (food business operator category); apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Bhubaneswar for export capability. File trademark with IP office. Brand name registration with state commerce department.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon and Flipkart with premium positioning (₹250-450 per 200g jar); approach Modern Retail procurement teams in Bhubaneswar; partner with 3-5 premium restaurants for trial; create WhatsApp bulk order lists for corporate gifting; reach out to online seafood retailers like Nimmifish.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range (dry fish, fish curry paste, smoked varieties); invest in cold storage and logistics; target tier-2 cities (Raipur, Ranchi, Kolkata); apply for SFURTI or state export subsidy; hire sales team for modern retail expansion.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for food processing MSMEs with 35% margin in urban areas.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster-based support for food processing units including infrastructure and branding.
🏚
Odisha Fish Feed and Seed Subsidy Scheme
support for fisheries-based MSMEs with input subsidy up to 25% on raw materials.
🏚
CM Swavalambi Yojana
financial assistance up to ₹5 lakh for self-employed in agriculture and allied sectors including fish processing.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
