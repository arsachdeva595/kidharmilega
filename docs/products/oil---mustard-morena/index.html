Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Morena ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Morena Tehsil's agricultural haat (Banmore or Morena town) to speak with mustard seed traders — call 3-4 suppliers to understand current seed pricing (₹50-65/kg range). Register free on Udyam (udyamregistration.gov.in) using Aadhaar. Attend local oil miller associations to learn equipment costs and production loss rates. Cost: ₹0, takes 2 weeks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mustard seeds from local traders in Morena town or Banmore tehsil at ₹50-65/kg. For first batch (500kg), cost ₹27,500-32,500. Negotiate 10-15% discount for repeat orders. Verify seed moisture (max 8%) and oil content (38-45%) with a simple test kit (₹500-1000 one-time).
3
Produce / Partner
Lease a 1,200-1,500 sq ft unit in Morena town industrial area (rent ₹12,000-18,000/month) or partner with established oil millers on contract processing (40-50% yield, ₹8-12/kg processing fee). Mini oil press setup (10-15 kg/hr capacity): ₹4-6L one-time. For Kachi Ghani (unrefined), cold-press method only; avoid heat to preserve premium pricing (+20-30%).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 10 mins). Obtain FSSAI license (₹2,000-4,000, 2-3 weeks). Apply for IEC (Import-Export Code) via DGFT if targeting exports (₹0, online). GI registration not applicable (Morena mustard oil has no GI tag). Get APEDA membership if exporting (₹5,000 annual). ISO 9001 optional but speeds Gulf buyer approvals (₹30,000-50,000, 4-6 weeks).
5
First Sale
List on BigBasket and Amazon Fresh in Gwalior/Indore regions with 500ml bottle (₹180-220 MRP). First order typically 50-100 cases via wholesale distributors. Expect ₹40,000-60,000 first month (10-15 cases/week). Simultaneously email APEDA-listed Gulf traders with product specs, pricing, MOQ (minimum 1,000L), and FSSAI/IEC documents. Export first sale takes 8-12 weeks negotiation.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month, hire 1 QA technician and 1 sales associate (₹25,000/month each). Upgrade to 25-30 kg/hr capacity press (₹8-12L). Exhibit at India Food Forum (Delhi, Oct-Nov annually), GFSI shows (Dubai), and attend Gulf trade delegations. Build B2B partnerships with food manufacturers. Invest ₹50,000 in brand photography and website. Set up cold storage for seasonal bulk mustard seed hoarding (cost ₹3-5L).

Honest assessment: Mustard oil is a commodity — price risk is real. If demand dries or prices crash (global glut scenario), you're squeezed. However, exit options exist: (1) Sell production capacity to larger mills. (2) Pivot to medicinal/cosmetic mustard oil formulations (same equipment, higher margins 60-75%). (3) Switch to groundnut/soybean oil using same press (local switching possible). (4) Work as contractor/millers for others (low margin ₹8-12/kg, but steady). Worst case: sell equipment, salvage 40-50% of capital. Morena's strong seed supply network means raw materials won't disappear — it's price volatility and buyer acquisition that kills startups, not inputs.
