Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Rajnandgaon ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Rajnandgaon Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) in city center and Manpur village cluster (known soybean hub). Meet 3-5 established soybean farmers and processors. Study production cycles at Indore Soybean Processors Association. Budget: ₹5,000 for travel and farm visits.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified soybean seeds from Rajnandgaon District Seed Association or Madhya Pradesh Seed Corporation. First-batch cost for 1 hectare: ₹35,000-45,000 (seeds ₹15-18K, fertilizers ₹12-15K, labor ₹8-12K). Bulk procurement reduces per-unit cost by 15-20% after 3 cycles.
3
Produce / Partner
Begin with contract farming model with 5-10 local farmers holding ₹2-3L upfront investment. Partner with Indore-based oil processing units (Chhattisgarh has 12 registered soybean processors) for initial output processing. Cold storage facility rental at APMC: ₹8,000-12,000/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (food processing) within 2 weeks online. Apply for IEC code from Nagpur or Raipur customs office for export. Register brand name with IP office. Obtain Food Safety License (FSSAI) within 4-6 weeks (₹2,000-5,000 for small business). Certifications: Organic (if applicable, ₹15,000-25,000), ISO 9001.
5
First Sale
List soybean oil on Amazon, Flipkart, and BigBasket within 3 months. Launch WhatsApp bulk buyer network of 50+ traders in Indore, Nagpur, Pune food clusters. Attend Rajnandgaon wholesale markets twice weekly. Direct B2B outreach to 20 food manufacturers per month. Budget: ₹50,000 for initial online marketplace setup and photography.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish own 500kg/day mini oil extraction unit (₹12-15L capital, process own and contract volumes). Launch DTC brand with soy milk powder and tofu as premium products. Secure contract with 2-3 regional FMCG companies for volume supply. Pursue organic certification for 20% price premium. Explore export via food consolidators in Mumbai/Bangalore.
Government Schemes

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan with 25-35% government subsidy for food processing business
🏛
MUDRA Yojana (Shishu/Kishore)
up to ₹50 lakh collateral-free loan for small agro-enterprises
🏚
Chhattisgarh Rajiv Gandhi Kisaan Nyay Yojana
₹10,000/acre compensation and subsidized inputs for registered farmers
🏚
Chhattisgarh Food Park Scheme
40% capital subsidy on food processing infrastructure if unit is established near notified food parks
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
