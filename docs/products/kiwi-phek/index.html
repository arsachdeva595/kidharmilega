Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Phek ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established kiwi farms in Phek (contact Phek District Agriculture Department) and Arunachal Pradesh (Papum Pare district). Attend workshops by ICAR-RC at Barapani, Meghalaya. Join Northeast Farmer Producer Organizations. Study pest & disease management specific to 1,600m altitude cultivation.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified kiwi saplings from Indian Institute of Horticulture Research (Bangalore) or State Horticulture Department nurseries (₹50-80 per sapling). Buy trellising materials from Dimapur or Kohima agri-supply vendors (₹30,000-50,000 for 0.5 hectare setup). First-batch cost: ₹250,000-400,000 for land preparation, saplings, and infrastructure on 0.5 hectare.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with trained farmers in Phek district through District Agriculture Cooperative Society. Establish nursery block first (3-4 months) before orchard planting. Contract with local farm laborers or form farmer producer groups. Begin with 0.5-1 hectare pilot; production starts in year 2, full yield by year 4.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (online via udyamregistration.gov.in) under agriculture processing. Obtain IEC code from DGFT for export readiness. Create brand identity: Design label emphasizing 'Nagaland Pure Kiwi' and GI potential. Register trademark with IP India. Get organic certification (APEDA/BJHC) for premium positioning—crucial for D2C and export.
5
First Sale
List on BigBasket, JioMart, and local agri-platforms like Ninjacart. Partner with premium supermarkets in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore (mother-daughter chains). Direct supply to 5-star hotels in Northeast India. Create Instagram/WhatsApp direct-sales channel with agriculture blogs focused on superfoods. Attend agricultural trade fairs in Guwahati, Shillong.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 2-3 hectares. Develop value-added product line (kiwi powder, energy bars). Build wholesale B2B relationships with food processors and juice manufacturers. Target national FMCG retailers. Explore exports to Southeast Asia via Nagaland Chamber of Commerce. Establish agritourism sideline (farm tours, farm stay) for secondary revenue.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general category) for agri-processing and horticulture enterprises in rural Nagaland.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
for horticultural clusters in Northeast India; offers grants up to ₹25L for establishing kiwi processing units.
🏚
Nagaland State Horticulture Mission
provides ₹50,000-₹1.5L subsidy per hectare for kiwi plantation with infrastructure support.
🏚
Nagaland Farmer Producer Company Incentive Scheme
up to ₹5L grant for forming FPCs for collective marketing of horticultural products.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
