Banka Silk ki
opportunity, Banka mein.

Banka has cultivated silk production for over 400 years with climate conditions ideal for mulberry cultivation. The district's artisan community possesses inherited expertise in traditional dyeing and weaving techniques that create distinctive silk characteristics unavailable elsewhere. Government support through DC Handloom initiatives has established dedicated silk clusters.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Banka ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Banka silk clusters in Amarpur and Banka town; meet weavers at Government Sericulture Center Banka; attend Handloom Expo Banka (annual); connect with DC Handloom office for market intelligence on pricing and artisan networks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cocoons from Banka Sericulture Department (₹4-6 per cocoon); procure natural dyes from Indigo suppliers in Varanasi; first-batch cost for 50kg production: ₹1.5-2.2L including labor; establish 3-month advance orders with established cocoon suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-10 established weaver families in Amarpur cluster through artisan networks; provide design templates and quality standards; implement weekly quality audits; consider operating a small pre-production unit for yarn preparation while outsourcing loom work to artisans on commission model (8-12% of final price).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in under Handloom Weaving; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Patna; register brand with DC Handloom for ODOP recognition; file trademark for brand name with IP India; obtain Handloom Mark certification from Ministry of Handloom.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram/Pinterest showcasing artisan stories and product process; list on Etsy, The Artisan Box, and Fab India; approach luxury hotel chains for bulk orders; cold-email designer boutiques in metro cities; participate in Handloom Week exhibitions in Delhi and Mumbai; establish WhatsApp Business catalog for B2B inquiries.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 25-30 weavers by month 7; introduce 3-4 new design collections monthly; launch influencer collaborations with fashion bloggers; establish exclusive retail partnerships in 5 metro cities; explore B2B government contracts; build export pipelines via trade missions to Japan and EU.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan at subsidized 5-6% with 35% subsidy for artisan-linked enterprises
🏛
Ministry of Handloom Amended Technology Upgradation Fund Scheme (ATUFS)
up to 40% subsidy for loom technology and design infrastructure
🏚
Bihar Artisan Livelihood Scheme
direct artisan wage subsidy up to ₹300/day and health insurance for registered weavers
🏚
Bihar Handloom Development Scheme
soft loans up to ₹5L for equipment and working capital at 4% interest with 2-year moratorium
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
