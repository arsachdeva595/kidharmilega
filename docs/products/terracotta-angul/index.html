Terracotta ki
opportunity, Angul mein.

Angul district has rich deposits of high-quality clay along the Brahmani river basin, supporting a multi-generational tradition of terracotta artisans primarily in villages like Nayagaon, Talcher, and Chhendipada. The region's proximity to tribal communities and rural populations ensures authentic design vocabulary and sustainable raw material sourcing. Local artisan clusters have developed deep expertise in both decorative and utility terracotta products.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Angul ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nayagaon terracotta cluster (35km from Angul town) and Talcher artisan villages; study 2-3 existing producers; attend clay expo at Indian Institute of Handicrafts, Delhi; benchmark Rajasthan terracotta pricing on Etsy and Pepperfry.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source clay directly from local pit mines near Brahmani river (₹300-400/quintal); establish relationship with 3-4 women's SHGs or individual artisans in Nayagaon cluster; first batch procurement: ₹35,000-50,000 for 500kg clay, natural pigments, and kiln wood for 2-3 months.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-8 experienced artisans in Nayagaon (skilled workers earn ₹8,000-12,000/month + piece-rate incentives); lease a small shared kiln space (₹5,000-8,000/month) or negotiate access to existing communal kilns; establish quality control checkpoints for drying, firing, and finishing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME with DC Handicraft Angul; obtain IEC code from nearest bank for export; trademark brand name at IP office; obtain Artisan Mark/Handmade Certification from AIHDC; create design portfolio and social media presence on Instagram and Pinterest.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Pepperfry within 60 days; reach out to 20 interior design studios in Tier-1 cities with WhatsApp catalog; secure first B2B order from 1-2 corporate gifting platforms; create Instagram content series #MadeInAngul and partner with 3-5 lifestyle influencers (10k-50k followers) for sponsored posts.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 15-20 workers; invest in kiln upgrade for batch consistency; launch curated collections on Luxe and Artisan brands on major platforms; establish export channel via trade houses; target 40% revenue from D2C by month 12; attend international craft fairs (Craft Council India, Diwali Bazaar USA).
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹3L interest-free loan for artisans and craftspeople with 5-year repayment window
🏛
PMEGP
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for SC/ST/Women in urban, 25% general) with 7-year loan tenure for manufacturing unit setup
🏚
Odisha Handicraft Promotion Scheme
subsidy up to 25% on export shipments and international fair participation
🏚
SFURTI (Scheme for Promoting Cluster Development)
for Nayagaon cluster development with ₹50L+ funding for common facility center, kiln upgrades, and artisan training
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
