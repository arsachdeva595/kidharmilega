opportunity, Sri Sathya Sai (Puttaparthi) mein.

Sri Sathya Sai district has a strong handloom tradition inherited from Rayalaseema's weaving heritage. The district's proximity to major silk markets in Karnataka and Tamil Nadu, combined with skilled artisan communities, makes it ideal for premium silk saree production. Local government support through handloom clusters and DC Handloom ministry backing creates an enabling ecosystem.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Sri Sathya Sai (Puttaparthi) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Handloom clusters in Puttaparthi and Dhone Bazaar in the district. Study the Sri Sathya Sai Handloom Park infrastructure. Connect with DC Handloom officials for mentorship. Attend handloom expos at Nandyal and Anantapur. Research competitor sarees on Amazon, Meesho, and specialized platforms.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mulberry silk from Belgaum and Ramanagaram (Karnataka) suppliers at ₹250-350/kg. Purchase natural dyes from Sanganer, Rajasthan or local suppliers (₹15,000-25,000 for trial batch). Budget ₹80,000-150,000 for first 50-saree batch including zari, cotton borders, and sizing materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established weavers in Puttaparthi through district handloom societies or directly approach master weavers near Sri Sathya Sai Handloom Park. Alternatively, lease 5-10 looms at ₹500-800/loom/month from cooperative societies. Train on Gadwal and Banarasi weave techniques; production timeline is 10-15 days per saree.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro-enterprise online in 5 mins). Obtain IEC code from DGFT for export readiness. Register brand name with IP India for trademark protection. Obtain handloom mark certification from DC Handloom department to claim authenticity premium and export benefits.
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart for domestic reach. Create Instagram shop showcasing weaver stories and heritage narrative. List on global platforms (Etsy, Global Sources). Approach ethnic wear specialty stores in metros. Pitch to wedding planners and corporate gifting companies. Start with 5-10 sarees/month for 3 months.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand SKU with fusion sarees (silk-cotton blends). Build 20-30 dedicated loom partnerships. Establish own showroom in Bangalore or Hyderabad. Launch subscription/seasonal boxes. Invest ₹2-3 Lakhs in digital marketing targeting NRI and export markets. Target ₹50 Lakh revenue by 18 months.

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹3 Lakh concessional loan for handloom artisans and entrepreneurs at 5% interest
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakh subsidy (25-35%) for handloom enterprises in non-metro areas
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Handloom Weavers Cooperative Society Scheme
interest-free loans up to ₹5 Lakh and free skill training through government institutes
🏚
AP Textile Infrastructure Development Scheme
subsidized loom rental at government handloom parks and ₹1-2 Lakh per annum production subsidies for registered units
