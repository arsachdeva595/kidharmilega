Faridkot district (4,652 km²) sits in central Punjab, 260 km from Delhi, 120 km from Chandigarh, with direct NH-44 access. The district produces basmati on 180,000 hectares with irrigation from Sutlej River, placing it within the legal basmati GI zone. Kotkapura mandi (40 km away) handles 180,000 tonnes of basmati annually; Faridkot city has 8+ large rice mills, 12+ drying/storage facilities, and direct rail connections to export hubs at Ludhiana and Amritsar.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Faridkot ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kotkapura Grain Mandi on a Tuesday morning (peak trading day). Call 3-4 rice mill owners directly (numbers available from district administration ODOP office). Attend Faridkot Kisan Haat (held first Saturday monthly). Zero cost, delivers intel on current paddy prices, milling costs, farmer payment cycles.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source basmati paddy from Kotkapura mandi or directly from 200+ farmer groups in Faridkot/Bathinda belt. First batch: 5 metric tonnes of premium paddy = ₹2.2-2.5L at current rates (₹44,000-50,000/tonne). Arrange via a mandi sammelan (broker) who takes 2% commission. Paddy moisture, variety (1121/Super Basmati), and harvest month critical — October-November paddy yields best quality.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a rice mill unit (350-500 sqft) in Kotkapura rice cluster for ₹8,000-12,000/month + ₹80,000-1.5L machinery deposit (hulling, drying, polishing machines available on rent or EMI). Option B: Partner with an existing miller on revenue-share (they charge 15-18% of output as milling fee). Start with 2-3 metric tonnes/week processing. Total outlay: ₹2-3L for first 3 months of milling, packaging, testing (basmati purity certification).
4
Brand & Register
Register on Udyam (10 mins, free, online). Get GI compliance certificate from State Agricultural Department (₹3,000-5,000, 15-20 days) — essential for export. File IEC with DGFT Chandigarh if exporting (₹0 cost, 7 days online). Open a bank account under your business entity. Trademark registration optional but recommended (₹5,000-8,000). Obtain FSSAI license (₹500-2,000, 10-15 days) for D2C sales.
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Fresh/BigBasket (7-10 day approval, need FSSAI + packaged goods license). Place first cold-call to 5 hotel procurement teams in Delhi/Chandigarh (get lists from hotel association); expect 500kg-2tonne pilot orders at ₹180-220/kg wholesale. First month realistic: ₹25,000-40,000 from mix of 2-3 D2C orders (avg ₹4,000) + 1 wholesale buyer.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire a sales rep (₹12,000/month) to handle 50+ restaurant accounts. Invest in 2-tonne per day drying capacity (₹2.5-3L fixed asset). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹25L project, get ₹10L as 25% subsidy) to upgrade polishing line. Target 150+ institutional buyers. By ₹30L/month: lease a 2,000 sqft warehouse, add sorting/grading automation (₹5-8L), hire 4 staff, explore direct export via APEDA LEADS portal and attend trade shows in Dubai, Japan.
