Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Singrauli ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Singrauli's agricultural extension centers in Chitrangi and Raipura blocks; connect with farmer producer groups (FPGs) at local ATMA (Agricultural Technology Management Agency) office; study millet cultivation methods at Indira Gandhi Krishi Vishwavidyalaya, Raipur; attend meetings at Singrauli Krishak Sangh cooperative
2
Source Raw Materials
Source seeds from certified agencies under National Seed Mission via Madhya Pradesh State Seed Certification Agency; establish relationships with 15-20 farmer suppliers in Singrauli millet belt; first batch procurement cost ₹3-5 lakhs for 10-15 tons of cleaned Kodo-Kutki mix; negotiate with local traders in Raipura mandi for quality seeds
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 25-50 small-scale farmers under contract farming model with fixed procurement prices; set up a small cleaning, grading, and packaging unit (₹8-12 lakhs) in Singrauli town; hire trained staff for quality control; alternatively work with existing agri-cooperatives like Singrauli District Cooperative Federation for processing support
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as food processing micro/small enterprise; obtain IEC code from nearest customs office (Jabalpur/Raipur); register brand name with state commercial tax department; get FSSAI certification (₹25,000-40,000) for food safety; consider GI application after 2 years of documented production; obtain organic certification (₹30,000-50,000) from APEDA-recognized certifiers
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon FreshAmazon Pantry, BigBasket, and Jio Mart with 500g and 1kg premium packs at ₹120-150; partner with health food retailers like Nature's Basket and local organic chains in MP; approach bulk buyers via LinkedIn and IndiaMART; conduct product sampling at health expos in Indore, Bhopal, and Jabalpur; sell to food manufacturers via B2B portals
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to value-added products (millet flour, instant upma mix, millet snacks) with ₹15-20 lakh additional investment; secure export contracts via APEDA or Indian spice board; scale farmer network to 100+ growers; establish branded retail presence in 50+ modern trade outlets across MP and CG; explore institutional supply contracts with hospitals and wellness centers; build D2C channel with Instagram and Google Shopping ads
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

PM Kisan Samman Nidhi
₹6,000 annual income support for farmer partners in your supply chain
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
up to ₹25 lakhs loan for food processing unit setup with 35% subsidy for general category
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Mukhyamantri Kanya Vivah Yojana
supports farmer producer organizations; accessible for cooperative formation
🏚
MP State Organic Farming Scheme
₹4,000-15,000 per hectare subsidy for certified organic millet cultivation through partner farmers
