Gaya district (population 4.89M, literacy 59.78%) sits 200km south of the Bhagalpur handloom cluster which supplies linen fabrics and Bhagalpuri silk using traditional eco-friendly methods. The district has multiple active garment factories producing shirts, t-shirts, pants, and sarees for domestic retail and export markets. Raw materials (cotton, silk, wool, jute, linen, flax) are sourced from Bihar agricultural zones and Bhagalpur cluster, with buyer markets in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Kolkata) and exports to USA, UK, Germany, and Middle East.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Gaya ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bhagalpur handloom cluster (200km from Gaya) on a Friday or Saturday to observe linen fabric and Bhagalpuri silk production firsthand. Call 3-5 garment factories in Gaya city (search 'garment factory Gaya' on Google Maps) and speak directly with production managers about their current output, pricing, and artisan contacts. Attend Gaya weekly haat or local fabric markets to see what's selling and at what price. Cost: only travel and phone — zero rupees for learning.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton fabric from Gaya local cotton suppliers or regional textile wholesalers (₹80-120 per meter). Source Bhagalpuri silk and linen from Bhagalpur cluster suppliers — ₹150-250 per meter depending on quality. First batch: 500 meters mixed fabric = ₹50,000-80,000. Supplier area: Bhagalpur textile market or Gaya cotton merchants near Gaya Railway Station.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a small tailoring unit (500-1000 sq ft) in Gaya city near existing garment factories, ₹8,000-15,000/month + ₹30,000 setup (machines, tables). Option B: Partnership model with existing artisans or small tailoring units who work on piece-rate (₹40-80 per garment depending on complexity). Recommended: Start with Option B to avoid fixed costs. Monthly production cost for 200 units/month: ₹40,000-60,000 depending on fabric and complexity.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in, 5 min). Open 9-digit Udyam number. Consider GI registration under Ministry of Textiles for 'Gaya Handloom Garments' — costs ₹2,000-5,000 and takes 18-24 months. Apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT at icegate.indiaportals.com — free, 3-5 days processing. Register GST if turnover >₹20L (₹100 cost). Get FSSAI food unit code if adding textile care/printing (not required initially).
5
First Sale
First sale: List 50 SKUs (10 designs × 5 sizes) on Meesho or Flipkart Seller (free listing, 18-25% commission). Simultaneously, send bulk samples (5-10 pieces) to 20 local retail clothing stores in Gaya city with price list. Expected first order: 20-50 units within 4 weeks from wholesale channel. Expected first-month revenue: ₹25,000-50,000 gross (depending on ASP ₹400-800 per unit).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (250+ units/month): hire 1 junior operations manager (₹8,000-12,000/month) to oversee artisans and quality checks. Start attending Bangalore Fashion Week or Suratex (Surat textile expo) to source bulk wholesale buyers. Launch Instagram/YouTube product photography content (20-30 reels/month showing fabric, stitching, fit). Approach corporates for uniform contracts (bulk 100+ units). Export: hire customs broker (₹2,000-5,000 per shipment) and cold-email 30-50 USA/UK wholesalers with sample kits (₹5,000 per kit).
