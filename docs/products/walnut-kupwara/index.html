Walnut ki
opportunity, Kupwara mein.

Kupwara district in north Kashmir sits in the ideal agro-climatic belt for walnut trees, with 3.44 lakh hectares under horticulture cultivation (up from 1.31 lakh in 1980). The district is designated as an export zone specifically for walnuts by Government of India, with established supply chains connecting growers to processing hubs in Budgam and Srinagar. Raw material is abundant and year-round available from local walnut orchards, with buyer access spanning metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) and direct export channels to US, UK, and European markets.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kupwara ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kupwara district walnut markets (Trekking, Kupwara town haat, or Shopian orchards) and speak directly with 3-4 walnut growers and processors to understand harvest season (Sept-Nov), prices (₹600-900 per kg raw), and supply consistency. Zero cost — do this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact walnut growers in Kupwara tehsil (via local agriculture office referral) or approach established processors in Srinagar's Dal Gate walnut market. First-batch cost: ₹1.5-2.5L for 200-300kg raw walnuts. Supplier area: Kupwara district directly or Budgam processing zone for pre-processed kernels.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 300-500 sq ft space in Srinagar or Kupwara (₹8-15K/month) with water, electricity, and storage for roasting/drying equipment. Alternatively, partner with existing walnut processors in Budgam on revenue-share basis (40-60% margin split). Equipment cost: ₹2-4L for basic roasting oven, grading machines, and packaging line. Start with 50kg batches, scale to 300kg/month by month 6.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 15 mins — udyamregistration.gov.in). For export, apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via dgft.gov.in (₹0 fee, 3-5 days). File GI registration for 'Kupwara Walnut' through IP Office India if targeting premium positioning (₹5000-10000, 18-24 months approval). Get FSSAI license for food processing (₹1500-3000, 5-7 days). Organic certification from Jaivik Bharat scheme if applicable (subsidy available).
5
First Sale
First sales: Create seller account on Amazon (commission 10-15%) or Flipkart (8-12%). Start with 50 units of 500g packs at ₹1500/pack. Expected first month: 100-200 units = ₹1.5-3L revenue. Simultaneously, reach out to 5-10 Delhi/Mumbai premium grocery retailers with samples (₹20-30 per unit wholesale margin). Target institutional buyers via email campaigns to bakeries in metros.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: Hire 1-2 full-time processors and 1 marketing person (₹20-25K/month each). Invest in cold storage facility (₹3-5L). Launch export operations to 2-3 US/UK importers (₹25L+ monthly). Attend food trade shows (Gulfood, Anuga) quarterly. Expand to walnut oil and walnut powder SKUs. Build brand via food blogger partnerships and nutrition influencer collaborations.
