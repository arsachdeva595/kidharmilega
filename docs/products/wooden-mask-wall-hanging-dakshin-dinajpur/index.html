Wooden Mask & Wall Hanging ki
opportunity, Dakshin Dinajpur mein.

Dakshin Dinajpur has a centuries-old tradition of skilled wood carvers who specialize in theatrical masks for Gambhira and Pala performances. The district's abundant sal and other timber forests provide direct access to raw materials, and artisans have inherited techniques passed down through generations, making it a natural hub for this specialized handicraft.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Dakshin Dinajpur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Raiganj and Balurghat in Dakshin Dinajpur to meet established mask carvers; study Gambhira theatre at Raiganj Centre for Performing Arts; connect with National Handicraft Board office in Kolkata for GI documentation and artisan databases; attend craft fairs at Handloom House, Kolkata
2
Source Raw Materials
Source timber from government-licensed sal wood suppliers in Dinajpur region (₹8,000-12,000 per 50kg block); procure mulberry gum powder, bleaching powder, and lacquer from wholesale suppliers in Kolkata (₹15,000-20,000 for starter stock); negotiate bulk pricing for first 100-150 mask units
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 registered master carvers in Raiganj or Balurghat cluster; establish work-sharing agreements with profit-sharing model (50-60% to artisans); coordinate weekly production batches; implement quality control checkpoints after curing and sun-drying stages
4
Brand & Register
Register as Micro Enterprise under Udyam (online, free); apply for IEC code from DGFT for export readiness; register GI tag usage with DC Handicraft Ministry; create brand identity emphasizing 'handcrafted by Dakshin Dinajpur artisans'; get ISO 9001 or craft-specific certifications for B2B credibility
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Amazon Handmade with detailed photography of carving process; contact theatre departments of Delhi, Mumbai, Kolkata universities directly with samples; pitch to premium home décor retailers (Fab India, Good Earth, local galleries); use Instagram Reels showing mask-carving process; attend India International Craft Fair, New Delhi; approach export agencies like APEDA
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish direct export relationships with European ethnic art dealers; hire a logistics partner for bulk shipments; develop a 2-3 SKU collection (classic, contemporary, custom); train additional artisans to increase production capacity by 40-50%; launch a brand website with artisan storytelling; pursue bulk contracts with international theatre festivals and museum gift shops
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹3L interest-free loan + ₹15,000 cash grant for artisan entrepreneurs + free digital marketing support + skill training
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan with 35% subsidy for manufacturing units employing 10+ people; suitable for scaling production
🏚
West Bengal Handicraft Development Scheme
subsidy on raw material purchase (15-20%), skill training grants, export promotion incentives for GI-tagged products
🏚
Bengal Artisan Protection & Promotion Scheme
artisan wage subsidies, health insurance, market linkage support, cluster infrastructure development for Dinajpur region
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
