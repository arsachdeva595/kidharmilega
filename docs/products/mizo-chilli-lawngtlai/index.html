Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Lawngtlai ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Lawngtlai Agricultural Produce Marketing Committee (APMC) in Aizawl; connect with farmer co-operatives like Mizo Chilli Growers Association; study GI certification requirements at the Geographical Indications Registry office in Kolkata or online at ipindia.gov.in.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source authentic Mizo chili seeds from Lawngtlai seed banks (₹2,000-5,000 per kg for heirloom seeds) or established farmers; budget ₹30,000-50,000 for first 0.5-acre trial cultivation including soil preparation, seeds, and labor for 4-5 months.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with farmer co-operatives in Lawngtlai for bulk procurement post-harvest (August-October season); establish drying and grading facility locally or partner with existing APMC facilities; consider forming a producer company (1-2 members initially) to aggregate and quality-control produce.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain Importer Exporter Code (IEC) via DGFT portal for export readiness; register brand name with Ministry of Consumer Affairs, use GI label on packaging (authorization from GI holder required); obtain FSSAI registration for processed products.
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and regional platforms like Northeast Organic Producers; engage spice traders in Mumbai, Delhi, and Chennai via LinkedIn and Spice Board networks; approach 2-3 ethnic grocery chains; start with farmers' markets in tier-2 cities like Shillong and Guwahati; leverage Spice Board export promotion schemes.
6
Scale
Achieve 2-3 tons monthly production by month 8-10; establish co-packing partnerships for value-added products (chili powder, flakes, oils); apply for PMEGP/SFURTI grants to scale processing; build B2B relationships with 5+ international distributors; launch branded retail presence in 20+ cities across India.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan assistance with 5-10% subsidy for food processing and agricultural export ventures in Mizoram.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹15 lakh for cluster-based spice processing and value-addition in Lawngtlai region.
🏚
Mizoram Agro-Industries Development Policy
subsidized loans and marketing support for GI-tagged agricultural products; up to 4% interest subsidy for first 3 years.
🏚
Mizoram Export Promotion Fund
5-10% subsidy on international freight and certification costs for registered exporters of GI products.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
