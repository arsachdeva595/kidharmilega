Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Samba ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established mushroom farms in Samba district (contact Samba Agricultural Department). Study operations at Katra and Vijapur clusters. Enroll in ICAR-certified mushroom cultivation course (duration 3-6 weeks, cost ₹5,000-15,000). Research export regulations on APEDA website.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source spawn cultures from Indian Mushroom Society (Delhi) or ICAR institutes (cost ₹2,000-5,000 per batch). Procure hardwood sawdust from local timber suppliers in Samba (₹5-8 per kg). First batch substrate cost: ₹15,000-25,000. Establish supply agreement with local agri-waste collectors for rice straw.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with 100-200 sq ft indoor growing space (₹50,000-80,000 for basic shed with humidity control). Partner with experienced growers in Samba's mushroom clusters for first 2-3 cycles while building expertise. Adopt bag cultivation or bed system based on capital. Invest in FAD (Foreign Agricultural Development) approved equipment for export-grade production.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry MSME portal, free, 5 minutes online). Obtain FSSAI License (₹100-500, 10-15 days). Apply for IEC code via DGFT (Directorate General of Foreign Trade) for export eligibility. Register trademark with Samba District Chamber of Commerce. Obtain organic certification (₹20,000-30,000) from accredited body like APOF or Bionutri.
5
First Sale
Launch on Udaan.com and IndiaMART with COD + Net 15 terms. Supply to premium restaurant chains in Jammu and nearby metros (direct outreach). List on BigBasket, Blinkit premium sections. Create WhatsApp Business catalog for B2B queries. Register as APEDA-approved exporter and approach frozen food exporters. Target 50-100 kg first monthly production.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand cultivation area to 500-1000 sq ft with climate-controlled structures (₹3-5L additional investment). Establish processing unit for dried mushrooms and extracts (FSSAI approval needed). Hire 2-3 farm workers. Partner with food tech startups for recipe-based offerings. Explore contract farming with restaurants and organic retailers. Apply for APEDA export incentives after 1-2 years.
