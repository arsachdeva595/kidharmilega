Orthodox Tea (black) and Green Tea ki
opportunity, Udalguri mein.

Udalguri lies in the heart of Assam's tea belt with ideal altitudes (100-400m), monsoon climate, and acidic soil perfect for tea cultivation. The district has over 40+ tea gardens producing premium Orthodox varieties. Generations of farming expertise and existing tea processing infrastructure make it naturally suited for both black and green tea production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Udalguri ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Udalguri tea clusters around Bhergaon and Harisinga; study Tocklai Tea Research Institute (Jorhat, 150km away); attend Assam Tea Auction in Guwahati; network with 5+ established tea garden owners; understand Orthodox vs Green processing methods
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh leaves from contracted tea gardens in Udalguri at ₹120-180/kg (seasonal rates); negotiate 500kg first batch; find processing partner with Orthodox/Green facilities in Biswanath or Udalguri (₹25-35/kg processing cost); first batch cost: ₹60-90 lakhs for 500kg finished product
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established tea gardens for leaf supply (60-70% of cost); either lease processing unit from existing tea factory or co-process with established garden owner; hire 2-3 skilled tea processors; establish quality control lab for grading and moisture testing; target production: 100-150kg monthly initially
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (MSE portal); apply for IEC code from nearest Customs office (Guwahati); register brand name with IP India; obtain FSSAI registration for food business; get organic certification (NPOP) if positioning as organic; set up GST registration; design packaging with GI aspirational branding (even without current GI tag)
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart with ₹5-8k premium pricing per 250g pack; directly approach specialty tea retailers (The Tea Board, Darjeeling outlets); pitch to corporate gifting portals (GiftNow, CorporateGifts); contact boutique cafés in Bangalore, Delhi, Mumbai; join regional spice/tea export associations for buyer lists
6
Scale
After 6 months, apply for PMEGP subsidy for processing equipment expansion; invest in cold chain logistics (₹15-20L); launch premium subscription model (monthly tea box); expand to 500kg+ monthly production; establish direct partnerships with 10+ specialty retailers; begin export registration and certifications (ISO 22000, organic); build brand presence via Instagram/YouTube with tea tasting videos and origin stories
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy (25% of project cost for general, 35% for SC/ST) for tea processing equipment and working capital
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
support for tea clusters with infrastructure development and common processing facility grants
🏚
Assam Agro-Business and Rural Transformation (AgriTech) scheme
subsidized loans up to ₹50 lakhs for value-added tea processing enterprises
🏚
Assam Export Promotion Policy
5-10% incentive on FOB value for agricultural/tea exports and infrastructure support for export-oriented units
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
