Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Deoria ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Deoria town artisan cluster (Salempur road) — call 3 existing producers, ask for workshop visits. Free. Chat with them about raw material costs, artisan wages, and buyer channels. No money needed. Do this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton and silk from Varanasi wholesale markets (Loom Valley, Bansphatak) — ₹35K-50K for first 50 units. Get jute and embroidery thread from local Deoria cloth merchants. Visit 4-5 suppliers, compare quality and credit terms.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 400-500 sq ft workshop in Deoria town (₹4K-6K/month) and hire 2 artisans (₹12K-15K/month each). Option B: Partnership model — work with 5-8 home-based artisans, supply materials, pay ₹300-500 per unit. Start with 50 units in month 1. Total month 1 cost: ₹60K-80K.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online free within 10 minutes. Apply for GI registration if copying traditional Deoria patterns (₹4K-8K cost, 6-8 month timeline through Ministry of Commerce). Get IEC code from nearest DGFT office (Varanasi, 80 km) — free, 10-day process if exporting.
5
First Sale
List 20 SKUs on Amazon Handmade (free). Create Instagram shop with 15-20 product photos. Join 2-3 WhatsApp groups of home decor sellers and interior designers. First order likely from Etsy or Instagram — average ₹4K-8K. First month revenue target: ₹50K-80K from 8-12 orders.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month: hire 1 full-time designer (₹20K/month), 1 social media manager (₹15K/month), expand to 12-15 home artisans. Apply for SFURTI cluster subsidy (₹25-45L over 3 years) to grow supplier network. Exhibit at India Art Fair (Delhi), iWeekend (Mumbai), Aashiyana (Bangalore). Partner with 3-5 home decor retailers nationwide.
