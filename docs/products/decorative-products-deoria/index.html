Deoria has a deep-rooted tradition of textile and craft work with a large population of skilled artisans trained in traditional handicraft techniques. The district's low cost of living and abundant labor force make it ideal for scaling decorative product manufacturing. Proximity to major craft clusters in Eastern UP and established supply chains for raw materials support rapid business growth.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Deoria ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Deoria's major craft clusters around Salempur and Barhaj blocks; connect with DC Handicraft office in Deoria district HQ; attend craft fairs at Lucknow Handicraft Fair; study competitor brands on Etsy and Amazon Handmade; interview 10-15 local artisans on production costs and timeline.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton and silk fabrics from Varanasi textile markets (wholesale cost ₹80-150/meter); procure embellishments from Delhi's craft bazaars (Chandni Chowk) or online from Alibaba; establish relationship with 2-3 local artisan cooperatives in Deoria for bulk orders at ₹2-8 per unit labor depending on complexity; budget ₹40-50K for first batch of 200-300 pieces.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing artisan groups or SHGs in Salempur/Barhaj blocks through DC Handicraft referrals; negotiate fixed piece-rate pricing (₹150-400 per finished item depending on design complexity); provide designs, raw materials, and quality specs; start with 100-200 units/month for first 3 months; scale to 500-800/month by month 6.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online via udyamregistration.gov.in (free, 10 minutes); apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via ICEGATE or customs office for export readiness; register trademark with IP India for brand name; obtain GST registration; create brand guidelines document emphasizing 'authentic handmade' and artisan-fair-trade positioning.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade and Etsy with 50+ high-quality photos per product (invest ₹5-10K in professional photography); run Google Shopping ads targeting 'handmade wall décor India' (budget ₹500/day); approach home décor retailers in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) with 30% wholesale discount; email 100 interior designers on LinkedIn; start Instagram content (3x/week behind-the-scenes artisan stories); seek initial 20-30 orders in month 1.
6
Scale
Expand product range to 15-20 SKUs based on customer feedback; hire a part-time designer or train 1 artisan as design lead; establish distribution in 3-5 major metro cities via retail partnerships; launch D2C website with Shopify (invest ₹20K/year); scale Instagram to 10K followers with influencer collaborations (micro-influencers in home décor niche); explore B2B corporate gifting via GiftedIndia or corporate procurement platforms; target ₹12-18L annual revenue by month 12.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
interest-free loans up to ₹15L for artisans and craft businesses, plus 8% state subsidy; direct benefit if partnering with registered artisans.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
grants up to ₹25L (35% subsidy in general category) for microenterprises in handicraft; applicable if registering as manufacturing unit.
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Handicraft Development Scheme
30% subsidy on machinery/equipment and interest-free loans up to ₹10L for registered handicraft businesses; administered via DC Handicraft.
🏚
UP SC/ST Handicraft Development Scheme
additional subsidies and skill training grants for SC/ST artisans; relevant if working with artisan SHGs in Deoria.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
