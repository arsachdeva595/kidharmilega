Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jalandhar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jalandhar's Paseli and Nakodar clusters to spend 2-3 weeks observing production processes, meet manufacturers, and understand supply chain. Connect with Jalandhar Sports Goods Manufacturers Association (JSGMA). Attend national sports exhibitions at Delhi or Mumbai.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Kashmir willow from Srinagar timber markets (₹8-12/kg), synthetic materials from Ludhiana suppliers, rubber and cork from Mumbai distributors. First batch of 500 cricket bats: ₹2-3 Lakh. Establish direct relationships with 3-4 trusted suppliers in Jalandhar.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing contract manufacturers in Paseli cluster (Jalandhar) for initial 6 months to avoid capital expenditure. Typical cost: ₹200-400 per unit depending on product complexity. Alternatively, lease a 2000 sq ft workshop in Nakodar (₹15-20k/month) and hire 3-4 skilled workers (₹10-15k/month each).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification) within 2 days online, apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Chandigarh (5 days), open current account with bank for B2B. File trademark for brand name. Get ISO 9001 certification (optional but helps exports) within 2-3 months, cost ₹50k.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Seller Central and Flipkart with 15-20 product listings within 30 days. Simultaneously contact 5-10 school procurement officers directly via WhatsApp/email with bulk pricing. Reach out to sports academy procurement leads via LinkedIn. Launch Instagram/YouTube content showing manufacturing quality and usage.
6
Scale
After 6 months, secure PAN India retail distribution through sports chains and local dealers. Invest in ED-M export certifications, attend international sports trade shows (ISPO Munich, ISG), build 2-3 international distributor partnerships. Scale workshop capacity to 5000 units/month by month 12.
