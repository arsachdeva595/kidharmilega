Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Damoh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Damoh APMC yard and Gandhisagar mandis to understand market pricing, connect with 10+ existing gram traders, attend Maharashtra Rajya Cooperative spinning mill meetings; study competitive pricing on IndiaFarming.com and trader networks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 10-20 quintals from established Damoh farmers through Krishak Samitis in Niwas or Tendukheda blocks; negotiate ₹3,500-4,200/quintal for raw gram; first batch cost approximately ₹4-5 lakhs including cleaning and grading.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established dal mills in Indore (50km away) for processing and cleaning under contract or invest ₹20-30 lakhs in small dal cleaning unit; employ 2-3 workers for sorting, packaging, and quality control.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online (udyamregistration.gov.in) as sole proprietor or MSME; obtain Food Safety License (FSSAI) from Damoh district office; register IEC (import-export code) via DGFT portal; trademark your brand name with IP office.
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket and Amazon Fresh via seller portal with minimum 5-quintal stock; pitch to local spice traders in Indore's Kumharwada market; partner with 5-10 retail health stores in Jabalpur and Bhopal for pilot sales.
6
Scale
Expand to Flipkart and direct e-commerce website by month 6; build B2B relationships with food companies (ITC, Britannia, Nestlé supplier networks); increase sourcing to 100+ quintals/month; explore organic certification (NPOP) for 20-30% premium pricing.

up to ₹10L unsecured loan for MSME production and working capital at 8-9% interest.

MADHYA PRADESH AGRICULTURAL COMPETITIVENESS SCHEME
50% subsidy on food processing equipment and cold chain infrastructure for agricultural produce.

MUKHYAMANTRI KISAN SAMRIDDHI YOJANA
direct income support ₹4,000/acre + input subsidies for gram farmers in Damoh district.
