Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Chitradurga ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Molakalmuru weaving cluster (30km from Chitradurga town) on a Tuesday or Thursday morning — meet 3-4 weaver families, understand turnaround time and cost structure (₹0 cost, 3-hour trip). Call Ajmera Fashion office in Molakalmuru or local ODOP coordinator at Chitradurga Collector Office to understand export logistics and buyer connections. Attend monthly Molakalmuru Handloom Haat (if active) or register on ODOP trade platform to see live pricing and order volumes.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source first batch of 5-10 sarees directly from weaver groups in Molakalmuru cluster — negotiate ₹4,000-6,000 per saree as first-time order (total ₹25,000-50,000). Pay 50% upfront, 50% on delivery. Lead time 10-15 days. Suppliers: Individual weavers or Molakalmuru Weavers Cooperative (if registered). Avoid middlemen in first 2 orders to learn direct cost.
3
Produce / Partner
Begin with artisan partnership model (no factory rent required). Identify 5-8 trusted weavers in Molakalmuru, commit ₹50,000-100,000 as standing orders over 3 months. This locks in production capacity without capex. If scaling beyond 50 sarees/month, rent 500 sqft storage + packaging unit near Molakalmuru bazaar (₹3,000-5,000/month). Alternatively, partner with a registered weaver society for bulk orders with quality guarantees.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online in 10 minutes — MSME portal). Apply for GI certification support under Dept of Commerce Handloom scheme (₹50,000-100,000 govt cost for trademark application on Molakalmuru Saree — typically subsidized 80%). If exporting, obtain IEC from Customs within 2 weeks (₹500 cost via FIRC agent). Register trademark for brand name at IP office (₹5,000-8,000). Timeline: 3-4 weeks for full compliance.
5
First Sale
First sale via Etsy or Amazon Handmade India — list 3 saree designs with 10+ product photos by day 30. Expected first order: 1-2 sarees at ₹18,000-22,000 retail (60% margin after cost). First month conservative target: ₹30,000-50,000 revenue from 2-3 D2C orders + 1 wholesale inquiry. Simultaneously email 10 ethnic wear retailers in Bangalore/Mumbai with wholesale price list (₹9,000 per saree) and 1-2 sample sarees.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time operations person to manage weaver coordination and order scheduling (₹15,000/month salary). Secure second and third weaver groups to increase capacity to 150+ sarees/month. Attend 2-3 B2B textiles exhibitions yearly (Vibrant Gujarat, India International Weaving Expo) to land bulk export orders. Invest ₹50,000 in product photography and content creation for Instagram/Pinterest to scale D2C channel. Open small showroom in Bangalore (₹100,000 initial rent + decor) to attract walk-in wedding customers.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 interest-free loan for handloom artisans and entrepreneurs, 18-month tenure, no collateral required
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy for handloom clusters with capex support, working capital, and common facility centers
🏚
Karnataka Handloom Development Scheme
25% subsidy on purchase of improved looms and weaving equipment, max ₹50,000 per beneficiary
🏚
Karnataka ODOP Export Support Scheme
10% subsidy on export documentation, certification, trade show participation costs (max ₹1 lakh per financial year)
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
