Ramgarh district has a centuries-old tradition of silver jewelry making passed down through generations of skilled artisans. The district's access to locally mined high-quality minerals and established artisan clusters provide both raw material advantage and trained labor. The cultural heritage and existing market ecosystem make it naturally suited for scaling silver jewelry production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Ramgarh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ramgarh's artisan clusters around Porahat and Ramgarh town; connect with 2-3 established jewelry makers; study the Ramgarh Crafts Cooperative Society; research Instagram and Pinterest for trending silver jewelry designs; attend Vibrant Jharkhand Summit or craft fairs.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw silver (99.9% purity) from Mumbai's Zaveri Bazaar or local suppliers in Ramgarh; connect with local stone suppliers in Dhanbad; first batch of 20-30 pieces requires approximately ₹80,000-120,000 in raw materials and semi-precious stones.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 artisans in Ramgarh as piece-rate workers (₹500-1500 per piece depending on complexity); establish a small workshop (500 sq ft rental ₹8,000-12,000/month); procure basic tools (polishing wheels, casting molds, heating equipment) costing ₹150,000-200,000; ensure quality checks and design innovation workshops monthly.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (Micro-enterprise); obtain IEC code from DGFT for export; register trademark for brand name; obtain FSSAI if gifting food items; create GST registration; design brand identity and packaging emphasizing Ramgarh's heritage.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram shop and Shopify store with high-quality photography; list on Amazon Handmade India; approach 10-15 ethnic boutiques in metros for wholesale; run Instagram Reels showing artisan process; collaborate with micro-influencers (100K-500K followers) in fashion/lifestyle; attend 2-3 craft exhibitions in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore in first 6 months.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 15-20 craftspeople; introduce 2-3 new design collections per quarter; establish B2B relationships with 50+ retailers; launch corporate gifting line; explore international e-commerce platforms (Etsy, Amazon Global); invest in video content showcasing heritage; plan brand pop-ups in 3-4 metro cities; consider wholesale to international distributors by month 12.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15,000 advance for tooling/equipment and 5% interest subvention on bank loans up to ₹1 lakh for artisans
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% for general category, 45% for SC/ST/OBC) for manufacturing units
🏚
Jharkhand Craft Promotion Scheme
10-15% subsidy on equipment, raw material purchases, and export promotion for registered craft enterprises
🏚
Jharkhand State MSME Advancement Scheme
₹5,000-50,000 subsidy for capacity building, brand development, and trade fair participation
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
