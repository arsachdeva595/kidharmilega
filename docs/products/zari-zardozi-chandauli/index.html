opportunity, Chandauli mein.

Chandauli district in eastern Uttar Pradesh is home to the Bareilly Zari cluster with 60 active production units specializing in hand-embroidered metallic thread work on silk, velvet, and brocade. Raw materials (gold/silver threads, gota ribbons, premium fabrics) are sourced within 100km from local suppliers and regional textile centers. Major buyers are concentrated in Delhi (bridal retailers), Mumbai (luxury wear), Lucknow (traditional wear), and increasingly international markets across North America and Gulf countries seeking authentic Mughal embroidery.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Chandauli ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bareilly Zari cluster (30km from Chandauli town) and spend 2-3 days walking unit-to-unit, speaking with 5-10 artisans about their pain points. Attend the nearest Chandauli haat or ODOP govt event (call Chandauli DC office at district level for schedule). Call 3-5 raw material suppliers in Bareilly and ask for wholesale pricing on gold/silver threads and gota. Register free on Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in). Zero cost, doable in 1 week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source first batch (₹40,000-₹80,000) from established Bareilly suppliers: contact registered units for bulk thread orders (50+ meters gold threads, 30+ meters silver, 20+ meters gota). Fabric (silk/velvet/brocade) sourced from Varanasi textile market (45km away, cheaper). Negotiate 15-30 day payment terms. First 5-10 pieces for testing; use a trusted artisan referral from existing unit owners.
3
Produce / Partner
Two paths: (a) Rent 400-600 sqft embroidery unit in Bareilly cluster at ₹4,000-₹8,000/month, hire 2-4 skilled artisans at ₹8,000-₹15,000/month each + materials, or (b) Partner directly with 1-2 existing artisans on revenue-share (40% you, 60% artisan) with you managing supply and sales. Path (b) costs ₹0 upfront but slower; Path (a) needs ₹150,000-₹250,000 for setup (unit rent 3 months advance, equipment, first 10 designs, 2-3 artisan salaries).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 15 mins). Apply for GI registration at Ministry of Commerce (non-GI status currently — explore Zari as a collective GI claim with DC office; ₹20,000-₹50,000 cost, 6-12 month process). If planning exports, register IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at DGFT website free. Get FSSAI if offering embroidered food-event garments. Total time: 2-3 months. Explore SFURTI subsidy from DC Handicraft office for equipment grants.
5
First Sale
First sale strategy: List 15-20 designs on Meesho (seller onboarding free, 10-15% commission). Simultaneously reach out to 10 bridal wear retailers in Delhi/Mumbai via email + WhatsApp with ₹5,000-₹15,000 sample pieces (offer 20% wholesale discount on 5+ pieces). Target first order: 3-5 customized sarees, expected first-month revenue ₹30,000-₹80,000 (conservative). Use TikTok/Reels showing live embroidery process (high engagement for Zari content).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 dedicated customer service person (₹8,000/month), shift to in-house production (move to 1000+ sqft unit, ₹15,000-₹20,000/month), onboard 5-8 artisans. Exhibit at Jaipur Jewellery Show, Delhi Handloom Expo, and IITF (annual events). Build WhatsApp bulk order system. Partner with 2-3 wedding planning platforms. Apply for PMEGP/MUDRA for ₹5-25L expansion loan. Explore export aggregator partnerships (e.g., Craft World, Exim).
