Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Ahmedabad ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Spend 1-2 weeks at Law Garden (wholesale fabric market), Mithakhali Six Roads (retail hub), and AAPI (Ahmedabad Association of Apparel Industry) office. Interview 5-10 established manufacturers. Visit Colaba Causeway Mumbai for trend research. Cost: ₹10,000-15,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact fabric suppliers in Law Garden, Rakhial (bulk cotton/synthetic), and Sarangpur (dyes/finishes). Place first batch order: 500 units minimum @ ₹150-300/unit depending on design. Budget ₹75,000-150,000 for initial fabric + findings. Negotiate 15-30 day payment terms.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 1-2 experienced master-tailors in Rakhial or Narol cluster (₹5-15/piece labor cost). Alternatively, rent 1,000 sq ft space in GIDC Naroda (₹8,000-12,000/month) and hire 5-8 stitching staff. Invest in 3-4 industrial sewing machines (₹40,000-60,000 each). Start with one simple design, scale to 5-8 SKUs by month 3.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise classification) — online via udyamregistration.gov.in (free). Obtain Importer-Exporter Code (IEC) from DGFT if targeting exports (₹5,000-10,000 via customs broker). Register trademark for brand name (₹8,000-12,000 via IP lawyer). Open GSTIN account (online, free). Design simple logo and packaging (₹5,000-10,000).
5
First Sale
Launch on Flipkart & Myntra using seller registration (0% upfront fee, take 15-25% commission). Create Instagram & TikTok shop with 10-15 high-quality product photos/reels (cost: ₹10,000-20,000 for photography). Reach out to 20-30 retail boutiques in Ahmedabad and Bangalore via WhatsApp with wholesale pricing (MOQ: 50 units). Attend GCCI (Gujarat Chamber of Commerce) bazaar events. Allocate ₹30,000 for first-month paid ads (Instagram/Google).
6
Scale
By month 6-8, expand to 3-4 popular SKUs based on sales data. Increase production capacity to 2,000 units/month. Hire a part-time logistics/ops person (₹15,000-20,000). Apply for PMEGP or state subsidy (if eligible) to fund second production line. Partner with 2-3 export aggregators (Aditya Birla Fashion, Welspun) for bulk orders. Launch wholesale B2B portal or Shopify store for B2B buyers. Target ₹20 Lakhs revenue by month 12.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakhs loan for MSMEs, 35% subsidy for SC/ST/women, 25% for others
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 direct credit for artisans + free skill training in traditional textile crafts
🏚
Gujarat Textile Policy 2019
5-10% capital subsidy on plant & machinery for garment units, up to ₹50 Lakhs per unit
🏚
GSTPIN (Gujarat State Trade & Industry Promotion Initiative)
reduced stamp duty on rented space in GIDC clusters, 50% subsidy on brand building & export promotion for 3 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Marketing

Ye marketing ideas try karna mat bhulna

  • Partner with 3-5 micro-influencers (10K-100K followers) from Ahmedabad and Mumbai focused on sustainable fashion. Provide free samples, ask for unboxing + styling videos on Instagram/TikTok. Cost: ₹2,000-5,000 per influencer. Target 15-20% conversion.
  • Run performance ads on Pinterest & Instagram targeting 'sustainable fashion' and 'ethnic wear' keywords in India (ages 22-35, urban). Budget: ₹1,000-2,000/day. Track via UTM codes and aim for 20-30% ROI.
  • Host 'Design-Your-Own' Instagram Live sessions where customers suggest fabric combos and designs. Offer 10% off first order codes. Partner with 2-3 local cafes in Ahmedabad for pop-up retail events. Cost: ₹5,000-10,000 per pop-up, expect ₹20,000-50,000 revenue per event.
