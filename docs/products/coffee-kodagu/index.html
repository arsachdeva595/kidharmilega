Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kodagu ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kodagu Coffee Association in Madikeri and Virajpet coffee clusters. Spend 2 weeks shadowing producers in Nishani Kodagu cooperative. Study Kodagu Coffee Board export standards. Cost: ₹15,000-25,000
2
Source Raw Materials
Connect with primary producers via Kodagu Coffee Board or CCQCS (Coffee Curing Quality Control System) in Kodagu. Source 500kg dried parchment coffee from established farmers at ₹300-350/kg. First batch cost: ₹165,000-175,000 including transport to processing unit
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with licensed coffee processing units in Virajpet or Madikeri (dry/wet processing, roasting, grinding). Contract processing at ₹60-80/kg. Alternatively, invest ₹800,000-1,200,000 in own roasting equipment (Loring, Probat machines) and processing machinery. Aim for 50kg/day production capacity initially
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) in agriculture processing category. Obtain IEC code from nearest Export Facilitation Centre. Apply for Kodagu Coffee GI protection usage rights. Register trademark on IPO website. Get FSSAI license for processing/packaging unit. Total regulatory cost: ₹45,000-75,000
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Specialty Foods, BlinkIt, Zepto with 250g packs at ₹599-799. Contact specialty coffee retailers in Bangalore and metros. Create Instagram/Facebook presence with farm-story content. Reach out to corporate gifting platforms (GiftYourChoice, Ferns N Petals). Target B2B via LinkedIn with international sample packs
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 5 specialty coffee subscription platforms. Launch instant coffee variant. Partner with 15-20 premium cafes across Tier-1 cities. Pursue ISO 9001 certification for export compliance. Build direct export relationships (target €8-12/kg in EU). Invest ₹400,000 in digital marketing and influencer partnerships
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Formalisation of Micro food processing Enterprises (PMFME)
up to ₹10L subsidy for processing unit setup
🏛
Production-Linked Incentive Scheme (PLI) for Food Processing
up to 4-6% incentive on incremental sales for certified units
🏚
Karnataka Agro-Export Policy
5% subsidy on export freight charges, export credit guarantee coverage
🏚
KIADB Agri-Processing Parks
subsidized land in Kodagu clusters for food processing units, infrastructure support
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
