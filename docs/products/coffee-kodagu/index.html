opportunity, Kodagu mein.

Kodagu district (4,102 sq km) sits in the Western Ghats of southwestern Karnataka at 900-1,600m elevation with monsoon-fed volcanic soil — the second-largest coffee region in India after Chikkamagaluru. Robusta and Arabica are abundantly grown in the hills near Madikeri with direct supply relationships to Naandi Foundation, AP Girijan Cooperative Corporation, and Mangalore-based exporters. Annual production spans 35,000-40,000 tonnes sold to specialty roasters in Germany, Italy, Belgium, USA, and Japan, plus domestic premium chains and online D2C platforms.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kodagu ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Madikeri Coffee Farmers' Cooperative (free tour), call 3-5 coffee suppliers listed on Kodagu NIC portal, attend Madikeri weekly haat market Wednesday morning, register Udyam online (free, 10 mins) — complete this within 5 days
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact Naandi Foundation's Kodagu office or AP Girijan Cooperative Corporation for first batch of 500kg green beans at ₹200-250/kg (₹1-1.25L cost). Alternatively, visit Madikeri Coffee Growers' Union to source direct from member farmers. First batch: ₹1-1.5L for 500-700kg green coffee beans, pickup from farms in Madikeri or Virajpet tehsils
3
Produce / Partner
Lease a 500-1000 sq ft roasting unit in Madikeri or hire established roasters (Coorg Roasters, Kodagu Coffee Works) on contract basis at ₹20-30 per kg roasted output. Initial investment: ₹3-5L for own unit (roaster machine ₹2L + packaging equipment ₹1-2L) OR ₹0 upfront for contract roasting model. Timeline: 2-3 weeks to set up or finalize roaster contract
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for GI certification under Coffee Board of India (₹10-15K, 6-8 weeks). Obtain FSSAI registration (₹10-15K, 4-6 weeks). If exporting, file IEC application at Mangalore Customs (₹0, 2-3 weeks online). Total compliance cost: ₹20-30K, 8-10 weeks timeline
5
First Sale
First sale: list on Amazon Specialty Foods or Flipkart under 'Premium Coffee' category (upload 3-4 product images, write 50-word description, set ₹699/250g). Expect first month: 20-40 units = ₹14-28K revenue. Parallel: contact 3-5 specialty cafés in Bangalore via email with sample packs + bulk pricing (₹400/kg), target ₹2-3K first wholesale order within 30 days
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 operations manager for farmer relations, 1 packer, 1 social media marketer. Allocate budget to booth at India Coffee Fest (₹2-3L/year), LinkedIn B2B content, guest spots on specialty coffee podcasts. Reach out to importers via Coffee Board of India's export directory. Scale production to 2,000-3,000kg/month by month 8-10

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
25% subsidy up to ₹10L for manufacturing unit setup, repayment over 7 years
🏛
Coffee Board of India Export Subsidy
₹5-10/kg export incentive + co-branded trade show booth assistance for APEDA-registered exporters
🏚
Karnataka ODOP Scheme
₹5-15L interest-free loan for registered ODOP units with GI tag, 3-year repayment grace period
🏚
Karnataka Agri-Export Subsidy
5% subsidy on export logistics (packaging, freight, certification) up to ₹2L/year for registered exporters
