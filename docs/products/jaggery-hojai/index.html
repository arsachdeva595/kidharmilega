The district's climate, soil, and water resources are ideally suited for high-quality sugarcane production. Local artisans possess specialized knowledge of traditional gur-making methods that yield superior taste and nutritional content.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Hojai ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Hojai's Barghat and Lumding clusters where 60+ traditional jaggery units operate. Meet established producers at Hojai Market Mandi and understand seasonal cycles (Oct-Feb peak). Study competing brands at Assam State Handicraft Emporium in Guwahati.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh sugarcane directly from Hojai farmers during Oct-Feb season at ₹250-350 per quintal (20-30 quintals ≈ ₹75,000 for first batch). Establish relationships with 3-4 farmer groups to ensure consistent supply. Budget ₹2,000-3,000 for limestone and fuel (bagasse).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with experienced jaggery artisans in Barghat village who operate traditional 'gur ghani' units. Negotiate revenue-sharing (typically 15-20% of output to partner). Alternatively, lease a small 2-acre gur production unit for ₹1,00,000/season or invest ₹5,00,000-8,00,000 for own equipment (vacuum pan, crusher, molds).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise) online on udyamregistration.gov.in (free, 15 mins). Obtain IEC code from DGFT portal for exports. Apply for FSSAI food safety license (₹500-2,000). File trademark for brand name at ipindia.gov.in (₹4,500-10,000). Create GI-inspired packaging emphasizing 'Hojai heritage' and organic practices.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh & Flipkart Food with 'organic jaggery' positioning; target ₹99-149 per 500g. Build email list via organic Google Ads (₹5,000/month) and Instagram (food blogger partnerships in metros). Partner with BigBasket, Nature's Basket for pan-India reach. Approach wholesale buyers at Nandini Market (Bangalore), Safdarjung Mandi (Delhi) in Dec-Jan.
6
Scale
Post 6 months, develop 3-4 value-added SKUs (powdered jaggery, jaggery + turmeric blocks, organic certification). Hire a sales team (₹2-3 people) for institutional B2B. Explore APEDA-backed export partnerships to Nepal, Bangladesh, and Singapore. Build production capacity to 50+ tons/season via multiple artisan partnerships.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for micro/small food processing units; approx. 35% subsidy available for Hojai entrepreneurs.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster-level support including equipment subsidy, training, and marketing; Hojai jaggery cluster eligible for ₹5-10 cr support.
🏚
Assam APIDC Food Processing Scheme
25-40% capital subsidy for food processing enterprises plus working capital loans at concessional rates.
🏚
Assam Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority (AAPFEDA)
export promotion, packaging subsidy, and international buyer linkage for ODOP products.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
