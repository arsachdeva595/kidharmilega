Kani Shawls ki
opportunity, Budgam mein.

Budgam has been the epicentre of Kani shawl weaving for over 400 years, with artisan communities in villages like Budgam, Charar-e-Sharief, and Sadura perfecting the technique across generations. The district's access to fine Merino wool, traditional dye-works, and master weavers makes it the only authentic source for genuine Kani shawls. The GI tag recognition reinforces Budgam's monopoly on this rare, high-value craft.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Budgam ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Budgam handloom clusters (Charar-e-Sharief village, Budgam town weaving units). Spend 2–3 weeks observing artisans, learning the 3–5 year production cycle. Meet master weavers like those registered with Budgam Handloom Cooperative Society. Study GI documentation and competitor pricing on luxury platforms.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw Merino wool from Kashmir Sheep Husbandry Board (₹400–600/kg), natural dyes from traditional dyers in Srinagar (₹5K–15K per batch for premium stock), silk/cotton from suppliers in Srinagar Old City (₹20K–50K initial order). First batch procurement: ₹100K–300K for materials to support 2–3 looms for 6 months.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with master weavers in Budgam (no middlemen) via written artisan contracts ensuring GI compliance and exclusivity. Employ 2–4 experienced weavers initially (₹15K–25K/month each). Use traditional wooden looms; outsource only to certified Kani artisans. Register partnership under Ministry of Handloom framework.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small Enterprise). Apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office (Srinagar) for export. Register trademark for brand name with IP India. Obtain GI-compliant certification from Budgam Handloom Cooperative/Government. Create heritage narrative & certificates of authenticity for each shawl.
5
First Sale
Launch branded Shopify/WooCommerce site with luxury aesthetics and storytelling. Sell first pieces via Instagram/Facebook (luxury audience targeting). Submit to Etsy, 1stDibs, Vestiaire Collective. Pitch to high-end wedding planners in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore. Email outreach to international boutiques (luxury retailers in London, NYC, Paris). PR strategy: luxury fashion press, heritage media.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 8–10 looms. Launch WhatsApp/direct consulting for custom orders. Build B2B relationships with luxury hotel chains and palace resorts. Increase international marketing budget. Consider pop-up stores in Delhi, Mumbai, London. Develop a waiting-list model (1–2 year lead time = premium positioning). Explore museum partnerships and heritage tourism tie-ups.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
Up to ₹15,000 toolkits + ₹5 Lakh credit at 0% for registered artisans and craftspeople
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
Up to ₹40 Lakh grant for cluster-level infrastructure and artisan skill upgradation in handlooms
🏚
Jammu & Kashmir Handicrafts Development Policy 2021
Market development fund, export subsidies, and capacity-building grants for artisans and entrepreneurs
🏚
Jammu & Kashmir Artisan Production Cluster Scheme
Infrastructure support, working capital loans at concessional rates for handloom businesses in ODOP districts
