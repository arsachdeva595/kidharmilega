Kani Shawls ki
opportunity, Budgam mein.

Budgam district in central Kashmir Valley produces Kani shawls in Arwah village, Beerwah tehsil, where artisan families have woven since the 14th century using pashmina from local Changra goats. The cluster contains approximately 120-150 active weaver families producing 15-20 shawls per artisan annually, with each piece selling for ₹1-10 lakhs in domestic and export markets. Raw material (premium pashmina wool, 12–15 microns) is sourced locally; finished goods reach luxury retailers in Delhi, Mumbai, Europe, and international collectors via specialized textile galleries.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Budgam ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Arwah village in Beerwah tehsil (50 km from Srinagar) this month — meet Abdul Rashid Sofi or Farooq Ahmed Reshi at their loom, observe a day of weaving, ask questions on cost structure. Call Budgam District Industries Centre (free consultation). Register free on Udyam portal today. Attend a local craft haat or exhibition.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium pashmina (12–15 micron wool) directly from Changra goat wool traders in Srinagar's Lal Chowk wool market (₹1200-1800 per kg). First batch: 2-3 kg for one shawl prototype costs ₹2400-5400. Alternatively, buy semi-processed pashm from established Budgam suppliers at ₹2000-2500/kg. Lead time: 1-2 weeks.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Recommended): Partner with 1-2 experienced artisans in Arwah village — rent shared loom space at ₹3000-5000/month, provide raw material and design briefs, pay artisan ₹15000-25000 per completed shawl (8-month production cycle). Option B: Rent your own loom unit (1000 sq ft) in Budgam city at ₹8000-12000/month, hire trained weaver on ₹8000/month salary. Initial working capital for first shawl (8 months): ₹40000-60000 (materials + labor advance).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 5 mins — MSME classification). File GI mark usage application with Budgam District Industries (₹5000-10000, 2-4 weeks approval — you can use GI tag on marketing once approved). If exporting: obtain IEC from DGFT (₹10000, 2-3 weeks via bank/chamber). Register brand name with trademark office (₹9000-12000, 4-6 months). Total: ₹24000-42000, timeline 6-8 weeks for operational setup.
5
First Sale
First sale: List 2-3 shawls on Sarangi (handcrafted luxury platform, 20% commission, 15-30 day turnaround to first order). Simultaneously, email photography + catalogs to Dilli Haat, Cottage Emporium, heritage boutiques in Delhi NCR. Expect first order within 4-8 weeks at ₹1.2-1.5L per shawl (wholesale). First-month revenue potential: ₹1.5-3L (1-2 shawls if direct sales, 0-1 if waiting for wholesale).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (8-10 shawls/month): hire a supply chain manager to coordinate multiple artisans, allocate ₹2L/month for raw material buffer stock, participate in 2-3 international craft fairs (Salon International in Paris, Tendence Frankfurt) costing ₹3-5L per fair. Build wholesale relationships with 5-7 boutiques. Invest ₹1-2L in professional photography and lookbook. Hire part-time social media manager (₹15000/month). Target: export 30-40% of revenue.
