Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Lalitpur ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Lalitpur's primary weaving clusters around Mehrauli and Bundelkhand areas; spend 2-3 weeks apprenticing with master weavers; understand zari sourcing from Varanasi suppliers; visit Chanderi and Maheshwar for competitive product analysis.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw silk from Bangalore Silk Market or Karnataka suppliers (₹1200-2000/kg); procure premium zari from Varanasi's Madiyahun area (₹400-800/spool); cotton base from Indore textile markets; first batch of 10 sarees requires ₹60,000-100,000 investment for raw materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 master weavers in Lalitpur (₹800-1200 per saree production cost); establish a small production hub with 2-3 handlooms; implement quality control checks at each stage; create artisan agreements for consistent supply and pricing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME under handloom category; apply for IEC code for exports; obtain GI certification application (even though current status is No); register trademark with IP office; obtain Handloom Mark certification from DC Handloom ministry; create brand identity emphasizing heritage.
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho and Amazon Seller Central with high-quality photography showing zari details; create Instagram account with behind-the-scenes artisan content and saree styling; approach 20-30 ethnic boutiques in NCR, Mumbai, Bangalore for wholesale; partner with wedding planners for custom orders; list on Etsy for international reach.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 15-20 weavers; introduce seasonal collections; launch YouTube channel on saree care and weaving process; attend trade shows (Craft India, textile exhibitions); establish D2C website; hire social media manager; expand to 3-5 metro cities with pop-up stores; explore institutional gifting market.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15,000 stipend for artisan training and toolkits + interest-free credit up to ₹40L for business expansion
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to 35% subsidy (₹10L for service/handloom) plus collateral-free loan up to ₹50L
🏚
UP Handloom Weaver Scheme
direct subsidy of ₹3,000-5,000 per saree for exports + training grants for cooperative groups
🏚
UP MSME Udyam Vikas Yojana
₹1L-2.5L subsidy for women entrepreneurs in handloom + interest rate subsidy of 5% for 5 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
