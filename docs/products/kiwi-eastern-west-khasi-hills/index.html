Kiwi ki
opportunity, Eastern West Khasi Hills mein.

Eastern West Khasi Hills operates at 1,200–1,800m elevation with 300cm annual rainfall and volcanic soil, making it one of only three kiwi-producing zones in India alongside Himachal Pradesh (60% national share) and Uttarakhand (20%). The district's estimated 400–600 hectares under kiwi cultivation supplies metro demand (Delhi, Mumbai, Bengaluru account for 50% of consumption) within 36–48 hours via NH-40. Cold-chain infrastructure spans 3,000km nationally with regional hubs at Shillong (45km away) connecting to consolidation centers in Guwahati and direct rail/air corridors to major cities.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Eastern West Khasi Hills ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3 existing kiwi growers in Shillong/Nongpoh (contact via Meghalaya Horticulture Department: 0364-2500160); visit a 2-hectare productive farm in March–April (peak harvest); attend bi-monthly haat at Shillong Market (Wednesday/Saturday) to observe fruit pricing, packaging, buyer profiles. Check Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in) for free registration eligibility. Zero cost—takes 1 week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified kiwi saplings (Allgold, Abbott, Hayward varieties—18-24 months old) from Nagaland Horticulture Nursery (Kohima, 180km) or Indian Institute of Horticulture (Bangalore, via courier ₹8-12K for 100 saplings). Soil testing via Meghalaya Agricultural Department soil lab (₹500, turnaround 2 weeks). First batch cost for 0.5-hectare: ₹1.8-2.2L (saplings ₹80K + soil prep ₹50K + trellising ₹40K + irrigation ₹60K).
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (land owner): Lease 0.5–1.5 hectare in Nongpoh/Mawshynrut/Pynursla tehsils at ₹8-15K/year; build trellis system (₹40-60K); invest in drip irrigation (₹35-50K); 2-year pre-revenue phase. Total ₹1.5-2.2L. Option B (partnership): Partner with 2-3 existing growers, take 15-25% of harvest post-production (splits capital risk, faster revenue, less control). Option C (agri-entrepreneur scheme): Apply under Meghalaya State Horticulture Mission for ₹2-3L subsidy (covers 40-50% setup), 6-month approval timeline.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 5 mins, online); obtain FSSAI registration (₹5K, 2 weeks) if packaging/processing; apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via APEDA portal (free, 1 week) if exporting; GI registration not required (no GI tag on district kiwi yet, but opportunity exists—consult IP law firm ₹1-2L if planning GI filing with 5+ farmer groups). Food safety audit ₹3-5K. Timeline: 4-6 weeks total, cost ₹10-15K.
5
First Sale
First sale: Register on Grofers/BigBasket farm-direct program (₹0 upfront, 10% commission); list 50kg initial batch at ₹160-180/kg (wholesale price ₹120-140/kg ex-farm, buyer margin ₹40-60/kg). Reach out to 5 premium restaurant chains in Delhi/Bengaluru via email (VP Procurement); supply samples (₹2K cost). Expected first-month revenue (if 200kg sold via aggregate platforms): ₹24-32K gross, ₹8-12K net margin.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (300+ kg/month sales): hire 1 farm supervisor (₹12K/month) + 1 logistics coordinator (₹10K/month); invest in cold-storage unit (₹50-80K) to extend shelf-life; apply for National Horticulture Mission subsidy (₹100 Cr pool nationwide, covers planting material + training + irrigation—state typically allocates ₹5-10L per applicant); exhibit at India Fruit Show (New Delhi, Nov), Foodtech Expo (Bangalore, Feb); build D2C brand via Instagram/YouTube (farm-to-table content, cold-chain transparency); expand to processed products (dried slices, concentrates) for margin boost (15% CAGR segment).

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
National Horticulture Mission (NHM)
covers up to 50% of cost for planting material, irrigation infrastructure, post-harvest equipment; ₹100 Cr allocated nationwide; apply via state horticulture department.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
₹25L subsidy for agri-based enterprises; 25% equity, 75% bank loan; margin money ₹6.25L, apply via KVIC/SIDBI.
🏚
Meghalaya State Horticulture Mission
₹2-3L subsidy per farmer for high-altitude fruit cultivation; preference to SC/ST/women farmers; apply via district horticulture office.
🏚
Meghalaya Agri-Entrepreneur Scheme
₹5L grant for setting up cold-chain/processing unit; 18-month approval, apply via State Cooperative Department.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
