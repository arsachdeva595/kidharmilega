Eastern West Khasi Hills' high altitude (1200-1800m), cool climate, and well-drained laterite soil create ideal micro-conditions for kiwi fruit cultivation. The region's consistent rainfall and morning mist provide natural pest management, while the terrain's slope ensures perfect water drainage—critical for quality kiwi production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Eastern West Khasi Hills ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established kiwi farms in Shillong (10km away) and adjacent West Khasi Hills clusters. Connect with Meghalaya Agriculture Department's Horticulture Division in Shillong. Study soil testing facilities at ICAR-ATARI Northeast in Umiam, Meghalaya. Attend kiwi farming workshops at State Agriculture Extension Centers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified kiwi saplings from IIHR Bangalore or Himachal Pradesh horticulture nurseries (₹20-30 per sapling; 500 saplings = ₹10,000-15,000 for 1 acre trial). Procure organic fertilizer (vermicompost, neem cake) from local suppliers in Shillong. First-batch setup for 2 acres: ₹50,000-80,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with State Department of Horticulture for technical guidance. Engage local farm laborers through village gram panchayats. Set up drip irrigation (estimated ₹2-3L for 2 acres) and shade netting in first year. Begin organic certification process via APEDA-approved certifiers (₹15,000-25,000 annually).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) on udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from APEDA for export eligibility. Register brand name with Ministry of Consumer Affairs. Get organic certification from APEDA or FABRIC. Create GST registration. Develop packaging with organic/high-altitude product claims.
5
First Sale
Begin with B2B sales to specialty retailers in Meghalaya (Nongthymmai Market, Shillong). List on BigBasket, Amazon Fresh, JioMart under organic premium fruit category. Contact export brokers registered with APEDA. Partner with organic food chains (Green Apple, Naturalia). Leverage Farmers' APMC markets in Northeast.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand acreage from 2 to 5-10 acres. Develop export certifications (ISO 22000, GlobalGAP). Launch value-added product line (jam, powder) via co-packing. Build farm-to-table restaurant partnerships in metros. Establish agri-tourism model with guided orchard visits and tasting rooms.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for agricultural enterprise with 25-35% margin subsidy for SC/ST applicants.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹50L cluster development grant for agri-processing and cold chain infrastructure in Meghalaya.
🏚
Meghalaya Agriculture Technology Mission (MATM)
₹5L to ₹15L for high-value horticulture projects with subsidy on drip irrigation and shade nets.
🏚
Meghalaya Organic Farming Scheme
50% subsidy on organic certification costs and 25% on agri-inputs for APEDA-certified organic farmers.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
