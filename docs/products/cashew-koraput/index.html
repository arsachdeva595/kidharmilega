Koraput sits in Odisha's southern region with 8807 sq km of laterite-soil territory ideal for cashew. Cashew clusters are concentrated in Koraput, Pottangi, Kotpad, and Nabarangpur blocks with over 15,000 farming households supplying raw nuts within 40-60km of processing units. Buyer markets: Odisha urban centres (Bhubaneswar, Raipur), metro chains (Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai), and direct export to Vietnam and Middle East ports.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Koraput ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Koraput town cashew market cluster and speak with 5-7 farmers at the weekly Saturday haat (Koraput block office area) — free. Call 2-3 SCSPC members (list available from District Agricultural Office, Koraput). Register free Udyam account on udyamregistration.gov.in using your Aadhar and address.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cashew nuts from Koraput district farming cooperatives (procurement rate: ₹120-140/kg raw nuts, bulk order minimum 500 kg). First batch cost: ₹60-70K for 500 kg raw nuts. Suppliers located in Koraput, Pottangi, Kotpad blocks. Get quotes from 3-4 farmer groups; negotiate payment terms (30-day credit is standard for repeat orders).
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent 1500-2000 sq ft processing unit (₹8-12K/month) in Koraput town or Pottangi town with roasting oven, sheller machine, grading table — total ₹18-25L capex. Option B: Artisan partnership with 8-12 women's SHGs (following Tulsi SHG model) for handshelling at ₹25-30/kg commission — lower capex (₹8-12L), higher labour flexibility. Equipment suppliers: Hyderabad-based Technomax Machinery, Surat-based Orient Machinery — delivery 4-6 weeks.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (2 days, free). Apply for FSSAI license online (₹5000-8000, 10-14 days). File GI registration application at IP India for 'Koraput Cashew' — expected ₹8-12L cost, 18-24 month timeline (Ministry of Commerce & Industry supports this). If exporting: Get IEC code from DGFT Bhubaneswar (₹2000, 5 days). Trademark brand name at CGPDTM (₹9000-12000, 12-18 months).
5
First Sale
First sale: Upload 100kg SKUs to Amazon Seller Central or BigBasket Kirana (vendor onboarding free, net-30 payment terms). First order size typically 50-100 units (10-20 kg) from B2B wholesalers at KSMTRADE Koraput market. Expected first-month revenue: ₹1.5-2.5L (conservative: 200 kg at ₹750/kg wholesale = ₹1.5L). Payment: 2-4 week credit standard.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (40-45 MT capacity), expand to 4000 sq ft facility (₹35-40L investment). Hire: Production manager (₹15-18K/month), 2-3 quality checkers (₹10K/month each), 1 export coordinator (₹18-20K/month). Exhibit at IHGF Delhi Fair (booth ₹60-80K) and Nutri-India Expo (₹40-50K). Establish direct relationships with 5-7 metro retail chains and 2-3 export trading houses.

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

Minimum ₹4-5L start-up cost: ₹1.5L raw material (500 kg cashew nuts), ₹1.8L equipment rental (roaster, sheller, grader for 6 months), ₹1L working capital, ₹0.7L licensing/registration. Can go up to ₹25L for own facility with full automation. Many founders start at ₹6-8L with artisan SHG partnerships.
Not essential but helpful: 3-6 months learning from existing roasters in Kotpad or Pottangi (free/low-cost apprenticeship). Must understand moisture content, roasting curves, quality grading, FSSAI rules. OLM training available free in Odisha. Most successful founders started with 0 experience but learned fast.
PM FORMALISATION OF MICRO FOOD PROCESSING (PMFME): ₹10L subsidy. MUDRA loan: ₹10L at 8-12% interest. Odisha Livelihoods Mission: Free training + ₹50-75K marketing support. GI registration support: Ministry of Commerce can reimburse 50% of filing cost (₹4-6L). APEDA export subsidy: 5-10% refund on FOB value if export >100 MT.
Wholesale buyers: Visit Spencer's, Big Bazaar sourcing offices in Bhubaneswar (contact via ORMAS). B2B platforms: Tradeindia, IndiaMART (get 5-8 inquiries/month post-listing). Export: Contact Vietnam cashew processors via trade bodies ICAR (Indian Cashew Association, Kollam). Farmer SHGs: Use ORMAS outlet network (existing at Koraput zilla parishad). First 10 MT usually go to local haats and bulk retailers within 3 months.
Yes, high demand. Top buyers: Vietnam (₹4-4.5/kg), Netherlands (₹4.8-5.2/kg), UAE (₹4.2-4.8/kg), USA (₹5.5-6.5/kg). Process: Get IEC code (₹2000, 5 days), register with APEDA (₹5000, 10 days), get FSSAI export approval (₹8000), arrange container shipment via Visakhapatnam port (₹80-120K per 20ft container, ~8 MT capacity). Minimum viable order: 5-10 MT per shipment. Margin: 35-50% FOB.
Honest downside: Cashew market is commodity-linked; price swings ±15-20% annually. If demand drops, sitting inventory (5-10 MT) can lose ₹2-4L value in 3-4 months. Exit options: (1) Sell raw cashew nuts back to traders at ₹5-8/kg loss, (2) Liquidate equipment via Indiamart at 30-40% of cost, (3) Pivot to cashew apple juice or CNSL production (₹10-15L additional investment). Survival: Keep 3-month working capital buffer. Start small, scale after 6 months profit proof.
