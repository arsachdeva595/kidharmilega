Kholar (Kidney Beans) ki
opportunity, Shamator mein.

Shamator district (469 sq km, population 34,223) sits in Nagaland's high hills at 1,400-1,800 meters elevation, providing natural growing conditions for kidney beans with superior nutrient density and earthy aroma. The district has 37 villages engaged in bean cultivation across 2 blocks, with documented export channels established to 14 countries (U.S., Canada, Mexico, Germany, U.K., France, China, Japan, South Korea, Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait, Qatar). Raw materials are sourced locally at near-zero cost; finished goods reach international buyers via road-to-port logistics through Dimapur (90 km) and Guwahati port (250 km).

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Shamator ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call 3-4 kidney bean farmers in Shamator town (Lihsai block); visit the Shamator haat/vegetable market on market day (typically Tuesday/Friday); register free Udyam account online; join WhatsApp groups of Nagaland agricultural exporters (search 'Nagaland kidney bean exporters'). Time: 2 weeks, zero cost.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source from Shamator district farmers directly (Lihsai block has highest concentration). First batch: 500 kg dried kidney beans from 2-3 farmer contacts at ₹200-220/kg (₹100-110K cost). Build relationship with APMC liaison in Dimapur for bulk supply chains. Cost: ₹0 to establish relationships, ₹100-120K for first 500 kg pilot batch.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Low-cost): Partner with existing bean processors in Dimapur (15 km away) — rent processing line capacity at ₹2-5/kg for cleaning, sorting, packaging. Cost: ₹50-80K/month for 500 kg batch production. Option B (Scale): Rent 500 sq ft unit near Shamator market (₹3-5K/month), install basic cleaning/grading machine (₹80-120K one-time), hire 2-3 workers. Setup: ₹150-200K initial.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online, 15 minutes). Obtain FSSAI license (₹5-10K, 30 days). For export: Apply IEC code through DGFT (free online, 3-5 days), file GST (free). Optional: Apply for GI registration through Ministry of Agriculture (support available but not mandatory for initial sales). Total cost: ₹5-15K over 45 days.
5
First Sale
First sale: Approach Dimapur natural foods distributor (e.g., Azelis, local chains) with 50 kg sample batch @ ₹250/kg net = ₹12.5K first order. Launch D2C on Blinkit/BigBasket simultaneously with 200 units 500g pack @ ₹320/pack net (retail ₹450). Expected first-month revenue: ₹25-35K from 3-4 wholesale + 150 D2C units.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (roughly 400-500 kg monthly): hire 1 sales manager for export buyer sourcing, 1 export documentation specialist (₹15-20K/month salary), attend 2-3 food trade fairs per year (BioFach India, Natural & Organic Expo Asia). Reduce production cost from ₹220/kg to ₹180/kg through bulk farmer contracts. Establish direct export containers to Germany, U.S. (2-3 containers/month). Invest ₹20-30K in brand building (Instagram, YouTube recipe content, testimonials from institutional buyers).

Frequently Asked Questions

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

₹1.5 lakhs minimum (beans ₹1L + packaging ₹30K + license ₹5-10K + buffer ₹10-15K). ₹3-4.5 lakh if you want to rent a small processing unit and hire help. First batch from farmers costs ₹1 lakh for 500 kg, then you need working capital for 2-3 months before first sale reaches bank.
Experience in agriculture or trading helps (20-30% faster), but not mandatory. You must be willing to learn food safety (FSSAI rules), talk to farmers weekly, and handle export paperwork. Most successful Shamator bean exporters started without prior experience but spent 3-6 months learning supply chain. You need patience with seasonal harvests and commodity price swings.
Central: PMEGP gives 25% subsidy (up to ₹10L) on your factory setup, MUDRA loan ₹10L at 8-11% interest. State: CMHIS health insurance (₹5L free cover), SFURTI grant potential for certification. You apply through Dimapur MSME office or Nagaland Industries Directorate. Timeline: 2-3 months to get approval, funds disburse in 4-6 months.
Start with Dimapur wholesale distributors (call 5-10, visit in person with 25 kg sample). Parallel: list on BigBasket/Blinkit within 2 weeks (takes 3-5 days approval). First big order typically comes from restaurant chains or corporate meal-prep in Delhi/Bangalore via e-commerce. Build relationships with 3-4 wholesale buyers in first 3 months before scaling exports.
Haan, Shamator kidney beans already export to USA, Canada, Germany, UK, France, Japan, China, Middle East. Process: Get IEC code (online, 3 days), FSSAI certificate, apply for APEDA registration (free), get exporter importer code. You ship via Guwahati Port. Margin: 35-50% FOB (after shipping), but containers are ₹75-90K minimum (6-8 tonnes per container). Buyers are usually importers in US/Canada who handle retail distribution.
Realistic downside: If market doesn't respond in 8 months despite 5+ buyer meetings, you've lost ₹2-3 lakhs. Exit option 1: Sell your processed inventory to wholesalers at 10-15% discount, close the unit. Exit option 2: Pivot to value-added (canned beans, cooked packs) — changes product positioning. Exit option 3: Become a supply-chain middleman (buy from farmers, sell to processors) with zero processing cost. Biggest risk: oversupply years when global prices drop 20-30%, so hedge with contract farming early.
