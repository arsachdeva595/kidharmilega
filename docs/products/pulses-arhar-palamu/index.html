Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Palamu ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Palamu agricultural cooperatives in Daltonganj and Barwadih; meet ICAR research center at Daltonganj; study arhar processing units in Chichind industrial area; interview 5-10 established dal millers
2
Source Raw Materials
Source arhar seeds from Palamu District Seed Association or farmer cooperatives; negotiate bulk purchase of 5-10 tons at ₹4,500-5,500 per quintal; establish relationship with 3-4 farmer groups for sustainable supply chain; first batch cost approximately ₹2.5-4 lakhs for processing setup
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing dal mills in Daltonganj (Chichind area) for contract processing initially; or install mini dal mill (₹1.5-2 lakhs) with vibratory separator and grader; engage with Palamu Farmer Producer Organization for quality assurance and collective marketing
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as food processing micro-enterprise; apply for IEC through DGFT for export eligibility; obtain FSSAI food license (₹5,000-10,000); register brand name with trademark application; comply with organic certification if targeting premium segment (NPOP or APEDA certification ₹50,000-80,000)
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and BigBasket with ₹2-3 per pack promotional pricing; create Instagram and WhatsApp Business for B2C direct orders; approach 20-30 local supermarkets and kirana chains; register with bulk B2B platforms like Tradeling and Globaltrade; participate in APEDA export fairs
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 3-5 dal mill partnerships; launch 2-3 value-added products (arhar soup, roasted arhar); establish company-owned collection centers in 2-3 villages; hire dedicated export manager for international logistics; scale marketing budget to ₹2-3 lakhs monthly
