Srikalahasthi Kalamkari fabrics/Handicrafts ki
opportunity, Tirupati mein.

Venkatagiri village sits 18km north of Tirupati city in YSR Kadapa region, housing 3064 weaver households specializing in cotton, silk-mix, and pure silk sarees woven on traditional pit looms. Raw material supply is secured: cotton sourced locally from Andhra Pradesh farms, pure zari threads imported directly from Kanchipuram weavers 45km away, eliminating middlemen. Buyer markets include domestic urban demand (₹80-120 per saree wholesale), export buyers in USA and EU (₹150-250 per saree FOB), temple pilgrims visiting Tirupati annually (2.3M visitors), and institutional buyers including hotels and gift retailers.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Tirupati ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Venkatagiri village cluster (18km from Tirupati city); spend 1 day meeting 3-4 established master weavers and artisan groups; call Tirupati ODOP coordinator at DC Handloom office for free cluster introduction; attend weekly Venkatagiri haat on Friday mornings to watch production and negotiate sample costs — zero rupees required
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact established Venkatagiri master weavers directly or through Tirupati Handloom Cooperative; order first batch of 20-30 cotton saree pieces at ₹3,500-4,500 per saree from artisans; negotiate payment terms: 30% advance, 40% on completion, 30% on delivery; first batch cost ₹1.2-1.5L; allow 45-60 days for completion
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner with 2-3 weaver families in Venkatagiri village as exclusive artisans — provide 40% advance on raw materials (you source cotton/silk/zari), they weave; no workspace rental needed, cost ₹8-12L/month for 150-200 pieces. Option B: Rent 600-800 sq ft workspace in Tirupati city near Chintadripet market (₹8,000-12,000/month) to house 2-3 in-house looms, hire trained operators at ₹12,000-15,000/month each
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (5 min online at udyamregistration.gov.in, free); apply for Tirupati ODOP Certificate (contact DC Handloom office, 2-3 weeks, free); file GI tag claim through Tirupati district government (co-filed under existing Srikalahasthi Kalamkari GI, supports your brand); apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) via DGFT website if targeting exports (₹0 filing, 1 week); total cost ₹5,000-8,000 for lawyer fees
5
First Sale
Create Etsy shop with 15-20 high-res product photos (₹3,000 for professional shoot); list 3-4 signature cotton and silk saree designs at ₹9,500-18,000; reach out directly to 10-15 ethnic wear boutiques in Bangalore via WhatsApp with product videos; first bulk order target: 30-50 pieces from 1-2 retailers at ₹4,500-5,000 wholesale = ₹1.35-2.5L first month revenue; expect 2-3 Etsy orders in first month at ₹12,000 average = ₹24-36K
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time operations person (₹25,000/month) to manage artisan relationships and order tracking; open a 1,200 sq ft retail-cum-workshop in Tirupati city (₹20,000/month) to display sarees and conduct tourist workshops; exhibit at 2-3 national textile fairs annually (Handloom Expo, Indian Handicraft Fair) at ₹1-2L per booth; partner with 6-8 weaver families instead of 2-3; launch B2B WhatsApp catalog and attend quarterly trade shows in Delhi and Mumbai

Frequently Asked Questions

Seedhe sawaal, seedhe jawab

₹2.5-8L. Minimum: ₹2.5L for artisan partnership model (20-30 saree first batch + basic inventory + registration + website). Maximum: ₹8L if you rent a workshop space in Tirupati city, invest in 2-3 pit looms, and buy 3 months raw material stock upfront. Most successful founders start at ₹4-5L with artisan partnership + Etsy store.
No prior weaving experience needed, but you must spend 2-3 weeks learning from master weavers in Venkatagiri village before starting. The real skill is managing artisan relationships, understanding saree grading (thread count, zari quality, pattern complexity), and sales. If you have textile/fashion background it helps, but 60% of successful operators come from zero textile background.
4 schemes available: (1) PM Vishwakarma — ₹15K toolkit + ₹5L interest-free loan for registered artisans; (2) PMEGP — 25-35% subsidy on setup (up to ₹25L) via DIC Tirupati; (3) AP Handloom Welfare Scheme — ₹5-10K annual raw material subsidy; (4) AP MSME Plug & Play Sheds — 70% discounted rent (₹8-10/sq ft) for 3 years. Total potential govt support: ₹1.5-2.5L in first year.
Three channels: (1) Local: Tirupati temple gift shops and tourist retail (walk in with samples, 1-2 weeks); (2) Online: Etsy listings attract international buyers automatically, expect 2-3 orders in first month; (3) Wholesale: Email saree boutiques in Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi with lookbook + WhatsApp samples, expect first bulk order (50+ pieces) in 4-6 weeks. Realistic first sale: ₹40-60K within 3 weeks through Etsy or local retail.
Haan, bilkul. Srikalahasthi Kalamkari has GI tag (2005) which supports export claims. Current buyers: USA (40% of exports), UK (25%), Germany (15%), France/Australia (20%). Export process: (1) Get IEC code (1 week, free); (2) Join APEDA (Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority) — free membership; (3) Meet quality standards (color fastness, shrinkage tests via AITA labs in Tirupati, ₹2,000-3,000 per batch); (4) Find buyer via TradeKey/Global Sources or direct emails; (5) Ship via air freight (₹400-600 per kg for 2-3kg samples) or sea freight (₹8,000-12,000 per container). FOB margin: 42-58% if you're selling direct to foreign retailers.
Honest risk: Saree demand drops if economy slows (happened 2020-21 during COVID). If 6 months in you're not hitting ₹1L/month, pivot options: (1) Switch to export-only model (B2B has better margins 45-55% vs 22-32% domestic wholesale); (2) Partner with existing retailers instead of building own brand (margins lower but faster sales); (3) Shift to contract manufacturing for larger brands like Fab India (stable ₹3-5L/month but no margin upside); (4) Switch to Kalamkari hand-painting instead of sarees (higher margins 50-65%, less capital needed ₹1.5-2L). Worst case: ₹2-3L loss if you quit in year 1, which is recoverable.
