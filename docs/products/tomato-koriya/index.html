Koriya district's alluvial and laterite soils combined with moderate rainfall and temperature fluctuations create ideal conditions for rich, full-bodied tomato cultivation. The region's agricultural heritage and established supply chains to Raipur and Bilaspur markets provide immediate distribution advantages. Local farming communities have developed specialized irrigation techniques suited to tomato production.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Koriya ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Koriya's Baikunthpur and Kartala tomato-growing clusters; attend Koriya District Agriculture Department workshops; connect with 3-5 established tomato farmers in the region; study Raipur vegetable mandi price trends for 12 months at krishi.farmer.gov.in
2
Source Raw Materials
Source F1 hybrid seeds from Anand Agricultural University or Advanta India, ₹25,000-40,000 for 500g initial batch; farmyard manure from local cattle breeders at ₹2-3/kg; drip systems from Jain Irrigation dealers in Raipur; first 1-acre batch costs ₹80,000-120,000
3
Produce / Partner
Lease 1-2 acres of farmland in Baikunthpur block (₹15,000-25,000/acre/season); engage local agricultural workers at prevailing daily rates; use cooperative society's drip irrigation systems on revenue-share basis; partner with Koriya District Vegetable Farmers Cooperative for knowledge and input supply
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam enterprise (udyamregistration.gov.in), apply for IEC code via APEDA (apeda.gov.in) if exporting; register trademark with 'Koriya Fresh' or similar at ipindia.gov.in; obtain organic certification from CCPTPP if pursuing premium positioning
5
First Sale
List on NinjaCart and BigBasket agri-seller program targeting Raipur-Bilaspur logistics zones; approach Dmart Regional Supply Manager for regional supermarket contracts; contact Koriya Mandi wholesalers directly with samples; partner with Facebook groups focused on Raipur-Durg organic farming for D2C pre-orders
6
Scale
After month 6, expand to 5-7 acres by leasing additional contiguous plots; invest in cold storage facility (₹15-25L) in Baikunthpur or Raipur; establish processing unit for tomato paste/powder; develop B2B subscriptions with 10+ restaurants in Raipur; explore export licensing and shipments to Nepal/Bangladesh via APEDA
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for agri-enterprises with 25-35% entrepreneur contribution
🏛
PM-KISAN SAMMAN NIDHI
₹6,000/year direct income support for registered farmers producing tomatoes; covers input costs
🏚
Chhattisgarh Rajiv Gandhi Kisan Nyay Yojana
direct cash transfer of ₹1-2/kg for farm produce sold through registered channels
🏚
Chhattisgarh Krishi Vikas Yojana
₹10-15 lakh for setting up irrigation, storage, and processing units for vegetable producers in identified clusters
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
