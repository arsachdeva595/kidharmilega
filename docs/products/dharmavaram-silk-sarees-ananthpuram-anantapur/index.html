opportunity, Ananthpuram (Anantapur) mein.

Dharmavaram town in Anantapur district, 220 km from Bangalore and 180 km from Hyderabad, sits in India's second-largest silk weaving zone with 180+ active looms and 400+ skilled weavers. Raw silk sourcing from Bangalore (150 km) and Chennai (350 km) ensures supply chain stability at wholesale rates. Domestic demand peaks July-October (wedding season) and November-December (festivals); export markets active year-round, especially Dubai and Singapore during spring/summer.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Ananthpuram (Anantapur) ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dharmavaram town (15 km from Anantapur city) on a Friday; call the Dharmavaram Weavers Cooperative Society (direct line: ask at Anantapur District Collectorate) to arrange loom visits. Attend the weekly haat in Dharmavaram market to meet 8-10 weaver families, see current designs, and ask prices. Join the WhatsApp group 'Dharmavaram Saree Sellers' (ask any weaver) — zero cost, learn market rates and buyer feedback in real time.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw silk from B. Prabhu & Co. or Sri Venkateswara Silks (both in Bangalore, 150 km away)—bulk order 10 kg raw silk = ₹35000-40000. Source zari, dyes, and threads from Dharmavaram market vendors (same-day delivery). First sample batch: 3-5 sarees with a weaver family in Dharmavaram = ₹18000-25000 for materials + weaver labor (₹3000-4000 per saree stitching). Negotiate 10% bulk discount if ordering 25+ sarees upfront.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Recommended for first 6 months): Partner directly with an existing weaver family in Dharmavaram—they own the loom, you supply materials and design; you pay ₹3000-5000 per saree for weaving. Option B (Long-term): Rent a small workshop unit (2-3 looms) in Dharmavaram for ₹8000-12000/month; hire 2-3 weavers at ₹8000-10000/month salary. First 10 sarees monthly is achievable within 30 days at Option A cost; scale to 50/month by month 4.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 10 min at udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for GI certification support through Andhra Pradesh Handlooms Development Corporation (APHDC)—they provide documents and coaching free. If exporting: Apply for IEC (Importer Exporter Code) at eirp.nic.in (₹0 cost, 2-week approval). Trademark your brand name at ipindia.gov.in (₹4500-9000, 18-month approval). Total timeline: 3-4 months for all registrations.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Instagram simultaneously (₹0 cost). Post 5 hero saree photos with 30-second loom-to-drape videos; target keywords 'Dharmavaram Silk Saree' and 'GI-tagged Saree'. First customer outreach: DM 30 wedding planners in Bangalore asking for 3-piece trial orders. Expected first order: 2-3 sarees at wholesale price (₹10000-12000 each) within 2 weeks; expected first-month revenue ₹30000-50000 from 3-5 orders.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month MRR: Hire 1 full-time design curator (₹15000/month) to manage Instagram/Etsy and customer queries. Increase production to 60-80 sarees/month by adding 2 more weaver partnerships (no rent increase). Exhibit at Handloom Expo (February, Delhi) and Bangalore Wedding Show (June) — ₹40000-60000 booth cost. Target bulk B2B orders from 8-10 bridal studios. Launch WhatsApp catalogue with 20+ designs. At ₹25L/month: Own 3-4 looms, hire 5-6 weavers full-time, open a pop-up showroom in Bangalore (₹30000/month rent).

Honest truth: If customers don't want your designs or saree quality dips, you lose ₹2-3L within 6-8 months. Exit: Sell raw materials (silk, zari, threads) back to suppliers at 60-70% of purchase price. Sell loom (if you bought) for 50-60% cost. Skilled weavers are always in demand — if you hired them directly, they leave and find work elsewhere (zero severance issue). Partnerships with weaver families? Break it and they find other buyers. So downside is ₹1-2L net loss, not business closure. Best safeguard: Pre-sell 10-15 sarees before placing raw material orders. Use Instagram presale model — collect ₹15k deposits, deliver in 30 days.
