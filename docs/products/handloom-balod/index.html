Balod has a centuries-old tradition of silk weaving inherited from the Gond and other indigenous communities. The region's cocoon production, access to tasar silkworms, and established weaver clusters make it naturally suited for silk and handloom production. The district's proximity to raw material sources and skilled artisan workforce provides competitive advantage.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Balod ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Balod weaver clusters in Manendragarh, Bemetara, and around Durg; connect with cluster associations under SFURTI program; study existing exporters like Chhattisgarh Handloom Board; attend textile fairs in Delhi (IHGF) and Bangalore (Handloom Expo).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Kosa cocoons from Chhattisgarh Cocoon Producers Cooperative or local cocoon traders in Durg district (₹150-200/kg); import natural dyes from Jaipur textile suppliers (₹2-5L for initial dye inventory); wooden looms cost ₹15000-30000 each from local carpenters in Balod town.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 10-20 registered weaver groups under SFURTI clusters; negotiate per-piece rates (₹800-1500 per saree depending on complexity); ensure fair-trade pricing and advance payments; document artisan details for export compliance.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (MSME portal) as proprietorship; obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office (Raipur); register brand name with IP India; obtain Handloom Mark certification from DC Handloom Ministry; GST registration with proper HSN codes (5007-silk fabric category).
5
First Sale
Launch D2C on Meesho, Instagram Shop, and custom Shopify store; list on Daraz, Flipkart, Amazon Artisans; partner with heritage boutiques in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore; reach out to ethnic wear aggregators like Craftsvilla; initiate first batch of 50-100 sarees via influencer gifting on Instagram and fashion blogs.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 40-50 weavers; introduce men's wear (stoles, shirts); launch corporate gifting vertical; open pop-up stores in metros; apply for export subsidy under MEIS/DEIS; participate in international textile fairs (Frankfurt, Paris); build retail partnerships with lifestyle chains.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15L interest-free loan for traditional craftspeople including weavers with mentorship and toolkit support.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L grant (25-35% subsidy) for setting up handloom micro-enterprises.
🏚
Chhattisgarh Haat Bazaar Vikas Yojana
subsidy on market linkage and branding for registered handloom artisans and enterprises.
🏚
Chhattisgarh Hastkala Vikas Nidhi
direct financial support and training grants for traditional handloom and craft entrepreneurs under DC Handloom.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
