Handloom ki
opportunity, Hardoi mein.

Hardoi district in central UP sits 240 km from Delhi and 90 km from Lucknow, with established handloom clusters in Madhogarh, Kanpur Road, and surrounding tehsils employing 8,000-12,000 weavers. Cotton and silk yarn suppliers are concentrated in Kanpur (50 km), reducing raw material lead time to 5-7 days. The district feeds into Lucknow's wholesale markets and Delhi's Chandni Chowk, while exporters in Varanasi and Surat use Hardoi production for bulk orders to US and European buyers.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Hardoi ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Madhogarh handloom cluster (25 km from Hardoi city) on any Wednesday market day; speak to 3-4 established weavers about their production setup, yarn costs, and monthly output. Call Hardoi District Handloom Office (0562-2520600) to understand current ODOP scheme benefits and artisan network. Register for free on Udyam at udyamregistration.gov.in using Aadhaar. Cost: ₹0, time: 4-6 hours, doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton and silk yarn from Kanpur yarn merchants in Govind Nagar (50 km, 5-7 day delivery). First batch: 10 kg cotton + 5 kg silk = ₹18,000-22,000. Partner with 2-3 artisan weavers in Madhogarh cluster or rent 200-300 sq ft space near cluster for ₹3,000-5,000/month if starting your own unit. Supplier contact: Call UP Handloom Federation or visit Kanpur Textile Trade Association.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (Low cost): Partner with 2-3 established weavers on profit-sharing (60-70% to artisan, 30-40% to you as designer/marketer). Lead time: 20-30 days per saree. Option B (Controlled): Rent 250 sq ft space in Madhogarh cluster (₹4,000-6,000/month), install 2-3 looms (₹35,000 each = ₹105,000), hire 1-2 artisans (₹8,000-10,000/month). First production batch cost: ₹120,000-150,000 for 20-25 pieces. Monthly fixed cost: ₹18,000-22,000.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online (free, 15 minutes) at udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at apeda.gov.in if targeting export (₹0, 2-3 days). File GI tag application with DC Handloom Hardoi if you develop unique pattern (₹5,000-8,000 filing cost, 18-24 month approval). Register brand name at ipindia.gov.in (₹4,500, 2-3 months). Total: ₹10,000-12,000, 1 month timeline.
5
First Sale
First sale: Post 5-6 pieces on Meesho shop (free listing, 2% commission) and Instagram Shop simultaneously. Target Meesho audience (Tier-2 city women, 24-45 years). First month: aim for 8-12 orders at ₹2,500 average = ₹20,000-30,000 revenue (₹13,000-22,500 net after COGS). Parallel: Email 20-30 Delhi ethnic wear retailers with catalog; expect 1-2 orders of 10-15 pieces by month 2.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (40 pieces/month wholesale, 15-20 D2C): Hire 1 quality-checker and 1 social media manager (₹8,000-12,000 combined). Increase artisan partnerships from 2-3 to 6-8 weavers. Exhibit at Delhi Handloom Expo (Feb-March, ₹15,000-25,000 stall cost) and Jaipur Crafts Fair (Nov-Dec). Launch export via Etsy/Amazon Global (₹3,000 one-time setup + 3-5% commission). Partner with 2-3 wedding planners in Delhi for bulk seasonal orders.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
₹15,000 interest-free loan + ₹500 toolkit subsidy per artisan partner enrolled; register partners at pmvishwakarma.gov.in.
🏛
MUDRA Loan (Shishu category)
up to ₹50,000 unsecured loan at 8-10% interest for business setup; apply via nearest bank branch.
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Handloom Policy 2022
₹2-5 Cr interest-free capital loan for cluster-based units + 10-year property tax waiver; apply via DC Handloom office.
🏚
HasthShilp Yojana (UP)
₹3-8L subsidy for artisan training, loom upgrades, and marketing; eligible after Udyam registration.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.

Honest answer: Handloom is seasonal (demand peaks Oct-Dec for diwali/weddings, Apr-Jun for summer). First year you may reach only ₹25-40L revenue if you start with ₹5L investment.
