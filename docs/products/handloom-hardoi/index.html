Hardoi has a centuries-old weaving tradition with concentrated artisan communities skilled in cotton and silk textile production. The district benefits from proximity to raw material suppliers in UP and established trade networks with buyers across North India. The local ecosystem includes trained weavers, supportive crafts cooperatives, and existing cluster infrastructure in towns like Bijnor road area.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Hardoi ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Hardoi handloom clusters around Kanpur Road and Saraiya; meet weavers at govt handloom centers; study competitor brands on Etsy, Fabindia, Jaypore; attend India Handloom Expo; join handloom associations for market intel.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton/silk yarn from Surat textile suppliers (approx ₹80K-150K for 100kg starter batch); natural dyes from Rajasthan (₹10-15K); approach established Hardoi weaver cooperatives for bulk orders at ₹300-600 per saree production cost.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 3-5 master weavers in Hardoi (Kanpur Road cluster preferred); visit their looms; place small pilot orders (10-20 pieces) to validate quality; establish payment terms (50% advance, 50% on delivery); consider registering as middleman with local handloom board.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME online (free); apply for IEC from DGFT for export eligibility; file trademark for brand name at IP India; get Handloom Mark certification from Ministry of Textiles; open business bank account; design product care labels and packaging.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (2.75% fees, global reach), Amazon Handmade India (8% commission); build Instagram presence with artisan storytelling and reels; approach boutique owners in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with sample packs; contact wedding planners and corporate gifting companies; run Pinterest ads targeting 'ethical handloom' and 'traditional sarees'.
6
Scale
Build inventory with 15-20 weavers by month 6; launch capsule collections seasonally; partner with lifestyle e-commerce (Myntra, Nykaa); attend Handloom Expo and trade shows; develop bulk B2B program for hotels and spas; launch subscription model for seasonal collection previews; explore international distributor partnerships.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 cashless toolkits and 5% interest subvention on loans up to ₹3L for artisan entrepreneurs
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan at 25% subsidy for micro-enterprises in handloom sector
🏚
UP Handloom Development Scheme
30% capex subsidy on looms, dye vats, and equipment for registered handloom units
🏚
UP MSME Loan Guarantee Scheme
100% guarantee on working capital loans up to ₹50L with minimal documentation
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
