Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Surendranagar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Surendranagar weaving clusters (Wadhwan, Dasada areas) and connect with master weavers; spend 2-3 weeks apprenticing on Single Ikat techniques; study competitor products on Etsy and Jaypore.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton yarn from Ahmedabad textile markets (Gidc area) at ₹80-120/kg; procure natural indigo from Rajasthan suppliers or synthetic dyes locally; first batch of 10kg yarn + dye costs ₹15,000-25,000; establish relationships with 2-3 dye suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established weaver families in Wadhwan or Dasada who own looms; negotiate per-piece production rates (₹800-2000/saree depending on complexity); or invest ₹1-1.5L in 1-2 looms and train 1-2 artisans; produce 15-20 pieces/month initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in, free); apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office for export capability; register brand name at IP India; obtain GST registration (12% for handloom); create brand identity emphasizing heritage and Single Ikat authenticity.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy with high-quality photography (₹5k for professional shoot); list on Jaypore, Craftroots, and own Shopify store; approach 5-10 boutiques in Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi; target interior design firms for customization; use Instagram Reels showing dyeing process for organic reach.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 3-4 weaver partnerships; develop collection-based releases (seasonal); apply for SFURTI cluster development support; participate in Maker Mela, Sonar Bangla, and international textile fairs; build B2B relationships with luxury brands; explore customization for corporate clients.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15,000 interest-free loan plus ₹500 toolkit assistance for registered artisans and master craftspeople
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy for micro-enterprises in handloom sector with 25% margin benefit
🏚
Gujarat Handloom Policy 2023
capital subsidy of 15-20% on machinery, 10% export promotion subsidy, and exemption on SGST for 5 years
🏚
Ambuja Resorls & Resorts Artisan Development Fund (Gujarat Textile Department)
working capital support and skill development subsidies for traditional weavers
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
