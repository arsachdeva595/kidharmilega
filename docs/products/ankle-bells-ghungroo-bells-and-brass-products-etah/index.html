Ankle Bells (Ghungroo), Bells and Brass Products ki
opportunity, Etah mein.

Etah district (4,446 sq km, 1.77M population) sits in Aligarh Division, 130 km south of Delhi and 60 km west of Moradabad, the metalwork hub. Brass ingots and raw metals flow in from Moradabad; finished ghungroo and bells flow out to dance schools in Mumbai, Chennai, Bangalore and Kolkata. The cluster is spread across 8 blocks with artisan concentrations in Etah town, Kasganj, and Bahbalpur — estimated 1,200-1,500 active producers and workshops.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Etah ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Etah town market on Saturday or Tuesday (haat days). Meet 3-4 established ghungroo makers and ask: production cost per unit, wholesale price, lead time, minimum order quantity. Call or visit Moradabad brass suppliers (Google: 'Moradabad brass bell suppliers'). Attend a local dance school in Etah (search 'Kathak academy Etah') and ask dancers what they pay for bells. Cost: ₹0 (fuel only).
2
Source Raw Materials
Primary suppliers in Etah town (near Cantonment Road and Hari Nagar) sell ghungroo at ₹35-70 per pair wholesale depending on size/design. Moradabad raw brass sheets (₹180-240/kg) from suppliers like Sharma Brass Works. First batch: 200 pairs × ₹50 average = ₹10K. Source directly from maker-artisans who work on commission or inventory consignment — no middleman.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent a 150-200 sq ft workshop in Etah town (₹3-5K/month) with basic tools (manual press, polishing wheel, soldering setup = ₹25-35K one-time). Hire 1-2 artisans at ₹8-12K/month. Option B: Partner with existing artisan on revenue-share (60:40 you:artisan) — no rent. Produce 50-100 pairs/month initially. Investment: ₹50-80K (tools + raw material buffer + wages for 1.5 months).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME): udyamregistration.gov.in, 15 mins, free, online. Open business bank account (HDFC/ICICI, ₹1-2K); get GST registration within 3-5 days if ₹20L turnover limit. File IEC (Import-Export Code) if exporting: portal.ecgc.in, ₹0 cost, 5-7 days. Consider GI registration if scaling: apply to Ministry of Commerce GI registry (₹5-10K filing + 18-24 month process) — but not mandatory to start.
5
First Sale
First sale: Post 15-20 product images on Etsy (₹6,500 shop setup + ₹300 listing fees for 5 items). Target first order: 10 pairs Ghungroo + 5 brass bells = ₹1,500 revenue. Parallel: DM 20 dance schools on Instagram with catalogue PDF (design your own or hire freelancer ₹500). First bulk order likely: 50-100 pairs for academy at ₹2,500-4,000. Timeline: first sale in 2-3 weeks. Expected first-month revenue: ₹8-15K (if 1-2 wholesale + 3-5 D2C orders).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire a dedicated designer (₹10K/month) to create new designs, hire sales/export person (₹12K/month), rent larger 400 sq ft unit (₹8K/month). Produce 800-1,000 pairs/month with 4-5 artisans. Exhibit at India International Trade Fair (IITF) Delhi in Nov (₹80-150K stall cost, but attracts B2B buyers). Approach Craftsvilla, FabIndia wholesale. Start LinkedIn outreach to corporate gifting houses. Scale D2C with paid Instagram/Google ads (₹500/day budget = ₹80-120K/month additional revenue).

Etah Handicraft Cluster (Etah town, Hari Nagar block)
Informal cluster of 150+ brass and bell workshops. Shared tool lending systems, common firing units. No formal plots; mostly ground-floor rented spaces (150-300 sq ft, ₹3-6K/month). Electricity and water available. No on-site office/admin.

One District One Product Scheme (ODOP): ₹1L grant for equipment + soft loan via SIDBI at 3%. PM Vishwakarma: ₹3L 0% interest loan + ₹15K toolkit if you register as artisan. PMEGP: ₹25L loan with 35% subsidy (apply via DIC, Etah). UP Kaushal Vikas: free training + ₹5K stipend/month if you hire trained workers. Total potential support: ₹2-5L depending on which you qualify for.
Three fastest channels: (1) Direct to dance schools: call/visit 20 Bharatanatyam/Kathak academies in Etah, Agra, Mathura — pitch bulk order (50-100 pairs at ₹50/pair wholesale). (2) Etsy + Amazon Handmade: first customer often appears 2-3 weeks after listing, usually ₹1,500-3,000 order. (3) Instagram: DM 30-40 dance teachers with product photos, expect 1-2 inquiries in first month. Bulk orders (₹5K+) faster than D2C.
Haan, bilkul. Ghungroo are popular in USA, UK, Germany, Australia among Indian diaspora and classical dance enthusiasts. Logistics: Register IEC (Import-Export Code, free, 5 days). Open import account with shipping forwarder (₹500-1K). Average order to USA: 100-200 pairs = ₹3-4K USD FOB. Shipping: ₹150-250 per kg (0.5-1 kg per 100 pairs). Margins: 40-50% if you sell at ₹20-25 USD per pair retail overseas. Export takes 4-6 weeks first time, 2 weeks after. Needs basic English, Etsy/Amazon Global, or direct B2B partnerships with US retail.
Real downside: if market doesn't respond, you're holding ₹50-80K inventory that's hard to liquidate locally (sunk cost). Demand is seasonal (Oct-Mar for Navratri/weddings). Exit options: (1) Sell stock to established wholesalers at 40-50% discount (₹3-5K loss on ₹10K batch). (2) Pivot to brass utensils or decorative items (same raw material, different market). (3) Wind down, sell workshop tools for ₹8-12K. Most failures happen in months 3-6 if you can't lock even 2-3 bulk institutional orders. Mitigation: presell via Etsy before making inventory.
