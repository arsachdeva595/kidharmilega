Ankle Bells (Ghungroo), Bells and Brass Products ki
opportunity, Etah mein.

Etah has a 300+ year legacy of brass artisanship with concentrated clusters of skilled workers trained in traditional bell-making techniques. The district's proximity to raw material suppliers in Moradabad and established networks with dance academies and religious institutions make it the natural hub for Ghungroo production. Local craftsmen possess specialized knowledge of sound quality, design variations, and market preferences that cannot be replicated elsewhere.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Etah ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Etah's main Ghungroo clusters in Kotwali and Sarai Mehta areas; spend 2-3 weeks learning production from established makers like those near Mathura Road; study sound quality variations by visiting Delhi dance academies (Kathak Kendra, Sangeet Natak Akademi) to understand buyer preferences.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw brass from Moradabad suppliers (contact clusters near Chandra Nagar area) at ₹180-220/kg; leather straps from local Etah tanneries; wood handles from carpentry units in Agra; initial 500-piece batch costs approximately ₹80,000-120,000 depending on size and design complexity.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established artisan families in Etah (negotiate revenue-sharing model of 40-50% per unit or fixed monthly retainer of ₹15,000-25,000); alternatively, hire and train 2-3 craftspeople through apprenticeship; establish small workshop with basic hand tools (cost ₹40,000-60,000).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small Enterprise classification takes 5 minutes online); apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Agra or Meerut (₹0 cost, online); create brand name and register domain; design packaging highlighting 'Etah Handcrafted' heritage; obtain food-grade certification if selling decorated items.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade and Etsy within 30 days (targeting $500-1000 monthly); approach 5-10 Delhi-NCR dance academies directly with samples; contact Crafts Council of India and regional craft bazaars for listing; create Instagram content showing artisan process to drive D2C sales.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to international B2B (reach out to dance supply stores in UK, US via LinkedIn); develop 3-4 signature product lines (student-grade, professional, export-premium); build wholesale partnerships with gift chains (FabIndia, Anokhi, Good Earth); hire dedicated digital marketing person; aim for ₹40-60L annual revenue by month 12.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (25-35%) for manufacturing micro-enterprises in handicraft sector

Uttar Pradesh ODOP Scheme
dedicated marketing support, participation in national/international craft fairs, subsidy on certification and branding

Marketing — Zero Budget Se Shuru Karo
  • Partner with 10-15 classical dance instructors on Instagram/YouTube to create demo videos (₹5,000-10,000 per instructor) showing Ghungroo sound quality and dance applications; target #KathakDance #BharatanatyamTraining hashtags with 2-3 posts weekly.
