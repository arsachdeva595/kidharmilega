Bhujodi Shawl weaving ki
opportunity, Kachchh mein.

Bhujodi is a weaver village 40km east of Bhuj city in Kachchh district, Gujarat, surrounded by pastoral lands supplying raw wool and cotton. The cluster contains two documented family cooperatives (52 total artisans: 32-member Shamji Valji family + 20-member Ramzu Ali Mohammed family) producing shawls, stoles, veils, blankets on pit looms and frame looms using traditional motifs (vakhiyo, chaumukh, satkani, hathi, dholki). Khamir, the apex support organization founded in 2005, generated ₹6.5 Cr revenue last year across 20 Kachchh crafts, providing market access to Delhi, Mumbai, and international buyers via e-commerce.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kachchh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bhujodi village (6 hours from Bhuj city center); meet Shamji Valji or reach Ramzu Ali Mohammed through Khamir (contact +91-2832-210-808); attend Bhuj Haat market on Saturday to see competitor pricing and weaving live. Zero cost — stay with a family contact or basic guesthouse. This week, call 3 past Khamir brand partners (Etsy sellers) to ask about their first-month numbers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Kachchh pasturalists (kala cotton, sheep wool, goat wool via Bhujodi village intermediaries; rayons/silks from Ahmedabad textile wholesalers on Relief Road. First batch cost: ₹40,000-60,000 for 10-15 shawls worth of materials. Supplier area: Bhujodi village itself for cotton/wool; Ahmedabad Relief Road for synthetics (30km drive, 40-50 suppliers).
3
Produce / Partner
Option A (recommended for first 6 months): Artisan partnership — pay Shamji Valji's family or Ramzu Ali Mohammed's cooperatives ₹2,500-3,500 per finished shawl for weaving + design consultation. You handle design, raw material purchasing, and finishing (hemming, labeling). Cost for initial 20-unit monthly production: ₹50,000-70,000 labor. Option B (longer lead time): Lease a 400 sqft space in Bhujodi (₹5,000-8,000/month) with 2-3 pit looms, hire 1-2 part-time weavers (₹12,000/month each), invest ₹1,50,000 in loom setup. Break-even at Option B happens at 80+ shawls/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 15 minutes online on udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for GI certification tag use via Kachchh Weavers' Association (₹5,000 one-time fee, 2-3 months processing). Secure IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) if planning export via DGFT portal (₹0 cost, 5 days processing). Get GST registration if revenue >₹20L (₹0 cost, online, 7 days).
5
First Sale
First sales via Etsy shop (0.2% listing fee + 6.5% transaction fee) — upload 5 hero shawls with 10+ photos each showing weave detail and care instructions. Expect first order in 10-14 days at Etsy average ₹9,000 shawl price. Simultaneously contact 5 luxury boutique owners in Delhi via WhatsApp with lookbook — expect first wholesale order of 3-5 shawls at ₹6,500 wholesale price within 3 weeks. Target first-month revenue: ₹35,000-50,000 gross (after COGS).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (approx 12-15 shawls/week selling D2C + wholesale): hire 1 full-time designer/photographer (₹25,000/month) and 1 operations person to manage artisan logistics (₹18,000/month). Expand loom capacity to 6-8 pit looms (invest ₹80,000 more). Exhibit at India International Handicraft Fair (Delhi, Feb) and Dastkar Mela (Delhi, Nov) — each costs ₹2-3L booth fee but generates 20-40 wholesale leads. Launch Instagram Reels showing weaving process (200 videos yearly at ₹500 each for a local videographer = ₹1 Lakh/year). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (20-25% capex support up to ₹25L) and SFURTI subsidy for cluster growth.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 direct transfer + free PMAY Jeevan Bima insurance for registered weavers; covers raw material cost 50% subsidy up to ₹5L per borrowing.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
25% subsidy on capex (₹1 Cr max loan × 25% = ₹25L subsidy) for manufacturing business in Kachchh MSME zone.
🏚
Gujarat ODOP Support Scheme
30% subsidy on machinery, loom upgrades, and design development cost; apply via dcmsme.gov.in (₹3-5L typical subsidy per beneficiary).
🏚
Gujarat Handloom Rebirth Scheme
provides design consultant stipend (₹50,000/year), market exposure subsidy, and free training at Kachchh Textile Design Institute for 5 artisans per enterprise.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
