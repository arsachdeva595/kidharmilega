Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Kachchh ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bhujodi village in Kachchh (2 hours from Bhuj city) to study weaving techniques with master weavers; connect with Kala Raksha Trust and Kutch Museum for heritage documentation; attend textile fairs at ICCW (Indian Chamber of Commerce & Wool) and Suratgarh Textile Cluster.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton and wool yarn from Indore textile market (₹80-120/kg) or directly from spinner units in Gujarat; procure natural dyes from Maheshwar (MP) or local Kachchh herbalists (₹200-500/kg); first batch of 50 shawls requires ₹120,000-180,000 in materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established weavers in Bhujodi village (minimum ₹15,000-20,000 per shawl labor cost for premium pieces); alternatively establish a small 4-6 loom cooperative with trained weavers; production timeline: 2-4 weeks per shawl depending on complexity.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification) for tax benefits; obtain IEC code for exports via DGFT; design brand identity reflecting Kachchh heritage; register trademark with IP India; apply for Geographical Indication (GI) tag through state textile ministry for long-term brand protection.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (15% commission) and Instagram Shop simultaneously; pitch to luxury portals (Taneira, FabIndia, Handloom.org); approach 5-8 high-end boutiques in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore for wholesale; run influencer campaigns on Instagram targeting lifestyle and ethical fashion creators.
6
Scale
After first 6 months, expand loom network to 10-15 artisans; develop 4-5 signature collections per season; establish pop-up stores in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore); build export relationships with international distributors in UK and USA; develop a school/workshop model for experiential tourism in Bhujodi.
Government Schemes

PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 credit for artisan skill development + 5% interest subsidy for MSME loans up to ₹5 lakhs
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs grant for textile enterprises with 25% subsidy for general category
🏚
Gujarat Handloom Policy 2017
subsidized raw material supply, exemption on electricity charges for registered handloom units
🏚
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster development support up to ₹10 crores for artisan clusters like Bhujodi
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
