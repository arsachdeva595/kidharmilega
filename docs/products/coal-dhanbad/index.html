Proximity to coal reserves eliminates sourcing friction; BCCL and private mining operators work from Dhanbad itself. Buyers span India's eastern industrial corridor (West Bengal, Odisha steel mills) and port cities (Kolkata, Vishakhapatnam) for export.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Dhanbad ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jharia Coalfield mineheads near Belgaum-Dhanbad border; call 5 BCCL contractors for raw material rates and supply terms; attend the bi-monthly coal trade meetups at Dhanbad Chamber of Commerce; register free Udyam profile online — zero cost, doable this week
2
Source Raw Materials
Source coal directly from BCCL (Bharat Coking Coal Limited) coal blocks or licensed contractors in Jharia. First batch: 100-500 MT costs ₹30-50L depending on grade (coking vs thermal). Supplier hub: Belgaum, Jharia, Muraidih blocks near Dhanbad city center.
3
Produce / Partner
Coal trading requires minimal processing if you buy ready-mined coal; partner with BCCL-licensed beneficiation units in Dhanbad (coal washing, sizing, blending) on a margin-share model, or rent a 2000-3000 sqm coal washing unit for ₹3-8L/month. Start with broker/trader model (zero processing) before upgrading to beneficiation.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 10 mins online). Apply for IEC from DGFT Kolkata (₹5000, 2 weeks). Obtain coal trader license from Odisha/Jharkhand coal regulatory board (₹10000-30000, 4-6 weeks). File GST (free, 1 week). No GI registration needed for coal — focus on ISO 9001 quality certification (₹30000-50000) to unlock premium buyers.
5
First Sale
First sale: Cold-call thermal power plant procurement heads (lists available via NTPC, DVC websites) or post on IndiaTradeSupport.com. Minimum order: 50 MT. Expected first-month revenue: ₹15-25L (at ₹3000-5000/MT wholesale rate). Payment terms: 15-30 days post-delivery.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue, hire 1 sales executive + 1 logistics coordinator (₹40000/month combined). Apply for PMEGP loan (up to ₹25L, 35% subsidy) to scale inventory. Connect with port export terminals (Kolkata, Vishakhapatnam) for containerized export. Attend Vibrant Gujarat or Energy Conclave expos to pitch to mills and power plants directly.

Thermal power plants (NTPC, DVC, state power stations) — lists on ministry websites, email their procurement teams. Steel mills (JSW, Tata, Durgapur Steel) — known buyers. Cement factories — constant demand. Cold-call 50 plants in first month, expect 2-3 serious inquiries. First order usually 50-100 MT.
Bilkul. Dhanbad coal's high calorific value makes it prime for export. Buyers: Germany, Belgium, Japan, South Korea, Vietnam, Thailand. Process: IEC registration (₹5000), DGFT approval (2 weeks), international logistics partner, port handling. Margins better (40-50% FOB vs 15-25% domestic). 30% of traders here export; you'll need 2-3 month lead time for first shipment.
Coal is unlikely to flop — it's core to India's energy security. But if your brand flops, you can always exit as a commodity trader (buy cheap, sell high, no branding needed). Inventory liquidation: sell to brokers at slight loss. Worst case: ₹5-10L loss on first batch. Risk mitigation: Start small (100 MT, not 1000 MT), test buyer relationships before scaling, keep 40% of capital in reserve.
