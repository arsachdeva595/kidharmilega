Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Jammu ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jammu district's primary agricultural clusters in Samba, Kathua, and Bishnah taluks. Meet with established Basmati farmers and cooperative societies like Jammu Agriculture Produce Cooperative. Attend the annual Basmati festival in Jammu and network with 50+ traders. Study GI certification requirements from Government of India's GI registry.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified Basmati seeds from SKUAST-K or PAU seed centers (₹8,000-12,000 per quintal). Contract with 5-10 farmers in Samba/Kathua districts for first season's production of 50-100 quintals. Expected first-batch cost: ₹4-6 lakhs for 100 quintals including procurement, drying, and storage.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with APEDA-registered rice millers in Jammu city (e.g., Rehmat Rice Mills, Jammu Rice Industries) for processing, grading, and packaging. Alternatively, lease milling infrastructure (₹80,000-1,20,000/month). Ensure GI certification compliance through Jammu Chamber of Commerce documentation.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online as rice trader/miller. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office (Jammu or Srinagar) for export eligibility. Register brand with Trademark office for pan-India recognition. Secure GI usage license from Ministry of Agriculture. Get FSSAI food business license (₹5,000-15,000).
5
First Sale
Launch D2C brand on Amazon, Flipkart, and Blink with ₹5kg premium packs at ₹800-1,200/kg. Simultaneously approach organized retail chains (Big Basket, Gourmet vendors in Delhi, Mumbai). Secure 3-5 hotel partnerships in Jammu/Srinagar as pilot customers. Use local Jammu traders' networks for distributor placement in North India.
6
Scale
After ₹50+ lakhs revenue, expand to 500+ quintal annual production by contracting more farmers. Launch organic and aged variants with 50-60% higher margins. Build export relationships with Gulf importers through APEDA trade fairs. Set up secondary processing unit for specialty products (rice flour, broken rice). Establish direct supply agreements with 20+ institutional buyers.
Government Schemes

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh government subsidy for manufacturing unit setup with 5-year GST holiday eligibility.
🏛
Pradhan Mantri Kisan Samman Nidhi (PM-KISAN)
₹6,000/year direct income support for contracted farmer partners improving supply chain sustainability.
🏚
Jammu & Kashmir Agri-Business Promotion Scheme
20-25% capital subsidy for agricultural processing units and modern milling infrastructure.
🏚
J&K Horticulture/Agriculture Export Development Scheme
Export credit guarantee coverage and logistics cost subsidies for GI products targeting international markets.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
