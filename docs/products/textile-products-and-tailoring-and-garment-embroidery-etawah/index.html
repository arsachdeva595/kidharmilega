Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Etawah ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Etawah's textile clusters in Sarai Tharah and Chakkar Nagar to understand embroidery techniques, meet master artisans, and study 5-6 successful exporters. Attend Etawah Textile Association meetings and conduct cost audits on existing product lines.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton and synthetic yarn from Jaipur wholesale markets or directly from Gujarat mills (₹3-8 per meter bulk). Purchase embroidery thread from SEIKO, Anchor, or local suppliers in Ludhiana (₹50-150 per spool bulk). Order beads and sequins from Delhi's Chandni Chowk or Surat. First-batch cost for 500 pieces: ₹80,000-150,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 registered embroidery units in Etawah (Sarai Tharah cluster has 200+ units) for quality consistency. Establish a quality control team. Alternatively, purchase semi-automatic embroidery machines (₹400K-800K) and hire 2-3 skilled operators. Implement batch production system with 20-50 pieces per cycle.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (MSME) within 10 days on udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal. Register trademark for brand name at IP India. Open Udyam-linked GST account. Obtain Textile Manufacturing License from UP MSME Department.
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho and Thesocialcommerce targeting tier-2 cities first (lower competition). Set up Instagram shop with 15-20 high-quality embroidery process videos and finished product reels. Partner with 3-5 boutiques in Kanpur and Lucknow for offline traction. Export via iCertify and TradeKey platforms targeting bulk B2B buyers.
6
Scale
After ₹15L quarterly revenue: Hire a dedicated export operations manager, apply for APEDA recognition, secure a 5,000 sqft production facility, add 5 more artisan partnerships, and launch premium D2C sub-brand targeting 50K+ ticket sizes. Explore bulk orders from Reliance Trends and FabIndia.

up to ₹25L subsidy for SC/ST and ₹10L for general category for micro-enterprise setup

PM Vishwakarma Yojana
₹15,000 direct benefit transfer and free training for traditional artisans, plus 5% concessional loan up to ₹3L
🏚
UTTAR PRADESH TEXTILE POLICY 2017
Capital subsidy of 15% up to ₹5L for new textile units and 10% for expansion in textile corridors including Etawah
🏚
STATE ODOP PROMOTION SCHEME
Free registration, capacity building, and export support for registered ODOP products from Etawah

Ye marketing ideas try karna mat bhulna

  • Partner with 10-15 micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in the Indian fashion space for Instagram Reels showcasing artisan embroidery process and final garments. Run 2-week campaigns during Diwali and wedding seasons with 15-20% discount codes.
