Thiruvananthapuram district in Kerala's south sits 20km from Kanyakumari, with established handloom clusters in Thiruvananthapuram city and surrounding towns accessing cotton suppliers in Tamil Nadu and local coconut fiber. The district's 1-million-person traditional industry workforce and literacy rate of 93.91% ensure skill availability and buyer sophistication. Buyers cluster in Kochi (180km), Bangalore (500km), and Chennai (250km) for wholesale, with direct-to-consumer access through Kerala's urban population.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Thiruvananthapuram ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Thiruvananthapuram handloom cluster in city center this week (free), meet 2-3 weaver groups, call Kerala State Handlooms Development Corporation at 0471-2321215, attend monthly haat at Akshaya Textile Park Thiruvananthapuram. Join Facebook groups: Kerala Handloom Weavers, Kasavu Saree Traders — zero cost.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton yarn from Kumbakonam (Tamil Nadu, 250km away) or Mumbai wholesale markets via established handloom input suppliers in Thiruvananthapuram's Statue junction area. First batch of 10 sarees requires ₹40,000-60,000 for yarn, dyes, and thread. Connect with Handloom Federation suppliers listed on Kerala Handloom Development Corporation website.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner with existing weaver groups (no investment, 40% revenue share, 4-6 week lead time per batch). Option B: Rent 800-1200 sq ft unit in Thiruvananthapuram MSME cluster at ₹3000-5000/month, hire 2-3 weavers at ₹8-12K/month each. Initial 4-loom setup: ₹2-3L. Go with partnership model first to test product-market fit.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 10 mins online), apply for PM Vishwakarma scheme (₹15,000 credit available per artisan), GI registration not required but helps premium positioning. Create basic QR-coded label with fiber content + care instructions. Open current account at SBI/Federal Bank (required for MUDRA loan if scaling). Timeline: 2-3 weeks.
5
First Sale
First sales via Instagram/Facebook posts with product photos + behind-the-scenes weaving videos (free, target diaspora in US/Canada). Target first bulk buyer: local wedding planner or retail shop in Thiruvananthapuram offering 10-15 pieces. Expected first order: ₹50-75K. First month realistic revenue: ₹60-100K from 10-15 sarees if 3 orders secured.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month (80-100 sarees/month), hire operational manager (₹15-20K), formalize with 3-4 dedicated weavers, apply for MUDRA loan (₹1-2L at 8% interest), exhibit at Handloom Expo Thiruvananthapuram (annual), register for export (IEC code via GeM portal, ₹500), target bulk exports to US wholesalers via LinkedIn outreach.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 credit per artisan, loan at 5% subsidy for first 2 years, training and toolkit support
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹20 lakh cluster grants, working capital support, product development funds for registered handloom clusters
🏚
Kerala State Handlooms Development Corporation Subsidy
20-30% subsidy on loom purchase, 15% subsidy on raw material, priority procurement for govt events
🏚
Kerala Artisan Welfare Fund
₹5000 annual stipend to registered weavers, health insurance coverage, pension eligibility after 5 years
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
