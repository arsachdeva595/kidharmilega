Thiruvananthapuram is the capital and cultural heart of Kerala with a strong heritage of cotton weaving traditions. The district has established handloom clusters with access to skilled artisans trained in traditional techniques passed down through generations. The natural availability of cotton and cultural affinity for fine fabrics makes it the epicenter of Kerala's handloom industry.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Thiruvananthapuram ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Thiruvananthapuram Handloom Weavers' Cooperative Society and Napier Museum's textile section; spend 2-3 weeks observing production at Kanjoor cluster and Pattom weaving communities; attend Kerala Handloom Expo (annual event) to understand market dynamics and connect with existing players
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton yarn from Coir Board approved suppliers in Thiruvananthapuram or Kochi (₹2,500-4,000/kg); natural dyes from local Ayurvedic suppliers; zari from authorized dealers in Ernakulathappon Street, Kochi; initial batch of 50 kg cotton yarn + dyes = ₹1,50,000-2,00,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered weaver collectives in Pattom or Kanjoor through Kerala Handloom Development Corporation (KHDC); negotiate per-piece production cost (₹800-2,500 per saree depending on complexity); ensure Udyam registration for cooperative to access subsidized raw materials
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (Micro/Small Business classification); apply for IEC code through Directorate of Foreign Trade; register brand name with Registrar of Trademarks; get Handloom Mark certification from Ministry of Textiles; create GST registration with 5% applicable tax for handloom products
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram/Facebook with behind-the-scenes weaver stories and process videos; list 20-30 signature designs on Amazon Handmade India and Flipkart Sarees; reach out to luxury ethnic wear retailers like Biba, Fabindia, and local boutiques in major metros; create WhatsApp business catalog for direct orders; participate in Kerala Handloom Expo and craft fairs
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product line to include dhotis, dress materials, and home textiles; establish showroom in Kochi or Bangalore; hire dedicated export logistics manager; apply for APEDA certification for organic cotton products; build corporate gifting B2B channel; collaborate with fashion influencers on Instagram for authentic storytelling
