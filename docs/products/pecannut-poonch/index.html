Pecannut ki
opportunity, Poonch mein.

Poonch district lies in the Himalayas at elevation suitable for pecan (Caryaillinoensis) cultivation, where apple and walnut cannot thrive. The seven-cluster zone (Poonch, Rajouri, Kishtwar, Doda, Ramban, Udhampur, Kathua) currently produces 75 MT/year on 507 ha, with many newly planted orchards coming into commercial yield within 3-4 years. Raw materials—quality saplings and seeds—are sourced from registered J&K nurseries; end buyers include Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore premium snack retailers, plus international markets in North America and Europe.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Poonch ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Call the Poonch District Agriculture Office (0194-2441890) and ask for list of registered pecan nurseries in Rajouri and Poonch blocks. Visit one active pecan orchard in Rajouri town (free, no cost). Attend the weekly Poonch mandi on Friday morning to observe pecan prices and buyer behavior. Join the Poonch ODOP WhatsApp group (ask block development office). Zero investment required—doable this week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Buy 500-1000 grafted pecan saplings from Rajouri District Nursery (₹40-60/sapling, ₹25,000-50,000 total) or private registered nurseries listed by Agriculture Dept. Procurement takes 2-3 weeks. Request 1-2 year old saplings for faster yield. Supplier areas: Rajouri town, Sunderbani block—45 km from Poonch city.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Lease 2-3 hectares in Poonch/Rajouri at ₹500-1000/month + one-time land prep (₹30,000-50,000). Plant 150-200 saplings per hectare. Total first-year cost: ₹80,000-120,000. Option B: Partner with 5-10 existing small farmers via contract farming model—you provide saplings and inputs, they provide land and labor (revenue-share 60:40). Orchard comes to commercial yield in 3-4 years (60-80 kg/tree). Maintenance: ₹15,000-20,000/ha/year.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, 10 min on udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for GI registration as part of Poonch Pecan Cluster through District ODOP committee (₹0, government-assisted—process ongoing). If exporting: Get IEC code from DGFT office in Jammu (₹500, 7 days). Get Food Safety License (FSSAI) from J&K Food Authority (₹500-2000, 15 days). Total timeline: 4-6 weeks, ₹1000-2500 total.
5
First Sale
Week 1: List on BigBasket Seller, Udaan, or Jumbostores with 10 kg initial stock. Price at ₹280/kg (vs. competitor California pecans at ₹400+). Target first order: 50 kg from a bakery chain in Srinagar or premium snack retailer. Expected first month revenue: ₹12,000-18,000 (assuming 50-75 kg sold). Use WhatsApp to reach out to 20 local restaurants in Srinagar, Jammu offering ₹150/kg bulk rate.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue (≈400 kg sales): Hire 1-2 processing staff for roasting/packing. Set up a 500 sq.ft. processing unit in Poonch town (₹40,000/month rent). Invest in small roaster (₹150,000), packaging machine (₹100,000). Apply for SFURTI cluster subsidy (30% capex support). Attend AAFED trade shows (Delhi, Mumbai) to land institutional and export orders. Build a brand website (₹5000) and run Instagram ads targeting health-conscious urban buyers. Expand to 10+ retail locations in Jammu and Srinagar.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM-KISAN Scheme
Direct cash transfer of ₹2000 per four-monthly installment to registered farmers.
🏛
PMEGP (Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Program)
₹25 lakh loan at 5% interest with 25% subsidy (SC/ST up to 35%) for processing unit setup.
🏚
J&K Horticulture Department Pecan Cultivation Subsidy
50% subsidy on saplings and inputs for first 2 years (up to ₹50,000/ha), currently under ODOP expansion.
🏚
SFURTI Scheme (J&K cluster component)
30% subsidy on equipment + shared infrastructure for pecan processing units in Poonch/Rajouri clusters.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
