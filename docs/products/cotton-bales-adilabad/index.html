Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Adilabad ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Adilabad cotton mandi (Adilabad Market Yard) and Nirmal ginning clusters; connect with Adilabad Cotton Growers Association; study cotton grading standards (IS 6644) and export quality benchmarks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw seed cotton directly from farmers in Adilabad taluk (October-January) at ₹45-55 per kg. First-batch requirement: 100 tons (₹45-55 lakhs). Partner with local ginning mills or lease ginning capacity at ₹2000-3000 per ton processed.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish a cotton ginning and baling unit (₹25-40 lakhs for machinery) or partner with existing ginners in Adilabad town. Employ 8-12 semi-skilled workers. Process 1-2 tons daily to start. Get machinery from suppliers in Aurangabad or Ujjain.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (https://udyamregistration.gov.in) as agro-processing. Obtain IEC code from DGFT for exports. Get APEDA registration for agricultural exports. Obtain ginning unit license from district textile authority. Consider ISO 9001 certification for premium positioning.
5
First Sale
List on TradeKey and Global Sources (B2B export platforms); approach textile mills via Adilabad Chamber of Commerce; partner with cotton trading houses like Jayshree Cotton and DCM Textiles; target D2C via textile marketplaces (Indigo, IGATEX). Attend India International Cotton Expo and regional textile trade shows.
6
Scale
Expand to 300+ tons annual capacity by Year 2; develop organic cotton certification for 20-30% premium pricing; establish forward contracts with 5-10 mill accounts; open satellite collection centers in Nirmal and Karimnagar; explore contract farming arrangements with 500+ farmers.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan subsidy (35% for general category) for textile processing units.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
support for cotton-based clusters with grants up to ₹3 crore for common facility centers.
🏚
Telangana Agri-Export Promotion Scheme
5-10% subsidy on export freight and certification costs for agricultural exports above ₹1 crore annually.
🏚
Telangana Textile Processing Cluster Scheme
capital subsidy of 20-30% for ginning and spinning unit setup in designated textile zones.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
