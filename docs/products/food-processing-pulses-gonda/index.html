Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Gonda ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gonda town's pulse mill cluster (Rajendra Nagar area) and Mankapur SFURTI cluster; interview 10+ existing mill operators on machinery, labor, sourcing. Attend Indian Pulses & Cereals Association meetings in Delhi. Study food safety compliance (FSSAI) requirements for packaged food.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw lentils from Gonda Agricultural Produce Market (APM) or direct farmers in Gonda/Uttar Pradeshpur. Budget ₹8–12 lakhs for first 50-tonne batch. Contact suppliers: Gonda Agricultural Department (0517-2233555) for government mandis list.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established mil operators in Gonda town for contract processing (sorting, cleaning, dehulling) at ₹2–4 per kg. Alternatively, lease/buy used dal mill machinery (₹18–25 lakhs) and hire 2–3 skilled operators from existing mills. Start with 2–3 tonnes/day capacity.
4
Brand & Register
Register for Udyam (micro/small enterprise) via udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain FSSAI food manufacturing license (₹12,000–20,000 application fee). Import-Export Code (IEC) from DGFT for export eligibility. Trademark brand name with IP India (₹9,000–15,000). Get ISO 22000 food safety certification within 6 months.
5
First Sale
Launch on Blinkit, Zepto, BigBasket with 2–3 SKUs (500g, 1kg, 5kg packs). Pitch to regional wholesalers (Metro Wholesale, Spencer Wholesale) in Lucknow/Kanpur. Register with ONDC (Open Network) for B2B marketplace visibility. Target government tender portals (GeM) for mid-day meal scheme contracts.
6
Scale
After ₹50 L revenue, upgrade to 10-tonne/day capacity with additional machinery. Launch 2–3 organic or specialty variants (organic moong, sprouted chana). Hire sales team for B2B regional expansion (UP, MP, Bihar). Begin export trials to UK/Canada via distributors. Pursue PMEGP subsidy for capacity expansion.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 L grant (35% subsidy for general, 45% for SC/ST) for machinery & working capital in micro-enterprises.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster development support, common facility centers, training, and up to ₹2.5 Cr for artisan groups.
🏚
UP Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises Policy 2023
10% subsidy on machinery, land lease waiver for 5 years, and GST refund for registered units.
🏚
UP Food Processing Sector Scheme
₹5–10 L capital subsidy for FSSAI-licensed food units + interest subvention on term loans.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
