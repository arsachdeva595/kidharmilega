opportunity, Darrang mein.

Darrang district's 1850.58 sq km Brahmaputra valley terrain produces brinjal, bottle gourd, tomato, and cabbage across two active clusters: Kharupetia and Besimari. The district benefits from seasonal monsoon irrigation and proximity to Guwahati (80km), which hosts 2M+ urban vegetable buyers. Local farmer base cultivates over 40,000 hectares of vegetables annually with documented supply chain efficiency, making farmer-to-buyer logistics cost-effective within 2-3 hour delivery radius.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Darrang ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kharupetia and Besimari clusters on Tuesday-Thursday morning (sabzi haat timing); talk to 3-4 active vegetable sellers about their sourcing, pricing, and seasonal challenges (₹0 cost, 4 hours). Call Darrang Agriculture Office (Mangaldoi) and ask for cluster coordinator contact. Free.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source saplings and seeds from local nurseries in Kharupetia (₹3-8k per acre, seasonal rate). For first-batch produce: partner with 2-3 established farmers in Besimari cluster (₹25-40k for 2-3 tons mixed vegetables weekly). Negotiate 7-day payment terms. Supplier contacts available through district ATMA office.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Rent 0.5-1 acre agricultural land in Kharupetia for ₹8-15k/month + ₹35-45k seasonal cultivation cost (seeds, fertilizer, labor). Option B: Direct partnership with 3-5 existing farmers in Besimari, negotiate 60-70% of sale price for produce delivery to you (₹0 land cost, ₹8-12k commission/week). Small collection unit (shed + cool storage) costs ₹75-150k setup. Most founders start with Option B.
4
Brand & Register
Free Udyam registration online (udyamregistration.gov.in, 15 mins). GI registration not applicable for raw vegetables but register under Agricultural Produce Business Standard with district APEDA (₹5-8k, 4-6 weeks). For export: IEC registration via DGFT online (₹500, 2-3 weeks) and APEDA registration (₹8-10k, 3-4 weeks). Total brand setup: ₹15-25k + 6-8 weeks.
5
First Sale
First sale: approach local BigBasket/Grofers warehouse manager in Guwahati with sample + Udyam cert (requires passing food safety audit ₹8-12k). Parallel: sell 200-300kg weekly to Kharupetia sabzi haat directly (no approval needed). First 30 days target: ₹35-55k (haat sales) + ₹12-18k (app-based orders if approved). Most starters hit ₹40-70k first month.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time supply coordinator (₹12-15k/month) and 2 part-time delivery agents. Invest ₹80-120k in cold storage expansion (2-ton capacity). Apply for SFURTI cluster development subsidy (₹20-30L for collective storage infrastructure, 40% cost subsidy). Exhibit at North-East Horticulture Fair (annual, Guwahati) to attract bulk institutional buyers. Negotiate exclusive supply contracts with 3-5 premium restaurants/catering.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PM-KISAN: ₹6000/year direct cash transfer for farmer landholders (you qualify if owning/leasing land); register at pmkisan.gov.in
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries): ₹20-30L cluster subsidy for vegetable storage + processing infrastructure, 40% cost subsidy for groups of 25+ farmers/traders
🏚
Assam Agricultural Marketing Board subsidy: 25% subsidy on cold storage units up to ₹2L investment; apply via district agriculture office
🏚
Assam ATMA (Agricultural Technology Management Agency) free capacity building: monthly training on vegetable cultivation, storage, food safety

SFURTI scheme deta hai ₹20-30L subsidy (40% cost subsidy) group cold storage ke liye. PM-KISAN deta hai ₹6000/saal. Assam Agricultural Marketing Board deta hai 25% subsidy cold storage unit pe (max ₹2L investment). Sab free training ATMA se. Total possible subsidy: ₹8-12L if eligible.
Local Kharupetia sabzi haat mein directly bech sakte ho (no approval needed), ₹35-50k/month se start. Parallel: BigBasket/Grofers ke liye apply karo (1-2 weeks approval), fir ₹12-18k/week orders. Hotels aur restaurants ko directly call karo (phone number ATMA se pata chal jayega) — institutional buyers ready hote hain.
Haan, Bangladesh/Nepal/Bhutan ko export kar sakte ho. But require hogi: IEC registration (₹500, 2-3 weeks), APEDA registration (₹8-10k, 3-4 weeks), food safety certificate (₹8-12k). Margin better hai (40-50% FOB) lekin minimum 500kg per shipment required, aur payment 30-45 days baad milta hai. Pehle domestic mein establish ho ja, fir export kar.

Ek din ka perishable product hai toh stuck inventory nahi hoga. Sabse badi risk: if you can't handle weather-dependent crop failure — toh ye business nahi hai.
