Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Baksa ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Baksa honey clusters around Mushalpur and Bajali blocks; connect with existing beekeepers through District Agriculture Office, Baksa; study Assam Horticulture Mission data on wildflower availability; visit apiary units in Nagaon district for comparative learning.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 5-10 bee colonies from registered apiary suppliers in Assam (cost ₹2000-3000 per colony); procure beekeeping equipment from suppliers in Guwahati (extractors, smokers, protective gear — approx. ₹150,000 for initial setup); negotiate bulk packaging (glass jars, labels) from suppliers in Kolkata or Bangalore for ₹50,000 first batch.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with 5-10 bee colonies on leased agricultural land in Baksa; partner with existing local beekeepers for knowledge transfer; establish honey extraction and processing facility with basic equipment (honey extractor, settling tanks, bottling setup); obtain food safety training from FSSAI-approved centers in Guwahati.
4
Brand & Register
Register MSME Udyam online (udyamregistration.gov.in); obtain IEC code from DGFT for export potential; register with FSSAI for food safety compliance; trademark brand name with IP India; get organic certification from NPOP-accredited certifier (NGO Samuchit or similar) — process takes 3-4 months; create packaging with GI-inspired storytelling (mention Assam provenance, wildflower origin).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and Flipkart with premium positioning (₹400-600/kg); create Instagram and Facebook pages with content on beekeeping process and health benefits; reach out to premium organic retailers (Organic Brands, Nature's Basket) in metros with samples; join APEDA export database for B2B buyer connections; attend Aahar or similar trade shows.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand bee colonies to 25-50 units; develop co-packing partnerships with other ODOP honey producers to meet bulk order demand; launch value-added line (turmeric honey, ginger honey); establish direct export relationships with EU importers via Alibaba or Trade Key; create institutional B2B contracts with hotels, bakeries, food manufacturers; consider FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) model to aggregate 10-15 local beekeepers for larger scale.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for MSME setup in honey production.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹10 lakh for cluster-based beekeeping units with skill development.
🏚
Assam Agriculture Mechanization Scheme
50% subsidy on beekeeping equipment purchase up to ₹2 lakh per unit.
🏚
Assam Organic Farming Mission
certification cost reimbursement up to ₹50,000 for producers pursuing organic/natural honey production.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
