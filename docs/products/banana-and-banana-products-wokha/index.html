Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Wokha ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Wokha farmer cooperatives like Wokha Organic Farmers Association and connect with existing processors at Wokha market. Study banana processing clusters in Tamil Nadu (Cauvery) and Kerala for best practices. Attend AgriTech expos in Delhi to understand modern processing equipment trends.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh bananas directly from Wokha farmer groups (₹8-12/kg seasonal pricing). First batch of 500kg costs ₹4,500-6,000. Connect with Nagaland Agricultural Department for certified organic farmer lists. Establish 3-4 farmer contracts for consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing small-scale processors in Wokha town or establish semi-mechanized unit (5,000-10,000 units/month capacity). Use solar drying for chips and mechanical flour mills. Partner with Wokha cooperative for shared cold storage (reduces capex by 40%).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME, obtain FSSAI license (₹2,000-5,000), apply for IEC code. Create brand identity emphasizing 'Wokha Organic' and 'North-East Natural' narrative. Register with Nagaland ODOP portal for government support access.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and Blink under 'Organic' category with 20% introductory discounts. Approach Modern Trade chains (Spencer's, Nature's Basket, Organic Stores). Send samples to bulk buyers in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai. Build Instagram/Facebook presence with farmer story content.
6
Scale
Expand product range to banana bread mix, banana jaggery, and dehydrated banana snacks. Secure distribution in 15-20 modern trade outlets by month 9. Establish export partnerships with Singapore and Japan importers by month 12. Build processing capacity to 25,000 units/month.
