opportunity, West Siang mein.

West Siang district spans 1,661.27 sq km across 5 blocks in Arunachal Pradesh's hilly terrain with 56,503 residents distributed across 180 villages, creating dispersed raw material access. The district's cool, humid climate and well-drained soil are naturally suited to premium pineapple cultivation with existing farmer networks ready for contract farming. Guwahati (200 km via road) serves as the primary distribution hub; Kolkata is reachable via NH 37, enabling access to eastern India's ₹18.2B global pineapple market segment.

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — West Siang ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit local mandis in Aalo and Kaying blocks this week (no cost); call 3-5 pineapple farmers through Arunachal Pradesh Agriculture Department hotline; attend the weekly Aalo farmers haat on Wednesday mornings; register free on KRISHIVERSE app to check local fruit prices and demand patterns. Target: understand current farm-gate price (₹15-25/kg) and existing supply chains.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh pineapples directly from Aalo (block HQ) and Kaying village clusters during peak season (March-September). First batch: 2-3 tonnes costs ₹30,000-45,000 from farmer collectives. Negotiate with block-level fruit cooperative societies (contact: Deputy Director, Horticulture, West Siang) for bulk supply at ₹18-22/kg.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 800-1000 sq ft cold storage + processing unit in Aalo town (₹8,000-12,000/month) or partner with existing juice manufacturers already operating there. Investment: ₹2.5L (refrigeration unit ₹1.5L + washing/cutting equipment ₹60K + packaging ₹40K). Alternative: Begin with contract processing at existing units (₹2-5/kg processing fee) with zero capex.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online at udyamregistration.gov.in, 5 minutes). Apply for APEDA Export Registration (₹0, process online). File GI registration petition under Ministry of Agriculture through IP India office in New Delhi (cost ₹5,000-8,000, timeline 18-24 months) if branding for premium export. Obtain FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) registration for ₹10,000-15,000 if sourcing from farmer groups.
5
First Sale
Day 1 sale target: Post 20 kg of premium pineapples on Udyam vendor platform, contact 5 local fruit wholesalers in Aalo for ₹35/kg orders. First institutional sale: call district hotels and restaurant chains in Itanagar (50 km away) with sample delivery. Expected first-month revenue: ₹40,000-60,000 (assuming 1.5-2 tonnes sold at ₹40-50/kg wholesale average).
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 2-3 full-time packers + 1 delivery manager (₹40K/month total). Invest ₹3L in additional cold storage to store 10 tonnes. Register on APEDA export network, target 15% of revenue from Bangladesh/Sri Lanka markets. Exhibit at North-East India Food & Beverage Expo (annual, Guwahati) and Arunachal Pradesh State Agricultural Fair (held in Itanagar each October) to secure hotel and institutional bulk contracts.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
Pradhan Mantri Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency (MUDRA) Loan
up to ₹10 lakh at 0% interest subsidy for first year; apply at nearest Bank of India or State Bank branch
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 first installment + ₹3,500/month stipend for 18 months + 5% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹2 lakh for agricultural processing MSME
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Agricultural Mechanization Subsidy
50% subsidy on cold storage equipment for agricultural entrepreneurs; apply through District Horticulture Office, West Siang
🏚
North-East Region Textile Promotion Scheme (adaptable for food processing units)
capital subsidy up to ₹25 lakh for food processing MSMEs in NE region; contact State Industries Department
