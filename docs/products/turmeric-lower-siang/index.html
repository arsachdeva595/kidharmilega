Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Lower Siang ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit turmeric growing clusters in Lower Siang (Pangin, Yingkiong villages) and connect with local farmer collectives. Study premium turmeric markets at Cochin Spice Exchange and Sangli Turmeric Market in Maharashtra. Research organic certification standards (NPOP/Jaivik Bharat) online.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 100-200 kg turmeric rhizomes (₹60,000-80,000) from certified organic farmers in Lower Siang via district agricultural department. Negotiate bulk discounts for seasonal harvesting (October-November). First-batch cost: ₹1.2-1.8 Lakhs including drying, storage, basic packaging.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing farmer cooperatives in Lower Siang (e.g., Lower Siang Farmers Collective) for cultivation on contract basis, or lease 0.5-1 acre land locally. Establish small drying and grading facility (₹3-5 Lakhs). Hire 2-3 local workers during harvest/processing. Use traditional sun-drying methods to retain curcumin.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME registration) with district MSME office; cost ₹0 (online). Apply for IEC (Import-Export Code) via DGFT portal (₹0). Get organic certification from APEDA-accredited certifier (₹40,000-80,000, 6-month validity). Register brand name at IPAB. Apply for FSSAI license (₹5,000-10,000).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade, Flipkart Seller, and Organic Brands marketplace with 500g premium packs (₹350-500 per pack). Approach Himalaya Herbals, Jiva Ayurveda, and spice exporters in Cochin for bulk contracts. Attend Gourmet Food Trade Show in Delhi (January). Cold email pharmaceutical buyers via TradeKey and Global Sources.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 2-3 acres cultivation. Launch value-added turmeric milk and capsule variants. Build B2B supply contracts with 5-10 organic food brands. Pursue organic certification premium pricing (20-30% markup). Explore direct exports via APEDA schemes. Invest in digital marketing: Instagram storytelling of farm-to-table journey.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakhs subsidy for processing/value-addition, 25% subsidy for general category entrepreneurs
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
support for agricultural cluster development, up to ₹50 Cr for processing infrastructure
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh State Agriculture Policy 2022
25% subsidy on farm mechanization, organic certification support, zero-interest crop loans via APL farmers
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh MSME Support Scheme
5% interest subsidy on term loans up to ₹50 Lakhs, marketing assistance for exportable products
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
