Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Barabanki ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Barabanki weaving clusters in Amaniyah, Chulla Ganj, and Balapur. Spend 2-3 weeks learning from established weavers. Attend Handloom Expo at regional textile fairs. Connect with Barabanki Handloom Association to understand quality standards, pricing, and GI compliance requirements.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium cotton and silk yarn from Varanasi yarn suppliers (₹8-15/meter cost) and natural dyes from Indore dye markets. Contact 3-4 artisan groups for first sample batch (2000-5000 meters). First-batch raw material cost: ₹80,000-150,000 for 50 premium sarees.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 5-10 master weavers in Barabanki clusters through Handloom Board liaison. Establish payment terms (50% advance, 50% on delivery). Quality check each piece against GI standards. Consider joining a registered handloom cooperative for better pricing and collective marketing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (free, 5 mins). Apply for IEC from DGFT portal for export eligibility. Register brand name at registrartrademarks.ipo.gov.in (₹4,500-9,000). Get GI usage certification from Ministry of Textiles. Obtain Quality Mark certification from Barabanki handloom authorities.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade, Meesho (artisan category), and Flipkart. Create Instagram shop showcasing artisan stories and weaving process. Approach boutique operators in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore. Participate in India Handloom Expo 2024. Email international fashion retailers with product catalog and GI certification.
6
Scale
After ₹20L first-year revenue, expand product lines (bed linens, curtains, fusion wear). Hire 2-3 full-time quality managers. Apply for SFURTI cluster development grant for production facility upgrade. Build B2B export partnerships through APEDA. Develop 3-4 seasonal collections. Collaborate with fashion designers for limited editions.
Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme) – up to ₹25 lakh loan with 25% subsidy for women entrepreneurs, 35% for SC/ST categories
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana – toolkit grant of ₹15,000 per artisan, credit up to ₹3 lakh at 5% interest subsidy, marketing support
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Handloom Policy 2023 – Subsidy of 25% on electricity costs, concessional credit from cooperative banks, export incentives of 5% for GI products
🏚
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries) – up to ₹50 lakh cluster development grant for handloom clusters in Barabanki
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
