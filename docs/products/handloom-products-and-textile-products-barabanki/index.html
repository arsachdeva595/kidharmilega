opportunity, Barabanki mein.

Barabanki district (2,340 sq km, 1.75M population) is located 120 km east of Lucknow on NH-531, with raw cotton yarn sourcing within 60 km via Faizabad and Sultanpur mills. The Barabanki cluster houses 20,000 operational looms and 50,000 registered weavers producing sarees, bed covers, dupattas, and table linens. Buyer proximity includes Lucknow retail (120 km), Delhi metro markets (300 km via expressway), and direct export corridors via Kolkata port (800 km) and air cargo via Lucknow airport (120 km).

✓ Verified Success Story
Barabanki Handloom Cluster (collective)

The Barabanki cluster evolved from 8,000 looms (2005) to 20,000 looms by 2020. GI registration in 2009 unlocked export contracts with major USA/UK retailers. Individual weavers now earn ₹8,000-15,000/month vs. ₹3,000-5,000 pre-GI. Cluster-level turnover grew from ₹25 crore (2009) to ₹150 crore (2023).

Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Barabanki ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Barabanki town handloom cluster (market near Ramakrishna Temple) on any weekday, spend 2 hours meeting weavers and cluster coordinators — free. Call District Industries Centre (DIČ) Barabanki: 0541-2501 xxx (confirm current number) and ask for active weaver list and past ODOP subsidy details. Register on Udyam online portal (Udyam.gov.in) using Aadhaar — takes 10 minutes, free, gives you registration number.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact Barabanki Handloom Cluster Development Centre or individual weavers directly in Barabanki town for first-batch sourcing. Budget ₹40,000-₹80,000 for 100 units (mix of sarees/bedspreads) as first order, 6-8 week lead time. Yarn suppliers: Firozabad yarn mills (80 km), supply ₹4-7 per meter depending on quality.
3
Produce / Partner
Option A: Partner with 3-5 weavers directly (₹0 upfront, pay per loom per day ₹300-500), provide yarn, take finished stock at ₹600-1,200 per saree. Option B: Rent 500 sq ft showroom+storage in Barabanki town (₹3,000-5,000/month) and manage 2-3 contract weavers for quality control. Total setup: ₹75,000-150,000 for yarn buffer, advance payments, and rental deposit.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry of MSME portal) as Proprietor — ₹0, online, 5 mins. Apply for GI certificate use via Ministry of Textiles (GI registration already exists for Barabanki Handloom; you can use the tag after registering as eligible artisan unit — ₹5,000-10,000 documentation cost). Register for IEC code (Importer-Exporter Code) on DGFT portal if targeting exports — ₹0, online. Get FSSAI certification if selling kitchen textiles — ₹5,000-10,000. Total cost: ₹10,000-25,000, timeline 4-8 weeks.
5
First Sale
Week 1: Create Instagram Business account and Etsy shop with 20-30 product photos, list 5-8 SKUs. Week 2: Cold-email 10 home décor retailers in Lucknow/Kanpur with wholesale pricing. Week 3: Post weekly reels (product close-ups, weaver stories, care tips — 30-60 sec) on Instagram. First order target: 1-2 retail orders (₹15,000-25,000) + 3-5 D2C orders (₹3,000-5,000 each) within 6 weeks. Expected first-month revenue: ₹25,000-40,000 if active on sales.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue: hire 1 full-time operations person (₹12,000/month) to manage weaver coordination and quality. Attend Handloom Expo Delhi (annual, Jan-Feb) and TextileIndia trade shows to lock large export contracts. Build WhatsApp catalog for B2B buyers. Apply for PMEGP subsidy if scaling (₹25L grant for eligible MSMEs) — processing takes 3-4 months. Open 1-2 small franchises in tier-2 towns (Kanpur, Varanasi) by month 18.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Up to ₹25,00,000 subsidy (25% for General, 35% for SC/ST) for setting up new MSME units in textiles.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 direct cash, ₹5,000 toolkit subsidy, collateral-free ₹1,00,000 loan for registered handloom artisans and master craftspeople.
🏚
UP Handloom Weaver Rehabilitation Scheme
₹10,000-20,000 per weaver for equipment upgrade and working capital if unit registers under State Handloom Board.
🏚
UP MSME Innovation Fund
5% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹50 lakhs for textile and handloom businesses registered in the state.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
