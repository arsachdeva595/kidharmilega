Step-by-Step Roadmap

Zero se pehli sale tak — actual kaam

Har step realistic hai — Mahisagar ke liye, as is.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mahisagar district papaya haat at Lunawada (Tuesdays 6-10 AM) to talk to 10-15 farmer-suppliers directly about yield, pricing, and variety sourcing. Call 3 existing papaya processors in Vadodara (numbers available from District Industries Centre) and ask about their supply models. Register free on UDYAM portal using Aadhaar (https://udyamregistration.gov.in) — takes 15 minutes.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw papaya from Lunawada-Vijapur cluster (35 km from district HQ), where 400+ smallholder farmers operate. First batch: 2 tons Red Lady papaya at ₹8-12/kg = ₹16,000-24,000. Negotiate directly with farmer cooperatives like Mahisagar Growers Association or through licensed dalals charging 5-7% commission.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent a 500-600 sq ft unit in Vadodara Food Processing Park (₹8,000-12,000/month) equipped with basic sterilization, pulping machine (₹80,000-1,20,000), pasteurizer (₹40,000-60,000), and cold storage. Alternatively, partner with an existing micro-unit owner for co-processing at ₹3-5 per kg. Total setup: ₹3.5-15L including equipment, FSS registration, and 3-month working capital.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free, online). Apply for GI certification for "Mahisagar Papaya Products" through ODOP scheme (₹15,000-25,000 filing cost, 18-24 month process). Obtain FSSAI registration (₹2,000 for new unit), and if exporting, register IEC code with DGFT Ahmedabad (₹500 fee, 7-10 days). Total non-product cost: ₹18,500-27,500.
5
First Sale
First sale: list papaya jam (200g jar) on Blinkit Merchant Portal or apply to BigBasket vendor program (7-10 day approval). Target 50-100 jars in first month at ₹180 MRP (₹110 wholesale cost, ₹70 margin). Alternatively, approach ITC Rajnigandha sourcing team directly with 10 kg sample batch (₹5,000-8,000 cost). Expected first-month revenue: ₹5,500-12,000 net.
6
Scale
At ₹10L/month revenue, move to 1,200 sq ft unit, hire 1 quality manager (₹18,000/month) and 2 production workers (₹12,000/month each). Apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹25L limit, 25-35% subsidy for SC/ST, 15-25% for general) to fund second production line. Exhibit at IITF Delhi (October) and Gulfood Dubai (February) with ₹2-3L budget to lock export contracts.

Government Schemes

Woh paisa jo tujhe pata nahi tha

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh credit at 0% interest for food processing units, with 25-35% subsidy for SC/ST and 15-25% for general category founders.
🏛
Credit Guarantee Scheme (CGT)
75-80% credit guarantee on bank loans up to ₹50 lakh for MSME food processing businesses, enabling easier lending access.
🏚
Gujarat Employment of Youth Scheme
direct subsidy of ₹2 lakh to ₹10 lakh for first-time entrepreneurs in food processing, plus 5-year property tax exemption.
🏚
Gujarat Food Processing Cluster Development Scheme
50% grant on machinery cost (max ₹25 lakh) and infrastructure development for clusters in designated food processing zones.
Pehla step: udyamregistration.gov.in — free, 10 minute. Bina iske koi scheme apply nahi hogi.
