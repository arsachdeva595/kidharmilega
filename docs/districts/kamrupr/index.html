Kamrup Rural has ideal tropical climate with 2200mm annual rainfall and alluvial soil from Brahmaputra plains, creating perfect conditions for banana cultivation. The district has over 15,000 hectares under banana cultivation with established farming communities and supply chains to Guwahati and Northeast markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kamrup(R).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kamrup (R) banana cultivation clusters around Kampur, Dekargaon, and Guwahati suburbs; meet 5-10 established banana farmers; study processing units in Kamrup district and Barpeta; visit fruit markets at Fancy Bazaar and Paltan Bazaar in Guwahati.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source banana saplings from Assam Agricultural Department nurseries (₹5-10 per sapling); partner with 2-3 established farmers for first batch of 500-1000 bunches; negotiate ₹15-20 per kg wholesale cost; first 3-month raw material cost ₹1-1.5L for processing venture.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with contract farming model partnering 3-4 farmers in Kamrup (R); establish small processing unit (1500-2000 sqft) for banana chips and powder making near Guwahati bypass; invest in food-grade drying, packaging equipment (₹8-12L); hire 4-5 skilled workers.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (Free); apply for IEC code via DGFT portal; get FSSAI license (₹12,000 for small scale); trademark brand name at Indian IP office; obtain state GST registration; get organic certification if sourcing organic bananas (₹15-25K annually).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, Blinkit, Zepto with WhatsApp direct ordering via status; partner with 2-3 premium grocery chains in Assam and Meghalaya; sell fresh bunches via local vegetable markets on weekends; create Instagram content showing farm-to-table story; supply 5-10 hotels/bakeries in Guwahati.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to value-added products (banana bread, smoothie mix, chips in multiple flavors); open presence in Bangalore, Hyderabad metros via Tasty Nuts, 1mg wellness platforms; establish co-packing partnerships for larger orders; build B2B contracts with institutional kitchens in 3-4 states; apply for PMEGP subsidy to scale processing capacity.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showing banana farm journey, harvesting process, and recipe videos; partner with micro-influencers in Assam and Northeast (10K-50K followers) for authentic storytelling; target health & wellness audience aged 25-45.
  • WhatsApp community engagement via Status updates offering bulk discounts for weekly orders; create WhatsApp Business catalog showcasing fresh, chips, and powder variants; offer referral discount (₹100 off per friend) for local Guwahati orders.
  • Sampling campaign at 5-6 premium grocery stores (Safal, Walmart) in Guwahati and Assam; sponsor local nutrition workshops at gynecology clinics and mother-child health centers; create educational content on banana health benefits via YouTube Shorts.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for processing unit setup with 35% margin for SC/ST, food processing sector priority.
🏛
MUDRA Loan
up to ₹10L unsecured loan for small agri-business ventures via registered banks in Assam.
🏚
Assam Agricultural Mechanisation Scheme
subsidies up to 50% on agricultural processing equipment like banana chip fryers and grinders.
🏚
Assam State Agricultural Export Scheme
support for export-oriented value-added agri-products with APEDA certification.
