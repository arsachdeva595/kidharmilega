ODOP Product

Jackfruit

Surguja's tropical climate, abundant rainfall (1400-1600mm annually), and rich forest ecosystem create ideal conditions for jackfruit cultivation. The district's established agricultural infrastructure and skilled farming communities have traditionally grown jackfruit for generations, making it naturally suited for value-added jackfruit processing and export.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Surguja.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Surguja Agricultural Produce Marketing Committee (APMC) in Manendragarh; connect with jackfruit farmers in Surguja cluster; study processing units in Chhattisgarh like those near Raipur; research Southeast Asian jackfruit processing methods through industry visits.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh jackfruit directly from Surguja farmers (June-September peak season) at ₹8-15/kg from local mandis; establish relationships with 5-10 farmers for consistent supply; first batch of 2 tonnes costs approximately ₹20,000-30,000; negotiate contracts for seasonal procurement.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing co-operative processing units in Surguja (check SFURTI clusters); lease processing space with cold storage in Manendragarh or Ambikapur for ₹15,000-25,000/month; hire trained processors; start with simple drying and packaging, then scale to canning; consider contract manufacturing with certified food units initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (aadhaar-based, free online); apply for IEC code from DGFT office in Raipur (10-15 days); obtain FSSAI food business license (category 2-3, cost ₹5,000-12,000); trademark brand name with IP India; get export certification for target countries.
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Fresh, Blinkit, and JioMart with minimum ₹50K inventory; approach regional supermarkets (D-Mart, Spencer's) with samples; sell B2B to food manufacturers and restaurants via LinkedIn/industry networks; launch Instagram/Facebook with farm visuals and recipes; start with ₹1.5L marketing budget across digital channels.
6
Scale
Expand to organic/certified production after 6 months; launch 3-4 product variants (chips, pulp, canned); establish direct export partnerships with importers in Bangladesh and Nepal; build B2B relationships with food chains; invest in cold chain logistics; consider contract farming model to increase supply.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels showing jackfruit farm-to-table journey in Surguja; partner with 10-15 micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) focused on healthy eating and regional products; budget ₹3-5L for 2-month campaign.
  • Launch B2B email campaign targeting 200+ food manufacturers, restaurants, and institutional buyers; attend Food Hotel India 2024 and industry trade shows in Delhi/Mumbai; offer bulk discounts and product trials.
  • Partner with 5-10 regional organic supermarkets (like Organic Mandate) for in-store sampling and recipe demonstrations; leverage local agricultural associations and farmer co-operatives for word-of-mouth and distributor networks.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for food processing with 35% subsidy (SC/ST/Women: 45%)
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster-based subsidy up to ₹40L for food processing units in identified clusters
🏚
Chhattisgarh Rajiv Gandhi Gram Nyaya Yojana
agricultural credit support and zero-interest loans for processing entrepreneurs in rural districts
🏚
CG Agriculture Export Policy 2023
export incentives including 5% subsidy on transport, certification support, and APEDA registration assistance for food exporters
