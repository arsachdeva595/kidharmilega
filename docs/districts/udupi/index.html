ODOP Product

Marine Products

Udupi's 90+ km Arabian Sea coastline with rich fishing grounds, cold-water currents supporting diverse marine biodiversity, and centuries-old traditional fishing communities make it India's premier marine products hub. The district's established export infrastructure, proximity to major ports, and reputation for freshness and quality provide competitive advantages unavailable elsewhere.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Udupi.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Udupi Fish Market and Malpe Fish Landing Center daily for 2 weeks; interview 10+ fishing cooperative leaders; study cold chain logistics at Udupi Port Authority; audit 3 existing seafood exporters' operations.
2
Source Raw Materials
Establish relationships with Udupi Fisheries Cooperative Society and individual boat owners; negotiate monthly supply contracts; budget ₹2-3L for first-month procurement (500kg daily fresh catch); arrange 5-ton refrigerated storage.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established fish processing units in Udupi (Malpe cluster has 40+ units); negotiate processing fees (₹5-8 per kg); or start with direct trading model (no processing initially); hire 1-2 experienced fish handlers at ₹12-15K monthly.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (Manufacturing/Trading); obtain IEC code from nearest customs office; acquire FSSAI registration and Cold Storage License from District Fisheries Department; trademark brand name; obtain export certification from MPEDA (Marine Products Export Development Authority).
5
First Sale
Launch on Blinkit/Zepto for hyperlocal delivery; list on Amazon Fresh and BigBasket; cold-email 20 premium restaurants weekly; register on B2B platforms (Tradeindia, IndiaMART); establish WhatsApp ordering for bulk buyers.
6
Scale
Expand to 2-3 additional processing units; build own cold chain logistics (₹50L investment); develop 5 value-added SKUs; target 3-4 export markets simultaneously; hire dedicated sales team for B2B segment.
