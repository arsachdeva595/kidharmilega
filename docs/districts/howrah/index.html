Gems & Jewellery

Howrah has a centuries-old goldsmith tradition with established clusters in Shibpur and Howrah city. The district's proximity to Kolkata's affluent market and its deep cultural roots in wedding jewellery, temple ornaments, and ceremonial pieces make it naturally suited for premium gems and jewellery production.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Howrah.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Howrah's primary jewellery cluster near Howrah Station and Shibpur (especially Panchanan Tola); spend 2-3 weeks observing artisan workshops, pricing structures, and supplier networks; interview 5-7 established goldsmith families to understand production timelines and material sourcing.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source gold from MMTC branches in Kolkata or certified dealers in Howrah; buy loose precious stones from Kolkata's Bowbazar gem market; establish credit relationships with 2-3 material suppliers; initial 50-gram gold + stone inventory costs ₹2-3L; join as a member of Bengal Jewellers Association for supplier discounts.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 freelance master craftsmen (Karigar) in Howrah who work on piece-rate basis (₹500-2000 per item depending on complexity); establish a small workshop (500-800 sq ft) in Howrah or Shibpur to handle design, quality control, and finishing; hire one senior designer with 5+ years experience at ₹15-20K/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) for tax benefits; apply for IEC code from DGFT for export eligibility; register brand name with Intellectual Property Office; get hallmarking certification from BIS-approved centres in Kolkata; obtain FSSAI registration if offering any packaged items; file for trademark protection.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart Artisan with 20-30 bestseller pieces (traditional Jhumko, Nath, Chur designs); create WhatsApp Business catalog and reach out to wedding planners in Delhi-NCR via LinkedIn; approach luxury boutiques in Mumbai and Bangalore for wholesale placement; list on Craftsvilla and IndieHaat; run Instagram/Facebook ads targeting engaged women 22-40 years with ₹5-10K daily budget.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire 2-3 apprentices for production scaling; expand product range to include fusion designs (modern + traditional); establish presence at 2-3 national trade shows (Mumbai Jewellery Fair, Delhi Craft Fair); secure a B2B wholesale distributor for pan-India retail reach; launch a WhatsApp community for repeat customers and referrals; invest in 4-5 Google Shopping campaigns for export queries.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 5-10 top wedding planners in Delhi, Mumbai, and Bangalore; offer 15% bulk discount on Jhumko and Chur sets; get featured in their portfolio and wedding lookbooks; track referral revenue separately.
  • Create 15-20 short-form videos (15-30 sec) on Instagram Reels/YouTube Shorts showing artisan craftsmanship, stone-setting process, and heritage stories; use hashtags #HowrahJewels #BengaliGold #ArtisanJewellery; target 10-15% engagement rate.
  • Launch a referral loyalty program: existing customers get ₹500 discount on next purchase for every successful referral; create printable referral cards with QR code; incentivize word-of-mouth from bride communities on Facebook Groups.
