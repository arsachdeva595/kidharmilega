ODOP Product

Moonj Grass product

Udham Singh Nagar has a rich tradition of moonj grass cultivation in its fertile plains and foothills, with skilled artisans trained over generations in weaving intricate patterns. The district's proximity to raw material sources and established cooperative networks make it ideal for scaling moonj grass products. The humid climate and availability of water support optimal moonj grass growth.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Udham Singh Nagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit moonj grass clusters in Bazpur and Khatima blocks; interact with local artisan cooperatives like Uttarakhand Handloom Board; study competitor products on Etsy and Amazon Artisan; attend Kangra or Delhi Craft Bazaar exhibitions
2
Source Raw Materials
Source dried moonj grass from local cultivators in Udham Singh Nagar (₹8,000-12,000 per quintal); connect with District Cooperative Market; budget ₹40,000-60,000 for first batch of materials covering 500-1000 units
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered artisan groups through SEWA Uttarakhand or local SHGs; hire 3-5 skilled weavers on piece-rate basis; establish small production unit in Rudrapur or Kashipur town; start with 50-100 units/month production
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro-enterprise) through udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT Lucknow for export readiness; register brand name with IP India; get FSSAI certification if selling food items; obtain NOC from District Administration
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy with 20-30 product listings and heritage storytelling; register on Amazon Handmade India; approach Fab India, Good Earth, and local craft boutiques for wholesale; create Instagram account with behind-the-scenes artisan content; list on Craftroots and Kupiyo
6
Scale
Expand product range (bags, wall hangings, lampshades); develop retail partnerships with 5-star hotels in Dehradun and Delhi; apply for SFURTI cluster development subsidy; hire marketing support; explore B2B exports to Germany and USA through craft aggregators
