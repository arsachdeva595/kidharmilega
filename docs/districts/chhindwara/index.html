Oranges & Orange Products

Chhindwara's elevated terrain (800-1000m altitude), rich black soil, and tropical-subtropical climate create ideal conditions for citrus cultivation. The district's proximity to Nagpur (neighboring state Maharashtra, famous for GI oranges) and established horticultural infrastructure make it naturally suited for large-scale orange production and processing.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Chhindwara.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chhindwara's Sohagpur and Pandhana blocks (known citrus clusters). Study Nagpur orange farming methods at ICAR-Citrus Research Station equivalent. Connect with existing producers at Chhindwara Haat markets and APMC, Indore.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 10-50 acres of cultivated orange orchards via lease or purchase from local farmers near Sohagpur for ₹5-15 lakhs per acre annually. Alternatively, source fresh oranges from district cooperatives at ₹8-12/kg in season (Sept-March). First batch: 5-10 tons for ₹40,000-80,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing cold-chain players in Indore (1.5hrs away) for storage and initial processing. Set up small juice extraction unit (₹2-3 lakhs) or contract with KRIBHCO, NAFED-affiliated processing centers in Madhya Pradesh. Train workforce from local agricultural colleges.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (MSME) portal for zero-cost registration. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Indore. File FSSAI license (₹5,000-10,000). Apply for state GI tag application if differentiating as 'Chhindwara Oranges'. Trademark brand name with IP India (₹10,000-15,000).
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket, Blinkit, JioMart for urban fresh sales. Approach juice manufacturers (Tropicana, Sula, local brands) for bulk concentrate supply. Partner with APEDA for export-ready certifications. Direct sales via WhatsApp Business to hotels, restaurants in Indore, Nagpur, Delhi NCR.
6
Scale
Expand from 10 to 50-100 acres via farmer aggregation models. Build own processing facility (₹30-50 lakhs). Develop 2-3 value-added products (organic juice, cold-pressed variants). Target export markets through APEDA and 1099 registered exporters. Secure B2B contracts with modern retail chains and e-commerce platforms.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showing farm-to-table orange juice journey; partner with micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in health & wellness niche. Cost: ₹1-3L per month.
  • WhatsApp Business catalog with bulk pricing for B2B (juice makers, retailers); send seasonal offers Sept-Feb. Cost: Free with setup.
  • Participate in ICCII food expos and Agrotech trade shows in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore to connect exporters and bulk buyers. Cost: ₹50K-1L per event.
