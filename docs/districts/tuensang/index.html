The district's traditional agricultural practices and farmer cooperatives create a strong ecosystem for sustainable legume farming. Local farmer networks have generations of expertise in crop rotation and soil enrichment using Kholar cultivation.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tuensang.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tuensang district agricultural office and connect with farmer cooperatives like Tuensang Farmers Association. Study cultivation practices at village clusters near Tuensang town. Interview 5-10 established Kholar cultivators to understand yield (15-20 quintals/acre), costs (₹8,000-12,000/acre), and seasonal cycles. Research competitor pricing on Jaivik Bazaar and local mandis.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified Kholar seeds from State Agriculture Department's seed bank or ICAR-affiliated centers in Nagaland. Negotiate bulk rates with 3-5 local farmer suppliers for ₹25-30 per kg for first harvest batch (500 kg). Contract farming agreements with 2-3 hectares of farmers for guaranteed supply. First batch sourcing cost: ₹12,500-15,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 10-15 local farmers under FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) model or direct contract farming. Establish small drying and grading facility in Tuensang town (₹100,000-150,000 setup). Hire 2 part-time processors for cleaning, sorting, packaging. Obtain organic certification from NPOP-accredited agency (₹20,000-30,000) if targeting premium segments.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small) classification with ₹0 cost. Apply for IEC code from DGFT regional office for exports. Trademark brand name with IP India (₹4,500-9,000). Obtain FSSAI registration (₹100-500) for food business. Join Nagaland ODOP network for government recognition and support.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Pantry and BigBasket with 25-50 kg initial inventory (target 15-20 orders/week at ₹400-500/kg). Create Instagram/Facebook presence showcasing farmer stories and soil-health benefits. Contact 5-10 premium restaurant chains in Kohima and Dimapur for B2B supply. List on Jaivik Bazaar and local Nagaland e-commerce platforms. Email outreach to 50+ food companies seeking organic legume suppliers.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand farmer network to 30-40 contract farmers (5-10 hectares total). Invest ₹150,000-200,000 in bulk processing and cold storage facility. Launch 2-3 value-added products (canned beans, flour). Secure B2B contracts with 3-5 food processors for ₹50,000+ monthly orders. Apply for SFURTI cluster development grant for processing infrastructure.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 10-15 short YouTube videos (30-60 sec) showing farmer testimonials and health benefits; distribute via Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts targeting health-conscious audiences aged 25-45 in metros.
  • Partner with 5-10 food bloggers/nutritionists in Kohima, Delhi, Bangalore for sponsored recipe content; offer free samples for reviews on Instagram and blogs.
  • Run Google Shopping campaigns targeting keywords like 'organic kidney beans India,' 'high-protein legumes,' 'Northeast organic food' with budget ₹10,000-15,000/month.
