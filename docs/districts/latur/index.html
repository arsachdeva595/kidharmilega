Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Latur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Latur Agricultural Cooperative Society and connect with soybean farming clusters in Renapur and Nilanga taluks. Study competitor brands like Urban Platter and Soulfull. Attend Ministry of Food Processing workshops in Aurangabad and Latur district office.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified soybean seeds from Latur District Seed Cooperative (approx. ₹1,200-1,500/quintal). First batch: 5 quintals (₹6,000-7,500). Partner with local wholesalers at Latur APMC Mandi for recurring supplies. Budget ₹40,000-60,000 for first-batch raw materials and packaging.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish partnership with DFRL (Defense Food Research Lab) certified co-packers in Aurangabad (40km away) or set up micro-processing unit with basic equipment (soymilk maker, tofu press, packaging machine). Estimated inline setup: ₹15-25L. Partner route (recommended initially): Identify 2-3 co-packers already FSSAI licensed in nearby districts.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) at udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC code at nearest DGFT office in Aurangabad. Obtain FSSAI food license (₹5,000-15,000) and Ayush certification if claiming health benefits. Register trademark at IP Office, Mumbai (₹10,000-20,000).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and Flipkart Supermart within 2 months. Approach 15-20 retail chains (Bhagirath, More Supermarkets, iShoppy) with samples and wholesale pricing. Create Instagram/Facebook pages with health content. Partner with online delivery apps (Blinkit, Zepto) for metro cities. Target B2B: contact school cafeteria managers and gym chains in Aurangabad, Pune, Mumbai.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand SKU range (flavored soy milk, fortified options, ready-to-eat tofu). Establish distribution in 3-4 metro cities via traditional and modern trade. Apply for PMEGP or SFURTI grant to scale production capacity. Build content marketing on YouTube with nutrition education. Target 30% YoY growth through partnerships with health apps (HealthifyMe, CureForce).

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with fitness influencers on Instagram/YouTube (100K+ followers in health/wellness niche) for product reviews and recipe content. Budget: ₹2-5L quarterly for 5-8 creators.
  • Launch health awareness campaigns in partnership with nutritionists and Ayush practitioners. Host free workshops in Pune/Mumbai malls on soy-based nutrition. Collect emails for email marketing. Budget: ₹3-5L quarterly.
  • Run Google Shopping Ads targeting 'soy milk near me,' 'vegan protein alternatives,' and 'plant-based dairy' in Tier 1 cities. Budget: ₹1.5-2.5L monthly with 4-5% conversion target.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general category) for food processing units; apply at KVIC or District Industries Centre, Latur
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹40L cluster development grant; contact Ministry of MSME regional office in Aurangabad for cluster eligibility
🏚
Maharashtra Food Processing Cluster Development Scheme
₹10-15L per unit for processing infrastructure within designated clusters; apply at District Food Processing Officer, Latur
🏚
Udyam Kranti Yojana
soft loans up to ₹10L at 5% interest for registered MSME food processors; contact Maharashtra State Food Processing Department or NABARD, Latur branch
Registration required: Required — udyamregistration.gov.in
