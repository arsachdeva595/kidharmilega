Banana

North Garo Hills has ideal tropical climate with high rainfall (2500-3500mm annually) and fertile volcanic soil perfect for banana cultivation. The region's geographical isolation and traditional farming practices ensure chemical-free, organic banana production. Local farming communities have multi-generational expertise in banana growing.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for North Garo Hills.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tura and Williamnagar markets where 60% of district bananas are traded. Connect with farmer groups like North Garo Hills Farmer Producer Organization. Study banana varieties grown locally (Musa sapientum, Mysore, Robusta). Spend 2 weeks observing harvest-to-market practices.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source banana saplings (₹5-8 per sapling) from local nurseries in Tura. Contract with 10-15 smallholder farmers for regular supply at ₹8-12/kg. First batch procurement cost: ₹2-3 lakhs for 25-30 tons through farmer clusters.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing farmer collectives rather than solo farming. Establish collection centers in Tura and Williamnagar. Implement basic post-harvest handling (ripening chambers, grading unit). Invest in cold storage van (₹8-12 lakhs) for supply chain.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise). Apply for IEC code via APEDA for export eligibility. Brand as 'Garo Bananas' or similar. Get FSSAI license for value-added products. Register farmer producer organization as formal entity.
5
First Sale
Launch on LocalOye, BigBasket vendor program targeting Northeast India. Sell to Meghalaya State Agricultural Marketing Board. Contact 30-40 fruit wholesalers in Guwahati and Assam. Participate in weekly Tura grain/fruit market. Create WhatsApp catalog for B2B buyers.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish ripening facility near Tura (₹15-20 lakhs). Expand farmer network to 30-40 growers. Launch banana chips production unit (FSSAI license, ₹5-8 lakhs equipment). Apply for cold chain subsidy under PMVSY. Target organized retail expansion and export licenses.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram & YouTube content series 'From Garo Farm to Your Table' showcasing farmer stories and cultivation process. Partner with 10 micro-influencers in Northeast India (50K-200K followers) for organic reach.
  • WhatsApp Business API setup with B2B buyer directory of 200+ wholesalers/retailers in Assam, Meghalaya, Tripura. Send weekly inventory and pricing updates with bulk order discounts.
  • Sponsorship of Tura Weekly Market & Garo cultural festivals. Create branded banana stalls, distribute samples, run 'Local Hero' farmer feature campaign in local newspapers.
