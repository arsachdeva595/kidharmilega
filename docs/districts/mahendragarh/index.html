Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Mahendragarh.

1
Learn / Research
Visit mustard oil clusters in Mahendragarh town and Kanina block; meet established oil-pressers and OSCB members; study cold-pressed vs. refined oil production at local cooperative units like Mahendragarh District Cooperative; attend NAFED training sessions on food processing standards.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mustard seeds from local farmers in Mahendragarh and neighboring Rohtak (₹45-50 per kg); establish supply agreements with 2-3 farmer clusters; first batch of 500 kg seeds (~₹25,000) sufficient for 300 liters of oil production; source packaging from Faridabad suppliers (₹80-120 per 1L bottle).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established cold-press units in Mahendragarh town (e.g., cooperative mills) on contract manufacturing basis initially; cost ₹8-12 per liter for cold-pressing and minimal processing; or invest in small 5-10 liter capacity wooden ghani press (₹150,000-300,000) and hire 1 skilled technician to start in-house production.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (micro enterprise) via udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT Faridabad; register brand name with IP India; get FSSAI license (₹8,000-15,000) from district health office; obtain ISO 22000 certification (₹40,000-60,000) for export readiness; file mustard oil under category 10.01 of FSS Act.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and Flipkart Supermart with premium cold-pressed positioning (₹200-250 per liter); approach modern trade chains like Big Basket and Blink; pitch to regional restaurant chains in Delhi-NCR for direct supply contracts; create Instagram and YouTube content showing traditional cold-pressing method; send samples to food bloggers and nutrition influencers; list on Jio Mart with BOGO offer in first month.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 2-3 new e-commerce platforms (Pepperfry, Incredibowl); establish wholesale distribution in 3-4 states via food distributors; develop 2-3 variants (organic, infused with fenugreek/cumin); launch subscription box on own website at ₹850/month for quarterly deliveries; secure bulk orders from food service companies and export 15-20% volume to UK/Canada; increase production capacity to 2,000 liters/month.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 30-60 second YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels showing traditional wooden ghani pressing method with local farmers; tag food influencers like Ranveer Brar and regional cooking channels; use hashtag #MahendragarHarvest and #TraditionalOilPress for organic reach.
  • Partner with 5-10 regional food blogs and recipe websites to create mustard oil-focused pickle and tempering guides; offer 20% affiliate commission on Amazon; target search terms like 'best mustard oil for pickles' and 'cold-pressed mustard oil online'.
  • Run Facebook/Instagram carousel ads targeting women aged 28-50 in Delhi-NCR metro with messaging 'Restaurant Quality Oil At Home'; A/B test messaging with health benefits vs. traditional taste angle; set daily budget ₹2,000-3,000 with 8-12% expected ROAS.
