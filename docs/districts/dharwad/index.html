Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Dharwad.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dharwad Food Processing Cluster at Belgaum Road Industrial Area; attend FMCG trade shows at Bangalore or Mumbai; connect with existing manufacturers like Anil Havelis and other players at Dharwad Chamber of Commerce.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source from local agricultural mandis (Dharwad Taluk Mandi, Hubli Mandi), sugarcane from surrounding villages. First batch raw material cost: ₹3-5 lakhs for 5000-10000 units; negotiate with 2-3 suppliers for 10-15% discount on volume commitment.
3
Produce / Partner
Lease 2000-3000 sq ft space in Belgaum Road or Navanagar industrial zones (rent: ₹15000-25000/month); install essential processing equipment (mixer, grinder, packing line) worth ₹5-8 lakhs; hire 8-12 workers; obtain Food Safety license from FSSAI within 30 days.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSMEs) online via udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC from nearest DGFT office; register brand at Intellectual Property Office; obtain FSSAI Food Business Operator License (₹5000-15000); get GST registration (18% standard rate for most FMCG).
5
First Sale
Launch on Blinkit and Zepto with 15-20% introductory discounts; approach Reliance Fresh, Big Bazaar, and D-Mart with B2B pricing; create WhatsApp Business catalog for neighborhood wholesalers; sponsor small local kirana shops with 20% margin.
6
Scale
Expand product line based on sales data; open 2-3 franchise outlets in Hubli, Belgaum, and Belagavi by month 8; negotiate shelf space in 50+ retail outlets across Karnataka; invest in micro-influencer campaigns on Instagram/YouTube targeting tier-2 cities; apply for SIDBI loans for equipment upgrade.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 20-30 local Kirana stores for in-store tasting events and offer temporary 30% margin boost on first 500 units sold; track QR codes on packaging for consumer engagement.
  • Run Instagram/YouTube ads targeting tier-2 cities (Belgaum, Hubli, Davangere) emphasizing local sourcing and agricultural authenticity; allocate ₹2-3 lakhs monthly ad budget in months 2-6.
  • Sponsor local food festivals and agricultural fairs in Dharwad and nearby districts; distribute free samples with brand stickers and collect 500+ customer emails for WhatsApp marketing.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidized loan for food processing startups with 15-35% subsidy component.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹10 lakhs for cluster-based food processing units in Dharwad industrial areas.
🏚
Karnataka Food Processing Policy 2023
up to 25% capital subsidy for food processing units with machinery purchase and building infrastructure.
🏚
NABARD Soft Loan Scheme for Food Processing
concessional interest (6-8%) loans up to ₹50 lakhs for women entrepreneurs and SC/ST applicants.
