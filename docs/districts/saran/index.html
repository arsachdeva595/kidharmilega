Tomato

Saran district has fertile alluvial soil, abundant water from the Ganges River system, and a semi-tropical climate ideal for year-round tomato cultivation. The region's established agricultural ecosystem and proximity to major vegetable markets in North India make it a natural hub for tomato farming and value-added products.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Saran.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chapra and Mahnar mandis in Saran to understand market dynamics, pricing, and buyer behavior. Connect with existing tomato farming cooperatives like Saran Krishak Samiti. Attend workshops at Bihar Agricultural Department's Saran office (Chapra) to learn best practices for hybrid varieties like Sungold and Pusa Ruby.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source seeds from IARI Delhi or Nuziveedu Seeds (available at Chapra agro-dealers). Procure organic inputs from local composting units in Saran. First-batch cost for 1 acre (2,500 saplings): ₹45,000-65,000 including seeds, fertilizers, and equipment.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing tomato farmers in Chapra or Maharajganj blocks under contract farming arrangement (80-20 profit split). Alternatively, lease 0.5-1 acre land at ₹8,000-12,000/year and hire local labor (₹300-400/day). Implement drip irrigation for water efficiency and yield optimization.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam for agricultural business at udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC code via Apeda for export-ready operations. Create GI-like certification via Participatory Guarantee System (PGS) if choosing organic route. Register brand with FSSAI if planning processed tomato products.
5
First Sale
List fresh tomatoes on Blinkit, JioMart, and BigBasket under your brand. Engage directly with 5-10 restaurants in Patna and Chapra for weekly supply. Supply to Agrawal Processors and other tomato paste manufacturers in Bihar. Attend Chapra vegetable mandi auctions for bulk wholesale transactions.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 2-3 acres through farmer network partnerships. Launch 1-2 value-added products (tomato paste, sauce) via FSSAI registration. Build supply chain with regional food processors. Explore export to Nepal/Bangladesh through APEDA-registered trading partners. Establish cold storage facility (₹30-50 lakh) for off-season price appreciation.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Influencer partnerships with Bihar-based food bloggers and chefs on Instagram and YouTube, showcasing farm-to-table tomato recipes and farming practices
  • WhatsApp Business catalog creation targeting local restaurant owners and institutional buyers with bulk pricing and weekly supply updates
  • Seasonal campaigns during monsoon and summer planting on Facebook and Google Ads targeting health-conscious urban consumers in Patna, Varanasi, and NCR region
