Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Siaha.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Siaha district's agricultural clusters around Siaha town, connect with local farming cooperatives (Siaha Multi Purpose Cooperative Society), and meet 3-5 established chilli farmers to understand harvest cycles, yield expectations (1.5-2.5 tonnes/hectare), and local supply networks. Study GI certification requirements from Ministry database.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source seeds from Mizoram Agricultural Research Institute (MARI), Aizawl or certified local farmers in Siaha; first batch of 1 hectare requires ₹30,000-50,000 for seeds, fertilizers, and labor. Identify 2-3 reliable farmer suppliers for consistent raw material within 20km radius.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-10 smallholder farmers in Siaha (via contract farming model offering ₹80-100/kg), establish drying sheds with solar driers (₹3-5L investment), and set up basic processing unit for grinding/packaging in Siaha town or Aizawl. Consider SFURTI cluster support for shared processing infrastructure.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (micro/small enterprise), obtain IEC code from nearest Customs office (Aizawl), register GI usage with Controller of Patents/Designs, get FSSAI license for food processing, and trademark brand name with IP office. Total cost: ₹80,000-120,000.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Global, Spice India, Jio Mart wholesale portal within 3 months; simultaneously reach out to specialty exporters in Kolkata and Mumbai (5+ pitches); partner with 10-15 ethnic food retailers in Delhi/Bangalore via direct B2B outreach; activate Instagram/YouTube with farm-to-table storytelling.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand farmer network to 20+ suppliers across Siaha/Lunglei, invest ₹50L in automated drying/grinding equipment, obtain organic certification (₹1.5-2L), establish export partnerships with 3+ international distributors, and launch 2-3 value-added products (fermented paste, smoked chilli flakes).

  • Partner with 5-10 top Indian spice YouTubers (Ranveer Brar, Dhruv Rathod) for blind taste tests and reviews, budget ₹2-4L for influencer seeding and affiliate commissions
  • Sponsored posts on 'India Gate' and ethnic food community FB/Insta pages (₹50K/month) highlighting GI tag authenticity and heat certification levels, with direct purchase links
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Up to ₹25L loan assistance at concessional rates for spice processing and export units
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹3-5L grant for cluster infrastructure, common processing facilities, and brand building for chilli cooperatives
🏚
Mizoram State Agricultural Development & Farmers' Welfare Scheme
Subsidies on agricultural equipment, seeds, and fertilizers for registered farmers in ODOP clusters
🏚
Mizoram Export Promotion Scheme
Tax rebates and logistics support for GI-tagged products exported to international markets
