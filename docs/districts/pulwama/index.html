Saffron

Pulwama's cold climate, well-drained soil, and altitude (1600m) create ideal conditions for saffron cultivation. Centuries of farming expertise and traditional knowledge passed through generations ensure consistent quality. The district's monopoly on Indian saffron production and GI certification make it the only authentic source.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Pulwama.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pulwama district's main saffron clusters in Pampoora and Lethpora villages; interact with established farmers through local cooperative societies like Pulwama Saffron Farmers Cooperative; attend harvest season (Oct-Nov) to understand cultivation and grading.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified saffron corms from registered nurseries in Pulwama (₹100-150 per gram for premium Grade A saffron); establish direct relationships with 3-5 small farmers; first batch cost: ₹3-5 lakhs for quality inventory.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established farmer collectives in Pulwama for cultivation or processing; hire local farmers as contract producers; set up a small processing and grading unit (₹10-15 lakhs) for quality control and moisture management.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) classification for ₹0; obtain IEC code from customs for export eligibility; register brand with Trademark Registry; obtain FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) membership for collective marketing benefits; ensure GI certification documentation from Geographical Indications Registry.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Global and Indiamart for B2B; create premium D2C website via Shopify with GI certification prominently displayed; partner with specialty retailers like ITC Aashirvaad and premium spice chains; cold-email international distributors with third-party lab certifications.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand into value-added products (saffron honey, saffron water); establish direct export channels to EU and US (requires APEDA certification); build subscription model for premium customers; invest in agritourism experiences; partner with Ayurvedic and cosmetic manufacturers.
