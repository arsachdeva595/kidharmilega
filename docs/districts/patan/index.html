Double Ikkat Patan

Patan has a 400+ year legacy of double ikat weaving passed down through generations of Muslim weaver families (Farakhs). The district's water quality, climate, and access to indigo dye historically suited the complex dyeing and weaving process. Today, the tight-knit weaver community and established supply chains make it the world's premier double ikat production hub.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Patan.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Patan's weaver clusters (Zarniyali and Tilibharti bastis) for 2-3 weeks; engage with master weavers like families in Patan city center. Study the double ikat process at the Patan Museum and connect with DC Handloom office for artisan databases. Attend textile trade shows like IITF Delhi and Craft Mela.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton and silk yarn from Ahmedabad's textile wholesalers (Jai Bhim Road, Girdharpura); indigo dye from traditional suppliers in Patan or Indore; first batch of 100 meters yarn + dye materials ≈ ₹30,000-50,000. Establish relationships directly with dye suppliers to negotiate bulk rates.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 master weaver families in Patan under revenue-share or per-piece commission model (not wage labor, to preserve quality). Negotiate 60-day lead times for custom designs. Start with 5-8 sarees/month; document the entire process for authenticity marketing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online via udyamregistration.gov.in (~15 mins, free). Apply for IEC code at DGFT portal for export eligibility. Register brand name with IP India (₹5,000-10,000). Get GI certification status documented from DC Handloom office for marketing. Create artisan heritage documentation (weaver biographies, village maps) for brand storytelling.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C website on Shopify or WordPress with high-resolution weaving process videos and artisan stories; list on Nykaa Luxe, Myntra Luxe, and Etsy (for export reach). Reach out to luxury boutiques in Delhi (Khan Market), Mumbai (Bandra), and Bangalore; offer 40% wholesale discount. Pitch to luxury wedding planners for bespoke offerings. Run Instagram/Pinterest content showing weaving process.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 8-10 families; introduce 3-4 new design collections per season. Launch corporate gifting division targeting luxury brands. Apply for SFURTI cluster development grants. Collaborate with fashion designers for limited collections. Open a small showroom/experience center in Patan or Ahmedabad metro. Explore B2B export partnerships with European luxury retailers. Train 2-3 younger artisans to ensure generational continuation.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showing the complete 60-day double ikat journey from yarn preparation to finished saree; tag luxury fashion influencers and saree bloggers; use #PatanDoubleIkkat and #HandwovenHeritage.
  • YouTube documentary-style videos (5-8 mins each) featuring individual weaver families, their stories, and the artistry; upload weekly; monetize and cross-promote on Facebook and TikTok for Gen-Z awareness.
  • Partner with luxury wedding platforms (Shaadi.com, WeddingWire) for featured artisan profiles; sponsor 'Heritage Bridal' collections on wedding fashion portals; host virtual trunk shows for wedding consultants.
