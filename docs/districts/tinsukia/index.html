Assam Lemon

Tinsukia's tropical climate with well-distributed rainfall and fertile alluvial soil create ideal conditions for lemon cultivation. The district's agricultural heritage and established farmer networks provide consistent supply chains, while its proximity to major markets via NH-37 reduces logistics costs significantly.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tinsukia.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tinsukia Agricultural Department office in Tinsukia town; network with established lemon farmers in Kakopat and Doomdooma blocks; attend Assam Agricultural University extension workshops; study production practices at 3-4 existing orchards over 2-3 months.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified lemon saplings (Kagzi, Eureka, or local Assam varieties) from Assam Agricultural University nurseries or registered nurseries in Tinsukia (₹30,000-50,000 for 500-1000 saplings); bulk buy organic fertilizer from cooperative societies in Jorhat district; first-batch setup: ₹150,000-300,000 for 1-2 acres including land preparation, saplings, initial fertilizer.
3
Produce / Partner
Lease or own 2-5 acres in Kakopat, Doomdooma, or Duliajan blocks; partner with local agricultural cooperative (Tinsukia District Farmers Co-op Society) for technical training; hire 2-3 farm laborers during harvesting season; implement drip irrigation for water efficiency; expect first harvest in 18-24 months.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (formerly MSME) at udyamregistration.gov.in under agricultural processing; apply for IEC code from local DGFT office in Tinsukia or Dibrugarh; create brand identity (name, packaging design, FSSAI license if adding value-addition); file for trademark with IP office if launching packaged products.
5
First Sale
Start B2B sales to Jorhat and Dibrugarh wholesale markets (contact 8-10 major vegetable traders); register on Jio Mart vendor portal and Big Basket farm-direct program; list on local e-commerce platforms (Assam-specific apps); cold-call 15-20 juice manufacturers, hotels, and restaurants in Dibrugarh and Jorhat; post daily harvest updates on WhatsApp Business and local Facebook groups; aim for ₹2-3 lakh monthly revenue in months 1-3.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 5-10 acres by leasing additional land; invest ₹50-75L in cold storage facility near Tinsukia town; launch value-added products (juice, concentrate) in partnership with food processing units; approach major retail chains (Reliance Fresh, Spencer's) for supply agreements; explore export to Bangladesh and Nepal via trade channels; establish farmer producer company (FPC) structure to aggregate supply from 20-30 neighboring farmers.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 5-10 health and wellness influencers in Assam (Instagram/YouTube) for farm-to-table content; send them organic lemon boxes monthly and encourage unboxing videos targeting urban health-conscious audiences.
  • Run WhatsApp Business campaigns targeting 500+ restaurant owners, juice bar franchisees, and cafe chains in Northeast India with bulk pricing; offer free sample shipments to top 20 prospects.
  • Create 'Tinsukia Lemon Story' content series on Facebook and Instagram showing traditional farming methods, nutrition facts, and recipes; run targeted ads to mothers aged 25-45 in urban Assam, targeting wellness and cooking categories.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)

PM-KISAN

40-50% subsidy on drip irrigation systems, tractors, and farm equipment for registered growers
🏚
