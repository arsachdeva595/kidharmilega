Soyabean

Khairagarh-Chhuikhadan-Gandai benefits from Chhattisgarh's black soil and semi-humid climate ideal for soyabean cultivation. The district has established agricultural infrastructure, farmer co-operatives, and proximity to major processing hubs in central India, making it a natural production and aggregation center.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Khairagarh-Chhuikhadan-Gandai.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Indore Soyabean Processor Association and connect with farmers in Khairagarh block; study production cycles at Central Institute of Agricultural Engineering (CIAE) Bhopal; attend SOPA conferences for market insights
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified soyabean seeds from IPSWICH seed bank (Indore); contract farmers in Khairagarh for bulk procurement at ₹35-45 per kg; initial 10-ton batch costs ₹4-4.5 lakh
3
Produce / Partner
Register with agricultural department for farmer aggregation license; partner with NAFED cooperatives or setup basic processing unit for soymilk/tofu; contract with Dal Mill operators in Raipur for oil extraction
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (food processing category); obtain IEC code from DGFT for exports; get FSSAI license for food products; trademark brand name at IP office
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket, Blinkit, and local Indore-Raipur distribution networks; approach NAFED and National Cooperative Marketing Federation for bulk orders; directly pitch to Japanese and Korean importers via TradeKey and Alibaba
6
Scale
Establish co-operative farmer groups for contract farming; invest in cold-storage and processing facility in Khairagarh; expand to value-added products (soy protein powder, tempeh); build B2B relationships with food manufacturers across India
