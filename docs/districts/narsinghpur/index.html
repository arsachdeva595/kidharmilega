Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Narsinghpur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Narsinghpur's agricultural markets in Gadarwara and Kareli to understand local farming practices. Meet farmer associations and dal processing units. Study pricing at Indore Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC). Analyze export regulations via APEDA guidelines.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw toor dal directly from farmer cooperatives in Narsinghpur (₹4500-5200 per quintal for first 10 quintals = ₹45,000-52,000). Establish contracts with 2-3 farmers to ensure consistent supply. First batch processing cost through local miller: ₹15,000-25,000 for grading, cleaning, and packaging.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with certified dal processing mills in Narsinghpur or Gadarwara (Dhanuka Mills, Aavishkar Agro) for cleaning, grading, and polishing. Alternatively, invest in small-scale cleaning and packaging equipment (₹300,000-500,000) and hire 2-3 workers. Obtain food safety certification (FSSAI) mandatory for packaged dal.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification) online at udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from customs portal if planning exports. Obtain FSSAI license (₹5,000-10,000). Register brand name with IP protection. Get organic certification if targeting premium segment (₹30,000-50,000).
5
First Sale
List on Amazon, Flipkart, JioMart with SEO-optimized dal product pages. Approach wholesale distributors in Mumbai, Delhi via TradeKey and IndiaTradeKey platforms. Contact food exporters via APEDA directory for export partnerships. Sell to local retail chains and Indian grocers in metros at ₹120-150 per kg (wholesale ₹90-100).
6
Scale
After month 6, introduce organic and premium varieties, expand to 5-7 SKUs (whole dal, split dal, roasted snacks). Build B2B contracts with food manufacturers and institutional buyers. Invest in marketing via Instagram, YouTube (recipe content). Aim to reach ₹5-10 Cr turnover by year 2 through geographic expansion and export focus.
