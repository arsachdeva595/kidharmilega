Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Mau.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mau textile clusters (Gharipur, Kotwali areas) to understand powerloom operations; connect with Mau Weavers Cooperative Society; study competitor catalogs on IndiaMART and Alibaba; attend textile trade shows like IBTEX in New Delhi
2
Source Raw Materials
Source polyester and cotton yarn from Surat yarn suppliers (₹15-25 per kg); purchase dyes from Ahmedabad chemical dealers; order sizing chemicals locally; first 500kg batch costs ₹12,000-18,000; establish 45-60 day credit terms with suppliers
3
Produce / Partner
Lease a 1000-2000 sq ft space with 3-5 powerloom machines (₹2-3L per machine) or partner with existing weavers in Mau clusters for job-work (10-15% production fee); establish quality control protocols for density, weave consistency, and color fastness
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in); apply for IEC code from DGFT (directgov.in); register trademark with IP India; obtain GST registration; comply with Textile Ministry labeling standards; create brand identity with saree/fabric-specific packaging
5
First Sale
List products on IndiaMART, TradeKey, and Global Sources; create Instagram and YouTube content showcasing weaving process and product details; contact 50+ B2B buyers from textile directories; start with ₹50K bulk orders to 5-10 regional wholesalers; use WhatsApp Business for B2B inquiries
6
Scale
Expand loom count from 5 to 15-20 machines; develop 3-5 signature collections (traditional, semi-silk, jacquard); hire dedicated sales and quality teams; pursue ISO 9001 certification; target government textile procurement (PM Mitra scheme); establish showrooms in Jaipur, Delhi, Mumbai textile markets

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create YouTube Shorts and Instagram Reels showing 60-second powerloom weaving process; target textile business hashtags (#PowerloomTextiles #IndianSarees); engage with interior designers and wedding planners for home textile orders
  • Sponsor textile expos like IBTEX Delhi and Textech Mumbai; set up booth with sample catalogs; collect buyer contacts for direct follow-up; offer 5% first-order discount
  • Partner with 10-15 regional saree retailers for exclusive collections; offer dropship program with 25-30% wholesale margin; create WhatsApp broadcast groups for new product launches and bulk deals
