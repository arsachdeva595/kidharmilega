Garlic

Mandsaur's black soil and semi-arid climate create ideal conditions for garlic cultivation with naturally higher allicin content. The district has 40+ years of concentrated garlic farming expertise and established cold storage infrastructure across 150+ villages. Proximity to national markets via NH-48 and strong farmer cooperative networks ensure consistent supply and price stability.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Mandsaur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mandsaur Mandi (grain market) and meet 5-10 established garlic farmers and cold storage operators. Study seasonal pricing at Ujjain Agricultural Produce Market. Connect with Mandsaur District Agriculture Department for soil testing and yield data. Explore existing cold storage units in Mandsaur city (3-4 major ones operate year-round).
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure certified garlic seed cloves from NHRDF (National Horticulture Research Development Foundation) Nashik or Mandsaur seed dealers (cost ₹80-120/kg for 500kg starter batch = ₹40,000-60,000). Source organic farmyard manure locally (₹2,000-3,000/ton). Budget for 2 months pre-harvest: ₹80,000-120,000 for initial inputs on 1-2 acres.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 contract farmers in Mandsaur taluka (sign Samjhauta Patra). Arrange cold storage rental at Mandsaur Cold Storage Association (₹1,500-2,500/ton/month). Hire a local aggregator/commission agent to coordinate harvest timing and grading. For packaging, work with Delhi-based spice packaging units or source food-grade jute bags locally.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSE online (₹0, 10 mins). Apply for IEC code via ICEGATE within 48 hours (₹0). Open RCMC (Regional cum Renewal of Certificate) with local Chamber of Commerce for export eligibility. Register trademark for brand name with IP India. Open IFSCA compliant bank account if targeting food exports.
5
First Sale
Week 1-2: List on Blinkit, Amazon Fresh, and BigBasket with ₹150-200/kg pricing. Week 3-4: Contact 10-15 regional spice wholesalers (Indore, Ujjain, Bhopal) with sample dispatch. Month 2: Approach 5-8 export-ready buyers via APEDA directory and TradeKey. Month 3: Negotiate contracts with institutional buyers (restaurants, food processing units). Launch Instagram + WhatsApp catalog for D2C orders.
6
Scale
Month 7: Expand to 5-10 acres through farmer networks; invest ₹50L in own cold storage facility (10-ton capacity). Launch 3 value-added SKUs (garlic powder, black garlic, garlic oil) for 60% margin uplift. Secure export contracts with 2-3 international importers for 50-ton annual shipments. Recruit supply chain manager and hire 2-3 operations staff. Target ₹3-5 Cr turnover by Month 18.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 20+ Instagram food bloggers and nutritionists; offer 5kg packs at ₹50 discount for 4-week unboxing campaign. Target posts: garlic health benefits, farm-to-table reels. Budget: ₹50,000/month.
  • Run WhatsApp community (500+ members) with exclusive wholesale rates for restaurant chefs and caterers. Weekly market price alerts + recipes + health tips. Drive 30% repeat orders via loyalty discount code.
  • Sponsor 10-15 farmer mandis in Indore-Ujjain region with branded signage and sampling kiosks. Distribute 100 packs/month free to top purchasers. Target: 1 in 4 triers converts to bulk buyer.
