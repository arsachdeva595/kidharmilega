ODOP Product

Tribal craft and Furniture

Shravasti's tribal communities have deep ancestral knowledge of bamboo weaving, basket-making, and pottery passed down through generations. The district's forest proximity provides abundant raw bamboo and clay resources, while low competition and government push for ODOP creates unique market opportunity. Strong cultural authenticity and untapped export potential make this ideal for heritage craft entrepreneurs.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Shravasti.

1
Learn / Research
Visit tribal clusters in Shravasti blocks (Itwa, Gyanpur) and connect with Shravasti Handicraft Association. Attend craft fairs in Lucknow (Feb-March) and study competitor products on Etsy, Amazon Handmade. Spend 2-3 weeks understanding production techniques, pricing at Shravasti crafts market.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source bamboo directly from forest departments or licensed suppliers in Shravasti (₹3,000-5,000 per quintal). Clay from local mines (₹500-1,000 per quintal). First batch: ₹40,000-60,000 for 50-100 items minimum. Connect with Uttar Pradesh Forest Department for sustainable sourcing certificates.
3
Produce / Partner
Register as artisan partner with 5-10 tribal craftspeople via memorandum agreement (no wages upfront, revenue-sharing model). Establish small production space (500-1000 sqft) in Khalilpur or Itwa block. Use WhatsApp groups for order communication. Plan 15-20 day production cycles for bulk orders.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (micro category, online at udyamregistration.gov.in, cost ₹0). Obtain IEC code from nearest APEDA office in Lucknow for export. Create brand name reflecting tribal heritage. Register trademark with IP India. Get Fair-Trade or Eco-certification (₹15,000-30,000) to justify premium pricing.
5
First Sale
List 20-30 products on Amazon Handmade and Etsy (2-3 month timeline). Launch Instagram account with artisan storytelling content weekly. Approach 10-15 boutique furniture stores in Delhi, Mumbai via WhatsApp with catalogs. Email 50 corporate procurement departments with bulk customization options. Attend Sanchar Trade Fair (Lucknow, June).
6
Scale
Post 6 months, launch DTC website (₹30,000 development) with storytelling blog. Expand artisan network to 25+ craftspeople with formal training. Apply for SFURTI cluster development grant. Pitch to international fair-trade importers at Global Sources Fair (Hong Kong). Build B2B wholesale presence in Bangalore and Pune design markets.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reel series 'Artisan Stories' — 60-second videos of tribal craftspeople creating pieces, shared 2x weekly with hashtags #ShravastiCraft #TribalHeritage. Drive traffic to Etsy and DTC site.
  • Partner with 5 eco-conscious micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in sustainable living/home décor space for product gifting and honest reviews. Budget ₹40,000 per influencer.
  • Email outreach to 100 interior designers and architects in metro cities with 'Tribal Craft Customization' angle, offering 20% margin and 30-day payment terms. Use LinkedIn for B2B targeting.
