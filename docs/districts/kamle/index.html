Kamle's subtropical highland climate, rich volcanic soil, and altitude of 800-1200m create ideal conditions for citrus cultivation. The region's organic farming traditions and minimal chemical pesticide use produce oranges with exceptional flavor, aroma, and nutritional density that command premium market prices.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kamle.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kamle district's fruit orchards (Rilo, Khoijang villages); connect with existing orange growers at Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Department, Itanagar; study cold chain logistics from Assam Agricultural Produce Market Committee (AAPMC), Guwahati
2
Source Raw Materials
Source nursery saplings from Arunachal Pradesh State Horticulture Mission or Directorate of Horticulture (budget ₹3-5L for 500 saplings + fertilizers); negotiate with local cooperatives for 15-20% discounts on bulk organic inputs
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing Kamle orange cooperatives (e.g., Kamle Farmer Producer Organization) for orchard access and training; hire 2-3 farm laborers; invest in basic drip irrigation system; production-sharing model: 60% founder, 40% landowner
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Single Window portal); apply for IEC from Directorate General of Foreign Trade; trademark brand name (₹5000-10000); obtain agricultural export certification from APEDA; get GI certificate usage approval from Ministry of Agriculture
5
First Sale
Launch on IndiaMART, TradeKey for B2B exports; sell on Blinkit, Unbox Collective for D2C in metros; partner with BigBasket, Grofers for modern trade supply; register with APEDA for export subsidies; attend Trade Fairs in Delhi (Pragati Maidan) and Mumbai
6
Scale
Establish cold storage facility (₹20-30L) in Itanagar; develop juice concentrate line with local food processing partner; expand to 50-100 acres through farmer franchising model; hire export compliance manager; target 30-40% annual growth via EU/Japan organic certifications
