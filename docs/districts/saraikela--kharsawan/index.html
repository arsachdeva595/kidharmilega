ODOP Product

Kharsawan Silk

Saraikela-Kharsawan has a long-established silk weaving heritage with a concentrated cluster of skilled weavers in the Kharsawan region. The district's traditional knowledge and existing infrastructure for sericulture and silk production make it naturally suited for high-quality silk manufacturing. Proximity to raw silk sources and a trained workforce of master craftsmen provide competitive advantage.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Saraikela- Kharsawan.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kharsawan weaving clusters in Saraikela district (Kharsawan, Porahat areas) to study traditional techniques. Connect with SRLM (State Rural Livelihood Mission) office and District Commerce Department. Attend textile workshops at NIFT Delhi or local government training centers. Study market dynamics via Indian Silk Weaving clusters databases.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw mulberry silk yarn from Bangalore sericulture centers or Tamil Nadu suppliers (₹800-1200/kg). First batch requirement: 50-100 kg for ₹50,000-1.2 Lakhs. Local dye suppliers in Ranchi or national suppliers (Coimbatore-based). Establish 3-month payment terms with suppliers via SIDBI-backed credit lines.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing master weavers in Kharsawan cluster (20-30 units) as subcontractors. Start with 2-3 looms; negotiate revenue-sharing at 60-40 (you-weaver). Alternatively, set up one 4-loom unit (₹2L investment). Register with Jharkhand Handicrafts Cooperative. Implement quality control checks for thread strength and weave consistency.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online for statutory recognition. Apply for IEC (Import-Export Code) from DGFT for export readiness. Register brand name at IP India. Create GST registration (6%). Join Jharkhand Handicrafts Board for government support. Get BIS mark certification for fabric durability (optional but improves export appeal).
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho, Amazon Seller Central, and Flipkart for mass reach. Direct B2B outreach to ethnic retailers in Delhi's Chandni Chowk, Mumbai's Mangaldas Market via LinkedIn/email. Start Instagram/Facebook seller account with weaving-process videos (builds authenticity). Partner with 3-5 regional wedding planners. Approach export agents in ICCW (Indian Chamber of Commerce) for international orders.
6
Scale
After ₹5L revenue milestone (6-8 months), hire 2-3 marketing staff. Invest in e-commerce SEO and influencer partnerships with ethnic fashion bloggers. Expand loom capacity to 8-10 looms. Open pop-up stores in tier-2 cities (Jaipur, Lucknow). Apply for PLI scheme for handlooms. Build B2B relationships with 10+ major retailers. Launch limited-edition designer collections.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create YouTube series 'From Cocoon to Saree' showing 4-week weaving journey per sari. Upload weekly 8-minute episodes showing artisan techniques, heritage stories, dyeing processes. Target 500K views/quarter; monetize via product affiliate links.
  • Partner with 5-7 premium ethnic fashion Instagram influencers (50K-500K followers) for monthly product collaborations and giveaways. Cost: ₹10,000-30,000 per collaboration. Aim for 2-3% conversion rate from their audience.
  • Sponsor 2-3 regional wedding expos per year in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore. Set up experiential booth where customers can touch silk, see live weaving demo, book custom orders. Budget: ₹1-2L per expo; expect 15-20% conversion from footfall.
