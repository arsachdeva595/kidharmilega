Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Nagaon.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nagaon's existing pearl cultivation units near Kapimala and Dekargaon; consult Central Institute of Fisheries Education (CIFE) Mumbai resources; study Japanese and Chinese pearl farming techniques; connect with Assam Fisheries Department for local expertise
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mussel seeds from CIFE or Assam Fisheries Department hatcheries (₹2-3 lakhs per 1000 juveniles); procure grafting tools from Hiroshima suppliers via direct import or Indian agents; first-batch raw material cost ₹8-12 lakhs for 5-acre farm setup
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established pearl farmers in Nagaon for initial training; lease/purchase 2-5 acres of freshwater pond space in Kapimala cluster; engage local artisan communities for mussel care and harvesting; establish water quality testing protocols with local fisheries labs
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) as aquaculture enterprise; obtain IEC code for export from DGFT; apply for Organic/Ecocert certification for premium positioning; register brand at IP India; obtain food safety/export licenses from marine products export authority
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Amazon Artisan with eco-story narrative; approach designer jewelry brands (Tanishq, Minerali, Tavola) with B2B samples; list on TradeKey and Global Sources for international pearl traders; engage luxury e-commerce platforms like Nykaa Fashion and FabIndia
6
Scale
Expand pond acreage to 15-20 acres by month 12; develop branded jewelry collection with 3-4 designer collaborations; establish export SOP for Japan/Korea/USA; implement blockchain traceability for premium 'farm-to-finger' positioning; train 20-30 local youth as pearl technicians
