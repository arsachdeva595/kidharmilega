Nuts - Groundnut

Wanaparthy's black soil and semi-arid climate create ideal conditions for groundnut cultivation with yields of 2,500-3,200 kg/ha. The district has established processing infrastructure and farmer networks with decades of groundnut expertise, making it naturally suited for value-added groundnut oil and kernel production.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Wanaparthy.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Wanaparthy groundnut processing clusters in Tandur and Zaheerabad mandals; meet 5-10 miller-traders at Wanaparthy Mandi; study processing yields, oil extraction rates (40-48%), and regulatory requirements from local Agriculture Officer.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw groundnuts directly from farmers in Tandur mandal (₹4,500-5,500 per quintal) or traders at Wanaparthy wholesale market; plan first batch of 5-10 quintals (₹22,500-55,000); identify cold storage facility (₹500-800/month for 10 MT capacity).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing Wanaparthy-based oil mills (contact District Industries Centre for list) for toll processing at ₹800-1,200 per quintal, OR invest in small 5-10 MT/day expeller unit (₹12-18 lakhs); hire 2-3 skilled operators familiar with groundnut processing.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (food processing MSME) and IEC code online; obtain FSSAI license (₹100-500 for small scale); design packaging with barcode; register brand name with Registrar of Companies; get state certification for organic if pursuing premium positioning.
5
First Sale
List on Blinkit, BigBasket, Amazon Fresh under private label; approach 50-100 local kirana stores with ₹15,000-30,000 first orders; supply to 3-5 institutional buyers (schools, hospitals, corporates); build WhatsApp distribution network with 20-30 bulk buyers in Hyderabad.
6
Scale
Invest in automated packaging and labeling (₹8-12 lakhs by month 8); expand to 3-5 variants (refined oil, cold-pressed oil, roasted kernels, flavored snacks); hire dedicated logistics partner; approach regional distributors in Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh; explore export partnerships for UAE and Singapore markets.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 30-second Instagram Reels showing farm-to-oil journey with local farmer testimonials; target 25-45 year old health-conscious women; run ₹500-1,000/day campaigns with 8-12% CTR target.
  • Partner with 10-15 regional health/fitness influencers (50K-200K followers) on YouTube and Instagram to review groundnut oil nutrition benefits; offer 10-15% affiliate commission on sales.
  • Launch corporate gifting program for Hyderabad IT/pharma companies; position as wellness gift during festivals; generate ₹2-3 lakh quarterly from bulk orders of 50-100 units.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (25% of project cost) for food processing unit setup with 5-10 year loan tenure.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹5 crore for groundnut processing clusters in Telangana for modernization and quality improvement.
🏚
Telangana Groundnut Crop Insurance Scheme
provides coverage for raw material cost fluctuations; premium support of ₹400-600 per quintal for registered processors.
🏚
Telangana MSME Loan Subsidy Scheme
5% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹50 lakhs for agro-processing ventures; applicable for machinery and working capital.
