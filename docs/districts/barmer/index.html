ODOP Product

Kasheedakari

Barmer has a centuries-old tradition of handloom embroidery rooted in Marwari and nomadic tribal cultures. The district's artisan community possesses deep expertise in mirror work, traditional dyes, and geometric motif design. The arid landscape and cultural heritage create a unique identity for Kasheedakari that cannot be replicated elsewhere.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Barmer.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Barmer town and Tilwara/Dhorimana villages (known embroidery hubs); spend 5-7 days with master artisans; attend NIFT/RMKHSP workshops; study competitor brands like Banaasthali Handlooms and local co-operatives via DC Handloom office in Barmer.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw fabric from Jaipur textile markets (cotton-silk blends ₹150-250/meter) or Barmer weavers directly; mirrors/sequins from Jaipur wholesale (₹2000-5000 per kg); traditional thread from Jodhpur suppliers (₹30-80/spool). First batch cost (100 dupattas): ₹80,000-120,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 registered artisan groups in Barmer via DC Handloom database; formalize tie-ups with written rate agreements (₹400-800 per dupatta based on complexity); or establish small 4-6 person unit in Barmer town with hired trained artisans; ensure QC sampling every 50 pieces.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) online via msme.gov.in; apply for IEC code from DGFT portal; register brand name at TMR India; apply for Barmer-based cluster GST registration; certify handmade status via Handicrafts Association of India for export benefits.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, Flipkart Saree/Dupatta; pitch to luxury ethnic brands (Fab India, Anita Dongre stockists); approach boutiques in Jaipur/Delhi metro areas; create Instagram account showcasing artisan stories and reels; partner with lifestyle blogger collaborations; launch website via Shopify with artisan bio pages.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range (cushion covers, bed-spreads, apparel); hire design-focused coordinator; invest in small-batch natural dye experimentation; establish direct export channel via B2B trade shows (IITF Delhi, IHGF); scale production to 300-500 units/month; build wholesale distribution to 15-20 retail partners across metros.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels series — 15-30 sec videos of artisans stitching mirror work with lo-fi music and story captions; post 3x/week; use hashtags #KasheedakariOfBarmer #RajasthanHandloom #EthnicEmbroidery to build organic reach.
  • Collaborate with 5-10 micro-influencers (50k-500k followers) in ethnic fashion/home decor space; send free dupattas with unboxing request; track UTM links to Etsy/website; target cost per acquisition: ₹150-200.
  • Email nurture campaign to boutique owners in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore — curated lookbook PDF, wholesale pricing sheet, 2-month return policy offer; follow up every 15 days; aim for 10-15 new retail partnerships in 3 months.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP
Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme: up to ₹25L loan at 5-6% interest for MSE in handloom sector with 25-35% government subsidy.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
artisan-focused skill training + working capital loans up to ₹3L at 0% interest for registered handloom artisans in Barmer cluster.
🏚
Rajasthan Handloom Policy 2022
5-year tax exemption + 10% capital subsidy for new handloom units + free Udyam registration + export incentive of 5% on FOB value.
🏚
Rajasthan Women Entrepreneur Scheme
additional ₹5L concessional loan + 2% interest subsidy if owner is woman; covers raw material and marketing costs for handloom MSEs.
