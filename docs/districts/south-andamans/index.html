ODOP Product

Marine Products

South Andaman's proximity to pristine marine waters and strategic location as a major fishing hub provides year-round access to jumbo prawns, tiger shrimps, squid, and deep-sea fish. The island's natural cold-chain advantage and established fishing infrastructure make it ideal for processing and exporting high-quality marine products with minimal spoilage.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for South Andamans.

1
Learn / Research
Visit South Andaman's primary fish landing centers at Junglighat and Haddo Jetty. Meet 5-10 fishermen cooperatives and established exporters. Spend 1 week understanding species availability, seasonal cycles, and current export pricing to markets like Vietnam and Thailand.
2
Source Raw Materials
Establish direct relationships with fishermen at Andaman Fisheries Cooperative. Source first batch (500 kg jumbo prawns + 300 kg squid) at ₹800-1200/kg. Budget ₹6-8 lakhs for ice, packaging, and cold-chain transport to processing unit.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing FSSAI-certified processing units in Port Blair (e.g., South Andaman Seafood Private Ltd). Alternatively, secure 2000 sqft cold storage facility (₹2-3 lakhs/month rental) and hire 8-10 trained processing staff. Implement IQF (Individually Quick Frozen) or vacuum-pack processes.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (SSI) and IEC code with Port Blair DGFT office. Obtain FSSAI license (₹5000-8000) and Apeda registration (₹0, free). Secure health certificate from South Andaman District Fisheries Department. Create brand name and design packaging compliant with seafood export standards.
5
First Sale
Launch on Udaan (B2B), BigBasket Wholesale, and local hospitality networks (5-star hotels in Port Blair, Andaman resorts). Approach 10-15 mid-sized exporters in Chennai/Cochin for co-export arrangements. Target 2-3 restaurant chains via LinkedIn outreach.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish own processing unit (₹40-50L capital investment). Build export relationships with 3-4 Southeast Asian importers. Launch D2C brand on Amazon Fresh and Blinkit targeting metro cities. Diversify into value-added products (shrimp paste, dried squid jerky) for retail margins.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • LinkedIn B2B outreach targeting hotel procurement teams, restaurant chains, and exporters with product certifications, cold-chain proof, and sustainability messaging.
  • Instagram/Facebook content featuring fishermen stories, processing transparency, and recipe videos using Andaman prawns/squid, partnering with 5-10 micro food influencers (50K-500K followers).
  • Email campaigns to ApEDA-listed importers in Vietnam, Thailand, UAE with product spec sheets, FSSAI/health certificates, and bulk pricing; sponsor 2 seafood trade shows annually.

Free money you didn't know about

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidized loan at 5% for processing setup with 25% government subsidy.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹50 lakh cluster development grant for fisheries processing units in island districts.
🏚
Andaman & Nicobar Islands Fisheries Development Scheme
₹5-10 lakh subsidy for cold storage facilities and export certification for marine product entrepreneurs.
🏚
Island Enterprise Development Scheme
20% subsidy on capital investment for seafood processing units in South Andaman district.
