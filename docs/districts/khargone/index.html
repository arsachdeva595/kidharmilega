ODOP Product

Chilli

Khargone's black soil and semi-arid climate create ideal growing conditions for high-pungency chillies with superior color and aroma. The district has over 50 years of chilli cultivation expertise with established farmer networks and processing infrastructure. Strategic location near major spice markets of Madhya Pradesh and proximity to Gujarat ensures easy market access.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Khargone.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khargone's Mahout and Kasrawad mandi clusters; spend 2-3 weeks with established chilli farmers; study cultivation cycles at Khargone Agriculture Department; analyze pricing at Indore Spice Market (40km away); learn from cooperative societies like Nimar Chilli Growers Cooperative
2
Source Raw Materials
Source quality seeds from Madhya Pradesh State Seeds Corporation (₹15,000-25,000 per acre for 500 kg); procure farmland or lease 2-3 acres from local farmers (₹4,000-8,000 per acre annually); bulk manure and pesticides from Khargone agricultural input dealers; budget ₹60,000-100,000 for first-batch cultivation inputs
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established farmers through contract farming model or lease agricultural land directly; set up basic drying yard (₹50,000-150,000); invest in small chilli grinder/powdering unit (₹200,000-500,000) or partner with SFURTI cluster units; engage with Khargone Spice Processing Units for outsourced powder production at ₹8-12 per kg
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (udyamregistration.gov.in); obtain IEC code from DGFT Indore office; get FSSAI food safety license (₹5,000-10,000); apply ISO 22000 certification for export readiness; register brand with Ministry of Commerce; trademark registration at ₹5,000-15,000
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, Blinkit with 500kg initial inventory; engage Indore-based wholesale spice traders for bulk orders (₹5-10L first order); approach local restaurants and home chefs via WhatsApp catalog; register on Khargone District e-marketplace portal; attend Indore Spice and Agriculture Expo for B2B connects
6
Scale
Expand to 5-10 acres by month 8; develop 3-5 branded products (powder, flakes, organic certified); secure export certification and approach Vietnam/Bangladesh importers; integrate cold storage and modern grinding facility (₹15-25L); hire 5-8 processing staff; target ₹1 Cr annual revenue by month 18

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts showing farm-to-table journey with 5-10 videos per month; partner with food bloggers and home chefs in Indore/Bhopal for unboxing and recipe content; target hashtags #KhargoneChilli #IndianSpices
  • WhatsApp Business group strategy — create community of 500+ restaurant owners, caterers, and bulk buyers in Madhya Pradesh; weekly offers and supply updates; minimum order bulk discounts (10kg = ₹5/kg less)
  • B2B trade show participation at Indore Food & Spice Expo, Bombay Spice Conference, and regional agricultural expos; distribute samples and secure 50-100kg initial orders; collect export buyer contacts
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy + ₹10-25L bank loan for setting up processing unit
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster-based support including common processing facility access, skill training, and marketing grants up to ₹5L per unit
🏚
Madhya Pradesh ODOP Promotion Scheme
₹5-10L grant for branding, packaging, and certification for identified district products; direct govt purchase orders
🏚
MP Agriculture Infrastructure Development Fund
concessional loans up to ₹50L for establishing drying yards, cold storage, and processing units at 5% interest
