Kotputli-Behror is strategically located on the Delhi-Jaipur corridor with proximity to major automotive hubs. The district has established industrial parks, skilled workforce trained in precision engineering, and existing supply relationships with Tier-1 and Tier-2 automotive suppliers. Its DMIC (Delhi-Mumbai Industrial Corridor) status provides logistics advantage and attracts continuous investment in automotive manufacturing.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kotputli-Behror.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kotputli-Behror Industrial Area and Neemrana Industrial Park to meet 15-20 established component manufacturers; study SIAM (Society of Indian Automobile Manufacturers) reports; attend AUTO EXPO and PRAGATI exhibitions in Delhi; join local FISME (Federation of Indian Micro and Small & Medium Enterprises) chapters
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Tiruppur steel suppliers (₹50-80L for first batch), aluminum from Bangalore vendors, electrical components from Delhi's Lajpat Rai Market; negotiate 30-45 day payment terms; budget ₹1.5-2.5 Cr for initial 3-month inventory depending on component type
3
Produce / Partner
Secure 5,000-10,000 sq ft facility in Kotputli-Behror Industrial Park (rent ₹50-100/sq ft/month); purchase CNC machines, testing equipment, and assembly lines (₹50-150L); hire 15-25 skilled technicians; obtain ISO 9001, TS 16949 certifications; partner with 2-3 OEM design consultants for product validation
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (online, free, 10 minutes); apply for IEC code from DGFT portal (₹500); file GST registration (18% slab for auto parts); trademark brand name (₹8,000-15,000); create ISO-compliant quality manuals; get NSIC registration for government purchase eligibility
5
First Sale
Reach out directly to Tier-1 suppliers (Motherson Sumi, Bosch, Mahindra) with samples and certifications; list on TradeKey and Global Sources for export inquiries; engage with APEDA for export documentation support; pitch to SIAM members for supply chain inclusion; attend Autotech exhibitions in Bangalore and Mumbai
6
Scale
After 6 months, pursue ISO TS 16949 renewal; expand to 2-3 additional OEM suppliers; invest in automation to reduce costs by 8-12%; open satellite facility in Chennai or Bangalore for southern India OEM proximity; hire business development manager for export markets; apply for PLI scheme incentives for advanced component manufacturing
