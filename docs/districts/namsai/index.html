ODOP Product

Turmeric

Namsai's unique geographical location in the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas provides ideal altitude, rainfall, and soil conditions for high-curcumin turmeric cultivation. The region's traditional farmers have perfected sustainable farming techniques over generations, producing turmeric that commands premium pricing in national and international markets. The cool climate and mineral-rich soil naturally enhance the bioactive compound concentration without chemical interventions.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Namsai.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Namsai district agricultural department and connect with turmeric farming clusters around Namti and Changlang areas; study curcumin testing standards at agricultural universities in Assam; analyze pricing on Agrimarket and Namaste India platform for current market rates.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source directly from Namsai farmer cooperatives (Namsai District Agricultural Cooperative Society); initial 5-ton batch costs ₹200,000-₹400,000 depending on organic certification status; negotiate bulk pricing from established growers in Namsai and Changlang.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 organic certified farmers in Namsai for guaranteed supply and quality control; establish turmeric curing and drying facility near Namsai town (rental ₹15,000-₹25,000/month); contract a dry-processing unit for powder production or partner with existing SFURTI clusters.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam certificate (online via udyamregistration.gov.in) in Agri-Processing category; obtain IEC code from Namsai Chamber of Commerce for export; get organic certification from APEDA/IFOAM-recognized agencies (₹30,000-₹50,000); file trademark and FSSAI registration for packaged products.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, Blinkit, and regional marketplaces; approach organic retail chains (Organic Brands, Nature's Basket) for B2B partnerships; create Instagram/YouTube content on curcumin benefits and sustainable farming; participate in natural products expos in Delhi and Mumbai; contact supplement manufacturers via LinkedIn for bulk orders.
6
Scale
Expand farmer network to 20+ organic growers; establish processing facility with quality testing lab; launch 3-5 value-added SKUs (capsules, turmeric paste, blended powders); build export partnerships through Trade India and indiatradefair.com; secure institutional buyers (hotels, wellness chains, hospitals); achieve FSSC 22000 certification for international expansion.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with Ayurveda and wellness influencers (10K-100K followers) on Instagram for curcumin science content; offer testing kits showing superior curcumin levels vs. competitors; tag micro-influencers in health and cooking niches for organic reach.
  • Launch 'Farmer Stories' video series on YouTube showing Namsai farming practices; create educational content on curcumin absorption, turmeric myths, and sustainable agriculture; target health-conscious Facebook groups and wellness communities.
  • Sponsor local food festivals and farmers markets in Tier-1 cities (Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai); distribute free turmeric samples with QR codes linking to educational content; partner with Ayurveda clinics and wellness centers for point-of-sale promotion.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)

concessional loans and grants for agricultural value addition enterprises in Arunachal Pradesh
