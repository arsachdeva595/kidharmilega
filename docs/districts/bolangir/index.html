Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bolangir.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bolangir cotton farming clusters near Titilagarh and Patnagarh towns; speak with 10-15 established cotton farmers and local agricultural officers; study soil composition and seasonal patterns at Bolangir Agricultural Science Centre
2
Source Raw Materials
Purchase high-quality cotton seeds from certified sources like Odisha State Seed Corporation or ICAR-ATARI Bhubaneswar; estimated first-batch cost ₹3-5 lakhs for 5-10 acres; negotiate with local fertiliser suppliers for bulk orders at 8-12% discount
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 20-30 smallholder cotton farmers in Bolangir block through cooperative model; provide inputs and technical support; establish ginning arrangement at nearest ginnery (Titilagarh or Patnagarh); invest ₹50-80K for quality testing and cleaning infrastructure
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam through https://udyamregistration.gov.in (free, 10 minutes); obtain IEC code from nearest Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) office in Cuttack (₹0, takes 7 days); apply for organic/sustainable certification if targeting premium segment (₹30-50K annually); brand identity cost ₹2-3L
5
First Sale
List on TradeKey and Global Sources for B2B textile mill inquiries; approach 5-10 local spinning mills directly with samples; register on Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) portals; partner with 1-2 cotton traders in Bolangir or Sonepur APMC for immediate sales; create WhatsApp business updates for regular buyers
6
Scale
After stabilising supply from 30 farmers, approach larger textile companies in Tamil Nadu (Tiruppur, Coimbatore) for bulk contracts; invest ₹50L in basic cotton grading and baling machinery; build export relationships through trade fairs (ITMA Asia) and cotton trader associations; expand to 100+ farmers by month 18 through cooperative expansion
