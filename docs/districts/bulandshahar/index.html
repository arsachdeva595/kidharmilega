Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bulandshahar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khurja pottery cluster (25km from Bulandshahar) to observe production techniques; connect with Bulandshahar Ceramic Association; attend potter workshops in Khanpur and Lohgarh villages; study GI certification standards from DC Handicraft office in Bulandshahar.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw clay from local suppliers in Bulandshahar and Khurja (approx ₹8-12/kg bulk); procure glazes from suppliers in Delhi (Karol Bagh) or Jaipur; first batch of 500 pieces requires ₹1.5-2.5L depending on complexity; establish relationships with 2-3 suppliers for consistent quality.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing potter collectives in Lohgarh and Khanpur villages or hire trained artisans (₹15-25K monthly per skilled potter); set up a 1000-1500 sq ft production workspace with potter's wheels, kilns, and drying racks; start with low-fire ceramic production (1000-1200°C) before moving to high-fire; maintain quality control through personal involvement.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (MSME) portal; obtain IEC code for export from DGFT; apply for GI-tagged product certification from DC Handicraft Bulandshahar; create brand identity reflecting Bulandshahar's heritage; register trademark with IP India; obtain FSSAI if producing food-contact ceramics.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram and Pinterest presence showcasing artisan stories and behind-the-scenes production; list on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart Artisan; pitch to home décor retailers like Craftsvilla, FabIndia, and The Artisan Box; initiate outreach to corporate gifting companies; create WhatsApp Business catalog for direct customer sales; attend IITF and other handicraft expos for B2B connections.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range to include tableware, planters, and statement pieces; hire additional artisans and mentor apprentices; explore design collaborations with fashion/lifestyle brands; establish export partnerships with European and US home décor distributors; set up a studio showroom in Delhi NCR for retail visibility; implement batch production for high-demand items while maintaining artisanal quality.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels series showing 15-30 second clay-to-ceramic transformations with artisan interviews; use hashtags #BulandshaharCeramics #IndianCraftmanship #EcoLiving; engage micro-influencers in home décor and sustainable living (10-50K followers).
  • Partner with eco-conscious lifestyle brands for collaborative gifting campaigns; create limited-edition co-branded collections; target corporate ESG buyers via LinkedIn B2B outreach and sustainability-focused publications.
  • Host virtual potter workshops and live glazing sessions on YouTube/Instagram Live to build community; create educational content on ceramic sustainability and traditional techniques; convert workshop attendees into product buyers through exclusive discounts.
