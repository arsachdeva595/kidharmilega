Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Alappuzha.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Alappuzha's Coir Board office and Kumarakom artisan clusters (Kainakari, Cheriyan Nagar) to understand production processes, meet established producers, and study fiber quality standards. Spend 2-3 weeks learning from established coir cooperatives like Alappuzha Coir Board.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source coconut husks directly from local coconut processors in Alappuzha (₹8-12/kg), coir fiber from established spinners in Cherthala region (₹80-120/kg), and natural dyes from Kerala spice markets. First batch of 500 kg coir fiber: ₹50,000-60,000. Establish relationships with 2-3 suppliers for consistency.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 15-25 registered artisans in Alappuzha's weaving clusters through cooperatives or direct contracts. Start with mat and doormat production (lower skill threshold), then expand to rugs and decorative items. Work with Alappuzha District Cooperative or Coir Board for artisan verification and quality assurance.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (Ministry of MSME) as Handicraft Artisan cluster enterprise. Obtain IEC code from DGFT for export eligibility. Apply for GI authentication certificate from DC Handicraft. Create brand identity emphasizing 'Handwoven Alappuzha Heritage.' Register trademark with IP office.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade and Flipkart Handicraft categories with 15-20 SKUs. Create Instagram presence showcasing artisan stories and production process. Approach 10-15 home decor retailers (Urban Ladder, Fabindia partners) with wholesale samples at ₹400-600 per mat. Email 50 corporate gifting companies (Giftzing, CorporateGifts.com) with bulk quotations.
6
Scale
After first ₹20L revenue: Hire operations manager, expand artisan network to 50+, develop 5-10 export markets through trade shows (Home Textile Expo, Germany's Heimtextil). Launch own e-commerce website with SEO focus on 'sustainable home decor.' Establish quality certification (ISO 9001). Explore B2B partnerships with international distributors.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 20-30 eco-conscious lifestyle Instagram influencers (10K-100K followers) for product reviews and behind-the-scenes artisan content. Budget: ₹50,000-100,000/month. Target: Sustainable living niche audience.
  • Create YouTube documentary series 'Hands of Alappuzha' showing artisan stories, fiber-to-product journey. Upload monthly. Optimize for SEO keywords: 'handmade coir mats,' 'eco-friendly home decor.' Target: Organic YouTube discovery.
  • Participate in 3-4 major trade shows annually (Heimtextil Frankfurt, Home & Gift Expo India, Decorex Mumbai). Budget: ₹300,000-500,000. Focus: B2B distributor acquisition and international visibility.
