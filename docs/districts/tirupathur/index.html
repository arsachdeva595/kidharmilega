ODOP Product

Leather products

Tirupathur has been a leather craft hub for over 200 years with a concentrated cluster of skilled leather artisans. The district's historical access to raw materials, established supply chains, and deep cultural knowledge of tanning and finishing techniques make it uniquely positioned for high-quality leather product manufacturing. The existing ODOP ecosystem provides ready access to master craftspeople and established distribution networks.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tirupathur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tirupathur leather cluster hub in Ambur town (30km from district HQ). Connect with SITRA (Southern India Textile Research Association) and tour established tanneries like those in Kasinathapuram leather zone. Spend 2-3 weeks understanding vegetable tanning vs chrome tanning processes. Connect with Tirupathur ODOP association for market insights.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source vegetable-tanned hides from established suppliers in Ambur (Karur Leathers Ltd, local SIDCO-registered units). For first batch of 50 bags + 20 wallets, allocate ₹50,000-75,000. Source fittings from Palar metal clusters nearby. Local thread from Erode suppliers. Negotiate credit terms (15-30 days) once relationship established.
3
Produce / Partner
Register with nearest District Industries Centre (DIC) in Tirupathur. Either establish small workshop (₹5-10L investment) or partner with existing artisan groups in Ambur-Kasinathapuram belt (revenue-share model 60-40). Start with 100-150 pieces monthly for first 3 months using 4-5 skilled craftspeople. Use traditional hand-stitching for premium positioning.
4
Brand & Register
File Udyam registration (micro-enterprise) immediately. Apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office. Register trademark with IP India (₹5,000-10,000). Design GI-authenticated label + certificate of origin tags. Obtain leather quality certification from leather testing labs. Set up FSSAI registration if offering any packaged product components.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C website with Instagram/Pinterest focus (leather craftspeople highly successful on visual platforms). Simultaneously register on Amazon Handmade (low commission, high brand visibility). Target Myntra's Artisan Collective. Reach out to 20-30 premium lifestyle boutiques in Chennai, Bangalore, Mumbai with wholesale rates. Use WhatsApp Business for personalized B2B outreach. Run micro-influencer campaigns with sustainable fashion creators.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand SKU range to belts, shoes, laptop bags. Hire 2-3 more artisans. Launch wholesale distribution via GITC centers in major metros. Enter international e-commerce (Etsy, Shopify, Amazon US). Apply for exports via SITRA support. Participate in trade shows (Gift Mela Delhi, International Leather Fair Mumbai). Build corporate gifting vertical with 20+ B2B clients.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with sustainable fashion micro-influencers (50K-500K followers) in India and US for 3-month product seeding campaigns, targeting conscious consumer segments with behind-the-scenes artisan story content on Instagram Reels and TikTok.
  • Launch 'Leather Stories' podcast featuring Tirupathur artisan interviews, distributed on Spotify and LinkedIn, positioning brand as heritage custodian; promote via LinkedIn ads to B2B corporate audiences.
  • Run Pinterest Ads targeting keywords like 'sustainable leather bags India' and 'ethical leather goods' with carousel pins showing product + artisan craftsmanship; Pinterest users show 70% purchase intent for handmade goods.
