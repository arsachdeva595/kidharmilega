ODOP Product

Venkatagiri Sarees

Chittoor has a 400+ year heritage of Venkatagiri saree weaving centered in Venkatagiri village. The district's skilled artisan communities, traditional pit-loom infrastructure, and proximity to cotton-growing regions make it the natural epicenter for authentic production. The GI tag (2009) protects the brand and ensures premium pricing globally.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Chittoor.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Venkatagiri village (35km from Chittoor town) and spend 2-3 weeks learning from master weavers. Visit Chittoor Handloom Cluster Centre and connect with Handloom Associations. Study 5-10 existing Venkatagiri saree businesses to understand pricing, design trends, and export channels.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium cotton yarn from IICT (Indian Institute of Cotton Technology) approved suppliers in Coimbatore or directly from textile mills in Tamil Nadu. Gold/silver threads from authorized suppliers in Surat (₹8,000-12,000 per kg). First batch of 20 sarees requires ₹1,50,000-2,00,000 in raw materials. Partner with 2-3 master weavers initially rather than setting up own looms.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish direct partnership with 3-5 experienced Venkatagiri weavers through local artisan networks or Chittoor Handicrafts Association. Negotiate per-saree production rates (typically ₹2,500-4,500 per saree in labor). Provide design specifications and quality checks. Consider joining SFURTI cluster initiative for technical support and collective marketing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro Business) on udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from DGFT for export eligibility (₹0, online). Register trademark with GI tag reference to strengthen IP. Obtain certification from Chittoor District Handloom Association confirming authentic Venkatagiri origin. Set up GST registration if turnover exceeds ₹20L.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C website with high-quality photography (₹50,000-1,00,000) showcasing weaving process. List on Amazon Handmade and Etsy (20-30% commission). Reach out to 50+ ethnic boutiques in Bangalore, Delhi, Mumbai with wholesale pricing. Partner with wedding platforms like Shaadi.com for occasion-based marketing. Send 10 samples to international retailers in USA/UK with GI certification documentation.
6
Scale
After 6 months of ₹5-10L monthly revenue, expand to 10-15 weaver partnerships. Invest in professional photography/styling (₹1,50,000). Launch Instagram/Pinterest content strategy with weaving process videos. Attend 2-3 major handloom/textile exhibitions (IITF, India Handloom Festival). Set up distribution in 3-5 major metro cities through ethical middlemen. Apply for export incentives under RODTEP scheme.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels (15-30 sec) showing real weaver hands at the loom with GI tag story. Use hashtags #VenkatagiriSaree #HandloomHeritag #ChittoorPride. Target affluent Indian women (INR 2L+ household income) aged 25-50. Budget: ₹30,000/month for influencer partnerships with ethnic fashion creators.
  • Host live Zoom demonstrations every 2 weeks: 'Meet the Weaver' sessions where customers interact directly with artisans. Offer 10% discount for attendees who make purchases within 48 hours. Drive attendance via email list (start with 500 warm contacts) and Pinterest ads targeting 'handloom sarees' + 'sustainable fashion'.
  • Partner with 5 wedding planners in Bangalore/Hyderabad to feature Venkatagiri sarees as 'Bride's Heritage Choice'. Offer 15% wholesale discount for bulk orders (10+ sarees). Create case studies of 3 real wedding stories with photos. Budget: ₹50,000 for collateral + relationship building.
