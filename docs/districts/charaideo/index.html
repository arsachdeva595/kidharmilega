ODOP Product

Aromatic Rice

Charaideo district's alluvial soil, favorable monsoon climate, and proximity to the Brahmaputra river create ideal conditions for cultivating aromatic rice varieties. The region has a 200+ year tradition of rice farming, with local farmer networks and established supply chains. The water-rich environment and temperature stability make it uniquely suited for maintaining the rice's signature aroma and flavor profile.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Charaideo.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Charaideo district's farming clusters in Naziira and Borholla blocks; attend Assam Agricultural Department workshops; meet 5-10 established rice dealers at Guwahati Grain Market and Charaideo rice mandi to understand local pricing, varietals, and seasonal patterns.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source aromatic rice seeds from Assam Agricultural University (Jorhat) or certified seed suppliers; negotiate first-batch purchase of 5-10 MT from farmer collectives in Charaideo (₹40,000-50,000 per MT); arrange storage at local cold storage facilities (₹15,000/month for 20 MT capacity).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established rice mills in Charaideo (Sivasagar or Naziira) for processing, sorting, and packaging (contract: ₹2-4 per kg processing fee); or invest in small rice mill machinery (₹8-15L) for in-house processing; maintain traceability through farmer contracts and batch tagging.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in as Food & Grain Processing enterprise; apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export capability; register brand name at Trademark Registry (₹5,000-10,000); obtain FSSAI registration (₹2,000-5,000) and state agricultural department certification.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and BigBasket with ₹500-1000/kg premium positioning; approach top 20 biryani restaurants in Bangalore, Hyderabad, Delhi via direct sales with samples; list on specialty platforms (Hoopla, Naturevibe); reach export distributors in Dubai (Spinneys, LuLu Group) via trade shows or Asenso platform.
6
Scale
After first 20 MT sold, secure farmer supply contracts for 100+ MT annually; invest in branded packaging and QR-tracked batches; establish presence in 50+ HORECA outlets across 5 metro cities; launch premium sub-brand for export markets; partner with food influencers and chefs for content marketing.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 10-15 top biryani chefs in metros; create Instagram/YouTube content showing farm-to-table journey from Charaideo fields to Michelin-starred kitchens; offer chef collaborations and limited-edition packaging.
  • Launch Google & Instagram ads targeting keywords 'premium basmati alternative' and 'aromatic rice biryani' in metros; geo-target Charaideo district exports to Gulf audiences via Facebook.
  • Sponsor agricultural and food festivals in Guwahati and Assam; participate in gourmet food expos in Delhi, Mumbai; set up trade stalls at international food import shows to build distributor networks.
