Granite and Marble Blocks

Banaskantha is strategically located on the Aravalli range with abundant granite and marble deposits. The district has established quarrying infrastructure, skilled labor force with decades of stone-cutting expertise, and proximity to major ports making it ideal for bulk dimensional stone production and export.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Banaskantha.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Palanpur and Ambaji quarry clusters in Banaskantha; meet 5-10 established stone exporters to understand pricing, quality grades, export logistics, and certification requirements. Attend stone expos in Mumbai and Jaipur.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact stone quarries near Palanpur (minimum order 50-100 blocks per transaction). First batch cost: ₹8-12 lakhs for 100 mixed grade blocks. Negotiate long-term supplier agreements to secure steady raw material.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing stone processing units in Banaskantha for cutting/finishing or establish a small finishing center (₹30-50 lakh setup). Hire experienced quarry workers and stone cutters from local labor pools.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (MSME), obtain IEC code from local customs authority in Ahmedabad. Create brand identity focusing on quality certifications (ISO 9001, SSPC membership). Set up warehouse in Palanpur or Ambaji.
5
First Sale
List on IndiaMART and TradeKey for B2B visibility; create WhatsApp catalog for direct contractor sales; contact 20 architecture firms monthly with samples; attend stone trade shows; build email outreach to importers in top export countries.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish own small quarrying lease or long-term quarry partnerships; hire dedicated export compliance officer; open small showroom in Ahmedabad or Mumbai; develop premium product lines (exotic finishes); explore marble block supply contracts with real estate groups.
