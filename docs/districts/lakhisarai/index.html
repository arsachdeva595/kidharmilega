Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Lakhisarai.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Lakhisarai farmer cooperative societies in Lakhisarai town and Rampur blocks. Study APEDA export data for masoor dal. Interview 5-10 established local masoor dal traders at Lakhisarai mandi. Understand moisture content, sorting standards, and GST implications.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified masoor seeds from IARI Delhi or local seed stores (₹120-150/kg). Initial batch: 10 quintals (₹15,000-20,000). Partner with established local farmers through written agreements. First procurement cost: ₹80,000-120,000 for 10-15 quintals raw masoor.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 farmers in Lakhisarai for contract farming. Invest in basic dal mill equipment (₹400,000-600,000) for sorting, cleaning, packaging OR outsource milling to existing facilities (₹8-12 per kg). Start with 50 quintals production in first season (June-August). Ensure FSSAI license and quality certification.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online for MSME status. Apply for IEC code from nearest APEDA office in Patna (45km away) for export eligibility. Brand setup: Design label (₹15,000-25,000), register trademark with ₹9,000-15,000 filing, obtain FSSAI license (₹7,000-12,000). Total: ₹35,000-60,000.
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket Fresh and Amazon Prime Now with 500kg initial stock (₹50,000 value). Simultaneously pitch to Spencer's, Reliance Smart Stores in Bihar. Create WhatsApp business broadcast list of 200 local retailers. Offer introductory 15% discount for first 30 days. Target 5,000 kg monthly sales by month 4.
6
Scale
After 6 months, launch masoor flour and sprouted masoor line via same channels. Hire 2-3 sales reps to cover Patna, Bhagalpur wholesale markets. Apply for PMEGP subsidy for dal mill expansion. Target 500kg daily output. Explore B2B tie-ups with ITC e-Choupal for farmer linkage programs.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 30-second Instagram Reels showing farm-to-table journey from Lakhisarai fields to kitchen, targeting health-conscious 25-45 age group. Run ₹500/day budget ads in Metros+Tier 2 cities. Partner with 10 micro-influencers (50k-100k followers) for unboxing videos.
  • Launch a YouTube channel 'Lakhisarai Masoor Chronicles' featuring recipes, nutritional content, and farmer interviews. Target 50k subscribers in year 1. Monetize through YouTube ads + affiliate links to BigBasket/Amazon.
  • Run WhatsApp marketing to corporate wellness programs in Bangalore, Pune, Delhi offering bulk discounts (₹400-450/kg vs retail ₹600/kg). Create 500-contact list of HR managers via LinkedIn. Offer ₹20,000 minimum orders with free delivery.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (up to 25% for general category) for food processing and packaging units in Tier 2/3 districts.
🏛
APEDA Export Promotion Scheme
subsidy up to 5% of FOB value for masoor dal exports to identified priority markets, training on export norms, and certification support.
🏚
Bihar Krishi Sinchayee Yojana (Bihar Agricultural Promotion Policy)
subsidized inputs, irrigation support, and ₹50,000-100,000 per hectare crop insurance for pulse farmers.
🏚
Bihar Laghu Udyog Scheme
₹5L-10L loans at 5% interest for MSME food processing units with repayment holiday of 18 months in agricultural sectors.
Registration required: Required — udyamregistration.gov.in
