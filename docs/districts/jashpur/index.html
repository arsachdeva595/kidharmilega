ODOP Product

Litchi

Jashpur's subtropical climate, well-drained laterite soil, and altitude (400-600m) create optimal conditions for litchi cultivation with extended flowering and fruiting periods. The district's farming community has developed specialized knowledge in litchi horticulture over the past two decades, making it a natural hub for quality litchi production and processing.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Jashpur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established litchi orchards in Jashpur block and Pathalgaon area; connect with Chhattisgarh Agricultural Department's Horticulture Division in Raipur; study litchi farming practices at IGKV (Indira Gandhi Krishi Vishwavidyalaya) research centers; analyze market data from Raipur and Delhi wholesale markets
2
Source Raw Materials
Source litchi saplings from ICAR Research Center at Joka (West Bengal) or local Jashpur nurseries; first-batch sourcing cost ₹2-4L for 100-150 saplings or ₹50-80K for 500kg fresh litchi from local growers; establish relationships with Jashpur district's 2,000+ litchi farmers
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established litchi orchards for contract farming or lease 1-2 acres (₹30-50K annually); set up small processing unit near Jashpur town with cold storage (₹8-15L investment) or collaborate with existing cooperative units; hire seasonal laborers during May-June harvest
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise) through udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT for export readiness; register brand with state trademark; obtain FSSAI registration for processed products (₹10-15K); get certification from organic bodies if pursuing organic litchi
5
First Sale
Launch on JioMart, Blinkit, and BigBasket with premium positioning; sell to HoReCa segment (hotel chains in Raipur, Bilaspur, Delhi); approach juice/beverage manufacturers like Tropicana and Hindustan Unilever for supply contracts; list on Udyam Marketplace and Amazon Business; attend Jashpur district agri-melas
6
Scale
Expand processing capacity to 50-100 tonnes annually; develop export certifications (Globalg.a.p., Agmark); build supply chain to 5-7 metros; create litchi-based derivative products; scale agri-tourism offering; partner with logistics providers for cold chain expansion across India

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram/YouTube content showing farm-to-table journey, harvest behind-the-scenes, nutritional benefits; partner with health and wellness influencers in metro cities for promotional campaigns targeting premium health-conscious consumers
  • B2B email outreach to 500+ restaurants, juice bars, and food manufacturers in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with product samples and bulk pricing; participate in Indian Food & Agriculture Export Council (IFAEC) trade shows
  • Launch seasonal WhatsApp Business campaigns and SMS promotions during litchi season (May-June) targeting previous customers and geo-targeted audiences; create educational content on litchi health benefits for wellness blogs and newsletters
