Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sonitpur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sonitpur rice mills cluster around Tezpur and Dhekelia; meet 5-10 mill owners and farmer cooperatives; study Assam Agricultural Department reports on rice yields and market trends; attend AICRA (All India Cereals & Pulses Association) meetings
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact primary rice farmers in Sonitpur through Sonitpur Krishi Bhavan; establish relationships with 2-3 mill owners; first batch of 50 tonnes costs ₹8-12 lakhs depending on quality grade and processing method; visit Tezpur wholesale mandi for supplier verification
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing FSSAI-certified rice mills in Tezpur (e.g., local mills processing parboiled rice); negotiate toll processing rates of ₹200-300 per tonne; or establish own small 1-2 tonne/day facility with steaming, drying, and packaging equipment at ₹30-50 lakhs
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry of MSME) online within 5 minutes; obtain IEC code from DGFT (Digital Gateway); apply for FSSAI license (₹8000-12000, 14-21 days approval); trademark brand name at IP India; get ISO 22000 certification for food safety
5
First Sale
Launch D2C on Amazon Seller Central and Flipkart with SEO-optimized listings; reach 1000 direct customers via Instagram/Facebook ads targeting premium food buyers; approach 20 modern retail chains (BigBasket, Grofers, local premium stores) with product samples; register on TradeKey and Global Food Partners for B2B wholesale inquiries
6
Scale
After 6 months with ₹15-20L monthly revenue, invest in own processing facility; expand product range to organic, fortified, and specialty rice varieties; hire export agent for 2-3 Asian markets; scale D2C through influencer partnerships with food/health creators; establish distribution in 5-10 major cities through regional distributors
