ODOP Product

Cashew

Kanyakumari's tropical climate with optimal temperature (20-30°C) and monsoon patterns create ideal growing conditions for high-quality cashew cultivation. The district has 80+ years of cashew farming heritage with established supply chains, skilled workforce, and proximity to major ports (Cochin, Tuticorin) for export. Local cooperative societies and processing units provide ready infrastructure for scaling cashew business operations.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kanyakumari.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nagercoil & Padmanabhapuram cashew clusters in Kanyakumari district; connect with Tamil Nadu Cashew Development Council; attend cashew processing seminars at Arippa cooperative society; study competitor brands like Kandhari and local processors
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cashews (RCN) from local farmers at Nagercoil mandis; cost approximately ₹8,000-10,000 per 50kg bag; establish direct relationships with 2-3 supplier families; initial stock: ₹2-3 lakhs for 1000kg batch
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established cashew processing units in Nagercoil or Padmanabhapuram offering contract processing (₹40-60 per kg); or invest in small roasting-packing equipment (₹2-4L) and hire 2-3 skilled processors; maintain ISO 22000 food safety compliance from day one
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) classification with district MSME center; apply for IEC code through customs at Tuticorin port; register trademark via IP India; obtain FSSAI license (₹5,000-15,000) for food processing; design packaging complying with AGMARK standards
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh & Flipkart Supermarket with ₹50-100L initial inventory; create Instagram/Facebook content showcasing harvest-to-table story from Kanyakumari; partner with premium online grocery platforms (Blinkit, Zepto) for D2C reach; approach corporate bulk buyers via LinkedIn with samples
6
Scale
After 6 months profitability, expand product line (cashew butter, flavored variants); secure B2B contracts with 2-3 major food companies; establish direct export partnerships with importers in US/EU; open small retail outlet in Cochin for regional visibility; consider contract farming agreements with 20-30 local farmers to secure supply

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% of project cost) for agri-processing startups; applicable for ₹5-10L project cost ventures
🏛
MUDRA Yojana
business loans up to ₹10L at subsidized rates for food processing micro-enterprises without collateral
🏚
Tamil Nadu Agro-Based Export Processing Scheme
subsidy for export-oriented cashew processing units; 5% interest subvention on term loans
🏚
NABARD Credit Linked Capital Subsidy Scheme
up to 35% subsidy for food processing equipment in rural areas of Kanyakumari district
