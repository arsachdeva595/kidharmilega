The district's cooperative farming networks and established cold chain infrastructure make it the fastest-growing agricultural export hub in South India.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Thiruvarur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Thiruvallur agricultural market (Thiruvallur Vellai Market, 8km from Tiruvarur town) and Karaikudi spice market; attend Tamil Nadu Agricultural University (TNAU) workshops in Coimbatore; connect with local farmer producer companies (FPCs) in Thiruvarur cooperative federation; study export standards at Cochin Port Authority website
2
Source Raw Materials
Source vegetable/rice seeds from Tamil Nadu Seeds Corporation (TNSC) offices in Thiruvallur (₹50,000-150,000 for mixed seeds). Purchase organic fertilizers from local cooperatives. First-batch cost for 2-5 acres: ₹300,000-600,000 including seeds, labor, water charges for 4-month cycle
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established small farmers in Nannilam or Mannargudi villages (30km from district HQ) through revenue-sharing model, or lease agricultural land via TNAU's farm-linking portal. Set up basic cold storage unit (₹800,000-1.2M) in Tiruvarur town for post-harvest handling. Join district farmer producer company network for collective harvesting and grading
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam status as agricultural enterprise; apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at Cochin/Chennai port for export eligibility; obtain Agmark certification from Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority (APEDA); design brand with GI-inspired Tamil artwork; register trademark with IP India office
5
First Sale
Launch on Flipkart Wholesale and Udaan B2B platforms; approach 5-10 premium retail chains (Spencer's, Aditya Birla's stores in Chennai/Tamil Nadu); list on Jio Mart for B2C; contact restaurant groups (Sangeetha, Rajdhani chains); approach corporate bulk buyers via LinkedIn; attend Agrotech India trade shows
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish processing unit for value-added products (packaged rice varieties, dried vegetable powders); hire export consultant to identify EU and US markets; expand to 10-15 acre farming partnerships; apply for PMEGP subsidy for processing equipment; develop organic certification for 30% price premium; partner with 2-3 logistics providers for pan-India delivery

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (25-35%) for agricultural processing unit setup with loan component
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
infrastructure grants up to ₹30 lakh for farm clusters and cold storage in Thiruvarur region
🏚
Tamil Nadu Agro-Processing Policy 2015
capital subsidy of 25% (capped ₹50L) for food processing units, plus GST refund on equipment
🏚
TNAU Farm Science Center Subsidy
40-50% cost support for certified organic farm conversion and soil health testing in Thiruvarur district
