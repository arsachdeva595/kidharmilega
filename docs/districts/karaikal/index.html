Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Karaikal.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Puducherry's chemical manufacturing clusters in Cuddalore and Pondicherry; connect with SIDBI Puducherry; study existing players like HPCL, IOC facilities; attend chemical trade shows at Chennai's TIDCO Industrial Park; review DCPP (Department of Chemicals and Petrochemicals) industry reports.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Vadodara (Gujarat) chemical suppliers or Surat textile chemical dealers; establish accounts with major suppliers like CHEMTECH GROUP or LOBA CHEMIE; first batch procurement (10-20 MT) estimated at ₹15-25 lakhs depending on product type; negotiate credit terms with suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with contract manufacturers in Cuddalore SIPCOT or rent space in Karaikal KIADB industrial park (₹8-15K/month for 2000 sq.ft.); hire experienced production chemists from BITS Pilani or NIT Trichy; install basic reactors and distillation units (₹20-30L); obtain industry-specific certifications (ISO 9001, ISO 14001).
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam for MSME classification; obtain IEC code from nearest customs office (Karaikal Port); file DPIIT recognition; register trademark with IP India; obtain FSSAI (if food-grade chemicals) or PCI (if pharmaceutical-grade); secure DSC for GST filing.
5
First Sale
Launch on IndiaMART and TradeKey with bulk catalogs; cold-contact 500 pharmaceutical and textile companies via LinkedIn; partner with chemical distributors like CHEMTECH or VINAYAK CHEMICALS for pan-India reach; leverage Karaikal Port for direct international shipments to ASEAN; attend ChemExpo India; join FICCI's chemical committee for B2B connections.
6
Scale
Expand product line to niche specialties (pharma intermediates, environmental-grade solvents); establish forward contracts with top 20 customers; invest in automated production (₹50L+) to reduce per-unit cost by 15-20%; hire export compliance officer; apply for DSIR R&D incentives; build distributor network in Gujarat, Maharashtra, and Tamil Nadu.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Run LinkedIn B2B campaigns targeting pharma procurement managers in Hyderabad, Mumbai, and Bangalore with case studies on cost reduction; budget ₹2-3L/month for 6 months.
  • Host quarterly webinars on 'Chemical Compliance & Export Strategy' via FICCI platform; position founder as thought leader; generate 20-30 qualified B2B leads per event.
  • Partner with logistics firms like ALLCARGO to sponsor chemical trade publications (IndiaChemical.IN, Chem Industry Magazine); secure 2-3 feature articles annually highlighting innovation and export success.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs grant for manufacturing units; applicable for first-time entrepreneurs in chemical sector with 10-year tax holiday.
🏛
DSIR R&D Incentive Scheme
20-30% subsidy on chemical innovation and technology upgrade; applicable for ISO-certified manufacturers developing specialty chemicals.
🏚
Puducherry Industrial Investment Promotion Scheme (PIIPS)
5-year property tax exemption and 5% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹5 crores for chemical manufacturers in notified zones.
🏚
Puducherry Export Promotion Scheme
2% subsidy on FOB value for chemical exports and ₹5 lakhs one-time assistance for export certification (ISO, CE, FDA).
