ODOP Product

Cotton based processing (ginning) & garmenting

Davanagere has a 150+ year legacy in cotton processing with established ginning mills and garmenting units. The district's strategic location, skilled workforce, and integrated cotton supply chain from raw fiber to finished garments make it uniquely positioned for textile manufacturing. Access to quality cotton from neighboring regions and well-developed infrastructure support sustainable production.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Davanagere.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Davanagere textile cluster (Harihar, Harihara industrial area) to study 10+ functioning ginning mills and garmenting units. Attend textile expos at Bangalore or Tiruppur. Connect with District Industries Center (DIC Davanagere) for market insights and existing ODOP producer networks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton directly from farmers or cotton commission agents in Davanagere (₹4,500-5,500/quintal). Partner with 2-3 established ginning mills for processed lint (₹8,000-10,000/quintal). First batch: 10 quintals raw cotton (~₹55-60 lakhs with processing and yarn sourcing).
3
Produce / Partner
Either lease existing garmenting unit in Davanagere (₹1.5-3L/month) or partner with contract manufacturers in Harihar industrial area. Hire 15-20 skilled stitchers (avg ₹12,000-15,000/month). Implement quality control protocols aligned with ISO 9001. Consider SFURTI cluster support for infrastructure.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification) online at udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office for export capability. Register trademark at IPO for brand name. Obtain textile testing certificates from ATIRA (Ahmedabad) or local testing labs. Open business bank account with HDFC/ICICI for bulk textile operations.
5
First Sale
List on B2B platforms: IndiaFilings, TradeKey, Global Sources. Launch Instagram/Facebook with product photos and bulk pricing. Approach 50+ regional distributors in Karnataka and Andhra Pradesh. Apply for government contracts via GeM portal. Attend Bangalore Textile Expo and Connect B2B events. Cold outreach to 100+ corporate uniform suppliers.
6
Scale
After ₹30L revenue: Invest ₹50L in automated cutting/sewing machines. Expand to 50+ skilled workers. Open warehouse in Bangalore (₹2.5L/month) for quick B2B fulfillment. Launch 3-4 branded product lines (formal wear, casual, activewear). Apply for SFURTI or PM Vishwakarma grants for capacity expansion. Target ₹5Cr revenue by month 24.

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries) - up to ₹30L per cluster for ginning/garmenting infrastructure and skill development
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program) - up to ₹10L (25% government subsidy) for textile manufacturing MSMEs
🏚
Karnataka Udyam Abhivruddhi Yojana - 5% interest subsidy on loans up to ₹50L for textile sector MSMEs
🏚
Karnataka Export Promotion Scheme - 5% subsidy on export value for textile products under ODOP category
