Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sundergarh.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sundergarh district headquarters and Rourkela town; meet artisan clusters around Rajganj and Bisra blocks. Study existing producers at Sundergarh Handloom & Handicraft Centre. Attend IITF or Surajkund Crafts Mela to benchmark market positioning.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw bamboo (₹40-60/kg) from local cooperatives like Sundergarh Bamboo Cooperative or ODOP suppliers in Rajganj block. Order initial 500kg batch at ₹25,000-30,000. Source jute and natural dyes from Kolkata suppliers (₹8,000). Total first batch raw material cost: ₹35,000-40,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established artisan families in Rajganj block via referrals from DC Handicraft office. Agree on per-piece rates (₹150-300 per basket). Start with 50-100 pieces monthly. Alternatively, hire 2-3 apprentices and set up a 600 sq ft workshop in Rourkela for ₹15,000/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (formerly MSME) at udyamregistration.gov.in for zero cost; obtain IEC from nearest DGFT office in Rourkela (₹0, online). Register brand name at ipindiaonline.gov.in (₹4,500-9,000). Create GST number registration. Design logo at Fiverr (₹3,000-5,000).
5
First Sale
List on Etsy with 20+ professional photos, emphasizing handmade + eco-certifications; target keyword 'bamboo baskets handmade.' Simultaneously launch Instagram account with artisan backstory content. Apply to Amazon Handmade Artisan Program. Approach boutique home décor stores in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with physical samples.
6
Scale
After 6 months, if achieving ₹50K+/month revenue: hire operations manager, expand artisan network to 15+ makers, launch branded packaging (₹1.5L), register for Fair Trade certification, pitch to European importers at trade shows (IITF, Heimtextil), build B2B SaaS order management tool.
