ODOP Product

Dry Fruits & Spices

Central Delhi has been the epicenter of India's spice trade for over 300 years, with established trader networks, warehousing infrastructure, and direct access to major supply chains. The region's proximity to major consumption markets and export hubs makes it ideal for processing, packaging, and distribution of premium dry fruits and spices. Generations of expertise in quality grading and blending ensure competitive advantage and customer trust.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Central.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khari Baoli and Chandni Chowk spice markets; spend 2 weeks shadowing 3-4 established traders; understand quality grading, seasonal pricing, and supplier relationships; join Delhi Spice Traders Association
2
Source Raw Materials
Source initial batch from Khari Baoli wholesale merchants (₹3-5 lakhs for 1-2 ton mixed order); negotiate payment terms; verify FSSAI certifications; get samples tested at government labs
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing processing units in Mayapuri or Okhla industrial areas for roasting, sorting, and packaging; alternatively lease 500-1000 sq ft space and install grading/packaging equipment; hire experienced quality checker
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSE classification; obtain IEC code for exports; acquire FSSAI registration; conduct food safety audits; register trademark with IP office; design premium packaging with nutritional labels
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, Blinkit within 2 months; approach 10-15 premium grocery chains; build WhatsApp B2B channel for restaurant suppliers; create Instagram/Facebook brand presence with recipe and health content
6
Scale
Expand product range to specialty blends; enter 2-3 new export markets via APEDA trade shows; develop private label partnerships with 5+ e-commerce platforms; open own retail counter at premium malls; invest in cold storage for seasonal supply buffering
