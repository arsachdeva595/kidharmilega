Risha

Gomati district has a centuries-old weaving tradition rooted in Tripura's tribal heritage, with abundant skilled artisans trained in traditional hand-loom techniques. The region's access to cotton and silk, combined with cultural continuity and government handloom support, makes it the natural home for Risha production.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Gomati.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gomati weaving clusters (Bisrampur, Udaipur block) and meet ATMA (Agricultural Technology Management Agency) Tripura for artisan network details; study Risha GI specifications at Tripura Handloom Board office in Agartala.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton yarn (₹80-120/kg) and silk yarn (₹350-500/kg) from Surat textile markets or ITNC (Indian Textile Network Centre) vendors; first-batch cost (50 kg mix) ≈ ₹35,000-50,000; establish direct relationships with local producers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered artisan cooperatives in Gomati (e.g., Gomati Weavers' Cooperative) or hire trained weavers directly; set up a small production unit with 2-4 traditional handlooms (cost ₹80,000-200,000 per loom); quality control via monthly sampling.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSE (online at udyamregistration.gov.in, free); obtain IEC code from DGFT in Agartala; apply for SFURTI cluster support if joining a cooperative; register brand on Udyam portal and apply for Tripura Handloom Mark certification.
5
First Sale
List on Etsy (handloom category), Flipkart Handloom Hub, and own Shopify store with heritage narrative; approach lifestyle boutiques in metros (Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai) with showroom samples; pitch to hotels on hospitality procurement portals (e.g., TrustRadius).
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 10-15 looms; secure bulk institutional orders from hotel chains; establish regional distribution in metros via agents; launch premium 'Risha Signature' line with custom designs; pursue export partnerships via APEDA (Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority).

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
up to ₹15 lakh interest-free credit for skilled artisans and clusters in handlooms, plus 5% credit subsidy for first 5 years.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹5 crore for cluster development, including artisan capacity building and market linkages in Gomati handloom sector.
🏚
Tripura Handloom Development Scheme
subsidy on looms, yarn input assistance, and export promotion for registered weaving units; administered via Tripura Handloom Board.
🏚
Tripura Self-Employment Scheme (TSES)
up to ₹10 lakh concessional loans for new enterprises, with 5% interest subsidy for 3 years, targeting artisan-led ventures.
