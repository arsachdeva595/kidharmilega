Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tirap.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tirap district agricultural cooperative societies and farming clusters around Depo Bazaar, Longleng region. Interview 5-10 established ginger farmers on yield, costs, markets. Connect with Arunachal Pradesh Organic Certification Agency (APOCA) for GI-tagged farmer networks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified ginger rhizomes from NAFED Tirap depot or directly from cooperative society clusters. First batch (5 acres) requires ₹1.5L for seeds/inputs. Negotiate bulk rates with local farming groups for 15-20% savings on inputs.
3
Produce / Partner
Either cultivate on leased/owned land (2-5 acres minimum) with guidance from District Agriculture Office, Tirap, or partner with 10-15 micro-farmers under contract farming model. Implement organic practices per GI certification standards; processing can be done at shared community processing units in Depo or Longding towns.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise) for ₹0. Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (₹0). Obtain Organic Certification from APOCA (₹15K-30K). Register brand trademark with IP India. Get FSSAI registration for packaged products (₹5K-10K).
5
First Sale
Launch on IndiaMART and TradeKey B2B platforms targeting spice traders and bulk buyers. Simultaneously apply for Amazon Global Selling for export-ready packaged ginger powder/candy. Contact organic exporters in Guwahati and Delhi for first bulk orders. Use bulk SMS campaigns to regional distributors.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand farmer network to 50+ partners, establish in-district processing unit, launch private label ginger products for e-commerce giants, seek ISO 22000 certification, develop agri-tourism component at farm sites, target institutional buyers (corporate gifting, airlines).
