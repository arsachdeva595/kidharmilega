Kosa

Sakti has a 300+ year weaving heritage with skilled artisans trained in traditional Kosa silk techniques. The district's proximity to silk-producing regions and established weaver communities make it the natural hub for authentic Kosa production. Local artisans have perfected hand-dyeing and spinning methods passed down through generations.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sakti.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sakti weaving clusters (Bada Bazaar, Weaver Cooperative Society) and spend 2-3 weeks learning from master weavers. Connect with Chhattisgarh Handloom Board at Raipur. Study market at Varanasi's Kosa markets and attend IHGF Delhi Expo to understand pricing and demand.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw Kosa cocoons from Karnataka/Jharkhand silk suppliers (₹15,000-25,000/kg for first 50 kg batch). Natural dyes from Indore dye merchants or local suppliers near Sakti (₹8,000-12,000 initial batch). Establish credit with 2-3 local cooperatives for consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established weaver families in Sakti (each producing 8-12 sarees/month). Formalize arrangements with written MOUs specifying designs, deadlines, and payment terms (₹3,500-5,500 per saree for artisan labor). Invest in natural dye vats and testing equipment locally.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE classification under handloom), apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for exports. Register brand name with IP India. Obtain Handloom Mark certification from Ministry of Handloom. Open current account with artisan-friendly bank (SIDBI or cooperative bank).
5
First Sale
Launch on Myntra, Meesho, and Ajio with 15-20 SKUs in first month. Build Instagram presence with behind-the-scenes weaver content (target #KosaWeaving #ChhattisgardhWeaving). Email outreach to 50+ high-end boutiques with samples. List on Etsy and craft-focused export platforms within 60 days.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 12-15 weavers post-breakeven. Develop sub-brands (Bridal Kosa, Fusion Kosa). Apply for SFURTI cluster development grant to upgrade weaving units. Target corporate gifting segment and enter 3-5 international fashion stores through distributors by month 12.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels series 'Weaver's Story' — 30-second clips showing Sakti artisans hand-spinning and dyeing, tagged with weaver names and village. Target affluent women 28-50 via geo-targeting metros.
  • Collaborate with 5-7 fashion influencers (20K-100K followers) for saree styling videos. Provide 2-3 free sarees in exchange for 3-4 posts each over 2 months. Use coupon codes to track ROI.
  • Partner with wedding planners in Delhi/Mumbai/Bangalore for bridal show sponsorships. Display live weaving demo and offer 15% bulk discount for bridesmaids' sarees. Collect 200+ wedding contact leads per event.
