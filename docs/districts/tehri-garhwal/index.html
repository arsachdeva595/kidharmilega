Natural fibre

Tehri Garhwal's high-altitude terrain and traditional weaving heritage spanning centuries make it ideal for natural fibre production. The region's skilled artisan community, access to hemp, jute, and wool from local sources, and strong cultural commitment to sustainable practices create a competitive advantage in the growing eco-conscious textile market.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tehri Garhwal.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Uttarkashi and Chopta weaving clusters in Tehri Garhwal; spend 2-3 weeks with KVIC (Khadi Village Industries Commission) Tehri office to understand local fibre sourcing, traditional techniques, and artisan networks; attend Uttarakhand Handloom Expo in Dehradun
2
Source Raw Materials
Source hemp and jute from local suppliers in Rishikesh and Dehradun (₹8-12/kg for bulk); connect with NABARD-supported producer groups in Gairsain taluk; first batch of 500 kg raw material costs ₹60,000-80,000; establish relationships with 2-3 certified organic cotton suppliers
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-8 registered artisans through KVIC Tehri centre; use existing community workshop spaces in Chopta or set up a small 1,500 sq ft unit in Dehradun outskirts (₹2L/month rent); implement production tracking via simple spreadsheets; produce initial 200-300 units (scarves, bags, home textiles) in 6-8 weeks
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro-enterprise) online on udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export readiness; register brand name with IP India; obtain FSSAI if including any food-related items; get handloom mark certification from Ministry of Textiles
5
First Sale
Launch D2C website on Shopify (₹1,000/month); list on Amazon Handmade India and Global within 3 months; create Instagram and Pinterest content (3-4 posts/week) showcasing artisan stories and production process; partner with 3-5 premium eco-brands on social commerce (Indie Picks, Sustainable Style); target 50-100 first orders via email outreach to eco-conscious micro-communities
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 15-20 partners by month 8; introduce 4-5 new product categories (cushion covers, wall hangings, apparel); apply for SFURTI cluster development grant (₹50L) by month 6; establish B2B relationships with 10-15 boutique retailers and home décor chains; explore European wholesale partnerships via Alibaba and trade shows; scale D2C marketing spend to ₹2L/month

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • TikTok & Instagram Reels series 'Artisan Stories' — 15-30 second behind-the-scenes videos of local weavers, production process, and raw material sourcing; aim for 100K+ views in 90 days to build community
  • Influencer partnerships with 5-10 micro eco-fashion influencers (50K-200K followers) in India and UK; provide 10-15 free products per influencer for unboxing/styling content; estimated reach 2M+ impressions for ₹1.5L spend
  • LinkedIn B2B campaign targeting corporate gifting decision-makers; create case studies of 3-4 corporate clients; run retargeting ads on LinkedIn showing custom orders and bulk discounts; allocate ₹50K for 6-month campaign
