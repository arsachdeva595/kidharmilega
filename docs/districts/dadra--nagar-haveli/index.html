ODOP Product

Textile (Yarn)

Dadra and Nagar Haveli has a deeply rooted handloom heritage with generations of skilled artisans trained in yarn production. The region's historical textile industry provides established supply chains, labor expertise, and cultural knowledge of fiber processing. Access to raw materials and low operational costs make it an ideal hub for yarn manufacturing at scale.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Dadra & Nagar Haveli.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Dadra & Nagar Haveli handloom cluster near Silvassa; connect with 5-10 established yarn producers; study traditional spinning techniques at local weavers' cooperatives; research export standards at Ministry of Textile helpdesk
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton from Gujarat cotton markets (Ahmedabad wholesale market ₹50-60/kg), silk cocoons from Karnataka silk board contacts, wool from Rajasthan suppliers; first batch of 100kg mixed yarn materials will cost ₹40,000-60,000; negotiate 30-day payment terms with suppliers
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 registered handloom cooperatives in Silvassa or Dadra town offering revenue-sharing (60-40 split); alternatively, set up small yarn-twisting unit with 2 spinners costing ₹1.2L; train 2-3 artisans on your specifications; start with 50kg monthly production
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under Micro or Small enterprise category; apply for IEC code from nearest customs office (Silvassa/Daman port); register trademark with brand name at IP office; obtain Textile Ministry certification for organic/eco-friendly yarns if applicable; get GTIN barcodes for retail
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Ajarist, and Amazon Handmade with high-resolution product photos and artisan stories (target ₹200-300/100g skein); approach textile wholesalers in Surat and Bhiwandi markets; pitch to eco-conscious fashion brands on Instagram and LinkedIn; partner with 2-3 curated online craft stores
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 10-15 partners; launch branded collection on Myntra or Flipkart; attend Handmade Expo India and Craft India exhibitions; start B2B export to EU countries through textile agents; develop 5-6 signature yarn weights and colors based on customer feedback

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels & TikTok showing artisans spinning yarn, weaving stories behind each color, and behind-the-scenes production; partner with 10-15 micro-influencers in craft/sustainability niche (10k-100k followers) for authentic product reviews
  • Email newsletter targeting textile designers and handloom exporters with monthly yarn trends, new colors, and bulk order incentives; join LinkedIn textile industry groups and share educational content on sustainable yarn production
  • YouTube channel documenting complete yarn-making process from raw fiber to finished product; create SEO-optimized blog content around handloom certification, natural dye recipes, and weaving tutorials to capture organic search traffic from DIY weavers
