ODOP Product

Wooden Carving

Shivamogga has a deep-rooted tradition of wood carving stemming from its proximity to sacred temples and dense sandalwood forests. The region's skilled artisan community has perfected the craft over generations, combining religious iconography with secular designs. Access to premium timber and established supply chains make it the ideal production hub.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Shivamogga.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Shivamogga's Dhavaleshwar and Basaveshwara woodcarving clusters; spend 2-3 weeks shadowing master artisans; document techniques and cost structures; attend DC Handicraft training workshops.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium teak and rosewood from Shivamogga's established timber merchants (Mallige Timber, Maharaja Wood) and Bengaluru's wood markets; first batch of raw materials: ₹40,000-₹60,000 for 10-15 pieces.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 local master carvers on commission basis (₹200-₹500 per piece depending on complexity) or hire 1-2 apprentices for in-house production; set up basic workshop (1,200 sq ft rented space: ₹8,000-₹12,000/month).
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (Micro/Small Enterprise); apply for IEC code via DGFT; register trademark with IP office; obtain handicraft certification from DC Handicraft Ministry; create GST registration.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Amazon Handmade with 20-30 product photos; reach out to 50 interior design studios in Bengaluru and Pune; pitch to temple committees via email; create Instagram with 3 posts/week showcasing process; target 5-8 first orders within month 1.
6
Scale
After profitability, hire 2-3 dedicated artisans; expand product range (furniture, decorative items); apply for SFURTI cluster grant; establish wholesale agreements with 10-15 home decor chains; launch WhatsApp catalog for B2B buyers; explore B2B export marketplaces.
