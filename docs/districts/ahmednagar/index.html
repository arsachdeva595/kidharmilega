Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ahmednagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ahmednagar sugar cooperative clusters in Rahuri, Sangamner, and Parner taluks. Study operations at Khandesh, Warana, or Lokmanya cooperatives. Understand FCI norms, ISMA guidelines, and seasonal processing cycles (Nov-Apr crushing season).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source sugarcane directly from cooperative unions or contract farmers in Ahmednagar at ₹290-320/quintal (seasonal rate). First batch requires 5,000-10,000 tons of cane; negotiate with Jaggery/Sugar Cooperative Unions. Estimate ₹1.5-2 Cr for first season raw material.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing sugar cooperative mills (lease model at ₹50-70L annually) or establish captive mill with ₹100-200 Cr capex. Join cooperative unions for collective crushing rights. Employ experienced mill managers from local defunct/smaller factories.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) or MSME. Obtain IEC code for export from DGFT. Get FSSAI registration for packaged sugar if entering retail. Trademark brand name with IP office. Obtain license from Sugar Commissioner, Maharashtra.
5
First Sale
Direct B2B sales to IndiaStack, TradeKey, Alibaba for bulk orders. Register on NCDEX for commodity trading. Approach FMCG distribution networks (ITC, Britannia, local brands). Attend Ahmednagar trade fairs. Leverage cooperative union's distribution network.
6
Scale
Invest in value-added product lines (organic, crystal, specialty sugars) after Year 1. Expand export to 10+ countries via trade bodies. Automate bagging and quality control. Launch private label contracts with retail chains. Diversify to ethanol/power co-generation.

How to get customers

  • Partner with ISMA (Indian Sugar Mills Association) for trade show participation and B2B buyer connect at food processing exhibitions in Mumbai and Delhi
  • LinkedIn + trade publication ads targeting FMCG procurement teams, highlighting cooperative credentials and export certifications
  • Contract with logistics aggregators (AWS, Shiprocket) for bulk order fulfillment; create case studies on supply reliability for B2B buyers
