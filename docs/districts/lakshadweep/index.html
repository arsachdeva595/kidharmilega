Coconut Derived Products

Lakshadweep has ideal tropical climate with abundant coconut plantations spanning over 90% of cultivable land, centuries-old artisanal processing knowledge, and zero industrial pollution ensuring organic certification-ready production. The island's geographic isolation and abundant rainfall create perfect conditions for premium coconut varieties with high oil content and natural pest resistance.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Lakshadweep.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Agatti and Kavaratti islands to study existing coconut processing units at Lakshadweep Cooperative Society; connect with 50+ artisan groups; analyze premium coconut oil market in Kerala's Kochi port hub where similar products command 3-4x markup; study export protocols via Mumbai and Kerala ports
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh coconuts directly from local farmers through Lakshadweep Horticultural Society at ₹8-12 per coconut; establish relationships with 3-5 primary suppliers; first batch of 500kg coconuts costs ₹40,000-60,000; negotiate direct supply contracts for 12-month commitment
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing KVIC-certified coconut processing units in Lakshadweep or set up own cold-pressed oil unit (₹10-15 lakh capex); hire trained artisans from local craft groups for handicraft production; source semi-automated copra drying equipment; establish food safety lab partnership with Kochi-based testing center for quality certification
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSMEs portal as food processing business; obtain IEC code from Cochin port authority for export readiness; apply for FSSAI license (₹5,000-10,000); register trademark with IP office; obtain organic certification from accredited body (₹40,000-80,000 for first year); create GMP-certified facility documentation
5
First Sale
Launch D2C via Amazon Fresh, Flipkart, and BigBasket with first consignment of 100 units; create Instagram/Facebook presence with island-origin storytelling; approach 20 premium wellness stores (Organic stores, Nature's Basket equivalents) across Mumbai and Bangalore; pitch to corporate gifting platforms; attend Kerala's Cochin Spice Trade Fair for B2B buyers; cold-email 30 international specialty retailers
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product SKUs (flavored oils, coconut-based granola); establish warehouse in Kerala port zone (₹1-2 lakh/month); hire sales team for B2B penetration; apply for PMEGP or SFURTI grants to expand capacity; export to 2-3 new markets via trading houses; develop white-label products for 5-10 corporate clients
