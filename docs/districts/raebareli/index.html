ODOP Product

Wood Work

Raebareli has a strong tradition of wood carving passed down through generations with abundant locally-sourced timber and established artisan clusters. The district's skilled workforce and proximity to raw material suppliers make it an ideal hub for scaling wooden handicrafts production. Government support through handicraft ministries has fostered a conducive ecosystem for wood-work enterprises.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Raebareli.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established wood-work clusters in Raebareli's Salon area and Lalganj block; meet 5-10 master artisans; study production methods at Central Institute of Plastics Engineering & Technology (CIPET) Lucknow handicraft extension programs; research competitors on Etsy and Instagram.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source timber from Raebareli Wood Market (Main Bazaar) and local sawmills in Salon; negotiate bulk rates starting ₹30K-50K for first batch of 100 pieces; establish credit with 2-3 verified suppliers; budget additional ₹20K for tools (chisels, saws, sanders).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 skilled artisans from Raebareli (commission-based: 8-12% per piece) or lease a 500 sq.ft. workshop (₹8K-15K/month) to train own team; start with 50-75 pieces per month; use WhatsApp groups to coordinate with artisans.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (free, MSE benefits); apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for exports; create brand on Canva (logo, packaging design); register trademark on ipindia.gov.in; open business account at nearest HDFC/ICICI.
5
First Sale
Launch Etsy shop (2.5% fee + $0.20 listing) with 20 products and professional photography; set up Instagram business account with Reels showing carving process; list on Amazon Handmade (8% commission); approach 10 local interior designers with product samples; join Indiegogo for pre-orders.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire 2 permanent artisans; expand product line to 50+ SKUs; partner with 5-8 national retailers (Fabindia, Local Artisan stores); apply for SFURTI cluster development grant; launch wholesale B2B portal; attend Delhi Craft Mela (Feb), International Handicraft Fair (Dec) for bulk orders.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create weekly Instagram Reels (10-15 sec) showing carving time-lapse, artisan interviews, and finished product unboxing; use #RaebareliBharati #HandmadeInIndia #SustainableLiving; target audience: design-conscious millennials and Gen-Z; budget ₹2K/month for micro-influencer collaborations.
  • Host virtual 'Artisan Live Sessions' on Facebook/Instagram every alternate Thursday (30 mins) where customers see live carving demos; drive e-commerce conversions with 15% discount codes; build email list for repeat sales; expected 30-50 signups per session.
  • Partner with 5-10 interior design Instagram accounts (50K-200K followers) for gifting and shoutout posts; negotiate 20% affiliate commission on sales; expected 100-200 clicks per influencer per month; track via UTM links.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan subsidy (35% credit guarantee) for new wood-work MSE units; eligible for women entrepreneurs and minorities.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster-level support up to ₹1Cr for artisan groups in Raebareli; includes common facility centers and design interventions.
🏚
UP Kamdhenu Pashu Rin Yojana variant for craftspeople
soft loans up to ₹10L at subsidized 2% interest for handicraft entrepreneurs; administered via SIDBI.
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Employment Exchange Registration & Skills Development Support
free training grants and market linkage for ODOP artisans; co-branded with DC Handicraft.
