Upper Subansiri's high-altitude cooler climate (1200-1800m) and well-drained soil create ideal conditions for orange cultivation with natural disease resistance. The district's geographical isolation has preserved heritage orange varieties with superior taste and nutritional density, supported by traditional farming practices spanning generations.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Upper Subansiri.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Upper Subansiri's Daporijo market and connect with local farmer collectives; study cold-chain infrastructure in Assam-Arunachal border; contact Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Department for cultivation protocols and GI certification details
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner with 2-3 established orange growers in Daporijo block (approximately ₹3-5L for 5-10 metric tonnes first harvest); identify cold storage facility in nearest town (Naharlagun, 150km away); source food-grade packaging from Delhi/Bangalore suppliers (₹50k-100k initial)
3
Produce / Partner
Begin with aggregation and sorting model partnering with 15-20 smallholder farmers; establish small processing unit for value-addition (juice, concentrate); obtain Food Safety license (FSSAI) and GI authentication documentation from Ministry of Agriculture
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise) and IEC code via DGFT for export eligibility; obtain GI usage rights from Agricultural Ministry; design packaging highlighting Himalayan origin and GI tag; register trademark for brand name with IP office
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket and Blinkit with ₹2-5L minimum order commitments; contact APEDA-certified export houses for international orders; pitch cold-pressed juice concentrate to Greenpepper, Raw Pressery, and Ayurvedic supplement manufacturers; create Instagram account with farm-to-table storytelling
6
Scale
Invest in cold-chain infrastructure (₹30-50L) connecting farm to 4 major metros; launch private-label juice concentrate for hotels and restaurants; establish organic certification (NPOP/GODREJ); expand to 50+ farmer partnerships and 2-hectare processing facility
