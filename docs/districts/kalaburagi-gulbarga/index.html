ODOP Product

Toor Daal

Kalaburagi's semi-arid climate, black soil composition, and centuries-old farming traditions make it ideally suited for high-quality toor dal cultivation. The district produces over 2 lakh metric tonnes annually with superior protein content and distinct flavor profile that commands premium prices in domestic and export markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kalaburagi (Gulbarga).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kalaburagi's primary agricultural markets at Chincholi Mandal and Tandur APMC to understand procurement dynamics; connect with established dal processors like Sai Durga Mills and Hyderabad Toor Dal Co-operative for best practices
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw toor dal directly from farmer cooperatives in Kalaburagi district at ₹60-75/kg; first batch of 5 MT will cost ₹3-3.75 lakhs; establish relationships with 3-4 farmer groups for consistent supply
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered dal mills in Kalaburagi (Gulbarga) city like Asha Dal Mill or start with contract processing; minimum viable production: 2-3 MT/month through hired mill capacity at ₹2000-2500/MT processing cost
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) immediately; obtain IEC code from Customs; get FSSAI registration (₹8000-15000); design GI-tagged packaging mentioning 'From GI-protected Kalaburagi'; register trademark with IP office
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and Blinkit with ₹500-1000/kg premium positioning; simultaneously approach regional distributors in Bangalore, Chennai, and Hyderabad; start WhatsApp-based B2B sales to canteens and food service providers
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 8-10 MT/month capacity; add 2-3 complementary pulses (chana, moong); launch export shipments through APEDA with certified agents; introduce organic certification; build 2-3 regional brand ambassadors for offline market penetration

  • Run Facebook/Google ads targeting NRI diaspora communities in UK, US, Canada with 'Authentic Kalaburagi Toor Dal' messaging; use GI certification as trust signal; allocate ₹2-3 lakhs quarterly for performance marketing
