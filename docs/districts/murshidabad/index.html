ODOP Product

Murshidabad Silk Saree/Scarf

Murshidabad has an unbroken 3-century tradition of silk sericulture and weaving with established supply chains for mulberry silk cocoons, dyes, and traditional looms. The district houses over 50,000 artisan weavers with deep expertise in traditional motifs and modern designs. Proximity to raw silk sources and existing export infrastructure makes it the natural epicenter for silk saree manufacturing.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Murshidabad.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Murshidabad Silk cluster (Balusters Road, Koshiganj weaving area); connect with Murshidabad Silk Industries Association; attend 2-3 IGATP exhibitions; learn about jacquard vs. handloom techniques from local master weavers
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mulberry silk yarn from Silk Board approved vendors in Murshidabad (₹600-800/kg); contract with 2-3 certified dyers for natural/synthetic dyes; first batch of 50 sarees requires ₹80,000-120,000 raw material investment; establish supplier agreements with 30-day payment terms
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 15-30 established weavers in Koshiganj cluster via artisan cooperatives or direct contracts; establish quality control checkpoints (loom inspection, dyeing tests, weaving standards); implement weekly production batches of 50-100 units; hire 1 QA coordinator and 1 design assistant; set up small design and finishing studio in Murshidabad city center
4
Brand & Register
Register business as Udyam MSE (online, ₹0 cost); apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export eligibility; register trademark for brand name (₹4,500); obtain Handloom Mark certification from Handloom Board; get GST registration (18% applicable); create brand story connecting heritage to modern wear
5
First Sale
Launch D2C website with high-quality product photography (15-20 images per saree); list on Meesho, Flipkart Handloom, Etsy, and Instagram Shop simultaneously; approach 10-15 premium ethnic wear boutiques (Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore) with wholesale samples (20% discount); launch Instagram Reels showing weaving process and customer testimonials; run Facebook/Instagram ads targeting 28-45 year old women (₹2,000/day budget); participate in online handloom exhibitions hosted by Craftsvilla
6
Scale
Expand weaver network to 50-75 artisans by month 6; introduce seasonal collections (festival, bridal, casual); establish B2B partnerships with 5-10 corporate gifting companies; open pop-up stores in 2-3 metro cities; hire content creator for UGC and influencer collaborations; implement subscription box model for loyal customers; apply for government export promotion schemes (DEPB, RODTEP); target ₹50L+ annual revenue by month 18

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 15-30 second Reels on Instagram/YouTube Shorts showing live weaving process, artisan stories, and behind-the-scenes dyeing; target 25-50 year old women interested in traditional crafts; aim for 10% engagement rate and viral reach on 2-3 videos monthly
  • Partner with 5-8 micro-influencers (50K-500K followers) in ethnic fashion niche for unboxing videos and styling content; offer 15-20% commission on each sale; cost ₹500-2000 per influencer per post; expect 8-12% conversion rate from influencer traffic
  • Run seasonal Google Shopping campaigns (₹5,000-10,000/month budget) targeting keywords like 'silk saree online', 'Murshidabad silk', 'handloom saree'; set up dynamic remarketing for cart abandoners; expect 2-3% ROAS in first 90 days

PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15,000 credit and 5% subsidy on interest for artisan-entrepreneurs and family units engaged in traditional crafts

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakh subsidy (35% for SC/ST/Women entrepreneurs) for business establishment in handloom sector

₹2-5 Lakh grant for new weaver units, raw material subsidies, and tech upgrades to traditional looms

Women entrepreneur fund offering ₹50,000-₹1 Lakh concessional loan at 4% interest for handloom and textile businesses in West Bengal
