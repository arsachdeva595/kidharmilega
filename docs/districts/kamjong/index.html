ODOP Product

King Chilli

Kamjong's high altitude (1,200-1,500m), cool temperate climate, and volcanic soil create ideal growing conditions for ultra-spicy chillies with superior flavor compounds. The district's agricultural heritage and farmer expertise in heirloom chilli cultivation make it naturally suited for King Chilli production. Local farming communities have perfected cultivation techniques over generations, ensuring consistent quality and heat levels.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kamjong.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kamjong district, meet farmer cooperatives in Kamjong town, study cultivation at Manipur Agricultural University, research competitors on Spice Board India database and explore chilli markets in Imphal and Dimapur
2
Source Raw Materials
Connect with Kamjong village farmers through District Agriculture Office, negotiate bulk supply contracts (500kg-2MT for first batch ₹40,000-₹80,000), ensure farmer certification for organic cultivation, visit local markets in Imphal to understand pricing
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with farmer groups in Kamjong cluster or lease farmland (₹5,000-₹15,000/acre), invest in drying equipment (₹50,000-₹200,000), establish processing unit in Imphal near spice market, or collaborate with existing spice processors in Manipur
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) on udyamregistration.gov.in, obtain IEC code from DGFT, get spice processing license from district food safety, secure FSSAi registration, trademark brand name on ipindia.gov.in
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Global Selling and Shopify for D2C, connect with spice exporters via IndiaTradeKey.com, approach specialty food retailers (Whole Foods, organic chains), pitch to hot sauce brands for B2B supply, leverage Instagram/YouTube with chilli challenge content
6
Scale
Establish processing facility in Imphal industrial area, expand farmer network to 50+ suppliers, build export partnerships with distributors in USA/EU, develop value-added products (infused oils, ready-mix powders), invest in cold storage and logistics infrastructure
