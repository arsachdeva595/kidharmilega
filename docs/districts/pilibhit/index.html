Flute and Wooden Products

Pilibhit has a 400+ year legacy of bamboo flute making passed down through generations of skilled artisans. The region's access to quality bamboo from nearby forests and the concentration of master craftsmen make it the natural hub for this traditional art. Local knowledge, cultural continuity, and established export networks create unmatched competitive advantage.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Pilibhit.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pilibhit's main artisan clusters in Puranpur and Bisalpur blocks; connect with Pilibhit Bamboo Flute Artisan Association; study export norms via APEDA (Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority); research competing sellers on Etsy and Amazon Handmade.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source bamboo directly from Pilibhit's local suppliers (₹8,000-15,000 per 100 pieces raw), hardwood from local timber merchants in Bareilly (2 hours away, ₹2,000-5,000 per kg), brass fittings from Jaipur suppliers (₹1,500-3,000 per kg); first batch of 200-300 units requires ₹40,000-80,000 investment.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 certified master artisans in Puranpur via informal contracts (₹80-150 per finished flute); or establish own small workshop with 2-3 trained craftsmen; ensure quality control via testing each instrument; target 50-100 units monthly initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online (udyamregistration.gov.in) for MSME benefits; apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (dgft.gov.in) for export; register brand name with IP India; obtain GI tag usage license from DC Handicraft Ministry; get FSSAI if including wooden food items; consider Craft Mark certification.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy with 20-30 high-quality product listings (₹20 per listing); simultaneously list on Amazon Handmade (10% commission); create Instagram/Facebook shop with artisan backstory; contact 50+ music schools via email with B2B pricing; reach out to international distributors via LinkedIn and global trade shows.
6
Scale
After 6 months profitability, hire 2 additional artisans; expand product line to include wooden utensils/toys (complementary categories); apply for export subsidies under MEIS/RoDTEP; establish distributor partnerships in 3-5 international countries; invest in branded packaging and video content; explore B2B contracts with large retailers.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create YouTube short-form content (60-90 sec reels) showing artisans crafting flutes step-by-step, post on YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, TikTok; target keywords: 'bamboo flute making', 'handmade Indian flute'; goal: 50K+ views monthly.
  • Partner with 10-15 classical music YouTubers/Spotify playlist curators for product placements and reviews; send them branded flutes; negotiate affiliate commission (15-20%); track ROI via discount codes.
  • Run Google Shopping ads targeting 'Indian flute', 'bamboo musical instruments', 'handmade wooden crafts' with ₹500/day budget in USA/UK markets; retarget website visitors with 20% discounts via Facebook/Instagram ads.
