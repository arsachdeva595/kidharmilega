The district has established agro-processing infrastructure, skilled labor, and proximity to major markets in Delhi-NCR and international ports. Strong dairy farming tradition supports integrated food processing opportunities.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kaithal.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established rice mills in Kaithal town and Guhna industrial area; interview 5-10 mill owners on machinery costs, yield rates, and buyer networks. Study Kaithal Dairy Cooperative and visit 2-3 dairy processing units. Attend agro-food trade shows in Delhi/Chandigarh. Join Kaithal District Industries Association.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source paddy from local farmers via Kaithal agricultural markets (peak harvest Oct-Dec) at ₹2000-2200/quintal. First-batch: 5-10 metric tons costs ₹1-2.2L. For dairy: partner with Kaithal Dairy Cooperative or source milk directly at ₹40-50/liter. Packaging materials from Yamunanagar or Delhi suppliers at ₹5-15 per unit depending on volume.
3
Produce / Partner
Option 1: Partner with existing rice mill operators (20-30 mills operate in Kaithal district) for co-packing on revenue-share basis. Option 2: Lease milling equipment and small space in Guhna industrial area (₹20-40K/month) to start with 500kg/day capacity. For dairy: join Kaithal Dairy or partner with 1-2 local dairy farms for processing arrangements.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 2 days). Apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Hisar (₹0, online). Get FSSAI food processing license (₹2000-5000, Kaithal office). Register trademark with IP office (₹4500 online). Obtain export certification if targeting international sales (ISO 9001, organic if applicable).
5
First Sale
First 3 months: Approach bulk buyers via B2B platforms (Tradeindia, IndiaMART) and local Kaithal retailers. Launch on Amazon, Flipkart under 'Basmati Rice' and 'Natural Dairy Products' categories with competitive pricing. Create WhatsApp business catalog for local distribution. Pitch to modern trade chains (DMart, Blinkit) via distributors. Email 20-30 hotel/restaurant groups in Delhi-NCR.
6
Scale
Months 6-12: Expand SKU range (flavored rice, organic variants, dairy range). Secure 2-3 institutional contracts (corporate cafeterias, school mid-day schemes, hospital canteens). Build distributor network in Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, UP. Launch own D2C website with subscription model. Apply for APEDA recognition for export promotion. Invest in upgraded packaging and quality certifications.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)

up to ₹10L for cluster-based rice mills and dairy processing units in Kaithal
