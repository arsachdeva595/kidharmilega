ODOP Product

Mica

Koderma district has geological formations rich in muscovite and phlogopite mica deposits spanning centuries of mining heritage. The district's established mining infrastructure, skilled workforce, and proximity to industrial clusters in Bengaluru and Mumbai make it uniquely positioned for mica processing and value-added product manufacturing. Over 300+ mica mines and processing units operate in Koderma, creating an established supply chain ecosystem.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Koderma.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Koderma's mining clusters around Pursainath, Domchanch, and Karwandha. Spend 2-3 weeks shadowing existing mica mining/processing units. Study mineral grading standards at Bureau of Indian Standards (BIS) office in Ranchi. Connect with Koderma District Mineral Foundation and chamber of commerce; cost ₹15,000-25,000 for travel and networking.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw mica ore directly from licensed mines in Koderma district (contact District Mining Authority for approved suppliers) at ₹2,000-4,000 per ton. Alternatively, partner with existing mining operators for supply agreements. First batch of 10 tons costs approximately ₹40,000-50,000; negotiate bulk pricing post-validation.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established mica processing units in Koderma (50+ operational mills available) for processing services rather than investing in equipment initially; cost ₹500-1,000 per ton for processing. Alternatively, lease a processing facility (₹30,000-50,000/month) or invest in basic grinding and grading equipment (₹15-30 lakhs). Visit Mica Processors Association, Koderma for vendor introductions.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (free, 15 mins online). Obtain IEC code from FIEO if planning exports (₹500). Register business name under Registrar of Companies (ROC) Ranchi (₹5,000-8,000). Obtain mining permissions and environmental clearance (SPCB Jharkhand) (₹20,000-50,000). Create basic branding with mica product photography and certifications from BIS/DGFT.
5
First Sale
Approach electronics OEMs and cosmetic manufacturers directly through B2B platforms like IndiaMART and TradeKey. Start with 500kg pilot orders at ₹80-120/kg wholesale. Simultaneously, list processed mica products on Amazon Business and Flipkart Wholesale. Negotiate supply contracts with paint and construction material companies; typical order sizes ₹50,000-200,000.
6
Scale
After 6 months, invest in ISO 9001 and ISO 14001 certifications (₹1-2 lakhs) to access premium export markets. Establish direct export channels to USA and Germany through trade fairs (fees ₹2-5 lakhs annually). Develop 2-3 specialized mica product variants for high-margin segments (aerospace-grade, cosmetic-grade). Hire 5-8 production staff and a sales executive; monthly payroll ₹200,000-300,000.
