Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Gondia.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gondia's primary rice clusters: Tiroda taluk (largest paddy belt) and Jabalpur-Gondia corridor. Meet with 3-5 established rice mill operators in Gondia city market. Attend Maharashtra Agricultural Department workshops. Analyze Ambemohar, Kolam, Mahamaya varietal demand via APEDA export data.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source paddy directly from farmer cooperatives in Tiroda (₹25,000-30,000/quintal) or mill-grade broken rice from established mills like Gondia Rice Mills Association members. First batch: 10-20 metric tons (₹3,00,000-5,00,000). Negotiate extended payment terms (30-45 days) with suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with Grade-A certified rice mills in Gondia city (Dhamangaon, Gondia) for processing (₹1,500-2,500/MT milling charge). Establish quality control: SGR testing, fragrance certification via ICAR lab in Nagpur. OR start small with ready-to-pack rice from ethical suppliers while building mill partnerships.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as MSME in agriculture processing; apply for IEC code from DGFT within 10 days (needed for exports). Obtain FSSAI license (₹5,000-20,000). Register trademark with IP India for brand name. Seek organic/GI certification for premium positioning (₹50,000-1,00,000).
5
First Sale
Launch D2C via Blinkit, BigBasket, Amazon Fresh with 500 kg initial stock (₹1,50,000 investment). Simultaneously approach 10-15 premium grocery stores in Nagpur & Pune directly with samples. Partner with export platform (Groww.com, Farmer Stories) for global reach. Host 2 tasting events at food festivals in Nagpur.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 3-5 state wholesale distributors; increase export to 5-7 countries via Tradekey. Introduce value-added products: rice flour, flakes, ready-to-cook rice (10% of revenue). Build direct B2B relationships with 20+ restaurants. Launch subscription model on website (10% of D2C revenue).

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram & YouTube storytelling: Document farmer stories from Tiroda & mill heritage; 2-3 posts/week + monthly 10-min farm-to-table videos. Partner with 5 food/wellness micro-influencers (50K-200K followers). Estimated ₹15,000/month
  • Participate in 3-4 regional food festivals annually (Pune, Nagpur, Aurangabad). Conduct on-ground tasting + sampling. Capture email lists. Allocate ₹50,000 per event.
  • Launch referral program: existing customers get ₹200 discount per successful referral; advertise via WhatsApp groups of HNI food communities in Pune & Mumbai. Track via unique codes. ₹5,000/month budget.
