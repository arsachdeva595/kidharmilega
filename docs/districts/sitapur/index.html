Carpets (Dari)

Sitapur has a 300+ year tradition of carpet weaving passed down through generations of master artisans. The district's proximity to wool suppliers in northern India, established weaver clusters, and skilled workforce make it ideal for scaling carpet production. Water quality and local dyeing expertise further strengthen the region's competitive advantage.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sitapur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sitapur's weaving clusters in Lohapur, Mahmoodpur, and Bangarmau. Connect with Sitapur Carpet Weavers Cooperative Society. Study designs at Delhi's Cottage Industries Emporium and Jaipur's heritage shops. Spend 2-3 weeks with master weavers to understand loom operation, dyeing processes, and cost structures.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source wool from Chandigarh and Punjab wool markets (₹400-600/kg for quality grades). Procure natural dyes from Jodhpur and Ajmer dye suppliers. First batch (10-15 carpets, 100-150 sq ft): approximately ₹2-3 lakhs including yarn, dyes, and loom preparation.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established artisans from Sitapur weaver clusters on contract basis (per-carpet payment model). Alternatively, employ 2-3 looms in rented workshop space (₹8000-12000/month) with trained weavers. Production capacity: 8-12 carpets (50-100 sq ft total) per month with 2 looms.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online on udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office for exports. File for TM registration for brand name (₹4500 for 5 classes). Get BIS certification for quality assurance. Open GST registration (0% for handloom products).
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram and Pinterest accounts with high-quality carpet photography from Sitapur artisans (storytelling approach). List on Etsy, Kraftly, and Artaza. Approach interior designers in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore via LinkedIn. Partner with 3-4 premium home decor boutiques for consignment sales. Email direct to embassies and 5-star hotels with portfolio.
6
Scale
After 6 months, increase loom capacity to 5-6 (hire 5-6 weavers). Invest in branded packaging and sustainability certification. Target B2B exports: connect with EU importers via trade shows (Heimtextil Frankfurt, trade fairs in Amsterdam). Launch limited-edition designer collaborations. Develop corporate gifting line. Explore subscription model for interior designers.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showing 60-second time-lapses of entire carpet creation — wool dyeing, loom setup, weaving progress — with artisan interviews and backstory. Target affluent 30-50 year old homeowners and interior designers with relevant hashtags.
  • Partner with 10-15 micro-influencers in home decor and sustainable fashion (50K-200K followers) for gifted carpets and styled photoshoots. Cost per campaign: ₹15,000-30,000 per influencer. Expected ROI: 8-12% conversion rate.
  • Email outreach to 200 5-star hotels, resort chains, and corporate offices quarterly with seasonal collections and custom quotations. Use Mailchimp for tracking. Target conversion: 2-3 corporate contracts per quarter at ₹1.5-3 lakh each.
