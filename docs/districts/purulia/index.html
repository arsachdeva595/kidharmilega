Lac & Lac Products

Purulia has a deep-rooted tradition of lac artisanship with clusters of skilled artisans concentrated in Raghunathpur, Joypur, and adjacent villages. The district's natural biodiversity supports natural resin sourcing, and the local workforce is trained in traditional lac beads, bangles, and ornamental work. Proximity to Eastern markets and established supply chains make it a natural hub for lac product manufacturing.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Purulia.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Raghunathpur lac cluster and Joypur artisan villages in Purulia; connect with local DC Handicraft office and SIDBI artisan networks; observe production at established units like Puruliya Handicraft Cooperative; spend 2-4 weeks understanding bangle-making, bead-polishing, and quality standards.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw shellac from Krishnanagar-based shellac suppliers in West Bengal or Jharkhand lac traders; first-batch costs approximately ₹50,000-₹100,000 for 50 kg quality shellac, metal foils, and dyes; establish credit relationships with local suppliers in Raghunathpur marketplace.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisan groups in Raghunathpur (avg 10-15 skilled artisans per group) through DC Handicraft referrals or SIDBI clusters; start with 3-5 artisans on piece-rate contracts (₹150-₹400 per dozen bangles depending on complexity); invest in basic tools (drying racks, polishing wheels, molds) costing ₹80,000-₹150,000.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) on udyamregistration.gov.in for GST exemption benefits; apply for IEC code through nearest DGFT office in Kolkata; register brand name with IP India; obtain Handmade India certification from DC Handicraft; ensure Hallmark certification if using metal components.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Amazon Handmade with high-quality artisan photography; approach boutiques in Kolkata (Park Street, Gariahat) and Delhi (Khan Market) for wholesale placement; participate in India Handmade platform and craft fairs in Mumbai, Bangalore during wedding season (Sept-Jan); reach out to Indian ethnic fashion brands (FabIndia, Anokhi) for B2B partnerships.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire additional artisan groups to scale production by 200-300%; develop niche product lines (fusion lac-metal jewelry, modern minimalist bangles); establish export partnerships through APEDA and RoO certification; build corporate gifting contracts with minimum order quantities; expand to international craft marketplaces and trade shows.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showcasing real artisans in Raghunathpur at work; humanize production through weekly artisan spotlights; partner with Indian fashion micro-influencers (50k-500k followers) for unboxing videos and styling posts.
  • Launch storytelling content on YouTube and TikTok: 'From Forest to Finger' series showing lac sourcing → processing → craft; target wedding season with trending sounds and festival styling guides.
  • Host virtual craft workshops on Instagram Live and Zoom for diaspora Indians and international craft enthusiasts; charge ₹500-₹1000 per session; position as heritage preservation + direct product upsell.
