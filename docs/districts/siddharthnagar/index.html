ODOP Product

Kalanamak Rice

Siddharthnagar's alluvial soil, moderate rainfall, and cooler winter climate create ideal conditions for Kalanamak rice cultivation. The district has a 300+ year heritage of growing this specific variety, with farmers possessing deep traditional knowledge of seed selection, soil preparation, and harvest timing that directly impact the rice's signature aroma and taste profile.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Siddharthnagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Siddharthnagar's primary farming clusters around Bhagwanpur and Bansi blocks; connect with NAFED cooperative offices and UP Agricultural Department representatives; attend Kalanamak Rice Association meetings; study processing units in Lucknow and Kanpur food parks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Purchase certified Kalanamak seeds from ICAR-RCER Masaurhi or local seed multiplication centers at ₹80-120 per kg; source raw paddy directly from farmer cooperatives in Siddharthnagar at ₹2000-2500 per quintal during Oct-Nov harvest season; first batch requirement: 20-30 quintals (₹4-7.5L investment).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing rice mills in Siddharthnagar town (Balrampur Road cluster) for custom milling on revenue-share basis (15-20% margin) OR invest in small-scale huller-cum-polisher unit (₹12-18L capital) with capacity 1-2 MT per day; hire experienced rice graders familiar with Kalanamak quality standards.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME online (₹0 cost); apply for IEC code from nearest ICEGATE center; register trademark with IP India for brand name; obtain Food Safety License (FSSAI) from district authority (₹500-1000); join APEDA for export certification; consider GI logo usage through Kalanamak Rice Association membership.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon India, BigBasket, Blinkit with 500g premium packs at ₹250-350; contact HoReCa groups via Nykaa Business and TraceLink; approach Fair Trade organic exporters (Terra Nuova, Organic Harvest); leverage LinkedIn B2B outreach to food processors; list on IndiaMART for bulk inquiries; approach 20 organic retail chains directly with samples.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand into rice-based value-added products (rice flour, bran oil); build direct relationships with 10-15 specialty exporters; open 50-100kg storage facilities in Lucknow and Kanpur food clusters; invest in co-packing relationships for instant mixes; target EU and Japan certifications (organic, NonGMO) by month 12-14; scale production capacity to 200-300 MT annually.
