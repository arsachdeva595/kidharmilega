ODOP Product

Cotton Ginning and pressing

Yavatmal is the second-largest cotton-growing district in Maharashtra with ideal climate, rich black soil, and established farmer networks. The district has over 150 cotton ginning units and strong supply chain linkages to textile mills in Nagpur, Indore, and Ahmedabad. Proximity to major cotton trading hubs and existing processing infrastructure makes it ideal for scaling cotton ginning and pressing operations.

5Warranty and maintenance services on ginning machinery for other small units
Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Yavatmal.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Yavatmal cotton ginning clusters in Ner, Pusad, and Digras; meet 5-10 existing gin unit owners; study cotton pricing at Yavatmal Agriculture Produce Market Committee; understand APMC regulations and cotton grading standards (DCSR & UGS).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton directly from APMC auctions in Yavatmal or contract with farmer groups; negotiate bulk purchase agreements with 2-3 large cotton growers; first batch of 50 bales (₹15-20L) available year-round from Sept-Dec peak season.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing ginning unit operators in Pusad or Digras for toll ginning (₹100-150 per quintal); alternatively, invest in semi-automatic ginning machinery from suppliers in Nagpur or Coimbatore (₹10-20L for 5-10 bale/hour capacity); establish processing facility near APMC or cotton-growing zones.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME online (msme.gov.in); obtain IEC code from DGFT for export; get APMC trader license; register with Textile Commissioner's office; obtain ISI or ICAR certification for lint quality; create brand identity with Yavatmal district origin marker.
5
First Sale
Direct contact with mill owners in Nagpur, Indore (Rajsthan), and Gujarat; list on textile B2B platforms (TradeKey, Global Sources); attend India International Textiles & Apparel Summit; supply to yarn spinner associations; approach exporters through TEXPROCIL network.
6
Scale
Increase capacity by adding 2-3 more ginning units in next 12 months; develop farmer purchasing network; obtain working capital loans from HDFC, ICICI, or Cooperative banks; explore exports to Vietnam, Bangladesh via freight forwarders; establish small yarning or fabric unit as forward integration.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create YouTube content series 'From Field to Fiber' showing cotton journey from Yavatmal farms to ginned lint; target textile mill owners and exporters with high-quality production footage.
  • Sponsor booth at India International Textiles & Apparel Summit (IITAS) in Delhi/Mumbai and Anar Exhibition (yarn sector); distribute samples and catalogs to 200+ mill decision-makers.
  • Partner with TEXPROCIL (textile exporters council) and join WhatsApp groups of cotton traders and yarn manufacturers; offer bulk pricing incentives for 3-month contracts.
