ODOP Product

Bomkai Saree & Fabrics

Sonepur has a 400+ year weaving heritage centered in villages like Bomkai and Sonepur town. The region's soil, water quality, and climate are naturally suited for cotton cultivation. Generations of artisans have perfected the Bomkai weaving technique, making it impossible to replicate authentically elsewhere.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sonepur (Subarnapur).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bomkai and Sonepur weaving clusters; spend 2-3 weeks with master weavers in villages like Bomkai, Gobindpur, Subalaya. Meet SONEPUR HANDLOOM COOPERATIVE SOCIETY and local weavers' associations. Study existing players like Utkalika, Sambalpuri House.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw silk from Tamil Nadu (Kanchipuram suppliers) or Jharkhand; cotton from Telangana/Gujarat mills. First batch cost: ₹80K-120K for 20-30 sarees. Partner directly with 5-8 artisans in Bomkai village or through the local handloom cooperative.
3
Produce / Partner
Register as artisan partner/aggregator with local weavers. Establish production agreement with 3-5 looms producing 8-12 sarees monthly. Quality check via WhatsApp/video; payment via bank transfer to artisans (₹3000-5000 per saree production cost).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online (ministry.msme.gov.in); apply for IEC code (customs portal) for export eligibility. Trademark brand name with IP India. Obtain GI certification tag usage authorization from Ministry of Textiles. Create brand story website emphasizing artisan heritage.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Myntra (luxury category). Pitch to boutiques in Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore. Use Instagram and Pinterest for visual storytelling; partner with lifestyle influencers (50K-500K followers) for brand ambassadorship. Attend craft fairs (Dastkar, Craftisan Festival).
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 15-20 looms; introduce fabric-only product line. Build B2B relationships with fashion designers and ethnic wear brands. Launch subscription box for seasonal collections. Explore international wholesale via trade fairs (Frankfurt Textile Fair, Premiere Vision Paris). Establish physical store in metro city (Delhi/Mumbai) by month 12-14.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels featuring artisans at looms + behind-the-scenes weaving videos; use hashtags #BomkaiSaree #OdishaTextiles #EthnicWear. Target affluent women aged 25-50 with carousel ads showing saree details.
  • Partner with wedding planners and bridal consultants in metros to showcase Bomkai sarees for pre-wedding and wedding events; offer 10-15% commission per referral.
  • Collaborate with fashion bloggers and YouTube creators (DIY styling, saree draping tutorials) to create authentic content; sponsor 3-5 micro-influencers monthly (5-50K followers) with free sarees for authentic reviews.
