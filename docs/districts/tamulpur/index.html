Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tamulpur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tamulpur honey clusters around Bheramara and Sonari villages (30-40km from Tamulpur town). Meet 5-10 traditional honey collectors to understand seasonal cycles (peak: March-June), harvest methods, and average yields (80-150kg per collector/season). Study export standards via APEDA guidelines.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw honey from established collector networks in Tamulpur at ₹300-400/kg (approximately ₹45,000-60,000 for first 150kg batch). Establish direct relationships with 3-4 collectors to ensure supply continuity. Budget additional ₹15,000-20,000 for initial filtering, bottling, and labeling materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with local honey collectors through a cooperative model rather than building facilities (reduces capital). Establish a small processing unit (200 sqft, rent ₹3,000-5,000/month) for filtering, packaging, and storage. Train collectors on quality standards and food safety protocols aligned with FSSAI requirements.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (online, free, 10 minutes). Obtain FSSAI license (₹5,000-8,000, 15-20 days online). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (free) for export readiness. Design label with GI-adjacent messaging emphasizing 'Forest-Harvested Assam Honey' with origin story and medicinal benefits.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart, and BigBasket with 500-1,000 units at ₹450-650/500ml. Simultaneously approach organic stores in Guwahati (10-12 outlets) with wholesale pricing ₹280/unit. Use Instagram and YouTube to build 'forest-to-table' narrative with short videos of collectors. Target Ayurveda practitioners via LinkedIn outreach.
6
Scale
After 6 months, introduce 3-5 value-added products (honey + turmeric, honey + ginger). Expand collector base to 15-20 for increased supply. Explore export via TrustIndia or Agrofoods aggregators targeting EU/UK (certifications: EU organic equivalent). Build B2B relationships with 20-30 premium cafes and wellness centers across Northeast India.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels + TikTok showcasing 3-5 minute mini-documentaries of honey collectors at work in forests, emphasizing sustainability and tradition. Partner with 10 micro-influencers (10-50K followers) in wellness/health niche for seeded products and commissions.
  • Content marketing on Medium and LinkedIn targeting organic food enthusiasts and Ayurveda professionals with blogs on honey's medicinal properties, beekeeping sustainability, and Tamulpur's biodiversity. Optimize for SEO keywords like 'wild Assam honey,' 'organic honey India export.'
  • B2B outreach via industry forums: participate in Natural Products Expo India, Organic World India, and health food expos. Create partnership proposals for wellness brands, corporate gifting companies, and Ayurveda chains with margin-friendly wholesale pricing.
