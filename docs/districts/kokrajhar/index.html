Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kokrajhar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kokrajhar Agricultural Department and local mushroom farmer cooperatives in Boitamari and Dhubri blocks; study 2-3 successful farms producing 5-10 tonnes monthly; understand Bodo tribal community's traditional forest knowledge on fungi.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source quality mushroom spawn from certified suppliers in Guwahati (₹800-1200 per kg) and local agricultural input dealers; source agricultural waste from rice mills in Kokrajhar for ₹2000-3000 per tonne; initial first batch requires ₹50,000-80,000 for inputs.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-10 farmers in Kokrajhar district under contract farming model or establish 2-3 small shade net structures (500 sqm each) on leased agricultural land; train local farmers in oyster and button mushroom cultivation techniques; engage Kokrajhar Krishi Vigyan Kendra for technical support.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam portal as agriculture enterprise; obtain IEC code from nearest customs office in Kokrajhar; apply for organic certification from NCOF or INDOCERT (₹15,000-25,000); create brand identity with local Bodo cultural elements; register trade mark with IP office.
5
First Sale
Launch on Jio Mart, BigBasket, and local Assam-focused platforms; supply to 10-15 restaurants in Kokrajhar and Bongaigaon; approach food exporters in Guwahati; establish weekly market stalls in Kokrajhar town; partner with organic food chains across Northeast India.
6
Scale
Expand to 15-20 farmer partnerships; establish processing unit for dried mushrooms and value-added products; target tier-2 cities across Northeast via distribution network; secure B2B contracts with institutional buyers; pursue export certifications (APEDA, EU organic standards) for international markets.
