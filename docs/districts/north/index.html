Light Engineering Goods

North Delhi hosts over 5,000+ light engineering units concentrated in clusters like Wazirpur Industrial Area, Rani Jhansi Road, and Karawal Nagar. The proximity to transportation networks, skilled labour force, and established supply chains make it ideal for manufacturing precision machinery, automotive components, and industrial tools. Decades of accumulated expertise and infrastructure create unmatched competitive advantage.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for North.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Wazirpur Industrial Area Phase 1 & 2 and Rani Jhansi Road clusters; meet 10+ established manufacturers to understand product range, pricing, and supply chains. Attend IISE (Indian Institute of Steel Excellence) workshops. Study market reports from ASSOCHAM and FICCI on light engineering sector.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Jangpura steel market (Delhi), Ludhiana fasteners suppliers, and Bangalore aluminum alloy dealers. First batch budget ₹2-3L for 500-1000 units of a single product (e.g., precision nuts/bolts or motor components).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing job-work units in Karawal Nagar or lease 500-1000 sq.ft. space in established industrial parks at ₹15-25/sq.ft. monthly. Hire 2-3 skilled technicians from local workforce (average cost ₹12-15K/month). Invest in CNC lathe or drilling machines (₹2-4L for basic setup).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME online (udyamregistration.gov.in); obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office (New Delhi DGFT: 011-2437-1711); trademark brand name via IP India portal (₹4500 for 10 years); get ISO 9001 certification (₹30-50K) for B2B credibility.
5
First Sale
Launch on IndiaMART and TradeKey with competitive pricing; contact automotive OEM procurement teams directly via LinkedIn; participate in Delhi Industrial Expo (March/Oct); send samples to 20-30 target manufacturers with pricing sheets; use WhatsApp for relationship building with bulk buyers.
6
Scale
After ₹20L turnover, expand product range by adding complementary items; hire sales representative for Bangalore/Chennai markets; apply for FICCI export promotion scheme; establish quality certifications (IATF 16949 for auto components); reinvest 30% profit into newer machinery (CNC milling, EDM machines).

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Run LinkedIn B2B campaign targeting automotive procurement managers in India; share technical specs, quality certifications, and case studies of successful supplies.
  • Sponsor booth at AutoIndEx and Engineering Expo India (₹50-75K); demonstrate live precision machining; collect 50+ qualified leads per event.
  • Create YouTube channel (TechTalk Manufacturing) showing product demos, customer testimonials, and manufacturing process; optimize for 'precision engineering Delhi' and 'automotive components supplier'.

up to ₹25L subsidy (25% for general category) for setting up manufacturing unit.

MUDRA Yojana
up to ₹10L collateral-free loan from banks for working capital and machinery.

Delhi MSME Development Scheme
interest subsidy of 5% on loans up to ₹50L for manufacturing units.
🏚
Delhi Export Promotion Scheme
2-5% rebate on export sales for registered exporters of engineering goods.
