ODOP Product

Handmade Paper Art

Jalaun has a rich tradition of artisanal paper craftsmanship spanning generations with abundant local availability of natural fibers and plant-based materials. The district's skilled workforce and established cluster infrastructure make it uniquely positioned for scalable handmade paper production. Strong government support through ODOP status and GI certification provides market credibility and export advantages.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Jalaun.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jalaun district handicraft cluster near Oraiyagarh and connect with existing artisan groups; attend IMRB (Indian Markets Research Bureau) handmade paper exhibitions; study competitor products on Etsy, Fab India, and local ODOP portals.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source natural fibers from local suppliers in Jalaun and nearby districts (₹8,000-15,000 for first 50kg batch); procure sizing agents and paints from Delhi NCR suppliers; establish direct relationships with 2-3 suppliers for regular procurement at ₹25,000-40,000 monthly.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established artisans in Jalaun cluster through local Handicraft Cooperative or SFURTI groups; alternatively, start in-house with 2-3 trained artisans and invest in basic tools (pressing frames, brushes, embossing tools) totaling ₹60,000-80,000.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam MSME portal (online, free); apply for IEC code from nearest APEDA office (Lucknow/Kanpur); obtain GI certification documentation from district handicraft office; register brand name on trademarkia.com; get ISO 9001 certification from recognized agency.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (15% fees), Amazon Handmade (8% fees), and own Shopify store; approach Fab India, Good Earth, and local heritage shops for wholesale placement; list on ODOP.gov.in and State Handicraft Development Corporation portal; execute Instagram/Pinterest content with product photography.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 10-15 skilled workers; introduce new product lines (wall hangings, custom stationery for corporates); set up distribution partnerships with premium gift chains; pursue bulk B2B contracts with hospitality and corporate sectors; explore participation in international craft fairs.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels showing 15-30 second process videos of artisans hand-making paper, embossing flowers, and finishing products; use hashtags #JalaunHandmadePaper #EcoArt #SustainableCraft.
  • Partner with 5-7 sustainable lifestyle influencers (50K-500K followers) for product seeding and review content; target eco-conscious micro-influencers in India and abroad.
  • Launch email campaigns to premium stationery buyers and corporate procurement teams highlighting customization, sustainability story, and GI certification; segment by corporate gift budgets (₹500-2000 per unit range).
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh grant with 25% subsidy for manufacturing units, applicable for artisan-led paper production startups.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 initial credit and ₹125,000 second tranche for traditional artisans; direct craft marketing support and certification.
🏚
UP Micro Credit Scheme
collateral-free loans up to ₹10 lakh for MSME handicraft businesses registered in Uttar Pradesh.
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Handicraft Cluster Scheme
infrastructure support, skill training, and working capital assistance for artisans in recognized clusters like Jalaun.
