Rice & Rice Products

Nalbari's fertile alluvial plains and abundant water resources from the Brahmaputra river system create ideal conditions for rice cultivation. The district's traditional expertise in rice farming spanning generations, combined with its favorable climate and soil composition, enables production of premium scented and glutinous rice varieties that command premium prices in domestic and export markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Nalbari.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nalbari district mandi in Nalbari town and neighboring Kharupetia cluster to understand paddy procurement, milling quality standards, and pricing. Spend 2-3 weeks shadowing established rice millers like those in Tihu or Nalbari town mandi. Understand seasonal variations and connect with 5-10 rice exporters to learn export compliance and quality requirements.
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure paddy directly from farmer cooperatives in Nalbari district during harvest (Oct-Nov) at ₹1,800-2,200 per quintal. For first batch of 50 quintals paddy, budget ₹1.5-1.8 Lakhs. Source packaging (jute sacks, kraft bags) from wholesale markets in Guwahati or Nalbari town at ₹8-12 per unit for bulk orders. Establish relationships with 2-3 reliable paddy suppliers for consistent quality.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing small rice mills in Tihu or Kharupetia cluster rather than investing in mill setup initially (eliminates ₹30-50L capex). Contract milling at ₹300-400 per quintal for processing and grading. Alternatively, start with contract farming arrangement with 5-10 farmers to control paddy quality from cultivation. Invest in simple grading and packaging at small unit (₹3-5L) before scaling to full milling operation.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online as "Rice Processing & Trading" at udyamregistration.gov.in (free). Obtain Spice Board registration for rice products. Apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Guwahati for export eligibility. Register brand name with IP India. Obtain FSSAI food license (₹5,000-10,000) and state agricultural department certification. Get organic certification if targeting premium segment (₹30,000-50,000 for first year).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, Grofers with branded packaged rice at ₹80-120 per kg (D2C). Create Instagram/Facebook presence with product photography and farming stories. Contact HoReCa (hotel-restaurant-café) chains in Assam and Northeast through direct sales. Approach rice exporters in Kolkata, Chennai, and Mumbai for B2B bulk contracts. Sell in local Nalbari markets and neighboring towns through retail shop partnerships. Participate in APEDA food export trade shows.
6
Scale
After 6 months, invest ₹20-30L in small milling setup to improve margins from 20% to 35-40%. Expand product line to include rice flour, puffed rice, and specialty glutinous rice varieties. Build direct export relationships in Bangladesh, Nepal, and Southeast Asia via APEDA support. Establish cold storage facility for paddy procurement during harvest season. Launch B2B platform targeting food manufacturers. Scale e-commerce to 10+ platforms and hire digital marketing specialist.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Launch Instagram series '#NalbariToYourTable' showcasing farmer stories, paddy-to-plate process, and recipe videos using Nalbari rice. Partner with food bloggers in Assam and Northeast for organic reach. Run carousel ads targeting affluent urban consumers aged 28-45 in metros.
  • Create B2B WhatsApp group with hotel chefs, caterers, and restaurant owners in Guwahati, Assam offering bulk discounts and quality guarantees. Conduct monthly virtual product demonstrations for bulk buyers.
  • Sponsor local Bihu festival events in Nalbari district and conduct tasting booths showcasing rice varieties and products. Generate word-of-mouth in farming communities and strengthen farmer supplier relationships.
