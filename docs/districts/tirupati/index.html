Srikalahasthi Kalamkari fabrics/Handicrafts

Srikalahasti town has 400+ years of kalamkari heritage with a concentrated cluster of skilled artisans trained in traditional hand-painting and natural dye techniques. The proximity to natural resources, established artisan communities, and temple-linked cultural patronage make it the authentic production hub for this craft. The GI tag ensures protected brand identity and premium positioning in domestic and international markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tirupati.

1
Learn / Research
Spend 2-3 weeks in Srikalahasti town; connect with artisans via Srikalahasti Artisans Cooperative and Tirupati District Handloom Development Society; study kalamkari techniques, visit museums, understand natural dye sourcing
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton from Andhra Pradesh cotton mills (Vijayawada, Guntur); procure natural dyes from Kailasagiri (indigo), local herbal suppliers (madder root); first batch of 500 meters fabric + dyes ≈ ₹80,000-120,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 artisans in Srikalahasti on piece-rate basis (₹200-400 per meter depending on design complexity); establish informal production agreement; begin with 100-150 pieces monthly
4
Brand & Register
Register as individual proprietor/MSME via Udyam portal; obtain IEC code from DGFT (online); apply for GI authentication certificate from Srikalahasti Weavers Cooperative; trademark brand name with IP office
5
First Sale
List 15-20 signature designs on Etsy (12% commission), Craft.co, and Pinterest; contact 30+ boutique stores in Bangalore/Delhi/Mumbai; pitch to international craft agencies; attend Handloom Export Promotion Council (HEPC) craft fairs
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire 2-3 full-time artisans; expand design portfolio; establish B2B relationships with 5-10 export wholesalers; apply for SFURTI cluster development grants; explore store franchising model in major metros

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram content series 'Artisan Stories' — daily behind-the-scenes reels of hand-painting process from Srikalahasti; tag lifestyle influencers (50K-500K followers); use #SrikalahastiBorn #KalamkariHeritage
  • Partner with 5 interior design Instagram accounts to gift 2-3 pieces for photoshoots; negotiate for product tags and story mentions; budget ₹20,000-30,000 for placements
  • Launch limited-edition monthly drops (10-15 pieces) on Etsy with 48-hour flash sales; use Pinterest for traffic (high conversion for handcrafts); create YouTube tutorial on how to style kalamkari sarees/fabrics
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹25 L for artisan cluster development, common facility centers, design intervention for Srikalahasti cluster
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 cash incentive, free toolkit worth ₹500-3000, 5% concessional credit up to ₹1 Cr for traditional artisans
🏚
Andhra Pradesh Handloom Policy 2019
Direct tax exemption for export-oriented handloom units, 100% subsidy on design certification, export subsidy 5-10% on FOB value
🏚
AP Artisan Pension Scheme
Monthly stipends for traditional artisans in registered cooperatives, healthcare coverage for artisans in Srikalahasti cluster
