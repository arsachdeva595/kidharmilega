Ready Made Garments

Dharmapuri has a strong textile heritage with skilled artisans trained in traditional weaving and embroidery techniques. The region's proximity to major cloth markets in Tamil Nadu and established supply chains for raw materials make it ideal for RMG manufacturing. The local labor force is experienced in garment production with competitive wage structures.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Dharmapuri.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dharmapuri textile clusters in Harur and Palacode taluk; connect with 5-10 existing RMG manufacturers; study designs at Kumbakonam saree market and Coimbatore textile hubs; analyze competitor pricing on Flipkart/Meesho
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton and silk fabrics from Erode textile traders (₹40-80/meter bulk); procure trims from Tiruppur suppliers; buy embroidery materials from Chennai wholesale markets; first batch of 500 units requires ₹2-3 lakhs
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 local tailoring units in Dharmapuri town; establish quality control checklist; hire a production manager; start with 100-200 units/month; consider SFURTI cluster training for artisan linkage
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam for MSME classification within 2 days online; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office (Bangalore); register trademark with IP India; set up GST registration; create brand identity with local designer
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho and Flipkart Marketplace with 50-75 SKUs; approach 20-30 ethnic wear retailers in metros via email/calls; create Instagram account with daily design posts; pitch to corporate gift providers; participate in local trade shows
6
Scale
Expand product range to include kurtas, lehengas, fusion wear; hire dedicated design and marketing team; open showroom in Bangalore or Chennai; apply for PMEGP/MUDRA for capacity expansion; collaborate with micro-influencers in fashion niche
