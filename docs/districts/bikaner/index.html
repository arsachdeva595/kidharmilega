ODOP Product

Bikaneri Namkeen

Bikaner's arid climate and centuries-old spice trade routes created the perfect environment for developing distinctive namkeen recipes. The region's access to premium chickpea, peanuts, and spices from Rajasthan and Guajarat makes it the natural production hub. Multi-generational artisan families have perfected the craft, making Bikaner synonymous with authentic, high-quality savory snacks across India and globally.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bikaner.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bikaner's namkeen cluster in Kote Gate and Jail Road areas; meet 3-5 established manufacturers (Haldiram's, Bhikaram's local units, smaller family businesses); understand GI certification requirements and food safety standards (FSSAI) by consulting Bikaner Chamber of Commerce & Industry
2
Source Raw Materials
Source besan from Jodhpur wholesale markets (₹40-50/kg), peanuts from Jaipur agricultural markets (₹60-80/kg), spices from Bikaner's traditional spice traders (₹150-300/kg depending on type); first batch of 500kg raw materials costs ₹25,000-35,000; partner with local farmers for long-term supply contracts
3
Produce / Partner
Either rent a co-kitchen in Bikaner (₹15,000-25,000/month for 1000 sq ft) with fryer and packing equipment, OR engage 2-3 artisan families on a production partnership model (₹5-8 per unit production cost); conduct FSSAI food safety training for all production staff; implement batch-wise quality testing
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on MSME portal (free, 5 days); apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office (₹0, 7-10 days); obtain FSSAI license (₹2000-5000, 15 days); apply for GI usage certificate from Ministry of Food Processing; register brand name with USPTO India (₹5000-10,000); design packaging complying with FSSAI labeling norms within ₹2,00,000 first batch
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and Flipkart Supermarket with 10-15 SKU variants (complete in 3 weeks); approach 5-10 premium supermarkets (Big Basket, Nature's Basket, local chains) with samples and wholesale terms; build Instagram/Facebook presence showcasing heritage story and artisan partners (₹30,000 initial content creation); offer 10-15% wholesale margins to secure initial shelf space
6
Scale
After 6 months, enter 50+ retail stores via modern trade aggregator partnerships; launch export via APEDA-approved food export agent; develop 3-5 premium variants (Organic, Low-Oil, Regional Spice Mixes) to capture higher margins; invest ₹50,000/month in performance marketing (Google Shopping, Facebook Ads) targeting diaspora audiences; explore co-packing arrangements with established food manufacturers to meet bulk orders

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Influencer storytelling on Instagram/YouTube with micro-influencers (50K-500K followers) from food heritage space; feature artisan family stories, production process transparency, and GI authenticity — budget ₹50,000-80,000 per influencer for 3-month partnership
  • Target diaspora communities on Facebook and WhatsApp with nostalgia-driven campaigns showing 'taste of home' messaging; partner with Indian grocery delivery apps in USA/UK for paid advertising — focus on Diwali/festive seasons
  • Launch referral program via email and SMS offering 15% discount for both referrer and referee; build an ambassador network of 20-30 food bloggers and corporate gifting coordinators in metros offering exclusive early access to new products
