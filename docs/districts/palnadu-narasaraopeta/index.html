Durgi Stone Carving

Palnadu district, particularly Durgi village, has a 300+ year legacy of stone carving with access to premium granite, marble, and sandstone quarries. The artisan community possesses hereditary skills passed through generations, making it the epicenter of traditional stone carving craftsmanship in Andhra Pradesh.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Palnadu (Narasaraopeta).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Durgi village in Narasaraopeta, Palnadu and spend 2-3 weeks with master stone carvers. Visit Hyderabad handicraft clusters (Charminar area) to understand market demand. Connect with Andhra Pradesh Handicrafts Development Corporation (APHDC) for industry insights.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source stone from local quarries in Palnadu (granite ₹15-25/kg, marble ₹20-35/kg, sandstone ₹10-15/kg). First batch budget: ₹2-3L for 500kg mixed stone + tools. Connect with quarry owners directly in Narasaraopeta area or through APHDC supplier network.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-10 skilled artisans in Durgi village on contract basis (₹300-500/day wages). Establish a small workshop (500 sq ft) in Durgi or Narasaraopeta with basic infrastructure. Use WhatsApp/video to manage quality and timelines. Start with pre-designed pieces before custom orders.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise) on udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from DGFT for export. Register GI certification usage with DC Handicraft ministry. Create brand identity reflecting Durgi heritage (logo, packaging with GI mark, Instagram handle). File trademark for brand name with IP India.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy.com, Amazon Handmade, and 1stDibs targeting international buyers (70% initial focus). Create Instagram/Pinterest visual stories of carving process. Approach interior design studios and 5-star hotels in Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi. Participate in India International Trade Fair (IITF) New Delhi in November.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire 2-3 junior carvers for apprenticeship. Expand product range with modern designs + traditional pieces. Launch WhatsApp catalog for B2B. Partner with architecture firms for regular contracts. Target design award platforms (India Design Council). Consider pop-up stores in Tier 1 cities (Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi).

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels: Weekly 30-sec videos of carving process (chisel-to-finish). Tag design influencers and interior architects. Use hashtags #DurgiStoneCarving #HandcraftedInIndia #GIProduct to reach 50K+ audience monthly.
  • Pinterest boards: Create 5 boards (Temple Art, Modern Interiors, Garden Sculptures, Corporate Art, Gifting) with high-quality product images. Link to Etsy/website. Target DIY and home decor audiences (15K+ monthly saves potential).
  • B2B outreach: Cold email to 200 interior design firms, architects, and 5-star hotel groups with portfolio PDF + WhatsApp. Offer 8-12% bulk discounts. Follow up with LinkedIn connection requests.
