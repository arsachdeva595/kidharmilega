Kheda district has ideal agroclimatic conditions for rice, wheat, pulses, and sugarcane cultivation, with established supply chains and cooperative networks in Nadiad. The region's strong agricultural base combined with existing food processing infrastructure makes it naturally suited for value-added food products. Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kheda (Nadiad).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nadiad's agricultural cooperative markets (NAFED offices, Nadiad APMC market) and meet established jaggery/rice processors. Connect with Kheda District Industries Centre (DIC) and attend food processing workshops. Study competitor brands like Aeroplane Jaggery, Organic brands selling in local markets.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source directly from Nadiad's sugar mills for jaggery base (₹35-45/kg), rice from Kheda cooperative societies (₹20-30/kg wholesale), pulses from regional dealers. Initial first batch cost: ₹2-3 lakhs for 5-10 MT trial production with packaging.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing FSSAI-certified food processing units in Nadiad (e.g., industrial zones near Rampura or Umreth) or set up your own with co-packing arrangements. Contract manufacturing available at ₹8-12/unit for jaggery packets, ₹6-10/kg for processed pulses.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) immediately online. Obtain FSSAI license (₹4000-12000, 7-10 days). Apply for IEC code via ICEGATE for export. Trademark brand name with IP office. Ensure GMP/HACCP certification for export readiness.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Launchpad or Flipkart Seller Pro (15-25% commission). Approach organic/natural food retailers like Naturals, Nature's Basket in Gujarat. Use WhatsApp Business + JioMart seller dashboard for direct retail. Target bulk orders via B2B platforms like IndiaMART or TradeKey.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product line to include ready-to-eat dal mixes, flavored rice, premium spice blends. Build retail presence in 10-15 cities via distribution partners. Invest in food packaging upgrades and sustainability certifications (organic, non-GMO). Pursue export certifications and target global e-commerce platforms.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram & YouTube Reels showing farm-to-table journey — short clips of harvesting, processing, and final product. Partner with micro-influencers (100K-500K followers) in food & health space for authentic reviews.
  • Run Google & Facebook ads targeting health-conscious audiences searching 'organic jaggery,' 'premium rice,' 'natural spices' with landing pages to your Shopify/WooCommerce store.
  • Email drip campaigns to health stores, restaurants, and corporate offices offering bulk discounts (10-20% for orders >₹50K). Use Mailchimp or similar tools with monthly product newsletters.
