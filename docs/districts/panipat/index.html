Handloom and Textiles

Panipat has an established 500+ year handloom weaving heritage with a concentrated cluster of 15,000+ looms and skilled artisans. The district's proximity to Delhi NCR provides easy market access while raw material sourcing from Punjab and UP is cost-efficient. State infrastructure support and existing export networks make it the ideal base for handloom textile ventures.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Panipat.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Panipat handloom clusters near Old City and Samalkha areas. Meet 5-10 established exporters and artisan groups. Study product pricing on Alibaba, Global Sources. Attend Indian Textile Exporters Association events. Shadow operations at existing blanket/carpet manufacturers for 2-3 weeks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source yarn from Ludhiana textile mills (cotton/wool @ ₹250-400/kg), dyes from Gujarat chemical suppliers, and packaging from Delhi. First batch (50 blankets or 20 rugs): ₹150,000-250,000. Negotiate 30-day payment terms with established suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-10 artisans/micro-units in Panipat (average 2-4 looms each) through formal production agreements. Provide yarn, designs, and quality specs; pay per piece (₹400-800 per blanket). Or invest ₹2-3M in 10-15 looms at shared workshop spaces and hire trained weavers (₹12,000-18,000/month).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (within 48 hrs at udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export eligibility. Create brand identity (name, logo, hang tags) with Panipat heritage narrative. Register trademark with IP India. Set up GST registration (5% for handlooms).
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Handmade, Flipkart Supermart, Pepperfry. Create Instagram/Pinterest with behind-the-scenes artisan content. Reach out to 50+ hospitality buyers via LinkedIn. Participate in Indian Textile Expo and IITF. Launch email campaigns to interior designers and architects. Offer first-time bulk discounts (10-15%) to institutional buyers.
6
Scale
After 6 months, onboard 20+ artisans to increase production capacity 3-4x. Launch premium designer collection (₹2000+ per piece). Establish B2B partnerships with 10+ hotel chains and exporters. Invest in digital marketing (₹50,000/month budget). Explore bulk export contracts via trade commissions in USA and Germany. Consider semi-automated processes for repeat designs.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 10 Instagram lifestyle influencers (50K-500K followers) to showcase handloom blankets and rugs in home décor settings; offer 20% commission on sales.
  • Create YouTube documentary-style series about artisan stories and traditional weaving techniques; upload monthly; optimize for 'handmade blankets' and 'handloom rugs India' keywords.
  • Run Pinterest campaigns targeting 'home décor trends' and 'luxury blankets'; design rich product pins with Panipat heritage messaging; drive traffic to e-commerce store.
