Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tikamgarh.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tikamgarh's ginger-growing villages (Niwari, Prithipur blocks) and connect with ATMA (Agricultural Technology Management Agency) office. Study production methods at Tikamgarh's agricultural extension center. Network with 3-5 established ginger farmers to understand yield, costs, and buyer connections. Review MP government agricultural department reports on ginger cultivation standards.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified ginger rhizomes from Tikamgarh's local seed farms or Madhya Pradesh Agriculture Department. First batch (1 acre): ₹80,000-120,000 for quality rhizomes, compost, and basic tools. Partner with local farmer cooperatives for bulk procurement discounts. Visit weekly mandis in Niwari and Jatara to establish supplier relationships.
3
Produce / Partner
Lease or own 1-2 acres in Tikamgarh district (₹15,000-25,000/acre/season). Work with local farmer cooperatives or hire experienced laborers for planting (May-June) and harvesting (December-January). Set up basic drying infrastructure (covered yard or solar dryer, ₹50,000-150,000). Partner with existing spice processors in Tikamgarh for small-batch drying or establish own drying unit.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (via udyamregistration.gov.in) as agriculture producer. Obtain IEC code from DGFT for export capability. Get organic certification if targeting premium segment (₹30,000-80,000, 12-18 months). Design packaging with local addresses (Tikamgarh origin) for brand authenticity. Trademark brand name with IP office.
5
First Sale
Launch on Indiamart, Alibaba, and TradeKey for B2B orders (target export buyers). Set up D2C storefront on Shopify or Pepperfry. List on BigBasket and Amazon Fresh for retail reach. Email outreach to 50+ food processors, supplement brands, and exporters with product samples. Register with APEDA (Agricultural and Processed Food Products Export Development Authority) for export certifications.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand acreage to 5-10 acres via contract farming partnerships. Develop 2-3 value-added products (powder, paste, essential oil extract). Build B2B relationships with national spice chains like MDH and Everest suppliers. Invest in mechanized drying and processing equipment (₹500,000-1,000,000). Establish export logistics partnerships with forwarders in Indore or Bhopal. Explore SFURTI cluster support for collaborative marketing and export.
