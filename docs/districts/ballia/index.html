ODOP Product

Bindi (Tikuli)

Ballia has a centuries-old tradition of bindi-making with skilled artisans concentrated in clusters like Naugaon and surrounding villages. The district's proximity to major markets (Varanasi, Lucknow) and established supply chains for raw materials make it ideal for scaling bindi production. The low cost of labour and existing cooperative networks support rapid expansion.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ballia.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Naugaon bindi cluster in Ballia; meet established producers like those in Ballia Bindi Makers Association; study designs at Ballia Haat market; spend 2-3 days understanding production, cost structure, and design trends.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Ballia's local suppliers (pigments, gum, mica) and larger vendors in Lucknow/Varanasi; obtain samples from 3-4 suppliers; plan first batch of 10,000 units at ₹0.50-1.50 per bindi depending on design complexity (₹5,000-15,000 total).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established artisan families in Naugaon cluster (15-20 women per unit can produce 200-300 units/day); negotiate piece rates (₹0.30-0.60 per bindi); visit weekly to maintain quality control; alternatively, invest in semi-automated tools for in-house production.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in) within 1 week; obtain IEC code from DGFT online portal (₹0); open GST account (18% applicable); design brand identity, packaging with Ballia heritage messaging; register trademark with IP India.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Seller Central and Flipkart with 5-7 SKUs (classic, glitter, stone-embedded, festival lines); target Meesho for tier-2/3 reseller reach; partner with 5-10 local beauty retailers in Lucknow and Varanasi; create Instagram account with daily design posts; join Ballia ODOP seller network for visibility.
6
Scale
Expand design portfolio by monthly; hire a sales coordinator; negotiate contracts with large retailers (Fabindia, beauty chains); launch exports through B2B platforms; introduce value-added products (bindi removal pads, sets); achieve ₹5-10L monthly revenue by month 12.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with beauty YouTubers and micro-influencers (50K-500K followers) in Hindi/Hindustani space; send curated bindi boxes monthly; target videos on 'traditional vs modern bindi styles' reaching 2-5M views.
  • Run festive-season Facebook and Instagram carousel ads targeting women 18-45 in Tier 2 cities (budget ₹500-1000/day during Diwali, Karva Chauth, Navratri); use user-generated content from customer reviews.
  • Launch 'Bindi of the Month' subscription on WhatsApp Business; segment audience by occasion (daily, bridal, festival); push monthly through direct messages to 1000+ customers; achieve 30-40% repeat orders.
