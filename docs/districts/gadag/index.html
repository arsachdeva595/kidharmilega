ODOP Product

Byadagi Chilies

Gadag's distinct soil composition, altitude (600m), and semi-arid climate create ideal conditions for Byadagi chili cultivation. The region has over 50 years of specialized farming expertise, established supply chains, and natural water availability from the Krishna River system. Local farmer cooperatives and traditional knowledge make scaling and quality control seamless.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Gadag.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Byadagi chili clusters in Gadag taluk (specifically Byadagi village and Nargund region); connect with APEDA (Agricultural & Processed Food Products Export Development Authority) regional office in Bengaluru; attend Gadag Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) auctions to understand pricing, quality grades, and seasonal supply patterns
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 500kg-1MT of dried Byadagi chilies from Gadag APMC or directly from farmer collectives like Gadag Ryot Sangh; expected cost ₹150-200/kg = ₹75K-₹2L for first batch; establish long-term contracts with 3-5 farmer suppliers for consistency
3
Produce / Partner
Either partner with local drying and packaging units in Gadag (e.g., existing SPICEs drying facilities) for processing, or invest in basic drying racks, grading tables, and packaging machinery (₹5-15L); ensure compliance with Food Safety & Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) standards; consider clustering with existing Byadagi chili processing cooperatives for economies of scale
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online MSME registration, free); apply for IEC code from Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT) for export eligibility; obtain FSSAI license (₹5K-15K); register brand name with Intellectual Property Office; source GI certification documentation for marketing advantage
5
First Sale
List on Amazon, Flipkart, and BigBasket with premium positioning; approach ethnic grocery chains (NaturallyIndian, Indian Grocery Stores) and food-service wholesalers via direct sales; partner with spice exporters (e.g., MDH, Everest, Shan) as B2B suppliers; launch Instagram and YouTube content featuring farm-to-table storytelling; target 50-100 direct consumers in first 3 months via email and social proof
6
Scale
After 6 months, invest in own cold storage and grading facility in Gadag (₹50-100L); expand B2B network to 10+ food manufacturers; launch 3-5 value-added products (chili powder, blends); build export contracts with 3+ international distributors via trade shows (Annapoorna, Spice Board events); scale social media to 10K+ followers; hire 2-3 full-time supply and quality staff

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for registered MSME in agri-processing; covers 25-35% of project cost for marginal/small farmers
🏛
Agricultural Infrastructure Fund (AIF)
zero-interest loans up to ₹2Cr for post-harvest infrastructure (cold storage, drying units, processing); repayment over 10 years
🏚
Karnataka Agri-Export Policy
5% subsidy on export value for processed agricultural products including spices; administered by Department of Commerce & Industries
🏚
Krishi Sinchayee Yojana
micro-irrigation subsidy up to 90% for chili farmers in Gadag; applicable if cultivating own chilis; improves yield and water efficiency
Registration required: Required — udyamregistration.gov.in
