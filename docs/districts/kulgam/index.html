The region's agricultural heritage and farmer expertise in garlic farming spanning generations make it naturally suited for ODOP development. The district's proximity to Kashmir's major trading hubs enables efficient supply chain access to national and international markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kulgam.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kulgam district's primary farming clusters in Qazigund and Vesu areas; interview 10-15 established garlic farmers; study production cycles at Krishi Vigyan Kendra (KVK) Kulgam; analyze export data from APEDA and Spices Board India
2
Source Raw Materials
Source quality garlic seed stock from certified agricultural nurseries in Srinagar (approx. ₹4-6 per kg); purchase organic fertilizers from local agricultural cooperatives; first-batch cost for 5-10 bigha farmland: ₹200,000-400,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 15-25 Kulgam-based farmer groups through government PACS (Primary Agricultural Cooperative Societies); establish small processing unit (drying, sorting, packing) in Kulgam town; hire local labor for harvesting and processing
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam for manufacturing (online at udyamregistration.gov.in); apply for IEC code via DGFT Srinagar portal; file FPI registration with FSSAI for processed products; create brand identity emphasizing 'High-Altitude Kashmir Premium Garlic'
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and Blinkit for D2C; approach wholesale spice traders in Delhi Spice Market and Mumbai APMC; partner with NaturalShrikhandi and Organic Brands India for retail distribution; contact exporters via APEDA membership
6
Scale
Expand farming partnership to 50-100 farmers by month 8; invest in cold storage facility in Kulgam (₹8-12 Lakh); launch own D2C website with SEO focus on 'organic garlic online' keywords; establish export contracts with 3-5 international importers; develop proprietary garlic supplements line
