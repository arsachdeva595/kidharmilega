Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kanpur Dehat.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kanpur Dehat's established utensil manufacturing clusters (Jajmau area and surrounding villages). Interview 5-10 existing zinc utensil manufacturers. Study market dynamics at Kanpur's wholesale markets (Jajmau wholesale market). Join local MSME networks and chambers of commerce. Research competitor brands on Amazon, Flipkart, and regional craft marketplaces.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source zinc ingots (99.99% purity) from certified suppliers in Kolkata or Jamnagar at ₹150-180/kg. Purchase tin and copper alloys from Kanpur metal dealers. First batch cost (500 units): ₹80,000-120,000. Build relationships with local artisan suppliers in Kanpur Dehat who can provide casting and finishing services at ₹15-25 per unit.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established artisan workshops in Kanpur Dehat (Bahalpur, Fatehpur, Ghatampur villages have active clusters). Begin with job-work arrangement (pay per unit) rather than owning equipment initially. Invest in quality control testing and design templates. Alternatively, rent small workshop space (₹5,000-8,000/month) and hire 2-3 skilled workers for casting, spinning, and finishing.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam MSME enterprise (online, free). Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office for export eligibility. Register trademark for brand name at Intellectual Property Office. Obtain Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) registration if selling food-contact utensils. Get BIS certification for durability and safety standards (optional but recommended for premium positioning).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon and Flipkart via seller central (take 15-25% commission initially). Build Instagram and Facebook presence with product photography and artisan storytelling. Approach regional kitchenware retailers (Mother India, BigBasket vendor partnerships). List on Udyam marketplace and regional craft platforms. Offer bulk discounts to corporate gifting companies and hotel suppliers. Target wedding registries and festive season bulk orders.
6
Scale
After stabilizing first 100 orders, invest in automation for repetitive tasks (polishing, packaging). Expand product range to specialized items (pressure cooker bases, serving pieces). Build export partnerships with distributors in Bangladesh, Nepal, and UAE. Recruit 2 full-time artisans and 1 sales/operations manager. Upgrade to BIS certification if not done. Expand D2C marketing budget and consider paid ads on Google and Instagram. Target corporate bulk orders during festival seasons.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showcasing artisan craftsmanship: document zinc-shaping process, polishing techniques, and finished products. Partner with micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in home decor and sustainable living niches. Tag #ZincUtensils #TraditionalCraft #KanpurHeritage. Allocate ₹15,000/month.
  • YouTube long-form content: Create 5-10 minute documentaries on artisan stories, product durability tests (heating, cooling, impact tests), and comparison with aluminum/stainless steel. Optimize for keywords: 'best zinc cooking utensils India,' 'traditional metalware,' 'Kanpur utensils.' Allocate ₹10,000/month.
  • Facebook community building: Create a private group for customers sharing recipes, cooking tips, and product care. Host monthly live sessions with artisans and cooking experts. Run targeted ads to women 25-45 in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) interested in traditional cookware. Allocate ₹20,000/month.
