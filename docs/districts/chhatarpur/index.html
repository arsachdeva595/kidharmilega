Furniture - Wooden

Chhatarpur's proximity to dense forests provides abundant high-quality timber and sandalwood, while the district has a long-established tradition of skilled woodworking artisans. The region's historical craftsmanship heritage and lower labor costs make it ideal for producing premium wooden furniture at competitive prices.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Chhatarpur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chhatarpur's main furniture clusters in Rajnagar and Beejapur; connect with District Industries Centre; interview 5-10 existing artisan groups; study Rajnagar Furniture Association operations; analyze competitors on Pepperfry and Urban Ladder.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact timber suppliers in Chhatarpur (e.g., Bansal Timber Merchants, local forest department authorized dealers); order first batch of 500-800 board feet at ₹180-250/board foot; negotiate credit terms; budget ₹120K-180K for materials, delivery, and storage setup.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established artisan groups in Rajnagar cluster under revenue-share model (40-50% to artisans); provide design specs; establish weekly production review; implement QC checklist; alternatively, set up small 500 sqft workshop with 5-8 skilled carpenters at ₹40K monthly combined wages.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (within 24 hours, free); obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office; register brand name with IP India; get ISO 9001 certification (optional, costs ₹35K-50K); open business bank account; apply for MSME Udyam rating.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C website with 3D product configurator; list 15-20 SKUs on Amazon and Flipkart; create Instagram account with artisan-in-action reels; approach 10 interior designers in Delhi/Bangalore with wholesale pricing; attend Furniture Mart in Nagpur (Feb); target luxury segment via LinkedIn B2B outreach.
6
Scale
Expand product range to modular furniture and outdoor pieces; hire 2 full-time salespeople for B2B; partner with logistics for pan-India delivery; launch corporate gifting line; apply for export subsidy under SFURTI scheme; build showroom in Tier 1 city (minimum ₹8L investment).

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels series '#ArtisanStories' featuring 60-90 second clips of craftsmen at work, furniture transformation time-lapses, and customer home showcases; post 3x weekly; target 35-55 age group with household income 15L+.
  • Partner with 8-10 micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in home decor niche; send 2-3 custom pieces monthly; negotiate ₹5K-15K per post; focus on Instagram and YouTube; measure via promo codes.
  • B2B outreach via LinkedIn and email to interior designers in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai; offer 35-40% wholesale discounts; include product lookbook, bulk order terms, and 45-day payment terms; host 2 quarterly webinars on wooden furniture trends.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for SC/ST/OBC, 25% for general); apply via District Industries Centre Chhatarpur.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
up to ₹15L interest-free credit for artisans and craftspeople; direct benefit to artisan partners; apply at MSME development institute.
🏚
Madhya Pradesh MSME Promotion Policy 2023
5% interest subsidy on term loans up to ₹1Cr for handicraft sector; apply through State Bank of India or eligible banks.
🏚
MP Kaushal Vikas Yojana
skill development grants for artisan training; relevant for upskilling workers in modern design and finishing techniques.
