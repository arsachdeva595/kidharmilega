ODOP Product

Zari-Zardozi

Chandauli has a 300+ year heritage of zari and zardozi craftsmanship with a concentrated artisan community in clusters like Baidyanathpur and Mundera. The district's proximity to Varanasi (40 km) provides access to raw materials, skilled labor, and a thriving wedding/festive wear market. Deep-rooted family traditions ensure consistent quality and design innovation.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Chandauli.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Baidyanathpur and Mundera zardozi clusters in Chandauli (2-3 days immersion); interview 5-8 master artisans; study price points at Varanasi's Thatheri Bazaar and Mysore Silk; analyze competitors on Sabyasachi and local bridal shops.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source zari thread from Jaipur's Johari Bazaar (₹8-15/meter raw zari, MOQ ₹50K); base fabrics from Surat textile markets (₹80-300/meter); beads/sequins from Delhi's Chandni Chowk (₹5-20K first batch). First-batch cost: ₹80,000-150,000 for 20-30 embroidered pieces.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 artisan families through local cooperatives or direct relationships; visit Chandauli Handicrafts Association to get referrals; start with piece-rate contracts at ₹500-2000 per item (based on complexity); establish quality checkpoints with photo documentation.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) online via msme.gov.in; apply for IEC code via DGFT portal; trademark brand name via ipindia.gov.in; get Handicraft Certificate from DC Handicraft office Chandauli; create GST account; obtain Artisan Card for partner artisans.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Amazon Handmade (2% commission) within month 1; parallel pitch to 10-15 ethnic wear boutiques in Delhi/Mumbai with ₹5-10K minimum orders; reach out to 5 wedding planners with portfolios; run Instagram/Pinterest Reels showing artisan stories; list on Jaypore and Fab India.
6
Scale
After ₹5-8L revenue, hire dedicated designer for new patterns; open showroom in Varanasi or Delhi; launch B2B portal for retailers; expand export via trade body partnerships; introduce adjacent products (zari borders, pre-embroidered fabrics); apply for SFURTI for cluster development support.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • YouTube documentary series showcasing artisan craftspeople; 8-10 minute episodes on techniques, family stories, and product creation to build authentic brand story; target wedding planners and ethnic wear enthusiasts.
  • Collaborate with 3-4 micro-influencers in bridal/ethnic wear space (20-50K followers) for Instagram Reels; provide free custom embroidered piece in exchange for 6-8 posts over 3 months; drive affiliate links.
  • Host 1 pop-up trunk show per quarter in Tier-1 cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) at high-end boutiques/wedding expos; offer 20% early-bird discount and referral bonuses; capture email list for retargeting.
