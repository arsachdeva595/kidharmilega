Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Narayanpet.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Narayanpet town weaving clusters (Pitlam Bazaar and Gandhi Nagar areas); spend 2-3 days with master weavers to understand loom types, yarn sourcing from Tiruppur, and quality grading; connect with Narayanpet Handloom Weavers Cooperative.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton from Tamil Nadu cotton mills (₹300-400/kg), mulberry silk from Karnataka suppliers (₹800-1200/kg), natural dyes locally or from Jaipur dye suppliers; first 10-saree batch costs ₹80,000-120,000 including artisan labor.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 registered weaver families through the cooperative; sign production agreements specifying design, timeline (4-6 weeks per saree), and quality standards; establish a small design studio to brief weavers on patterns and color combinations.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam MSME portal as Partnership/Proprietorship; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office; register brand name with IP office; obtain textile export license; get GI certification documentation from Ministry of Textiles for marketing claims.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade, Meesho, and Etsy with high-quality photography; create Instagram/Pinterest accounts highlighting artisan stories and heritage; partner with 5-10 premium ethnic wear boutiques in Hyderabad, Bangalore, Mumbai for initial distribution; leverage wedding blogs and influencer collaborations.
6
Scale
Expand to 15-20 weaver partnerships; introduce seasonal collections; open a physical showroom in Hyderabad or Bangalore; launch B2B corporate gifting division; establish export channels to UK and USA through trade partners; invest in online marketing and SEO.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 10-15 bridal wear influencers on Instagram to feature sarees in styled photoshoots; create behind-the-scenes weaver documentation videos for YouTube and TikTok highlighting craftsmanship.
  • Launch seasonal collections with cultural storytelling campaigns on Facebook and Instagram; run targeted ads during wedding season (Oct-Jan) to high-income women aged 28-50 in metros.
  • Create content partnerships with wedding magazines (The Wedding Journal, Bridelan) and lifestyle blogs; sponsor 3-4 premium ethnic wear fashion shows in major cities annually.
