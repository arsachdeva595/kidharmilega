Bell Metal (Dhokra)

Bastar has been a metallurgical hub for millennia with artisan communities possessing hereditary knowledge of bell metal alloys and lost-wax casting techniques. The region's access to tin, brass, and iron deposits combined with a thriving artisan ecosystem makes it the natural epicenter of authentic Dhokra production in India.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bastar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jagdalpur (Bastar headquarters) and Kondagaon artisan clusters; spend 2-3 weeks with master craftsmen like those in the Dhokra Artisan Association; study GI certification standards and lost-wax casting techniques at source
2
Source Raw Materials
Source brass scrap (₹300-350/kg), tin ingots (₹600-700/kg), beeswax (₹800-1000/kg) from Jagdalpur metal suppliers and Delhi-based smelters; budget ₹80K-120K for first batch of 100 pieces
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with hereditary Bastar Dhokra artisan groups (Kondagaon cooperative); negotiate co-production terms (₹800-2000/piece labor + materials); or hire 2-3 trained apprentices for ₹8K-12K/month and operate from a small workshop in Jagdalpur
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) and IEC code immediately for export eligibility; apply for GI certification holder status through DC Handicraft Ministry; create brand identity emphasizing '100% Bastar Legacy' positioning; file for PM Vishwakarma artisan certification
5
First Sale
List on Etsy and Amazon Handmade with 15-20 high-res lifestyle images; launch Instagram account with artisan-at-work reels (target design/home decor accounts); reach out to 50+ heritage retail curators via email; participate in India Craft Week and Acharya Craft Fair
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 8-10 craftspeople by month 6; build B2B relationships with 5-7 heritage home decor chains; establish export distributor in 2 Western countries by month 9; launch limited-edition designer collaborations; invest in a branded showroom in Jagdalpur
