Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Virudhunagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Virudhunagar textile cluster in areas like Nagercoil Road, Kamarajar Salai — spend 2-3 days meeting established gauze manufacturers, observe production processes, understand quality certifications (ISO 13485, FDA approval pathways). Connect with Virudhunagar District Industries Centre for vendor lists.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton from Chennai Cotton Traders or NAFED Tamil Nadu (budget ₹8-12L for first 5-ton batch); source bleaching chemicals from Coimbatore chemical suppliers. Total first-batch raw material cost: ₹18-25L. Establish credit terms with 2-3 suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established handloom weavers in Virudhunagar cluster via District Industries Centre referrals, or lease a small 20-loom production unit (rental ₹3-5L/month). Start with contract manufacturing model before in-house setup. Ensure ISO 13485 quality management system adoption.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam MSME (online, 10 minutes), obtain IEC code from DGFT portal for exports, secure ISO 13485:2016 medical device certification (₹2-3L via TUV/NABL), register business name with ROC/Registrar, obtain GST registration. File trademark for brand name.
5
First Sale
Launch B2B outreach to 50+ hospitals via LinkedIn/direct calls; list on 1mg Wholesale, Udaan, and TradeKey; attend medical equipment expos in Mumbai/Delhi (IMDEX, Medtech conferences). Cold email hospital procurement teams with sample kits and certification documents. Target 5-10 bulk orders of 500kg+ in first 3 months.
6
Scale
After ₹1Cr revenue: invest in own looms (₹15-20L), hire quality manager, establish ISO 14644 clean-room facility (₹10-15L capex), expand to 3+ product variants (sterile, non-sterile, specialty weaves), target EU CE mark certification, establish direct export relationships with 5-8 international distributors, scale workforce to 25-30.
