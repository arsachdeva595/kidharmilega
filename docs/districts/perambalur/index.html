Handicraft Products

Perambalur has a strong legacy of skilled artisans trained in traditional weaving, pottery, and woodcarving passed down through generations. The district's access to locally sourced raw materials—clay, wood, and natural fibers—provides cost advantages and sustainability. The cultural heritage and existing artisan clusters make it ideal for scaling traditional handicraft production while preserving authenticity.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Perambalur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Perambalur's artisan clusters in Neelampatnam and Vellore Road; connect with Tamil Nadu Handicrafts Development Corporation (TNHDC); attend textile and pottery fairs; interview 5+ established artisans on techniques, costs, and supply chains.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source clay from local suppliers near Perambalur pottery zones (₹2,000-5,000 per ton); cotton yarn from Tiruppur textile markets (₹120-180/kg); wood from licensed timber dealers in Erode (₹50,000-100,000 for first batch); natural dyes from herbal suppliers in Chennai (₹5,000-15,000).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 master artisans from Perambalur (initial rate ₹300-500 per piece depending on complexity); establish a small workshop (500-800 sq ft, ₹8,000-15,000/month); implement quality checkpoints; create production SOPs; aim for 200-500 pieces/month initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online within 1 day; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Chennai; trademark brand name (₹4,500-9,000); create GST registration; obtain artisan certification from TNHDC; design packaging reflecting regional art.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Amazon Handmade with high-quality photos and artisan storytelling; reach out to 20-30 home décor retailers in Bangalore/Hyderabad; pitch to corporate gifting platforms (GiftaLove, Giftr); create Instagram with reels showing artisan process; contact interior designers via LinkedIn.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 8-10 craftspeople; establish a retail pop-up in Bangalore/Chennai; apply for SFURTI cluster development support; launch subscription box for curated handicrafts; develop export partnerships with fair-trade organizations; implement inventory management system.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels showing 30-second artisan process videos (weaving, carving, pottery); use hashtags #PerambalurHandicraft #IndianCraftsmanship; post 3x/week with behind-the-scenes storytelling to build emotional connection.
  • Partner with 5 micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in home décor and sustainable fashion niches; send product samples; negotiate affiliate commissions (10-15%); track ROI via unique discount codes.
  • Launch a LinkedIn B2B outreach campaign targeting corporate procurement teams and interior design studios in Tier 1 cities; offer bulk discounts (12%+ on 50+ pieces); include artisan certification and sustainability messaging.
