ODOP Product

Copper based Products

Bageshwar has been a copper crafting hub for centuries with deep-rooted artisan communities who possess hereditary skills in copper beating and finishing. The region's proximity to raw material sources and a thriving local market of pilgrims and tourists visiting the sacred Bageshwar temple provides consistent demand. The district's cool climate and abundant water resources from the Gomti River support the traditional copper processing techniques.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bageshwar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bageshwar town copper craft clusters (especially Garuda Basti and Kanda areas) to meet master artisans; attend DC Handicraft training centers and connect with existing cooperatives like Bageshwar Copper Craft Association; benchmark with successful players like Copper Art Bageshwar.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw copper sheets from Jamnagar (Gujarat) copper suppliers or Mumbai wholesale markets at ₹400-600/kg; establish relationships with local scrap dealers in Bageshwar for recycled copper; first batch of 50kg copper + tin/zinc alloys costs approximately ₹35,000-45,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with local master artisans in Bageshwar (minimum 3-5 experienced craftsmen) on revenue-sharing (60-40 split typical); set up a small workshop with basic tools (copper vessels, hammers, anvils, furnaces) costing ₹3-8L; alternatively, work with existing SFURTI clusters or cooperative societies that provide shared facilities.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME within 15 days via udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC code from DGFT within 30 days; file Artistic Registration with DC Handicraft (non-GI status requires documentation); create brand identity emphasizing artisan heritage and 'Made in Bageshwar' positioning.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart Handloom within 2 months; build Instagram presence with behind-the-scenes artisan content (post 3x weekly); reach out to 50+ hospitality chains via LinkedIn with catalog and samples; partner with handicraft export houses in Delhi/Mumbai for bulk orders.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 10-15 craftspeople; introduce 5-7 new product SKUs (deepen product lines); launch wholesale program targeting interior designers and e-commerce aggregators; apply for SFURTI cluster development grant to establish shared workshop infrastructure.

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels (2-3 per week) showing live artisan copper beating process with heritage storytelling; use hashtags #BageshwarCopper #ArtisanHeartbeat #IndianHeritage to reach conscious consumers.
  • Launch 'Adopt an Artisan' program where customers fund specific craftspeople's work and receive personalized updates; promote via LinkedIn and Facebook to premium audience segments.
  • Partner with luxury hospitality chains (Taj, ITC, Le Méridien) via direct outreach offering exclusive bespoke copper collections; negotiate placement in 5-10 properties within first year.
