Food Processing- Banana

Kaushambi has a well-established banana cultivation ecosystem across its fertile plains, providing consistent supply of quality raw bananas. The district's agricultural infrastructure, proximity to major trade corridors, and existing food processing units make it ideal for scaling banana-based products. Government support through ODOP and state food processing schemes further strengthen the viability of banana processing businesses here.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kaushambi.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Kaushambi ODOP cluster in Chaudhary Gunj and Shohratgarh areas; interact with existing banana processing units; attend training at the District Food Processing Training Center; study market trends via APEDA and IndiaStacktrade for banana products exports.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh bananas from local cultivators in Kaushambi and neighboring districts at ₹15-25 per kg; bulk orders (5-10 MT) reduce costs to ₹12-18/kg; establish direct relationships with cooperative societies for consistent supply; budget ₹2-3 lakhs for first 2-3 weeks of raw material inventory.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing Common Facility Centers (CFC) in Kaushambi run by District Administration or SFURTI; alternatively, set up a 500-600 sqft processing unit with IQF machines, slicing equipment, and oil fryers; hire 3-5 trained operators; initial production: 200-300 kg/day banana chips; begin with manual processing if capital-constrained.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (SSI) online at udyamregistration.gov.in within 2 days; obtain Food Safety License (FSSAI) for ₹3,000-5,000; apply for IEC from DGFT for export readiness; register trademark with IPO; get organic certification if targeting premium segment (₹30-50K); compliance with Kaushambi District Food Safety Authority.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart, and Jio Mart with initial 100+ kg inventory; approach local retailers and kirana chains in UP towns; pitch to modern trade (Big Bazaar, Reliance Fresh) with samples and compliance docs; join APEDA-registered exporter databases; leverage Instagram, YouTube for D2C awareness with local food blogger tie-ups.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand SKUs (banana powder, banana jaggery, banana stem) with additional machinery; build distribution network across North India (Delhi, Punjab, Rajasthan) via food distributors; apply for PMEGP expansion subsidy for equipment; explore institutional contracts (schools, canteens); launch co-packing for e-commerce brands.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with micro-influencers (50K-500K followers) on Instagram focused on healthy snacks; create 30-second Reels showing farm-to-chip journey in Kaushambi; allocate ₹2-3K monthly.
  • Run Facebook/Google Shopping ads targeting 'healthy snacks' and 'banana chips' keywords in UP, Delhi, NCR with 30% discount for first 100 buyers; allocate ₹5K/month.
  • Sponsor local food festivals and health expos in Allahabad, Varanasi, and Lucknow; distribute free samples; build email list for repeat purchases.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan subsidy (25% for general) for food processing micro-enterprises.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
Common Facility Centers and training support for food processing clusters in Kaushambi.
🏚
UP Laghu Udyog Yojana
5% subsidy on machinery and equipment for registered food processing units in ODOP districts.
🏚
Kaushambi ODOP Special Incentive Scheme
export promotion assistance, brand support, and market linkage for banana products.
