Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Panchkula.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Panchkula Auto Parts Cluster (near Sector 20-26) and study 10+ existing manufacturers. Attend Automotive Component Manufacturers Association (ACMA) meetings in Chandigarh. Study ISO/TS 16949 and AIS (Automotive Industry Standard) requirements. Interview OEM procurement teams at Maruti Suzuki (Manesar), Hero MotoCorp (Gurgaon), and Bajaj suppliers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source steel from Tata Steel, JSW, or local suppliers in Panchkula; aluminum from Hindalco; bearings from SKF distributors in Chandigarh. First batch raw material cost approximately ₹15-25 lakhs. Establish supplier relationships with 3-4 primary vendors for consistent quality and pricing.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing precision machining units in Panchkula or lease 2000-5000 sq ft industrial space in Sector 20/26 with CNC machines, VMC, and testing equipment. Alternatively, begin with contract manufacturing model by partnering with established unit owners for pilot production. Hire experienced production manager familiar with automotive quality protocols.
4
Brand & Register
Register business as manufacturing MSME under Udyam portal; obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Chandigarh for export eligibility. Apply for ISO/TS 16949 certification (cost ₹3-5 lakhs, timeline 6-8 weeks). Register brand name and design with Brand Registry. Obtain EPF/ESIC registration and factory license from District Industrial Office, Panchkula.
5
First Sale
Approach OEM procurement departments directly with product samples, technical drawings, and capability statements. Register on global B2B platforms: Global Trade Net, Tradekey, Alibaba (for exports). Participate in Auto Sector Skill Council events and SIAM (Society of Indian Automobile Manufacturers) exhibitions in Delhi-NCR. Begin with 2-3 anchor customers with ₹10-20L monthly orders.
6
Scale
After securing anchor OEM clients, invest in quality automation and expand CNC capacity. Pursue ISO 9001:2015 and ISO 45001:2018 certifications. Establish export partnership with freight forwarders in Ludhiana/Delhi. Open sales office in Bangalore/Chennai for South India OEM customers. Develop 3-5 proprietary component designs and scale production to ₹5-10 Cr revenue by month 18.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Technical B2B LinkedIn strategy: Post case studies of successful OEM integrations, quality certifications, and production capacity updates. Engage automotive procurement managers with precision engineering insights. Target 500+ relevant accounts monthly.
  • Industry event strategy: Sponsor ACMA conference booth and SIAM Auto Expo with live CNC demonstrations. Collect 200+ qualified leads per event. Follow-up with technical presentations within 7 days.
  • Export promotion via TePP (Trade Extension Promotion Scheme): Attend international auto fairs in Detroit, Frankfurt, Tokyo. Build distributor network in 3-4 countries with margin structure of 15-18% for partners.
