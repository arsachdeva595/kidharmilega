ODOP Product

Stone Work

Chamarajanagara's limestone and granite quarries provide abundant raw material for stone work. The region has a centuries-old tradition of stone carving passed down through generations of artisan families, with skills honed specifically for temple and monument construction that remain in high demand.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Chamarajanagara.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chamarajanagara stone carving clusters in Yalandur and Hanur taluk; connect with Chamarajanagara Handicrafts Association; study competitor work at local temples like Chamundeshwari Temple; attend Surajkund Crafts Mela to understand market demand
2
Source Raw Materials
Source granite and limestone from Chamarajanagara quarries (Yalandur region) at ₹80-150 per sq ft; establish relationships with local stone suppliers; first batch of raw material: ₹60,000-100,000; source chisels and tools from Bangalore's industrial markets
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisan families in Yalandur cluster or hire trained stone carvers; set up workshop in industrial area with ventilation (rent ₹8,000-15,000/month); begin with 2-3 artisans; hire a master sculptor as guide for designs
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME within 10 days online; apply for IEC code from DGFT Bangalore office for export; register trade name with State Registrar; obtain GST registration; design logo incorporating temple motifs; create social media brand presence
5
First Sale
List on Etsy and Indiamart targeting heritage buyers; reach out to temple committees in neighboring districts; partner with heritage hotel chains in Bangalore/Mysore; exhibit at Indian Heritage Society events; contact interior designers through Bangalore design studios; pitch to luxury resort chains
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish online store with 3D sculpture photography; hire 2-3 additional artisans; secure institutional contracts with 2-3 temples; develop export partnerships with Singapore and UAE galleries; launch artisan workshops for heritage tourism; create video content of craft process for social proof
