Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Vidisha.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established farm equipment clusters in Vidisha near Lateri and Basoda areas; spend 2 weeks learning from 3-5 established manufacturers; join Vidisha District Industry Association and attend monthly meetings; study farmer needs through visits to 10+ villages in Raisen and Vidisha blocks
2
Source Raw Materials
Source iron and steel from suppliers in Ujjain or Indore industrial areas (bulk order: ₹150,000-200,000 for 5 MT); rubber and springs from Nagpur suppliers; fasteners from Dewas; negotiate 30-45 day payment terms; first batch material cost approximately ₹200,000-300,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 experienced welders and machinists in Lateri-Basoda cluster on profit-sharing basis initially; establish small workshop (500-800 sq ft) with basic lathe, welding equipment, and hand tools costing ₹300,000-400,000; produce 3-5 high-demand items (seed drills, cultivators) in first 3 months
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME online immediately; obtain IEC code from Bhopal customs; register GST as manufacturer (18% on equipment); create brand name with Vidisha heritage connection; file trademark with IP office; display 'Local Manufacturing' and 'Made in Vidisha' certifications
5
First Sale
Target farmer collectives and input dealers through direct meetings (visit 15-20 dealers in Vidisha, Raisen, Guna districts); list on AgriTech platforms (AgroStar, CropIn); use WhatsApp groups to reach agricultural societies; attend quarterly district farmer meets and exhibitions; offer 3-month payment terms to registered dealers
6
Scale
Expand product range to 8-10 variants based on farmer feedback; hire 2-3 additional skilled workers; establish dealer network across 50 km radius (target 20-25 dealers); invest ₹500,000 in equipment to increase production capacity 3x; explore exports to Nepal through agricultural input traders; develop simple B2B portal; pursue SFURTI cluster development support

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create YouTube channel demonstrating equipment functionality in actual farmer fields; partner with 2-3 respected farmers to create testimonial videos; target agricultural communities through Facebook groups focused on farming techniques
  • Exhibit at Indore Agriculture Fair, Bhopal Agro Expo, and bi-annual Vidisha district farm exhibitions; distribute brochures with farmer testimonials and soil-specific equipment recommendations at these venues
  • Sponsor local farming clubs and agricultural societies; provide free equipment demonstrations in 5-8 villages quarterly; create WhatsApp farmer groups to share tips on equipment maintenance and efficient usage
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh loan at concessional interest rate (0% for SC/ST, 4% for general category) for manufacturing units
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹25 lakh cluster-level support for equipment upgradation and skill development in Vidisha farm equipment cluster
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Agriculture Implement Subsidy Scheme
direct subsidy to farmers (up to 40-50%) for purchase of approved agricultural equipment from registered manufacturers
🏚
MP MSME Loan Subsidy Scheme
interest subsidy of 3-4% on bank loans up to ₹50 lakh for registered manufacturing units in ODOP categories
