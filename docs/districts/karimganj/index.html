Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Karimganj.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Karimganj town market and arecanut plantation areas around Silchar. Connect with existing arecanut farmers and local artisans near Karimganj bazaar. Study areca-based product clusters in Assam (especially Silchar region). Spend 2-3 weeks understanding supply chains, cost structures, and existing competitors.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source arecanut leaf sheaths directly from plantation owners in Karimganj district (₹8-12 per kg). Identify 2-3 reliable suppliers within 50 km radius. Negotiate bulk rates for first batch of 500-1000 kg at ₹10,000-12,000. Connect with local treatment centers or set up basic drying/treatment facility.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 trained artisans in Karimganj (average artisan produces 50-80 pieces/day). Establish a small workshop (500-800 sq ft) with basic tools and drying racks. Start with manual production, focus on quality consistency. Contract manufacturing can begin at ₹2-4 per plate, scaling to ₹1.50-2 at volume.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) at https://udyamregistration.gov.in (free, online). Apply for IEC code from DGFT Guwahati office for export. Register brand name with IP office. Get ISO 22000 or food safety certification for institutional sales. Budget ₹15,000-25,000 for certifications.
5
First Sale
Launch on Udaan and IndiaMART targeting B2B buyers (restaurants, caterers). Open Flipkart seller account and Amazon brand page targeting D2C eco-conscious buyers. Approach 5-10 local restaurants in Silchar/Assam with free samples. Attend food and hospitality trade shows in Northeast India. Target LinkedIn for corporate gifting inquiries.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish dedicated production facility with 10-15 artisans. Expand product line (lunch boxes, serving trays). Launch WhatsApp business and chatbot for bulk B2B orders. Hire distribution partner for pan-India delivery. Apply for SFURTI scheme for cluster-level scaling. Target international markets via Amazon Global and direct B2B partnerships.

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy/loan for manufacturing enterprises with 35-40% margin subsidy for women/SC/ST entrepreneurs.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹50 lakh for cluster-level product improvement, market linkage, and capacity building.
🏚
Assam Startup Scheme
up to ₹10 lakh for registered startups in manufacturing with tax benefits and mentoring support.
🏚
Assam ODOP Promotion Scheme
direct financial support and exhibition access for registered ODOP manufacturers under MSME Ministry.
