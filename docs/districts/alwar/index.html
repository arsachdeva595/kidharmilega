Automobile Parts

Alwar district has a rich deposit of high-quality iron ore, the primary raw material for automobile parts manufacturing. The region hosts a well-established industrial ecosystem with advanced manufacturing units, skilled workforce trained in precision engineering, and proximity to major automotive hubs like Gurugram and Manesar, enabling direct supply to OEMs.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Alwar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Alwar Industrial Area and Bhiwadi RIICO cluster; connect with established manufacturers like SKF, Timken India, and Schaeffler; attend Auto Cluster Development Council meetings; study supply chains at Maruti Suzuki plant in Manesar (40 km away)
2
Source Raw Materials
Source iron ore from local mines in Alwar (₹15,000-20,000 per tonne); steel billets from Jamshedpur or local Alwar suppliers (₹35,000-45,000 per tonne); first batch raw material cost ₹25-35 lakhs for 50-tonne minimum order
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing job-work units in Bhiwadi or Alwar Industrial Estate for CNC machining, heat treatment, and finishing; alternatively, set up mini manufacturing unit with 5-10 CNC machines (₹30-40 lakh investment); hire experienced production manager from nearby automotive clusters
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (MSME online portal); obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Jaipur; get ISO 9001:2015 and TS 16949 certifications (₹2-3 lakh, critical for OEM supply); register brand name with IP cell; set up GST registration
5
First Sale
Approach Maruti, Hero, Bajaj supplier networks directly with ISO certificates; list on automotive B2B platforms like IndiaMART and TradeKey; attend Auto Cluster Trade Fair in Delhi; connect with automotive export houses in Gurugram; target aftermarket through auto parts distributors in Mumbai and Chennai
6
Scale
Secure OEM contracts (highest volume, 6-12 month lead time); invest in advanced CNC machines and automation; open regional sales offices in Mumbai, Bangalore, and Pune; develop export logistics through freight forwarders; build relationships with tier-2 OEMs and component assemblers; achieve annual capacity of 500+ tonnes by month 18
