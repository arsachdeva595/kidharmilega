ODOP Product

Chillies

Tawang's high-altitude terrain (9,600-13,000 ft), cool climate, and rich volcanic soil create ideal conditions for growing exceptionally pungent and flavourful chillies. The region's isolation ensures minimal pesticide use and organic farming practices, making it attractive for premium organic chilli production with superior heat and aroma profiles.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tawang.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tawang agricultural cluster and connect with district agriculture office (Tawang Horticulture Department). Study successful farmers in Tawang town and Jang villages. Research chilli varieties at Arunachal Pradesh Agricultural University, Pasighat. Attend Spice Board of India regional workshops.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified chilli seeds from National Seed Association certified suppliers in Delhi/Bangalore (₹4,000-8,000 per kg for 10-20 kg initial batch). Organic compost available from local farmers at ₹300-400/ton. First-season raw material cost: ₹80,000-120,000 for 0.5-1 hectare pilot.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-10 established Tawang chilli farmers through revenue-sharing model (60% farmer, 40% operator) or lease 0.5-1 hectare directly. Set up small drying infrastructure (solar tunnel or mechanized dryer ₹50,000-100,000). Engage Tawang Horticulture Department for training and certifications.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME online (udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal. Obtain organic certification from NPOP-accredited agencies (APEDA, ICCOA — ₹15,000-25,000 for 1-2 years). File trademark with IP India (₹4,500). Get food license from district health department.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Pantry, BigBasket, Flipkart for retail. Create wholesale network via IndiaMART and TradeKey. Direct B2B outreach to spice companies (MDH, Everest, local brands). Start Instagram/Facebook for D2C with local farmer story angle. Partner with heritage/organic platforms (FreshtoHome, Farmer's Connect).
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand cultivation area to 2-3 hectares. Develop chilli powder/paste products under FSSAI license. Build B2B SaaS relationships with 20+ regional spice manufacturers. Explore export licensing via APEDA for SAARC markets. Launch agritourism with farm visits (₹500-1,000 per person). Invest in cold storage facility (₹2-3 Lakh) by month 12.
