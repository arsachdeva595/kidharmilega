Black Gram

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Narayanpur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Narayanpur district's major agricultural cooperative offices in Narayanpur town; connect with Rajnandgaon IARI research center (60km away) for cultivation best practices; study black gram clusters in Madhya Pradesh (Indore) for comparative market insights
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified black gram seeds from NRCAF (National Research Centre for Agroforestry, Jhansi) or district seed multiplication centers; first batch of 10 quintals costs ₹2.5-3.5 Lakhs from established farmer collectives in Narayanpur; arrange storage at local cold warehouses (₹5000/month per quintal capacity)
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 50-100 certified organic farmer groups in Narayanpur via cooperative unions; ensure organic certification from ECOCERT or Indian Organic Certification Authority; establish grading/processing unit in Narayanpur town (₹40-60L for machinery) or outsource to existing pulses mills in Rajnandgaon
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, 5 mins) under agricultural processing; obtain IEC code from DGFT office in Raipur (30km); apply for Organic certification from state agriculture dept; trademark brand name at Delhi IP office; FSSAI license for packaged products from Narayanpur health authority
5
First Sale
List on BigBasket, Amazon Fresh, and JioMart with ₹50-75k platform fees; approach wholesale buyers at Delhi's Azadpur Mandi via broker networks; initiate direct B2B outreach to ITC, Britannia, Everest Spices procurement teams; register with APEDA for export eligibility
6
Scale
After 6 months, scale farmer network to 300+ across Narayanpur and Dantewada; launch 5-6 SKU variants (whole, split, flour); establish own packaging/co-packing facility (₹1.5Cr investment); build export relationships via trade missions to Middle East and Southeast Asia; secure organic premium certification (Rainforest Alliance, Fair Trade) for 20-30% margin uplift

How to get customers

  • Partner with health/wellness influencers on Instagram and YouTube (10k-100k followers in food/nutrition niches) for authentic farm-to-table content; target cost ₹3-8k per micro-influencer collaboration
  • Launch Facebook/Google ads targeting metro food-conscious women 25-45 with keywords like 'organic pulses', 'high protein dal', 'Chhattisgarh organic' with ₹5-10k/week budget
  • Create B2B content (whitepapers, webinars) on LinkedIn targeting food company procurement managers; attend food trade shows in Mumbai (Anufood India), Delhi, Bangalore with sample kits and certifications
