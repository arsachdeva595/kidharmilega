ODOP Product

Hand Embroidery

Buxar has a centuries-old tradition of fine embroidery work rooted in its historical proximity to Mughal cultural centers. The district's skilled artisan community has preserved traditional techniques passed down through generations, making it a natural hub for intricate hand embroidery on premium fabrics like silk and chiffon.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Buxar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Buxar's main embroidery clusters in Dumraon and Mushabani areas; meet 5-10 master artisans to understand production timelines, costs, and design capabilities; attend textile fairs in Delhi (India Habitat Centre) and Jaipur to see market demand
2
Source Raw Materials
Source silk and cotton from Varanasi fabric markets (₹200-400/meter); embroidery threads from Benaras or online suppliers like Threadneedle India; initial batch of 20-30 sarees/dress pieces requires ₹50,000-80,000 raw material investment
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established artisan groups in Buxar through district cooperative societies; provide design sketches and fabric; negotiate 15-20% commission on finished pieces; start with 50-100 pieces/month production capacity
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online; apply for IEC code from DGFT for export readiness; create brand identity with local heritage narrative; obtain GST registration; trademark brand name at IP Office, Delhi
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram shop with high-quality product photography; list on Etsy (12% commission) and Amazon Handmade; reach out to 20 boutique ethnic wear brands via email with wholesale catalogs; participate in craft fairs in Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi; offer pre-orders for custom designs
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 10-15 makers; invest in branded packaging (₹2-3 per unit); hire social media manager; launch TikTok/Reels content showing artisan process; establish partnerships with 5-10 high-end wedding planners; explore bulk corporate gifting contracts

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 60-second Reels on Instagram/YouTube Shorts showing entire embroidery process from design to finished saree, with artisan testimonials; target hashtags #handembroidery #indiancrafts #slowfashion; post 3x weekly
  • Partner with 10-15 micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in wedding fashion and ethnic wear niches; send samples for unboxing and styling videos; aim for 8-10% affiliate commission on sales
  • Launch email marketing campaign targeting wedding planners, boutique owners, and luxury fashion brands; offer 15-20% bulk discounts for first orders; segment list by geography and send quarterly lookbooks
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
free toolkit, ₹15,000 credit, and certification for registered artisans and small enterprises in handicraft sector
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy/loan for manufacturing of handicrafts with 10-35% subsidy and concessional 5% interest
🏚
Bihar ODOP Promotion Scheme
free training, design support, and ₹50,000-₹2 lakh subsidy for registered ODOP businesses
🏚
Bihar Handicraft Development Scheme
market linkage support, export promotion subsidy (10-15% of FOB value), and artisan skill upgradation training
