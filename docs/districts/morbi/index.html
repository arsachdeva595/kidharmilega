Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Morbi.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Morbi's Ceramic Zone (MIE cluster) and meet 5-10 established manufacturers. Attend ICCW (International Ceramic & Tableware Summit) held in Morbi. Join Gujarat Ceramic Manufacturers Association. Study competitor tile designs on Amazon, Flipkart, and Jaquar/Hindware pricing.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source kaolin clay from local suppliers in Morbi district (avg ₹8,000-12,000/ton), feldspathic materials from Rajasthan suppliers, glazes from established ceramic chemical firms in Morbi (Chemtec, Aarti, Shepherd). First batch (500 pieces tiles + 100 sanitaryware units) will cost ₹3.5-4.5L including material, kiln firing, and labor.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing ceramic production units in Morbi for contract manufacturing (avoid ₹10L+ capital outlay initially). Alternatively, lease a 2,000-3,000 sqft unit in Morbi Ceramic Zone, install small kiln (₹8-12L), and hire 8-12 skilled workers. Start with 2-3 tile designs and 4-5 sanitaryware SKUs.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME (online at udyamregistration.gov.in), apply for IEC code via ICEGATE for export readiness. Trademark brand name at IP India. Obtain BIS certification for ceramic tiles (IS 1237) and sanitaryware (IS 2876). Get GFCI (Gujarat Federation of Ceramic Industries) membership for credibility.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Business, Flipkart B2B, and TradeKey for wholesale. Pitch directly to 20 real estate developers in Gujarat/Mumbai via LinkedIn. List on Jijibii wholesale marketplace. Create product catalog and email 100+ architects and interior designers via Justdial and Sulekha. Start Instagram and YouTube channels showcasing tile installation and durability.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range to 15+ designs. Hire sales team to pitch to tier-1 real estate groups and hospitality chains. Establish direct export relationships with 3-5 importers in USA/UK/Germany via trade shows (COVERINGS USA, CERSAIE Italy). Launch branded retail partnerships with Apollo Architects, Nippon Paint experience centers. Implement ERP for order management.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 10 top interior designers in metros (via Instagram and design blogs) to feature your tiles in 5 completed projects. Offer 15% B2B discount and 30-day payment terms. Generate 50+ case studies.
  • Run Google Shopping Ads and YouTube ads targeting 'ceramic tiles near me' and 'bathroom tiles design' in metros. Budget ₹1.5-2L/month. Target 12-15% CTR by showcasing durability and design.
  • Sponsor tile/construction expos in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore (ACETECH, Infratech). Set up booth showcasing 8-10 trending designs and live demonstrations. Generate 200-300 B2B leads per expo.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general category) for manufacturing units in non-metro areas, covers machinery and working capital.
🏛
MUDRA Scheme (Stand-Up India component)
collateral-free loan up to ₹1Cr for manufacturing MSMEs, 5-7 year tenor, 8-10% interest rate.
🏚
Gujarat Industrial Policy 2020
5% SGST reimbursement for manufacturing units in notified areas, technology upgradation subsidy up to ₹50L.
🏚
GFCI Export Promotion Scheme
4% export rebate on ceramic tiles, free participation in international trade fairs, technical support for international certification.
