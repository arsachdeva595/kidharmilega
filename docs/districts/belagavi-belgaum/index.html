Auto components

Belagavi has established itself as a major automotive manufacturing corridor with over 500+ registered auto component units, a trained workforce, established supply chain infrastructure, and proximity to major automotive clusters in Karnataka and neighboring states. The district's history of precision engineering, coupled with presence of tier-1 and tier-2 suppliers already serving global automakers like Bosch, Rane, and Motherson Sumi, creates an ideal ecosystem for new entrants.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Belagavi (Belgaum).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Belagavi's established auto component clusters in Londa, Soundatti, and Bailhongal. Meet with 5-10 existing manufacturers, visit Belagavi Chamber of Commerce & Industries, and study ISO/TS 16949 quality standards required for automotive supply.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Pune-based suppliers (Kore Industries, Mittal Steel), JSW Steel Bengaluru, and Kalyani Group. First batch of ₹25-40 lakhs for die-casting or machining components; establish credit lines with 30-45 day payment terms.
3
Produce / Partner
Secure 1000-2000 sq.ft. industrial space in Belagavi's KIADB industrial area with power backup. Invest in CNC machines, hydraulic presses, quality testing equipment. Partner with existing job-workers initially (₹8-12 per unit outsourcing cost) while building in-house capacity.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam for MSME status (within 5 days online), obtain IEC code from Belagavi customs office for export eligibility. Register brand with IP India. Obtain ISO/TS 16949:2016 certification (₹1.5-2.5 lakhs, 4-6 months), DGFT registration for exports.
5
First Sale
Reach out to Tier-1 suppliers like Bosch India, Rane, Bharat Seats in Belagavi directly with samples. Register on ThomasNet, Global Sources, and Alibaba for international inquiries. Participate in Bangalore Auto Show (biennial), participate in automotive trade fairs in Mumbai and Delhi.
6
Scale
After first ₹50L revenue, invest in additional machinery and skilled technicians. Expand to niche high-margin segments (EV components, sensor housings). Establish direct relationships with 2-3 major OEM accounts. Consider capacity expansion in adjacent KIADB parks or partner states.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create technical whitepapers on component innovation and ISO/TS certifications; distribute via LinkedIn to procurement teams of top 50 Indian OEMs; target ads to automotive engineers in Karnataka, Maharashtra, Gujarat.
  • Sponsor booth at Auto Tech India 2024 (Delhi) and ASME Auto Summit; conduct live CNC demonstrations; collect 50+ qualified B2B leads per event.
  • Partner with Belagavi Chamber of Commerce to host monthly 'Supplier Connect' webinars; invite tier-1 buyers and position as expert on cost-effective manufacturing solutions.
