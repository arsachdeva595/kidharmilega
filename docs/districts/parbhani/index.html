ODOP Product

Grams (Hara bhara)

Parbhani district has ideal agro-climatic conditions (black soil, 700-800mm rainfall) for gram cultivation. The district produces over 2.5 lakh tonnes of grams annually, making it one of Maharashtra's top gram-growing regions. Well-established supply chains and farmer networks ensure consistent raw material availability at competitive prices.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Parbhani.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Parbhani gram mandi (main agricultural market) and connect with 5+ large traders. Study pricing at Nanded-Parbhani corridor. Attend Maharashtra Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) workshops. Network with FPOs in Parbhani district.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 10-15 MT first batch from licensed APMC traders in Parbhani mandi at ₹5,500-6,500/MT. Budget ₹75-100L for first inventory including transport, cleaning, and packaging. Visit farms in Sonepat, Pathri blocks to negotiate farmer-direct contracts for future batches.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish small processing unit (cleaning, grading, packaging) in Parbhani industrial area or partner with existing dal millers (Shivaji Chakki, Arjun Chakki). Install storage capacity of 50-100 MT with proper moisture control. Obtain Food License (FSSAI) from Parbhani Municipal Corporation.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam portal as small manufacturer. Obtain IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT. Register brand name with Trademark Office. Apply for FSSAI food business license (₹2,000-5,000). Certify for organic if applicable (NPOP certification for export).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart with ₹1,500-2,500/kg pricing. Create WhatsApp bulk order group targeting restaurants in Pune, Mumbai. Approach HOAR (Hotel & Restaurant Association) members. Register with Tradepathmark and Globaltrade for B2B export inquiries. Start with 2-3 key institutional customers (NGOs, schools).
6
Scale
Secure MUDRA loan (₹3-5L) for working capital expansion. Build direct contracts with 20+ farmers for supply consistency. Launch value-added products (gram flour, sprouts). Set up B2B export orders via APEDA-registered trading platforms. Expand to adjacent markets (Aurangabad, Nashik). Hire sales team for institutional sales.
