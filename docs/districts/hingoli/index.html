ODOP Product

Turmeric

Hingoli's agro-climatic conditions, volcanic soil, and monsoon patterns are ideal for high-yield, high-quality turmeric cultivation. The district's 60+ year expertise in turmeric farming and established farmer networks provide direct access to raw materials at competitive rates. Proximity to processing hubs in Aurangabad and existing spice export corridors reduce logistics costs.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Hingoli.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Hingoli's Selam turmeric cluster in Basmath and Aundha Nagnath talukas; meet 5-10 established farmers; study farm costs at Maharashtra Turmeric Farmers Cooperative; attend Hingoli Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) auctions to understand pricing cycles.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh turmeric directly from APMC Hingoli or farmer cooperative networks at ₹4,500-5,500/quintal; budget ₹2-3 lakhs for 40-50 quintals first batch; establish relationships with 2-3 reliable farmers for consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered turmeric processing units in Aurangabad (45km away) for drying, powdering, and packaging via job work; alternately, lease 5,000-10,000 sq ft space in Aurangabad agro-processing zone and import processing machinery (₹15-25 lakhs) with 2-3 technicians.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in); obtain IEC from nearest DGFT office (Aurangabad); file FSSAI registration for food processing; register brand with Aurangabad District Industries Center; apply for organic certification via APEDA if targeting premium segment.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart, BigBasket with minimum 50kg inventory; list on IndiaMART for B2B buyers; approach HoReCa chains (Mumbai, Pune regional distributors); pitch to 10-15 food manufacturers in Maharashtra and Gujarat.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish own processing unit if job-working proves successful; launch organic/curcumin-enriched variants; hire 1-2 salespeople for institutional sales; build export relationships via APEDA and SEZs in Aurangabad/Nashik.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with wellness micro-influencers on Instagram & YouTube (50K-200K followers) focused on Ayurveda and organic health; target monthly budget ₹30K for 8-10 collaborations.
  • Launch geo-targeted Facebook ads in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) highlighting 'Farm-to-Table Hingoli Turmeric' with farmer testimonials; weekly budget ₹15K-20K.
  • Attend 3-4 major spice expos annually (Spice India Expo, Gulfood) and B2B food trade shows in Mumbai/Pune; budget ₹2-3 lakhs for booth and travel per event.
