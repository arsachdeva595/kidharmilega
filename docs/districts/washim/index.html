ODOP Product

Soya bean and soya bean products

Washim has established soybean cultivation infrastructure across 80,000+ hectares with favorable agro-climatic conditions for crop production. The district's agricultural supply chains, cooperative societies, and proximity to food processing clusters in Vidarbha region make it ideal for scaling soy-based product manufacturing. Government support through ODOP and existing food processing units provide both raw material security and technical expertise.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Washim.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Washim Agricultural Produce Market Committee (APMC) to understand soybean supply dynamics; connect with existing soy processors in Akola and Amravati clusters; attend food processing expos in Nagpur and Pune.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source soybeans directly from farmers at Washim APMC at ₹4,500-5,500/quintal (minimum 500 kg for first batch, ₹225,000-275,000); identify local cooperative societies like Washim District Cooperative Union for bulk supply agreements.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with government-recognized food processing centers in Washim district or lease small manufacturing space near Malegaon with water supply and power backup; hire experienced soy processor or take 2-week training from ATMA (Agricultural Technology Management Agency) Washim.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam at udyamregistration.gov.in (10 minutes); apply for IEC code via ezeichen.gov.in; obtain Food Safety License from FSSAI (portal.fssai.gov.in) costing ₹5,000-10,000; file trademark with IP India for brand name.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and Blinkit with ₹50-100/unit margin; pitch to modern retailers (Spencer's, Nature's Basket, Reliance Fresh) with 10-12 units MOQ; create WhatsApp distribution network with local kirana shops in Akola, Amravati, and Washim towns.
6
Scale
Invest in cold storage and packaging machinery (₹10-15 L) after breakeven; expand to adjacent districts via distributor model; develop 3-5 new SKUs (soy protein isolate, flavored soy milk); target institutional canteens in Nagpur and Pune with bulk contracts.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Run Instagram Reels showing farm-to-table soybean journey; partner with 10-15 micro-influencers (50k-200k followers) in health and vegetarian niches for ₹1,000-3,000 per post.
  • Create content on YouTube educating about soy nutrition benefits; target keywords like 'plant-based protein India' and 'soy milk health benefits' to drive organic traffic to e-commerce links.
  • Launch sampling campaigns at health expos in Pune and Nagpur; sponsor vegetarian food festivals in Maharashtra; distribute at Ayurvedic clinics and yoga centers with 15% margin for retailers.
