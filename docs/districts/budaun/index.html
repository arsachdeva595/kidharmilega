ODOP Product

Zari and Zardozi

Budaun has a 400+ year legacy of zari and zardozi craftsmanship with thousands of skilled artisans concentrated in clusters like Ahar Mandi. The district's cultural heritage, affordable skilled labor, and established supply chains for metallic threads make it the natural hub for this intricate embroidery. Access to raw materials and existing B2B networks with wedding wear and ethnic fashion brands amplify production efficiency.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Budaun.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ahar Mandi and Naya Bans clusters in Budaun; spend 2-3 weeks observing master artisans; attend workshops at Government Institute of Embroidery, Budaun; connect with Budaun Zari Zardozi Association to understand pricing, design trends, and supplier networks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source zari thread from established vendors in Ahar Mandi (₹200-500/kg); import quality silk from Varanasi suppliers (₹300-400/kg); procure base fabrics from Delhi textile markets or Surat; first batch budget: ₹30,000-50,000 for materials to produce 10-15 finished pieces.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 skilled artisans in Budaun clusters on piece-rate basis (₹500-2000 per finished item depending on complexity); provide design templates and quality briefs; establish a small 500-800 sqft workspace for design finalization and quality check; begin with 20-30 pieces/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online (udyamregistration.gov.in); obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Lucknow for export eligibility; register business under GST; create brand identity with Hindi-English bilingual packaging; apply for DC Handicraft certification to strengthen ODOP positioning.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Amazon Handmade targeting diaspora buyers; set up Instagram shop with 3-4 reel posts per week showing artisan process; approach 10-15 ethnic wear brands (Anita Dongre, Sabyasachi stockists, local lehenga designers) with wholesale catalogs; list on LocalCircles and Justdial for local high-value orders; initial target: 5-10 bulk orders/month.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire a logistics partner for fulfillment; expand artisan network to 10-15 workers; launch seasonal collections aligned with wedding and festival calendars; open B2B partnerships with 5-10 international wholesale platforms; attend 2-3 trade shows (Delhi Craft Bazaar, Textile Mela); invest ₹50,000 in Facebook/Instagram paid ads targeting high-net-worth individuals and brand collaborations.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create behind-the-scenes Instagram Reels showing artisan hands crafting zardozi; tag micro-influencers in bridal fashion (50k-200k followers) and offer 2-3 free pieces for review; target hashtags #ZardoziArt #BridalEmbroidery #ArtisanIndia.
  • Partner with 5-10 wedding designers for co-branded collections; offer them 40% wholesale discount and social media credit; encourage them to feature your zardozi in their lookbooks and wedding shoots.
  • Run seasonal campaigns on Google Shopping and Facebook Ads during wedding season (Oct-Feb) targeting 25-50 age group, HHI >₹15L, interested in ethnic wear; allocate ₹5,000-10,000/month budget with focus on custom zardozi inquiries.
