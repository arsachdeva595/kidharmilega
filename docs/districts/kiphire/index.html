Kholar

Kiphire's high-altitude terrain (1,500–2,000m), cool climate, and naturally fertile volcanic soil create ideal conditions for kidney bean cultivation. The district's isolated geography and traditional farming practices ensure zero pesticide contamination, producing beans with superior flavor and texture that command premium prices in both domestic and export markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kiphire.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kiphire district's farming clusters in villages like Thenuopilou and Sero; interview 5-10 traditional farmers; study bean cultivation at Nagaland Agricultural Department (Kohima). Audit existing supply chains to Guwahati and Dibrugarh markets. Learn seasonal calendar and yield expectations (750kg–1.5T per acre).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Kiphire heirloom kidney bean seeds directly from farmer collectives in Kiphire town (₹60–80/kg, bulk 100kg = ₹6,000–8,000). Establish relationships with 3-5 progressive farmers willing to supply 2–5T for first season. First-batch sourcing cost: ₹1.2–1.8L for 2T.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing farmer groups in Kiphire (Kiphire Farmers Cooperative, Women's Self-Help Groups) for aggregation. Alternatively, lease 2–3 acres of farmland in Kiphire district, hire local agricultural laborer (₹300–400/day), follow organic protocol. First harvest in 4–5 months. Arrange drying, sorting, and packaging at community center or small processing unit in Kiphire town.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) as agricultural produce trader. Obtain IEC code from Nagaland's DGFT office (Kohima) if exporting. Get organic certification via IFOAM-accredited body (e.g., IMO, Naturland). Design brand (label, packaging with Kiphire origin story) and register trademark with IP India. Set up food license via FSSAI.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and Blinkit with ₹500–1000/kg premium pricing. Approach regional chains (Healthy Basket, Organic Stores in Delhi/Bangalore). Contact food importers in Singapore, Nepal via TradeKey. Host 2–3 tasting events at premium grocery stores in NCR. Build email list and Instagram presence (@KiolarKidneyBeans). Negotiate ₹800–1200/kg ex-farm wholesale rate with Guwahati traders for bulk off-take.
6
Scale
After 6 months and ₹30–50L revenue, expand farmer-producer network from 5 to 20 farmers. Invest in small-scale processing unit (sorting, drying, packaging) in Kiphire town (₹50–80L). Launch value-added products (roasted snacks, bean flour). Apply for SFURTI cluster development grant (₹50L max) to build processing hub. Establish export channel via freight forwarder or export house for SAARC markets. Target ₹3–5Cr annual revenue by Year 2.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with wellness influencers (100K–500K followers on Instagram) to feature Kholar in plant-based/protein-rich meal prep content. Offer samples + ₹2000 creator fee. Target hashtags: #PlantBasedProtein #OrganicIndia #HealthyEating.
  • Launch referral program on BigBasket/Amazon: ₹100 discount for buyer + ₹150 credit for referrer. Run geo-targeted Google Ads in Delhi-NCR, Bangalore, Mumbai targeting 'organic kidney beans' and 'premium pulses' (₹15–20 CPC budget: ₹30K/month).
  • Create YouTube short-form content (reels, shorts) showing traditional Kiphire farming, sorting, and recipes. Partner with regional food bloggers (Assam/Nagaland). Run TikTok/Instagram ads targeting health-conscious 25–45 age group, budget ₹20K/month.
