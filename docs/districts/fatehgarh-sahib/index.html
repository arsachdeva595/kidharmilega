ODOP Product

Forged Items

Fatehgarh Sahib has an unbroken 300+ year legacy of metalworking and blacksmithing, with hundreds of established forges and artisans concentrated in clusters like Sirhind and Amarkot. The district's proximity to raw material suppliers in northern India, skilled generational craftsmen, and established export networks make it uniquely suited for scaling forged item production. Strong local supply chains for steel, alloys, and finishing materials reduce sourcing costs and production timelines.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Fatehgarh Sahib.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Sirhind-Amarkot forging clusters in Fatehgarh Sahib; spend 2-3 days observing production processes, visiting 10+ established forges, meeting artisan associations. Interview existing manufacturers about quality standards, cost structures, and export networks. Contact the Fatehgarh Sahib Chamber of Commerce and District Administration's MSME cell for baseline data.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source first batch of raw materials (steel, alloys) from established suppliers in Ludhiana or Delhi-NCR (₹1.5-2.5L for initial 5-10 ton inventory). Partner with local artisan clusters to source finished or semi-finished forgings initially. Connect with timber suppliers for handles (₹30-50K per batch).
3
Produce / Partner
Rather than setting up own forge initially, partner with 2-3 established micro-forges in Sirhind cluster for outsourced production on retainer model (minimum order 500-1000 units monthly). Negotiate quality agreements and exclusivity clauses. Alternatively, hire experienced master craftsmen and set up a small 500 sq ft workshop with basic forging equipment (furnace, anvil, hammers, finishing tools).
4
Brand & Register
Register as micro-enterprise under Udyam (online, 15 mins, ₹0). Apply for IEC code from customs authority (₹500-1000). Register trademark for brand name with IP India (₹4500-9000). Obtain ISO 9001 certification or AGMARK if selling agricultural tools (₹30-50K). Open DGFT account for exports.
5
First Sale
Launch on IndiaMART, TradeKey, and Global Trade Atlas for B2B wholesale; target bulk orders from hardware retailers in Punjab, Haryana, UP. List on Amazon and flipkart for D2C. Create WhatsApp catalog and direct contact agricultural equipment associations. Attend trade fairs like Delhi Hardware Show, Mumbai Design Fair. Send product samples to 50+ overseas distributors identified via Google Trade Directories.
6
Scale
After 6 months of ₹10-15L monthly revenue, invest ₹15-25L in own 1000 sq ft production facility with basic automation. Hire 5-8 skilled workers. Launch branded product line with premium packaging for D2C. Establish partnerships with 5+ domestic distributors. Target 30% export revenue within 18 months by leveraging existing B2B networks.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 15-20 YouTube shorts (30-60 sec) showing behind-the-scenes forging process, artisan interviews, before-after tool usage. Target DIY enthusiasts, farmers, and contractors; optimize for 'best agricultural tools' and 'hand forged items' keywords.
  • Partner with 5-10 micro-influencers in farming/agriculture niche on Instagram (50K-200K followers) for product seeding and unboxing content; offer 20% commission on referred sales. Target farmer communities and sustainability-focused audiences.
  • Launch B2B direct outreach campaign via LinkedIn targeting hardware retailers, distributors, and procurement managers; offer 15-18% wholesale margins and MOQ-based tiering. Share case studies of successful retailers in Haryana and UP.
