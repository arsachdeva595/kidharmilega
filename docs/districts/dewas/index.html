ODOP Product

Bamboo

Dewas district has rich bamboo forests and a strong tradition of bamboo handicraft production passed down through generations. The region's favorable climate, proximity to raw bamboo sources in Vindhya forests, and established artisan communities make it an ideal hub for bamboo product manufacturing. The district's lower labor costs and infrastructure support make scaling bamboo enterprises economically viable.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Dewas.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dewas bamboo clusters in Tonk Khurd and Kannod villages; connect with existing artisan groups under SFURTI scheme; study competitors on Etsy, Indieweave, and Amazon Handmade; attend Craft Mela exhibitions in Indore.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw bamboo from Dewas forest cooperatives (₹15-25/kg) and sustainable suppliers like Bamboo India Foundation; negotiate first batch of 500 kg culms at ₹12,000-15,000; establish supply agreement with 2-3 local suppliers for continuity.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-8 established artisans in Dewas district (₹300-500 per piece labor); formalize through Shramik Suvidha card registration; set up small production hub (500 sq ft) or operate on distributed artisan model; invest in basic tools like splitting chisels, sanders, and finishing supplies.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise) online; apply for IEC code through DGFT portal for export readiness; register trademark on IPO website; obtain Madhya Pradesh handicraft certification and GST registration; create FSSAI compliance if producing food-contact items.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Instagram Shop with high-quality product photography; approach fair-trade platforms (Global Goods Partners, Trades of Hope); pitch to boutique home décor chains in metro cities; secure first corporate order through LinkedIn outreach to CSR teams.
6
Scale
Expand artisan network to 20-30 craftspeople; invest in product design innovation (contemporary bamboo furniture lines); build B2B relationships with 50+ retailers; establish export partnerships with Germany and Netherlands; apply for PMEGP subsidy (₹5-10L) for scaling production capacity and marketing.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Launch Instagram reels showing bamboo harvesting → crafting → final product journey; partner with eco-influencers (50K-200K followers) in sustainable living niche for product features and coupon codes.
  • Create content on sustainable home décor trends and bamboo's carbon footprint benefits; publish on Medium and LinkedIn; run Google Shopping ads targeting 'eco-friendly furniture India' and 'sustainable home décor'.
  • Partner with corporate sustainability officers; offer bulk customized bamboo gifting with brand engraving; exhibit at India Exim Fair and National Handicraft Fair in Delhi; run email campaigns to interior design studios and hospitality businesses.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% for women, 25% for general) for micro-enterprise setup in handicraft sector.
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 stipend + interest-free loans up to ₹3 lakh for artisans; free skill training and toolkits for traditional craftspeople.
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Laghu Udyog Vikas Nidhi
₹10 lakh concessional loans at 4% interest for handicraft MSMEs; easy credit without collateral.
🏚
MP Handicraft Cluster Development Scheme
infrastructure support, common facility centers, and marketing subsidies for artisans in notified clusters.
