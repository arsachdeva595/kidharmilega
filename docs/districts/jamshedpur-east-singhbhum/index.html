ODOP Product

Paithkar Painting

East Singhbhum has a centuries-old tradition of folk painting among tribal and local communities. The district's natural abundance of plant-based dyes—indigo, turmeric, henna, and mineral pigments—combined with a skilled artisan base makes it the natural home for Paithkar painting. The cultural continuity and proximity to craft clusters ensures access to trained practitioners.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Jamshedpur (East Singhbhum).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Paithkar artisan clusters in Jamshedpur (Adityapur, Sonari, Gamharia areas) and connect with DC Handicraft office. Study existing practitioners, document techniques, visit Jharkhand State Museum. Attend craft fairs at Jamshedpur Craft Fair (annual) and Delhi Craft Week.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source natural dyes from local herbalists in Jamshedpur and Ranchi (approx. ₹15,000-25,000 for starter kit). Partner with handmade paper makers in West Bengal or source from Khadi Gramodyog shops. Budget ₹50,000-80,000 for first batch of materials including brushes, canvas, and binders.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish 2-3 partnerships with registered Paithkar artisans through DC Handicraft introductions. Formalize through artisan agreements. Begin with 50-100 pieces monthly. Set up a small studio space (200-300 sq ft) at ₹8,000-12,000/month in Adityapur or similar artisan hub. Implement quality control and documentation systems.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSE (online, free at udyamregistration.gov.in). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (₹0, online). Register brand name with IP India for trademark protection (₹9,000-15,000). Obtain Handicraft Artisan Certificate from DC office. Create GI origin documentation for future GI tagging.
5
First Sale
List on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart Handloom (0% commission initially). Build Instagram account showcasing artisan stories and process videos. Approach interior designers and home décor chains (Fabindia, Good Earth, Chumbak). Submit to heritage art platforms (iWeaves, Crafts Council India). Target first sales via Instagram DMs and WhatsApp to warm audience (₹8,000-15,000 per piece).
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 5-10 practitioners. Launch branded gift boxes and smaller format prints (₹500-2,000). Open pop-up stores at malls in Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi. Partner with luxury hotels and resorts for commissioned installations. Launch online subscription box (quarterly curated pieces). Invest in professional photography and video marketing.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 60-second process videos showing dye extraction and painting on Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts. Partner with eco-lifestyle influencers (200K-500K followers) for unboxing and art room styling content.
  • Host monthly virtual artisan talk sessions on Instagram Live featuring the master painters. Offer exclusive discounts (15% off) to live attendees to drive sales.
  • Collaborate with interior design studios and architects in Tier-1 cities via LinkedIn outreach. Offer 20% B2B margins and custom catalog designs. Feature their installations as case studies.
