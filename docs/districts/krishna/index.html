ODOP Product

Machilipatnam Kalamkari

Krishna district is the historic birthplace of Machilipatnam Kalamkari, where artisan communities have practiced this craft for 400+ years. The district's access to cotton, natural dyes, and a skilled artisan base makes it the only authentic source. The proximity to export hubs and established trade networks in Machilipatnam town further strengthen this monopoly.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Krishna.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Machilipatnam town (30 km from Krishna HQ) and spend 2–4 weeks with established Kalamkari artisans in the old city (near Machilipatnam Fort area). Study design libraries at Handloom Export Promotion Council office. Join APLI (Andhra Pradesh Leather Industries) or local artisan co-operatives to understand market gaps and pricing.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton from Guntur cotton mills (₹80–120/meter for quality base fabric). Procure natural dyes from Indigo suppliers in Warangal or import madder from Tamil Nadu suppliers. First batch of 500 meters + dyes: ₹60,000–80,000. Connect with local dye-makers in Machilipatnam for bulk orders at ₹40/meter.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3–5 master artisans in Machilipatnam through local co-operatives (Adarsha Weavers' Co-operative, Machilipatnam Kalamkari Artisans Guild). Outsource design documentation and block-carving to specialist workshops. Set up a quality-check and finishing unit locally. Expected production: 50–100 sarees/month at ₹1,200–2,500 per piece.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (online, free). Apply for IEC code via e-Biz portal for export eligibility. Trademark brand name with IP India. Get GI-compliant certification from APLI or DC Handloom office to authenticate Machilipatnam origin. Obtain Handloom Mark certification.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C via Shopify or WooCommerce (initial cost ₹15,000/month). List on Etsy (10% commission, high international traffic). Pitch to Fabindia, Anokhi, and heritage boutiques via email + product samples. Attend Handloom Expo 2024 (Delhi, Mumbai) for bulk buyer meetings. Instagram Reels showing artisan process (5–10 posts weekly).
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire a design team (₹80,000–120,000/month for 2 designers). Expand to 10–15 artisans. Launch B2B panel for interior designers on own platform. Pitch to luxury hospitality chains for commissioned collections. Set up a micro-workshop in Machilipatnam for heritage tourism experiences (₹50,000 setup, ₹5,000–8,000/person). Explore sustainable dye patents and scale exports to 5+ countries.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels series 'Artisan Stories' — 60-second clips of master artisans hand-painting motifs with trending audio. Post 3x weekly. Use hashtags #MaclilipatnamKalamkari #HandpaintedIndia #SlowFashion.
  • Partner with 5–10 micro-influencers in sustainable fashion & home décor (10K–100K followers) for unboxing + styling videos. Offer 20% commission on sales. Target US & EU audiences.
  • Content marketing: publish blog posts on Medium & LinkedIn on '400 Years of Kalamkari,' 'Natural Dyes Science,' 'How to Care for Hand-painted Textiles.' Drive SEO traffic and build brand authority.
