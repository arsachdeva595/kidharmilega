Handicrafts - Tribal

Churachandpur is the cultural heartland of Manipur's tribal communities, home to skilled Kuki, Chin, and Meitei artisans with generations of expertise in handloom weaving, bamboo work, and tribal embroidery. The district's remote terrain and tight-knit craft communities have preserved authentic traditional techniques that command premium prices in both domestic and export markets. Local availability of natural materials like bamboo, cotton, and dyes from forest sources makes production cost-efficient.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Churachandpur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Churachandpur's main craft clusters in Lamka town, Singat, and Tuivai villages. Connect with Manipur Handloom Development Corporation (MHDC) and local artisan cooperatives. Study competitors on Etsy, Jaypore, and Nativeplanet. Spend 2-3 weeks immersed in 2-3 artisan families to understand production timelines and capability.
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner directly with MHDC registered suppliers or village cooperatives for yarn (₹15-25/kg for quality cotton), natural dyes from forest traders in Imphal (₹8-12/kg), and wood from registered timber merchants. First batch of 50 units: ₹80,000-120,000. Source samples from 3-5 artisans before bulk commitment.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish direct relationships with 5-10 core artisans through documented partnership agreements specifying quality, timeline, and fair wages (₹400-800/day). Conduct monthly quality audits. Consider forming a Small Producer Group (SPG) under DC Handicraft ministry for collective scaling and subsidy eligibility.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification takes 10 minutes online). Apply for IEC from APEDA for export readiness. Secure trademark for brand name via IP India. Obtain Handicraft Certification from DC Handicraft, Manipur. Create brand story document linking tribal heritage, artisan names, and sustainable practices.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy with professional product photography (₹8,000-15,000) highlighting artisan stories. Simultaneously pitch to Jaypore, Nativeplanet, and Godrej Interio's artisan partner program. Reach out to 20-30 interior design studios in Mumbai, Delhi, Bangalore with sample kits and 20% designer discounts. Launch Instagram account with behind-the-scenes artisan content.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 25-50 skilled workers through SPG formation. Invest ₹3-5L in professional photography studio and packaging upgrade. Target bulk corporate gifting (Diwali/wedding season). Explore fair-trade certifications (World Fair Trade Organization, CRAFT India). Open a pop-up store in Imphal or Delhi NCR during peak season.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels campaign featuring 60-second artisan studio videos (production, dyeing, weaving process). Tag lifestyle influencers (100K-500K followers) focused on ethical fashion and home décor. Budget: ₹25,000/month for micro-influencer partnerships.
  • Host virtual artisan-to-consumer live sessions on Instagram/Facebook weekly, allowing customers to meet makers, ask questions, and receive 10% instant discounts. Drive to email list for repeat sales.
  • Partner with 5-10 high-traffic boutique hotels and resorts across Northeast India to display products in lobby retail sections with 40% wholesale discount. Include product cards with QR codes linking to your e-commerce site.
