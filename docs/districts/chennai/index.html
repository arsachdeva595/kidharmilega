Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Chennai.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sriperumbudur industrial cluster (40km from Chennai city), tour factories in SIPCOT and TIDEL parks, attend NASSCOM manufacturing events, connect with FADA and SIAM auto associations to understand OEM requirements and supply chain dynamics.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Madras Industrial Park suppliers, Tamil Nadu Steel suppliers in Perambur, and industrial distributors in Ranipet. First-batch raw material cost approximately ₹8-15 lakhs for a small production run; establish bulk supplier relationships to reduce unit costs by 15-20%.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing job-work facilities in Sriperumbudur or lease space in SIPCOT industrial park (₹500-1500/sqft/month), or collaborate with established manufacturers for contract manufacturing at 12-18% overhead. Hire experienced production engineers (₹2.5-4.5L annually) and invest in CNC/VMC machinery (₹15-30L for starter setup).
4
Brand & Register
Register with Udyam MSME portal immediately, apply for IEC code through DGFT online portal, obtain ISO 9001:2015 certification (essential for auto suppliers), register company under GST, and establish brand presence as B2B supplier with technical product documentation and quality certifications.
5
First Sale
Identify target OEM via SIAM database and FADA, pitch prototype batches directly to procurement teams, leverage IndiaStack B2B platform for supplier listing, participate in AutoTech India exhibitions, use email outreach to Tier-1 suppliers with quality certifications, and register on TradingIndia and Global Trade.com for export inquiries.
6
Scale
After first ₹50L revenue, invest in advanced CNC machinery and automation, hire quality control staff, pursue ISO 16949 TS certification (automotive-specific), establish separate export logistics (partner with freight forwarders in Chennai port), and develop product variants based on OEM feedback to increase order sizes.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create technical case studies showcasing defect-free batches delivered to OEM clients, publish on LinkedIn and email to procurement teams with ROI metrics on quality improvements.
  • Sponsor booth at AutoTech India and SEA Auto Salon to showcase prototypes, collect qualified leads, and demonstrate manufacturing capabilities in real-time to buyer delegations.
  • Partner with industry consultants to pitch capabilities to Tier-1 suppliers via structured supplier development programs and ISO certifications as proof of capability.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP
Up to ₹25L subsidy (25% of project cost) for manufacturing units with loan component from banks, suitable for setting up auto component facility.
🏛
MUDRA Yojana
Up to ₹10L unsecured business loans for working capital and machinery purchase for micro and small manufacturing enterprises.
🏚
Tamil Nadu MSME Advancement Society (TNSMEADO)
Offers 15% subsidy on fixed assets, 10% on technology upgrades, and export promotion support up to ₹5L annually.
🏚
Tamil Nadu SIPCOT Industrial Parks Incentive
Provides land lease discounts, utility rebates, and 5-year property tax holidays for manufacturing units in approved industrial parks.
