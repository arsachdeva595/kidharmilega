ODOP Product

Coriander

Neemuch's semi-arid climate, black soil composition, and altitude of 600-800m create ideal conditions for high-yield, aromatic coriander cultivation. The district has a 50+ year legacy of spice farming with established farmer networks and supply chains. Cool winters and moderate rainfall produce coriander with superior flavor profiles compared to other regions.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Neemuch.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Neemuch district spice markets (Manasa, Jaora mandis), connect with 3-5 leading coriander farmers and exporters. Attend APEDA-listed spice export clusters. Study quality grading standards at Indian Spice Association. Budget: ₹15,000-25,000 for 1-week field research.
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner directly with 5-10 registered farmers in Neemuch for contracted supply of 50 MT first batch. Source at ₹5,500-6,500 per quintal (wholesale). Procure food-grade jute/HDPE bags from Indore suppliers (₹2-3/kg). First batch sourcing cost: ₹3-3.5 lakhs for 50 MT + packaging.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish a small processing unit (cleaning, grading, packaging) within Neemuch or Manasa with capacity 10-15 MT/month. Partner with SFURTI cluster units for toll processing initially. Invest in seed cleaning machines (winnower, grader) costing ₹1.5-2 lakhs. Hire 2-3 skilled workers for quality control.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise) and obtain IEC code from Neemuch APEDA office for exports. Get FSSAI registration (₹5,000-10,000). Pursue Organic certification (APEDA/IFOAM) if targeting premium segment (₹25,000-50,000). File trademark for brand name at IP office.
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket, Amazon Fresh, and Blinkit within Month 2 (B2C). Simultaneously pitch to 10-15 food manufacturers and exporters via LinkedIn/email. Attend IEFT (Indian Export Forum Trade) shows. Register with APEDA-approved exporters list. Target first export order by Month 4-5.
6
Scale
After ₹5 Cr revenue, invest in cold storage and cleaning infrastructure (₹50-75 lakhs). Develop organic/premium variants. Expand farmer partnerships to 50+ growers. Launch branded retail presence in top 10 metro supermarkets. Set up export logistics through dedicated freight partners. Hire category manager for B2B market development.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 5-10 micro-influencers in food/cooking vertical on Instagram/YouTube (50K-200K followers) for organic recipe content and product unboxing. Cost: ₹20,000-50,000/month. Focus on story-telling around Neemuch farmer partnerships.
  • Execute B2B demand generation campaign on LinkedIn targeting food manufacturing decision-makers. Develop case study 'How Brand X increased spice potency with Neemuch Coriander'. Invest ₹30,000-40,000/month in paid ads.
  • Launch 'Farm-to-Kitchen' storytelling campaign using TikTok/Instagram Reels showing farmer harvesting → processing → packaging. Partner with 2-3 local farmers for content. Run UGC contests. Budget: ₹10,000-15,000/month.
