Shawl-Kullu

Kullu's high-altitude climate and centuries-old weaving tradition, combined with locally available wool from Himalayan sheep, make it the authentic epicenter of shawl production. The district's skilled artisan community has perfected unique design motifs passed down through generations, creating an unmatched competitive advantage in the handloom segment.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kullu.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kullu town and Himachal Pradesh Handloom Board office; spend time in weaver colonies in Bhuntar and Sultanpur to understand production timelines (4-8 weeks per shawl), quality grading, and cost structures. Connect with SEWA Himachal and local artisan co-operatives.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium wool from wool suppliers in Kangra Valley (₹200-400/kg for local wool) and merino from Delhi wool markets. Establish relationships with natural dye suppliers in Kullu or nearby towns. First batch of materials for 50 shawls: ₹80,000-120,000. Consider partnering with Himachal Pradesh Handloom Development Corporation for bulk procurement discounts.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 registered master weavers in Kullu clusters (Bhuntar, Sultanpur) on commission basis (₹800-1,500 per shawl depending on complexity). Alternative: Establish your own 2-loom unit (₹50,000 per loom) and hire trained weavers at ₹8,000-12,000/month. Visit Himachal Pradesh Handloom Board for weaver registries and incentive schemes.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam through udyamregistration.gov.in (free, online); obtain IEC code from APEDA for export eligibility; register GI certificate holder status with DC Handloom, Kullu; trademark your brand name at ipindia.gov.in (₹4,500-9,000); obtain GST registration (optional until ₹20L turnover but recommended).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade, Flipkart Sellers, and Meesho with high-resolution photos and heritage storytelling; reach out to 20 boutique hotels in Manali for B2B orders; use Instagram Reels & Pinterest to showcase weaver stories; participate in Craft Mela at Himachal Pradesh Tourism Development Corporation; list on Etsy with international shipping to tap UK, US, Germany markets.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 5-8 weaver partnerships; launch a signature collection with contemporary designs; establish presence in 50+ heritage/lifestyle retail stores pan-India; target corporate gifting with 10% annual growth; explore international exhibitions (Handmade India @ trade shows); build email list of 500+ tourists and develop repeat purchase program.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showing 60-second weaver profiles from partner artisans in Bhuntar — humanize the product, drive emotional connection, use hashtags #KulluShawlStory #HandwovenlnIndia #HeritageFashion. Target 18-45 year old urban women and international tourists.
