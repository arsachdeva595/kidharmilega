Berhampur Patta (Phoda Kumbha) Saree

Boudh has a deeply rooted weaving tradition spanning centuries with skilled artisans trained in traditional loom techniques. The district's socio-cultural fabric is intertwined with textile production, making it the natural hub for authentic handloom patta sarees. Local availability of skilled weavers and established supply chains for raw materials make it ideal for scaling production.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Boudh.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Boudh district handloom clusters around Baunsura and Boudh town; interact with established weavers and trader associations; study pricing at Odisha State Handloom Emporium (Bhubaneswar); attend ICCW (Indian Chamber of Commerce & Weaving) workshops.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium cotton (40s-60s) and silk yarn from wholesale suppliers in Surat (₹8,000-12,000/10kg) or Erode; natural dyes from specialized suppliers in Bangalore/Chennai (₹2,000-5,000/batch); first batch raw material cost approximately ₹50,000-80,000 for 5-10 sarees.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish direct partnership with 3-5 master weavers in Boudh (minimum order 2-3 sarees/month at ₹3,000-5,000/saree production cost); or setup own 2-3 looms with trained operators at workshop space (rent ₹8,000-15,000/month in Boudh town).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro classification: udyamregistration.gov.in); apply for IEC code via DGFT; register brand name and design copyrights at IP Office India; obtain GI certification application (even though currently no GI tag) to strengthen authenticity claims.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade and Meesho in Month 1 with 5-8 SKUs; approach 15-20 saree boutiques in metro cities (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) with wholesale pricing; run Instagram/Facebook content marketing targeting saree-buying community; partner with 2-3 niche e-commerce platforms (StoryLoom, Craftberry, Vastra).
6
Scale
After 6 months and ₹25L revenue: expand to 8-10 weaver partnerships; launch own D2C website with SEO; secure wholesale space in regional textile hubs (Chandni Chowk Delhi, Tunis Mumbai); apply for export orders via Rajasthan/Gujarat trade associations; scale online ads spend to ₹30,000/month.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 60-second Instagram Reels showcasing entire weaving process from yarn to finished saree, posted 3x/week to build craft credibility and target saree enthusiast communities.
  • Partner with 10-15 micro-influencers in ethnic wear space (50K-200K followers) for gifting sarees in exchange for authentic review content; track via unique coupon codes.
  • Launch 'Artisan Stories' blog/YouTube series featuring individual weavers' backgrounds, family history, and craft philosophy to create emotional connection and justify premium pricing (₹5,000-10,000/saree).
