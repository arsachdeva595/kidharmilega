Menthol

Rohtas district benefits from favorable monsoon patterns, alluvial soil rich in organic matter, and established mint farming traditions. The region's cool winters and moderate summers create ideal conditions for high-menthol-content mint varieties, making it naturally suited for menthol extraction and processing.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Rohtas.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Rohtas mint farming clusters near Sasaram and Dalmianagar to understand local cultivation practices; connect with Bihar Agricultural Department office in Rohtas; study menthol extraction at CSIR-CIMAP institute connections in neighboring states
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh mint from established farmers in Rohtas district's Karamnasa valley region; negotiate 5-10 ton monthly supply at ₹40-60 per kg; first-batch cost approximately ₹3-5 lakhs for equipment and initial raw materials
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established mint oil distilleries in Rohtas or set up steam distillation unit; consider contract manufacturing with units in Sasaram; obtain ISO 9001 certification for quality assurance; target 500-800 kg monthly menthol extraction capacity
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam for MSME status; obtain IEC code from Patna regional office for export; file FSSAI approval for any food-grade products; register GI application if quality meets standards; trademark brand name with IP office
5
First Sale
Approach pharmaceutical companies in Gurgaon and Hyderabad with samples and certifications; list on TradeKey and Global Sources for B2B export inquiries; establish partnerships with cosmetic brands through industry associations; use IndiaFilings e-marketplace for FMCG connections
6
Scale
Expand farmer network to 50-100 contracted growers in Rohtas; invest in advanced distillation technology for higher extraction yields; develop branded menthol products for D2C channels; establish export relationships with 5-8 countries; target ₹2-3 Cr annual revenue by month 18
