Tourism

East Sikkim is home to Gangtok, India's highest capital by altitude, positioned as a premier Himalayan tourism destination with unique biodiversity, cultural heritage, and spiritual significance. The district offers natural attractions (Tsomgo Lake, Banjhakri Falls), religious sites (Rumtek Monastery), adventure opportunities, and year-round footfall from domestic and international tourists.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for East Sikkim (Gangtok).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Gangtok tourism cluster; meet local tour operators around MG Marg; interview 10+ existing hoteliers and guides; study Sikkim Tourism Department data; understand permit requirements from District Tourism Office, Gangtok.
2
Source Raw Materials
Identify property or homestay partner in central Gangtok (₹15-40L lease/purchase); source basic hospitality inventory (bedding, furniture ₹5-10L); partner with local guides (₹2-5L annual commitment); first-batch cost ₹25-55L for modest guesthouse setup.
3
Produce / Partner
Register with Sikkim Tourism Department; obtain trade license from Gangtok Municipal Corporation; partner with 3-5 local guides and experience providers; establish relationships with local handicraft artisans; develop tour packages around Rumtek Monastery, Tsomgo Lake, Banjhakri Falls.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification); apply for IEC code if targeting international visitors; register brand with IP office; obtain GST registration; get homestay/hotel grading certification from Sikkim Tourism Board.
5
First Sale
Launch on OYO, Airbnb, Booking.com, Makemytrip, Tripadvisor; build WhatsApp/local community presence; partner with travel agencies in Delhi, Bangalore, Kolkata; offer first-month 15-20% discounts; target travel bloggers with free stays for content.
6
Scale
Add 2-3 new experience packages (cultural tours, monastery visits, local cuisine experiences); expand to nearby districts (West Sikkim for Khemdzing); hire dedicated operations manager; invest in digital marketing (Instagram reels, YouTube vlogs); establish group tour partnerships with corporate travel firms.

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L government-backed loan for tourism micro-enterprises with 5-6% interest subsidy.
🏛
MUDRA Yojana (Shishu/Kishor scheme)
₹50L unsecured loan for registered tourism businesses without collateral.
🏚
Sikkim Tourism Policy 2022
hospitality infrastructure grants up to ₹50L for accredited homestays and guesthouses in priority zones.
🏚
Sikkim MSME Development Scheme
5% interest subsidy on working capital loans for registered tourism enterprises, up to ₹10L loan value.
