ODOP Product

Brass Utensils

Sant Kabir Nagar has a centuries-old brass crafting tradition rooted in the region's cultural heritage and spiritual significance. The district's artisan community has perfected the techniques of hand-hammering and engraving brass into functional art pieces. Access to skilled labor and established supply chains for brass ingots make it the natural hub for this craft.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sant Kabir Nagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sant Kabir Nagar's main brass cluster around Nizzamabad locality. Spend 2-3 weeks observing master artisans, understanding design complexity, tool requirements, and pricing. Connect with Sant Kabir Nagar ODOP nodal officer and Handicraft Cooperative Society. Study GI certificate specifics and market reports from Ministry of Handicraft.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source brass ingots (60-70% copper) from Moradabad suppliers (3-4 hours away) or Delhi wholesalers — approximate first batch cost ₹50,000-75,000 for 100-150 kg. Contact registered suppliers on IndiaStack or through SFURTI clusters. Negotiate bulk rates for tools from Agra or local iron mongers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 certified local artisans under a revenue-sharing model (15-25% of sale price) or hire trained apprentices. Begin with signature products (small bowls, lamps, decorative plates). Invest in small workshop space (200-300 sq ft) costing ₹15,000-20,000/month. Set up basic setup: 3 hammering stations, engraving tools, lacquer booth.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam MSME portal (online, free). Apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office for export eligibility. Register GI-certified brand name with Intellectual Property Office. Obtain hallmark certification from BIS for authenticity. Create GST registration and get ₹2-3 lakh insurance for craftsman liability.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy with 15-20 product photos under 'GI Certified Sant Kabir Nagar Brass' tag (fees: 6.5% transaction + 3% payment). Simultaneously list on Amazon Handmade (seller fee ₹99/month). Create Instagram account with reels showing craft process (target handmade/heritage audience). Reach out to 5-10 premium gift retailers in metros with catalog and wholesale pricing. Attend India International Mega Trade Fair (IIMTF) for B2B connects.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range to 30+ SKUs (utensils, lamps, decorative pieces). Open second artisan team and move to 500 sq ft workshop. Invest ₹2-3 lakh in paid ads (Google Shopping, Instagram). Approach corporate gifting companies and premium hotels for bulk orders. Export to 2-3 countries (start with US/UK via Alibaba or direct B2B). Hire social media manager (₹15,000-20,000/month) and apply for SFURTI subsidy for cluster expansion.
