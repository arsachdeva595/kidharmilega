Kangra Tea

Kangra's elevation of 1,200–2,250 meters, cool climate, monsoon rainfall, and mineral-rich soil create ideal conditions for premium oolong-style tea production. The region's 150-year tea-growing heritage and established infrastructure of estates, processing units, and skilled labor make it the natural home for Kangra Tea.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kangra.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established Kangra tea estates (Palampur cluster) such as Baijnath, Ahju, or Ranital estates. Connect with Kangra Tea Association, spend 2–3 weeks learning fermentation, rolling, and grading. Study competitor brands (Kangra Herbals, Goodwyn Tea) and visit Kangra Tea Museum.
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner with certified Kangra tea growers or small producers in Palampur (via Kangra Tea Cooperative or individual estates). Source 200–500 kg of raw leaf for first batch at ₹150–200/kg. Budget ₹40,000–80,000 for first procurement and testing.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with an existing APEDA-certified processing unit in Palampur or Baijnath for initial batches (avoiding high capex). Agree on 15–20% processing fee. Alternatively, invest in small-scale rolling and drying equipment (₹3–5 lakh) and hire trained tea rollers from local labor pools.
4
Brand & Register
Register for Udyam (online, free). Apply for IEC code from APEDA (for export eligibility). File GI certification copy and origin certificate. Register brand name with IP office. Get FSSAI food business license (₹5,000–10,000). Ensure packaging lists 'Kangra Tea GI' prominently.
5
First Sale
List on Amazon, Flipkart, and specialty tea platforms (Tea Chai-se, The Cha Box) by month 2. Launch Instagram & Pinterest with estate stories and brewing guides. Reach out to 50–100 specialty cafés and wellness stores for B2B partnerships. Target 10–15 corporate gifting clients via LinkedIn outreach.
6
Scale
By month 7, secure 3–5 retail partnerships. Launch a paid Facebook/Instagram campaign targeting affluent metros (₹30k/month budget). Introduce a monthly subscription box (₹800–1,200/box). Explore export channels via tea importers in UK and USA (APEDA-registered). Hire a second team member for operations.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 3–5 wellness influencers (50k–200k followers) in the sustainable/health space. Send free tasting packs and request authentic brewing reels on Instagram/YouTube targeting metro audiences.
  • Launch a referral program: Existing customers get ₹200 credit for every new customer referral. Promote via email and WhatsApp to build a loyal base rapidly.
  • Host virtual tea tasting webinars (free, 45 min) on monthly basis via Zoom/YouTube Live. Invite tea experts and sell curated packs during the session. Target registered participants and tea enthusiasts groups.
