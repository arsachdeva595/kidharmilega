Alphonso Mangoes

Ratnagiri's coastal climate, rich black soil, and centuries-old mango farming tradition create ideal conditions for Alphonso cultivation. The district's microclimate — with monsoon rains and specific temperature patterns — produces mangoes with superior sugar content and distinctive aroma that cannot be replicated elsewhere.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ratnagiri.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ratnagiri mango clusters at Rajapur and Khed taluks; study orchards around Ratnagiri town. Connect with 5-10 established mango farmers and exporters. Attend Ratnagiri Mango Festival (April-May) to understand supply chain, quality standards, and market dynamics. Cost: ₹20,000-30,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source saplings from Ratnagiri Agriculture Department nurseries or certified cultivators near Rajapur. For trading model, establish relationships with 3-5 orchard owners for direct procurement agreements. First batch sourcing (200-500 crates): ₹2-4 lakhs. Register with APMC Ratnagiri for mandal licensing.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established orchard owners via contract farming (60% farmer, 40% investor) or lease 2-5 acres of mango land. Alternatively, source directly from aggregators like Ratnagiri Fruit Cluster. Invest in cold storage facility (₹15-25 lakhs) near Ratnagiri town for post-harvest handling.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (agricultural business). Obtain IEC from nearest DGFT office (Fort area, Mumbai — 100 km away) for exports. Get GI tag certification use approval from Ratnagiri Mango GI Registry. Register brand on ipindia.gov.in. Obtain FSSAI license for value-added products.
5
First Sale
Launch on JioMart, BigBasket, Amazon Fresh targeting tier-1 cities (Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi). Simultaneously approach export traders at APMC Ratnagiri and connect with Middle East distributors via TradeKey, GlobalSources. Target premium retailers like Nature's Basket, Spencer's. Tie-up with restaurants and hotel chains in Mumbai for premium pricing.
6
Scale
After 6 months of consistent sales, expand to value-added products (mango pulp for B2B food industry). Scale exports through APEDA registration and government export subsidies. Establish agritourism model offering farm tours during season. Build contract farming network with 20+ small orchard owners to ensure supply consistency.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram influencer partnerships with food & wellness creators; post orchard behind-the-scenes content and GI certification story. Budget: ₹2-3 lakhs for 5-10 micro-influencers. Target foodie communities.
  • Premium sampling campaign at high-end supermarkets (Spencer's, Nature's Basket) in Mumbai, Bangalore, Delhi during peak season (April-June). Include QR codes linking to GI story and farm origin. Budget: ₹8-10 lakhs.
  • B2B export webinars on LinkedIn targeting Middle East importers and food distributors. Emphasize GI certification, quality standards, MOQ flexibility. Partner with APEDA for credibility. Monthly 2-3 webinars, budget ₹1-2 lakhs.
