Cotton Yarn

Wardha has a 100+ year legacy of cotton textile production with established spinning mills, skilled labor, and proximity to raw cotton sources in the Vidarbha region. The district's existing infrastructure, supply chain networks, and presence of multiple medium and small-scale yarn mills make it an ideal hub for cotton yarn manufacturing and export.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Wardha.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established spinning mills in Wardha town and Hinganghat cluster (Hinganghat Spinning Mills Association); interact with yarn traders at Wardha Cotton Market; study competitor operations like Pratibha Mills and Vardhman Group facilities; attend Indian Yarn Spinners Association (IYSA) meetings.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton from Vidarbha cotton farmers or Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Board; expect ₹35-45 per kg for raw cotton; for 1-ton first batch, budget ₹40-50 lakhs including processing chemicals and auxiliaries from suppliers in Nagpur or Wardha.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing job-spinning facilities in Wardha (per-kg contract basis) to avoid ₹1+ Cr capex initially; alternatively acquire used spinning frames from Wardha Mills Liquidation Auctions; hire experienced spinners from local labor pool at ₹15,000-25,000/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry of MSME online portal); obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Nagpur; register brand name with Trademark Registry; obtain textile mill license from District Industries Center Wardha; apply for ISO 9001 or OEKO-TEX certification for premium positioning.
5
First Sale
List on Alibaba and Global Sources for export queries; approach garment manufacturers in Tiruppur, Surat, and Jalgaon through email and tradeshows; join TieXpo (Textile India Expo) and IGATEX; leverage WhatsApp Business for B2B inquiries; start D2C via Shopify, Amazon Handmade, and Instagram.
6
Scale
After 6 months, invest in own spinning frames (capacity 1-2 TPD) targeting ₹3-5 Cr turnover; build brand via participatory tradeshows; hire sales team for direct mill visits; expand yarn variants (organic, blended, dyed); explore backward integration into cotton ginning; secure bank credit (MUDRA/PMEGP subsidy) to fund growth.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram/Facebook carousel ads targeting garment manufacturers with testimonials from 5-10 existing mills; focus on Surat, Tiruppur, Jalgaon clusters with geo-targeted ads; budge ₹50k/month.
  • Attend and sponsor booth at TieXpo (Mumbai), IGATEX (Ahmedabad), and Cotton & Allied Textiles Expo; collect leads, follow up via email sequences; allocate ₹3-5L per show.
  • Create WhatsApp Business channel with daily yarn price updates, quality bulletins, and product videos; build 500+ mill owner/manager contacts; send weekly newsletters on market trends and sustainability messaging.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh government subsidy + ₹5 lakh bank loan for textile manufacturing units
🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 stipend + ₹1 lakh interest-free loan for textile artisans and small spinners
🏚
Maharashtra Textile Policy (2019)
capital subsidy up to 25% on spinning/weaving machinery; SSCLIP (State Spinning Cluster Linkage & Incentive Project) offers equipment subsidies
🏚
Maharashtra Integrated Cotton Textile Cluster (MICTC)
common facility centers, testing labs, and export promotion support in Wardha and Nagpur region
