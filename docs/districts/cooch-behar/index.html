ODOP Product

Sital pati & its products

Cooch Behar has centuries-old tradition of Sital Pati weaving with established artisan clusters in Sagareswar, Ghugumari, and Pashnadanga. The district's access to natural fibers like murta plants and skilled weavers makes it the primary production hub. UNESCO recognition of this craft (for Sylhet region) validates the cultural and commercial potential.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Cooch Behar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sagareswar, Ghugumari, and Pashnadanga weaving clusters in Cooch Behar. Spend 3-5 days with master weavers, understand production timelines (7-15 days per mat), material costs (₹150-400 per mat), and quality grading. Connect with Cooch Behar District Handicraft Association and ODOP nodal officers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source murta plant fibers directly from local suppliers in Cooch Behar district (₹800-1200 per kg). First batch of 50 mats requires ₹15,000-20,000 raw materials. Establish relationships with 3-4 supplier clusters to ensure year-round availability and negotiate bulk discounts.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisan groups in Sagareswar (primary cluster). Start with 2-3 lead weavers, provide advance material + 40% payment upfront, 60% on completion. Implement quality control checklist for design consistency. Use WhatsApp groups for daily production tracking. Plan for 20-30 mats/month in Year 1.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (GST mandatory). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export eligibility. Brand name registration on IP India portal. Get Handicraft Product Certificate from DC Handicraft office, Cooch Behar. Document UNESCO heritage angle in brand story for premium positioning.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade and Etsy with high-quality photography (mat in home setting). Create Instagram account highlighting artisan stories and UNESCO heritage angle — target 500 followers first month. Reach out to 20 eco-home décor retailers (Urban Ladder, Pepperfry, local) for wholesale. Run initial ₹10K Google/Instagram ads targeting 'natural bedding India' and 'heritage home décor'.
6
Scale
After 6 months, if hitting 50+ monthly sales, hire 1 full-time production coordinator. Expand to 4-5 artisan partners. Launch Nakshi Pati (decorative design) variant at 30% premium. Approach 5-star hotels in Delhi/Mumbai for corporate gifting. Apply for SFURTI cluster development grant. Explore Amazon Global Selling and Etsy International Shipping; target 15-20% export revenue.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reel series 'Artisan's Day' — showcase 60-second clips of weaving process, fiber sourcing, and finished product in home settings. Post 3x/week, use hashtags #SitalPati #HandwovenMats #EcoHome #IndianCraft. Target yoga and wellness influencers for collabs (₹500-2K per post).
  • Partnership with 5-10 eco-home décor micro-influencers (10K-50K followers) in India. Send 2-3 sample mats, ask for unboxing reels on Instagram/YouTube. Allocate ₹30K/month for influencer seeding. Track coupon-code-driven sales to measure ROI.
  • Email marketing to B2B buyers (hotels, wellness centers, corporate gift managers). Create PDF catalog with product variants, bulk pricing, and Heritage UNESCO angle. Weekly emails to 500 curated B2B prospects (₹200/month tool cost). Offer 10% bulk discounts + free shipping for orders >₹50K.

₹15K toolkit grant, ₹10L collateral-free loan, interest subsidy 5.5%, branding/marketing support for traditional artisans

Direct grants up to ₹10L for cluster development, material cost subsidy 20-30%, export promotion fund ₹2-5L per unit

Health insurance, skill training subsidies, working capital at 6% interest for registered handicraft entrepreneurs
