Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Araria.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Araria jute clusters near Forbesganj and Jokihat towns; connect with Araria Jute Farmers Cooperative Society; study retting and fiber extraction at local processing units; understand export quality standards for jute at Kolkata Jute Exchange.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source jute seeds from Bihar Agricultural Department certified vendors (cost: ₹2-3L per ton); establish relationships with local farmers for raw jute procurement; first batch sourcing cost: ₹15-25L for 5-10 tons.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing jute processing units in Forbesganj (retting, spinning, weaving); or set up small-scale processing unit with Government of Bihar subsidy; hire experienced jute workers from local farming communities.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam MSME (Micro: <₹25L turnover); apply for IEC from Directorate General of Foreign Trade (DGFT); register brand on Ministry of Commerce portal; obtain APEDA certification for agricultural exports.
5
First Sale
Launch on India Exim Geek, Alibaba, and Global Sources for B2B; list on Amazon Handmade and Flipkart for D2C; approach sustainable brands on LinkedIn; pitch to textile mills via industry associations.
6
Scale
Expand farmer networks through cooperatives; invest in mechanized processing (spinning, weaving); build D2C brand presence through sustainability narratives; explore Fair Trade certifications; set up warehousing in major ports for export logistics.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create LinkedIn thought leadership content on sustainable jute production and agricultural innovation targeting textile mill executives; share farmer testimonials and production process videos.
  • Launch Instagram campaign highlighting eco-friendly jute products with influencer partnerships in sustainability and zero-waste lifestyle niches; use #SustainableJute #BiharODOP hashtags.
  • Participate in international trade shows (Première Vision Paris, ITMA Asia) and Indian textile exhibitions; distribute product samples and IEC-certified documentation to 50+ B2B buyers.
