ODOP Product

Engineering and Auto components

Thiruvallur hosts one of Asia's largest auto component clusters in Sriperumbudur, home to OEM suppliers like Bosch, Amara Raja, and hundreds of Tier-1 and Tier-2 manufacturers. The region has developed a mature supply chain ecosystem, skilled workforce trained in CNC machining and quality systems (ISO, TS16949), and proximity to major markets (Chennai port, airport). Two decades of automotive manufacturing FDI have created world-class testing labs, tooling infrastructure, and logistics networks.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Thiruvallur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sriperumbudur SIPCOT cluster and tour 5-10 component manufacturers; study TS16949 requirements; visit Ranipet engineering cluster; speak with suppliers at SIAM (Society of Indian Automobile Manufacturers) network events; analyze cost structures from existing players like Bosch, Amara Raja, and mid-tier suppliers.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from steel suppliers in Thiruvallur (near Ranipet); contact distributors of CNC cutting tools (Hyderabad or Bangalore hubs); first batch cost ₹15-25 lakhs for materials; establish credit terms with 2-3 steel distributors; inspect ISO-certified suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Rent 2000-5000 sq ft space in Sriperumbudur SIPCOT or Ranipet industrial park (₹20-40 per sq ft monthly); acquire 2-4 used CNC machines (₹30-80 lakhs per machine); hire 5-8 skilled operators trained in ISO and quality systems; partner with testing labs (ACIL, NABL) for certification; consider job-work initially to reduce capex.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (single day online); apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office; obtain TS16949 certification (₹8-12 lakhs, 6 months process); register brand name with IP office; get ISO 9001 and ISO 14001 certifications; file for BIS standards where applicable.
5
First Sale
Approach Tier-1 suppliers in Sriperumbudur cluster with samples and price quotes; register on automotive B2B portals (ThomasNet, IndiaMART); attend SIAM and AutoTech India exhibitions (3-4 annually); email OEM procurement teams directly; join Sriperumbudur Auto Component Association; engage export trading houses for EXIM opportunities.
6
Scale
Reinvest 40% profits into 2-3 additional CNC machines; achieve TS16949 tier-2 upgrades; expand to ₹5-10 Cr revenue by targeting 3-5 OEM contracts; build export relationships with EU/US distributors; consider equity funding from impact investors or SIDBI for ₹5 Cr expansion; open second facility for high-volume components.
