Applique Work

Sitamarhi has a 300+ year tradition of applique work passed down through generations of artisan families. The district's cultural heritage, combined with access to skilled labor and proximity to fabric markets in Patna and Varanasi, makes it the natural hub for this craft. The GI recognition has already validated the authenticity and market value of Sitamarhi applique globally.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sitamarhi.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sitamarhi's applique clusters in Ramnarayan Pur and Manjhiya villages; connect with Sitamarhi Handicraft Association; study existing GI-certified units like Sitamarhi Craft Collective; attend ITC Sambhav or SEWA meetings.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton base fabric (₹80-120/meter) from Varanasi cloth markets (Godowlia area) or Patna wholesale; buy colored fabric scraps (₹2000-5000/50kg) from Surat textile waste dealers; needles/threads from local shops. First batch (100 pieces) costs ₹25000-40000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 3-5 artisan families in Ramnarayan Pur village (average ₹3000-6000/month per artisan); provide design templates & material supply; establish quality control checks; formalize through written contracts specifying delivery timelines and piece rates.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in (ITC HS code 5810.99); apply for IEC code via ICEGATE; register GI tag usage with DC Handicraft Bihar; get ISO 9001 certification (optional but premium); create brand identity emphasizing 'Sitamarhi GI Certified' tag.
5
First Sale
List on Etsy (15% commission, target $8-25/piece), Amazon Handmade India (flat rate), Arunachalam Murugavel's FABRIC-I platform, own Shopify store; reach out to exporters in Bangalore (Fab Exports), Delhi (Craft Export); cold email 20 boutique hotels and 15 interior designers with product shots.
6
Scale
After ₹5L revenue, hire a part-time designer to create seasonal collections; expand artisan network to 15+ workers; establish wholesale partnerships with 3-4 tier-1 Indian retailers; launch limited Instagram/TikTok campaigns highlighting 'slow fashion' narrative; attend Handloom Export Promotion Council (HEPC) trade shows.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels (2x/week) showing 60-second time-lapses of artisan hands stitching appliqué pieces; partner with 5-10 home décor micro-influencers (10K-100K followers) for unboxing videos; target hashtags #SlowFashion #EthicalTextiles #ArtisanMade.
  • Launch Pinterest boards with high-quality product photography pinned to 'Home Décor', 'Textile Art', and 'Sustainable Fashion' boards; run Pinterest ads (₹200-500/day) targeting 35-60F, affluent, design-conscious audience in USA/UK.
  • Email outreach to 500+ interior designers (via IIDA India directory) offering 20% B2B wholesale discounts on bulk orders (20+ pieces); include GI certificate, artisan story, and sample product with pitch.
