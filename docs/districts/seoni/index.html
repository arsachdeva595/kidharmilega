Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Seoni.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Seoni district's agricultural office and connect with local cilantro growers in Seoni, Chhindwara, and Balaghat regions. Study cultivation practices at Indore Agricultural University. Analyze demand at major markets: Indore Wholesale Market, Mumbai's Vile Parle Market, and Delhi's Azadpur Market.
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner with 2-3 local farmers in Seoni for contract farming or buy directly from Seoni's gram panchayats. Expect ₹80,000-120,000 for first 2-3 acre cultivation lease and seeds. Source packaging and cold-storage containers from suppliers in Bhopal or online (₹30,000-50,000).
3
Produce / Partner
Begin with 1-2 acres of owned or leased farmland in Seoni district. Use conventional or organic methods based on market demand. Partner with local agricultural societies for extension support and certification. Invest in small refrigeration unit (₹60,000-80,000) for post-harvest storage and freshness retention.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small Enterprise) with Ministry of MSME. Obtain IEC code from APEDA for export eligibility. Register FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) to qualify for government subsidies. Create brand identity with FSSAI license for packaged products. Use registration name like '[Brand] Seoni Cilantro Pvt Ltd'.
5
First Sale
Approach IndiaMART and TraceNaturals for B2B orders. Launch on Jio Mart, BigBasket's wholesale, and Amazon Fresh in Madhya Pradesh. Contact restaurant groups in Indore (Tusker House, Opium Den) and Mumbai directly. Export via APEDA-registered dealers or spice export houses in Surat. Target 2-3 restaurants + 1 retail partner in first month.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 5-10 acres using profits or PMEGP subsidy. Build cold-chain infrastructure (₹25-50L) via SFURTI fund. Launch packaged dried and powdered cilantro variants targeting Ayurvedic brands. Target international markets via export houses; aim for ₹100L+ revenue in year 2.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with regional food bloggers and Instagram influencers (Chennai, Delhi, Mumbai) to showcase farm-to-table cilantro freshness; offer free samples and behind-the-scenes farm videos.
  • Run WhatsApp broadcast campaigns targeting restaurant owners and caterers in tier-1 cities with bulk order discounts and same-day delivery guarantees from partner logistics.
  • Create TikTok/YouTube short-form content on cilantro recipes, cultivation story, and export journey; tag food channels and Ayurvedic wellness brands for cross-promotion.
