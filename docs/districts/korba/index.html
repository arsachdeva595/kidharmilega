ODOP Product

Minor Forest Produce - Mahua

Korba district has dense forest cover spanning over 3,500 sq km with natural Mahua tree populations thriving in the tropical deciduous forests. The region's tribal communities have centuries-old traditional knowledge of Mahua harvesting, processing, and utilization. The proximity to both raw materials and a skilled artisan base makes it ideal for value-added Mahua products.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Korba.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Korba forest clusters in Katghora and Kusmunda blocks; meet NTPC Community Forest Management Groups and tribal collector networks. Connect with Chhattisgarh State Minor Forest Produce Federation office in Raipur to understand seasonal cycles, harvest timings (April-June), and current market rates (₹12-18/kg for flowers, ₹3-5/L for oil).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Mahua flowers from tribal collectors through Korba District Forest Department's FPO network or directly negotiate with cooperative unions like Chhattisgarh Van Dhan Vikas Samiti. First-batch cost: ₹2-3L for 500 kg dried flowers to test oil extraction and product development. Establish relationships with 5-10 local collectors to ensure consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with artisan women groups in Manendragarh and Kusumkela villages for traditional oil extraction (cold-pressed method) or set up a small extraction unit (₹3-5L investment). Alternatively, contract manufacturing through SFURTI-supported units in Korba. Start with 50-100 L monthly production using manual extraction, then scale to mechanized processing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online on udyamregistration.gov.in), obtain IEC code from DGFT portal, and register as Ayurveda/Herbal cosmetic product under Drugs & Cosmetics Act with State Licensing Authority, Chhattisgarh. Create brand identity with local tribal motifs; register trademark on IP India portal. Obtain Forest Produce collection certificates from District Forest Office.
5
First Sale
Launch on Nykaa, Amazon Beauty, Flipkart Health & Wellness, and Bozic with 40-50 SKUs minimum. Build D2C website using Shopify or WooCommerce. Simultaneously pitch B2B to Himalaya, Patanjali, Forest Essentials through LinkedIn and trade shows. Target ayurvedic retailers in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore via direct outreach. Distribute through local FPC channels in Chhattisgarh.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product portfolio to 15+ SKUs (body butters, masks, capsules). Secure certification (Organic/FSSAI/GMP) to unlock premium pricing and export channels. Launch influencer partnerships on Instagram with wellness and ayurveda micro-influencers (10K-100K followers). Apply for PMEGP or state subsidies to scale extraction capacity. Target institutional bulk orders from spa chains, beauty schools, and wellness retreats.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels (15-30 sec) showing traditional Mahua flower harvesting by tribal women, oil extraction process, and skincare application routines. Target beauty & wellness hashtags (#ayurvedicbeauty #forestproduce #organic). Partner with 5-7 micro-influencers in skincare niche to get 20-30% discount codes for followers.
  • Launch YouTube educational content (10-15 min videos) on 'Mahua Oil Benefits for Skin' and 'Traditional Forest Beauty Secrets of Central India.' Target SEO keywords like 'natural oils for glowing skin' and 'Mahua benefits.' Drive traffic to landing pages with waitlist capture for new product launches.
  • Execute B2B outreach via LinkedIn to 500+ Ayurveda/Herbal brands and wellness retailers with case studies showing 35-40% cost savings vs. imported alternatives. Host 2-3 virtual product demos per month. Attend Ayurveda Expo (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) and cosmetic trade shows to sign 3-5 bulk supply contracts annually.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L grant for SC/ST entrepreneurs or ₹10L for general, covering 25-35% project cost for forest product processing units.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹50L-2Cr cluster development grant for Mahua processing clusters in Korba, including common facility centers and quality testing labs.
🏚
Chhattisgarh Van Dhan Scheme
provides market linkage, training, and ₹5000-10000 annual support per tribal collector for minor forest produce. Links directly to cooperative networks for bulk supply.
🏚
Chhattisgarh Mahila Kalyan Yojana
subsidized processing equipment (₹1-2L discount) and skill training for women entrepreneurs in forest product value addition.
