Citrus Fruits (Kinnow)

Fazilka's semi-arid climate, loamy soil, and canal irrigation from the Sutlej River create ideal conditions for Kinnow cultivation. The district has 60+ years of citrus farming expertise and produces 8-10 lakh tonnes annually, making it India's largest Kinnow producer with consistent yield and superior sweetness (12-13° Brix).

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Fazilka.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Fazilka's prominent citrus clusters around Abohar and Mamdot towns; meet 3-5 established Kinnow growers; study production cycles at Agricultural Department office in Fazilka; attend Punjab Agricultural University's citrus training programs.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified Kinnow grafts from Rajasthan nurseries (₹40-60 per sapling). For 1 acre: 200-250 grafts (₹10-15K), fertilizers (₹8-10K), irrigation setup (₹50-80K). Total first-batch cost for 1 acre: ₹70-105K.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established Fazilka farmers on contract farming or lease land in Abohar block. Use drip irrigation + fertigation best practices. Implement IPM (Integrated Pest Management) for disease control. Engage with Punjab Agricultural University's extension services for technical support.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 5 mins). Obtain IEC code from DGFT for exports. Register as agricultural producer organization or food business under FSSAI. Create brand identity focused on Fazilka's terroir and organic/natural positioning. Get phytosanitary certificate from Plant Protection Directorate, Chandigarh.
5
First Sale
List on Blinkit, JioMart, and BigBasket (DCC registration required). Approach wholesale mandis in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore. Contact direct exporters in Abohar for first bulk order. Participate in India Expo Center trade shows and agricultural fairs in Delhi.
6
Scale
Post 6 months, establish private label with premium packaging for D2C. Build backward linkage with 10-15 farming partners. Invest in cold storage facility (₹20-30L) for year-round supply. Launch value-added products (juice, pulp). Target institutional buyers (hotels, corporate offices) and expand to 2-3 new geographies.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram + YouTube video series on farm-to-table journey: Behind-the-scenes drone footage of Abohar orchards, harvesting techniques, nutritional benefits. Partner with 5-10 food bloggers for unboxing videos. Budget: ₹2-5L for 3-month campaign.
  • WhatsApp Business API for bulk buyers: Create catalog of product grades, pricing tiers, seasonal availability. Run targeted campaigns to fruit wholesalers, importers, and retail chains. Daily stock updates and direct B2B ordering.
  • Offline partnerships: Sponsor local mandis and agricultural expos in Punjab and Haryana; set up tasting booths at farmer markets in Delhi NCR; collaborate with organic supermarkets (Big Basket stores, NaturalFresh) for in-store promotions.
