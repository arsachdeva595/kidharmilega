ODOP Product

Bengal Patachitra

Paschim Medinipur has a 500+ year tradition of Patachitra painting, with concentrated artisan communities in villages like Naya, Kumartuli clusters, and established knowledge transfer systems. The district's proximity to clay sources and natural dye plants makes it the epicenter of this GI-tagged art form in India.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Paschim Medinipur (West Medinipur).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Naya village artisan clusters (30 km from Kharagpur) and Kumartuli workshops. Spend 1-2 weeks with master artisans learning techniques. Connect with Paschim Medinipur MSME cluster office. Study GI certification standards from DC Handicraft Ministry portal.
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure natural dyes from local villages (₹2,000-3,000/kg) or traditional suppliers in Bengal. Source cotton cloth from Kolkata textile markets (₹50-80/meter). Buy brushes and tools from Kumartuli craft suppliers. First batch (20-30 paintings) costs ₹25,000-40,000 including materials and artisan labor.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 master artisans in Naya/Kumartuli on revenue-share (40-50% artisan cut). Train 1-2 junior artisans to increase production capacity. Establish a small workshop (200-300 sqft) for material prep and finishing. Target production: 15-20 paintings/month at scale.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small) online on udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC code from DGFT for export. Register GI certification holder status with Ministry of Commerce. Create brand identity emphasizing heritage + sustainability. File trademark for brand name.
5
First Sale
Launch Etsy shop with 50-word storytelling per piece (₹0 setup). List on Craftroots, Indian Heritage, Artisera platforms. Reach out to 10 premium interior design firms in metros with WhatsApp catalogs. Start Instagram/Pinterest content marketing with artisan stories and process videos. Target first 3 sales through DM outreach to micro-boutique owners.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 5-7 partners. Apply for SFURTI cluster development funds. Export via Amazon Global, iShop, and direct B2B partnerships. Launch 3-5 SKU variations (framed, scroll, commissions). Open pop-up gallery at craft fairs (Crafts Council, Indian Heritage exhibitions). Build subscription model for corporate art leasing.
