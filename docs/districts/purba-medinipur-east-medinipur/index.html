Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Purba Medinipur (East Medinipur).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kolkata Food Processing Cluster (Rabindra Sarovar area) and WBMSME clusters in Haldia. Interview 3-4 existing cashew processors in West Bengal. Study imports at Haldia port and connect with commodity traders. Spend 2 weeks learning roasting, grading, and packaging standards from established units.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cashew kernels (W180, W210 grade) from Goa or Karnataka suppliers via commodity exchanges (NCDEX). Initial batch: 5 MT will cost ₹30-35 lakhs. Secondary materials from Delhi packaging suppliers. Negotiate 45-day credit terms. Visit suppliers in Belgaum or contact via Indian Cashew Processing Association.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established food processing units in Haldia industrial area (co-processing model to start) or invest in roasting, sorting, and packaging machinery (₹20-25 lakhs). Ensure FSSAI food safety license and ISO 22000 certification before production. Hire 8-10 skilled workers trained in cashew processing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification) online via udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from DGFT for exports. Apply for FSSAI license (₹5-10k). Register trademark with IP India. Design packaging with nutritional labels compliant with Food Safety Standards. Create GST registration within 30 days of commencement.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Business and Flipkart Wholesale with minimum 500kg inventory. Create Instagram/Facebook store with lifestyle content of cashew recipes. Approach 20-30 retail chains in Kolkata, Howrah via direct sales team. Partner with 2-3 regional distributors. Cold-email 10 export companies monthly with samples and CoA.
6
Scale
After 6 months, add value-added products (roasted-salted variants, flavored cashews). Expand export to 5+ countries via freight forwarders. Establish regional distribution hubs in Mumbai and Delhi. Launch B2B bulk subscriptions. Achieve FSSAI certification for 'Foods for Special Dietary Use'. Target ₹5 Cr revenue by Year 2.

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP
Up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy (35% of project cost for general category) for setting up food processing unit with government backing.
🏛
SFURTI
Cluster development support up to ₹3 crores for food processing clusters; access machinery subsidies and common facility centers in Haldia.
🏚
West Bengal Food Processing Policy 2022
5% capital subsidy on machinery, 3% interest subsidy on bank loans, land allocation support for units in designated clusters.

Bengal Enterprise
State MSME promotion scheme offering ₹10-50 lakhs interest-free loans, skill training subsidies, and export promotion support for food processing entrepreneurs.
