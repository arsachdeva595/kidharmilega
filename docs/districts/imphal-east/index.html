ODOP Product

Wangkhei Phee

Imphal East is the historical heartland of Manipuri handloom tradition, home to master weavers of Wangkhei village who have perfected silk-cotton weaving techniques over centuries. The region's cultural ethos, access to fine silk and cotton fibers, and established artisan networks make it the only authentic production hub for this GI-tagged product. Local water quality and climate conditions are also ideal for natural fiber preparation.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Imphal East.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Wangkhei village (10 km from Imphal city center) and spend 2-4 weeks with master weavers understanding traditional techniques. Connect with All Manipur Handloom Weavers Association and Ministry of Textiles' regional office in Imphal to access technical training programs and production benchmarks. Research competitors on Etsy, Aayopata, and Indian Heritage boutiques.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mulberry silk yarn from Bangalore silk suppliers (₹800-1200/kg) or Varanasi silk markets; cotton from Tiruppur mills (₹120-180/kg). Connect with local Imphal raw material dealers near Cheirap Market. First batch (100 meters of 60-inch width fabric): ₹80,000-120,000 including dyes and sizing paste. Order minimum 3-5 kg silk and 8-10 kg cotton for 200-300 meters initial production.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 5-10 registered weavers in Wangkhei village through cooperative agreements or employment contracts. Alternatively, register with Manipur Handloom Development Corporation (MHDC) to access weaver networks. Setup small design studio (150-200 sq ft) in Imphal city center for design finalization and quality checks. Budget ₹30,000-50,000/month for renting artisan workshop space.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online for MSME certification (₹0 cost, 10 minutes). Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office (₹0 cost). Register trademark with IP India for brand name (₹4,500-9,000). Apply for GI-tag holder certification from Wangkhei Weavers Cooperative Society to use official GI mark. Open GSTIN and bank account. Create brand guideline document emphasizing heritage and authenticity.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C website using Shopify or WooCommerce (₹5,000-10,000 setup). List on Etsy, Aayopata, and Amazon Handmade with premium pricing (₹1,500-4,500 per meter). Approach luxury boutiques in Delhi (Anaam, Okhai), Mumbai (Ensemble, Bombay Textile Company), and Bangalore (organic/heritage concept stores) with samples. Email high-fashion magazines and bloggers for PR coverage. Attend India Handicrafts & Gifts Fair (IHGF) in Delhi biannually.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 15-20 weavers. Introduce 3-4 new design collections based on market feedback. Launch wholesale distribution partnerships with 10-15 multibrand heritage stores nationally. Establish export distribution through Indian handicraft exporters (Artisans' Guild, Fabindia exports division). Hire designer and logistics manager. Target ₹50+ Lakhs annual revenue by month 18.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 5-10 Instagram lifestyle influencers (50K-500K followers) focused on sustainable fashion and Indian heritage; send them Wangkhei Phee samples for unboxing reels and educational content about weaving process. Target 15-20% conversion.
  • Create 'Meet the Weaver' video series featuring master artisans from Wangkhei village; distribute on YouTube, Facebook, and TikTok highlighting craft authenticity and cultural significance. Aim for 100K+ views and 10% click-through to e-commerce.
  • Approach 3-5 luxury fashion columnists at Vogue India, Harper's Bazaar India, and Indian Fashion Blogger Network; offer exclusive early-access collections and behind-the-scenes content for feature articles and brand collaborations.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
up to ₹3 Lakh loan at 0% interest for traditional artisans and craftspeople in handloom sector; covers working capital and tools
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakhs subsidy for manufacturing enterprises in textile handloom sector with 25% applicant contribution
🏚
Manipur Handloom Policy 2018
25% subsidy on raw material purchase, export incentives, and free MSME registration for weavers and textile entrepreneurs
🏚
Manipur Women Entrepreneurship Scheme
additional 10% subsidy for women entrepreneurs in handloom and textile sector; priority lending through cooperative banks
