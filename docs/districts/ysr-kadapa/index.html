YSR Kadapa has a centuries-old tradition of brass metalwork inherited from Mughal-era craftsmen. The region's skilled artisan community, access to quality brass raw materials, and established manufacturing ecosystem make it naturally suited for Vonipenta Brass production. The district's cultural legacy in metalcraft ensures authentic techniques and market credibility.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for YSR Kadapa.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kadapa's Brass Cluster in Pulivendula and Rajampet areas; connect with established artisan groups through DC Handicraft office. Attend Indian Handicrafts Trade Fair (IHTF) in Delhi. Spend 2-3 weeks shadowing existing producers to understand mold-making, casting, finishing, and quality standards.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source brass ingots (90-92% purity) from Rajampet brass suppliers (₹450-500/kg). Order first batch of 500-1000 kg from local vendors; approximate cost ₹2.5-3 lakhs. Connect with Andhra Pradesh State Handicrafts Development Corporation (APSHDC) for bulk material supply discounts.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-8 skilled artisans in Pulivendula-Rajampet cluster for casting and design work. Establish a small studio (500-800 sq ft) in Kadapa town for finishing, polishing, and quality control. Source traditional molds and furnaces; budget ₹4-6 lakhs for equipment and working capital.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) classification as Artisan/Handicraft producer. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office for export eligibility. Register brand name with IP office (trademark). Get BIS certification for metalware (IS 212) and safety compliance. Apply for Handicraft Cluster Certificate from DC Handicraft ministry.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C storefront on Shopify or WordPress with 20-30 initial SKUs (bowls, vases, decorative plates, serving utensils). List on Flipkart Handicraft, Amazon Artisan, and Meesho within 6 weeks. Pitch to HomeShop18, FabFurnish, and Urban Ladder for bulk partnerships. Attend Gift Fair India (Kolkata, Mumbai, Delhi) in Jan-Feb to meet retail buyers and exporters.
6
Scale
After ₹10-15L revenue milestone (months 6-9), hire export consultant to target USA and EU markets via B2B portals. Expand artisan network to 15-20 skilled workers. Launch Instagram/Pinterest content strategy with lifestyle photography. Partner with 3-5 interior designers for recommendations. Apply for APEDA or IMRB export incentives. Build private label offerings for retail chains (target 2-3 partnerships).

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels (30-60 sec) showing live casting, molding, and polishing process; focus on craftsmanship storytelling. Tag lifestyle influencers (500K-2M followers) in home décor niche for gifting campaigns. Budget: ₹20K/month for influencer partnerships and paid social.
  • Partner with 3-4 interior design studios in Hyderabad, Bangalore, and Chennai to showcase Vonipenta Brass in their project portfolios. Offer 20-25% designer discount for bulk orders. Generate case studies and before-after room photos for website and LinkedIn.
  • Launch email nurture sequence targeting corporate bulk buyers (hotels, offices, restaurants). Create 'Artisan Stories' blog series (bi-weekly) profiling master craftsmen. Submit press releases to Deccan Chronicle, Times of India, and Handicraft news portals about heritage preservation angle.
