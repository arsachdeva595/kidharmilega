ODOP Product

Handicraft Items

Kachch has a 5,000-year-old artisan tradition with concentrated clusters of master craftspeople in Bhuj, Mundra, and surrounding villages. The region's unique socio-cultural fabric—including communities like Rabaris, Meghwals, and Ahirs—has preserved techniques across generations. Geographic isolation historically protected and refined these crafts, making Kachch the epicenter of Indian textile and ceramic innovation.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kachch.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bhuj's Shrujan cluster and Kutch Museum to understand regional styles. Spend 2-3 weeks embedded with artisan communities in Ajrakhpur, Nirona, and Dhamadka villages. Network at KVIC-supported centers. Cost: ₹15,000-25,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Establish direct relationships with raw material suppliers in Bhuj textile market and Mundra pottery workshops. Source cotton yarn (₹100-150/kg), silk (₹400-600/kg), natural indigo (₹800-1,200/kg), mirrors (₹2-5/piece). First batch raw materials: ₹80,000-150,000 for mixed product range.
3
Produce / Partner
Register as artisan partner with KVIC or partner with 3-5 established cooperative groups (Shrujan, Kachch Crafts, Craft Link). Negotiate production timelines (expect 4-8 weeks for custom orders). Start with 200-500 pieces first batch. Establish quality control checkpoints with artisans.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, ₹0 cost, 10 mins). Apply for IEC code at nearest DGFT office (₹1,000-2,000). Register brand trademark with IP India (₹5,000-10,000). Open MSME account for GST (18% applicable). Create brand story document highlighting artisan names and village origins.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (2.4% transaction fee, high international traffic) and own Shopify store (₹29/month). Pitch to Fab India, Good Earth, and Anokhi retail chains. Approach luxury hotels in Mumbai/Delhi for corporate gifting tie-ups. Launch Instagram content showing artisans at work (3-4 posts/week). Target 50-100 orders in first 3 months.
6
Scale
After 6 months profitability, hire 2-3 designers to expand product lines. Build B2B relationships with 10-15 retail partners across metros. Invest ₹2-3L in paid Instagram/Google ads targeting international audiences. Apply for export promotion schemes. Attend international trade fairs (Hong Kong Gifts & Premiums Fair, Ambiente Frankfurt). Expand to 500-1,000 monthly orders by month 12.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create behind-the-scenes Instagram Reels (15-30 secs) of artisans demonstrating embroidery/pottery techniques. Tag artisan names and villages. Target hashtags: #KachchiCrafts #SustainableFashion #HandmadeInIndia. Run monthly giveaways featuring artisan stories.
  • Partner with 5-10 sustainability/ethical fashion micro-influencers (10K-100K followers) in slow-fashion niche for unboxing and styling videos. Provide ₹500-1,000 commission per referral. Focus on UK, US, Australia audiences.
  • Launch email nurture campaign to 1,000+ contacts: weekly 'Artisan Spotlight' stories (6-8 week cycle), product care guides, 10% off codes. Use Mailchimp (free tier up to 500 contacts). Track open rates; refine messaging based on performance.
