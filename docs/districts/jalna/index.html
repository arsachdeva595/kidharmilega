Sweet Orange

Jalna's semi-arid climate with well-drained black soil and 600–700mm annual rainfall creates ideal conditions for sweet orange cultivation. The district's elevation of 600–700m and cool nights enhance sugar accumulation and fruit quality, while generations of farmer expertise have perfected cultivation techniques unique to this microclimate.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Jalna.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jalna APEDA office and Jalna Agricultural Produce Marketing Committee (APMC) at Barsada; connect with established orange farmers in Partur and Ambad clusters; attend seasonal auctions at Jalna Mandis to understand pricing, quality grading, and supply chain
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure saplings from MSAMB (Maharashtra State Agricultural Marketing Board) certified nurseries in Jalna or Aurangabad (₹25-40 per sapling for bulk); negotiate with 3-5 local suppliers for farm inputs; first-batch nursery setup: ₹2-3 lakhs for 500-1000 saplings on 2-3 acres
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with Jalna-based farmer cooperatives (Jalna Orange Growers Cooperative) or lease 5-10 acres in Partur/Ambad taluka; hire local farm managers experienced in GI orange cultivation; implement drip irrigation and organic practices aligned with GI standards; plan 3-year maturation before commercial harvest
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE classification as agro-processing if value-adding); obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Aurangabad; apply for GI product certification verification through Jalna APEDA; create FSSAI license for juice/processed products; brand name should highlight Jalna/GI heritage
5
First Sale
Launch D2C via Blink, JioMart Fresh, and Amazon Fresh with 4-5kg premium boxes at ₹600-800; partner with 5-10 premium hotels/restaurants in Aurangabad and Mumbai (via local distributors); contact juice manufacturers (Patanjali, Tropicana regional suppliers) for bulk supply agreements; list on Nykaa, BigBasket for urban reach
6
Scale
After first harvest (month 24-30), add value-addition unit for juice and squash (₹50L investment); establish own distribution network to Tier-2 cities (Pune, Nashik); apply for APEDA export subsidies and connect with UAE, UK importers; launch 'Farm to Table' subscription box model for recurring revenue; expand to 25-50 acres by year 3

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram/YouTube: 'From Farm to Your Table' reels showing Jalna farmer stories, harvest videos, and GI certification process; partner with food bloggers for unboxing content; target metro food enthusiasts aged 25-45
  • B2B LinkedIn campaigns targeting juice companies, hotel procurement teams, and export businesses; emphasize GI certification and supply chain traceability; offer 5-10% bulk discounts for first orders
  • Hyperlocal Aurangabad/Jalna: Roadside billboards with 'Jalna Orange — Grown Here, Loved Everywhere'; sample distribution at mandis, markets, and farmer events; tie-up with local chai vendors for visibility
