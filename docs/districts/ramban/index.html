Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ramban.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ramban beekeeping clusters in Sumb and Gool villages; connect with Jammu & Kashmir Beekeepers Association; study GI certification standards; interact with existing honey producers near Samba and Kathua districts
2
Source Raw Materials
Source bee colonies from local apiaries in Ramban or neighboring districts (₹3,000-5,000 per colony); establish partnerships with 5-10 local beekeepers; first batch: 50-100 kg honey requires ₹80,000-150,000 including equipment and labor
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered beekeepers in Ramban; establish a honey collection and processing unit near Ramban town; install honey extraction, filtering, and bottling equipment; ensure compliance with Food Safety and Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) regulations
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) with district industrial office; apply for FSSAI license (₹5,000-10,000); obtain IEC code from nearest Customs office; create trademark and branding; obtain GI certification compliance documentation; register with local agricultural department
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart, and JioMart with GI-tagged branding; approach premium grocery chains in Srinagar, Jammu, and Delhi; contact online health platforms like Wellcurry and Organic Brands; offer samples to wellness influencers; participate in organic food expos
6
Scale
Expand to 5-10 apiaries by month 6; develop secondary products (honey lemon, honey with herbs); establish export partnerships with European distributors; open distribution in 50+ cities; build B2B relationships with restaurants and food manufacturers
