Banana

Goalpara's fertile alluvial soil from the Brahmaputra river system, combined with its humid subtropical climate and abundant water resources, creates ideal conditions for banana cultivation. The district has a decades-long tradition of banana farming with established farmer networks and local expertise in varietals suited to regional demand.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Goalpara.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Goalpara district banana clusters around Salmara, Lakhipur, and Dudhnoi; meet with Goalpara Banana Growers Association; study climate patterns, varietal preferences (Martaman, Elaichi), and current wholesale pricing at Assam Agricultural Produce Market Committee (AAPMC) auctions.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified banana saplings from Assam Agricultural University nursery (cost: ₹15-20 per sapling for 5000+ units) or local nurseries; negotiate bulk manure and fertilizer supply from agricultural input dealers in Goalpara town; budget ₹50,000-100,000 for first-batch inputs covering 0.5-1 hectare.
3
Produce / Partner
Lease 0.5-1 hectare agricultural land in Goalpara (₹20,000-30,000/year); establish partnership with 2-3 established banana farmers for technical mentorship; set up basic irrigation, mulching, and disease management protocols; target first harvest in 9-11 months.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under horticulture/agriculture classification online; apply for IEC code with nearest Export Promotion Council (EPC) in Guwahati; register brand name with Assam Department of Agriculture; obtain FSSAI license if pursuing value-added products.
5
First Sale
Launch on AgroConnect, Mandi.in, and local AAPMC auctions for wholesale; approach modern retail chains (Big Basket, Blink, local supermarkets) with product samples; leverage WhatsApp Business and Facebook groups targeting fruit traders and exporters in Guwahati; partner with 1-2 export agents for initial cross-border sales.
6
Scale
After 6 months, explore cold chain logistics partnerships for pan-India distribution; develop value-added product line (banana chips via co-packing); launch D2C website targeting Assam and North India; establish contracts with 2-3 food processors for bulk supply; increase cultivated area to 2-3 hectares via farmer collectives.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with regional e-commerce platforms (MilkBasket, Blinkit in Assam) to offer fresh Goalpara bananas with hyper-local positioning and sustainability messaging targeting urban professionals in Guwahati and Northeast metros.
  • Launch Instagram Reels and YouTube shorts showing farm-to-table journey, local farmer stories, and nutritional benefits; target food influencers and health-conscious segments in Northeast India with monthly engagement contests.
  • Attend agricultural export shows and APEDA-sponsored trade fairs in Kolkata, Delhi, and Guwahati; build direct B2B relationships with fruit importers in Bangladesh and Nepal through targeted outreach and sample distribution.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidized loan for setting up agricultural processing units with 25-35% government subsidy.
🏛
APEDA Market Access Initiative
up to ₹2 lakh subsidy for export promotion and participation in international agri-trade fairs.
🏚
Assam Agribusiness and Rural Transformation Scheme (AARTS)
financial support for farm mechanization, cold storage, and value addition for horticultural crops.
🏚
State Horticulture Mission Assam
subsidy on drip irrigation, mulching, and organic farming practices for banana cultivation; up to 50% cost support for farmers.
