ODOP Product

Silk and Silk products

Malda has a centuries-old tradition of tussar silk production with ideal agro-climatic conditions for mulberry and tropical tussar cultivation. The district hosts a large population of skilled tribal artisans with hereditary expertise in silk weaving, and local sericulture is deeply embedded in the regional economy, making it the natural hub for authentic tussar silk products.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Malda.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Malda silk clusters (Harendranath, Bamangola, Basantpur) and meet master weavers; attend Kolkata Textile Expo; study competitor collections on Etsy, Instagram, and Madurai/Varanasi silk platforms to understand pricing and design trends.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw tussar cocoons from Malda sericulture farms (₹80-120 per kg cocoons); establish supply agreements with 2-3 cocoon producers; budget ₹2-5 lakhs for first batch of 500 kg cocoons to yield 50-75 finished pieces.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established weaver cooperatives (e.g., Malda Weavers Association) or individual master weavers; negotiate terms (typically 40-50% of finished product cost for labor); start with 30-50 handloom units to manage quality and delivery timelines.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) and IEC for export; file trademark for brand name; obtain KVIC certification for handmade authenticity; ensure natural dye certifications (OEKO-TEX or similar) for premium positioning.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram shop and Shopify store with high-quality product photography and artisan storytelling; list on Etsy (strong international reach); approach luxury Indian e-commerce (Pernia's Pop-Up Shop, The Label Life); pitch to 20-30 boutiques in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with sampling.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand SKU range (home textiles—cushion covers, wall hangings); hire freelance marketing for Instagram/TikTok; explore wholesale partnerships with 10-15 new retailers per quarter; launch seasonal collections tied to festivals; consider pop-up shows in metros.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels featuring artisan weavers at looms with 15-20 sec clips on product creation; use hashtags #MaldaSilk #TussarWeaving #SlowFashion to build organic reach.
  • Partner with 5-10 micro-influencers (10K-100K followers) in sustainable fashion and traditional textiles for gifting and reviews; target 2-3 posts per month.
  • Launch a 'Weaver Stories' blog/YouTube series profiling artisans and their families; embed product links and offer 10% discount codes to viewers—drives SEO and emotional brand loyalty.
