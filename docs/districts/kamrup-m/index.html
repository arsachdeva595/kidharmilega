ODOP Product

Home Furnishing made of Eri , Muga & Assam Silk, etc.

Kamrup has a centuries-old sericulture tradition with abundant mulberry and castor plants ideal for Eri and Muga silk production. The district is home to skilled silk weavers who have perfected traditional techniques, and the unique tropical climate supports silk cocoon cultivation. Government support and established supply chains make it the natural hub for GI-certified Assam silk furnishings.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kamrup (M).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Guwahati's Sualkuchi weaving cluster (20km from city) and meet 5-7 weavers; attend Assam Silk Festival in Jan-Feb; connect with Assam Textile Association for market insights and GI producer networks
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact sericulture farms in Kamrup (Borsil, Nalbari areas); bulk order 50kg raw silk yarn (₹40-60/gram) from established suppliers like Assam Silk Board vendors; first batch cost ₹1.5-2.5L for 500m furnishing length inventory
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 registered weavers in Sualkuchi cluster (average cost ₹80-150 per meter for labor); sign MoU with ASDC (Assam Sericulture Development Corporation) for consistent supply; alternatively, start with co-working with established weaver cooperatives on revenue-sharing model
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry of MSME portal) as Textile manufacturing micro-enterprise; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office; file GI producer certificate application at IP India; register brand name with TMO and design copyrights with IGRCCR Kolkata
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Flipkart (luxury home segment); approach 20-30 interior design studios in Bangalore, Mumbai, Delhi via LinkedIn with product samples; cold-contact 10-15 luxury 5-star hotels and resorts for bulk orders; build Instagram content around weaver stories and process videos
6
Scale
Establish retail tie-ups with 10+ premium home décor chains (Fabindia, Cottage Emporium); hire 2-3 sales reps for B2B account management; expand weaver network to 15-20 artisans; develop 3-4 new product lines (table linens, wall hangings); target export markets via trade shows (Heimtextil Frankfurt, Maison & Objet Paris)

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create YouTube documentary-style series (10-15 min) featuring individual weavers' stories and traditional dyeing processes; publish monthly on brand channel and partner creator channels to build authenticity and drive 15-20% monthly engagement
  • Partner with 15-20 interior designers on Instagram reels showing before-after room transformations using your silk furnishings; offer 10% commission per sale; target reach 50K+ monthly impressions in metro markets
  • Sponsor 3-4 luxury lifestyle events and design festivals (Design India Fair, Delhi Design Week) with exhibition booth and live weaving demonstrations; collect 200+ qualified B2B leads per event
