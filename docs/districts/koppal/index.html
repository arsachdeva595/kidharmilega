ODOP Product

Kinhal Toys

Koppal has a centuries-old tradition of wooden toy craftsmanship centered in Kinhal village, with artisan communities skilled in intricate carving and natural dyeing. The district's access to locally sourced timber and proximity to natural dye sources makes it ideally positioned for sustainable toy production. Deep cultural roots and established supply chains of raw materials ensure consistent quality and authenticity.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Koppal.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kinhal village in Koppal district to spend 2-3 days with established artisans; study the craft techniques and visit the Koppal ODOP cluster office. Connect with Craft Council of India's Karnataka chapter for heritage documentation and GI compliance details.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source wood from licensed timber merchants in Belgaum or Hubli (₹8,000-15,000 for first batch of 100 kg). Source natural dyes from organic suppliers in Bengaluru or directly from farmers in Deccan region (₹3,000-5,000 for 50 kg mixed dyes). Total first-batch raw material cost: ₹20,000-25,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 2-3 established Kinhal artisans in the village through a revenue-share model (40-50% to artisans) or establish a small workshop with 4-5 trained craftspeople. Begin with batch production of 200-300 pieces monthly. Document production process for GI certification compliance.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (Micro-enterprise) on udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC code from DGFT for export readiness. Obtain GI certification endorsement from DC Handicraft Ministry. Register brand name at trademarkia.com. Create product certification for natural dyes and eco-friendly materials.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (15% fees), Amazon Handmade (8% fees), Flipkart Handicrafts; build Instagram presence with behind-the-scenes artisan content; approach boutique gift retailers in Bangalore and Mumbai; offer pre-order campaigns highlighting GI status and eco-friendly narrative.
6
Scale
After 6 months, scale to 500-800 pieces monthly; expand artisan partnerships to 8-10 craftspeople; develop 5-6 new product designs; launch corporate gifting program; approach international craft fairs and establish distributor relationships in 2-3 export countries.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showing 30-second artisan craft-making videos with GI tag and eco-story; run carousel ads targeting parents aged 28-45 interested in sustainable toys; collaborate with eco-parenting influencers (50K-200K followers).
  • Partner with gift platforms (FabFitFun, Greetabl) to include Kinhal toys in curated sustainability boxes; target corporate CSR buyers with customized bulk orders and heritage story packaging.
  • Launch YouTube channel documenting the Kinhal craft heritage and artisan stories; optimize for search terms 'eco-friendly wooden toys India,' 'sustainable handmade toys,' 'GI toys'; drive traffic to Shopify store.
