Amravati has a 100+ year legacy of textile manufacturing with established mills, skilled labor force, and robust supply chain infrastructure. Existing textile clusters like Hinganghat provide ready access to machinery, expertise, and buyer networks.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Amravati.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Hinganghat textile cluster (30km from Amravati city) to study existing mills, meet manufacturers at Amravati Chamber of Commerce, and attend TextileWeek showcases. Interview 5-10 mill owners to understand production economics, margins, and buyer networks. Research competing products on TEXPROCIL database.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton from Maharashtra Cotton Federation or Gujarat suppliers (approx ₹80-100/kg). Procure synthetic fibers from Mumbai textile material markets or Surat bulk suppliers (₹150-200/kg). First batch cost for 5 tons mixed materials: ₹7-10 lakhs. Establish relationships with Amravati-based dye houses for chemical supplies.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing Hinganghat mills offering spare loom capacity (₹500-800 per loom per day), or lease aging machinery from mill auctions at ₹3-5 lakhs. Alternatively, contract with 2-3 established mills in Amravati as co-manufacturers with 30% markup on their base cost. Start with 2-3 loom partnerships before investing in owned machinery.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME portal) as micro-enterprise. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office (Amravati). Register brand name with Amravati District Industries Center (DIC). Obtain BIS certification for cotton fabrics and pollution control board clearance. Set up GST registration with Amravati tax office.
5
First Sale
Launch B2B presence on 1) IndiaMART (create catalog of 20+ products, offer ₹500+ discounts for bulk orders), 2) TradeKey (focus on export inquiries), 3) Direct outreach to 50 garment factories in Tamil Nadu, Gujarat using LinkedIn Sales Navigator. Attend TEXFAIR Surat (quarterly) to meet 200+ bulk buyers. Offer 7% discount for first bulk order, net 30 payment terms.
6
Scale
After ₹20L revenue: Invest in second batch of owned looms (₹10L), hire 2 quality supervisors, expand product range to 40+ SKUs. Launch D2C website with Shopify (₹1000/month) targeting premium home furnishing segment at 60% margin. Apply for bank credit under MSME loans to fund working capital. Establish export partnerships with agents in Dubai and Dhaka at 10% commission.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 15-second Instagram Reels showing yarn-to-fabric production process, posted 3x weekly, targeting garment manufacturers with hashtags #TextileManufacturer #BulkFabric #MadeInIndia — aim for 500 monthly inquiries.
  • Launch WhatsApp broadcast group of 200 garment factory owners in Tamil Nadu and Gujarat with weekly product updates, bulk discounts, and industry news. Convert 15-20% to monthly repeat orders.
  • Sponsor booth at India Tex 2024 (Feb, Delhi) and Texfair Surat (quarterly) with 3 colleagues, distribute 1000 product samples, collect 100+ qualified B2B leads, convert 10% to ₹2L+ first orders.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for textile manufacturing micro-enterprises with 35% subsidy on capital investment.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹5-10Cr cluster development fund for Hinganghat textile cluster with machinery upgradation grants.
🏚
Maharashtra Enterprise Promotion Scheme
5% interest subvention on bank loans up to ₹5Cr for textile MSME units registered in Amravati district.
🏚
Technology Upgradation Fund Scheme (TUFS)
central government support for machinery upgradation, up to 50% subsidy for modernizing looms and dyeing units.
