ODOP Product

Custard Apple

West Singhbhum has ideal agro-climatic conditions with warm temperatures, moderate rainfall, and well-drained laterite soil that custard apples thrive in. The region's established farming community and decades of cultivation experience make it a natural hub for quality Sitaphal production. Proximity to major markets like Kolkata and well-connected transport routes enable efficient distribution.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for West Singhbhum (chaibassa).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chaibassa and Porahat blocks in West Singhbhum to study existing custard apple orchards; connect with ICAR-RCER (Indian Council of Agricultural Research - Regional Centre) Chaibassa for technical training; attend state agricultural melas to understand best practices and market dynamics.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified custard apple grafted saplings from ICAR stations in Jharkhand (Ranchi) or from government nurseries in West Singhbhum; budget ₹15,000-25,000 for 50 saplings; arrange vermicompost from local producers (₹3,000-5,000 for initial 2 months supply).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established custard apple farmers in Chaibassa or Porahat for land-lease arrangements (₹2-4 lakhs per acre annually); install drip irrigation systems (₹60,000-80,000 per acre); follow organic certification pathway (NPOP) through local ORGANIC certification bodies for premium pricing.
4
Brand & Register
Register business as Proprietorship under Udyam portal (udyamregistration.gov.in); obtain FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) registration to access subsidies; apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT if exporting; register trademark for brand name with IP India.
5
First Sale
Launch on Blinkit, JioMart, and local BigBasket supply chains with ₹5-10 per kg wholesale margin; create Instagram/Facebook presence with farm visuals and customer testimonials; contact 3-4 premium restaurants in Ranchi and Jamshedpur for bulk contracts; register with APEDA for export certification.
6
Scale
Expand to 3-5 acres by month 12; develop value-added product line (pulp, jam, dried chips) to capture 20-30% price premium; establish forward contracts with 2-3 exporters; build agri-tourism module (orchard visits, farm stays) for 15-20% additional revenue; target ₹30-40 lakh annual turnover by year 2.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with premium fruit delivery apps (Blinkit, Zepto) to offer 'Chaibassa Sitaphal' as a specialty product with farm-origin storytelling; run geo-targeted ads in Ranchi, Jamshedpur, and Kolkata.
  • Create YouTube series 'From Chaibassa to Your Table' documenting orchard-to-delivery journey; partner with micro-influencers (50k-500k followers) focused on organic/healthy living.
  • Host tasting events at premium restaurants and hotels in tier-1 cities; develop B2B relationships with dessert makers, juice bars, and premium bakeries offering bulk contracts at ₹80-100/kg.
