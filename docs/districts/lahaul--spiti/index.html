ODOP Product

Processing of Seabuckthorn (Charma)

Lahaul & Spiti's extreme altitude, cold desert climate, and pristine ecosystem create ideal conditions for seabuckthorn cultivation with naturally high nutrient density. The district has established seabuckthorn plantations across 1,200+ hectares managed by HIMCOSTE and local farmers, creating consistent raw material availability. The region's isolation and organic farming practices ensure premium quality products with strong health-conscious market positioning.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Lahaul & Spiti.

1
Learn / Research
Visit HIMCOSTE (Himachal Institute of Cold Desert Studies and Technology) in Leh/Keylong to understand cultivation practices and access farmer networks. Study processed product market at health stores in Bangalore, Delhi NCR (Apollo Pharmacy, Nature's Basket). Analyze competitors like Himalayan Organics, Mediker on Amazon and Nykaa. Cost: ₹25,000-40,000 for 2-week field research trip.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contract with HIMCOSTE-registered seabuckthorn growers in Keylong or Sarchu block (₹80-120/kg for fresh berries, seasonal July-September). First batch (500kg berries) costs ₹40,000-60,000. Identify oil extraction partner through District Industries Center (DIC) Lahaul & Spiti or engage mobile processing units. Capsule sourcing from Bangalore suppliers (₹8-12 per capsule). First inventory cost: ₹3,00,000-5,00,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with HIMCOSTE-certified processing units in Keylong or Leh for juice/oil extraction (they have cold-press machinery). Outsource capsule filling to contract manufacturers in Himachal (Baddi or Nalagarh). Alternatively, set up small batch juice production with 500L capacity (₹15-20L investment). Begin with 2,000 units/month production targeting ₹8-12L revenue by month 3.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) within 5 minutes on udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC code via ICEGATE/DGFT for exports. Trademark brand name via ipindia.gov.in (₹10,000). Get Food Safety License from FSSAI (₹3,000-5,000, 20-30 days). Partner with Himachal Pradesh Export Promotion Council for export documentation. Obtain organic certification from APEDA-accredited agency (₹20,000-30,000 for 6-month audit).
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon (seller account + ₹500/month + 15% commission), Flipkart, and Nykaa (health & wellness sections). Create Instagram/Facebook with 'Himalayan superfruit' positioning (₹2,000/month content budget). Engage with Ayurveda wellness influencers on YouTube (1-2 affiliate deals, ₹50,000 budget). Email health bloggers for reviews. Approach BigBasket and Grofers for bulk orders. First month target: 500 units across channels.
6
Scale
By month 6, expand to 5,000 units/month through B2B partnerships with wellness chains (Patanjali retail partners, Yoga studios, gym chains). Launch export channel via Alibaba and German health distributor networks (₹50,000 export packaging investment). Develop private-label capsules for Ayurvedic brands (30% margin improvement). Hire 2-3 field coordinators to manage farmer contracts directly. Target ₹50-75L annual revenue by month 12.
