Turmeric

Shahdol's black soil, moderate rainfall, and semi-humid subtropical climate create ideal conditions for high-curcumin turmeric cultivation. The district has a centuries-old tradition of turmeric farming with established farmer networks and processing infrastructure. Local expertise in organic farming practices ensures premium quality suited for both domestic culinary and international pharmaceutical markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Shahdol.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Shahdol's farming clusters in Jaitpur and Raigaon blocks; connect with Shahdol District Agricultural Department; attend Indore Spice Market (IMCL) auctions; study competitor pricing at Sanwer turmeric market, 150km away.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source seed turmeric from local farmer cooperatives or Bhadbhadi village (turmeric hub); expect ₹50,000–₹100,000 for 1,000 kg first batch; establish contracts directly with 3-5 progressive farmers for consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing turmeric drying units in Shahdol town (30-40 such units operational); or lease 1,000 sqft dry-cure facility for ₹10,000–₹15,000/month; hire local processing labor at ₹300–₹400/day; start with curing, drying, and powder production.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) as agricultural processing unit; obtain IEC code for export; get FSSAI registration for processed turmeric; design packaging with curcumin percentage claim (lab-certified); register brand with IP office.
5
First Sale
List on Udaan and IndiaMART targeting bulk food/supplement buyers; reach spice traders via Indore Spice Market; approach local organic retailers (Bhadbhadi district cooperatives); launch WhatsApp-based bulk B2B sales to food manufacturers in Gujarat and Maharashtra.
6
Scale
Expand to certified organic cultivation (₹1–₁.5L per acre subsidy under state scheme); establish cold-storage facility for year-round supply; launch D2C brand on Amazon/Flipkart/own website; target European organic certifications (EU-compliant) for premium export pricing; collaborate with 10+ farmers via contract farming.
