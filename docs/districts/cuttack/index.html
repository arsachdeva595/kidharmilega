Silver Filigree (Tarakashi Art)

Cuttack has been the filigree capital of India for over 500 years with hereditary artisan communities possessing unparalleled skill in wire-work precision. The district's geographic proximity to raw silver markets, established export networks, and deep cultural heritage make it the only authentic source for genuine Tarakashi art. Government support through handicraft ministry and established artisan clusters provide infrastructure and mentorship unavailable elsewhere.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Cuttack.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Cuttack's Tarakashi artisan clusters (Raghurajpur & Barabati Fort area), spend 2-3 weeks learning from master craftsmen; connect with Cuttack Chamber of Commerce and DC Handicraft office for market intelligence; study competitors on Saangi, Craftsvilla, and Amazon Artisan Collective
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 99.9% pure silver wire and sheets from Zaveri Bazaar (Mumbai) or local Cuttack silver suppliers (₹40,000-60,000 for first batch of 5kg); purchase traditional tools from local blacksmiths in Cuttack (₹8,000-12,000); establish credit account with 2-3 suppliers for consistent raw material flow
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established Tarakashi artisans in Cuttack's workshops (standard revenue share: 30-40% to artisans); or apprentice under a master craftsman for 3-6 months to gain direct production capability; register your workshop with DC Handicraft for quality certification and GI tag licensing
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME registration online); apply for IEC code via DGFT for export readiness; register brand name with Registrar of Companies; obtain GI certification license from DC Handicraft Ministry (mandatory for authenticity); get BIS certification for jewelry quality if targeting high-end retail
5
First Sale
Launch on Saangi.com and Craftsvilla (ODOP-dedicated platforms); list on Etsy and Luxury jewelry marketplaces; approach luxury hotel chains (Taj, ITC) for gift shop placements; use Instagram Reels and TikTok to showcase artisan-making videos; reach out to wedding planners for custom orders; attend Buyer-Seller meets organized by DC Handicraft
6
Scale
Expand artisan network from 2-3 to 8-10 skilled craftsmen; launch B2B export program targeting luxury retailers in USA/Germany; develop sub-product lines (temple jewelry, fusion pieces); secure PMEGP/SFURTI grant for larger workshop setup; hire content creator for luxury brand storytelling; establish retail showroom in tourist zones (Bhubaneswar/Cuttack railway station area)
