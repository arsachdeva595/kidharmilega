Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Vadodara.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Vadodara's pharma clusters around Makarpura industrial area and Waghodia to understand production standards. Connect with Gujarat Pharmaceutical Association and study WHO-GMP, ISO 13485, and ISO 9001 requirements. Attend pharma exhibitions at GUJPHARMA or Pharma Tech India conferences.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source APIs and excipients from established suppliers in Vadodara (Makarpura area), Ahmedabad, or CHEMEXCELS clusters. First batch of 100,000 tablets/capsules costs approximately ₹8-15 lakhs depending on formulation. Negotiate 30-45 day payment terms with 2-3 suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish manufacturing facility with WHO-GMP compliance (₹20-30 Cr investment) OR partner with contract manufacturers in Vadodara like Echuka Pharmaceuticals, Intas, or Cadila for low-volume production (1000-10000 units monthly at ₹2-4 per unit). File manufacturing license with Gujarat Food and Drug Administration (GFDA).
4
Brand & Register
Register business as Udyam (MSME), obtain PAN and IEC for exports. Register brand name at Intellectual Property Office. Secure pharma licenses: Manufacturing License (Form 18), Drug License (Form 20), GST registration (5% for pharma). Budget ₹50,000-2,00,000 for complete regulatory filing.
5
First Sale
List on Practo Plus, Netmeds, Apollo Pharmacy, and Jiva platforms for B2C. Approach Pharmaswipe, IQVIA, and Alkem for B2B distributor networks. Start with government tenders via GeM portal. Build direct relationships with 50-100 local pharmacies in Vadodara-Ahmedabad region for initial ₹20-30 lakh revenue.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range from 3-5 SKUs to 15-20 SKUs. Obtain ISO certifications and WHO-GMP pre-qualification. Launch export sales to SAARC countries, then US/EU markets. Invest in digital marketing budget of ₹5-10 lakhs monthly. Consider capacity expansion to 10-20 million units annually.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with healthcare influencers and doctors on YouTube/Instagram to create product education content focusing on quality standards and manufacturing transparency
  • Run targeted LinkedIn campaigns to hospital procurement departments and pharmacy chains in tier-1 and tier-2 cities showcasing certifications, quality metrics, and cost advantages
  • Sponsor healthcare conferences and trade shows like Pharma Tech India, Gujarat Pharma Week to build B2B relationships and showcase products to distributors
