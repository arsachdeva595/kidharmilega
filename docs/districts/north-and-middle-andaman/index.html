ODOP Product

Fisheries/Marine products

North and Middle Andaman islands are surrounded by deep-water fishing zones with abundant fish, shellfish, and crustacean populations. The district's geographic isolation ensures pristine marine ecosystems and minimal pollution, making it ideal for producing premium quality seafood. Direct access to major ocean currents and coral reef ecosystems supports diverse fishery operations.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for North and Middle Andaman.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Port Blair fish market, connect with existing fishing cooperative societies, attend Marine Products Export Development Authority (MPEDA) workshops in Andaman; study cold-chain logistics and export compliance at APEDA or MPEDA offices
2
Source Raw Materials
Source directly from local fishing trawlers at Port Blair harbor (negotiate bulk rates), establish contracts with licensed fishermen associations; first batch: ₹2-4 lakhs for mixed seafood stock (100-200 kg variety)
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing fish processing units in Port Blair/Diglipur or set up own small processing unit with FSSAI-approved cold storage; hire trained fish handlers; establish relationships with daily catch suppliers
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise), obtain IEC code from APEDA for exports, get FSSAI food license, register trademark on IPIND; consider brand names emphasizing island origin and freshness
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, Local Andaman e-commerce platforms; pitch to seafood wholesalers via JustDial and B2B portals; directly approach 4-5 star hotels and restaurants in Port Blair for bulk supply contracts
6
Scale
Expand cold-chain infrastructure, diversify into 3-4 processed products (dried fish, fish oil), establish export documentation partnerships, scale to Mumbai/Chennai distribution hubs, launch brand on national D2C platforms

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram reels showcasing daily fishing process and ocean-to-table freshness; partner with 5-10 micro-influencers in food/travel niche targeting urban coastal city audiences
  • B2B LinkedIn outreach to hotel procurement teams and export companies; create case studies of successful exports to Southeast Asia highlighting premium quality and certification
  • Local PR in Port Blair newspapers and tourism magazines emphasizing island origin story; sponsor seafood festivals and food events in Andaman to build brand awareness
