ODOP Product

Bhagalpur Silk

Bhagalpur has ideal agro-climatic conditions for mulberry cultivation and sericulture. The region has a 2000-year-old silk weaving tradition with deep expertise in traditional techniques and a concentrated cluster of skilled artisans. The district's geographic proximity to raw material sources and established export networks make it the natural hub for premium silk production.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bhagalpur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bhagalpur silk cluster (Weavers' Colony, Ramgarh, Sujapur areas). Meet 5-10 established weavers and traders at Bhagalpur Silk Board office. Understand pricing, quality grades, production timelines, and supplier networks. Attend a sericulture workshop at CSRTI (Central Silk Technological Research Institute) to learn raw material sourcing.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mulberry silk cocoons from local sericulturists in Bhagalpur or Bihta region (approximately ₹3,000-5,000 per kg). First batch: 50 kg cocoons (₹150,000-250,000). Partner with Bhagalpur Silk Board for vetted supplier introductions. Budget additional ₹100,000 for silk yarn procurement.
3
Produce / Partner
Identify 3-5 master weavers in Ramgarh or Weavers' Colony through Bhagalpur Chamber of Commerce. Negotiate per-piece or per-loom rental (₹50-100 per saree per month). Start with 20 pieces/month. Establish clear design specifications, quality checks, and payment schedules. Consider partnership with SFURTI cluster for shared infrastructure and quality certification.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise) with state; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Patna. Obtain GI certification from DC Handloom using Bhagalpur Silk Producers Association. File FSSAI if adding value-added products. Create brand identity with Sanskrit/Hindi nomenclature reflecting heritage.
5
First Sale
List on Marut Suzuki Premium Bazaar, The Jute Shop, and Sarangi (heritage marketplace) within month 1. Launch Instagram Reels showcasing weaver stories and process videos. Pitch to luxury hotel chains (Taj, Oberoi) in Delhi/Mumbai for corporate gifting. Attend trade fairs: Handmade India, Anurupa Craft Fair (Delhi), IITF.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 50 pieces/month by partnering with 8-10 weavers. Invest ₹300,000 in in-house quality lab and packaging. Approach luxury e-commerce (LBB, Elililly, Aza Fashions). Export via silk aggregators or direct B2B outreach. Open pop-up stores in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram storytelling — feature 1 weaver per week with behind-the-scenes Reels of dyeing, weaving, and finishing. Target hashtags: #BhagalpurSilk #SlowFashion #ArtisanCraft. Collaborate with 5 micro-influencers (50k-200k followers) in sustainable fashion.
  • LinkedIn B2B outreach — showcase product ranges, MOQs, and export certifications to luxury fashion houses, department stores, and corporate gifting teams. Host quarterly webinars on silk sourcing and sustainability.
  • WhatsApp Business + Telegram communities for VIP customers — offer early access to new designs, limited editions, and bulk discounts. Monthly lookbooks and design previews drive repeat orders.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
Up to ₹15,000 grant + 5% interest subvention on loans up to ₹1L for registered artisans and micro-entrepreneurs.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Up to ₹25L subsidy (25% for general category) for setting up handloom micro-units in backward areas.
🏚
Bihar State Handloom Policy 2023
10% subsidy on machinery, 5% on raw materials, and marketing support for GI-certified products.
🏚
Mukhya Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana
Free training in silk dyeing, weaving, and design for unemployed youth; wage subsidies for 1 year post-placement.
