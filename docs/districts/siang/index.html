ODOP Product

Large Cardamom

Siang's elevation (1200-2500m), high rainfall, and cool misty climate create ideal conditions for large cardamom cultivation. The region's biodiversity-rich ecosystem and traditional farming practices by indigenous Adi communities ensure sustainable production of premium-quality cardamom with distinctive aromatic properties.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Siang.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Siang cardamom-growing villages (Boleng, Kardi) and connect with farmer cooperatives; study cultivation at Central Institute of Spices Research, Calicut; attend spice exporter meetings in Kochi spice markets
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 5-10 kg dried cardamom from Siang farmer groups (₹15,000-25,000 per kg for raw material); establish direct relationships with 3-5 farming families; first batch procurement cost: ₹75,000-150,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing Siang farming cooperatives or lease 0.5-1 hectare hillside land; hire local farmers for cultivation and harvesting; invest in small drying unit (₹200,000-400,000) for value addition; initial production timeline 18-20 months from planting to harvest
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) for ₹0; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Itanagar; obtain organic certification from APEDA-recognized agency (₹50,000-100,000); register trademark with IP office; get FSSAI license for value-added products
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, Flipkart Fresh, JioMart with minimum 50kg stock; contact spice exporters in Kochi, Cochin, and Mumbai directly; pitch to specialty retailers (Godrej Nature's Basket, Modern Bazaar); create Instagram/YouTube content on farm-to-table journey; partner with premium gift brands for bulk orders
6
Scale
Secure ₹20-40L working capital loan via MUDRA/PMEGP for capacity expansion; establish processing unit for value-added products; develop B2B relationships with 5-10 Ayurvedic companies; expand to 5-10 farmer partnerships; launch private label for bulk buyers; attend international spice trade fairs (GwFeed, Anuga)
