ODOP Product

Pochampalli Ikat

Yadadri Bhuvanagiri has a 400+ year weaving heritage rooted in the Pochampalli village cluster, where master weavers have perfected the tie-dye Ikat technique. The district's access to quality cotton, traditional dyeing expertise, and established artisan networks make it the natural epicenter for authentic Ikat production. The region benefits from established supply chains and a reputation that commands premium pricing in global fashion markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Yadadri Bhuvanagiri.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pochampalli village cluster (30km from Hyderabad) and Bhongir town; connect with weavers' cooperatives like Pochampalli Weavers Cooperative Society; attend local handloom exhibitions; study competitor brands on Etsy and LBB (Little Black Book)
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton yarn from Telangana Cotton Cooperative or Hyderabad's Himayatnagar market (₹40-60/kg); natural dyes from Jaipur or local suppliers (₹800-1500/kg); contact Pochampalli dyers directly for bulk orders; first batch of 50 sarees requires ₹80,000-150,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with master weavers in Pochampalli village through cooperatives (contract weaving at ₹400-800/saree); visit NIFT or local design schools for pattern innovation; establish relationship with 2-3 weaver families for consistent production; consider co-working space in Hyderabad for branding and fulfillment
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry of MSME portal); obtain IEC code from DGFT for exports; register trademark with IP India; GST registration (5% slab for handlooms); get DC Handloom certification from Telangana Handlooms Department; establish brand identity with logo and packaging
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (2.75% + $0.20 fees), Ajio, Myntra, and own Shopify store; approach boutiques in Hyderabad (Banjara Hills, Jubilee Hills); pitch to ethical fashion aggregators like Ekaya, Karigari, and StyleCraze; create Instagram content showing weaver stories; collaborate with fashion influencers in sustainable fashion niche
6
Scale
Expand to 5-10 weaver partnerships; introduce new designs (dupattas, dress materials, home décor); launch seasonal collections; apply for export subsidies through APEDA; participate in International Handloom Expo and Global Buyer-Seller Meets; establish a physical showroom in Hyderabad; develop wholesale distributor network in metros
