ODOP Product

Orange

East Kameng's high altitude (800-1200m), cool climate, and well-drained soil create ideal conditions for cultivating sweet, vitamin-rich oranges. The region's minimal chemical pollution and traditional farming practices ensure premium quality fruit. Centuries of cultivation have perfected local techniques for disease-resistant, high-yield orange varieties.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for East Kameng.

1
Learn / Research
Visit East Kameng orange clusters in Seppa and Roing; connect with District Agriculture Office in Seppa; shadow 2-3 established growers (Sept-Jan harvest season); study GI certification requirements from Arunachal Pradesh government
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified saplings from Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Department nurseries (₹30-40 per sapling). Negotiate land lease in Seppa/Roing belt at ₹5,000-8,000/acre/season or contract-farm with existing farmers. Initial ₹2-3L for 2 acres setup including soil preparation, drip irrigation
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 8-10 smallholder farmers in East Kameng on contract-farming model (you provide inputs, they provide land/labor, profit-share 60:40). Establish cold storage unit (₹50-80L) in Seppa town for post-harvest management. Hire local seasonal laborers during October-November harvest
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online via udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC code through DGFT portal; register brand name at Chandigarh IP Office; obtain GI certification recognition from Ministry of Agriculture (coordinate via Arunachal Pradesh); get FSSAI license for processed products if planning juice/concentrate
5
First Sale
Week 1-4: Sell fresh oranges to Azadpur Mandi Delhi and APMC Mumbai wholesalers (₹40-60/kg). Week 5-8: Launch Instagram/Facebook account with farm photos, testimonials; contact JioMart, Amazon Fresh, Blinkit vendors in Delhi with samples. Week 9-12: Approach 15-20 premium restaurants in Guwahati, Assam (nearby market) with B2B pricing
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 5-8 acres, integrate cold chain logistics with Arunachal Pradesh government support. Launch packaged juice line under brand name (₹30-50L investment). Build direct B2B contracts with 3-4 major juice companies. Explore export to SAARC countries via APEDA registration; develop agritourism component in peak season
