Agarwood & Its Derivatives

Golaghat's tropical monsoon climate, rich forest biodiversity, and centuries-old agarwood cultivation tradition make it naturally suited for producing premium agarwood. The district has established supply chains and expertise in agarwood processing, with local artisans skilled in oil extraction and derivative creation.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Golaghat.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Golaghat's Agarwood Cluster in Kaziranga zone; connect with Assam Agarwood Producers Association; study operations at Bokakhat agarwood cooperative units; attend international fragrance trade shows (IIFF Dubai, Grasse International).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source wild-harvested or plantation agarwood from licensed collectors in Golaghat district (₹2-5L for 10kg premium grade); establish relationships with local cooperative societies; first batch cost ₹5-15L depending on quality grade.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established distilleries in Golaghat town (Bokakhat area has 20+ operational units) for oil extraction; or invest in small-scale distillation setup (₹15-30L); engage local artisans for resin processing and derivative creation.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (MSME portal); obtain IEC code from DGFT for export; apply for Agmark certification for quality assurance; trademark brand name with IP office; get FSSAI license if entering cosmetics/medicine.
5
First Sale
List on Global Trade platforms (Global Sources, Alibaba); pitch to international fragrance distributors via LinkedIn; launch D2C on Nykaa, Amazon Luxury Stores; partner with high-end boutique perfume retailers; attend Grasse and Middle East fragrance B2B trade shows.
6
Scale
Build private label partnerships with luxury brands; expand product line to agarwood skincare and wellness; establish certified plantation partnerships for sustainable supply chain; open flagship retail stores in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore); hire export specialists and hire regional distributors in Middle East and Europe.
