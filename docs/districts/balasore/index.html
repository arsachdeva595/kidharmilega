Marine Products

Balasore boasts 80+ km of pristine Arabian Sea coastline with a 500-year fishing heritage, making it Odisha's leading marine products hub. The district's traditional fishing communities, modern processing infrastructure, and proximity to export hubs like Paradip Port provide unmatched supply chain advantages. Cold storage facilities and APEDA-registered seafood units ensure consistent quality for domestic retail and global export.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Balasore.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Balasore fish markets (Balasore Port, Chandipur Landing Center) to understand catch quality, seasonal supply, and pricing. Network with 20+ existing APEDA-registered exporters in Balasore; study their processing models. Spend 2 weeks at Paradip Port logistics hub to understand export procedures.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw fish/shrimp directly from Balasore Port auctions (opens 4 AM daily) at ₹150-400/kg depending on species. Negotiate 10-15 tons first-batch supply from 3-4 boat operators. First-batch raw cost: ₹15-20L. Establish credit lines with ice and salt suppliers near the port.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing APEDA-certified processing units in Balasore (capacity: 50-500 tons/month) under co-packing model at ₹20-35/kg processing fee. Alternatively, lease cold storage + processing shed near Balasore Port (₹2-4L/month). Hire 10-15 skilled seafood processors and quality checkers from local fishing communities.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE category) and obtain IEC code from DGFT Kolkata office within 7 days. Apply for FSSAI license (Marine Products category) — processing unit inspection takes 15-20 days. Secure APEDA registration for export eligibility. Create brand with coastal/fishery imagery; file trademark via IP India.
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket B2B and Blinkit Fresh within 30 days. Approach 15-20 Modern Trade chains (Reliance Fresh, D-Mart, Spencer's) with samples and APEDA cert. Register on Seafood Exim portal for export inquiries. Engage 3-4 seafood export traders in Mumbai/Chennai to access international buyers.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand SKU range to 8-10 products (frozen shrimp, fish fillets, value-added items). Secure 2-3 modern cold storage franchises in Odisha metros (Bhubaneswar, Cuttack). Launch own D2C brand website with subscription model. Target ₹5-10Cr revenue by month 18 via multi-channel strategy.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels campaign: 15-second farm-to-table videos showing Balasore fishermen, landing, processing, packaging. Target urban 25-45 audience with educational content on seafood freshness indicators. Collaborate with 10 micro-influencers (50K-500K followers) in metros.
  • Partnership with 5-star hotel chefs: Host 'Balasore Seafood Tasting' events in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore. Provide chef-curated seafood bundles. Generate PR through food/lifestyle media. Convert hotel demand into bulk B2B orders.
  • YouTube channel: 'Balasore Fishing Stories' — weekly 8-10 min documentaries on fishing traditions, sustainability, recipes. Monetize via AdSense + embedded affiliate e-com links. Target 50K subscribers in 6 months, drive 15-20% traffic to e-commerce.
