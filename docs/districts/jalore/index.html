Granite Products

Jalore sits atop vast granite deposits with naturally occurring mineral-rich formations that yield some of India's finest granite. The district has a long tradition of stone quarrying and artisanal craft, with skilled labor and established supply chains supporting large-scale production and export.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Jalore.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jalore's granite clusters around Bhimarlai, Bhadrwah, and Sumerpur; interview 5+ established granite producers; study pricing on Alibaba, TradeKey, and ArchiPro; attend GEM & Stone India Expo in Jaipur to understand market dynamics.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact Jalore Granite Producers Association or visit mines near Bhimarlai for block sourcing (₹2-4 lakh per batch of 10-15 blocks); secure polishing powder and abrasives from Ludhiana suppliers (₹50,000 for first stock); negotiate 30-day payment terms with 2-3 suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisan workshops in Jalore town offering semi-mechanized cutting and polishing (revenue share 10-15% or fixed ₹1-1.5L monthly); alternatively invest ₹15-20L in basic CNC cutting and polishing equipment; hire 8-12 skilled craftsmen locally.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) immediately; apply for IEC code from DGFT Jaipur office; file brand name with IP India; get GST registration; obtain ISO 9001 and quarry compliance certifications from state mining department.
5
First Sale
List on Jaipur Stone Directory and Rajasthan ODOP portal; pitch 10+ architects via LinkedIn and email; reach out to 5 real estate groups (DLF, Lodha, Godrej) with samples; list on Snapdeal B2B and Amazon Business with minimum 100-piece SKUs; attend Builders Association meets in Delhi and Mumbai.
6
Scale
Hire sales manager to manage B2B pipeline; partner with 2-3 logistics firms for bulk export; invest in digital showroom on own website; expand to Italian, Brazilian, and African import buyers via trade missions; add custom engraving and heritage pattern licensing.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with top 5 Indian architecture magazines (AD India, Architectural Digest) for feature stories on sustainable granite quarrying and Jalore's craft heritage; include QR codes linking to product catalog.
  • Run targeted LinkedIn ads to architects, interior designers, and project managers in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Pune) highlighting durability, export quality, and 3D texture galleries; offer free sample delivery.
  • Sponsor booths at India Builders Summit and Marble & Granite Expo; host virtual quarry tours for international buyers showing extraction-to-finish process; create YouTube documentary series on artisan stories.
