Mokokchung's elevation of 1,400-1,600m, cool monsoon climate, and nutrient-rich soil create ideal conditions for specialty arabica coffee cultivation. The region's strong agricultural tradition and sustainable farming practices ensure consistent quality and environmental stewardship, differentiating Mokokchung coffee in premium global markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Mokokchung.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mokokchung coffee clusters like Longding sub-division and Chungtia village; connect with local cooperatives (Mokokchung Coffee Planters Association); study processing methods at Nagaland Agricultural Department's coffee extension center in Dimapur
2
Source Raw Materials
Source arabica seeds from Nagaland Horticulture Department or Coffee Board nurseries in Chikmagalur; initial 5-acre cultivation or partner with 2-3 existing farmers (₹4-6L for seed, fertilizers, tools for first season)
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established local coffee growers or lease 3-5 acres; hire local labor for picking and processing; install wet-mill or partner with Mokokchung Coffee Processing Center; train workers on specialty coffee standards
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as Micro/Small enterprise; obtain FSSAi registration for processing; apply for IEC code from nearest customs office; register brand with Nagaland Intellectual Property cell; get organic certification from NPOP if applicable
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, Nykaa Marketplace, and direct Shopify store with 250g and 500g packs; partner with specialty café chains in Bangalore, Delhi, Mumbai; contact international importers via Coffee Board trade fairs; launch Instagram content showing farm-to-cup journey
6
Scale
Expand cultivation to 15+ acres; develop 2-3 SKUs (single-origin, blend, cold brew); establish B2B sales to 20+ cafés; secure first international export contract (minimum 2-5 MT); invest in micro-roastery setup in Mokokchung or Kohima

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create farm-story content on Instagram and YouTube showing harvest-to-cup process; partner with 5 micro-influencers in specialty coffee niche to gift products and create unboxing videos
  • Launch 'Taste the Northeast' campaign on Facebook targeting metro-city premium coffee drinkers; offer first-buy 20% discount and free sampler pack with every 500g purchase
  • Participate in specialty coffee expos (India Coffee Festival, Bangalore; International Coffee Week, Delhi) and direct-sell to B2B café owners; create QR code on packaging linking to farm tour video
