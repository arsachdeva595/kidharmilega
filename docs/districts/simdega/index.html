Minor Forest Produce

Simdega is surrounded by the Chotanagpur Plateau's ancient forests covering over 35% of district land, providing year-round access to diverse forest products. The tribal and local communities have centuries-old knowledge systems for sustainable harvesting and processing of these materials, making it naturally suited for MFP-based enterprises.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Simdega.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Simdega Forest Department office and connect with NTFP collector groups in Porahat and Jadugora blocks. Study honey and herb processing at local cooperative societies. Attend workshops at Jharkhand State Livelihoods Mission (JSLM) in Ranchi for MFP best practices.
2
Source Raw Materials
Contact tribal collectors through district NTFP cooperative unions (first batch: ₹2-5L for diverse assortment). Source honey from local beekeepers around Porahat (₹80-120/kg wholesale). Medicinal herbs available through Simdega Forest Produce Collectors Association. Initial inventory cost: ₹3-8L for mixed products.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing NTFP processing units in Simdega or establish small-scale drying and packaging facility (2000-3000 sq ft, ₹15-25L investment). Train with Khadi & Village Industries Commission (KVIC) for honey processing certification. Engage tribal artisans for value addition and handicraft bamboo products.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on msme.gov.in (free, online). Obtain IEC from nearest DGFT office in Ranchi for exports. Get FSSAI license for honey/food products (₹5000-15000). Trademark brand name through IP India. Aim for organic certification through NPOP (₹30-50K) for premium positioning.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart, and JioMart with 'forest origin' storytelling. Create D2C website using Shopify (₹29/month) focusing on sustainability narrative. Sell to Ayurveda brands via LinkedIn B2B outreach and industry directories (Pharmaceutical Directory India). Approach organic retail chains (Nature's Basket, Organic Stores) with product samples. Use WhatsApp Business for direct B2B orders.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range to herbal teas, cosmetic-grade powders, and supplements. Build supply agreements with 5-10 tribal collector groups for consistent volume. Launch corporate CSR partnerships for sustainable sourcing. Explore Certification from Fair Trade International. Set up bulk export operations to Germany and Japan via trade associations. Consider contract manufacturing for wellness brands.

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general, 25% for SC/ST) for micro/small enterprises in manufacturing of NTFP products.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹10L for cluster development and product design in forest-based artisan groups.
🏚
Jharkhand Bamboo Mission
subsidy up to ₹50,000 per hectare for bamboo cultivation and ₹5L for processing units; export incentives available.
🏚
Jharkhand NTFP Mission (State Livelihood Promotion Scheme)
direct support for collection, value addition, and marketing of minor forest products with interest-free loans up to ₹10L for producers.
