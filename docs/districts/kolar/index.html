Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kolar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established automotive clusters at Kolar Industrial Area and Bangalore Automotive Hub. Meet with existing component manufacturers and vehicle assemblers. Study supply chains at Maruti, Ashok Leyland, and Tata Motors facilities. Attend SIAM Auto Industry Summit and VAHAN conference.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Jindal Steel, TATA Steel suppliers in South India. Electrical components from Bangalore Electronic City vendors. First-batch investment: ₹5-10 crore for steel, aluminum, and engine components. Establish long-term contracts with 3-4 major suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with assembly-line production partnering with established manufacturers like Ashok Leyland or Swaraj Tractors initially. Build dedicated manufacturing facility with 2-5 acre land in Kolar Industrial Estate. Invest in CNC machines, welding equipment, and testing facilities. Hire automotive engineers from IIIT-Bangalore and NIT-Surathkal.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small/Medium as applicable). Obtain IEC code from DGFT for exports. Register company under Companies Act. ISO 9001 and ISO/TS 16949 automotive quality certifications mandatory. Brand registry with business name, trademark registration with IP office.
5
First Sale
Pitch directly to government transport tenders (GeM portal). Connect with fleet operators via NADA (auto dealers association). List on B2B platforms like IndiaStack, EURO2india. Create LinkedIn B2B strategy targeting logistics company CTOs. Attend SIAM and CII auto summits for bulk orders.
6
Scale
Post-6 months: Establish 2-3 dealer networks across India. Set up spare parts distribution centers. Export to Sri Lanka and Bangladesh through appointed distributors. Develop electric or hybrid variants. Invest in R&D for newer technologies. Expand production capacity to 10,000+ units annually.
