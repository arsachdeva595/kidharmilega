Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tiruchi (Thiruchirappalli).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tiruchi's engineering clusters — Lalgudi SIPCOT industrial area, Thiruverumbur precision manufacturing zone, and Srirangam engineering parks. Spend 2 weeks understanding production standards, ISO certifications required (ISO 9001, TS 16949 for automotive), and meet 5-10 established unit owners to understand margins, supplier chains, and export protocols.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Chennai steel suppliers (Apollo Steel, Jindal) and Tiruchi local vendors (Srirangam ferrous scrap yards). Budget ₹15-20L for first batch of raw materials (steel sheets, castings, fasteners). Connect with Tiruchi Chamber of Commerce for vendor networks and negotiate 30-45 day payment terms.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing job-work units in Lalgudi (5-15 available) initially for CNC machining, welding, and assembly on contract basis to avoid heavy capex. Alternatively, lease 2000-3000 sq ft space in SIPCOT Lalgudi at ₹8-12/sq ft with basic CNC machines (₹50-80L for 3-4 machines) and hire 5-8 skilled technicians from Tiruchi polytechnic institutes.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (MSMEOnline.gov.in) as medium or small manufacturing unit. Obtain IEC code from DGFT (within 7-10 days). Get ISO 9001:2015 certification (₹2-3L, 3-4 months process via TÜV SÜD or BSI in Tiruchi). Brand name registration at TMR office, Chennai. Open current/CC bank account with credit limit ₹25-50L for working capital.
5
First Sale
Launch LinkedIn B2B profile with portfolio of engineering specs and certifications. Register on IndiaMART as premium member and TradKey. Contact automotive OEMs (Bharat Heavy Electricals, Ashok Leyland, TVS, Bosch) in Chennai and Pune through industry directory. Attend IISE (Indian Industrial & Scientific Exhibition) and Tiruchi Engineering Expo for direct buyer meetings. Allocate ₹5L for initial marketing and trade show participation.
6
Scale
After first ₹1 Cr revenue, invest in proprietary CNC equipment and hire design engineer for product innovation. Build export partnerships via EEPC (Engineering Export Promotion Council) India and apply for APEDA/EEPC export support schemes. Target 30% export revenue within 18 months. Expand product range based on OEM feedback and enter adjacent segments (construction machinery, agricultural equipment).

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • LinkedIn B2B thought leadership — publish 2-3 case studies monthly on engineering precision, quality certifications, and successful OEM partnerships. Target 5K+ followers in automotive and industrial sectors within 6 months.
  • Trade magazine advertising in 'Automobile Engineering', 'Indian Metals & Minerals News'. Run ₹3-5L quarterly campaigns targeting purchasing managers of OEMs and tier-1 suppliers.
  • Email-based lead generation — create targeted lists of 500+ automotive OEM buyers quarterly (via SIAM directory) and send technical brochures, certifications, and competitive pricing. Expect 3-5% response with ₹2-5L orders.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy for manufacturing units; 35% grant for general category, processing at DIC Tiruchi
🏛
MUDRA Yojana
up to ₹10L unsecured working capital loan at 8-9% interest for registered manufacturing MSMEs via bank tie-ups
🏚
Tamil Nadu Industries Incentive Scheme (TNIIS)
5-year capital subsidy on plant & machinery (10-15%), electricity subsidy for manufacturing units in notified areas including Lalgudi SIPCOT
🏚
Tamil Nadu Export Promotion Scheme
subsidy on export-related certifications (ISO, quality testing), up to ₹5L per unit; administered via EEPC Tamil Nadu
