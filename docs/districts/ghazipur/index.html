ODOP Product

Ghazipur Wall Hangings

Ghazipur has a 200+ year legacy of jute weaving passed down through generations of artisan families. The district's proximity to raw jute sources, skilled labor pool, and established export corridors via Varanasi make it the natural hub for premium jute wall hangings. The Ghazipur Sadar cluster has over 3,000 registered jute artisans.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ghazipur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ghazipur Sadar jute cluster (near Sarai Husaini) to meet established artisan groups. Connect with SIDCO (State Industries Development Corporation) and District Administration for GI product standards. Study 5-10 active exporters via Ghazipur Chamber of Commerce. Budget: ₹8,000-12,000 for 3-4 day research trip.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw jute from Ghazipur jute mills (Ghazipur Jute Mills, Private mills in Sadar) at ₹35-45/kg. For first 200-piece batch (mixed designs), procure ₹40,000-60,000 raw materials. Connect with local dye suppliers for natural and synthetic dyes (₹5,000-8,000). First-batch total: ₹50,000-70,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered artisan groups in Ghazipur Sadar cluster (minimum 15-20 artisans) on per-piece basis (₹300-800 per hanging depending on complexity). Establish production contracts via SIDCO-recognized microenterprises. Alternatively, co-invest with 2-3 lead artisans as equity partners. Prototype production: 50 pieces in first month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online via udyamregistration.gov.in, free). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (₹100 processing). Trademark brand name with IP India (₹4,500-9,000). Obtain GI certification compliance certificate from DC Handicraft office, Ghazipur. Register MSME with handicraft category. Total cost: ₹15,000-20,000.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (11% commission) with 30+ product photos and artisan stories. Set up basic Shopify store (₹1,500/month). Simultaneously pitch to 20-30 Indian interior design studios and boutique hotels (Varanasi corridor) with wholesale catalogs (20-30% margin). Use WhatsApp B2B groups and LinkedIn for direct corporate inquiries. First-month target: 15-20 units.
6
Scale
After ₹8-12L revenue (6 months), hire a logistics coordinator (₹12,000-15,000/month). Expand to 50-80 artisans with quality control system. Negotiate wholesale rates with large home décor chains (Urban Ladder, Fab India). Launch Instagram/Pinterest content marketing with behind-the-scenes artisan videos (₹5,000-8,000/month for freelancer). Target ₹30-40L revenue by month 14.

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15,000 turnover-linked incentive + 5% interest subvention on up to ₹3L loan for handicraft artisans and micro-enterprises
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Up to ₹25L subsidy (25-35% of project cost) for manufacturing units in handicraft sector
🏚
UP Handicraft Cluster Development Scheme
Infrastructure grant of ₹10-15L per cluster + artisan training subsidy, direct support for Ghazipur cluster
🏚
UP Micro and Small Enterprises Promotion Policy
10% subsidy on capital investment (max ₹10L) + 5% interest subvention for 3 years for registered MSMEs
