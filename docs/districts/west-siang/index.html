ODOP Product

Pineapple

West Siang's cool, misty climate and well-drained hilly soil create ideal conditions for pineapple cultivation, yielding superior taste and sugar content. The region's low population density and minimal pesticide exposure produce premium organic pineapples. Strategic location near Arunachal Pradesh agricultural belt provides supply chain proximity to northeast markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for West Siang.

1
Learn / Research
Visit operational pineapple farms in West Siang district (Aalo, Kaying villages); attend ICAR-NE training at Papun Research Station (Arunachal); study Arunachal Pradesh Horticulture Department reports; connect with existing producer groups via APEDA.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified pineapple suckers/crowns from ICAR-NE certified nurseries in Arunachal Pradesh (₹2-3 lakh per acre for 2000 plants); buy organic compost from local suppliers in Guwahati (₹500/ton); first batch land preparation costs ₹1-1.5 lakh per acre.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with experienced West Siang farmers for contract farming; establish 0.5-1 acre trial farm; hire trained agricultural workers (₹8-10k/month); implement drip irrigation system; follow Arunachal Pradesh Organic Certification standards.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under Ministry of MSME (free); obtain IEC code from APEDA Guwahati office for export eligibility; register FPO (Farmer Producer Organization) with state department; secure organic certification from APOF or JSPL; register trademark with IP India.
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket, Blinkit, JioMart with farm-direct sourcing; establish direct contracts with Doon Valley and northeast hotel chains; participate in APEDA-backed export consortiums; contact fruit wholesalers at Guwahati's Fancy Bazaar wholesale market.
6
Scale
Expand to 2-3 acres by month 6; develop cold chain storage (₹20-30 lakh) in Itanagar/Guwahati; launch canned/juiced product line by month 8; hire export logistics partner for Myanmar/Thailand shipments; establish supply contracts with 5-10 retail outlets.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram storytelling campaign showcasing misty West Siang hill farms — 3x weekly reels targeting urban metro fruit buyers; partner with micro-influencers (50-100k followers) from Northeast India.
  • B2B WhatsApp & email campaigns to 200+ northeast fruit traders, hotels, juice manufacturers with bulk pricing; highlight organic certification and taste superiority.
  • Participate in APEDA-organized northeast horticulture expos in Guwahati, Kolkata; sponsor agri-conference stalls to build supplier relationships with exporters.
