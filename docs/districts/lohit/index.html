Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Lohit.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tezu and Chowkham mustard farming clusters in Lohit district; connect with farmer collectives under NRLM and government agriculture extension offices. Study mustard seed quality standards (IS 1731) and visit similar operations in Assam's spice zones around Silchar.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw mustard seeds directly from 50-100 local farmers in Lohit at ₹4,500-5,200/quintal during October-November harvest. Initial batch: 20 quintals (~₹1,00,000). Establish farmer agreements through Lohit District Agriculture Department channels.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with manual cleaning and grading using basic machinery (cost ₹4-6 lakhs for seed cleaner + grader). Partner with existing small mills in Tezu town or set up mini processing unit. Obtain food safety license (FSSAI) before commercial operations. Contract with 3-5 local mustard oil pressers initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) and IEC (Import-Export Code) within 7 days online. Obtain FSSAI food business license (₹5,000-10,000). File trademark for brand name with IP India. Get state excise clearance for oil production. Total registration cost: ₹35,000-50,000.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and Blinkit with 2-5kg premium packs at ₹180-220/kg. Partner with 15-20 retail supermarkets in Guwahati and Dibrugarh. Approach spice wholesalers in Kolkata and Delhi wholesale markets. Start B2B sales to restaurants and food processors via IndiaMART and TradeKey.
6
Scale
After first 6 months profitability, expand to 100+ quintals annual processing. Invest in cold storage (₹15-20 lakhs) and oil extraction unit. Launch 3-4 SKUs (whole seeds, powdered, oil, paste). Pursue GI tag certification to command premium pricing. Establish direct export relationships with Bangladesh and Myanmar importers.

Free money you didn't know about

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy (25% for general, 35% for SC/ST) for food processing micro-enterprises in Tier 2/3 cities
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants up to ₹3 crores for cluster development and infrastructure including cold storage and processing units
🏚
Arunachal Pradesh Agriculture Infrastructure Development Fund
subsidy for processing equipment and cold chain infrastructure up to ₹15 lakhs for registered MSME agribusinesses
🏚

10% subsidy on export value and ₹2 lakh one-time certification support for food products with IEC registration
