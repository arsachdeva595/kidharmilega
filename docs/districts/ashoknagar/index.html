Chanderi Handloom

Ashoknagar is home to the historic Chanderi weaving cluster with 400+ years of artisan heritage. The region's water quality and proximity to silk and cotton sources make it ideal for Chanderi weaving. Multi-generational weaver families have perfected the craft, creating an unmatched ecosystem for authentic production.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ashoknagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chanderi town (30km from Ashoknagar); spend 1-2 weeks with master weavers; visit clusters near Chanderi Mandi; understand traditional motifs (Buti, Tanchoi, Phool-Patti); join local weaver associations; connect with DC Handloom office in Ashoknagar
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure silk/cotton yarn from suppliers in Chanderi or Jaipur (₹80-120/kg); zari from Surat traders (₹200-500/kg); natural dyes from Madhya Pradesh cooperatives; first batch of yarn for 10 sarees ≈ ₹35,000-50,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered weaver cooperatives in Chanderi (e.g., Chanderi Weavers Society); or contract individual master weavers; negotiate per-piece rates (₹5,000-15,000/saree depending on complexity); ensure GI tag eligibility; maintain artisan traceability documentation
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) at udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC code at DGFT (required for exports); trademark brand name at ipindia.gov.in; obtain GI certification compliance documentation from DC Handloom; register at GST portal
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, own Shopify store with artisan story; post 3x/week on Instagram with loom videos and weaver profiles; connect with luxury saree retailers (Sabyasachi, Tussar, regional chains); pitch to wedding planners; reach out to NRI groups on Facebook
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range (dress materials, dupattas, blouses); build WhatsApp B2B network with 50+ boutiques; apply for SFURTI cluster subsidy for shared dyeing/finishing facility; hire logistics partner; target international craft fairs (Cannes, London Fashion Week); develop sustainable/organic variant

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels: Behind-the-loom artisan videos (3-5 min); tag fashion bloggers; use #ChanderiHandloom #INCrafted #SlowFashion; target affluent women 35-55 with carousel ads spending ₹5,000/week
  • Pinterest strategy: High-res saree images with wedding, festival themes; link to Etsy/website; run 6-month pins; reach wedding planners and global saree enthusiasts organically
  • Influencer collaboration: Partner with 5-10 micro-influencers (20k-100k followers) in ethnic wear niche; send review pieces; get 10-15 reels; budget ₹15,000-30,000/month for 3 months
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 toolkit grant + loans up to ₹100,000 at subsidized rates for registered artisans and cluster participants
🏛
SFURTI (Amended)
up to ₹10 Cr for cluster infrastructure (common dyeing facility, design center); direct benefit to handloom clusters
🏚
Madhya Pradesh Handloom Development Scheme
interest subsidy on loans, skill development subsidies, export promotion grants up to 10% of invoice value
🏚
MP MSME Subsidy Scheme
15-25% capex subsidy on equipment for registered MSMEs in handloom sector; online portal at mpssme.gov.in
Registration required: Required — udyamregistration.gov.in
