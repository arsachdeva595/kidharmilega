Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ranga Reddy.

1
Learn / Research
Visit HITEC City's food processing cluster near Hyderabad and Ranga Reddy's Jinnaram industrial area; interview 5-8 existing biscuit manufacturers; attend quarterly food processing expos at Hyderabad Trade Tower; study FSSAI licensing requirements for confectionery
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium maida from Cargill or Hindustan Unilever distributor in Hyderabad (₹45-55/kg), butter from Amul or Britannia suppliers (₹400-480/kg), cocoa from Mumbai wholesale markets via agro-commodity brokers; estimate ₹180K-250K for first batch of 2000 kg production run
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered co-packing facilities in Jinnaram or Kurnool (10km radius) offering FDA/FSSAI-certified production lines; alternatively lease 1500 sq ft unit in food parks with pre-installed ovens and packing lines (₹25K-40K/month)
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam via udyamregistration.gov.in (free, online), apply IEC code at DGFT portal, obtain FSSAI registration (₹7K-12K), register trademark at IP India (₹4500-9000), set up GST registration for 5% slab on food products
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and Flipkart Supermart (requires FSSAI + ISO certification + ₹50K security deposit); simultaneously approach 50+ local kirana stores in Hyderabad via direct field sales; partner with 2-3 gift box companies (HK Gift House, Archies) for bulk orders; create WhatsApp business catalog and email B2B wholesalers
6
Scale
After 6 months, add 3-4 new SKUs (sugar-free biscuits, regional flavors); expand distribution to 200+ retail points via food distributors like Kern's or Aditya Birla; launch own D2C app; approach 10-15 corporate offices for monthly hampers; apply for PMEGP/SFURTI grant to add automated packaging line
