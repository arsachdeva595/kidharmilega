ODOP Product

Madhubhani Paintings

Darbhanga is the epicenter of Mithila region where Madhubani painting originated as a sacred ritual art form. The district has 5,000+ active artisans, established supply chains for natural dyes and materials, and deep cultural roots that ensure authenticity and skill transmission across generations. UNESCO recognition and international demand make it the natural hub for scaling this art form globally.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Darbhanga.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mithila Art Village cluster near Madhupur village, Darbhanga; connect with CRIDA (Central Research Institute of Dryland Agriculture) and local artisan cooperatives; study existing players like Manjusha or Leela Wyzol; attend Darbhanga Mela art exhibitions
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure natural dyes from local suppliers in Darbhanga town (approx ₹5,000-8,000 for starter kit); source handmade paper from Khadi Vikas Bhawan or local paper makers (₹2,000-3,000 per ream); brushes from SEWA Bharat or local tool makers (₹1,500-2,000); first batch materials cost ~₹15,000-20,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established Madhubani artists in Madhupur or Kishanpur villages through cooperative networks; work on revenue-share (typically 40-50% to artist); begin with 10-15 pieces per month; use platforms like Artisan Connect to find verified painters
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as micro-enterprise (handicraft category); obtain IEC code for export; register GI-certified status with DC Handicraft Ministry; file trademark for brand name; join Darbhanga Madhubani Painters Association for credibility
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Handmade, Etsy (target international buyers), Instagram shopping, and own Shopify store; approach luxury décor platforms like The Artmen or FabFurnish; partner with 2-3 boutique galleries in metros; list on LocalBuy and Jaypore for domestic reach
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artist base to 10-15 painters; invest in packaging and branding (₹50,000-100,000); launch corporate gifting B2B program; explore export partnerships through craft councils; open experience center or pop-up in Delhi/Mumbai; develop signature collections with seasonal themes

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create short-form video content on Instagram/TikTok showing the 5-step Madhubani process — from natural dye-making to final brushstroke; partner with 3-5 micro-influencers in sustainable living and home décor (50K-200K followers) for authentic storytelling
  • Run educational webinar series on YouTube titled 'Stories Behind Each Stroke' featuring master artists; repurpose as blog content for SEO to capture searches like 'authentic Madhubani paintings online'
  • Launch 'Sponsor an Artist' campaign on Facebook/LinkedIn targeting corporate buyers and NGOs; offer CSR certification and behind-the-scenes access; target 5-10 corporate partners at ₹50K-100K per sponsorship
