Aamla Products

Pratapgarh has ideal agro-climatic conditions for aamla cultivation with a legacy of traditional processing techniques. The district's established aamla farming cooperatives and access to pure water sources make it uniquely positioned for high-quality product innovation. Strong artisan networks and GI certification provide authentic sourcing and premium market positioning.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Pratapgarh.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pratapgarh's main aamla processing clusters near Rajapur and Tundla towns; connect with SFURTI-supported artisan groups through the District Industries Centre (DIC) office; study existing GI-certified producers' supply chains and pricing.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh aamla directly from Pratapgarh farmer cooperatives during harvest (July-September); typical first-batch cost ₹40,000-60,000 for 2,000 kg fresh fruit; establish relationships with local spice suppliers in Pratapgarh town market for consistent quality.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing SFURTI-registered artisan groups or hire 2-3 trained processors from local communities; alternatively rent food-processing units in Pratapgarh district's mini industrial areas; ensure FSSAI licensing and GI certification alignment through District Industries Centre guidance.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam portal (MSME registration), obtain IEC code for exports from nearest Customs office in Lucknow; file GI authorized user application with IP India; get FSSAI food processing license; design packaging emphasizing GI tag, Ayurvedic benefits, and heritage story.
5
First Sale
Launch on Flipkart and Amazon with premium positioning; reach out to specialty retailers (Nature's Basket, Healthy Buddha, local organic chains); send samples to Ayurveda clinics and wellness resorts in Delhi-NCR; run Instagram/Facebook campaigns targeting health and wellness audiences.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product line (aamla murabba, aamla juice, aamla powder); secure B2B contracts with modern retail for shelf space; pursue ISO 22000 certification; establish export partnerships with 2-3 countries; hire dedicated e-commerce and sales teams; explore franchise/distribution model in tier-1 cities.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with micro-influencers in ayurveda and wellness niches (50K-500K followers) for authentic product reviews on Instagram/YouTube; cost ₹5,000-15,000 per collaboration targeting 25-45 age group.
  • Launch seasonal campaigns during Diwali, Dussehra, and New Year emphasizing immunity and natural health; create gift bundles; target corporate gifting via LinkedIn ads to HR departments.
  • Create content hub on website explaining GI certification, aamla benefits, traditional recipes; optimize for SEO keywords 'organic aamla products India', 'GI certified Indian gooseberry'; invest ₹20,000-30,000/month in Google Shopping ads.
