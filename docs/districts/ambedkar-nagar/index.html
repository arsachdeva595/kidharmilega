ODOP Product

Textiles

Ambedkar Nagar has a centuries-old textile weaving tradition with skilled artisan communities specializing in silk and cotton fabric production. The district's access to quality raw materials, established supply chains, and a labor force trained in traditional textile techniques make it ideal for scaling artisanal textile production. The proximity to major trading hubs and existing cooperative structures support both domestic and export markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ambedkar Nagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ambedkar Nagar textile clusters (Akbarpur, Jajmau areas) and spend 2-3 weeks learning from established weavers. Connect with District Industries Center and MSME Development Institute. Study competitors on Etsy, Flipkart, and specialty textile platforms. Budget: ₹15,000-25,000 for research travel and documentation.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw silk from Bengaluru silk markets or Mysore suppliers (₹400-600/kg). Procure cotton yarn from Ludhiana or local UP mills (₹150-250/kg). Identify natural dye suppliers in Jaipur or use local indigo sources. First batch sample order (50 meters mixed fabric): ₹40,000-60,000 through established supplier networks in the district.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 registered artisan weavers in Akbarpur (average ₹400-800 per piece production cost). Form a cooperative or register as a sole proprietor with artisan contracts. Set up a small finishing unit (₹1.5-2L) for washing, pressing, and quality control. Alternatively, work with established textile cooperative unions in the district.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free). Obtain IEC code from APEDA in Lucknow (₹500, 2 weeks). Create brand identity (logo, packaging design ₹25,000-50,000). File trademark application (₹9,000-15,000). Setup GST registration (18% on textiles). Create basic website and social media (₹20,000-40,000).
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, Flipkart Sarees category with 10-15 hero products. Reach out to 50-100 boutiques across Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with wholesale samples. Create Instagram and Pinterest accounts with high-quality product photography. Partner with 2-3 sustainable fashion marketplaces (Conscious, Jaypore, FabIndia-like platforms). Target 5-10 initial orders within 60 days.
6
Scale
After first 6 months, expand artisan network to 15-20 weavers. Launch seasonal collections (wedding, festive). Build corporate bulk order pipeline. Establish presence in 20-30 high-traffic retail boutiques. Scale social media to 10K followers and implement influencer collaborations (micro-influencers in sustainable fashion). Explore international fair participation and B2B export channels through APEDA support.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Run Instagram/Pinterest carousel campaigns showcasing artisan weaving process videos. Tag sustainable fashion influencers and emphasize ethical production. Allocate ₹10,000/month for targeted ads to 25-50 year old women interested in eco-fashion and heritage products.
  • Create brand storytelling content highlighting specific artisan craftspeople and their techniques. Partner with 5-10 micro-influencers (10K-100K followers) in sustainable fashion for affiliate sales (5-8% commission). Expected reach: 50,000 impressions/month.
  • Launch referral program offering 15% discount for customer referrals. Build email list via website and send monthly lookbooks, artisan stories, and new collection launches. Start with 500 subscribers, target 3,000 by month 6.
