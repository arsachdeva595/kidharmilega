Embroidery

Amritsar has a 400+ year legacy of textile and embroidery craftsmanship influenced by Sikh and Mughal heritage. The district's proximity to traditional embroidery clusters like Tarn Taran and established textile networks provides consistent access to skilled artisans and raw materials. Strong tourist footfall and religious significance make it a natural hub for handicraft sales.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Amritsar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Amritsar's embroidery clusters in Civil Lines and Tarn Taran; spend 2 weeks shadowing master embroiderers; attend Punjab Crafts Council meetings; study competitor pricing on Etsy, Jaypore, and Instagram.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source silk thread and metallic thread from Ludhiana textile wholesalers (₹8-12k per variety); cotton and silk fabrics from Amritsar's Mehta Cloth House or online (₹3-5k per bolt); first batch raw material cost: ₹40-60k.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established embroidery ateliers in Amritsar's Ranjit Avenue or Civil Lines area; negotiate piece rates at ₹150-300 per item depending on complexity; start with 50-100 pieces per month; or invest in training 3-5 home artisans.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam at udyamregistration.gov.in (free, online); apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) at the port authority; register brand name on trademark database; get GST registration (18% applicable); apply for Handloom Mark certification from Ministry of Handloom.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram profile with high-quality embroidery process videos; list on Etsy (6.5% fee), Amazon Handmade (15% fee), and Jaypore; reach out directly to 20 boutiques via email with product catalogs; attend Handloom Expo (annual, Delhi/Mumbai); partner with 2-3 wedding planners in Amritsar for custom orders.
6
Scale
Hire 2-3 additional artisan teams by month 6; expand product line to include home decor (cushions, wall hangings); establish wholesale relationships with 10+ regional retailers; launch export via FedEx/DHL to US and UK; create YouTube tutorial series to build brand authority and organic traffic.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create 15-30 second Instagram Reels showing artisans hand-stitching intricate designs; use hashtags #AmritsarEmbroidery #SlowFashion #HandmadeInIndia; target ads to women aged 25-45 interested in sustainable fashion.
  • Pitch product stories to lifestyle bloggers and Pinterest influencers (100k-500k followers) in home decor and slow fashion niches; offer 30% commission on first 3 months of sales.
  • Partner with Airbnb and local hotels to display embroidered items in tourist areas; create QR codes linking to your Etsy shop; sponsor Golden Temple heritage tours with branded gift packs.
