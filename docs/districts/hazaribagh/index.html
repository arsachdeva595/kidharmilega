ODOP Product

Sohrai Painting

Hazaribagh is the cultural heartland of Sohrai art, practiced for centuries by tribal communities (primarily Kharwar and Chero tribes). The district's rich tradition of natural pigment use and strong community-based artisan networks make it the authentic source. The GI status (2009) validates Hazaribagh as the exclusive origin.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Hazaribagh.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Hazaribagh district clusters (Porahat block, Jaridih village) to study authentic Sohrai painting techniques. Connect with GI-certified artisan cooperatives like Hazaribagh Women's Art Collective. Spend 2-3 weeks shadowing senior artists. Budget: ₹15,000-25,000 for travel and learning.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source natural pigments from local mineral merchants in Ramgarh or directly from tribal suppliers. Obtain clay/mud from construction material suppliers in Hazaribagh town. First batch (50 paintings, 1-2 sq ft each): ₹12,000-18,000. Build relationships with 5-8 certified artisans for consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered women artisan groups under PM Vishwakarma scheme. Establish a small production center (home-based or rented 300 sq ft space, ₹5,000/month) in Hazaribagh town. Train 3-5 artisans on demand forecasting and timeline management. Start with 20 paintings/month, scale to 50-80 by month 6.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise classification). Obtain IEC code for export from nearest Chamber of Commerce in Ranchi. Register GI-certified claim with DC Handicraft Hazaribagh for authenticity certification. Create brand identity emphasizing tribal origin, sustainability, and artisan empowerment. Budget: ₹8,000-12,000.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (15-20% commission) and Amazon Handmade (8% commission) with high-quality photography. Target art collector Facebook groups and Instagram (#SohraiForgotten, #HazaribaughTribalArt). Email global gallery networks and interior designers. Participate in virtual craft bazaars on Amar Artisans platform. First sale target: Month 2.
6
Scale
After ₹8-10 Lakh revenue at 6 months: expand artisan network to 15-20 women, apply for SFURTI cluster development grant, launch branded packaging and certification cards, establish wholesale partnerships with 5-10 interior design studios in metro cities, launch corporate gifting B2B portal, develop online workshop series for cultural tourism revenue.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • YouTube documentary series: 'Hands that Paint History' — 8-10 short videos (3-5 min) profiling individual artists, their techniques, and cultural significance. Post on YouTube, Instagram Reels, TikTok. Target: 50K+ views, viral growth.
  • Instagram Influencer partnerships: Collaborate with 10-15 micro-influencers (20K-100K followers) in sustainable home décor, ethical fashion, and heritage spaces. Provide 3-5 paintings as gifted inventory for unboxing/review content. ROI target: 25% of sales from influencer traffic.
  • Email marketing to art gallery networks: Build curated list of 500+ international galleries, interior designers, and museum curators. Send quarterly lookbooks with artist bios, GI certification details, and bulk pricing. Target: 2-3 B2B orders per quarter at ₹50K-150K each.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
₹15,000 interest-free credit for first 18 months + skill training for artisan members; up to 5% annual interest subsidy post-18 months
🏛
PMEGP (Pradhan Mantri Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 Lakh subsidy (25% for SC/ST, 35% women) for setting up production unit and working capital
🏚
Jharkhand State Handicraft Grant Scheme
₹5-15 Lakh one-time capital grant for registered artisan groups; additional ₹2 Lakh for GI-certified products
🏚
Jharkhand Cottage Industries Development Scheme
working capital subsidy up to ₹10 Lakh and machinery grant of 25-30% for handicraft exporters
Registration required: Required — udyamregistration.gov.in
