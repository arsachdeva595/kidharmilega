Potato

Indore district benefits from fertile black soil, semi-arid climate with adequate monsoon, and proximity to major markets like Mumbai and Delhi. The region's established agricultural infrastructure, storage facilities, and organized wholesale markets make it ideal for potato cultivation and export. Strong cold chain networks and logistics connectivity provide competitive advantages for fresh produce handling.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Indore.

1
Learn / Research
Visit APMC Indore market and Mhow potato cluster to understand supply chains, pricing trends, and seasonal patterns. Connect with existing potato farmers in Mahidpur and Mhow talukas. Study cold storage facilities at Indore Cold Storage Association and connect with 3-4 successful potato exporters.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source seed potatoes from NPTC Pune-certified suppliers or local seed farms in Indore for ₹30-40 per kg. Purchase farmyard manure from local suppliers (₹5000-8000 per tonne). First-batch setup for 5 acres: ₹250,000-300,000 (seeds, fertilizers, pesticides, labor for 4-5 months).
3
Produce / Partner
Lease 5-10 acres in Mahidpur or Mhow talukas (₹8000-12000 per acre per season). Partner with local agricultural extension agents for optimal cultivation practices. Arrange cold storage partnership with existing facilities in Indore city (₹5000-8000 per tonne per month). Consider forming FPO to access government support and bulk buyer contracts.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam via udyamregistration.gov.in for MSME benefits. Obtain IEC from DGFT for export eligibility. File FSSAI registration for any processing activities. Register brand name with IP office. Get phytosanitary certification from APEDA for export.
5
First Sale
Approach APMC Indore traders and register as direct supplier. List on e-commerce platforms (BigBasket, Blinkit, JioMart) via distributor partnerships. Contact food processors (Kellogg's, Lay's, ITC) directly with samples and certifications. Engage export houses in Indore for Nepal and Bangladesh markets. Participate in trade fairs at IAMAI.
6
Scale
Expand acreage to 25-50 acres by month 8-10 using PMEGP subsidy. Establish own cold storage facility (₹15-20 lakhs for 500-tonne capacity). Develop branded packaged products (fries, chips) for retail expansion. Build direct export network in SE Asian markets. Implement blockchain traceability for premium positioning.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with BigBasket and Blinkit for 'Farmer-Direct' collections with farmer's story and certification badges; run targeted ads in Tier-2 cities emphasizing freshness and nutrition.
  • Create Instagram content showcasing farm-to-table journey; engage food bloggers and Instagram influencers (50K-200K followers) for product unboxing and recipe content in Hindi/English.
  • B2B LinkedIn campaign targeting food processors and exporters with ROI-focused case studies; attend food processing exhibitions like Anutec India and Alimentaria.
