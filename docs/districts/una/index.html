Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Una.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Una Light Engineering Industry cluster near Bangana and Haroli towns; meet with established manufacturers at the local MSME Association; study product specifications at nearby units producing textile machinery, agricultural equipment, and industrial components.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mild steel and stainless steel from suppliers in Ludhiana (Punjab) or directly from SAIL/TATA Steel; source fasteners and bearings from Jalandhar industrial suppliers; initial first batch raw material cost approximately ₹8-12 lakhs depending on product type.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish or lease a small engineering workshop (500-1500 sq ft) with basic CNC machines and fabrication equipment; partner with existing Una-based engineering units for contract manufacturing; hire skilled technicians from local labor markets in Una town.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (MSME classification); obtain IEC Code from nearest Customs office for export capability; register business under Shops and Establishments Act; create technical product documentation and ISO certifications (ISO 9001 for quality management); design brand identity emphasizing 'Made in Una, Trusted Across India'.
5
First Sale
Approach manufacturing units directly in Himachal, Punjab, and Haryana with product samples; list on IndiaMART and TradeKey for B2B visibility; attend trade shows like India Manufacturing Expo and regional industrial exhibitions; build relationships with procurement officers through LinkedIn and industrial associations.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish a dedicated sales team for 3-4 key regions; invest in upgraded CNC machinery to increase capacity; develop 2-3 product variants based on market feedback; explore export markets through trade missions; consider ISO certifications (ISO 13485 for medical/pharma equipment if pivoting); build distributor network across North India.
