The region's climate and soil are ideal for pulse cultivation, ensuring year-round raw material availability at competitive prices.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Balrampur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Balrampur's pulse processing clusters in Lalganj and Deva areas; connect with Balrampur District Industries Centre; attend quarterly food processing meets at Lucknow's NAFED office to understand market trends, competitor pricing, and supply chain logistics.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source pulses from NAFED procurement centers in Balrampur or directly from cooperative societies like Balrampur Krishi Utpadan Samiti; budget ₹100,000-150,000 for first 500kg mixed dal batch at wholesale rates of ₹45-65 per kg depending on dal type.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisan groups certified by FSSAI in Balrampur town; alternatively set up micro-production unit with 1-2 semi-automatic roasting and grinding machines (₹800K-1.2M); hire 2-3 trained processors at ₹8,000-12,000/month; target initial monthly capacity of 2-3 tons.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam on udyamregistration.gov.in, obtain FSSAI license (₹5,000-20,000 depending on category), apply for IEC code via directorate of foreign trade; design packaging with nutrition labels in Hindi and English; trademark brand name via IP India; register GST as food processing unit.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh and Flipkart Supermart with ₹50K per SKU investment; simultaneously approach 20-30 local Bikaneri and regional snack distributors; pitch to 10 organized retail store chains (Big Bazaar, More, Reliance Fresh); set up WhatsApp groups for B2B buyers and corporate gifting clients.
6
Scale
After ₹15-20L revenue milestone, expand product line to include flavored variants and ready-to-eat formats; negotiate shelf space in premium malls via food courts; launch subscription boxes for health-conscious consumers; explore co-manufacturing partnerships with established regional brands; invest in food safety certifications (ISO 22000) to unlock institutional contracts.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • YouTube channel featuring artisan documentary-style videos showing traditional pulse processing methods, garnering 50K+ subscribers for organic reach to health-conscious millennials; partner with fitness influencers for product reviews.
  • Instagram Reels campaign highlighting colorful product photography, heritage stories, and user-generated content from customers; run ads targeting high-income zip codes in metros with ₹10K/month budget; use location tags for Balrampur tourism connection.
  • Corporate bulk gifting partnerships with B2B platforms like Giftology and Smiriti; offer customized hampers for Diwali, weddings, and employee recognition programs; cold-email 500+ HR departments in Delhi NCR with 15-20% discount for bulk orders.
