Handloom Silk Sarees

Bengaluru Urban is home to a significant community of skilled silk weavers who have migrated from traditional textile hubs, combined with high purchasing power of urban consumers seeking premium handloom products. The district's cosmopolitan nature and strong e-commerce ecosystem provide unique advantages for marketing and scaling handloom silk sarees to domestic and international markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bengaluru Urban.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Handloom Clusters around Bengaluru (Yelahanka, Horamavu); connect with Karnataka State Handloom Department; attend textile fairs at India Handloom (Whitefield); meet 3-5 master weavers to understand production timelines, costs, and quality variations
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw silk yarn from suppliers in Bangalore Silk Market (KR Puram area) — expect ₹150-200/kg for quality mulberry silk; contact natural dye suppliers in Bengaluru; negotiate artisan partnerships for production at ₹2,000-4,000 per saree depending on complexity; plan first batch of 20-30 pieces at ₹60,000-120,000 total material cost
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 2-3 skilled handloom weavers in Bengaluru Urban (engage through KHADI & Village Industries Board); establish quality control checklist; plan 30-45 day production cycle per batch; visit looms weekly to monitor progress and ensure design consistency
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE) online portal; apply for IEC code from DGFT; register trademark with 'Handloom' or 'Artisan' positioning; create GI claim awareness even without GI tag; get Textile Committee certification; obtain FSSAI if planning food/gift hamper bundles
5
First Sale
Launch branded Instagram account with weaver storytelling; list on Meesho, Amazon Handmade, Etsy; approach 10-15 premium ethnic wear boutiques in Indiranagar, Koramangala, Whitefield for wholesale partnership; create WhatsApp Business for direct orders; run targeted Google Shopping ads targeting 'handloom silk sarees' keywords with 15% first-buyer discount
6
Scale
After first 6 months and ₹15L+ revenue, expand to 5-7 artisan partners; launch seasonal collections; open pop-up stores during Diwali/wedding seasons; build B2B export partnerships; hire a design consultant; establish social media presence with 50K+ followers; target premium wedding planning platforms like WedMeGood

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels series 'Meet the Weaver' — 30-second clips showing weaving process, dye mixing, and finished saree; post 3x weekly with hashtags #HandloomSarees #BengaluruArtisan; target audience aged 25-40; expect 2-3% conversion from 10K monthly views
  • Partner with 5-10 wedding bloggers/influencers in Bangalore for styled photoshoots wearing your sarees; provide 2-3 pieces at 30% margin; generate 20-30 direct orders per influencer collaboration; budget ₹40,000-80,000 per influencer
  • Run Facebook + Instagram carousel ads targeting 'luxury traditional wear' and 'ethnic sarees' keywords in metros; use customer testimonial videos; budget ₹50,000/month; aim for ₹800-1,200 CPA; retarget website visitors with 20% discount offer
