ODOP Product

Onion

Khandwa district's rich alluvial soil along the Narmada river valley creates ideal conditions for onion cultivation with naturally sweet and pungent varieties. The region's semi-arid climate and well-drained soil ensure optimal bulb development and long shelf life, making it a natural hub for high-quality onion production.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Khandwa.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khandwa's primary onion markets (Harihar Naka wholesale market, Khandwa mandis) during October-December harvest season. Connect with established traders like those in Indore Vegetable Market. Study soil testing labs in Khandwa (JNKVV extension) and attend agricultural seminars; benchmark production with 8-12 ton/hectare yields.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified onion seeds from JNKVV (Jabalpur) or private breeders (₹50,000-100,000 per 10 hectare). Arrange bulk manure from local composting units near Khandwa town (₹2,000-3,000/ton). First-batch organic farming input cost: ₹8-12 lakhs for 5 hectares including land preparation, seeds, and 6-month fertilizer supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 15-25 small farmers in Khandwa block via cooperative model or direct contracting (reduce capital burden). Set up cold storage facility (100-200 ton capacity) in Khandwa town (₹20-30 lakhs); alternatively lease existing cold storage from NAFED or local operators. Begin with contract farming on 5-10 hectares, then scale to 20+ hectares by year 2.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam as agricultural produce business (MSME); apply for IEC code through DGFT portal for export eligibility. Brand registration (₹5,000-15,000): register brand name with state agriculture department and food safety authority. FSSAI license (₹10,000-25,000) if value-adding (peeling, packaging). Get agricultural produce certificate from Khandwa Collector's office.
5
First Sale
Launch on BigBasket, Jio Mart, and local WhatsApp business networks targeting restaurants in Indore/Nagpur. Approach APMC mandis in Khandwa, Indore, and Bhopal directly with samples; negotiate contracts with established wholesale traders. Contact food processors in Madhya Pradesh (tomato sauce, pickle manufacturers). Initial target: 10-15 ton/month wholesale; scale to 30+ ton via e-commerce by month 4.
6
Scale
Expand to 30-40 hectares by year 2; add value-added products (fried onions, onion powder, paste). Build export logistics partnership with agri-exporters in Indore (contact APEDA-registered companies). Launch branded "Khandwa Gold Onion" on Amazon and Flipkart; target ₹50-100 crore turnover by year 3 through wholesale + retail hybrid model.

  • B2B LinkedIn campaign targeting food processing companies and restaurants in Central India; attend AgroTech India Conference and AgriInnovate exhibitions in Indore to network with bulk buyers.
