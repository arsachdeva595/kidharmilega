Silk Carpet (Hand Knotted Carpet)

Srinagar has a centuries-old tradition of sericulture and carpet weaving with skilled artisans trained in Mughal-era techniques. The region's climate and access to premium silk fibers make it the natural hub for authentic hand-knotted carpets. The GI tag certifies authenticity and commands premium pricing in global markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Srinagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Srinagar Carpet Weaving Cluster in Zadibal and Khanqah areas; connect with master weavers at Weavers Society Srinagar; study GI standards at Ministry of Handicrafts online portal; attend carpet trade shows in Delhi (India International Trade Fair) and Mumbai.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium mulberry silk from sericulture farms in Srinagar (₹2000-3000/kg); source natural dyes from local suppliers in Srinagar's Lal Bazar; establish relationships with 2-3 master weavers for initial ₹3-5L investment in raw material batch; negotiate 30-day payment terms.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established master artisans in Zadibal cooperative; commission 3-4 small carpets (3x5 ft) for portfolio; invest ₹1.5-2L in loom setup if establishing own unit; use traditional knot-tying methods; ensure 6-8 weeks per carpet production timeline.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (VC category) via udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT for exports; apply for GI certification verification (already awarded, ensure compliance); register trademark with IP office; get CAP (Carpet Authentication Protocol) certification for authentic Srinagar carpets.
5
First Sale
Launch on Lakshya (luxury segment), 1stDibs, and Artisan's Collective; create Instagram and Pinterest accounts with high-quality carpet photography; pitch to 10 interior design studios in Delhi-NCR; reach out to luxury hotel procurement teams; list on Abodhi marketplace for GI-certified crafts; partner with Khadi India for retail presence.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 8-10 active weavers; develop seasonal collections; apply for MSME export promotion subsidy (EPCG scheme); open showroom in Delhi or Mumbai; build B2B wholesale relationships with 20+ interior design firms; launch custom design service; explore international trade shows (Stockholm Furniture Fair, Design Week Paris).

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with interior design influencers (15K-50K followers) on Instagram for styled room makeover reels featuring carpets; offer 15-20% affiliate commission; target hashtags #LuxuryHomeDecor #ArtisanalCrafts #IndianHeritageDesign.
  • Create YouTube documentary-style content showing 360° carpet weaving process from silk dyeing to final knot; target home décor and luxury lifestyle channels; aim for 500K+ views; include artisan stories and GI heritage angle.
  • Launch collaborative limited-edition collections with 2-3 celebrated Indian fashion designers; create exclusivity narrative; price at premium 50% markup; distribute via high-end boutiques in Mumbai, Delhi, and Bangalore; generate PR through design publications.
