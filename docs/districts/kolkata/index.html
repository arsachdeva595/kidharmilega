Leather goods

Kolkata has a rich legacy of leather craftsmanship dating back centuries with established artisan communities in Barabazar, Entally, and Bagree markets. The city's proximity to major trade hubs, access to quality leather suppliers, and developed supply chains make it an ideal location. The existing cluster of skilled leather workers and established retail networks provide immediate market access and mentorship opportunities.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kolkata.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Barabazar, Entally, and Bagree leather markets in Kolkata to study existing products, pricing, and craftsmanship. Spend 2-3 weeks shadowing established leather artisans. Research leather tanning processes at local tanneries. Cost: ₹5,000-10,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Establish relationships with leather suppliers in Barabazar (vegetable-tanned leather at ₹600-1,200/sqft) and hardware suppliers in Park Circus. First batch of 50 units requires ₹40,000-60,000 for premium leather, hardware, and dyes from established suppliers like Kolkata Leather House.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing artisan clusters in Entally or contract with master craftspeople in Barabazar on piece-rate basis (₹150-300 per item depending on complexity). Alternatively, establish small 2-3 person workshop with hand tools (cutting knives, stitching equipment, edge bevelers). Production capacity: 30-50 units/month initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register as MSME (Udyam) under Handicraft sector. Obtain IEC code for export. Register trademark for brand name at Indian IP office (₹5,000-10,000). Get Shop & Establishment license from local BMC. Obtain Hallmark/authenticity certification from All India Artisans & Craftworkers Association.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (15% commission), Amazon Handmade, and own Shopify store. Approach boutiques in Park Street and South Kolkata for wholesale stocking. Create Instagram/Pinterest showcase of designs and artisan stories. Partner with corporate gifting platforms like GiftNiftY and Woovly for bulk orders.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product range to include footwear and home décor items. Build export channel through leather trade associations. Hire 2-3 additional artisans. Apply for SFURTI cluster development grant. Target international trade shows and B2B platforms like Global Sources and Alibaba.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create behind-the-scenes Instagram Reels and TikTok videos showing artisans handcrafting leather goods in Barabazar markets. Humanize the craft story. Target hashtags: #HandmadeLeather #KolkataArtisans #VintageLeatherGoods. Budget: ₹5,000/month.
  • Partner with lifestyle bloggers and YouTube creators focused on sustainable fashion and artisan goods. Send sample products for unboxing reviews. Focus on eco-conscious positioning. Budget: ₹30,000-50,000 per collaboration.
  • Run targeted Google Shopping ads and Facebook ads focusing on gift-giving occasions (Diwali, anniversaries) with emphasis on durability and Indian craftsmanship. Budget: ₹15,000/month.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (25-35% of project cost) for self-employment/group entrepreneurship in handicraft sector

PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹3 lakh loans at 5% interest for traditional craftspeople in leather, plus toolkit assistance of ₹15,000

subsidized raw material procurement, export promotion support, and artisan welfare benefits
