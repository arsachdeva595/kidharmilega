ODOP Product

Ponduru Cotton Sarees

Srikakulam district has a centuries-old handloom weaving tradition centered in the Ponduru cluster, with abundant access to fine cotton yarns and a community of skilled pit-loom weavers who have perfected the craft across generations. The region's climate and proximity to coastal areas historically supported cotton cultivation and natural dye sourcing, making it the epicenter of authentic cotton saree production.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Srikakulam.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ponduru village (near Srikakulam town) to observe pit-loom weaving clusters; meet weavers at Weavers Service Centre, Ponduru. Study natural dye techniques at local dye houses. Connect with APCO (AP Handlooms) office for artisan database and quality standards.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton yarn (40s-60s counts) from Srikakulam cotton mills or Tamil Nadu spinners; cost ₹800-1200/kg. Procure natural indigo from Telangana/Rajasthan suppliers (₹400-600/kg). First batch of 10 sarees requires ~₹60,000-80,000 in raw materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with Ponduru weaver groups (5-10 weavers minimum) via direct engagement or SFURTI-supported collectives. Establish quality contracts specifying thread count, weave density, and dyeing standards. Visit weekly to ensure consistency. Budget ₹15,000-25,000 per saree for weaving labor.
4
Brand & Register
Register as Udyam (Micro) on udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC code via egov.eidb.gov.in. File trademark for brand name with IP India. Obtain handloom certification from APHandlooms.in or district cooperative. Create GI awareness campaign even without formal tag.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy (10-15% fees), own Shopify store with Razorpay/CCAvenue, Instagram Shop with Direct Messaging. Price at ₹8,000-15,000 per saree wholesale, ₹18,000-28,000 D2C. Contact niche boutiques in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai; use WhatsApp catalogs for B2B. Run first campaign with 20-30 pieces via micro-influencers (heritage/ethnic fashion niche).
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand weaver network to 20-30 artisans; invest in natural dye lab for consistent color batches. Launch capsule collections quarterly. Partner with wedding planners and corporate offices for bulk orders. Hire operations lead to manage artisan payments/QA. Explore Amazon Handmade and Nykaa partnerships. Target ₹1-2 Cr ARR by year 2.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Launch 'Weaver Stories' video content on YouTube/TikTok featuring actual Ponduru artisans — 3-5 min mini-docs on dyeing, weaving, family history — optimize for 'handmade saree' SEO keywords
  • Run Facebook & Google Ads targeting 'heritage cotton sarees' + 'ethical handloom' + 'natural dye sarees' for metros; seasonal campaigns for Diwali/weddings; retarget website visitors with ₹2-5 ROAS target
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
SFURTI Scheme
₹25-50 Lakhs for cluster development, artisan training, quality improvement, marketing (handlooms category under Ministry of Textiles)
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹3 Lakh collateral-free loan for artisans/weavers with 0% interest first 12 months; also includes business training and toolkit assistance
🏚
AP Handlooms Development Scheme
rebate on loom investment (30-50%), dye subsidy (20-30%), market linkage support through AP Handlooms Corporation
🏚
AP Women Entrepreneurs Development & Facilitation Scheme
₹10 Lakhs loan at 5% interest for women-led handloom enterprises; technical support from district MSME office
