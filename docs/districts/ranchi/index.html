ODOP Product

Ready Made Garments

Ranchi has established itself as a major RMG production hub due to abundant skilled textile labor, proximity to cotton-producing regions, well-developed supply chain infrastructure, and presence of both large manufacturers and small-scale units. The city's textile cluster attracts national brands and exporters, making it ideal for garment entrepreneurs seeking established market linkages and workforce availability.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ranchi.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ranchi's garment clusters in Oraon Basti and Namkum areas; meet 5-10 established manufacturers; attend IGTEX (Indian Garment & Textile Expo) or local trade fairs; study competitor pricing and production capacities; understand GST compliance (5% for RMG).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cotton/polyester blended fabrics from Surat or Tiruppur suppliers (₹150-300/meter bulk pricing); procure trims, buttons, zippers from Ranchi's local suppliers or Delhi's Chandni Chowk; negotiate 30-45 day payment terms; first batch of 500 units budget: ₹2-3 lakhs.
3
Produce / Partner
Register with existing garment workshops in Ranchi's Oraon or Namkum clusters for contract manufacturing; alternatively, lease 500-1000 sq.ft. space (₹8-12k/month) and hire 5-8 skilled tailors; invest in basic sewing machines (₹30-40k each); establish quality control process; target 100-150 units/week initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification); obtain IEC code from DGFT Ranchi office for export eligibility; register trademark with IP India; get GST registration; obtain Apparel Export Promotion Council (AEPC) membership for export benefits; design logo and packaging with local printer (₹10-15k).
5
First Sale
List on B2B platforms (TradeKey, Alibaba, Global Sources); contact Myntra, Flipkart, Amazon B2B teams for bulk contracts; reach out to retail chains (Reliance Trends, Westside) with samples; join local GTDC (Garment Trade Development Corporation) for buyer connections; approach export houses for contract orders; use WhatsApp Business for supplier-to-retailer direct sales.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand capacity to 300-400 units/week by hiring 15-20 tailors; diversify into ethnic wear and corporate wear segments; develop relationships with 3-5 anchor brands for recurring orders; invest in finishing units (pressing, packaging); explore government export promotion schemes; target ₹2-3 Cr annual turnover by month 18.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • LinkedIn B2B outreach targeting procurement managers at retail chains and e-commerce brands; showcase product samples and manufacturing capacity with facility videos.
  • Instagram & TikTok content showing behind-the-scenes artisan tailoring and ethical manufacturing story; target fashion influencers and micro-brands seeking ethical manufacturers.
  • Email campaigns to 200+ apparel importers and distributors in UK, USA, Germany with samples; partner with India Trade Promotion Organisation (ITPO) for virtual trade shows.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
