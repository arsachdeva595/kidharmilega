Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Lunglei.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Lunglei district's agricultural department and connect with established banana farmers in Aizawl Market. Study the Mizoram Horticulture Mission clusters. Spend 2-3 weeks shadowing 2-3 banana farms in the district to understand cultivation cycles, pest management, and local supply chain. Cost: ₹15,000-25,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source 500-1000 banana plantlets from Mizoram State Horticultural Department nursery or ICAR institute in Assam (Jorhat). Land preparation and initial plantation material will cost ₹80,000-150,000 for 0.5-1 acre. Establish relationships with local organic input suppliers for manure and fertilizers.
3
Produce / Partner
Begin with 0.5-1 acre plantation on leased or owned land. Partner with local agricultural extension officers for soil testing and crop planning. Engage daily laborers from Lunglei town (₹300-400/day) for maintenance. Use drip irrigation for efficiency. First harvest typically 9-12 months after planting. Yield: 25-30 tons/acre annually.
4
Brand & Register
Register UDYAM (online at udyamregistration.gov.in) as a small agricultural enterprise. Obtain IEC from nearest DGFT office in Aizawl for export readiness. Create brand identity around 'Organic Lunglei Bananas' with NPOP certification from authorized certifying agency (₹15,000-30,000). File trademark with Indian IP Office.
5
First Sale
Launch on JioMart, Flipkart, Amazon Fresh in Tier 1 cities with premium positioning (₹60-80/kg). Simultaneously approach Aditya Birla, ITC, and local organic retail chains. Contact fruit processors in Tamil Nadu and Karnataka for bulk supply. Attend agricultural trade shows in Mumbai and Delhi. Expected first-month sales: ₹50,000-100,000.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand plantation to 3-5 acres. Develop value-added products (banana chips, banana flour) through contract with food processor in Assam. Secure Organic certification to command 20-30% price premium. Establish supply contracts with 5-10 retail chains. Explore export partnerships with Bangladesh and Myanmar importers. Hire farm manager and 2-3 permanent staff.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts showcasing farm-to-table content from Lunglei plantations; partner with 5-10 micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in health/organic food niche to reach millennial urban buyers.
  • Run WhatsApp Business API campaigns with local organic food retailers and restaurant chains in Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Mumbai offering bulk pricing and first-order discounts.
  • Create limited-edition 'Lunglei Organic Banana' packaging and participate in farmer markets in tier-1 cities (Every weekend in South Delhi, Bandra Mumbai, Indiranagar Bangalore) with promotional sampling.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM-KISAN: Scheme provides ₹6,000 annually to farmer registered in AADHAAR for direct income support during cultivation cycle.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme): Up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general category) for setting up agricultural processing or export unit.
🏚
Mizoram State Horticultural Mission: Provides 50% subsidy on high-density plantation inputs and technical support for fruit crops including bananas.
🏚
Mizoram Agriculture Mechanization Scheme: Offers 50% grant on farm equipment like drip irrigation systems and harvesting tools for smallholder farmers.
Registration required: Required — udyamregistration.gov.in
