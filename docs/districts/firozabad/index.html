ODOP Product

Firozabad Glass

Firozabad has a 300+ year legacy of glassmaking with generations of skilled artisans, established supply chains for raw materials like soda ash and silica, and a fully developed ecosystem of glass furnaces and workshops. The district's geographic location near Delhi and proximity to major markets makes it ideal for distribution. Government support through ODOP framework has further strengthened the cluster.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Firozabad.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Firozabad Glass cluster headquarters in Firozabad city; connect with 5-6 established glass artisan groups like Firozabad Glass Association; spend 2-3 weeks shadowing production at workshops in Shastri Nagar and Banasthali areas; understand GI certification standards and current export protocols.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from certified suppliers in Firozabad (Soda ash from GFCL Baroda, Silica sand from Rajasthan quarries); build relationship with local cullet dealers; first batch raw material cost ₹150K-₹300K depending on product line chosen.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established artisan workshops in Firozabad offering production-on-demand initially; negotiate per-piece rates (₹150-₹1500 per item based on complexity); alternatively, lease a small kiln space (₹50K-₹100K monthly) and hire 2-3 trained glassblowers at ₹15K-₹20K monthly wages.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME within 15 days (online, free); apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office (₹0, online); register GI-certified product status with District Industries Centre; file trademark for brand name with IP office; obtain GST registration (18% applicable); get NOC from municipal authority for kiln operation.
5
First Sale
List on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and own Shopify store with professional product photography; approach 20-30 premium home décor stores (Fabindia, The Bombay Store, Good Earth) for wholesale; apply to Export Promotion Council (EPC) for export support; attend India Expo Centre trade shows in Greater Noida; pitch to corporate gifting platforms like Giftxoxo and Artisan Box.
6
Scale
After 6 months: expand product range (add home décor, drinkware, artistic pieces); hire dedicated sales & marketing team; establish direct export relationships with 3-4 foreign distributors; apply for SFURTI support for cluster infrastructure; launch B2B platform presence; open experience center in Delhi/Mumbai for retail visibility.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels campaign featuring artisan glassblowing process, craftsmanship behind each piece, and customer testimonials; target premium lifestyle hashtags; partner with 15-20 home décor influencers (50K-500K followers) for gifting collaborations.
  • Google Shopping + Facebook dynamic ads targeting high-income households searching for 'handmade glassware,' 'heritage home décor,' and 'luxury tableware' within India and USA; budget ₹30K-₹50K monthly.
  • Participate in premium curated marketplaces like Artisan Collective, Heritage Arts, and Craft Culture; sponsor 2-3 high-end wedding expos in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore annually; generate PR coverage in Condé Nast Traveller and Architectural Digest India.
