Carpet

Kaimur district has a centuries-old carpet weaving tradition with a concentrated population of skilled artisans in Bhabua town. The region's cultural heritage and established weaver communities make it naturally suited for carpet production. Local availability of skilled labor and existing market networks provide competitive advantages.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kaimur (Bhabua).

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bhabua weaving clusters in Kaimur district; meet with established weaver cooperatives like Kaimur Handloom Federation; study pricing at Jaipur carpet markets (Bapu Bazaar); attend Craft Mela exhibitions in Delhi.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw wool from Bikaner wool markets (₹400-600/kg); cotton from Gujarat textile hubs (₹80-120/kg); natural dyes from Indigo suppliers in Rajasthan. First batch of materials for 5-10 carpets: ₹80,000-150,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established weavers in Bhabua town; visit weaver associations to identify artisans; establish fair-wage contracts (typically ₹300-500 per day per weaver); allocate 4-6 weeks per carpet production cycle.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME classification); apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Patna; register trademark with IP India; obtain GST registration; create brand identity reflecting traditional Bhabua weaving heritage.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Instagram Shop; reach out to 50+ interior designers via LinkedIn; partner with 5-10 premium home décor boutiques; participate in Craft Mela and Handmade at India exhibitions; build email list from website.
6
Scale
Expand weaver network to 10-15 artisans; establish own showroom in Delhi or Mumbai; hire dedicated export manager; develop 3-5 signature carpet designs; apply for PMEGP subsidy for production infrastructure expansion.
