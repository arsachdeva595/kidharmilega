Tarkashi Art

Mainpuri has been the seat of Tarkashi artistry for over 400 years, with hereditary artisan families perfecting the technique under Mughal patronage. The district has a concentrated cluster of skilled craftspeople and established supply chains for fine metal wires and base materials. The GI tag validates authenticity and enables premium positioning.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Mainpuri.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mainpuri town's Tarkashi cluster (main artisan areas near Jajmau and city center); spend 2-3 weeks learning from master craftspeople like those associated with Mainpuri Handicraft Cooperative Society. Connect with DC Handicraft office for artisan directory. Budget ₹5,000-10,000 for initial research and artisan interaction.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source brass and copper wires from Jaipur's Kishanpole Bazaar or Mumbai's Zaveri Bazaar (bulk order ₹30,000-50,000 for first batch). Purchase ebony/rosewood from timber suppliers in Agra or Saharanpur (₹20,000-35,000). Direct artisans in Mainpuri will advise on adhesives and hand tools (₹10,000-15,000). Total first-batch material cost: ₹60,000-100,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Formalize partnership with 2-3 established Tarkashi artisan families in Mainpuri through written agreements outlining pricing and exclusivity. Begin with custom orders (₹5,000-20,000 per piece depending on complexity). Use co-working spaces or small workshops to handle design, finishing, and quality control. Consider registering as an artisan collective or sole proprietorship initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small Enterprise) on udyamregistration.gov.in within 2 weeks. Apply for IEC code via ICEGATE for export eligibility (₹500). Develop brand identity emphasizing GI tag heritage—register trademark with IP India (₹4,500-9,000). Create GI-compliant product documentation and certificates of authenticity. Set up Instagram and website showcasing artisan stories.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and 1stDibs for international luxury buyers (list 10-15 pieces, 15-20% commission). Simultaneously approach premium Indian platforms: Jaypore, Godrej & Boyce design partners, and luxury hotel chains. Pitch to corporate buyers via LinkedIn and email for bulk gifting. Participate in Delhi Crafts Bazaar and Jaipur Craft events. Target interior designer networks in Mumbai and Delhi through WhatsApp B2B groups.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire 1-2 full-time quality checkers and design coordinators. Expand artisan partner network to 8-10 families. Launch international Shopify store with multi-currency support. Pursue B2B contracts with 5-star hotel chains and luxury furniture retailers. Apply for export subsidies via APEDA and participate in Frankfurt or Milan design fairs. Consider limited-edition collaborative designs with international designers.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels showing 60-second artisan process videos (wire bending, inlaying, finishing). Partner with 5-10 luxury interior design influencers (100K-500K followers) in India and Europe for product placement and gifting. Target hashtags: #TarkashiArt #HeritageHandcraft #LuxuryHomeDecor. Allocate ₹30,000/month.
  • Write SEO-optimized blog content on luxury décor blogs and Medium about 'Tarkashi art history', 'how to invest in heritage crafts', and 'sustainable luxury home goods'. Partner with 2-3 heritage tourism websites to list workshops. Guest post on design publications. Organic reach, minimal cost.
  • Run targeted Facebook/Instagram ads (₹20,000/month) to high-income audiences in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore, Pune) aged 35-65 interested in 'artisan crafts', 'luxury home décor', 'Indian heritage'. Retarget website visitors with carousel ads showing different product categories.
