Morigaon's alluvial plains and riverine deposits from the Brahmaputra create naturally fertile soil ideal for maize cultivation. The district's cool monsoon climate and well-distributed rainfall (2200mm annually) provide optimal growing conditions without excessive chemical inputs, enabling naturally healthier, flavorful maize varieties that command premium prices.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Morigaon.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Morigaon Agricultural Department office (Jagiroad) and connect with farming clusters in Kampur, Laharighat, and Bhuragaon blocks. Attend Assam Agricultural Fair (annual) and meet traders at Guwahati Grain Market. Study yield data from local cooperative societies and understand monsoon cultivation cycles (June-October).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source hybrid & local maize seeds from state seed farms (Assam State Seed & Organic Certification Agency). First batch: 5 acres @ ₹2.5L (seeds, labor, basic inputs). Partner with local framers in Gauripur or Kampur. Budget initial procurement cost at ₹35,000-50,000 per acre for 10-acre pilot.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish partnership with 3-5 farmer groups via FPC (Farmer Producer Company) registration or work with existing Morigaon-based cooperatives like Kamrup District Farmers' Cooperative. Arrange contract farming with fixed buyback rates. Install basic grain processing unit (drying, cleaning) at cost of ₹60-80L for medium-scale operation.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry of MSME portal) as agricultural producer/processor. Obtain IEC code from DGFT Guwahati office for export readiness. Get organic certification from APEDA-recognized agency (₹20-30K). Trademark brand name with IP office. Secure FSSAI registration for food products (₹5-10K).
5
First Sale
Launch on Udaan, BigBasket Business, and Agritech platforms (DeHaat, Farmer Fresh Now). Contact bulk buyers: Hindustan Unilever (popcorn sourcing), Nestlé India, and regional dal mills. Approach Assam-based cooperatives for distribution. Create WhatsApp bulk order groups with resellers in Guwahati, Silchar, and Dibrugarh markets. Start with 2-3 ton monthly orders.
6
Scale
After 6 months, establish processing unit for value-added products (maize flour, flakes). Expand farmer base from 5 to 20+ villages. Launch branded retail presence on Amazon, Flipkart. Target export markets through APEDA support. Build B2B relationships with 5-10 bulk buyers with annual contracts worth ₹2-5 Cr annually.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with Instagram/YouTube agri-influencers (Farming Geek, Kheti Kisaan) to showcase organic farming practices in Morigaon. Create 15-20 second videos on maize harvesting, nutrition facts, and farmer testimonials. Target urban millennials interested in organic foods. Cost: ₹2-5L for 3-month campaign.
  • Launch Udaan B2B campaigns targeting food manufacturers and traders in 50+ Indian cities. Offer first-time buyer discounts (10-15% off) on bulk orders of 500+ kg. Use Udaan's in-app ads and seller rating optimization. Expected ROI: 180-220% in 4 months.
  • Host farm-to-table events in Guwahati, Assam Agri Expo, and regional trade shows. Distribute free samples of maize products with QR codes linking to online store. Engage farmers as brand ambassadors. Create emotional storytelling content about farmer families and soil conservation.
