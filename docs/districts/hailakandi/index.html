ODOP Product

Arecanut

Hailakandi has ideal tropical-subtropical climate, well-drained laterite soils, and monsoon patterns perfect for arecanut palms. The district has a 70-year legacy of arecanut cultivation with established farmer networks, local processing units, and direct market access to betel nut trading hubs. Proximity to Bangladesh border ensures cross-border trade opportunities.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Hailakandi.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Hailakandi's Katlicherra and Haila arecanut clusters; meet 5-10 established farmers and traders; study market dynamics at Silchar APMC; understand drying, curing, and grading standards; attend Assam Agricultural Ministry webinars on arecanut cultivation
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure arecanut saplings (₹15-20 per sapling) from Assam Agricultural Department nurseries or Hailakandi cooperative societies; source fertilizers from local agricultural dealers (₹50K-80K for 2 acres annual requirement); first-batch cost for 2-acre plot: ₹200K-300K including labor, materials, and setup
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 smallholder farmers in Hailakandi under contract farming model (guaranteed buyback at MSP+10%); set up small curing unit (drying beds, fumigation chambers) costing ₹150K-250K; hire local labor experienced in harvesting and post-harvest handling; coordinate with cooperative unions for aggregation and quality control
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under 'Processing and Preservation of Fruit and Vegetable Products'; obtain IEC from local DGFT office; get FSSAI license for processed products; register brand name with Trademark Registry; obtain Organic certification from NPOP-accredited certifier (if premium positioning); join Hailakandi APMC for direct market access
5
First Sale
Launch on B2B platforms (IndiaMART, TradeKey) targeting wholesale traders and processors; direct outreach to 20+ paan shops and retailers in Silchar, Assam; contact food processors manufacturing gutka and pan masala; approach South Asian importers via LinkedIn and Trade India; list on regional e-commerce (BigBasket, LocalOkay); supply to HORECA chains in Northeast states
6
Scale
After 6 months, invest in mechanized drying units (₹300K-500K) to increase capacity and consistency; develop 2-3 value-added SKUs (powdered arecanut, spiced supari, organic variants) for D2C via Shopify/WooCommerce; establish 5-10 farmer partnerships to aggregate 50+ tonnes annually; explore export certifications (ISO 9001, GMP) and target direct-to-export model through APEDA tie-ups
