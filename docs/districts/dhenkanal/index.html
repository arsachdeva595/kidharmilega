ODOP Product

Handicrafts

Dhenkanal has preserved indigenous handicraft traditions for over 500 years, with artisan communities specializing in metal casting, wood carving, and tribal jewellery. The district's tribal population and access to raw materials like brass, bamboo, and stone make it a natural hub for authentic handcrafted goods with cultural authenticity and fair-trade appeal.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Dhenkanal.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dhenkanal's Brassinagar and Dokra clusters in Kamakhyanagar block; spend 2-3 weeks learning from established artisan collectives like Dhenkanal Brass Artisans Association; document production timelines, costs, and quality grades
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw brass from Delhi's Lajpat Rai Market or Moradabad; buy bamboo from local suppliers in Dhenkanal; first batch procurement for 10-15 signature items: ₹80,000-150,000; establish relationship with 3-4 primary material suppliers for 30-45 day payment terms
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 8-12 registered artisans in Kamakhyanagar or Dhenkanal town via written agreements; establish micro-production unit (rented 1000 sq ft space ₹5,000-8,000/month) for quality control, finishing, and packaging; implement simple tracking via Google Sheets or Trello for order management
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small Enterprise) classification within 2 weeks; apply for IEC code from Paradip Port authority; register brand name with Government of Odisha MSME department; obtain Food/Artisan certification if required; file GST registration as composition scheme (6%)
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram shop with 30-40 high-quality product photos (₹15,000 for professional photography); list 20 core products on Etsy (2-3% + $0.20 fee) and Amazon Handmade; approach 5-10 local heritage boutiques (Bhubaneswar/Cuttack) with wholesale samples; post weekly maker stories on Instagram/TikTok featuring artisan work-in-progress
6
Scale
After ₹8-12L revenue, expand artisan network to 20-25 craftspeople; launch own e-commerce website (₹50,000 setup + ₹3,000/month); apply for PMEGP/SFURTI grant for production facility upgrade; partner with 2-3 international fair-trade exporters (Mela, India Craft) for bulk orders; hire 1 full-time operations manager

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • YouTube channel 'From Forge to Home' featuring 8-12 min videos of artisan craft-making process; target DIY, heritage, and slow-living audiences; optimize for keywords like 'handmade brass décor', 'tribal jewellery', 'Odisha handicraft'
  • Partner with 5-10 Instagram lifestyle/home décor accounts (10K-100K followers) for sponsored posts and artisan takeovers; use hashtags #OdishaHeritage #SupportArtisans #EthicalCraft; aim for 15-20 collaborations in first 6 months
  • Attend IHGF Delhi (Jan/Aug), INACRAFT New Delhi, and Virasat Heritage Fair (Jaipur); collect 100+ international buyer contacts; send quarterly email newsletters with new collections and artisan stories
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general category) for setting up handicraft production unit; interest subvention + business mentoring
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹5L grant per artisan cluster for equipment, training, and marketing; no loan obligation
🏚
Odisha Handicrafts Development Scheme
₹3-10L interest-free loans for registered artisans; subsidy on raw materials, tool kits, and export promotion
🏚
Chief Minister's Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises (MSME) Promotion Scheme
5% interest subvention on loans up to ₹50L; working capital support for 2 years
Registration required: Required — udyamregistration.gov.in
