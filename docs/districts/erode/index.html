ODOP Product

Handloom and Powerloom products

Erode has 150+ years of textile weaving heritage with over 400,000 looms (handloom and powerloom combined). The district's proximity to cotton mills, established supply chains, skilled artisan workforce, and an ecosystem of dyers, bleachers, and finishers make it uniquely positioned for textile manufacturing. Over 30% of India's domestic saree production originates from Erode's textile cluster.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Erode.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Erode's textile cluster around Lakshmi Mill Road and Kumaran Nagar; spend 2-3 days meeting weavers, dyers, and cluster associations like Tamil Nadu Powerloom Association; attend monthly textile auctions at Erode Textile Market to understand pricing
2
Source Raw Materials
Source yarn from Erode's yarn suppliers (e.g., around Mogalaipuram or Lakshmi Mills area) — first batch of 500 meters costs ₹50,000-₹1,50,000 depending on quality; establish credit terms with 2-3 suppliers; visit dyeing units in Tiruppur (45 km away) for bulk dyeing at ₹40-80/meter
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 powerloom or handloom operators in Erode (production cost ₹80-250/meter depending on design complexity); sign contracts ensuring quality and delivery timelines; for handlooms, work with artisan cooperatives like Erode Handloom Weavers Association for authentic collections
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small enterprise) at udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office in Coimbatore; register business name and GST in Erode; file trademark for brand at IP India website; set up social media handles and basic website
5
First Sale
Launch on Meesho and Amazon Handmade to reach tier-2/3 cities; create WhatsApp Business catalog and run targeted ads to women's groups; sell bulk orders to regional retailers in Tamil Nadu, Telangana, Karnataka; approach export houses in Coimbatore for international orders; organize pop-up stores during festival seasons
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire a design team and launch 2-3 seasonal collections; establish direct export relationships with 2-3 overseas distributors; build B2B partnerships with fashion retail chains like Westside or FabIndia; invest in digital marketing (Instagram/Pinterest); expand to bed linen and home furnishing lines; consider contract manufacturing for established brands

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showcasing weaver profiles and behind-the-scenes loom work, tagged with #ErodeTextiles #ArtisanPride — drive to Etsy and website
  • Partner with wedding planners in Bangalore and Chennai for bulk saree orders; offer 15-20% B2B discounts for bulk minimum 10 pieces
  • Run TikTok/Instagram campaigns targeting affluent women (25-45 years) with reels about textile history and authenticity; collaborate with micro-influencers in lifestyle/fashion niches for 4-5 sponsored posts monthly
