ODOP Product

Crewal Based Shawl

Bandipora has a deep-rooted tradition of fine textile craftsmanship, particularly in crewel embroidery passed down through generations. The district's proximity to Kashmir Valley's wool sourcing and its skilled artisan community make it the natural hub for authentic, handmade crewel-based shawls. The cool climate and cultural heritage of the region have sustained this craft for centuries.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bandipora.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bandipora's Crewal Cluster and connect with master artisans at the Bandipora Handloom Cooperative; spend 2 weeks learning crewel techniques and understanding cost structures; engage with established exporters in Srinagar's Lal Chowk market.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fine Merino wool from Srinagar wholesale markets (₹400-600/kg) and crewel threads from Agra thread dealers; establish relationships with 2-3 reliable suppliers for first batch of 50-100 shawls costing ₹3-5L for materials.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 10-15 skilled artisans from Bandipora through the local cooperative; provide design briefs and material supply; establish quality checks every 2 weeks; consider starting with semi-finished shawls and adding embroidery to control costs.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam for MSME benefits; apply for IEC code from Srinagar DGFT office for export eligibility; file trademark for brand name with IP office; obtain Handloom Mark certification from Ministry of Textiles to authenticate handmade status.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Jaypore, and Mirraw with 20-30 product listings; pitch to luxury marketplaces like Sarai and Anavila; approach high-end hotel gift shops in Delhi, Mumbai, and Goa; create Instagram content showcasing artisan stories and behind-the-scenes production.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire a marketing manager to manage B2B wholesale relationships; expand to 30-40 artisans; launch a brand website with SEO optimization; participate in international textile fairs (Dubai, London); explore corporate gifting partnerships with luxury hotels and airlines.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showing real artisans stitching shawls with storytelling about their craft heritage; partner with micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in luxury lifestyle and heritage fashion niches.
  • Email marketing campaigns to high-net-worth individuals and luxury fashion enthusiasts with personalized shawl design options; offer limited-edition seasonal collections to create urgency.
  • Content marketing through a blog on sustainable luxury and Kashmir textile history; target organic search for 'handmade crewel shawl' and 'Kashmiri embroidery' keywords; publish on Medium and LinkedIn.
