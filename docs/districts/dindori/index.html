ODOP Product

Kodo Kutki

Dindori's semi-arid climate, tribal farming heritage, and soil composition are naturally suited to millet cultivation. The region's traditional knowledge of sustainable farming and organic practices has preserved Kodo Kutki cultivation for centuries, making it an authentic source. Government push for millets under PM Poshan and nutritional security initiatives has revived commercial interest in this district.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Dindori.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dindori millet clusters in Amba, Khargapur, and Barhi villages. Spend 2-3 weeks understanding farming cycles with local cooperative societies like Dindori Farmer Producer Company. Study competitor packaging at health stores in Indore and Bhopal. Cost: ₹15,000-25,000.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Kodo Kutki seeds from Dindori district cooperative unions or direct farmer networks. For first batch (500 kg), expect ₹40,000-60,000. Establish relationships with 10-15 farmers; arrange pre-season contracts at ₹18-22 per kg. Storage facility near Dindori town: ₹5,000-8,000/month.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with SFURTI-supported processing units in Dindori or neighboring Mandla for cleaning, sorting, and packaging. Initial outsourced processing: ₹2-3/kg. Alternatively, invest in small-scale pulverizer and packaging machine (₹400,000-600,000) for in-house flour production. Train 2-3 local workers.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free, 15 mins) under food manufacturing. Obtain IEC code from nearest DGFT office (Jabalpur). Get FSSAI registration (₹4,000-6,000) for packaged food. Trademark brand name with IP office (₹10,000-15,000). Design packaging emphasizing 'Pure Dindori Millet' and nutritional benefits.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, Blinkit, BigBasket as 'Kodo Kutki Hero' or similar. Direct approach to Aashirvaad, Patanjali distributors. Start WhatsApp community with 500+ health-conscious users in Indore, Bhopal, Mumbai metro areas. Partner with 20-30 nature stores and organic marts. Launch basic website with Shopify. Initial target: 2,000 units/month at ₹250-350/kg.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand product line to ready-to-cook mixes and breakfast bars. Open regional distribution hub in Indore. Secure B2B contracts with 5-10 flour mills. Apply for PM Poshan subsidy program for school canteen supplies. Grow digital presence via Instagram reels on nutrition + farm stories. Target: ₹20L revenue by month 18.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 10-15 nutritionists and fitness influencers on Instagram/YouTube to create recipe videos and health benefits posts. Offer affiliate commissions (10-15%). Target: 50,000 followers, 500 orders/month within 6 months.
  • Run contextual Google and Facebook ads targeting 'millet flour', 'healthy grains', 'diabetes diet' keywords in Tier 1 cities. Budget: ₹20,000/month. Expected CAC: ₹60-80, LTV: ₹800-1,200.
  • Organize 'Farm-to-Table' agri-tourism experiences for 10-20 urban professionals per month at ₹2,000-3,000 per head. Combine farm visit, hands-on harvesting, cooking workshop. Use testimonials for B2C marketing and media coverage.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general category in rural areas) for food processing entrepreneurs in Dindori.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grants for cluster development, provides shared processing units and technical support for millets in MP.
🏚
MP Paramparagat Krishi Vikas Yojana
subsidizes organic certification and provides ₹30,000/acre support for organic millet farming in tribal blocks like Dindori.
🏚
MP Kisan Kalyan Samman Nidhi
direct cash support up to ₹2,000/month for registered farmers transitioning to value-added millet products.
Registration required: Required — udyamregistration.gov.in
