Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sri Muktsar Sahib.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sri Muktsar Sahib's main manufacturing cluster near Okha Road and Barnala Road; meet 5-10 existing implement makers to understand production processes, supplier networks, and market margins. Attend Punjab Agricultural University seminars to understand farmer pain points.
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure steel sheets and iron castings from Ludhiana's steel suppliers (Sharma Steel, Jindal Steel) and local foundries; expect ₹50,000-₹100,000 for first-batch raw materials covering 50-100 units of basic implements like ploughs or harrows.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 local fabricators and welders in Sri Muktsar Sahib on a job-work basis to avoid capital-heavy setup; begin with 2-3 standardized implement designs (plough, harrow, cultivator); invest ₹2-3 Lakh in basic workshop space with welding equipment and quality testing tools.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under MSME (Ministry of MSME) for GST and subsidy eligibility; apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export licensing; file trademark for brand name with IP office; get ISO 9001 certification to strengthen B2B credibility.
5
First Sale
Launch WhatsApp-based marketing to agricultural input dealers and village-level agents; list products on AgroStar and Ninjacart platforms; participate in APEDA and state agricultural ministry trade fairs; approach government agriculture departments for bulk tenders under MMJJSA or state schemes.
6
Scale
Expand product range to 10-15 implement types; build direct distribution network through hired agents in 5-10 districts; apply for SFURTI cluster development subsidy; explore contract manufacturing for established farm equipment brands; establish quality control lab to pursue GI certification.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create YouTube channel showcasing implement usage and farmer testimonials; target agri-tech keywords; partner with agricultural YouTubers for sponsored content and reach 50,000+ farmers monthly.
  • Run hyper-local WhatsApp and Telegram groups for village-level input dealers; share bulk order discounts and new product previews; offer 5% referral bonus to dealers bringing new bulk customers.
  • Sponsor and exhibit at major state/national agricultural fairs (Punjab Kisan Mela, Dilli Kisan Ghat) with working demos; collect farmer contacts for direct B2B outreach campaigns; offer first-order discounts to fair visitors.
