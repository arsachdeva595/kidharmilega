Molakalmuru Sarees

Chitradurga has a rich tradition of handloom weaving spanning generations, with Molakalmuru taluk being the epicenter of this craft. The district's cotton cultivation and skilled artisan base provide natural supply chains. The GI certification (2005) ensures authenticity and market differentiation, making it ideal for premium product positioning.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Chitradurga.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Molakalmuru weaving clusters in Chitradurga (40km from district HQ); connect with weavers via Chitradurga District Handloom Development Society; study 3-4 established brands like Krishnapura, Malwa Sarees; spend 2 weeks learning loom mechanics and design variations.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fine cotton yarn from Coimbatore spinning mills (bulk 100kg = ₹45,000-55,000); natural dyes from Jaipur suppliers (₹8,000-12,000 first order); engage 2-3 trusted weaver families in Molakalmuru for consistent supply; first batch cost (50 sarees) = ₹2.5L-3.2L including dyes and labor.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 3-5 experienced weavers in Molakalmuru taluk offering 35-40% commission per saree; establish quality checkpoints for thread count (120s-180s cotton), design consistency, and defect rejection; negotiate production timeline (12-15 sarees/loom/month); organize monthly design innovation meetings.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Ministry of MSME portal) as handicraft business; apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for exports; trademark brand name via IP India; obtain GI product certificate confirmation letter from District Handloom Office; print tag with GI certification number; set up GST registration as regular taxpayer.
5
First Sale
Launch Instagram/Pinterest with behind-the-scenes weaver stories and 3-4 saree posts weekly; list on Amazon Handmade India and Flipkart Sarees category; create Shopify store with 3-5 high-resolution photos per saree; reach out to 20 ethnic wear Instagram influencers (10-100K followers) for gifting/review; target Mehndi season, Diwali, and wedding season with email campaigns; offer free shipping on orders above ₹8,000.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 8-10 weaver partnerships; launch a 'Saree-of-the-Month' subscription box at ₹12,000/quarter; apply for SFURTI cluster development grant to support weaver collectives; establish pop-up stores in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai metros during festival seasons; develop B2B relationships with 15-20 ethnic boutiques; explore export to Singapore and Dubai Indian diaspora markets via trade portals.

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
up to ₹15,000 interest-free credit + 5% govt subsidy on raw material purchases for handloom artisans and entrepreneurs.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (25-35% of project cost) for manufacturing enterprise in handloom sector with fallback loan from NBFC.
🏚
Karnataka Handloom Policy 2024
provides 50% subsidy on power loom machinery, raw material cost rebate, and skill development grants for weavers.
🏚
Karnataka MSME Cluster Development Scheme
support for GI-tagged product clusters including common facility centers, training, and market linkage in Molakalmuru.
