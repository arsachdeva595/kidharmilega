ODOP Product

Chillies

Khammam's black soil, semi-arid climate with 750-850mm rainfall, and 25-year tradition of chilli cultivation create ideal conditions for high-capsaicin varieties. The district produces 18% of India's chillies with established farmer networks and direct market linkages to spice exporters in Guntur, Visakhapatnam, and national processors.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Khammam.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Khammam's Jagityal and Shadnagar mandals (major chilli hubs); connect with Khammam District Farmers' Association and APMAS (Andhra Pradesh Mutually Aided Co-operative Societies); study Guntur spice export networks; attend Spice Board's CDRI (Central Institute for Research on Cotton Technology) workshops.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw chillies from farmer collectives in Khammam during Oct-Apr harvest; first batch of 5 MT costs ₹8-12 L; establish contracts with 20-30 farmers at MSP+10% or direct purchase from mandis (Khammam, Jagityal, Warangal mandis).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing SFURTI clusters in Khammam for drying, grinding, and packaging; or lease a 2000 sq ft processing unit (₹30-50K/month) with drying equipment (₹50-80L), mills (₹15-25L); hire 8-12 workers trained in food safety (FSSAI standards).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (Micro/Small Enterprise); obtain FSSAI License (₹5000-20K); apply for IEC code via DGFT portal; register brand name (₹10-15K); obtain GradeA food processing certification; comply with Spice Board standards for export packaging.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart, JioMart with ₹2-5L initial investment; approach regional spice wholesalers in Hyderabad, Mumbai, Chennai directly; pitch to organized retail (DMart, Bisleri, ITC) with samples; use B2B platforms like TradeKey, Global Sources for export leads.
6
Scale
After 6 months, scale processing capacity to 25 MT annually; build direct farmer contracts with 50+ growers; establish export certifications (ISO 22000, organic certification if applicable); expand to 5-7 SKUs (whole dried, powder, organic, smoked variants); hire dedicated export sales team; target 40% revenue from exports by year 2.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram & YouTube strategy: Partner with food vloggers and cooking channels to showcase Khammam chillies in regional Indian recipes; use UGC from home chefs; target 500K followers in 6 months with ₹2-3L ad spend.
  • B2B LinkedIn campaigns: Target food manufacturers, restaurant groups, export traders in India and SE Asia with case studies on yield, flavor profiles, and certifications; webinars on supply chain consistency.
  • Amazon A+ content & SEO: Invest ₹50K in high-quality product photography, detailed descriptions with heat levels (Scoville scale), origin story, and recipe ideas; optimize for keywords like 'Khammam chillies,' 'Indian red chillies,' 'export-grade spices.'
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (25% for SC/ST/OBC, 15% general) for new food processing units.
🏛
MUDRA Loan (PMMY)
up to ₹10L collateral-free loans for small agribusiness ventures at 8-10% interest via SBI, ICICI, RBI-notified banks.
🏚
Telangana Agri-Export Promotion Scheme
5% subsidy on export value, support for certification, transport rebates for agricultural products.
🏚
Telangana Farmer Producer Organization (FPO) Support
subsidized cluster formation, training, equipment grants up to ₹25L for collective processing and marketing.
