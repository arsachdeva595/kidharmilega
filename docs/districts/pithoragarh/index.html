ODOP Product

Woollen Product

Pithoragarh's elevation (1,200-3,600m) and cold climate create ideal conditions for raising wool-bearing livestock. The region has a centuries-old tradition of hand-spinning and weaving wool, with local artisans maintaining traditional techniques. Access to pure, high-quality wool from local herds provides a sustainable raw material advantage.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Pithoragarh.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pithoragarh town and Dharchula cluster; meet artisans at handloom centers under District Handloom Office; study competitor products at Uttarakhand Handloom Board showroom in Dehradun; attend regional craft fairs.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw wool from Pithoragarh livestock breeding centers (₹400-600/kg) and natural dye suppliers in Champawat district; negotiate first batch of 500kg wool at approximately ₹2.5-3L; establish relationships with 2-3 local wool suppliers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered artisan groups like Pithoragarh Mahila Kumhar Samiti or freelance weavers; start with 5-8 skilled weavers producing 20-30 pieces/month; invest in basic dyeing vats and hand looms if starting in-house unit.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise) with district office; apply for IEC code from DGFT Dehradun; register brand with IP India and obtain GI certification (process: 2-3 years, but position as 'handcrafted Himalayan wool'); obtain Handloom Mark certification from DC Handloom.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Amazon Handmade, and Instagram Shop; partner with local Pithoragarh tourism websites; reach out to premium wellness brands for co-branded products; list on regional platforms like Ishan and Baggit; attend craft exhibitions in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 15-20 weavers; develop 3-4 signature product lines (blankets, accessories, home décor); establish wholesale relationships with 5-8 premium retail chains; launch B2B corporate gifting vertical; explore export channels through craft export councils.
