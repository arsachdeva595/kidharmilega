Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bahraich.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bahraich's artisan clusters in Nanpara and Hajipur blocks; spend 2-3 weeks learning from master craftspeople; study product quality, pricing at local mandi and Craft Village cooperative outlets.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source wheat stalks directly from farmers post-harvest (September-November) at ₹150-200/quintal; buy natural dyes from Lucknow spice markets; first batch (500 units) costs approximately ₹120,000-180,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 8-12 artisans via registered SHG (Self-Help Group); establish production hub in Bahraich town; set up modular assembly line for baskets, wall hangings, and table mats with quality control checkpoints.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under Ministry of Handicrafts; apply for IEC code for exports via Lucknow office; trademark brand name with IP office; obtain eco-labeling certification (Indian Ecomark) to enhance positioning.
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Handmade, Etsy, and Indiegene; approach interior design studios in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore; pitch to corporate gifting firms; launch Instagram content showing artisan stories; partner with local Bahraich tourism board.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 25+ artisans; introduce product line extensions (larger furniture items, custom designs); establish showroom in Lucknow; apply for SFURTI cluster upgrade grant; target 50% export revenue by month 18.
