ODOP Product

Zari Zardosi

Bhopal has a centuries-old tradition of Zari Zardosi embroidery dating back to the Mughal era, with established clusters of master artisans in localities like Chowk Bazaar and Motia Pool. The district's cultural heritage, proximity to fabric markets, and existing skilled workforce make it the natural hub for this metallic embroidery craft. Government support through handicraft ministries and existing export networks strengthen its competitive advantage.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bhopal.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Chowk Bazaar and Motia Pool clusters in Bhopal to study existing Zari Zardosi artisans, their techniques, pricing, and supply chains. Attend Bhopal Handicraft Mela and connect with DC Handicraft office for market intelligence. Spend 2-3 weeks understanding cost structures and buyer networks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Zari threads from Surat's textile markets (Ashish Silk House, RSS Zari Warehouse) and local Bhopal thread merchants. Initial batch of 50 kg mixed Zari thread, beads, and sequins costs approximately ₹80,000-120,000. Establish bulk relationships to negotiate 8-12% discounts after first order.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 established Zari Zardosi artisans in Chowk Bazaar through 80/20 revenue-sharing model or fixed monthly commission. Alternatively, register as cooperative through SFURTI scheme to access subsidized workspace and shared tools. Start with 100-150 pieces/month in first quarter.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) certificate online via udyamregistration.gov.in. Apply for IEC code via DGFT within 2 weeks. Create brand identity reflecting Bhopal's heritage—design logo, website on Shopify, and secure social handles. Pursue Craft Mark certification from Ministry of Commerce for export credibility.
5
First Sale
Launch D2C via Instagram, Pinterest, and TikTok showcasing artisan stories and craftsmanship videos. List on Etsy, Craftsvilla Premium, and Amazon Handmade within month 1. Reach out to 50+ bridal wear retailers in Delhi, Bangalore, Mumbai with wholesale catalog and samples. Partner with 2-3 export trading houses in Bhopal for initial overseas orders.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 10-12 partners across Bhopal. Develop 3-4 signature collections aligned with seasonal trends. Invest ₹50,000 in influencer collaboration with 3-4 bridal fashion and ethnic wear creators. Pursue government export schemes to subsidize shipping and certifications. Target ₹15 Lakh monthly revenue by month 12.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels and TikTok videos showing 30-60 second snippets of artisans hand-stitching Zari designs, highlighting the meticulous process. Target bridal hashtags (#ZariEmbroidery #BridalEmbroidery #IndianCraft) and collaborate with 5-10 micro-influencers (50K-500K followers) in bridal/ethnic wear niche.
  • Launch Pinterest-focused strategy with high-quality product photography and design inspiration boards. Target keywords like 'Zari Zardosi work', 'Bridal embroidery India', 'Luxury ethnic wear'. Pinterest drives 35-45% of luxury craft traffic; budget ₹5,000/month for Promoted Pins.
  • Execute email marketing to luxury fashion retailers and wedding planners in top 15 Indian cities. Personalize outreach with wholesale catalogs, bulk order discounts (10% for 50+ units), and bespoke customization offers. Achieve 8-12% response rate within 60 days.
