ODOP Product

Terracotta Craft

Bankura has a centuries-old tradition of terracotta craftsmanship centered in villages like Bishnupur. The district's clay quality and established artisan communities make it the natural hub for this heritage craft. The Bankura horse's global recognition and UNESCO acknowledgment provide immediate brand equity.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bankura.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bishnupur pottery cluster in Bankura (30km from Bankura town); spend 3-5 days with master artisans; study at Indian Institute of Handloom Technology, Varanasi; research GI regulations via DC Handicraft office in Kolkata.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source clay directly from local mines near Bishnupur (₹500-800 per ton); establish relationships with 3-4 local clay suppliers; first batch of 100 units requires ₹8,000-12,000 raw material cost.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established Bishnupur artisan collectives (Bishnupur Pottery Association, Sarbojanin Club); negotiate piece rates of ₹120-250 per unit; start with 50-100 pieces monthly; hire 2-3 skilled artisans initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam via udyamregistration.gov.in (free); obtain GI certification through DC Handicraft Bankura office (₹5,000-10,000); register IEC with DGFT for export; apply for Udyam loan (₹25-50L available).
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy with 20-30 product listings (photography cost ₹3,000); list on Amazon Handmade and Flipkart; approach luxury home décor retailers (Fab India, Good Earth, local boutiques); send samples to 5 museum gift shops; post weekly on Instagram/Pinterest with artisan stories.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire 1 dedicated quality manager; expand to 200+ monthly units; launch subscription box with curated sets; establish pop-up stores in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore); seek export orders via TradeKey and GlobalSources platforms; apply for SFURTI cluster development funds.
