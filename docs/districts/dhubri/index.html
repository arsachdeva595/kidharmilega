Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Dhubri.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dhubri district and connect with farming cooperatives in Golakganj and Dhubri town blocks. Study chilli cultivation practices at Assam Agricultural University's regional extension centre. Attend spice trader meetings at Guwahati's Fancy Bazaar and Fancy Market to understand market dynamics and pricing.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source hybrid chilli seeds (Byadgi, Guntur, or local heat varieties) from registered seed dealers in Guwahati or directly from farmer groups in Dhubri. First-batch cost for 1 acre farming: ₹80,000-120,000 (seeds ₹8,000, fertilizers ₹25,000, labour ₹60,000, miscellaneous ₹25,000).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established farmer cooperatives like Dhubri District Agricultural Cooperative Society or individual progressive farmers with 2+ acres already cultivating chillies. Negotiate contract farming arrangements at ₹40-50 per kg of fresh chillies, or establish your own farming with hired labour through Gram Panchayat networks.
4
Brand & Register
Register business under Udyam portal (micro-enterprise classification). Obtain Spice Board registration for processing/trading chillies. Apply for IEC code from DGFT for export eligibility. Get FSSAI license (₹5,000-10,000) for value-added products. Brand examples: 'DhubriHeat', 'NortheastSpice', 'AssameseFire'.
5
First Sale
List on BigBasket, Jio Mart, Flipkart Premium for D2C sales. Approach regional spice traders in Assam (contact Guwahati Spice Association). Supply to restaurants and mess operators in Assam via WhatsApp groups and local contacts. Use Instagram and YouTube to showcase farming/processing, tag food bloggers.
6
Scale
After 6 months, invest in drying and grinding facilities (₹500,000-800,000) to launch chilli powder and value-added products with 60%+ margins. Build B2B relationships with larger food companies. Explore export partnerships with Bangladesh and Nepal spice traders. Develop branded retail packaging and expand to premium chains like Nature's Basket.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with food vloggers and cooking channels on YouTube to create 'Spicy Food Challenge' videos using Dhubri chillies, targeting young audiences aged 18-35 interested in authentic regional cuisine.
  • Launch Instagram Stories series showing farm-to-table journey with farmer testimonials and usage recipes, use hashtags #DhubriChillies #NortheastSpice #AssameseKitchen to reach regional food enthusiasts.
  • Organize tasting events at Guwahati's premium restaurants and food festivals (Assam Food & Beverage Fair). Provide samples to food bloggers and restaurant chefs with wholesale pricing sheets.
