ODOP Product

Carpets

Sonbhadra has a deep-rooted carpet weaving tradition spanning generations, with skilled artisans trained in intricate hand-knotting techniques. The district's access to quality wool and cotton, combined with a large pool of experienced weavers, makes it naturally suited for premium carpet production. Historical presence of carpet clusters and community knowledge transfer ensure consistent quality and design authenticity.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sonbhadra.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sonbhadra's carpet weaving clusters near Robertsganj and Duddhi tehsil. Connect with existing cooperative societies (Sonbhadra Carpet Weaver's Cooperative) and attend textile expos at New Delhi World Trade Centre to understand market positioning and competitor strategies.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source premium wool from Rajasthan (Jaipur wholesale markets) and cotton from Gujarat at ₹800-1,200/kg. Establish relationships with natural dye suppliers in Indigo. First batch cost (50 sq.ft.) approximately ₹2.5L. Partner with local artisans for raw material pooling to reduce per-unit cost.
3
Produce / Partner
Collaborate with 5-8 experienced weavers in Sonbhadra via Udyam registration agreements (ensure GST registration). Set up 2-3 handlooms or partner with existing cooperative workshops. Production cycle: 4-6 weeks per 10x8 ft carpet. Consider partnering with SFURTI-registered cooperative clusters for subsidized workspace.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) with GTIN barcode. Apply for IEC code via online portal (60 days). Create brand identity (logo, packaging, story card highlighting artisan names). Get GI registration initiated (₹2-5L legal cost, 18-24 month process) to unlock premium pricing and export certifications. Obtain ISO 9001 certification (₹50K) for B2B clients.
5
First Sale
Launch on Flipkart & Amazon with professional photography of individual carpets and artisan stories. Create Instagram & Pinterest accounts showcasing design process and heritage narrative — target accounts with 100K+ home décor followers for collaborations. Email corporate offices in NCR & Mumbai with 8-10 sample catalogs. List on 1stDibs, Etsy, and trade platforms (Global Sources) for international visibility. Run Google Shopping ads targeting 'handwoven carpet India' and 'luxury home décor.' Attend Delhi Home Expo or Mumbai Design Week (stall cost ₹1.5-3L) for B2B connections.
6
Scale
After first ₹20-30L revenue (6 months): expand artisan network to 20+ weavers. Launch 3-4 signature collections based on bestseller patterns. Hire a full-time export coordinator and apply for VKGUY scheme for branding support. Establish logistics partnerships with FedEx/DHL for export management. Set up a showroom in Delhi/Bangalore (₹5-10L annual rent) targeting interior designers. Launch subscription model for seasonal collections. Pursue B2B contracts with 5-star hotel chains and luxury residential projects.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels series 'Hands Behind the Craft' — 30-second videos of individual weavers at looms, driving engagement and emotional connection. Target hashtags #IndianHandcrafts #SlowDesign #InteriorDesign. Partner with 10-15 micro-influencers in home décor (50K-200K followers) for UGC and affiliate commissions (15% per sale).
  • Content marketing via Medium, LinkedIn articles on 'Sustainable Luxury: Why Handwoven Carpets Outlast Fast Furniture.' Submit to home décor publications (Elle Décor India, Architectural Digest India) for feature stories. SEO-optimize website for 'sustainable carpets India,' 'natural dye handwoven rugs,' ranking in 3-4 months.
  • B2B direct outreach via LinkedIn to interior designers, property consultants, and FM heads at top 50 hotels in India. Offer 10% project-specific discounts and 60-day payment terms. Create case studies with 3-4 completed projects (hotels, residences) with before/after photos and client testimonials.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma Yojana
up to ₹15L credit-linked subsidy for registered artisans and micro-enterprises in traditional crafts, plus tool kits and skill training.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan with 35% subsidy (25% for general category) for new enterprises in manufacturing sector.
🏚
UP Micro, Small & Medium Enterprises Policy (MSME)
5% interest subvention on loans up to ₹1 Cr for textile artisans in notified clusters.
🏚
Uttar Pradesh Handicraft Development Scheme
₹5-10L grant for setting up carpet weaving units and marketing support for artisans registered with cooperative societies.
