Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sarangarh-Bilaigarh.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sarangarh-Bilaigarh's primary agricultural research center and connect with established tomato farmers in Raipur and Durg districts. Study supply chains at Durg Mandi and Raipur vegetable markets. Attend Chhattisgarh Agricultural University seminars on tomato cultivation.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source hybrid tomato seeds (Sungold, Arka Vikram varieties) from IARI or regional agro-dealers in Raipur (₹15,000-25,000 per acre for 1-acre trial). Purchase organic fertilizer and supplies from Chhattisgarh State Agricultural Department networks. First-batch sourcing cost: ₹80,000-150,000 for 1-acre trial cultivation.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 15-20 smallholder farmers in Sarangarh-Bilaigarh using contract farming models (MSP guarantee). Set up a 50-ton capacity cold storage unit (₹20-30 lakhs) in district headquarters. Invest in basic grading/sorting equipment and packaging line. Start with 5-10 acres, scale to 50+ acres by year 2.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSME within 15 days online. Apply for IEC (Import-Export Code) via DGFT portal for export readiness. Register food business license (FSSAI) for any processing activity. Trademark brand name through IP India. Create GST compliance structure for B2B and D2C channels.
5
First Sale
List on BigBasket, Blinkit, Zepto with fresh tomato SKUs (₹30-45 per kg, 5-7% commission). Approach regional restaurant chains (Dominoes, Pizza Hut suppliers) for bulk contracts. Supply to organized retail (Reliance Fresh, Spencer's) via distributor partnerships. Launch WhatsApp/Telegram group for direct-to-consumer pre-orders in Bilaspur and Raipur.
6
Scale
Expand farmer network to 50+ contract growers by month 9. Invest ₹40-50 lakhs in tomato processing unit (paste/sauce production). Obtain organic certification to command 15-20% premium. Target regional hotel chains and food manufacturers for B2B contracts. Explore export partnerships with APEDA-registered buyers for Bangladesh and UAE.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram & YouTube Reels campaign showing farm-to-table story with farmer testimonials and recipe videos, targeting urban metros. Partner with food/nutrition influencers for organic reach.
  • B2B WhatsApp/Telegram marketing to restaurant owners and food manufacturers with daily inventory updates, pricing, and bulk order discounts. Create exclusive buyer groups for contract pricing.
  • Participate in Chhattisgarh AgriExpo and Durg Mandi festivals with product sampling and direct buyer meetings. Sponsor local food festivals to build brand awareness in tier-2 cities.
