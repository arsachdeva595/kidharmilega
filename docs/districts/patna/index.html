ODOP Product

Brass Metal Work

Patna has a deeply rooted artisan community practicing brass metalwork for over 500 years, with concentrated clusters in Kumhrar and surrounding neighborhoods. The proximity to raw material suppliers, established distribution networks through pilgrim tourism, and the presence of skilled hereditary craftsmen make it ideal for scaling brass metal products. The city's religious significance as a pilgrimage destination ensures constant demand for brass religious artifacts.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Patna.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kumhrar brass cluster in Patna (hub of 200+ artisans); spend 2-3 weeks observing production at 5-6 workshops; connect with Patna Brass Artisans Association; study competitor pricing on Etsy and Amazon Handmade for similar items (₹1,500-₹15,000 range).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source brass ingots/sheets from authorized dealers near Patna railway station (₹400-₹550/kg); purchase basic tools from Prem Nagar metalwork market; first batch of 100 small items (diyas, bowls) costs ₹40,000-₹60,000; negotiate 30-day payment terms.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 experienced artisans from Kumhrar on contract basis (revenue-sharing model: 40% artisan, 60% you); or establish a small 500 sq ft workshop with 3-4 semi-skilled workers and 1 master craftsman; invest in basic hand tools and 1 electric polishing machine (₹25,000).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam for MSME benefits; apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office (Patna); file brand name at IP office; get BIS certification for food-contact brass items; obtain FSSAI license if selling utensils; create GST registration.
5
First Sale
List 50-100 products on Etsy with detailed photography and heritage storytelling (target 200-500 first orders); simultaneously approach 10-15 home décor retailers in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with catalog; approach 5 pilgrimage centers (Gaya, Varanasi) with bulk offers; start Instagram content around artisan stories (₹2,000/month budget).
6
Scale
After 6 months (target ₹15-20L revenue): hire dedicated e-commerce manager; expand to 3-4 additional online platforms (Amazon, Flipkart); establish showroom in New Delhi or Mumbai; apply for SFURTI cluster support for 5-10 artisans; develop 5-6 new product lines based on customer feedback; invest in Instagram/Facebook ads (₹10,000/month); target corporate bulk orders worth ₹50,000+.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create short-form video content on Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts (1-2 per week) showing artisans hand-crafting pieces from raw brass; use hashtags #PatnaBrassWork #ArtisanHeritage #BrassMetalcraft; target DIY and home décor audiences; goal: 50K followers in 6 months.
  • Partner with 5-10 micro-influencers (50K-500K followers) in home décor and spiritual wellness niches; send them ₹3,000-₹5,000 curated brass sets; request unboxing and styling videos; track affiliate links for sales attribution.
  • Launch email marketing campaign to 5,000 Etsy/Shopify store owners selling home décor; offer wholesale pricing ₹400-₹800 per item with 15% bulk discount; segment by region and send region-specific product recommendations monthly.

PM Vishwakarma Yojana
up to ₹15,000 toolkit support + ₹5,000 monthly stipend for 3 months for registered artisans; covers tool purchase and working capital

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy (35% of project cost) for manufacturing; processing through District Industries Centre Patna

₹5 lakh interest-free loan for youth entrepreneurs in traditional crafts; processing through BRLPS

Bihar Heritage Crafts Development Scheme
up to ₹10 lakh grant for artisans scaling traditional metalwork; includes raw material subsidy of 10-15%
