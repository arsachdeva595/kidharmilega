ODOP Product

Mango

Jagtial's semi-arid climate, black soil composition, and irrigation infrastructure from the Godavari river create ideal conditions for cultivating premium mango varieties. The district has over 150 years of mango farming heritage with established farmer networks and cold-chain infrastructure, making it a natural hub for commercial mango trade and value-addition.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Jagtial.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Jagtial town's wholesale mango markets (near APMC yards) during March-June season; connect with established exporters in Jyothinagar and Tandur Road areas; study cold-chain networks at Jagtial and Karimnagar; interview 10+ farmers on yield, pricing, and logistics.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh mangoes directly from farmer cooperatives (Jagtial District Horticulture Department) or APMC during peak season (April-June); first batch cost: ₹2-3L for 20 metric tons; establish direct relationships with 5-7 farmers for consistent supply.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing cold-storage facilities in Jagtial town (₹15K-25K/month rental) or lease a small unit; invest in grading, washing, and packaging equipment (₹2-3L); hire 5-8 trained staff for sorting and packing; alternatively, outsource packaging to SFURTI clusters in district.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam MSE (udyamregistration.gov.in); apply for IEC code from DGFT (Delhi) within 7 days; obtain FSSAI license for food processing if making value-added products; register brand name with IP office; get phytosanitary certification from Agricultural Export Inspection Council.
5
First Sale
List on JioMart, Blinkit, and BigBasket as a supplier; join B2B agritech platforms (AgroStar, DeHaat, Ninjacart); connect with export houses through TradeKey and Global Sources; sell to premium retailers in Hyderabad, Bangalore, Mumbai via direct pitches; launch WhatsApp Business catalog for corporate gifting.
6
Scale
After 6 months, invest in own cold-storage unit (₹25-40L) for 100+ MT capacity; develop 2-3 value-added products (mango pulp, dried chips); establish direct contracts with 3-5 export companies; scale to other seasons (supply dried mango in off-season); open small processing unit under SFURTI scheme.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels showing mango farm-to-table journey; partner with food influencers in metros for unboxing videos; run seasonal campaigns during March-June peak harvest showing real farmer stories.
  • Launch corporate gifting campaigns on LinkedIn targeting bulk buyers; offer branded gift boxes with custom packaging; partner with corporate event planners in Hyderabad and Mumbai.
  • Run WhatsApp marketing to export houses and bulk buyers with price catalogs, supply capacity, and quality certifications; create exclusive early-bird deals for bulk pre-bookings.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan at subsidized rate with 5-year repayment for agricultural enterprises.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
grant of ₹2-5L for cluster-based cold chain and processing units in Jagtial mango cluster.
🏚
Horticulture Mission for North-East and Himalayan States extended to Telangana
subsidy on cold-storage and post-harvest equipment up to 40-50% of cost.
🏚
Telangana Krishak Samman Nidhi
support for agricultural exports and value-addition with interest-free loans up to ₹10L for farmers and agri-entrepreneurs.
