ODOP Product

King Chilli

Leparada's high-altitude terrain and cool climate create ideal conditions for cultivating intensely hot chillis with superior capsaicin levels. The district has a 200+ year tradition of King Chilli cultivation passed through generations of local farmers, making it the authentic source for this prized ingredient.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Leparada.

1
Learn / Research
Visit local farming clusters in Pangin and Namsai blocks where King Chilli is predominantly grown; connect with Leparada Agricultural Department and meet 3-4 established local farmers to understand yield, pricing, and seasonal cycles
2
Source Raw Materials
Source seeds from certified farmers in Leparada district (₹5,000-10,000 for 500g); buy organic fertilizers from local agricultural input shops in Itanagar; first-batch cost for 0.5 hectare trial ₹40,000-60,000
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-8 local farmers via contract farming agreements (minimum 0.5-1 hectare); establish a small processing unit (100-200 sq ft rented space) in Pangin or district headquarters for value-addition; hire 2-3 local laborers initially
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam under Ministry of MSME (online, free); apply for IEC code from nearest DIRECTORATE OF FOREIGN TRADE (Guwahati or Delhi); register brand name with Intellectual Property Office; obtain FSSAI license (₹5,000) for food processing; get organic certification if applicable
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and JioMart with fresh and processed variants; create Instagram/Facebook pages showcasing farm-to-table story; approach 5-10 regional spice exporters and hotel chains; list on organic marketplace like Jivocart and FreshToHome
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 2-3 hectares of contract farming; introduce 3-4 product variants (flakes, powder, sauce); set up cold-storage facility (₹2-3L) in Itanagar; hire dedicated export coordinator; approach international B2B portals like Alibaba and Global Sources
