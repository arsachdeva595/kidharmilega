Jaggery

Mandya has ideal climate, fertile black soil, and abundant water resources from the Kaveri River system, making it India's prime sugarcane-growing region. The district has established infrastructure, skilled artisans with multi-generational jaggery-making expertise, and a 200+ year legacy of traditional jaggery production that commands premium prices in domestic and export markets.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Mandya.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mandya town's sugarcane market (Mandya Agricultural Produce Market Committee) and establish relationships with local sugarcane farmers in Krishnarajpet taluk. Attend the annual Mandya Sugarcane Festival (Oct-Nov) and study 3-4 existing jaggery units in Nagamangala and Maddur taluks. Cost: ₹5,000-10,000 travel.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh sugarcane from licensed farmers in Mandya district at ₹350-400 per quintal (200 quintals = ₹70,000-80,000 for first batch). Partner with existing jaggery processing clusters in Maddur (35 km from Mandya town) for initial production runs. First batch cost: ₹85,000-95,000 including ingredients and processing.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established jaggery artisans in Maddur cluster (known for 150+ traditional units) through revenue-sharing agreements (20-25% artisan margin). Alternatively, lease a 500 sq ft unit with a bagasse-fired furnace (₹15,000-20,000/month) near sugarcane mandis. Produce 50 kg batches weekly initially.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) online at udyamregistration.gov.in within 2 days, cost-free. Obtain IEC from DGFT portal (₹500) for export. Secure FSSAI registration (₹2,000-5,000) and ISO 9001 certification (₹15,000-25,000). Register brand name under TMR India and design packaging meeting exported country standards.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Pantry & Flipkart Wholesale with 500 units initial listing at ₹180-220/kg. Simultaneously approach organic retailers (Nature's Basket, Whole Foods type stores) and ayurvedic brands (Patanjali distributors) for bulk orders (minimum 100 kg). Use Instagram/Facebook with farmer-to-table storytelling videos. Cost: ₹20,000-30,000 for photography and content.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 3-4 production centers across Mandya to meet seasonal demand. Apply for SFURTI cluster certification to access ₹1 Cr cluster development fund. Launch premium gifting line for festive season targeting tier-1 cities. Establish direct exports to 2-3 countries via trade shows (annual Gulfood, Organic World Expo). Target ₹1 Cr revenue by month 18.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 5-10 Instagram micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) in health/wellness space for 2-week product placement with farmer-origin storytelling content. Cost: ₹30,000-50,000 per influencer.
  • Create YouTube channel featuring 'From Farm to Kitchen' documentary-style content showing Mandya sugarcane harvesting, traditional jaggery-making, and recipe tutorials. Target 1M views in 6 months via organic SEO. Cost: ₹40,000-60,000 for production.
  • Launch WhatsApp Business channel for B2B buyers with bulk ordering functionality, compliance certifications, and real-time supply updates. Join 10-15 trade B2B groups targeting bakeries, ayurvedic companies, and exporters. Cost: ₹5,000-10,000 for platform setup and account manager.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidy for MSMEs in food processing with 35% subsidy for general category.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹1 Cr cluster development fund for identified traditional jaggery clusters in Karnataka.
🏚
Karnataka Food Processing Policy 2023
25% subsidy on capital investment for units processing traditional foods, including jaggery; max ₹50 lakh.
🏚
Karnataka State ODOP Development Scheme
dedicated support for agri-processing MSMEs with export orientation, including branding, certification, and logistics subsidies.
Registration required: Required — udyamregistration.gov.in
