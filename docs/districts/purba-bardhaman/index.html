ODOP Product

Bengal Dokra

Purba Bardhaman (formerly East Bardhaman) is home to a centuries-old Dokra artisan community, particularly in villages like Bankura and Birbhum adjacent areas, with inherited expertise in lost-wax metal casting. The district has deep cultural roots in metalwork and access to raw materials like brass and copper through established supply networks. Government support through handicraft ministry and existing GI certification provides market credibility and export advantages.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Purba Bardhaman.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Dokra clusters in Purba Bardhaman (Vishnupur, Mallarpur) and adjacent Bankura district. Connect with GI certification authority at DC Handicraft office, Purba Bardhaman. Study 3-5 established Dokra exporters on Alibaba/Global Sources. Attend ITC Shakti Bazaar or Sambhali handcraft fairs in Kolkata to understand pricing and market demand.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source brass ingots (₹400-500/kg) and copper from Kolkata metal suppliers (Barabazar area) or Jamshedpur distributors. Purchase casting-grade wax from industrial suppliers in Mumbai or source locally. First batch: 30-50 pieces requiring ₹25,000-40,000 raw material investment. Identify 2-3 artisan partners in Purba Bardhaman through local Dokra associations (contact block development office).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established Dokra artisans in Vishnupur or Mallarpur villages (₹8,000-15,000 per artisan per month for ongoing production). Alternatively, lease a small 300-400 sq ft workspace (₹5,000-8,000/month) in Bardhaman town and hire trained apprentices. Establish quality control: photograph each piece, maintain production logs, ensure GI tagging on all items.
4
Brand & Register
Register business as Udyam (MSME Classification). Apply for IEC code from nearest DGFT office for export capability. Register brand name with IP office. Obtain GI license certificate from DC Handicraft (list GI certificate number on all packaging). Get product certification from BIS for metal items. Use district name prominently: 'Purba Bardhaman Dokra' in all marketing materials.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy with 15-20 hero pieces in first month (₹500-2,000 per item retail). Simultaneously open Amazon Handmade seller account (target 50+ SKUs within 60 days). Create Instagram (@PurbaBardhamanDokra) with behind-the-scenes artisan content and reels of casting process. Pitch to Fabindia, Anaar, Good Earth luxury retail networks. Email 20-30 interior designers in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore with wholesale pricing.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire a logistics coordinator for bulk shipments. Expand export via Indigo Crafts, GI Collective, or direct shipping partnerships. Introduce seasonal collections (festive, wedding, gifting). Establish pop-up shops in Delhi, Mumbai metros during peak seasons. Create corporate bulk orders program with 20% premiums. Launch video content series on YouTube and Instagram Reels showcasing artisan stories. Target B2B hospitality (5-star hotels, resorts) for large installations.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Influencer partnerships: Send 3-5 pieces to 15-20 Instagram micro-influencers (50K-200K followers) focused on sustainable living, interior design, and Indian craft. Target hashtags: #DokraArt #SustainableLiving #IndianCraft. Expected ROI: 5-8% conversion at ₹2,000-5,000 per piece.
  • Content series on Instagram Reels and YouTube Shorts: 30-60 second videos of the lost-wax casting process, artisan spotlights, and product reveals. Post 3x/week. Partner with 2-3 eco-lifestyle YouTube channels (100K+ subscribers) for collaborative video features. Estimated reach: 50K-100K per video in 3 months.
  • Google Shopping Ads + Pinterest for high-intent traffic: Target keywords 'brass Dokra figurines,' 'lost-wax metal art India,' 'Handmade brass home décor.' Allocate ₹20,000/month. Focus on retargeting website visitors. Expected CAC: ₹400-600 per customer for ₹2,500+ orders.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PM Vishwakarma
Free toolkit and ₹15,000 interest-free credit for craftspeople, 5% government-backed loan guarantee up to ₹2L.
🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
Subsidy up to 35% (₹10L max) for manufacturing units + bank loan at 5-6% interest for eligible applicants.
🏚
Bengal Handicraft Development Corporation (BHDC)
₹3-5L working capital subsidy, direct market access through state-run showrooms in Kolkata and metros.
🏚
West Bengal Artisan Welfare Scheme
Free skill upgradation training, health insurance for registered artisans, export promotion grants of ₹50,000-₹2L for first-time exporters.
