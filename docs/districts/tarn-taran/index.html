Basmati (Rice)

Tarn Taran's alluvial soil, optimal water table, and climate create ideal conditions for Basmati cultivation. The district has 40+ years of heritage farming expertise and established supply chains connecting to 80% of India's Basmati export hubs. The region's traditional paddy rotation practices naturally preserve grain quality and aroma.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Tarn Taran.

1
Learn / Research
Visit the Basmati grower clusters around Amarkot, Khanda, and Patti Ghadian in Tarn Taran. Meet established farmers and traders at the Tarn Taran Mandi (agricultural market). Study GI certification requirements via Department of Agriculture office. Attend Punjab Agricultural University webinars on Basmati quality standards. Benchmark costs at local processing mills near Amritsar (₹8-12/kg milling rates).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source certified Basmati seed from IARI New Delhi or local certified seed dealers in Ludhiana (₹80-120/kg). Negotiate 20-tonne first batch purchase from established growers in Tarn Taran (₹25-30/kg raw rice). Budget ₹5-7 lakhs for seed, fertilizer, and pesticides. Secure water access via tubewell or canal permits (verify with district irrigation office).
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 5-10 farmer collectives or lease 50+ acres directly. Engage registered milling units in Tarn Taran or nearby Amritsar for processing (negotiate ₹10-15/kg). Use traditional drying methods to preserve aroma. Obtain quality certification from APEDA or state agricultural lab. Batch testing costs: ₹2000-3000 per lot.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online via udyamregistration.gov.in (free, 5-min process). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal (₹500, needed for exports). Trademark brand name at IP India office (₹4500-9000). Get GI certification usage approval from Department of Commerce (1-2 months). FSSAI registration for packaged variant (₹100, if repackaging).
5
First Sale
List on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and Blinkit in bulk packs (₹500-1500 per pack). Contact rice exporters via ITC e-Choupal platform and Indiamart. Pitch to premium chains like Nature's Basket and Godrej Nature's Basket. Join Punjab Federation of Basmati Growers for institutional buyers. Launch Instagram + YouTube content on cultivation and recipe ideas (target 2000 followers for first bulk order inquiry).
6
Scale
Expand to 200+ acres by month 9 via farmer tie-ups. Establish own milling facility (₹50-80L capex, 3-5 tonnes/day capacity). Export 500+ tonnes annually via APEDA-registered export houses. Develop 3-5 premium sub-brands (organic, premium select, heritage blend). Launch D2C subscription model on Shopify (target ₹5Cr annual D2C revenue by month 24).
