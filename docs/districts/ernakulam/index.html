Oleo Raisins

Ernakulam has a strong historical presence in spice processing and trade, with access to high-quality spices from Western Ghats and established supply chains. The district's robust infrastructure, skilled labor force, and proximity to ports make it ideal for oleoresin extraction and export. Traditional knowledge combined with modern processing capabilities positions Ernakulam as a natural hub for value-added spice products.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ernakulam.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Spice Board of India (Kochi) and tour established oleoresin units in Mattancherry and Kumbalangi clusters. Attend Kerala Spice Merchants Association meetings; interview 3-5 existing oleoresin producers to understand extraction methods, certifications, and buyer networks.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw spices from Kochi spice markets (Mattancherry, Spice Garden Road) or direct from farmers in Idukki/Wayanad districts. Budget ₹15-20 lakhs for 5-ton starter batch of mixed spices; connect with supplier collectives like Spice Growers' Association.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established solvent extraction units in Kachumbalam or Kumbalangi (contract processing model) initially to avoid ₹15Cr+ equipment capex. After 12-18 months of market validation, invest in in-house micro/pilot extraction facility. Comply with Food Safety & Standards Authority of India (FSSAI) and ISO 22000 certifications.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in) as food processing micro/small enterprise. Apply for IEC code from DGFT (online). Obtain FSSAI license (food processing), pesticide residue testing certificates from KEBS/NABL labs. File trademark for brand at IPv India. Label packaging per Food Safety Act with extraction method, origin, batch number.
5
First Sale
Launch direct outreach to 20-30 food manufacturers via Indiamart/Global Trade Platform with COAs (certificates of analysis). Set up profile on TradeKey and Join2Exports portals. Contact APEDA-registered spice exporters for distribution. Attend Annapoorna Food Expo (Mumbai) and Spice & Natural Products Expo (Cochin). Pitch to 5-10 pharma companies via IndiAce platform.
6
Scale
After first 6 months, invest in own extraction facility (₹8-12Cr) to scale to 50+ MT capacity. Expand product range to 8-10 oleoresin variants. Target exports via food trading houses in Mumbai/Chennai; pursue ISO certifications (ISO 9001, ISO 22000). Build direct D2C channel via Shopify for bulk B2B orders; negotiate supply agreements with top 10 Indian spice brands.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create LinkedIn & B2B marketing campaign targeting food tech startups and mid-size food manufacturers with technical whitepapers on oleoresin benefits vs. whole spices; case studies with 2-3 existing clients showing cost savings.
  • Sponsor/exhibit at Annapoorna Food Expo (Mumbai) and Natural Products Expo (Las Vegas via virtual booth) to build international B2B leads; distribute samples with detailed COAs and pricing.
  • Partner with 5-10 food blogger/chef influencers to create content on oleoresin advantages (Instagram Reels & YouTube); offer commission-based affiliate program for bulk orders from manufacturers they recommend.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakhs subsidy for food processing micro-enterprises with 25% applicant contribution
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹10 crore cluster-level support for spice processing in Ernakulam; apply through Kerala SIDBI office
🏚
Kerala Food Processing Incentive Scheme
20-25% subsidy on capex for licensed food processing units; apply at Kerala Industries Department
🏚
KSWIM (Kerala Social Welfare & Investment Multiplier)
interest subsidy (2% for 5 years) on bank loans for agri-processing enterprises; available through scheduled banks in Kerala
