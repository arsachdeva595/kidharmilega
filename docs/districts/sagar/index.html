Farm Equipment

Sagar district has a strong tradition of skilled metalworkers and blacksmiths with generations of expertise in crafting agricultural machinery. The region's agricultural economy creates constant local demand for farming equipment, and proximity to major grain-growing areas in central India ensures a ready market for both traditional and modern farm tools.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sagar's established equipment clusters around Rahatgarh and Rehli blocks; connect with existing manufacturers via Sagar Chamber of Commerce; study ICAR research stations in MP for emerging equipment trends; analyze Bihar and UP farm equipment markets for benchmarking.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source iron/steel from Jabalpur or Raipur suppliers (₹15-20L for first 10-ton batch); rubber and bearings from Mumbai or Bangalore distributors; establish relationships with 3-4 metal suppliers for 45-day credit terms; estimated first batch ₹25-30L.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with established artisans in Rahatgarh cluster on revenue-sharing model (15-20% of sales); alternatively, rent 1000-1500 sq.ft. workshop space in Sagar industrial area (₹8-12K/month); hire 4-6 skilled workers; set up basic welding, drilling, and assembly stations.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, free); apply for IEC code from Sagar DGFT office for export eligibility; trademark brand name at IP office; obtain ISO 9001 certification (₹40-50K); register with Sagar MSME cluster; get GST registration.
5
First Sale
List on IndiaMART and TradeKey with 10-15 product variants; approach 15-20 district-level agricultural dealers directly with samples; participate in Sagar District Agriculture Fair (Feb-March annually); create WhatsApp catalog for local farmers; submit tenders on Government e-Marketplace (GeM) portal.
6
Scale
Expand to 2 production units in adjacent districts (Damoh, Chhatarpur) after 6 months; develop 3-4 specialized equipment lines based on demand data; hire dedicated B2B sales team; target 2-3 major states (UP, Bihar, Gujarat); invest in 1-2 prototype R&D projects for modern mechanized tools.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • YouTube channel 'SagarKisaan' demonstrating equipment use in real farms; partner with 5-10 farmers in Sagar for testimonial videos; target farmer audiences on YouTube and Facebook with village-level success stories.
  • Attend 6-8 district-level Kisan Melas annually; sponsor local agriculture exhibitions; distribute product catalogs through block-level agriculture extension officers; offer 10% early-buyer discount.
  • WhatsApp business groups with local farmer associations; direct SMS campaigns to registered farmers in MP; create Telegram channel for equipment maintenance tips; run Google Ads targeting 'farm equipment near me' searches in 5 key districts.
