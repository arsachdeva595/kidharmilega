ODOP Product

Handloom Products of Silk & Cotton

Lakhimpur has a 500+ year tradition of sericulture and handloom weaving, with ideal climate for mulberry and Eri silk cultivation. The district is home to thousands of skilled artisans trained in traditional Assamese weaving techniques. Natural water resources and proximity to silk cocoon farms make it the natural epicenter for premium silk-cotton blends in Northeast India.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Lakhimpur.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Lakhimpur Handloom Cluster in North Lakhimpur town and meet weavers at Mandira, Lakhimpur Handloom Society. Study SARD (Silk and Art Reflections Department) documentation. Spend 2 weeks understanding Muga vs Eri characteristics. Visit Jorhat sericulture farms 45km away for supply chain understanding.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source Muga silk cocoons from Assam Silk Board certified cocoon markets in Lakhimpur (₹600-800/kg). Cotton yarn from Amritsar or Erode wholesale markets (₹180-250/kg). First batch: 100kg silk + 150kg cotton ≈ ₹1,20,000. Establish direct relationships with 2-3 cocoon farmers.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with registered weaver co-operatives like Lakhimpur Handloom Cooperative or individual Master Weavers (avg. 8-12 looms). Agree on ₹150-300/meter production cost based on complexity. Start with 200-300 meters/month. Provide design inputs, quality check on 15% of output. Consider renting 2-3 looms (₹5000/month each) for in-house experimentation.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME Portal) as Handloom Manufacturing Unit. Apply for IEC (Importer-Exporter Code) from DGFT Guwahati office. Register trademark for brand name at IP India (₹4500). Get GI certification support from Ministry of Textiles for eventual geographic indication. Obtain Handloom Mark certification from Assam Handloom Board.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy and Amazon Handmade with 30-40 curated listings (sarees, dupattas, cushion covers). Partner with 3-5 ethical fashion boutiques in Bangalore and Delhi offering 35-40% margin. Use Instagram storytelling showing weaver profiles and process videos. Join heritage fashion platforms like Fairkraft and Rang Decor. Target early sales through LinkedIn to corporate buyers for bulk orders.
6
Scale
After ₹50L revenue: Hire 1 designer and 1 sales person. Expand to 8-10 artisan partnerships. Develop 3-4 signature collections annually. Build B2B wholesale pipeline to 20+ boutiques. Apply for SFURTI cluster grant for shared facilities. Launch limited edition collaborations with fashion designers. Explore Flipkart Sarees section. Target ₹5-8L monthly revenue by month 18.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels showing 60-second weaver profiles + close-ups of loom work, tagged #SustainableFashion #MugaSilk #ArtisanMade. Post 3x/week. Run carousel ads targeting fashion-conscious women 28-45 in metros (₹2000/week budget).
  • Partner with 5 micro-influencers (10k-50k followers) in sustainable fashion niche for gifting 1-2 pieces each. Ask for authentic unboxing reels. Cost: ₹5000-15000 per influencer. Target nano-influencers in ethical fashion communities.
  • Create documentary-style YouTube videos (5-7 mins) on 'A day in Lakhimpur weaver's life' and 'How Muga Silk is Made'. SEO optimize for 'handloom saree Assam', 'Muga silk fabric'. Build email list of 500+ buyers for monthly newsletter about new collections.
