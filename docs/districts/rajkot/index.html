Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Rajkot.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Rajkot's Lodhika Industrial Area and GIDC clusters (30+ km from city center) to study 200+ established machine product manufacturers. Connect with Rajkot Chamber of Commerce, attend IITMA trade shows, and review supplier networks. Study ISO 9001, TS 16949 certification requirements (2-3 months, ₹3-5L investment).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw materials from Rajkot's established suppliers: Steel from Essar Steel (Hazira, Gujarat) or ArcelorMittal (Durgapur). Aluminum/non-ferrous metals from local suppliers in Rajkot GIDC. First batch estimated at ₹15-30L for 1000 units depending on product type. Negotiate 30-45 day payment terms.
3
Produce / Partner
Establish 2000-5000 sq ft manufacturing unit in Rajkot GIDC or Lodhika area (rent ₹1-2L/month). Partner with existing job-work units initially to reduce capex. Invest in CNC machines, lathes, drilling machines (₹50-150L). Hire 8-15 skilled workers from local labor market at ₹12-18K/month.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) classification within 2 weeks (free). Obtain IEC code from DGFT in 1 week (₹500). Apply for ISO 9001:2015 certification (₹2-4L, 4-6 weeks). Register trademark with TM office (₹5-10K). Obtain BIS registration if required for product category.
5
First Sale
Launch B2B sales through TradeKey, Global Trade Atlas, and Alibaba for exports (₹5-10K/month). Create LinkedIn B2B profile and connect with 500+ OEM procurement teams. Attend 2-3 major trade shows: INDIA ENGINEERING, AUTO COMPONENT EXPO (Rajkot). Use local sales team to visit OEM plants within 200 km radius. Target initial orders of ₹5-10L from established clients.
6
Scale
After 6 months, add 1-2 new product lines based on market demand. Expand capacity by 30-50% through additional machinery (₹30-50L). Build export sales team and establish 2-3 international distributors. Achieve ISO TS16949 for automotive clients (₹5-8L). Target ₹3-5 Cr revenue by end of year 2.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • B2B LinkedIn campaigns targeting OEM procurement heads with case studies of parts supplied to Maruti, Tata, Hero, Bajaj. Budget: ₹2-3L/month for 3 months.
  • Trade show presence at INDIA ENGINEERING (Bangalore), AUTO COMPONENT EXPO (Rajkot), and IMTEX (Bangalore) with live product demonstrations. Allocate ₹8-12L per show.
  • Industry publication advertising in magazines like Automobile Industry Magazine, Metal Working Fluids India, and NASSCOM manufacturing reports. Monthly spend: ₹1-2L.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L subsidy (35% for general category, 45% for SC/ST) for new manufacturing units in non-notified areas
🏛
MUDRA Loan Scheme
up to ₹10L collateral-free loans for MSME units with 5-7 year repayment and government interest subsidy of 2%
🏚
Gujarat Enterprise Assistance Scheme
₹2 Cr interest-free soft loan for manufacturing enterprises, 50% subsidy on machinery cost up to ₹25L
🏚
Technology Acquisition Fund (TAF)
30-40% subsidy on certified CNC machines, testing equipment, and automation tools for registered manufacturing units
