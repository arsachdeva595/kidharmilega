Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Bhind.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Bhind's mustard clusters in Lateri, Gohpad, and Chakhopar; connect with NCDMA (National Cereals & Oilseeds Development Mission) office and local farmer co-operatives; study export protocols at Gwalior APMC market.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source mustard seeds directly from Bhind-based seed companies like Kaveri Seeds or from NCDMA certified farmers; first batch of 5 tonnes costs ₹70,000-₹90,000; approach local mandis in Bhind or Morena for better rates.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing RKVY-supported processing units in Bhind district or lease small-scale oil extraction machinery (₹400,000-₹800,000); outsource initial processing to established millers while building your own unit.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online at udyamregistration.gov.in) as micro-enterprise; obtain IEC code from local DGFT office (Indore); get APEDA certification for exports; register food business license under FSSAI; design GI-inspired packaging with 'Bhind' origin marker.
5
First Sale
Launch on Bijnirman (B2B), Indiamart, and Alibaba for bulk orders; list branded packs on Amazon Fresh, Flipkart, and local Grofers; pitch directly to restaurants and food manufacturers in Indore, Bhopal, and Delhi NCR.
6
Scale
Achieve ₹50 Lakh revenue by month 12 through export partnerships; apply for PMEGP or MUDRA loans for processing unit expansion; develop organic certification to access premium segments; build co-packing partnerships with larger FMCG brands.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with regional food bloggers and Instagram food influencers for unboxing and recipe videos featuring Bhind mustard in traditional and fusion cuisines; target hashtags #BhindMustard #IndianSpices.
  • Run Google Shopping campaigns targeting keywords 'pure mustard seeds', 'cold-pressed mustard oil', 'Bhind mustard export' with focus on health benefits and origin story.
  • Create B2B WhatsApp groups with restaurant owners, cloud kitchen networks, and food exporters; offer bulk discounts and direct farmer-story content to build trust and repeat orders.
