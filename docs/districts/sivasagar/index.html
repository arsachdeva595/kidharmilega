Rice & Rice Products

Sivasagar district has ideal agro-climatic conditions with abundant water resources from the Brahmaputra valley, ensuring consistent high-yield rice cultivation. The region has over a century of rice farming heritage with skilled farmers and established supply chains. Proximity to Assam's major consumption centers and neighboring states makes logistics cost-effective.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sivasagar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sivasagar's major rice milling clusters in Nazira and Demow towns; interview 10-15 established rice traders and mill operators; study parboiling techniques at Sivasagar Cooperative Rice Mill; understand GST compliance and food safety codes for rice products.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source paddy directly from farmer cooperatives in Sivasagar (contact Sivasagar District Agriculture Office); first batch of 50 quintals paddy = ₹1.2-1.5 lakhs; identify local rice millers offering custom milling services at ₹80-120 per quintal.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing FSSAI-certified rice mills in Sivasagar (e.g., local cooperative mills in Nazira) for processing rather than building own mill initially; negotiate contract milling at ₹2,500-4,000 per ton; ensure 10-ton initial production batch with quality sampling.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSMEs) online; apply for IEC code via e-Saksham portal; obtain FSSAI license (food safety) from Sivasagar District Administration; trademark brand name via Indian IP Office; create GS1 barcodes for retail packaging.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon and Flipkart with Focus India (Assam-focused) listings; partner with Assam-based online grocers (NaturalCart, Big Basket); set up WhatsApp ordering with local delivery in Sivasagar and Dibrugarh; negotiate placement in 20-30 Modern Trade outlets across Assam in first quarter.
6
Scale
Introduce 3-4 rice variants (organic, basmati blend, red rice) by month 8; secure distributor partnerships in Bihar, West Bengal, Odisha; explore B2B contracts with food manufacturers; build farm-direct sourcing to 200+ farmers to ensure consistent supply and premiumization; target ₹2 Cr turnover by year 2.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create Instagram Reels showing farm-to-table journey with Sivasagar farmer interviews; partner with 20 micro-influencers in Assam and diaspora foodie communities for authentic storytelling (₹2,000-5,000 per reel).
  • Run Google Shopping campaigns targeting keywords 'organic rice Assam', 'parboiled rice online', 'Sivasagar rice' across India with ₹40,000/month budget; use YouTube pre-roll ads in regional food and wellness channels.
  • Sponsor local food festivals (Assam Tea & Food Festival, Sivasagar Sankranti melas) with sampling booths; create TikTok/Instagram content around traditional rice-based Assamese recipes (Pitha, Poha); partner with 5-10 local Assamese restaurants for co-branded menu items.

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)

SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
up to ₹20 lakh cluster development grant for rice mills and processors in Sivasagar cluster.

50% subsidy on rice processing equipment (destoners, polishers) up to ₹2 lakhs per unit.

₹5-15 lakh grant for storage silos and food safety infrastructure for FSSAI-certified processors.
