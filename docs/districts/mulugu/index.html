Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Mulugu.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mulugu mandals (Luxettipet, Bheemini) and connect with farmer associations. Study drying-cum-storage centers in Atmakur and Bhimini. Spend 2-3 weeks observing farm-to-powder workflows; budget ₹30-50K for this research trip.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source fresh chillies directly from Mulugu farmers during peak harvest (Aug-Oct) at ₹25-35/kg. For first 5 tons, expect ₹125-175K for raw material. Partner with 2-3 farmers for consistent supply. Establish a local contact in Siddipet mandi for reference pricing.
3
Produce / Partner
Either set up a small drying and grinding unit (₹4-8L initial capex) or partner with existing spice processors in Warangal (20km away, established MSME hubs). Begin with 500kg/month pilot batches. Hire experienced drying supervisors familiar with chilli moisture control (critical for shelf life).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (online, 5 min), obtain IEC code from APEDA (Delhi) for export readiness. Register FSSAI license (₹2K, 20 days). Trademark your brand name with IP office (₹5K). Design packaging (₹15-30K for initial 5,000 units) with QR codes linking to farm story.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon, Flipkart, and Blinkit with 500-unit inventory at ₹120-150/100g. Simultaneously approach regional wholesalers in Hyderabad and bulk buyers via LinkedIn. Offer samples to top 50 food manufacturers; target first order within month 2-3. Run Instagram + Pinterest campaigns highlighting farm heritage.
6
Scale
After 6 months and ₹20-30L monthly revenue, expand to 10-ton/month capacity. Launch 2-3 specialty variants (smoked, organic, heirloom). Secure export certification (ISO 22000) and approach APEDA's export promotion programs. Build B2B relationships with 3-5 major distributors nationally.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 10-15 micro-influencers (100K-500K followers) in food/cooking niches on Instagram/YouTube for unboxing and recipe content; allocate ₹2-3L/quarter for collaborations.
  • Run targeted Google & Facebook ads (₹50K/month budget) to bulk buyers and restaurant owners searching 'chilli powder wholesale Telangana' and 'organic spice suppliers'.
  • Host 2-3 'farm-to-table' tasting events at premium restaurants in Hyderabad, Bangalore quarterly; invite food critics, chefs, and media to build brand credibility and B2B relationships.
