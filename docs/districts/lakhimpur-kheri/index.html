ODOP Product

Tribal craft

Lakhimpur Kheri's significant tribal population (primarily Tharu and other indigenous communities) maintains authentic traditional craft knowledge passed through generations. The district's forested landscape provides abundant raw materials like wood, bamboo, and natural fibers essential for tribal craft production. The cultural continuity and community-based production structure create an ideal ecosystem for scaling authentic tribal handicrafts.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Lakhimpur Kheri.

1
Learn / Research
Visit tribal settlements in Lakhimpur Kheri (Naimipur, Palia, Kheri clusters), spend 2-3 weeks learning from established artisan groups like Tharu craft collectives. Connect with DC Handicraft office in Lucknow for baseline data. Study successful models at Nand Nivas (tribal craft center) and Craftroots India initiatives in UP.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source wood from licensed timber suppliers in Lakhimpur Kheri (₹15,000-25,000 for initial 50kg batch), natural dyes from Herbal Bazaar Lucknow (₹8,000-12,000), cotton/jute from textile markets in Kanpur (₹10,000-15,000). First batch total material cost: ₹35,000-50,000 for mixed craft items.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with 5-8 tribal artisan families through cooperative model (ensure 40-50% fair trade pricing). Establish a small production workshop in Kheri town (rent: ₹5,000-8,000/month). Hire 1-2 logistics coordinators. Implement quality control via photographic documentation of each piece before dispatch.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise classification). Apply for IEC code via DGFT portal for export eligibility. Trademark brand name with IP India. Create GI certification application for tribal craft sub-category (2-3 year process). Register on Udyam Sakhi portal. Obtain Export Promotion Council (EPC) membership.
5
First Sale
Launch Etsy shop with 50+ high-quality product images showcasing tribal motifs and artisan stories. List on IndiaArtCollective.com and ArtisanSquare.com. Use Instagram (focus on behind-the-scenes artisan content, 3 posts/week). Partner with 3-5 premium home décor retailers in Delhi NCR for initial stocking. Pitch to GlobalCraft, FabIndia for wholesale relationships.
6
Scale
After 6 months: expand artisan network to 15-20 craftspeople, launch seasonal collections aligned to global décor trends (Diwali, Christmas, spring), secure 2-3 premium retail partnerships outside India, hire dedicated social media manager, establish logistics with DHL/FedEx for regular international shipments, explore corporate bulk orders via LinkedIn B2B outreach.
