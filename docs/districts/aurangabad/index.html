Marathwada Kesar Mango

Aurangabad's black soil and semi-arid climate create ideal conditions for Kesar mango cultivation with 50,000+ hectares under production. The district has 200+ years of mango farming heritage and proximity to major wholesale markets in Mumbai and Pune ensures strong supply chains and market access.

Revenue Streams

How you'll make money

Primary buyer: Premium fruit retailers, organized retail chains (BigBasket, Blink, Grofers), export-focused wholesalers, hotels/restaurants, and health-conscious urban consumers seeking affordable premium mangoes.

1Direct wholesale supply to modern retail chains and e-commerce platforms (₹80-120 per kg, 30% margin)
2Export to Middle East and Europe (premium pricing ₹150-200 per kg, higher margins)
3Value-added products — mango pulp, frozen slices, mango leather, jams (₹400-800 per unit, 55%+ margin)
4Direct-to-consumer through own e-commerce site or Keshri boxes (₹500-1200 per kg, 60% margin)

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Aurangabad.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Paithan and Vaijapur talukas in Aurangabad (mango production clusters); connect with Marathwada Mango Growers Association; attend the Aurangabad Agricultural Produce Market (AAPM) auctions to understand pricing and supply dynamics.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source directly from 10-15 farmer cooperatives in Paithan taluka (₹15-25 lakhs for 20 tonnes first batch, June-August season); negotiate contracts 3-4 months in advance; storage cost ₹50,000-100,000/month in cold chain facilities near Aurangabad city.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing mango processing units in Aurangabad (50+ small units) for pulp/value-add, or set up own cold storage and grading facility (₹8-12 lakhs); hire trained sorters and packers; obtain Food Safety License (FSSAI) for any processing.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSE/MSME) on udyamregistration.gov.in; obtain IEC code from DGFT for export readiness; register GI product certification under Ministry of Agriculture; trademark brand name with IP Office (₹20,000-30,000); cold chain/food handling certifications from APEDA.
5
First Sale
Launch on JioMart, BigBasket, Blink, and Grofers with competitive ₹120-150/kg pricing; approach horticultural export firms (APEDA-listed traders in Mumbai); contact hospitality chains (ITC, Marriott) for bulk supply; build D2C presence on Instamojo/Shopify with Instagram + Facebook ads targeting metro health-conscious consumers.
6
Scale
Aggregate supply from 50+ farmers by month 6 (achieve 100+ tonnes/season); invest ₹30-50 lakhs in own processing unit for value-added products (pulp, dried mango) by month 12; establish distribution hub in Mumbai/Pune; apply for APEDA export subsidy and CAPT scheme for cold chain investment; expand to B2B restaurant/hotel supply contracts.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels campaign with farmers and orchards (#MarathwadaKesar, #AurangabadMango, #KesariPride) targeting urban food bloggers; partner with 20+ micro-influencers in metro cities (₹30,000-50,000 per campaign).
  • Seasonal email campaigns to organized retail procurement teams (BigBasket, Blink, Swiggy Instamart) 4-6 weeks before season with sample boxes (₹5,000 per campaign); highlight GI status and cost advantage vs. Alphonso.
  • WhatsApp group outreach to 500+ restaurant and hotel chefs in Mumbai/Pune offering bulk supply at ₹110-120/kg; create recipe content (mango lassi, salads) to increase usage occasions.
