ODOP Product

Tasar Silk

Godda has ideal agro-climatic conditions for rearing Tasar silkworms, with abundant sal forests that provide natural host plants. The district has a deeply rooted artisan community with multi-generational weaving expertise and strong government support through the DC Handloom Ministry, making it the natural hub for Tasar silk production in eastern India.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Godda.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Tasar Silk clusters in Godda (Basantpur, Godda town) and meet weavers; study production at Godda Govt Silk Weaving Center; connect with MSME clusters through Godda DIC; analyze competitor products on Nykaa, Saree.com, Beuna
2
Source Raw Materials
Source cocoons from Jharkhand Govt Silk Board depots in Godda district (₹8-12 per cocoon); establish relationships with 5-10 local sericulture farmers; first batch of 100 kg cocoons costs ₹1.2-1.5L; negotiate dyeing services at Maheshpur or Kolkata dye houses
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 10-15 registered Tasar weaver households in Basantpur cluster; visit Godda Handloom Development Center for artisan links; start with contract weaving at ₹150-300 per meter labor; ensure Udyam registration for quality certifications; consider co-working with established weavers before independent setup
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (micro/small enterprise) on udyamregistration.gov.in; apply for IEC code from DGFT portal for exports; register brand name on Trademarks IPR; obtain quality certifications (ISI 6117 for silk fabric); file GI application if craftsmanship qualifies; set up GST registration for inter-state B2B
5
First Sale
Launch D2C store on Shopify or WooCommerce; list on Nykaa Fashion, Saree.com, and Beuna; create content on Instagram and Pinterest with production behind-the-scenes; approach luxury brand buyers through LinkedIn; participate in Handloom Expo in New Delhi; engage domestic premium retailers in metros
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 30+ weavers; introduce designer collaborations with upcoming fashion brands; launch heritage tourism experiences in Godda; apply for SFURTI cluster development grant; explore B2B exports through APEDA registered exporters; develop a second product line (home decor, accessories) using same supply chain

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram Reels campaign showing real weaver families at looms with storytelling voiceover; partner with 5-10 sustainable fashion micro-influencers (10K-50K followers) for gifting and storytelling
  • Content marketing blog posts on luxury lifestyle platforms (Vogue India, Indian Express Lifestyle) about Tasar silk heritage, thermoregulation benefits, and artisan stories; target SEO keywords like 'premium Tasar silk sarees India'
  • B2B networking at International Handloom Expo (New Delhi, twice yearly), PV Expo, and through targeted LinkedIn outreach to fashion brands, luxury retailers; offer samples with margin incentives

PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25L loan with 35% subsidy for micro, 25% for small enterprises in textile handloom
🏛
PM Vishwakarma Scheme
₹15K cash assistance, free toolkit, 5% concessional credit, and skill training for registered artisans and craft entrepreneurs
🏚
Jharkhand Artisan Incentive Scheme
direct support to registered weavers, export incentive up to 10% of FOB value for Tasar products

interest subsidy on term loans for handloom businesses, technology upgradation grant, and cluster development support through SFURTI
