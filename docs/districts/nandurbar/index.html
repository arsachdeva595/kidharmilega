The district's reputation for quality chillis ensures consistent buyer demand across India and international markets. Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Nandurbar.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Nandurbar chilli mandi in Tirupati or local farmer cooperatives; connect with existing processors in Tirupati village cluster; study competitor brands on Amazon and local retail; understand GI authentication process
2
Source Raw Materials
Source dried chillies directly from Nandurbar farmers or cooperative societies (cost: ₹150-200 per kg for first batch of 500 kg = ₹75,000-100,000); establish relationships through Agriculture Produce Market Committee (APMC) contacts
3
Produce / Partner
Either: (a) Partner with existing licensed chilli processing units in Nandurbar for contract manufacturing, or (b) Set up small processing unit with grinder, roasting equipment (₹400,000-600,000); hire experienced processor; ensure food license and quality testing compliance
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam online (free); apply for Food Safety and Standards Authority (FSSAI) license (₹5,000-15,000); secure IEC code from nearest Export Promotion Council for export capability; file GI-branded trademark; obtain State Agricultural Department certification for GI-tagged product authenticity
5
First Sale
Launch on Udaan (B2B) targeting regional traders; list on Amazon Pantry and Flipkart Super for retail; cold-contact 50+ specialty spice retailers across Maharashtra via WhatsApp with product samples; participate in Nandurbar ODOP seller network for bulk buyers; approach food processing units with contract manufacturing quotes
6
Scale
After 6 months: achieve ₹3-5L monthly revenue, expand to 3-4 e-commerce platforms, establish presence in 2-3 neighbouring states via local distributors, develop premium packaging variant for premium segment (₹80-100/100g), explore organic certification for premium pricing, target HoReCa (Hotels-Restaurants-Cafés) segment with bulk contracts

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Create YouTube cooking channel featuring chilli powder in 5-7 minute regional recipes (Marathi, Hindi); target home cooks aged 25-50 with SEO-optimized titles; include GI certification story in intro
  • Run Instagram Reels campaign showcasing farm-to-table journey of Nandurbar chillies; partner with 10-15 micro-influencers (10k-50k followers) in food/cooking niche for authentic reviews and giveaways
  • Sponsor food blogger taste-test events in Mumbai, Pune, Nagpur; distribute free samples with QR code linking to Amazon product page; encourage user-generated content with hashtag #NandurbarChilliChallenge
