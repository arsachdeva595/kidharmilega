Santiniketan Leather goods

Birbhum's Santiniketan village has a 70+ year legacy of leather craftsmanship rooted in Visva-Bharati University's Rural Development Programme. The region has established artisan clusters, access to quality vegetable-tanned leather suppliers, and deep knowledge of traditional art dyeing techniques passed through generations.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Birbhum.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Santiniketan artisan clusters (Kona Expressway area) and Visva-Bharati's Craft Centre. Meet master craftsmen at cooperative societies. Study existing brands like Anandapur Leather Works and Santipur Crafts. Attend leather and handicraft expos in Mumbai and Delhi. Budget 2 weeks research.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source vegetable-tanned leather from Kolkata-based tanneries (Tangra area) or ethical suppliers like Agrotech Leather. Connect with dye suppliers in Santiniketan village directly. First batch: 100-200 pieces requires ₹150K-250K for 50-70 kg vegetable leather + dyes + hardware.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing Santiniketan artisan cooperatives (15-20 available) under contract model, or hire 3-5 skilled craftsmen directly. Start with 5-10 signature designs. Establish production setup in Bolpur town (20km from Santiniketan) where rental costs are ₹8K-15K/month. Invest in 2-3 touch-dyeing blocks and basic tools.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam immediately under MSME. Apply for IEC code within 1 week from DGFT portal (Kolkata office). Register brand name and logo with IP cell. Get GI-certified product documentation from Department of Commerce. Obtain BIS certification for leather goods (optional but adds premium). Establish GST registration.
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Artisan Box, and Godrej & Boyce's Nappsa platform (premium Indian marketplace). Approach boutiques in Kolkata (Park Street, Gariahat), Delhi (Khan Market, Hauz Khas), Mumbai (Bandra, Fort). Contact luxury export aggregators like CraftsVilla and IndianRoots. Start with 5-10 high-quality wholesale accounts in first 60 days.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire marketing specialist for social media. Expand product line to 25+ SKUs. Establish tie-ups with 5-10 boutique hotels and heritage properties for bulk orders. Apply for export credit facility from EXIM Bank. Scale artisan team to 15-25. Target €50K monthly export revenue by month 12.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with Instagram influencers in sustainable fashion (50K-500K followers) for product placement; offer 15-20% commission on conversions. Budget: ₹2-5L/month for 3-5 influencers.
  • Create storytelling content around artisan profiles and vegetable tanning process on YouTube and LinkedIn; target eco-conscious B2B buyers and corporate gifting teams. 2 videos/week, ₹50K production budget.
  • Run retargeting Google and Facebook campaigns to Etsy/website visitors with 25% discount codes; focus on high-intent audiences from Germany, France, UK with €50+ AOV. ₹3-8L/month CAC budget.
