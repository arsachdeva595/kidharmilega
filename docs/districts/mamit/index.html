Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Mamit.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Mamit district turmeric farms and connect with farming associations; study turmeric clusters in Telangana (Nizamabad) and Maharashtra (Sangli) to understand processing, grading, and export protocols; attend spice trade exhibitions in Kolkata and Cochin.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source turmeric rhizomes directly from Mamit farmers during harvest (September-December) at ₹40-50/kg; establish relationships with 3-4 primary growers for consistent supply; first batch: 2-3 tons for ₹80K-120K including harvesting and initial processing.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with local artisan processors in Mamit for drying, polishing, and grinding; alternatively, set up small-scale processing unit (500kg/month capacity) costing ₹200K-300K; ensure proper drying tunnels and storage to maintain quality; obtain FSSAI license for value-added products (turmeric powder).
4
Brand & Register
Register business under Udyam (MSME) portal; obtain IEC code from nearest bank for export eligibility; apply for FSSAI license (₹5K-10K); get Organic certification from ICCOA or Vriksha Organic (₹25K-40K for initial audit); register trademark for brand name with USPTO for international protection.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, Blinkit, and BigBasket with minimum order batches of 50kg; approach premium spice retailers (MDH, Everest suppliers) in metro cities; list on Global Trade Platform and Alibaba for international bulk inquiries; conduct direct outreach to nutraceutical companies via LinkedIn with product samples.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 10-15 ton annual capacity; secure bulk export orders from Southeast Asian importers; develop value-added products (turmeric paste, turmeric latte mix); build direct-to-consumer subscription model on Shopify; seek Udyam upgrade and PMEGP subsidy for expansion infrastructure.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with health and wellness influencers on Instagram/YouTube for turmeric recipes, health benefits, and farm-to-table stories; budget ₹2L-3L per quarter for 5-7 micro-influencers with 50K-200K followers.
  • Create content series on LinkedIn targeting B2B nutraceutical buyers highlighting curcumin content, organic certifications, and bulk pricing; establish thought leadership through whitepapers on Mamit turmeric's medicinal properties.
  • Run geo-targeted Google Shopping and Facebook ads in metros (Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore) promoting turmeric as 'Northeastern Organic' with health claims; focus on keywords: organic turmeric, high curcumin, pure spices; budget ₹1L-1.5L monthly.
Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Program)
up to ₹25L grant (35% subsidy for general entrepreneurs, 45% for SC/ST/women/minorities) for processing and packaging unit setup in Mamit.
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
₹50L-200L cluster development grant for spice processing clusters; applicable if forming farmer cooperative in Mamit.
🏚
Mizoram Industries Policy 2018
5-10 year tax exemption and ₹5L-25L capital subsidy for agricultural processing enterprises in notified areas.
🏚
Mizoram Agricultural Promotion Scheme
support for organic certification, farm mechanization, and input subsidies up to 50% cost for registered farmer groups.
