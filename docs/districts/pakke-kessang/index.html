Pakke Kessang's high-altitude terrain, rich volcanic soil, and cool climate create ideal conditions for premium ginger cultivation. The district's remote location and traditional farming practices ensure pesticide-free, organic produce that commands premium prices in specialty markets. Indigenous farming knowledge combined with natural biodiversity makes this ginger distinctly aromatic and potent.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Pakke Kessang.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Pakke Kessang village clusters (Seijosa, Pala) and connect with existing ginger farmer collectives; attend Arunachal Pradesh Agricultural Department workshops; study organic ginger farming at Roing Agricultural Research Station (Dibang Valley, nearby district)
2
Source Raw Materials
Procure GI-certified ginger rhizomes from local farmer cooperatives in Pakke Kessang at ₹60-80 per kg; arrange organic manure from livestock farms nearby; initial 1-acre setup requires ₹3-4 lakhs for quality seeds and inputs
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 3-5 certified organic farmers in Pakke Kessang for contract farming; establish your own 1-2 acre organic plot near Seijosa market hub; hire local labor at ₹300-400/day; process through local agricultural processing units or establish small drying/powder unit (₹2-3 lakh)
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) for agricultural processing; obtain Organic certification from NPOP/IFOAM-accredited bodies (6-12 months, ₹40-60K); apply for IEC code from Guwahati or Itanagar office; trademark brand name with 'Arunachal Ginger' or GI-tag reference
5
First Sale
Launch on Organic portals (Patanjali Wellness, Sahaja Organics, Bigbasket Organic); approach spice exporters in Roing, Pasighat, and Itanagar; supply to Ayurvedic companies (Arya Vaidya Sala, Himalaya Herbals via B2B); establish Instagram/Facebook presence highlighting GI heritage; list on Amazon Wonderland (organic category)
6
Scale
Expand farmer network to 10-15 producers by month 6; launch value-added products (ginger powder, dried ginger, ginger extract); explore institutional B2B sales to institutional buyers and FMCG companies; establish export network through Arunachal Pradesh Bamboo Mission office connections; consider cooperative model for collective branding
