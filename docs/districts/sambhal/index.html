Handicrafts - Horn Bone

Sambhal has a centuries-old tradition of horn-bone artisanship passed down through generations. The district's artisans possess unparalleled skill in carving, inlay work, and design execution. Local availability of raw materials and established export networks make it the hub for premium decorative horn-bone items.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Sambhal.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Sambhal's horn-bone cluster in Juwahri area and Behat village; meet 3-5 master artisans to understand production, time, quality tiers; study export pricing at Delhi Handicraft Cooperative showroom and EPCH (Export Promotion Council for Handicrafts).
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw buffalo horn and bone from local suppliers in Sambhal (₹8-12/kg) and Meerut; order first batch of 50-100 pieces via artisan partnership; estimated first-batch cost ₹40,000-80,000 for materials, labor, and finishing.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner directly with Sambhal artisans (commission-based or equity partnership); establish small workshop with 2-3 artisans; invest in basic carving, finishing, and quality-checking setup; plan 2-3 month lead times for bespoke orders.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (free online at udyamregistration.gov.in); obtain IEC code from DGFT for exports; register brand name with IP India; get Handicraft Certification from Ministry of Commerce; open MSME bank account with eligibility for subsidized credit.
5
First Sale
List on Artisan Collective, Aachho, Crafts Council; approach interior designers via LinkedIn and Houzz; contact export aggregators like GreenShift and Fairstep; pitch to luxury hotels and Airbnb-listed homestays; launch Instagram with behind-the-scenes artisan videos.
6
Scale
After first ₹20L revenue, apply for SFURTI cluster development funds; expand to 5-8 artisans; launch custom design service for corporate clients; set up own micro-showroom in Delhi/Mumbai; partner with international gift fairs in Frankfurt and Birmingham.

How to get customers

  • Create YouTube series 'Master Carver Diaries' documenting Sambhal artisans' craft process; share on Instagram Reels and TikTok to build heritage storytelling brand; target design influencers and home décor accounts for collaboration.
  • Attend international trade shows (Frankfurt Handcraft Fair, Nuremberg Toy Fair); network with luxury gifting distributors and corporate procurement teams; secure 2-3 bulk orders per quarter from US/EU corporate buyers.
