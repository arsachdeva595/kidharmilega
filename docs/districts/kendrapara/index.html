Golden Grass Product

Kendrapara's riverside villages have abundant natural Golden Grass growth in ideal climate conditions. The district has centuries-old weaving traditions and a concentrated artisan population skilled in natural fiber work. The product is 100% biodegradable, aligning with global sustainability demand and offering significant export potential.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Kendrapara.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Kendrapara district artisan clusters in villages like Marshagaon and Ramnagar; connect with DC Handicraft office in Cuttack; study competitor products on fair-trade platforms like Craftroots and Aravind Handicraft.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source dried Golden Grass directly from riverside harvesting villages during Sept-Nov peak season at ₹80-120/kg; negotiate first batch of 500 kg (~₹50K-60K). Contact Kendrapara Handicraft Cooperative Society for bulk supply agreements.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 8-12 skilled artisans from local villages (hourly rate ₹150-200) through village self-help groups; establish workshop in Bahabalpur or Rajkanika town; begin with 50 units/month of mixed products (mats, baskets, boxes).
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam at udyamregistration.gov.in as handicraft micro-enterprise; apply for IEC code via DGFT portal; trademark brand name with IP office; obtain state handicraft certification from Odisha Handicraft Department.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Artisans Hub, Flipkart Samarth, and Etsy with 'handcrafted eco-friendly' positioning; reach out to Nature & Bloom, FabIndia, and The Better India marketplace; leverage Pinterest with DIY styling; initial target 20-30 units/month.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand artisan network to 30+ craftspeople; introduce 8-10 new product SKUs (decorative boxes, lampshades, office organizers); establish export partnerships with German/UK eco-retailers; apply for SFURTI cluster development grant; hire e-commerce manager.
