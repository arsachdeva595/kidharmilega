Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Dehradun.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established bakeries on Rajpur Road and Mall Road (Crown Bakery, Snowman, Moti Bakery); spend 2-3 weeks learning production processes, meeting bakery owners, visiting local flour mills in Chakrata Road area; attend 1-week baking foundation course at Institute of Hotel Management Dehradun.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source refined wheat flour from Bikaner Flour Mills (₹45/kg), eggs from local poultry suppliers in Saharanpur (₹6/piece), butter from Amul distributor near Clock Tower (₹450/500g), yeast from Lesaffre distributor in Delhi (contact through local bakeries); first batch of 50kg flour + ingredients ~₹12,000-15,000.
3
Produce / Partner
Start with small commercial kitchen rental in Prem Nagar (₹8,000-12,000/month with shared oven access) or partner with existing bakery for night-time oven access; alternatively invest in 2-deck commercial oven (₹200k-350k) and small work space; produce 20-30 units daily initially (cakes, pastries, bread).
4
Brand & Register
Register as Micro-enterprise under Udyam (udyamregistration.gov.in); apply for IEC code if exporting; obtain FSSAI registration (food business license) from Uttarakhand District Administration; create brand identity with Dehradun-local naming; register trademark with IP India; get NOC from Municipal Corporation Dehradun.
5
First Sale
Launch on Swiggy Food & Zomato with premium product photos (invest ₹5k in professional shoot); create WhatsApp catalog with daily specials; approach 10-15 corporate offices in Chakrata Road business district with trial samples; distribute flyers at hotels and tourism spots; post daily stories on Instagram/Facebook showing baking process; offer 1-week free delivery trial to first 50 orders.
6
Scale
After ₹15L revenue, expand product line (cookies, breads, diet-specific items); secure permanent shop on Rajpur Road (₹30k-50k/month rent); hire 2-3 trained bakers and 1 delivery person; supply to 15-20 hotels and restaurants; launch subscription model for office pantries; start exporting packaged products to Nepal through established distributors.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 5-star hotels (Auli & Chopta resort chains) for in-room fresh pastries delivery; co-brand as 'Local Doon Favorite' in hotel menus, with 25% commission to bakery.
  • Launch 'Bake with Us' Instagram Reels showing behind-the-scenes production from 4 AM; run weekly baking tutorials; offer first-time buyers ₹100 coupon code #DoonBakesExperience.
  • Create WhatsApp community for 'Daily Special Alerts' to 500+ subscribers; offer bulk discounts for office pantries (min ₹2k/month) with free delivery in 2km radius.
