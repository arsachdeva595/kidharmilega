ODOP Product

Tea

Biswanath benefits from Assam's unique geography — high rainfall, moderate temperatures, and well-drained laterite soil that impart the characteristic astringency and aroma to Assam Orthodox tea. The district sits at the foothills of the Eastern Himalayas, providing ideal altitude and climate for second flush teas prized globally.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Biswanath.

1
Learn / Research
Visit established tea gardens in Biswanath (Namrup area), Dibrugarh, and meet planters at Assam Tea Planters Association. Study processing at Tocklai Tea Research Institute (Jorhat). Attend Tea Board India workshops in Guwahati. Budget: ₹30,000 for 2-week research.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source tea saplings from IBFC Seuj (Biswanath) or Assam Agricultural University nurseries. For fresh leaves, negotiate contracts with existing garden owners. First batch cost for 5 acres cultivation: ₹4-5L including land preparation, saplings, and initial care.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with licensed tea processors in Biswanath or neighboring Dhemaji district (DTPC facilities available). Alternative: Install small-scale processing unit (CTC/Orthodox) costing ₹25-40L. Hire experienced rollers/processors from local labor pools trained under Tea Board schemes.
4
Brand & Register
Register under Udyam (MSME) portal for ₹0 cost. Obtain IEC code from DGFT Guwahati for exports. Get FSSAI food license (₹5,000). Register trademark with IP India. Obtain Tea Board registration certificate.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, BigBasket, and Flipkart under premium tea category. Sell directly via WhatsApp and Instagram to 50+ initial customers. Approach specialty tea retailers (Tea Boutique chains). Contact B2B importers via TradeKey and Global Sources platforms. Target first 5,000 units in first 3 months.
6
Scale
After 6 months, expand to 3-4 branded SKUs (Organic, Orthodox, CTC blends). Establish direct export partnerships with European distributors. Open tea tasting cafes in Guwahati and Assam urban centers. Build agro-tourism revenue (garden stays, processing demos). Invest in certifications: Organic, Rainforest Alliance, Fair Trade.
