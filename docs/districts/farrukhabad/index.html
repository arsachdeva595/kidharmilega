ODOP Product

Farrukhabad Prints

Farrukhabad has a 400-year-old tradition of textile printing with established clusters of skilled artisans in Kali Nadi area. The district's proximity to cotton sources, water availability, and a concentrated ecosystem of dyers, printers, and traders make it the ideal hub for this craft. The GI-tagged Farrukhabad Print is globally recognized and commands premium pricing.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Farrukhabad.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Farrukhabad's Kali Nadi cluster (main printing hub) and Sadar Bazaar to understand production methods, costs, and artisan networks. Spend 5-7 days observing hand-block and screen printing units. Connect with 5-10 established printers and master artisans to learn pricing, lead times, and quality standards.
2
Source Raw Materials
Source raw cotton fabric (60-100 GSM) from Farrukhabad's wholesale markets (₹80-150/meter) or directly from mills in Kanpur (30 km away). Purchase natural dyes from local suppliers or specialist chemical houses in Farrukhabad (₹2000-5000 per kg for indigo, madder). First batch cost (500 meters): ₹45,000-60,000 including dyes, blocks, and artisan fees.
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with 2-3 established printing artisans in Farrukhabad's cluster on commission basis (₹10-25 per meter depending on complexity) rather than owning machines initially. Alternatively, engage SFURTI cluster members who offer subsidized workspace. Start with 5-10 unique design templates and test-produce 200-500 meters across different designs.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME) within 1 week online at udyamregistration.gov.in. Obtain IEC code from DGFT (online, ₹0) for export readiness. Register brand name at Indian Intellectual Property Office. Obtain GI-tagged label certificate from Ministry of Textile to authenticate Farrukhabad origin. Compliance: pollution certificate from district authority (mandatory for textile business).
5
First Sale
Launch on Etsy, Asos Marketplace, and Amazon Handmade for D2C (10% commission). Simultaneously pitch bulk orders to fashion brands via Instagram/email (target indie designers, eco-conscious labels). Join Farrukhabad ODOP exporters association for bulk order aggregation. Attend one textile trade fair (like IITF Delhi or Tex-Fashion in Mumbai) with 50-100 meter samples. Minimum first-month goal: ₹1-1.5L revenue from 3-5 initial orders.
6
Scale
After 6 months, hire 1-2 dedicated design staff and engage 5-8 artisan partnerships to scale production to 3000-5000 meters/month. Build in-house QC lab for quality consistency. Launch proprietary brand website with high-quality photography. Establish B2B distributor relationships in Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore. Target ₹5-8L monthly revenue by month 12. Explore export partnership with direct shipment to USA/EU boutiques.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Instagram/TikTok content series: 60-second videos of artisans hand-printing fabrics, drying processes, and final unfolding. Partner with 5-10 micro-fashion influencers (10k-100k followers) in sustainable fashion niche for organic reach (₹5k-15k per collaboration).
  • B2B email campaigns targeting 200+ indie fashion brands in India/USA with product samples and 10-15% bulk discounts. Personalized pitches emphasizing GI certification and heritage storytelling. Expected 2-3% conversion rate in first quarter.
  • Google Shopping Ads and Pinterest Ads targeting high-intent buyers searching 'hand-printed fabrics,' 'Indian block prints,' 'eco-friendly textiles' (₹1.5-2L quarterly budget). Landing page emphasis on GI certification and artisan fair wages.
