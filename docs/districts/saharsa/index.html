Makhana (Foxnut)

Saharsa's extensive wetlands, flood plains, and shallow water bodies create ideal conditions for makhana cultivation. The region's cool climate, high water retention capacity, and skilled farmer base accumulated over centuries make it uniquely suited for consistent, high-quality makhana production that commands premium global prices.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Saharsa.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Saharsa makhana clusters in Darbhanganj, Kush Nagar and Mahnar block; spend 2-3 weeks with established farmers and processors to understand cultivation cycles, drying, grading, and current export channels; attend Saharsa Makhana Farmers Cooperative meetings.
2
Source Raw Materials
Partner with existing farmer collectives in Saharsa (e.g., Makhana Farmers Federation); source 500-1000 kg raw makhana during harvest (Aug-Oct) at ₹400-600/kg = ₹2-6 lakhs for first batch; identify equipment suppliers in nearby Madhubani for roasting machines.
3
Produce / Partner
Either lease 2-5 acres of existing ponds (₹50-100k/season) or collaborate with existing farmer-processors through contract farming; invest ₹15-25 lakhs in basic processing unit (dryer, roaster, grinder) located in Saharsa town for easy access to farmers and transport.
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam (MSME portal) and IEC code (Directorate General of Foreign Trade); apply for GI certification benefit through District Industries Centre; register brand name on trademark portal; obtain FSSAI licence for food processing (₹5-10k); set up basic website with GI logo.
5
First Sale
Launch on Amazon Fresh, Flipkart, JioMart with premium positioning; contact specialty exporters in Kolkata/Mumbai for bulk orders; reach out to health brands like True Elements and Conscious Food for B2B supply; run targeted Instagram/Facebook ads to metro fitness communities; list on IndianSuperiorSnacks.com and export marketplaces.
6
Scale
Establish your own farmer aggregation network (500+ farmers by month 18); set up export-grade cold storage (₹30-50L) in Saharsa; invest in automated processing to reduce per-unit cost by 20%; launch 3-4 value-added product lines (flavoured, blended, chocolate-coated); hire dedicated export manager; apply for APEDA recognition and GODREJ cold chain support.

Marketing Ideas

How to get customers

  • Partner with 10-15 micro-influencers (50k-500k followers) in fitness/health niche for unboxing and recipe videos; offer 20% discount code; target food bloggers in tier-1 cities with free sample packs.
  • Run Google Search campaigns targeting 'makhana online India', 'foxnut snacks', 'superfood low-calorie' with GI badge emphasis; set daily budget ₹500-1000 for first 3 months.
  • Create LinkedIn B2B content highlighting GI certification, sustainable farming practices, and bulk export capability; reach out directly to nutraceutical and premium food brand procurement teams with sample kits.
