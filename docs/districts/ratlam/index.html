Ratlami Namkeen

Ratlam district has a centuries-old tradition of spice blending and snack preparation passed down through generations. The local climate, soil, and access to premium spices from nearby agricultural zones create ideal conditions for authentic Ratlami Namkeen production. The district's established supply chains with local farmers and existing artisan networks make it the natural hub for this product.

Step-by-Step Guide

How to start

From zero to first sale — concrete steps for Ratlam.

1
Learn / Research
Visit Ratlam's established Namkeen clusters in Sadar Bazar and Chandra Bagh area; interview 10-15 existing producers to understand spice ratios, production techniques, cost structures, and regulatory requirements; attend food processing seminars at CIPET Ratlam
2
Source Raw Materials
Source besan and spices from Ratlam spice markets (Indore Spice Market as backup); negotiate with 3-4 suppliers for raw materials; first batch of 500 kg will cost ₹45,000-55,000; establish relationships with local gram flour mills and certified spice suppliers for consistent quality
3
Produce / Partner
Partner with existing Ratlami Namkeen artisans in Ratlam city through sub-contracting model for initial 6 months (avoid large capex); alternatively, lease a 2,000 sq ft FSSAI-compliant facility near Ratlam industrial area (₹12,000-18,000/month) and hire 3-4 skilled workers familiar with traditional recipes
4
Brand & Register
Register Udyam classification as micro/small enterprise; obtain IEC code for export readiness; get FSSAI registration (Food License Type-Manufacturer); trademark your brand name at IP office; create GMP certification documentation; design packaging with GI mark prominently featured; obtain 80G if planning CSR sales
5
First Sale
Launch on KidharMilega ODOP marketplace within 4 weeks; simultaneously register on Amazon Fresh and BigBasket; pitch to regional distributors like Bibo, Jio Mart, and local wholesalers; attend food expos (Gourmet Food Fest, Regional Food Trade Shows in Mumbai/Delhi); leverage WhatsApp and Instagram for direct B2C initially with 500-gram trial packs
6
Scale
After 6 months with ₹8-12L in revenue, expand packaging formats (200g, 500g, 1kg gift boxes); add variant flavors (mild, extra spicy, chaat masala twist); establish tie-ups with 5-10 regional distribution partners; invest ₹40-50L in food processing automation (mixing, packaging machines); target export markets through Indian Spices Board networking; build B2B relationships with top 20 institutional buyers

Government Schemes

Free money you didn't know about

🏛
PMEGP (Prime Minister Employment Generation Programme)
up to ₹25 lakh subsidized loan at 5% interest for food processing MSMEs with 15-25% government subsidy on capex
🏛
SFURTI (Scheme of Fund for Regeneration of Traditional Industries)
cluster-based support up to ₹3 Cr for multiple units in Ratlam; provides training, testing facilities, and brand promotion
🏚
MP Laghu Udyog Loan Yojana
state government soft loans up to ₹50 lakh at 6% interest for food processing units with 2-year moratorium period
🏚
MP State Food Processing Cluster Development Scheme
40% subsidy on machinery and equipment for registered food processing units in designated clusters including Ratlam district
